## Virtuosity

**Virtuosity** is a [Shard](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shard "Shard") of [Adonalsium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adonalsium "Adonalsium") who [Splintered](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Splinter#Formation "Splinter") herself. The Vessel was an unknown female,[^1] she is possibly one of the scholars Hoid questioned about talent.[^2]

## Intent

Virtuosity's [Intent](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Intent "Intent") concerns artistic intent, talent, and appreciation.[^3]

It appears to have some association with mathematics, as its [Investiture](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Investiture "Investiture") and [Splinters](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Splinter "Splinter") are attracted by numbers in the [Artist's sequence of progress](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Artist%27s_sequence_of_progress "Artist's sequence of progress").[^4]

## Associated Magics

### Hijo

[Hijo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hijo "Hijo") are spirits found on [Komashi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Komashi "Komashi").[^5] They naturally reside underground, and are attracted by thoughts, emotions, and artistic expressions.[^5] They are also attracted to expressions of the [Artist's sequence of progress](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Artist%27s_sequence_of_progress "Artist's sequence of progress"), especially as it relates to artistic expression.[^4]

Hijo are a major part of the lives of people all over Komashi, and are the key to their survival on the planet.[^5] They can be bound by a [yoki-hijo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yoki-hijo "Yoki-hijo") into useful tools and devices,[^4] or be utilized to produce and supply [hion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hion "Hion").[^6] The hijo are related to Komashi's strange geothermal activity, apparently being directly responsible for the intense heating of the ground in the region of [Torio](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torio "Torio").[^4]

## History

Like all Shards, Virtuosity was created at the [Shattering](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shattering "Shattering") of [Adonalsium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adonalsium "Adonalsium") and picked up by an unknown Vessel.[^7] [^8] In the millennia that followed, Virtuosity primarily spent her time exploring various artistic expressions throughout the [cosmere](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cosmere "Cosmere"), and consequently never settled in one location for very long.[^9]

At some point, Virtuosity Splintered herself for unknown reasons in the [UTol system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/UTol_system "UTol system"), in the vicinity of [Komashi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Komashi "Komashi").[^1] [Design](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Design "Design") asserts that the entire region is filled with Splinters of Virtuosity in the wake of this.[^5] [Hoid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hoid "Hoid") believes that her Splintering may have contributed to the odd way [UTol](https://coppermind.net/wiki/UTol "UTol") is visible through the [shroud](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shroud "Shroud") on Komashi.[^1] Either her Splintering remained unknown to the cosmere’s general public, or she was reconstructed after the disappearance of Komashi’s shroud and went into hiding.[^10] [^11]

## Trivia

- The word "Virtuosity" is often mistaken to be related to the notion of " [virtue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtue) ". The term actually refers to the characteristics of a [virtuoso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtuoso): someone having outstanding talents or technical abilities, especially in the arts.
- Brandon considered naming the Shard "Artistry", but felt the name "Virtuosity" fit with other Shard names, like [Odium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Odium "Odium"), better.[^3]
- A future book will explore Virtuosity's story.[^12]
- The sacred number of Virtuosity is most likely 13.[^13]
- The Push-Pull theme can be seen in all magic systems, but is most pronounced with Virtuosity's Investiture.[^14]
