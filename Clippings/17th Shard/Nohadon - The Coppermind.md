## Nohadon

> “ *I think life **is** fair. It's merely that often, you can’t immediately see what balances it.* ”
> 
> —Nohadon in [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar's]] vision [^5]

**Nohadon** is a historic [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Rosharan]] king and early [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]] who lived during the [[Heraldic Epochs - The Coppermind|Heraldic Epochs]]. He is most famous as the author of [*The Way of Kings*](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)"), a philosophical treatise in the form of a collection of stories.[^6]

## Name

> “ *"Well, I was born with the name Bajerden, but no one seems to like that one.”* ”
> 
> —Nohadon in Dalinar's vision [^7]

Nohadon's given name was **Bajerden**.[^7] "Nohadon" is not his real name, but rather a holy honorific given to him by the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|ardents]] thousands of years after his death [^8] (under Vorin rules, the letter "h" can stand in for any other letter, making "Nohadon" perfectly symmetrical and thus holy).[^9] Histories occasionally call him **Behardan**.[^10] [^8]

## Appearance and Personality

In his youth, Nohadon had dark hair, which he wore pulled back into a braid, and a short, pointy beard.[^8] It can be presumed he was [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyed]].[^11] As he got older, his hair went gray and he gained numerous wrinkles, but the pointy beard remained.[^5] When dressed as a king, he had golden threads winding through his hair, coming together on his forehead in the shape of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] symbol, and wore white-and-gold robes with voluminous sleeves.[^8]

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] thought that the [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] would look similar to Nohadon, though that turned out not to be the case.[^12]

> “ *He realized that people couldn’t continue to lean on the Heralds. His was the era where powers came to the ordinary people, not just their demigods. He was the one who realized that without change, they’d always fall once the Heralds left. Because the Heralds couldn’t always protect the people.*”
> 
> —Hoid talking about Nohadon [^4]

Young Nohadon was an angry, exhausted man, likely due to having just suffered through a war. He was uncertain of himself and his goals, and sought advice on statecraft from his advisors. At some point, he even considered abdicating the throne. Nonetheless, he had a clear vision of what he wanted to accomplish: to have the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] serve people, rather than rule over them. He believed that he had to enforce this, even if he had to go to war to do so.[^8] He also realised that mankind could never end the cycle of Desolations and win the war while relying on the Heralds to fight for them.[^4]

In his older age, Nohadon became significantly more glib, whimsical and better-natured, having greatly matured over the decades. He had a wry, somewhat sarcastic sense of humor, and a charitable nature, chatting with common citizens and giving food out to the poor. He enjoyed the simple pleasures of life, like chatting with his people or shopping. He didn't hesitate to use his powers for the most banal things, like jumping several stories out the window rather than climbing down the stairs. He was, in fact, very cavalier with his safety, never taking guards with him even when going out of the palace. Nonetheless, he was sombre when recalling the dark events of the past, and had no problem dispensing wisdom he had gathered over the years.[^5]

## Attributes and Abilities

Nohadon is implied to have been an absolute monarch.[^13] His ability to completely rebuild a ruined kingdom over several decades implies considerable amount of administrative talent. Additionally, he was capable of projecting military power across vast distances, being able to take a large scout group all the way to the foothills of the [[Misted Mountains - The Coppermind|Misted Mountains]], on the opposite side of the continent.[^5]

He was a [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]] from a young age,[^8] and was said to be considered special among them,[^14] suggesting that he may have been a [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]].[^15] Nonetheless, he possessed enough ability to easily survive a jump from a considerable height. He also appears to have had some knowledge of [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realmatic Theory]], as he educates Highprince [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] on the existence and nature of [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical]], [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive]] and [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realms]] in a vision.[^5]

Most famously, Nohadon was a philosopher-king. His best-known work is *[The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)")*, but it's implied that he wrote several other texts on logic and philosophy.[^16]

He was also a gifted haggler, and knew how to make [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] loaf bread, both of which were apparently his hobbies.[^5]

Due to his persistence in the spiritual realm (which is shown to be different to the fabrications of historical figures in Dalinar's visions) him being able to reference [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] directly and answer questions outside of the script) It has been theorized that he is a highly invested being, his actions being most similar to [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] and the Dragons, who are both seen to be able to communicate through the spiritual realm. This combined with his attitude towards the shard [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] make a convincing case that Nohadon is more on the level of the [[Shards]] than [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].

## History

### Life

Nohadon was a king of one of the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]], [[Alethela - The Coppermind|Alethela]], some time prior to year 31 of the Sixth Epoch.[^3] [^17] [^18] He became a [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]] early in his life, and took the throne young, during a [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]] that lasted for eleven years, killing nine out of ten of his subjects. This, and a prior conflict with another Surgebinder named [Alakavish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alakavish "Alakavish") who also started the conflict, left his kingdom in ruins and him jaded to the point where he considered abdicating.[^8] He eventually attempted to unite other Surgebinders by force, but failed. This forced him to find a different, more peaceful path.[^5]

As decades passed, Nohadon rebuilt his kingdom, until eventually it became a bustling, colorful place. At some point, he went to war over access to trade routes of [Makabak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makabak "Makabak"), which took him near the border of [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin Kak Nish]]. He would stay there for a long time, in a house of a woman who would later meet a tragic fate. It was then that he learned to bake [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] bread, a habit he enjoyed during later years of his life.[^5]

Around that time, Nohadon started a family and made his famous journey from [[Abamabar - The Coppermind|Abamabar]] to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]]. He walked alone, without any guards or attendants, and no money, instead making a living through singing and manual labor.[^10] The trip took a very long time - the lowest number given is sixty days, and that was when he was still within his own lands - and when he eventually made it to Urithiru's gates, he was all but unrecognizable.[^19] [^10]

In his old age, Nohadon remained in his capital, the city now known as [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"). He would often take walks outside of his palace, shopping and haggling without any guards.[^5]

### In Visions

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Nohadon---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Palomaloid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Palomaloid "Coppermind:Artists/Palomaloid")

In a vision with [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] and the nascent personality of the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]]

> “ *Sometimes, a hypocrite is nothing more than a man who is in the process of changing.* ”

Nohadon appeared in three of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin's]] visions. The first vision was one of the regular ones, sent by the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] during a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]], and cast Dalinar in the role of [Karm](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Karm "Karm"), an advisor to the king. In it, Nohadon shared his anxieties about ruling, and his frustration with other [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]]. The vision was important mainly thanks to the very last thing Dalinar said in it, which was a sentence [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] recognized, allowing her to begin the translation of [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]].[^8] Later, Dalinar brought [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]] into the same vision in an attempt to parlay with her.[^20]

The second vision was far stranger, ocurring without Stormfather's input while Dalinar was asleep. In it, Dalinar spoke with older Nohadon, and walked through the city with him. Unlike the regular visions, here Nohadon was keenly aware who he was speaking to, and referenced things that had happened to Dalinar before. While this, as well as the vision's dramatic end, imply that this was a nightmare, Nohadon talked about things Dalinar didn't know at the time, such as the [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|three Realms]] or the ancient conflict near [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin Kak Nish]], suggesting that this might've been the real Nohadon, or some preserved memory of him.[^5]

The third one happened just after [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] Ascended and became [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] Vessel. After an initial confrontation between [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and Honor following the Ascension, Dalinar found himself inside a vision once again. On this occasion, Nohadon talked to Dalinar as if he were an old friend and commented on events that happened the last time they met, showing that he remembered the events of the last vision. Dalinar began to question Nohadon, trying to find a way to win against Odium, realizing how a direct confrontation would just rend the world asunder. While he was discussing with Nohadon about what victory looked like to him and what could be the best possible outcome, the power of the Shard of Honor that had become self-aware, joined in the conversation. The vision was important because it let Dalinar know that even the power of the Shards, same as people and [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]], could learn and change, which helped him make the difficult choice that followed it.[^7]

## Legacy

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Nohadon---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [botanicaxu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu "Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu")

*[The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)")* is the oldest preserved text in [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar's]] human history. Among others, [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]] and [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] were heavily impacted by it in their middle age, as it led them to rethink their life philosophy and, in Dalinar's case, led to him becoming a [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]].[^10] [^21] Nohadon's influence, however, spreads far beyond [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") royalty. He's one of the most influential writers in the history of Roshar, with his texts being read and analyzed by scholars to this very day. His name is immediately recognizable for people who study ancient history, logic and philosophy.[^22] [^16] [^1]

It's highly possible that Nohadon had a hand in organizing the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] into the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], as the ancient Knights were said to have based their philosophies and ideals off *The Way of Kings*.[^1]

He's one of the few people to ever be praised by the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], with the great spren considering him to have been "a good man".[^19]

## Trivia

- The idea that Nohadon was one of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") has been directly debunked.[^14]
- In [*The Way of Kings* Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_Prime "The Way of Kings Prime"), Nohadon was known only by his alternative name of Bajerdan, with *The Way of Kings* being a far more revered book that served as a general rulebook for monarchs.
- Nohadon was based on [King Benjamin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Benjamin "wikipedia:King Benjamin"), a figure from the Book of Mormon.[^23]
