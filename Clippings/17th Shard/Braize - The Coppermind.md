## Braize

> “ *The place is barren, devoid of life, merely a dark sky, endless windswept crags, and a broken landscape. And a lot of souls, a lot of not particularly sane souls.* ”

**Braize** is a [[17th Shard#Planets|planet]] in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system").[^3] It is the third planet from the sun, after [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] and [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].

## Etymology

Braize is named after [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]].[^4] Further details of its derivation are currently unknown.

## Ecology and Geography

> “ *Of course, I would need to send them back to Braize anyway. The planet had its strange properties, attracting souls, but I needed them to … act as a lock, so to speak. Their souls worked after the fashion of my own—which trapped Odium in this system—upon the Fused. This was a wonderful solution. It let them experience, in a small way, what it was to be divine.*”
> 
> — [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast]] on Braize and the Oathpact [^5]

Braize is a cold, barren and inhospitable world that is farther out from the sun than Roshar. It contains no biological life on its surface.[^2] Though it is habitable, albeit barely.[^6] Instead, the planet is full of nothing more than a broken, rocky landscape full of crags and chasms with no sources of light available. Even the sky is described as being dark at all times.[^2] [^7] During [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin's]] vision of the planet he experiences strong winds as well as flashes of light, potentially lightning strikes, but it is unclear how much of this was accurate and how much was added by [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] to frighten him.[^7] The planet does contain an atmosphere, albeit a weak one.[^2] [^7]

Due to the extreme nature of its landscape the only life on the planet is that of self-aware [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]], as well as the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]].[^2] [^8] Despite this however only Odium's spren are welcomed; for those not aligned with him visiting is both difficult and dangerous.[^8] [^9] One such danger that [Raboniel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel") spoke of while discouraging [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] of ever visiting was that of a "storm" that apparently surrounds the planet in [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), though it is unclear however what she meant by this.[^2]

Braize possesses a metallic core, and the metal at its core inherently attracts Investiture and souls to itself.[^5] [^6] [^10] [^11] [^12] Through this attractive force applied to Investiture and souls, it is stated to create a "well of souls." [^12] This was a key aspect of why it was chosen as the place to seal away the Fused, as it is able to attract them to the planet.[^5] [^11] Even Vessels, with their increased knowledge and understanding of reality, find its properties strange and fascinating.[^4] [^5]

## History

Prior to the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]], Braize was formed in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system") by [Adonalsium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adonalsium "Adonalsium").[^13] [^14] For the majority of its history the planet was inhospitable and uninhabited.

Honor showed great interest in the planet when he arrived at the Rosharan system, intrigued by the Investiture-attracting properties of its metallic core.[^6]

It was during the initial clashes between [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] on its closest neighbor, [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], that its first inhabitants would arrive to Braize. As part of an effort to halt the rival [[Shards|Shard]], Honor deliberately bound Odium to the planet in order to stop his conquest of the [[17th Shard|Cosmere]].[^15] [^16] [^17] As part of this the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]] was formed which also bound the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") and [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] to the planet. As per the pact so long as the Heralds did not choose to leave the planet the Fused would also be unable to escape; however, once even a single Herald broke and chose to return to Roshar the Fused would follow suit causing a [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]] on Roshar.[^16] [^17] After many years of torture the Heralds began losing their sanity and decided to abandon the Oathpact at the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], leaving only [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] behind.[^18] This exile from Roshar lasted 4500 years [^19] before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] began -- allowing the Fused to permanently live on Roshar.

Braize primarily served as a prison planet for the Fused and holds no inhabitants besides them;[^8] [^20] this makes it the third of the three planets in the Rosharan system to hold sentient life.[^21] The only type of [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] that are welcomed on world are Voidspren.[^9] Because of its inhabitants and the presence of Odium the world is considered unsafe by outsiders.[^8] Despite the native population being bound on Braize they can still influence Roshar and [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] due to their proximity and the way [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] and [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] works between the three worlds.[^22] [^23]

## Prison

For more information on Braize's role as a prison, see [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].

Braize's most important function is that of a prison.[^20] [^11] [^5] After every [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]], the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] are forced to return to Braize, and are locked into it through the process of Isolation.[^17] They are kept sealed by the presence of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), who are able to create a "lock" exploiting their strong [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to Odium.[^5] [^11] [^24] As long as the Heralds remain on Braize, Odium's forces cannot travel to [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. To counteract this, Fused hunt them down and torture them until at least one gives up, restarting the cycle. Because of this, [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]] has spent the past four and a half thousand years there.[^16] Dead [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], on the other hand, do not go to Braize.[^25]

Even following a Herald breaking, departing from Braize is difficult, and grows more so the more Invested the prisoner is. Some smaller [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]], like [Ulim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulim "Ulim"), are capable of sneaking out even while the torture remains ongoing; the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] and [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclasts]] can only come out once the Heralds leave; and Odium's most powerful servants take a long time to cross over even after a Desolation is already in full swing.[^26] [^27]

## Culture

In the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] religion, Braize is known as [Damnation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Damnation "Damnation"), although it is referred to by its proper name in older songs.[^28] Those who have committed particularly grave sins are supposed to go there after death.[^29] It's unclear how much it figures into other Rosharan cultures -- the Shin know about it, but the [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iriali]] Long Trail does not include it.[^30] [^31]

Unlike the rest of [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system"), Braize is based around the number nine rather than ten.[^32] Like all other planets in the cosmere, it has a representation in [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), although its nature is unknown.[^33]

## Trivia

- Some people on Roshar believe that Braize is inhabited with people.[^34]
- The Iriali never visited Braize.[^31]
