## First of the Sun

> “ *If you wish more, seek these waters in person and overcome the tests we have created. Only in this will you earn our respect.* ”

**First of the Sun**, commonly referred to as **Drominad** by the wider [[17th Shard|cosmere]], is the first planet in the [Drominad system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Drominad_system "Drominad system").[^3] [^5] The planet possesses a single moon, called **First of the First**.[^3] Though it is not directly inhabited by a [[Shards|Shard]], the planet has a [perpendicularity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji%27s_Eye "Patji's Eye").[^6]

The planet is inhabited by an ethnic group called the [Eelakin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eelakin "Eelakin"), though natives are by outsiders often simply referred to as Drominadians.[^7] [^8] The planet is particularly noteworthy because of the magical birds, called [Aviar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar"), that are endemic to it.[^9] These birds can bestow talents to their owners, and has made First of the Sun a target for more technologically advanced planets.[^10]

## Geography and Ecology

### The Pantheon

The Pantheon is an archipelago of around forty islands, about three weeks of rowing away from the [Eelakin homeisles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Homeisles "Homeisles").[^7] Viewed from a distance, the islands of the Pantheon appear similar to the relatively harmless homeisles, but are in reality far more lethal.[^7] Some of the Pantheon's islands have sheltered and gradual beaches, while others, such as [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(island\) "Patji (island)"), have steep and rocky ones.[^11] Though the Pantheon Islands are traditionally considered holy, the Eelakin began industrializing the islands as they advanced technologically.[^11] [^12]

The terrestrial flora and fauna that inhabits the Pantheon is generally extremely deadly. These dangers were created by Patji, an avatar of [[Autonomy - The Coppermind|Autonomy]], as tests for any who chooses to visit.[^4] [^13] Despite this, the most noteworthy creatures on the islands are the Aviar, Invested birds that can bestow talents to their owners. They inhabit several, if not all, of the Pantheon's islands, though they are most common on Patji, as it is there they gain their magical abilities in their youths.[^14] [^9] The marine life that resides in the Pantheon is lethal as well. Among the beings that reside there are the [deepwalkers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deepwalker "Deepwalker"), which are enormous crustacean creatures native to the Pantheon.[^7] [^15] As the islands were industrialized, the deepwalkers were either killed or driven off.[^16] [Slimfish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Slimfish "Slimfish") and octopuses also inhabit the waters within the Pantheon.[^7] [^11]

#### Patji

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/First-of-the-Sun---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Esther Hi'ilani Candari](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Esther_Hi%27ilani_Candari "Coppermind:Artists/Esther Hi'ilani Candari")

Industrialized Patji.

Patji is the largest island in the Pantheon, located at the heart of the islands. The island towers like a wedge rising from the sea, and is an inhospitable place of sharp peaks and deep jungle.[^7] Its shores are rocky, with steep drop-offs, and for this reason it can be dangerous to land on the island by boat.[^11] Further inland, thick jungle dominates the island. Near the top of Patji's main peak, located on the easternmost side of the island, a river inside of a canyon leads to the planet's perpendicularity.[^17] [^18] Around this perpendicularity, hundreds of Aviar may roost at a time, and it is home to the [glowing worms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Glowing_worm "Glowing worm") that gift the Aviar with their magical powers.[^19] As the island became more industrialized by the Eelakin people, many of the island's dangers were driven away, and the perpendicularity became accessible by train and by car.[^18] [^20] Wooden docks were built to neutralize Patji's treacherous shores, and large Aviaries mass-produce Aviar.[^16] The island's climate is hotter than that of the homeisles.[^20]

Patji is said to be the most perilous of all the islands in the Pantheon, being home to a large amount of dangerous plants and animals.[^21] Its beaches are most dangerous, as they are vulnerable to both terrestrial and marine predators, many of whom hunt by sensing the minds of their prey.[^11] The jungle further inland, only slightly more safe, is home to many dangerous species, such as [deathants](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deathant "Deathant"), [Cakoban's fingers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cakoban%27s_finger "Cakoban's finger"), and [tokkas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tokka "Tokka"). The [nightmaws](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nightmaw "Nightmaw") are, however, considered the apex predators on Patji.[^19] They are almost blind and deaf, but hunt by tracking the minds of their prey. For this reason, the safest places on Patji are around Aviar, who usually roost in trees, since they often have abilities to hide the minds of nearby creatures from predators.[^22]

#### Other Islands

Aside from Patji, there are around forty other islands in the Pantheon. Among them is the small island of [Sori](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sori "Sori"), which doesn't contain much, to a [trapper](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trapper "Trapper"), valuable game, and is considered the least of the Pantheon's islands.[^7] Other islands include [Gofi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gofi "Gofi") and an unnamed island or group of islands home to the 'streamer' variety of Aviar.[^15] [^14]

### The Homeisles

The homeisles are made up of a number of islands, and are inhabited by the Eelakin people, who have built cities on them. They islands vary greatly in distance from the capital, with some of the ones farther away being named the outer isles.[^23] The plants and animals that exist on the homeisles are far less dangerous than in the Pantheon, with the island being described as tamed and manicured.[^21] [^23] Food is more plentiful than in the Pantheon, with at least fish being available.[^7] [^10] The islands also have domesticated dogs.[^18]

### The Mainland

The mainland is a landmass to the south of the homeisles. It is described as a frozen wasteland, and contains no known archaeological record of humans ever having lived there.[^24] [^25] The mainland is not completely devoid of life, however, with ravens being native to it.[^18] [^26]

## History

### Creation and Early History

First of the Sun was formed at an unknown date. The planet's Investiture predates the involvement of any Shard, and exists rather as natural, residue Investiture from [Adonalsium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adonalsium "Adonalsium").[^27] [^13] Though the planet has never been the residence of a Shard, several have meddled with it in different ways.[^13] Of particular note is the Shard Autonomy, who directly created [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(being\) "Patji (being)") as an avatar on the planet.[^13] [^28] [^29] The associated perpendicularity, however, was created naturally, independent of the influence of Autonomy or any other Shard.[^27] This perpendicularity is unique to First of the Sun, as no other planet in the Drominad system has one.[^30]

### Early Arrivals

#### Cakoban and the Eelakin

The Eelakin people arrived at First of the Sun at an unknown date after traveling through [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar").[^31] It is unknown from which world they originate, and the reason for their migration remains unclear. During this journey, they were led by [Cakoban the Navigator](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cakoban "Cakoban"), and after a time they discovered the perpendicularity and went through to the Pantheon, where they tamed the Aviar.[^31] Most stayed, though a contingent, including Cakoban, left again and eventually found the [Dakwara](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dakwara "Dakwara"), which would go on to serve the Eelakin for the subsequent hundred years.[^32] [^20] After a time, most of the Eelakin that now inhabited First of the Sun left the Pantheon for the homeisles, though some seemingly remained as trappers.[^31]

#### The Iriali

At some point, according to an ancient record from the [Iriali Long Trail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iriali#History "Iriali"), the [Iriali](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iriali "Iriali") investigated First of the Sun as a potential homeland. In the record, they write of birds, bugs, a deadly island, and a perpendicularity.[^6] It is unknown when this journey took place, but it predates the Middle-old Iriali language shift during the 3rd millennium,[^1] and postdates the creation of [[Silverlight - The Coppermind|Silverlight]].[^6]

#### Silverlight Expeditions

According to records that [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") connected with the First of the Sun perpendicularity, expeditions from Silverlight encountered a well of power in Shadesmar and traveled through it a couple of times.[^6] None of the expeditions returned, and it is unknown when they were undertaken.[^30] Up until the Eelakin revealed the perpendicularity's existence to the wider cosmere, these expeditions and their discoveries seem to have been outside general knowledge, with many Silverlight [arcanists](https://coppermind.net/edit/Arcanist?redlink=1 "Arcanist (page does not exist)") even doubting the perpendicularity's existence.[^6]

### Patji's Challenge to Hoid

During the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] requested assistance from Autonomy in combating [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^33] [^34] The letter was answered by Patji, an avatar of Autonomy, who declined the offer unless Hoid earned their respect by surviving the challenges posed by the Pantheon.[^35] [^4] [^13] Hoid never visited First of the Sun, and so never completed this challenge.[^36] He did previously attempt to travel to the planet, however.[^37]

### Arrival of the Ones Above

As many of the cosmere's worlds advanced technologically, First of the Sun became a target for conquest due to their Aviar and perpendicularity. The first outsiders to approach them were the [Malwish Empire](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Malwish_Empire "Malwish Empire"), who were by the Eelakin referred to as the Ones Above.[^38] The Malwish did not intentionally seek out First of the Sun, but rather accidentally encountered it in the Cognitive Realm, whereafter they visited it in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]].[^39] Due to interplanetary laws, the Malwish were prevented from outright conquering the Eelakin by force.[^40] Instead, they brought gifts of technology and medicine, hoping to make the Eelakin technologically advanced enough that the interplanetary laws would no longer apply.[^41] The Eelakin eventually caught on, and [Vathi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vathi "Vathi"), their president, delayed the advancement to a rate that could still be supported by their infrastructure.[^12]

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/First-of-the-Sun---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Esther Hi'ilani Candari](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Esther_Hi%27ilani_Candari "Coppermind:Artists/Esther Hi'ilani Candari")

Sixth of the Dusk's expedition into Shadesmar.

The conflict spanned eight years, and toward the end, the Eelakin were growing more dependent on the technology, medicine, and power supply from the Malwish.[^8] In attempts to force the Eelakin to agree to their demands, the Malwish intentionally caused upheaval by, for example, delaying power shipments.[^10] Eventually, other outsiders approached them as well, including an emissary of the [Skybreakers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreaker "Skybreaker"), from [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^10] As a desperate last attempt to remain independent, the Eelakin sent out explorers into Shadesmar in order to find allies. Though their first expedition failed, the second one, bearing only the trapper Sixth of the Dusk, succeeded. Sixth of the Dusk returned to the Eelakin, having rediscovered the Eelakin's ancient talent of [Navigation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Navigator "Navigator"), accompanied by both the Dakwara and by allies from Silverlight. The Dakwara helped them with defending their own perpendicularity and reinforcing their independence, while their Silverlight allies aided them with negotiations and interplanetary communication.[^42]

### Entering the Space Age

As the independence of the Eelakin was strengthened, they began training new Navigators to navigate the unexplored parts of the Cognitive Realm. The estimated monetary value of this talent was immense, and the Eelakin began making plans to rent out these Navigators. They also initiated contact with [[Xisisrefliel - The Coppermind|Xisisrefliel]], a [dragon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragon_\(cosmere\) "Dragon (cosmere)") on Silverlight. Xisisrefliel agreed to publicize the news of their Navigators, and argued that the Eelakin would have little to fear from invaders in the future, due to the leverage they now had with their Navigators. In addition to that, Xisisrefliel also gave the Eelakin free translation devices, lodestars, information, and a method of traveling to and from Silverlight, in return for the right of first negotiation for reduced tariffs. With this contact, the Eelakin began making preparations for opening lines to more impartial Silverlight communications hubs, as well as building infrastructure to support the influx of foreign ships.[^42]

## Peoples

### The Eelakin

The Eelakin are an ethnic group on First of the Sun, indigenous to the homeisles. Originally, they settled on the planet after traveling through the Cognitive Realm.[^32] Though they have stories of empires and great wars, their world of origin is unknown.[^25] In general, the Eelakin have brown skin, and often carry Aviar as companions.[^23] They revere the islands of the Pantheon, with Patji being considered the greatest and the creator of the world.[^7]

### Others

As other planets became more technologically advanced, First of the Sun was turned into a target for conquest, primarily for their Aviar and perpendicularity. When these outsiders began to visit, they were, by the Eelakin, referred to as the Ones Above. Both the Malwish Empire, from Scadrial, and the Skybreakers, from Roshar, vied for control, seeking to force the Eelakin into allying with them.[^10] Ultimately, both parties were rejected. Other than that, there are also several of the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] inhabiting First of the Sun, observing the Eelakin in secret. In general, the Sleepless, even those who haven't visited, seem more informed about the planet than many other peoples.[^31]
