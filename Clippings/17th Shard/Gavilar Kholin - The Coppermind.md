## Gavilar Kholin

> “ *My people need to be united, and I need an empire that won't simply turn to infighting once I am gone.* ”

**Gavilar Kholin** is the King of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), a nation on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], until his [[Assassination of Gavilar - The Coppermind|assassination]] by [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] under the orders of the [[Listener - The Coppermind|Parshendi]]. He is the brother of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] and married to [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani Kholin]], with whom he has two children, [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] and [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]].

## Appearance and Personality

> “ *My husband was an excellent king, (...) but he didn't have a single scholarly finger on his left hand. (...) He was a man built after the classical masculine ideal.* ”
> 
> —From Navani's biography of Gavilar [^6]

### Appearance

Gavilar looks like a true Alethi. He’s tall and has tan skin. He also has strong features, a square chin, a black beard with a hint of white, and pale green eyes.[^1] He used to be handsome, but he once broke his nose and suffered a scar on his chin.[^7] He is regal with his beard combed, glowing gemstones on the fingers and uniform of newer styles.[^8] He has a set of bright blue Shardplate,[^9] which he often lends to others in favor of wearing a modern suit, and wears a black iron circlet as crown.[^10]

### Personality

He's an ambitious man who is obsessed with the rise of House Kholin and overshadowing the Sunmaker and his legacy.[^9] He wants to lead a sustainable kingdom and doesn't overlook responsibility. He is very knowledgeable of history; he has theories on why Sunmaker’s kingdom did not stand as well as knowledge on the history of some blades including Oathbringer that would eventually be Dalinar's.[^9] He is known for his political skill for making alliances [^8] and is more inclined to trick his enemies into turning against each other,[^9] though he doesn’t feel guilty if his strategy included pillaging or brutal killing,[^11] as his best friend and trusted adviser Torol Sadeas would push for.[^12] He had a tendency to use those around him, even if he cared for them.[^13]

If he was still alive, Gavilar would be proud of some aspects of Dalinar and less proud of others; for example, he would think Dalinar not ambitious enough.[^14]

In his youth, he had a habit of summoning and dismissing his Blade when getting ready for a battle, perhaps as a way to relieve stress.[^9] It's unknown if he retained it in his middle age.

## Attributes and Abilities

### Shardwielding

Gavilar's [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), [Firestorm](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Firestorm "Firestorm"),[^15] was six feet long with a design along the Blade that resembled burning flames that seems to glow. Gavilar's Plate is painted blue with gold bands around the edge of each plate. The helm has three waves of wings, which are small and almost horn-like. People who see his Plate consider it beautiful. Gavilar's skill in using the Blade and Plate can be matched by very few people.[^1] Gavilar's Shards are now used for training purposes.[^16]

### Visions

Sometime before his death, Gavilar begins receiving [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") from the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] during every [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]].[^17] [^18] These visions feel real to him.[^19] He experiences them as himself, but other people in the visions see him as someone else, not as an outsider. At the end of every one of them, Gavilar hears the voice of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] speaking to him, commanding him to "unite them." As the visions are pre-recorded, Gavilar cannot communicate with Honor.[^20] Much like his brother, he was on the path of becoming a [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]], but unlike Dalinar, he never reached that stage.[^21] He would use these visions and the Stormfather to try and become a [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald").[^22]

## History

### Early Life

Gavilar and Dalinar were born into a provincial branch of the Kholin family, with Gavilar being born some time before.[^23] His mother died when he and his brother were young, and his father was absent from his life and spent his time dueling. After their father suffered a permanent head injury in a duel, Gavilar took over a paternal role for his younger brother Dalinar, teaching him things like how to shave.[^24]

Gavilar was only fourth dahn, and not considered a serious contender for highprince. He eventually conquered the [Kholin princedom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholin_princedom "Kholin princedom") however, killing some of his relatives and taking the title of highprince for himself.[^25]

From the start, Gavilar was supported by his brother. Dalinar would often introduce Gavilar to people he felt Gavilar should know. This led to some confusion when Gavilar was introduced by his brother Dalinar to [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]]. Dalinar introduced them because he wished to court Navani himself, but Gavilar believed Dalinar intended him to court Navani with the introduction.[^26]

### Unification of Alethkar

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] remembers the beginning of the war as he and Gavilar wanting "stuff" that others had and fighting to get the "stuff." Gavilar never refuted that story at the beginning, but, by around the time of the battle on Rathalas, he was looking more towards building a kingdom that would mean something.[^8] This seems to be connected to the birth of his first child [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]].[^9]

Gavilar, [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") and Dalinar led the war. Gavilar claimed the throne and made alliances; Sadeas dealt with problems; and Dalinar won battles.[^8] Gavilar was more inclined for a strategy that would turn the highprinces against each other. Sadeas, however, pulled him towards brutality;[^9] he did not seem to be concerned if his troops pillaged a city.[^11] It is implied that the war was long and bloody, but Gavilar eventually won it after the defeat of Highprince [Kalanor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kalanor "Kalanor"), who was pushed into battle after Gavilar's forces pillaged his villages and killed all peacemakers.[^11]

Towards the end of the war, Gavilar started talking of the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War"), likely after he started receiving [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") from the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]].[^8]

### Ruling Alethkar

> “ *He is exactly what we need... Hard enough to lead a war; soft enough to be beloved during peace. He has foresight and wisdom.*”
> 
> —Sadeas on Gavilar [^27]

After winning the war, Gavilar took the throne as King of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"). Gavilar did prove to be a proficient leader; he made sure the vision was towards a unified kingdom, and politics were the main tool for doing so. He wanted to ensure Elhokar would be able to hold it together without need of "putting out fires." [^28]

He was not soft, but approached things differently, using propaganda,[^28] talking to heirs of the highprinces on civic projects, censuring Dalinar and Sadeas on losing the Rift,[^29] and even the initial approach towards rebels, trying to scare them by fighting on the borders.[^10] Some highprinces considered this softness or weakness, yet he could be hard; some examples are his disappointment on knowing Dalinar had spared the heir of [Tanalan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tanalan "Tanalan") [^28] or the way he talked of attacking [[Herdaz - The Coppermind|Herdaz]] or [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved").[^10]

It was during this time that he was most committed to the Codes of War. He talked of the Radiants as truly great when Alethkar was looked up to.[^10]

### Gavilar and the Listeners ( - )

> “ *We live without honor, for your gods once brought ours. Without them, we have no **power.** This world is trapped, Eshonai! Stuck in a dull, lifeless state of transition. Unite them. I need a threat. Only danger will unite them.* ”
> 
> —Gavilar to Eshonai [^3]

In approximately, Gavilar and Dalinar made an expedition to the south of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] where they discovered the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]], referred to by the humans as Parshendi. Dalinar, while scouting at a campsite, was the first to meet them. Although the other members of the expedition initially did not believe tales of intelligent parshmen, [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai's]] group came to the camp the next day. At first they couldn't believe parshmen could live on their own, but they found that these parshmen were friendly, curious and learned the Alethi language quite easily. Gavilar's [stormwardens](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormwarden "Stormwarden") began calling them Parshendi. Although Gavilar had never before been scholarly, he grew more and more fascinated by the Parshendi. Despite Dalinar's attempts to return to Alethkar, Gavilar wanted to stay on the Shattered Plains to learn more about the Parshendi.[^6] Gavilar's officers thought the Parshendi were savages, but Gavilar thought the Parshendi were "an enclave of memories. A window into the past." [^3]

It's unknown why Gavilar made the treaty with the Parshendi. Many people believe it was because he knew the listeners had shardblades, but as a member of the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]], perhaps Gavilar wished to start a Desolation to return the Heralds, but in reality he was only using them to try and become a Herald and immortal.[^3] [^22] He believed bringing back the Parshendi's old gods would benefit both the Parshendi and the humans, despite [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai's]] insistence that the Parshendi abandoned their gods. Even while witnessing her horror, he believed the Parshendi would be pleased that the Alethi were allies in seeking the return of their old gods.[^3]

### Assassination ()

The night of his assassination, Gavilar was attending a feast celebrating a treaty with the listeners. Before the feast he spoke with [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar") via [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] about their dealings, including information about the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") in exchange for [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]]. He then spoke with Kalak and [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] about transporting [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]] from [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]].[^4] [^22] He would then have a conversation with Navani, which degenerated into a fight. This was their last true conversation before he died.[^4] [^22] Next he meet in the lower palace floor with five people, including [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") and [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]]; in his conversation with Taravangian, he discussed the [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") he had been seeing and warned the other king that a storm was coming.[^18] [^3] [^22] When he discovered that [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai]] had seen the meeting, he decided to speak to her about his plans. Gavilar wanted to bring back the listeners' gods, the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]], and start a new [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]]. Despite Eshonai's protests to the contrary, he believed this would benefit everyone, including the listeners. He then gave Eshonai a [black sphere](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Black_sphere "Black sphere") and asked her to take it to [[Listener - The Coppermind|The Five]] and tell them what he had told her.[^3]

At some point during the feast, Gavilar slipped into the hallway to speak privately with Amaram. It is not known what they discussed. His daughter, Jasnah, found them talking. He rebuked her for her contempt for the party and expressed an interest that she and Amaram become closer acquainted. When Amaram left, Gavilar refused to tell Jasnah what they were discussing and went back into the party.[^2]

> “ *Tell…tell my brother…he must find the most important words a man can say….*”
> 
> —Gavilar's last words [^1]

When Gavilar learned that [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] was in the palace to assassinate him, he put on his [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] and prepared to act as a bodyguard while Sadeas prepared to act as the fleeing king. Szeth initially fell for it, fighting him and leaving to pursue Sadeas after he temporarily incapacitates Gavilar. When Gavilar does not follow Szeth, however, Szeth realizes the ruse. He returns and continues fighting Gavilar. Gavilar's helm was shattered and his plate was damaged, but he managed to injure Szeth by punching his face. In order to finish the fight, Szeth lured Gavilar onto the balcony and broke it using [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Lashings]].

A piece of wood gravely injured him by stabbing him in the side where his plate was damaged. As he was dying, Gavilar had a small, strange [black sphere](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Black_sphere "Black sphere") that he gave to Szeth and told him not to let some unknown group get it. He wished his brother to know the "most important words a man can say".[^1] Szeth then proceeded to write in Alethi those words in Gavilar's blood on a wooden block, which was later deciphered by [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]]. [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] then took this to mean the words of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^1]

At the end of his life, he believed he was being watched.[^3] Gavilar expected an attempt on his life, but not by the [[Listener - The Coppermind|Parshendi]]. His suspects included [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar"), [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), and [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas"), despite being on apparent good terms with Sadeas at the time.[^1] The Parshendi ordered his assassination because he was going to bring back their old gods, but he never became aware of this reason.[^1] [^3]

### Legacy

> “ *Gavilar the Majestic, first king of Alethkar in the new Kholin Dynasty, thirty-second highprince of the Kholin princedom, heir of the Sunmaker and blessed of the Almighty. His accomplishments will be lauded by all, and his dominion extends to the hereafter.* ”
> 
> —Spoken at Gavilar's holy interment [^5]

At Gavilar's holy interment, he was lauded by the presiding ardent. His body was Soulcast into a stone sculpture. At the interment, the Alethi highprinces swore the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|Vengeance Pact]] and would go on to fight the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] to avenge his death.[^5]

A number of works about Gavilar's life exist, including "Gavilar's Account",[^30] dictated by Gavilar to Jasnah a year before his murder; "Matain's account";[^6] and "King Gavilar Kholin, A Biography" by Navani.[^6] Navani's biography crafted Gavilar a selective legacy, leaving out his abusive, dismissive treatment of her and his family so only she would have to bear the burden of who he really was.[^31]

Even after nearly a decade, Dalinar would look up to him as a paragon of honor.[^7] Also the Stormfather would not trust him after his death which would continue on for almost a decade.[^22] [^32]

## Relationships

### House Kholin

There is not much information on Gavilar's family dynamics, but there are hints of complex relationships.

He is rather abusive and dismissive towards his family. Despite this, after his death most of the Kholins have remember him positively and even revere him.

#### Dalinar Kholin

> “ *"My brother was a liar, Sebarial"* ”
> 
> —Dalinar Kholin to Highprince Sebarial

[^33]

Gavilar feels affection for his brother [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]], but he tends to use people around him as tools to further his goals.[^13] After their mother died and their father suffered a brain injury and was left unable to take care of them, he took on a sort of paternal role over Dalinar.[^24] On the night of his death Gavilar would meet with Dalinar one last time and would use the Rift and the Codes to get Dalinar to drink and out of his way.[^22]

When Gavilar was still alive, Dalinar often introduced him to people that he felt that he should know.[^26] Dalinar refuses to allow criticism of Gavilar and devotes himself to protecting Elhokar, the remaining piece of his brother.[^34] [^35] Dalinar feels responsibility for his brother's death, believing that he could have stopped the assassination; he had counseled Gavilar to follow the Codes up until his death.[^5] His guilt and grief (related to Gavilar and other people he believed he had failed) were major factors in his fundamental change as a person.[^7] [^36] [^37]

#### Elhokar Kholin

Late in his life Gavilar had a low opinion of Elhokar, telling Navani he is not even capable of mediocrity. Due to his feelings on Elhokar, he felt as if he was not a worthy heir to the throne.[^4]

Elhokar himself remembered Gavilar as a perfect king he would never live up to, so he disliked when Gavilar was mentioned.[^7]

#### Navani Kholin

> “ *You have no fame, accomplishment, or capacity of your own. Everything distinctive about you came from someone else.* ”
> 
> —Gavilar to Navani [^4]

Although he and his wife Navani were once close and plotted the conquest of Alethkar together, when Navani spoke with passion on her spren research, Gavilar simply ignored her.[^28] Later in life as Gavilar developed larger goals, he stopped including Navani in his plans. He left her to handle the public-facing duties of the monarchy alone and repeatedly told her she is worthless, and was very dismissive of her passions and her intelligence.[^4]

Due to how openly abusive he was towards her, Navani saw the darker side of his character. On the day of his assassination, he pushed her over the edge and caused her to burn a glyphward asking for the Almighty to kill him. Due to this, she felt responsible for his death when he was assassinated later that day.[^4] Despite her hatred for him, she chose to hide it, writing him a legacy of being the ideal Alethi man.[^4] [^31] She considers this illusion that she conjured to be what he once was.[^4]

#### Jasnah Kholin

Jasnah spent a lot of time with Gavilar discussing the Parshendi, and later says it was a rare time when she truly connected with him. This suggests that they were never that close, and always had a very strained relationship.[^6] Though she seems to respect his opinion, Gavilar often treats Jasnah as part of his legacy rather than a person, ignoring her resistance to marrying Amaram and demanding she follow the Vorin church in spite of her convictions.[^2] [^4]

Dalinar references Gavilar's problems with young Jasnah and what he describes as her lunacy.[^10] Jasnah herself mentioned a childhood disease nobody remembered.[^38] As her father, he was likely a large factor in her treatment during this period, locking her away in a padded cell and leaving her with lasting trauma that still haunts her in adulthood.[^38] [^10]

Jasnah takes great interest in the changes near the end of his life.[^6]

### Sadees the Sunmaker

> “ *Hopefully my accomplishments won’t be as ephemeral as his.* ”
> 
> —Gavilar to Toh in reference to the Sunmaker.[^8]

During his conquests and reign, Gavilar looked towards the [[Sadees - The Coppermind|Sunmaker]] for guidance. Although the Sunmaker is revered by most in Alethkar for creating a united empire, Gavilar had a more negative view of him. Gavilar blamed the destruction of the Sunmaker's empire on his greed and inability to be satisfied with what he conquered. He hoped that he'll be able to create a longer-lasting empire and overshadow the Sunmaker in the hearts and minds of the people.[^8]

Despite his criticisms of the Sunmaker, Gavilar was willing to use his connection to him to his advantage. For example, Gavilar did not wish to wear a crown because the Sunmaker refused to.[^10]

### Sons of Honor

Although Gavilar was not in charge of the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]], he was an influential member of the group. He was responsible for an expansion in the ranks of members, including bringing in [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram").[^39] He was an important enough member to have access to and knowledge of their plan to return the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]], including the black spheres.[^3] Despite this, Gavilar suspected [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), leader of the Sons of Honor, of his assassination, indicating that there might have been some conflict or suspicion between the two.[^1]

### Thaidakar

He had some working relationship with Thaidakar and the Ghostbloods, although it is suggested that this relationship was far from amicable and trustworthy. Gavilar deeply distrusted Thaidakar, to the extent that when he was assassinated, he was the first person he suspected of hiring the assassin.[^1] They would speak on the night of Gavilar’s death via [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] and it appeared that they had some dealings mostly involving Restares.[^22]

### The Stormfather

Near the end of his life, he began to be given visions by the Stormfather.[^18] [^17] He was on his way to become a Bondsmith, but was never able to complete the bond before his death.[^17] He would use the Stormfather to try and find a way to become a Herald.[^22] The Stormfather did not trust Gavilar after the night of his death and would consider his family unworthy of being Bondsmiths.[^32]

### The Heralds

#### Kalak

He had some working relationship with Kalak, also known as Restares, who spoke to him in his palace on the day of his assassination. The exact details of this relationship, however, are unknown.[^2] [^4] [^40] It most likely had to do with his spheres of Voidlight and Anti-Voidlight, and in his position in the Sons of Honor, of which Restares is the leader of.[^4] [^39] Gavilar did not believe that Kalak was who he claimed to be.[^22]

#### Nale

He had only met Nale on the night of his and spoke to him in his palace on the day of his assassination.[^2] [^4] [^40] It most likely had to do with his spheres of Voidlight and Anti-Voidlight, of which was apparently the topic of discussion during their meeting.[^4]
