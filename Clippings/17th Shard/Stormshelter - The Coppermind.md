## Stormshelter

> “ *He hated being in stormshelters. They felt too much like prisons.* ”
> 
> —Kaladin [^1]

A **stormshelter**, or a **storm bunker**, is a type of building on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] where people hide during [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]].[^2]

## Appearance

Stormshelters are constructed out of durable materials, such as stone or bricks. Some are built, while others are [[Soulcasting - The Coppermind|Soulcast]].[^3] [^4] They are designed to give the storm as little purchase as possible, with rounded, low roofs, making them look like loaves of bread.[^5] [^6] While they're made to be secure, at least some of them are built with windows.[^7] [^8]

## Usage

The primary purpose of a stormshelter is to protect the people and livestock inside from a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] -- and, later, the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]].[^5] Though most people hide in their homes, the government maintains a number of public stormshelters available to the travelers and the homeless, although they have limited capacity.[^1] Moreover, the stormshelters are of paramount importance in warfare.[^9] Armies on the move are housed in them, and any force planning a siege will focus on constructing them as fast as possible.[^6] [^3] During battle, civilians hide in them while soldiers fight.[^10]

Apart from that, stormshelters are a place of social interaction. Some storm bunkers are built specifically for rich people who come there to enjoy themselves and mingle during highstorms, with many becoming popular hangout spots.[^11] [^12] For the people of lower classes, the stormshelter can serve as a public meeting spot or a dance hall.[^13]

## Trivia

- The name "stormshelter" was first used to describe those buildings in *[Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer "Oathbringer")*; until then, they were only known as "storm bunkers".
