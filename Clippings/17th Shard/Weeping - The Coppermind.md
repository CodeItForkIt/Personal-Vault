## Weeping

> “ *Never furious, never passionate like a highstorm. Slow, steady. Like the blood of a dying year that was taking its last few shambling steps toward the cairn.* ”
> 
> —Kaladin's thoughts on the Weeping [^2]

The **Weeping** is a four weeks long period of time on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], marking the end of the planet's five-hundred-day year.[^2] The [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] call it the lull.[^3]

## Description

In the weeks preceding the Weeping, [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]] grow more and more infrequent, before eventually stopping altogether.[^4] A few days after the last highstorm, the skies cloud over and it begins to rain. The rain continues ceaselessly for two weeks until **Lightday**, the Rosharan New Year.[^2] On Lightday, one of two things happen. If the Lightday marks the start of an even-numbered year, the day will be calm - such years are called Light Years. At the start of odd-numbered years, the Weeping is instead interrupted by a highstorm.[^5] Following Lightday are two more weeks of constant rain.[^6] [^1] At the end of the Weeping, the highstorms return, albeit initially weaker than usual.[^7]

The Weepings are characterized by slow, steady, unstopping rainfall that doesn't deposit [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem"). There are no heavy winds or other extreme conditions generally found during a highstorm.[^2] [^8] The sky is perpetually overcast, bathing the land in twilight.[^9] [Rainspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rainspren "Rainspren") are commonplace.[^2]

Halfway between the Weepings is [[Midpeace - The Coppermind|Midpeace]], another prolonged period of time with no highstorms.[^10] In contrast to the Weeping, storms are more frequent before and after Midpeace, and the Midpeace itself doesn't see much, if any rain.[^11] [^12]

## Influence

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Weeping---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Ari Ibarra](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ari_Ibarra "Coppermind:Artists/Ari Ibarra")

[[Tien - The Coppermind|Tien]] on the roof of his house during the Weeping

The people of Roshar are used to the Weepings. While they do wear rain-proof clothing, such as long coats and wide-brimmed hats, it's generally understood that everyone and everything will be utterly soaked throughout.[^13] [^14] Most Rosharans approach this stoically, weathering the rain while enjoying the break from the highstorms.[^14] [^15] Sometimes, they measure their age in Weepings, saying "sixteen Weepings old" or "hadn't reached her twentieth Weeping".[^16] [^17]

In the [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") religion, the Weeping is supposed to be a time for self-reflection and contemplating the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty").[^18] In [[Yeddaw - The Coppermind|Yeddaw]], the Weeping is celebrated by eating the city's ten famous pancakes.[^19] [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], in turn, isn't affected by the Weepings, as the tower stands far above the clouds.[^20]

The prolonged period of no highstorms leads to a lack of [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]]. Rosharans try to preserve it throughout by using enormous gems capable of storing vast amounts of it, with a specific, multi-faceted cut that further prolongs the Stormlight retention. Despite that, few gemstones make it to the next highstorm still infused.[^8] A [perfect gemstone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Perfect_gemstone "Perfect gemstone") could keep Stormlight for that long, but those are extremely rare.[^21] The lack of highstorms also presents a problem to the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], as they cannot change forms without them.[^3]

### Military

The peace of the Weeping is an important factor in strategy. The lack of highstorms allows for greater mobility, as soldiers don't require [[Stormshelter - The Coppermind|stormshelters]] during that time, allowing them to resupply and quickly cross large distances.[^22] Among others, [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") armies sometimes set out on short military excursions during the Weeping to take advantage of this.[^15] [^23] This being said, actually battling is more difficult -- among others, the rain makes it hard to utilize archers.[^24]

### Industry

Farmers generally don't work during the Weeping, as any seeds or seed-paste planted during that time would be washed away by constant rain, and some plants might even die from excess water.[^25] [^13] The period of calm highstorms directly following, however, is a prime time for planting.[^7] In contrast, fishermen work especially hard during the Weepings, as their ships are constantly flooding.[^26]

In some places, the Weeping is harnessed for cleaning and washing areas that would normally be too difficult or time-consuming to tidy up.[^8] [^27]

## The Weird Weeping

The Weeping between the years 1173/1174 was markedly different from the usual, owning to the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], which took place two days before the Lightday.[^28] Even before the battle began, people noted unusually strong winds, far in excess of what could normally be expected.[^18] During the fighting, the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] summoned the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], while the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] sent a highstorm to oppose it.[^29] This caused the regular pattern of the Weeping to be altered.

Elsewhere in the world, the Weeping stopped, then started, then stopped again.[^19] The out-of-pattern highstorm arrived during that time without warning, leading to only some spheres capturing [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]].[^30] The Everstorm came a few days later, throwing people off further.[^31] What followed was a period of drought that lasted until, when the highstorm returned.[^32] Overall, the strange non-Weeping lasted seven days longer than a regular one.

How this sequence of events, as well as the addition of the Everstorm, will impact future Weepings is yet unknown.

## Trivia

- [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin's]] [seasonal depression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seasonal_affective_disorder "wikipedia:Seasonal affective disorder") affects him the most during the Weeping.[^9] [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], by contrast, considers it to be her favorite part of the year.[^14]
- The occurence of the Weeping is influenced by Roshar's orbital position during that time of the year.[^33] It is not, however, tied to the position of [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] relative to Roshar.[^34]
- Weepings not carrying [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem") is motivated by needs of the narrative rather than any in-universe mechanics, as Brandon didn't want there to be an overabundance of mud and slurry during the finale of *[Words of Radiance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Words_of_Radiance "Words of Radiance")*.[^35]
