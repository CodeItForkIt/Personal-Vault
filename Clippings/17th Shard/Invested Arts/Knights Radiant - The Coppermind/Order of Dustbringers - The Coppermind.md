## Order of Dustbringers

> “ *Smoke curled from the occasional patches of growth or heaps of burning corpses. Even some sections of rock smoldered. The Dustbringers had done their work well.* ”
> 
> — [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak's]] thoughts in the wake of [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam") [^2]

The **Order of Dustbringers** is an order of [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^3]

The Dustbringers are [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] who can control the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Division and Abrasion; they are associated with the ruby [[Polestone - The Coppermind|polestone]]. They form [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bonds]] with [ashspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashspren "Ashspren").[^4]

Many Dustbringers tend to dislike their name due to its similarity to Voidbringer, and prefer to be called **Releasers**.[^3] [^5]

## Philosophy

> “ *I will seek self-mastery.*”
> 
> —The core philosophy of the Dustbringers [^6]

The Dustbringers believe that great power requires a strong will to control it, and their oaths are designed to teach them to control their destructive power.[^7] While their powers can be used to destroy large swaths of land, acting like artillery, they prefer to work in a more controlled and nuanced manner.[^6] They aim to avoid civilian casualties, even in the largest of conflicts.

Most Dustbringers are tinkerers who like to take things apart to figure out how they work. They often object to those who only focus on the destructive side of their powers, as they feel that it is impossible to create something without understanding how its pieces work.[^5] [^6] This desire to understand how things work applies to people as well, with them wanting to deeply understand people to know how to incite or avoid certain emotional responses.[^7] The order also attracts foolhardy-but-brave soldiers who try to control the destructive nature of war to protect innocents.[^5] They also tend to have the most variety among the Radiant orders.[^8]

## Ideals of the Dustbringers

The Ideals of the Knights Radiant, also known as the [Immortal Words](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words"), are a set of rules by which the Radiants live. The First Ideal is shared by all of the orders, and is used as a motto for the Knights Radiant as a whole. The four remaining Ideals are unique to each order.[^9]

The Ideals of the Order of Dustbringers all focus on responsibility and controlling one's power, and are designed to teach lessons about the nature of power and the responsibility that comes with it. Unlike members of most of the other orders, the Dustbringers cannot access all of their [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] powers until they have sworn some of the higher oaths.[^5]

### The First Ideal

> “ *Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination.* ”
> 
> —The First Ideal of the Knights Radiant [^9]

The Dustbringers share the first ideal with all other orders of Radiants, which serves as the motto of the organization as a whole.[^9] The words are broadly interpreted to state the value of life, the importance of strength and service and the importance of the methods by which one accomplishes a task.

### The Second Ideal

> “ *To control my power, I will control myself.* ”
> 
> —The Second Ideal of the Dustbringer order [^6]

This oath is centered around self-mastery, regardless of the individuals own desires and wills.[^6] Dustbringers strive to demonstrate their discipline to achieve their desires, which help serve as a basis for trusting themselves with their destructive Surgebinding potential. It is the minimum oath required before the Surge of Division can be used.

### The Third Ideal

> “ *To understand my power, I will understand what power is.* ”
> 
> —The Third Ideal of the Dustbringer order [^10]

This oath focuses on mastering power itself.[^10] This includes Surgebinding, political position, societal echelon, wealth, physical aptitude, and other avenues of influence. It must be reached before a Dustbringer can manifest their ashspren as a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade").[^11]

### The Fourth Ideal

The specific words of this Ideal are individualized, but overarching themes are acknowledging that all power is accompanied by obligation, and that ignoring your responsibility can have dire consequences.[^10] Upon achieving this Ideal, a Dustbringer gains their [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]], which is formed of [flamespren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Flamespren "Flamespren").[^12] [^11]

## Abilities

Dustbringers can use the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of **Division** and **Abrasion**. Like most Knights Radiant, the Dustbringers have squires; one Dustbringer from one of [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") had about a dozen.[^13] Many strategies used by Dustbringers involve intimidation.[^14]

### Division

[[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|**Division**]] gives Dustbringers the ability to cause objects to burn, degrade, or turn to dust. They appear to have a great amount of control over this ability, as [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]] engraves a table using this Surge. Division also works on living flesh.[^4] Dustbringers do not have access to the Surge of Division until later Ideals.[^5] Restricting access to Surges based on oaths is rare among the orders and few do it. The only other order currently known to do so are the [Skybreakers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreakers "Skybreakers"), who also restrict the Surge of Division, but not their other surge.[^15]

### Abrasion

[[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|**Abrasion**]] gives Dustbringers the ability to alter the frictional force between two surfaces. One Dustbringer used this ability to decrease friction between herself and the water of the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]], allowing her to move through it unimpeded.[^13] It can be used to decrease friction between their bodies and other surfaces, allowing them to move around with ease, and to make objects slippery to anyone other than themselves.[^16] Abrasion can also be used to increase the friction between two objects.[^14]

### Shardplate Abilities

Dustbringers' [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] is formed of [flamespren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Flamespren "Flamespren").[^1] [^12]

## History

> “ *And when they were spoken of by the common folk, the Releasers claimed to be misjudged because of the dreadful nature of their power; and when they dealt with others, always were they firm in their claim that other epithets, notably "Dustbringers," often heard in the common speech, were unacceptable substitutions, in particular for their similarity to the word "Voidbringers." They did also exercise anger in great prejudice regarding it, though to many who speak, there was little difference between these two assemblies.* ”
> 
> — *[Words of Radiance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Words_of_Radiance_\(in-world\) "Words of Radiance (in-world)")*, chapter 17 [^3]

### The Ancient Radiants

Before the [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Day of Recreance]], many Dustbringers objected to the name of their order, as they felt it was too similar to " [Voidbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Voidbringers "Voidbringers")," and encouraged others to instead call them "Releasers," [^3] [^5] though most people still referred to them as "Dustbringers".[^17] This naming preference may have been influenced by their spren, as [[Spark - The Coppermind|Spark]] apparently passed it on to [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]].[^18] The Dustbringers were often misjudged by people because of the destructive nature of their powers,[^3] and even some of their fellow Knights Radiant felt they were "on the edge of evil" and did not get along with them.[^19] [^20] The Skybreakers, who often made sure the Radiants didn't abuse their power,[^21] were particularly concerned about the Dustbringers, despite the fact that the two orders had much in common.[^19]

Historically, the Dustbringers often functioned as sappers, engineers, and strategists for the Knights Radiant,[^5] [^7] though they were mainly used to cause widespread destruction.[^20] During the final battle of the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], they used their powers to great effect, resulting in burnt plants and corpses and smoldering rock.[^2] In one of [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions"), a Dustbringer and several of her squires hunted a [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] that had been corrupted by [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], and then proceeded to fight a [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclast]].[^13]

### True Desolation

The first known [ashspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashspren "Ashspren") to initiate a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] since the Recreance was Spark, who bonded Malata. Many of Spark's friends were killed in the Recreance and she claimed that her only objectives with the bond are to get revenge and break things (a noted deviance from the traditional attitude of the Dustbringers); this attitude appears to have played a role in Malata's acceptance of the [Diagram's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") decision to side with [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^4] It appears that at least a few other ashspren had initiated bonds since Spark did, but it was unclear whether any of them and their Dustbringers would also decide to side with Odium.[^22]

By the time of the [Wandersail's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail_\(1174_ship\) "Wandersail (1174 ship)") journey to [[Aimia - The Coppermind|Aimia]] in, [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar's]] coalition began to suspect that not all Dustbringers are loyal to their cause,[^20] as [Lopen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lopen "Lopen") asked [Ral-na](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ral-na "Ral-na") not to talk with the other Dustbringers so that he could find his own path, and said there were political reasons for that request.[^22] Jasnah also suspected them of rebellion.[^23]
