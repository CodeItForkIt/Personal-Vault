## Steel

**Steel** is the External Physical Pushing metal, made from [[Iron - The Coppermind|iron]] and carbon;[^1] it is the alloy paired with iron in the Metallic Arts.

An Allomancer burning steel can Push bits of metal away from their "center of self," [^2] and can also use steel to sense the presence of metal nearby. Steelpushing is not dependent on magnetic forces or Allomantic power -- most metals (with the exception of [[Aluminum - The Coppermind|aluminum]] and some of its alloys) can be Pushed. Feruchemists can use steel to store physical speed, and when used as a hemalurgic spike, steel steals Physical Allomantic powers.

## Allomantic Use

A steel [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Misting]] is known as a **Coinshot**; Coinshots are one of the most common types of Allomancer, especially among criminals.[^3] Steelpushing, or at times called Coinshotting,[^4] is the art of burning steel to push metals away (in a straight line) from the user's "center of self," [^2] [^5] which is likely tied to the Allomancer's [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive]] and [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual]] aspects. There are two general rules for Steelpushing, which also apply to Ironpulling, its paired opposite. The first rule is that the strength of your push is roughly proportional to your physical weight. This means that larger Allomancers can generally Steelpush and Ironpull more powerfully than a smaller counterpart. While proportional, the force exerted by a Steelpush is much greater than the Coinshot's weight, allowing them to push themselves into the air by pushing on a piece of metal that is on the ground (or is anchored to something that weighs more than they do). The second rule is that metal that is at least partially inside a person's body cannot be affected by steel. Both of these rules can be overcome by a sufficiently strong Coinshot, however.

The force of the Push upon an object is inversely proportional to the Coinshot's distance to said object. This continues until the Coinshot hits a zenith, or point of maximum altitude. This zenith is higher for more powerful Steelpushes, such as those burned in conjunction with [[Duralumin - The Coppermind|duralumin]], or with more massive steel anchors.[^6] [^7] [^8]

When burning steel, blue lines emerge from the Coinshot and connect themselves to pieces of nearby metal, with the size of the steel line indicating how big the metal is. The steel lines can typically only be seen by the Coinshot.[^9] The steel lines manifest themselves on the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] and can be cut or interfered with.[^10] Supposedly due to their lack of physicality, they can pass through physical objects such as walls, allowing a Coinshot to ascertain the location of metals outside of their line of sight and to detect such things as metal girders within walls.[^11] [^12] It is also possible to use the steel lines to see via the trace metals in everyone's bodies and in the objects around them, or even [[Axon - The Coppermind|axi]] and electromagnetic bonds.[^13] [^14] [^15] [^16] A Coinshot could identify specific metals, or even colors, with the steel lines.[^17] [^18]

Steelpushing is commonly considered a flashy ability. It allows the user to fly through the air in quite spectacular ways, using only coins (or other small metal objects) placed on the ground. This functions through essentially attempting to push the planet itself, which, as an object far more massive than one's self, causes one to be pushed away.[^9] It can be used offensively, as the name suggests, to shoot metal objects as projectiles. As the coins are much smaller than the Coinshot, they gain quite a bit of speed over time. Steelpushing can also be used defensively, by creating a bubble around the user to deflect incoming metal objects, although in case of very fast objects (such as bullets) it doesn't provide full protection.[^19] Steelpushing liquid metals works similarly to a ferrofluid.[^20]

When used in conjunction with firearms, Steelpushing is an incredibly powerful tool. By shooting a regular bullet and then pushing on it as it leaves the barrel, the bullet can be given a large amount of force, enough to pierce straight through wood and other cover and kill anyone on the other side unexpectedly.[^19] [^21] While flying on Steelpushes, unpracticed Coinshots usually only look for anchors below them, while forgetting those behind them.[^11] With Scadrial's rapid modernization, people's lives began including more and more metal, which allows practiced Coinshots to interact with metal to greater effects, such as putting out lights by interacting with copper wires.[^12] Coinshots commonly crouch down before they push, to give the push a little more lift.[^22]

Steel Allomancy is strongly resisted by the body of sophonts, thus making metalminds embedded into the body difficult to push. Due to this, some Feruchemists choose to surgically implant their metalminds into their body.[^11] [^23] [^24] However, a strong enough Steelpush, such as one powered by the [mists](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mist "Mist"), is able to overcome this resistance.[^11] [^23] The degree of resistance is proportional to how Invested the target is, and would therefore require more force to push metal in the body of someone such as [[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]] and an average Scadrian, who would be harder than a Drab.[^25] The same limitations apply to Invested metal.[^26]

It is also possible for a skilled Allomancer to push on a specific part of a metal object.[^27] They could also have better control over their Pushes, such as creating a steel bubble that deflects projectiles while not affecting the metal on the user.[^28] Through unknown means, a Coinshot could push on non-metal objects.[^29] It is possible to affect the polarity of [[Axon - The Coppermind|axi]] via a Steelpush, under the right circumstances.[^16]

### Synergy with Iron Feruchemy

The Law of Conservation of Momentum holds when using iron Feruchemy. Therefore, a Coinshot who has access to iron Feruchemy can alter their mass in order to increase or decrease their velocity. When one increases their mass, their velocity would decrease in order to equalize their momentum, and vice versa.[^11] [^30] [^31] When drag is put into the equation this creates, one gets an equation with explicit maxima; more simply, when one decreases their mass too much, the force of drag overcomes the increase in velocity via Conservation of Momentum, and therefore causes one to decrease in velocity; this also means there is a specific amount of stored mass where one's velocity hits its maximum.[^11]

Steelpushing can also be used alongside iron Feruchemy in order to take advantage of Steelpushing's proportionality to the mass of the user, allowing them to increase the strength of their Steelpushes by increasing mass, or allow them to more easily push themselves away from an object by decreasing their mass.[^32]

Twinborn with steel Allomancy and iron Feruchemy are known as **Crashers**. Only three known Crashers exist in recorded history, and [Waxillium Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Waxillium_Ladrian "Waxillium Ladrian") is one of these three.[^30]

## Feruchemical Use

A steel [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Ferring]] is known as a **Steelrunner**. Steel is used to store physical speed. Then, by tapping their steelmind, a Steelrunner can move many times faster than a normal human, even one burning [[Pewter - The Coppermind|pewter]] with [[Duralumin - The Coppermind|duralumin]], as well as burn metals faster.[^33] While storing speed, a Steelrunner is very sluggish and is described to feel like moving through molasses.[^5] [[Sazed - The Coppermind|Sazed]], a notable Feruchemist during the days between the Final Empire and the Catacendre, regards steelminds as one of the most difficult metalminds to fill.[^34] Steelrunners can withstand the g-forces they are put through, but they can't ignore wind resistance and friction and will burn up if they start running too quickly.[^35] This can be overcome with [[Order of Edgedancers - The Coppermind|Edgedancers']] abilities.[^36]

### Compounding

A steel [[Compounding - The Coppermind|Compounder]] would be able to use steel to have a nearly infinite supply of physical speed. They would not be able to run faster-than-light.[^37] If not careful, the Steelrunner could kill themselves from running too quickly by factors like air resistance.[^38]

## Hemalurgic Use

When used as a Hemalurgic spike, steel steals the Physical Allomantic powers ([[Iron - The Coppermind|iron]], steel, [[Tin - The Coppermind|tin]], and [[Pewter - The Coppermind|pewter]]).[^39] [^40]

### Bind Points

- Allomantic Steel: Allomantic steel can be stolen by placing a steel spike through the sternum.[^41] [^42]
- Allomantic Pewter: Allomantic pewter can be stolen by placing a steel spike through the left shoulder.[^43] [^44]
- Cognitive Shadow: A steel spike through the right eye socket is able to hold a [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]] to a body in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]].[^16]

## Fabrial Use

A [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrial]] with a steel cage can change the fabrial's polarity, pushing specific elements from itself.[^45]

## Aetheric Use

Putting a steel tool near growing [[Aether - The Coppermind|aether]] substance repels the substance away from the tool as it grows; due to this, it is a tool commonly used among [Sprouters](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sprouters "Sprouters") to create [[Aether - The Coppermind|Roseite]] plugs on holes in a ship's hull.[^46]

## Invested Technology

[[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrian]] airships rely on Steelpushing fans to stay in the air.[^47]

During the space age of the [[17th Shard|cosmere]], Scadrian research spaceships have an [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakened]] steelmind.[^48] [^49] The steelmind is capable of giving status reports.
