## Hemalurgy

> “ *Hemalurgy is a power about which I wish I knew far less.* ”
> 
> —The [Hero of Ages](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hero_of_Ages "Hero of Ages") [^1]

**Hemalurgy** is one of the three prime manifestations of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] on [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]]. The natives of that world consider it one of the three [[Metallic Arts]]. Practitioners of Hemalurgy are referred to as **Hemalurgists**.[^2]

## Mechanics

Hemalurgy is the transfer of attributes, such as [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]], [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemy]], or innate human strength via [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|spiritweb]] theft and splicing. Hemalurgy can transfer almost any type of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]].[^3] In the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], a metallic spike is inserted into a bind point, such as the heart, in order to Hemalurgically charge it. The spike, now containing a fragment of stolen spiritweb, is then ripped out and embedded in the recipient, stapling the ability directly into their spiritual DNA (sDNA).[^4] This causes wear and tear on the spiritweb, creating "holes" through which [[Shards]] or a sufficiently powerful [[Zinc - The Coppermind|Rioter]] or [Soother](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Brass "Brass") can whisper to or even control a Hemalurgical subject.[^5] [^6]

Hemalurgy is named for a quirk in bind point placement: due to the interaction between the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual]] and Physical Realms, the spike must come in contact with moving blood in order to remove or add an attribute.[^7] [^8] [^9] A spike can be charged if the Hemalurgist stabs the donor with the spike or if it is thrown at them, as long as there is [Intent](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Intention "Intention") and it hits the right bind point. Because of this, spike darts or spike guns would be Hemalurgically viable.[^10]

Hemalurgy can be used anywhere in the [[17th Shard|cosmere]],[^11] [^12] [^13] as long as there is an Intent to create a Hemalurgic spike.[^14] [^15] [^16] Hemalurgy can also steal attributes from other forms of Investiture.[^17] Hemalurgy can be used on animals [^18] but not on plants.[^19]

For some reason, Hemalurgic spikes don't cause infections.[^20]

### Stealing of Attributes

Since Hemalurgy involves the transfer of Investiture from one person to another, if the donor is killed, they will enter the Cognitive Realm in a damaged state and be pulled to the [[Beyond - The Coppermind|Beyond]] faster. Likewise, if a recipient dies, they will take longer to leave, since they are more Invested.[^21]

Creating a Hemalurgic charge requires physical injury, and generally results in the death of the victim.[^22] It is possible, however, to charge a spike without killing the target,[^23] [^24] [^25] in which case they would be left in a similar spiritual condition to a [Drab](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Drab "Drab"), but in a worse condition.[^26] [^27] In order to charge a spike without killing the victim, one must have the proper Intent, a very thin spike, and to speak a [Command](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Command "Command") (although this step is not strictly necessary). It apparently requires the needle be threaded carefully through the flesh, as to not strike organs. Doing this leaves the victim with a small portion of their spiritweb torn off, but leaves them alive. This also allows one to collect Investiture from non-Allomancers.[^24]

It is not thought to be possible for multiple attributes to be stolen from the same person.[^28] However, a single spike can take the same attribute from multiple people.[^24] Attributes can also be stolen by spiking a person's Investiture, such as [Breath](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Breath "Breath").[^25]

### Receiving of Attributes

There is a maximum number of spikes a person can have before causing issues of mental and/or physical limitations.[^29] [^30] The number of spikes a person can take has been reduced since Ruin was merged with Preservation. If too many spikes are added, the soul stops gaining powers. [[Compounding - The Coppermind|Compounding]] is also inaccessible, under normal methods, to any Hemalurgist made after the Shards merged.[^31] [^29] There are ways to bypass this limitation, but said ways are currently unknown. This relates to the nature of Ruin's subservience to Preservation in the current dual vessel of Harmony and something known as "Identity contamination".[^31] [^29]

This was likely done without a conscious decision by Harmony, and more of a byproduct of Ruin no longer pushing on the fabric of Scadrial.[^29]

### Law of Hemalurgic Decay

After the spike pierces through the initial person and is Hemalurgically charged, the **Law of Hemalurgic Decay** states that the spike loses some of its potency.[^32] The longer a spike is outside a body, the weaker it becomes, and a spike will lose most of the power it can lose on the first day outside of a body.[^33] [^34] The spikes lose power at a logarithmic rate, causing spikes that have been out of the body for a long time to become a Hemalurgic spike in name only.[^34] An example of this are the Prayer earrings in Era two, which don't give added power.[^35] Thus, when the [Steel Ministry](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Steel_Ministry "Steel Ministry") created an [Inquisitor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Inquisitor "Inquisitor"), the spikes were driven directly through a victim into the bindpoint of the Inquisitor below to minimize the spike's time spent outside of a person.[^36] Additionally, spikes which are physically broken into several pieces will split the charge into the broken pieces, and more Hemalurgic power will be lost when spikes are split.[^37]

Despite the decay, a spike will never completely lose its charge.[^38] [^34] As a spike loses its charge, the stolen soul will move on to the Beyond.[^39]

#### Reduction of Hemalurgic Decay

Coating a spike with blood can reduce the Hemalurgic decay significantly. It is unknown how much blood is necessary to make that effect happen;[^40] however, the blood does not need to be fresh nor separated from a body, with cuts of meat large enough to contain the spike also sufficing to reduce decay. This works by 'tricking' the spike into believing that it is buried in a body.[^41] It is possible that encasing spikes in [[Aluminum - The Coppermind|aluminum]] may prevent decay.[^42]

Eventually, Arcanists discovered a way to completely avoid Hemalurgic decay outside of the body.[^29] [^43] However, there is still an amount of Investiture lost during the creation of the spike.[^43] The exact method behind this is unknown.

## Side Effects of Hemalurgy

When an Allomancer can sense Allomantic pulses through a [[Copper - The Coppermind|coppercloud]] (known as piercing a coppercloud), it is most often due to a Hemalurgic spike. It's simply a matter of Allomantic strength to pierce copperclouds, so if a [[Bronze - The Coppermind|Seeker]] has a spike charged with Allomantic bronze, that essentially makes them twice as powerful, allowing them to overpower a coppercloud.

Hemalurgy is a destructive art that twists the body into something almost inhuman (particularly if the spike stole an attribute which is neither Allomantic nor Feruchemical). This twisting is how Inquisitors survive with spikes through their eyes and other vital places, and explains the physical deformation of the koloss.[^44] In the case of Inquisitors, it is known that their heart is in a different place and their brain is shaped around their eye-spikes. Extracting the spikes of an Inquisitor at such important places inverts the transformation, causing their death.[^45] The more spikes a person has, the higher the chance that there could be issues of mental or physical limitations, and the greater the degree of strain upon the body and soul.[^29] [^30] Additionally, any children produced by someone with a large number of Hemalurgic spikes, such as an Inquisitor, are likely to encounter complications, although they will also have a higher chance of being an Allomancer.[^46]

Hemalurgy was not specifically created by [[Ruin - The Coppermind|Ruin]] but rather arose from the interactions between Preservation, Ruin, and Scadrial itself.[^47] This Metallic Art has an inherent flaw: each spike creates holes in the mind, making individuals who have received spikes easier to manipulate. This damage to the soul also repulses [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Radiant]] [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]].[^6] The influence of Ruin and presumably emotional Allomancers can be reduced by aluminum.[^48]

Damage done to a person's spiritweb by Hemalurgy will have implications for said person during the transition period between physical death and passing into the Beyond. These effects are lessened after they pass to the Beyond.[^49]

Some binding oaths and vows will be stolen as well.[^50]

### Hemalurgic Control

Due to the tear on the spiritweb creating "holes" in the recipient, they are left vulnerable to being manipulated by others. This includes [[Shards]], a sufficiently powerful [[Zinc - The Coppermind|Rioter]] or [Soother](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Brass "Brass"),[^51] or a [[Duralumin - The Coppermind|Connector]] [[Compounding - The Coppermind|Compounder]].[^52]

The level of control can range from whispers to full control.[^5] [^6] The control is exerted in the form of mental pressure, which changes the way the mind thinks, tricking the creature into doing exactly what they want.[^2] Those in control are able to sense where the creature is, and must concentrate for direct orders. Otherwise, they will follow general orders.[^53] Multiple Hemalurgic creatures can be controlled at once.[^54]

The more spikes a creature has, the easier it is to take control.[^55] At least four spikes are needed before [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] can take control of humans, but there are ways around this.[^56] A kandra with one spike cannot be controlled by Ruin or Harmony.[^57] Harmony can theoretically influence off-planet Hemalurgists, but Shards on those planets and other forms of interference can muddy this.[^58]

Hemalurgic creatures can fight the control with mental fortitude.[^59] [^60] [^61] [^62] They are easier to subdue when they are feeling strong emotions.[^55] [[Sliver - The Coppermind|Slivers]] are more resistant to being controlled.[^63] The control of Hemalurgic creatures can be stolen by others, particularly if they are stronger.[^64] When stolen, those in control will feel a powerful ripping sensation.

For emotional Allomancy, once controlled, the Soothing or Rioting is no longer required.[^65] The control will last even while the Allomancer is sleeping or out of metals. This can be achieved by a single [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Mistborn]] or Hemalurgist burning [[Duralumin - The Coppermind|duralumin]] and brass/zinc together, a combination of a [[Nicrosil - The Coppermind|Nicroburst]] and a Soother/Rioter, or a group of Soothers and/or Rioters working together, though in the early days of [[Final Empire - The Coppermind|Final Empire]], when Allomancy was much more powerful, a single Soother or Rioter was able to do it.[^5] [^66] [^67]

## Variant Systems

A variant of Hemalurgy was discovered on Roshar,[^16] in some part by the Herald [[Battar - The Coppermind|Battar]], using [crystalline spikes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crystal_spike "Crystal spike") instead of metallic ones, in order to impart various effects upon the recipient.[^15] A notable recipient of such power was [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]], who had his eyes pierced by crystalline spikes, allowing him to see Invested entities via the sensing of Investiture, even when they are not making themselves visible.[^68] [^69] [^70] It is unclear how else this system, or manifestation of a singular larger system, differs from Scadrian Hemalurgy.

This appears to still possess the aspect of Hemalurgy that warps the biology of the staked individual, as to keep them alive despite the spikes passing through where internal organs should be, as Moash was able to survive these spikes being driven through his eyes.[^69]

On [[Canticle - The Coppermind|Canticle]] the [Cinder King](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cinder_King "Cinder King") was taught by Scadrian [TimeTellers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/TimeTellers "TimeTellers") how to corrupt [sunhearts](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sunheart "Sunheart") into [cinderhearts](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cinderheart "Cinderheart") via Hemalurgic intent.[^71] This allowed him to create the [Charred](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Charred "Charred"), constructs passingly similar to Steel Inquisitors.[^72]

> — [Spook](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spook "Spook") [^73]

Any Hemalurgy warps the spiritweb, but physical change is required for a subject to be classified as a Hemalurgic Construct. Four types of Hemalugic Construct are known on Scadrial:

- [[Steel Inquisitor - The Coppermind|Steel Inquisitors]] are former humans with nine to eleven spikes, each charged with an Allomantic or Feruchemical power. Their characteristic eye-spikes grant them Allomantic [[Iron - The Coppermind|iron]] and [[Steel - The Coppermind|steel]] to "see" in lieu of eyes. A linchpin spike, placed in the upper back between the shoulder blades, holds their fraying [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|spiritwebs]] together; removal results in death. Mistborn and [Seekers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Seeker "Seeker") are preferred candidates to become Inquisitors, due to their ability stacking with spikes granting Allomantic bronze, allowing Inquisitors to pierce [Copperclouds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppercloud "Coppercloud").[^54] [^74]
- [[Koloss - The Coppermind|Koloss]] are brutish, monstrous beings created with four iron spikes, each charged with human strength. They lose their intelligence, but gain immense strength. They continue to grow until they die. After the [Final Ascension](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Final_Ascension "Final Ascension") Harmony changed them into a breeding race.[^75]
- [[Kandra - The Coppermind|Kandra]] are former [Mistwraiths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistwraith "Mistwraith") given sapience by two spikes, both charged with the same human attribute. To the Kandra this is known as a [Blessing](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blessing "Blessing"). It is possible for a [[Kandra - The Coppermind|Kandra]] such as [Bleeder](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bleeder "Bleeder") to have sapience with only one spike, although their behavior will be more erratic as a result.[^76]
- [[Hemalurgic chimera - The Coppermind|Chimeras]] are former humans, and are created with a single [[Trellium - The Coppermind|Trellium]] spike, charged with an unknown attribute.[^76] Their single spike makes them invulnerable to [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony's]] control, which requires two. They are quadrupedal, and their features have been twisted in such a way that they now more closely resemble canines, in addition to having thicker skulls to resist modern firearms.[^77] [^76] [^78] The Set has developed a strain of Chimera with two spikes rather than one.[^79] This strain is informally known as "Metal Mutants".[^80]

Non Scadrian Hemalurgic Constructs:

- [Charred](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Charred "Charred") are former humans pierced with a [sunheart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sunheart "Sunheart") that has been corrupted into a [cinderheart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cinderheart "Cinderheart").[^81] [^82] The cinderheart replaces the Charred's heart and lungs providing the energy necessary to keep them alive.[^83] The process heavily damages the soul and destroys most of their memories.[^84] [^85] The Charred are granted increased strength, speed, and durability as well as compulsion to be active.[^83] [^86] [^87] [^88] If the process is interrupted a Sunlit Charred is created.[^84] [^89] They retain their memories, organs, and can control the other Charred in a manner similar to Emotional Allomancy.[^82] [^84]

## Bind Points

> “ *For all that it disgusts me, I cannot help but be impressed by Hemalurgy as an art. In Allomancy and Feruchemy, skill and subtlety come through the application of one's powers. The best Allomancer might not be the most powerful, but instead the one who can best manipulate the Pushes and Pulls of metals. The best Feruchemist is the one who is most capable of sorting the information in his [copperminds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind "Coppermind"), or best able to manipulate his weight with iron.*
> 
> *
> 
> The art that is unique to Hemalurgy, however, is the knowledge of where to place the spikes.
> 
> *
> 
> ”
> 
> — The [Hero of Ages](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hero_of_Ages "Hero of Ages") [^91]

The points which can be used for Hemalurgy are known as "bind points" and there are between 200 and 300 in the human body with different points being useful for distinct sets of effects.[^7] Seeing into the Spiritual Realm, perhaps by burning atium, helps with placing spikes.[^92]

Another important aspect of Hemalurgy is spike location. The spike must be placed in certain locations on the human body in order to steal a power or attribute, and then placed in another point on the recipient to tap into their spiritweb. Most common attributes are stolen through the heart. If a spike is placed in the wrong location it can have unforeseen effects, such as killing the recipient, granting the wrong power, or possibly not granting any power at all.[^93] Beyond that, bind points may function differently if the victim has a sufficiently altered spiritweb.[^94]

There are other ways to use spikes than to place them in bind points.[^95]

## Other Uses

> “ *I maintain, however, that the one of these with the largest potential impact on the cosmere is Hemalurgy. Usable by anyone with the right knowledge, this dangerous creation has proven able to warp souls regardless of planet or Investiture, creating false Connections that no Shard designed or intended.* ”
> 
> — [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") discussing Hemalurgy [^11]

### Scadrial

- Allomantic and Feruchemical abilities granted with Hemalurgy cannot be inherited.[^107]
- If an Allomancer attempted to burn a Hemalurgic spike, the Allomancer's spiritual DNA would be spliced to that of the person's contained in the spike. The consequences of this are as of yet unknown.[^108]
- Allomancers cannot burn Hemalurgic spikes that have extracted powers from others; they would only be able to burn a spike that was used to steal power from them.[^109]
- An atium spike granting Allomantic atium could be briefly burned by the recipient.[^110]
- The same piece of metal can be Hemalurgically and Feruchemically charged at the same time.[^111]
- When a Feruchemical ability is stolen, the spike's recipient is able to access any metalminds created by the donor.[^112] If that spike was split into two pieces and given to two different people, they would both be able to access their donor's metalminds but not those made by the other person, due to the recipients' own identities "muddying" the metalminds' keying.[^112]

### Sel

- More could be taken from an [Elantrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantrian "Elantrian") than most other beings.[^113] Reod Elantrians can also be spiked. Two spikes would be required to steal an Elantrian's powers, with one spike stealing the powers and the other spike stealing the Elantrian's [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]].[^114]
- It is possible to steal Investiture a [Forger](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Forger "Forger") has given someone (e.g. if a Forger made someone an Allomancer, a Hemalurgist could steal that ability and become an Allomancer without needing the [soulstamp](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulstamp "Soulstamp")).[^115]

### Nalthis

- It is possible for a spike to be [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakened]], but it would be more difficult than Awakening metal without a Hemalurgic charge.[^116] It is theoretically possible to spike a [[Lifeless - The Coppermind|Lifeless]], if "there was enough of the soul left for the spike to take".[^117] Hemalurgy can also be used to spike a [divine Breath](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Divine_Breath "Divine Breath") from a [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]],[^118] and the recipient would not need to consume a Breath a week.[^119] Normal Breaths can be stolen using a nicrosil spike.[^120]

### Roshar

- It is possible to steal [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], but it is more difficult compared to other Investitures due to the [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]].[^121] Since a [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] still has autonomy over the bond,[^122] they can break the bond and the Hemalurgist could immediately lose the power.[^123] A Hemalurgist can also spike spren [^124] [^125] and spike the Nahel bond from the spren.[^126] Doing so would have weird ramifications.[^127] In order to effectively steal Surgebinding, the Hemalurgist would need to spike both the Surgebinder and the spren.[^128]
- It is possible to steal a dead [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), although Hemalurgic decay would lengthen the summoning time.[^129]
- It is possible to remove a boon and curse granted to someone else from the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]]. It is also possible, though much more difficult, to separate the boon and curse.[^130]
- Hemalurgy can be used to replicate the transformation of a [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] Forms.[^131] It can also be used to spike the ability to grow a [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemheart]].[^132]

### Cosmere

- Hemalurgy is able to hold a [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]] to a body in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] by spiking them through the right eye socket.[^133] This also grants iron/steel sight for the spiked eye.[^134]
- Dawnshards, even Dawnshards without any other Investiture, protect themselves, so a Hemalurgist attempting to steal a Dawnshard would have "a very bad time." [^135]
- Hemalurgically granted abilities will not create [[Resonance - The Coppermind 7|resonances]] most of the time, though a person can still become a [[Savant - The Coppermind|savant]] with them.[^136] The extra abilities gained by a savant cannot be transferred through Hemalurgy.[^137]
- Though difficult, it is possible to spike a [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] via their hordelings, similar to how a body is spiked by only touching some of a person's cells.[^138]
