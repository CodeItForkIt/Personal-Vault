## Compounding

**Compounding** is a technique for using [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]] to enhance [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemy]]. A person capable of Compounding is referred to as a **Compounder**.

## Mechanics

> “ *However, if the Feruchemist were also an Allomancer, he might be able to burn his own metal storages, releasing the energy within them tenfold. Mistress Vin tried to burn some of my metals earlier, but couldn't access the power. However, if you were able to make up the Feruchemical storages yourself, then burn them for the extra power...*”
> 
> —Sazed [^1]

When a [Metalborn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Metalborn "Metalborn") has Allomantic and Feruchemical abilities in the same metal, they may store an attribute inside of a [metalmind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Metalmind "Metalmind") and then Allomantically burn the power. This gives a supercharged burst of the Feruchemical attribute, rather than the typical Allomantic ability.[^2] For example, a [[Twinborn - The Coppermind|Twinborn]] with both abilities in [[Iron - The Coppermind|iron]] can store weight briefly in a metalmind and then burn the metalmind to draw out an order of magnitude more weight than was originally stored (instead of receiving the usual Pulling ability associated with the Allomantic burning of iron). Both [[Sazed - The Coppermind|Sazed]] [^1] and [Waxillium Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Waxillium_Ladrian "Waxillium Ladrian") [^2] suggest that the Feruchemical power withdrawn is multiplied tenfold, but it is unclear how accurate this number is.

To understand the mechanics for this ability, one must first understand the differences between Allomancy and Feruchemy. Allomancy is considered an end-positive system, meaning power is drawn from an outside source--in this case, from [[Preservation - The Coppermind|Preservation]].[^3] Feruchemy is an end-neutral system, with the power for the magic being drawn directly from the magic user--the amount of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] drawn out of a metalmind is equal to, and limited by, the amount of Investiture that was stored in the first place. In both cases, the type of metal acts as a focus, determining the form in which the magic manifests.[^4] When Compounding is performed, the nature of the Investiture already stored in the metalmind determines the form taken by the magic--thus Feruchemical attributes are released.[^5] However, because the Allomancy is fueled by Preservation, the amount of Investiture released is amplified. It is as if a previously hand-powered device were hooked up to an electrical cord to provide greater power output.[^6]

The power of Compounding is even greater than it may appear at first glance because the Feruchemical attributes being released may also be stored, rather than used, and the process repeated with a greater reserve of Investiture at the Compounder's disposal.[^1] Repeated cycles allow for exponential (and effectively infinite) access to Feruchemical attributes.

While Compounding typically refers to Allomancy enhancing Feruchemy, there is likely a way for Feruchemy to enhance Allomantic abilities as well.[^7]

## Limitations

> “ *However, Feruchemy gives decreasing returns—it takes more than the proportionate amount of strength, for instance, to make yourself four times as strong as a regular man, as opposed to simply twice as strong. In the Lord Ruler's case, this meant that he had to spend more and more youth to keep from aging.* ”
> 
> —Sazed [^1]

Despite the use of Allomancy to burn the Feruchemically charged metalminds, there is no Allomantic effect when Compounding, only the enhanced Feruchemical effect.[^8]

Even for someone who knows it is possible, Compounding is not an intuitive ability, but rather takes time to learn.[^9] Hemalurgists also need to learn to overcome " [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] contamination" to be able to Compound,[^10] though it is unclear whether this has always been the case or if it is an additional restriction that originated after the Ascension of Harmony.[^11]

To perform Compounding, the metalminds themselves must be burned. Thus a person can only Compound only so long as a supply of metal is accessible for the purpose of producing more metalminds.[^12] These metalminds also must be small enough to swallow or insert into the body, such that they can be Allomantically burned. These restrictions effectively impose an upper limit on how much of a Feruchemical attribute a Compounder can draw on continuously.

Compounding is also only possible with a metalmind that is not "locked" by Spiritual Identity--that is, the metalmind must either have been created by the user themself or it must be "unkeyed".[^13] [^14] Any Allomancer is technically capable of Compounding--even without Feruchemical abilities--if the metalmind has been "unkeyed" by means of [[Aluminum - The Coppermind#Feruchemical use|Feruchemical aluminum]].[^15]

## Dangers

Compounding brass too vigorously could also result in overheating, leading to heatstroke and other heat related maladies, including death.[^16]

According to rumor, Miles Dagouter would constantly tap his metalminds for healing. Waxillium took this to confirm his suspicions that Compounding could be dangerous to stop once started--presumably because the Compounder's body comes to rely on the stores provided.[^12] This suggests that Compounding often leads to [[Savant - The Coppermind|savanthood]].

Steel Compounders, while unaffected by G forces, have an upper limit where air resistance and friction will burn them up and potentially kill them if they are not careful with their speed.[^17] [^18]

## History

> “ *The man's virtually immortal. I heard he once took a shotgun blast to the face point-blank and walked away from it.*”

The [Lord Ruler](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lord_Ruler "Lord Ruler") used this ability extensively, as he was both [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Mistborn]] and a full [Feruchemist](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Feruchemist "Feruchemist"), so he could Compound in every metal. He would age briefly to store youth in [[Atium - The Coppermind|atium]] and then burn it to effectively live forever.[^1] He also used gold Compounding to heal very rapidly, leading to rumors that he survived flaying and decapitation. Compounding is also one potential reason the Lord Ruler persecuted Feruchemists so extensively--he did not want Feruchemists to mix with Allomancers and create more Compounders.[^1]

During the [[Final Empire - The Coppermind|Final Empire]], [[Steel Inquisitor - The Coppermind|Steel Inquisitors]] were not taught how to Compound and usually did not have a combination of abilities that would allow for Compounding, though some Inquisitors may have figured it out.[^19] [^1] [^20] While the additional spikes they gained after the death of the Lord Ruler would have enabled them to Compound at least some metals, even with [[Ruin - The Coppermind|Ruin's]] help, they did not have time to learn how to do so before the [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|Catacendre]].[^9] However, [Marsh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Marsh "Marsh"), the sole Inquisitor still alive after the Catacendre, eventually learned to Compound and used a bag of atium taken from [KanPaar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/KanPaar "KanPaar") to extend his lifespan just as the Lord Ruler had done.[^21] [^10]

[Miles Dagouter](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Miles_Dagouter "Miles Dagouter") also used gold Compounding to heal himself incredibly fast with an effectively limitless supply of health--hence his nickname, Miles Hundredlives.[^2]
