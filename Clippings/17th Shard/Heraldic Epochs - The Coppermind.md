## Heraldic Epochs

The **Heraldic Epochs** is a period in history of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that preceded the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]].[^1] It's sometimes known as the **Silver Kingdoms Epoch**.[^2]

## History

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Heraldic-Epochs---The-Coppermind-1.png]]

by [taratjah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/taratjah "Coppermind:Artists/taratjah")

The Heraldic Epochs began with the conflict between humans and [Singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers"), when the latter decided to ally with [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^3] To combat this, [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] chose ten [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), thus forming the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].[^4] In short, the Oathpact stated that as long as the Heralds were on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], Odium could not start another [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]]; to combat this, he tortured the Heralds until at least one of them broke. A single such cycle - a period of peace punctuated by a Desolation - was a single Heraldic Epoch. As each time, the Heralds broke faster, the Epochs grew shorter and shorter, with the initial ones separated by over a century, while the very last were only years apart.[^5] In total, there were somewhere between fifteen and fifty Desolations.[^6] While many chronological details are uncertain, it is known that the [Worldsinger](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldsinger "Worldsinger") [[Senne Khald - The Coppermind|Senne Khald]] lived in the Sixth Epoch,[^7] and that [Dalinar's vision](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") of [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|Midnight Essence]] creatures attacking the town of Starfalls takes place in the Eighth.[^8]

Though initially only the Heralds had the access to [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], eventually the [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] sought to mimic those powers and began bonding humans.[^9] Initially, those new Surgebinders were unorganized and uncoordinated, with some even turning on fellow men.[^10] Sometime during the reign of [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]], the Herald [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] imposed order on them, leading to the formation of [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^11] Henceforth, the Knights would battle the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]] alongside the Heralds.

The city of [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]] was destroyed by unknown means during one of the Desolations, leading to the formation of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]].[^12] Sometime after, a legion of [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singers]] was sent there for unknown reasons and chose to defect, becoming the [[Listener - The Coppermind|Listeners]].[^13]

The Heraldic Epochs came to an end with the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], or the [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam"). At the end of that Desolation, only the Herald [Taln](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taln "Taln") was dead. The other Heralds decided to leave the Oathpact, theorizing that one of them would be enough to keep it functioning, and abandoned their [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]], then told mankind that this day marked the final victory over the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]].[^4] With them vanishing - supposedly to battle in the [[Tranquiline Halls - The Coppermind|Tranquiline Halls]], while in fact they simply went into hiding - Roshar entered the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]].[^1]

## Culture and Politics

Though the Heraldic Epochs are considered to be Roshar's golden age, in truth they saw a period of significant civillizational decline.[^14] [^4] Each [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]] brought with it a vast loss of knowledge and technical know-how, to the point where the Heralds would leave mankind with the knowledge of forging bronze and return to find them in stone age.[^4] With every return, they had to re-teach mankind a number of important skills.[^15]

It's possible that this was not merely an accident of constant war, but a deliberate effort by Odium, as the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-Nar]] is mentioned as deliberately targeting wordsmen, the literate people of [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon's]] era.[^10] Even the periods between Desolations were fraught with peril, as creatures such as [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|Midnight Essence]] were a constant threat.[^2]

Politically, Roshar was divided into ten [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]]. Each Kingdom had its own purpose in the Desolations, and each had its own [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] leading to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], the seat of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] and the functional capital of the world at the time. Those kingdoms were built by the Heralds and, at least according to myths, led by them.[^1] However, smaller countries appear to have existed as well, such as [[Eiliz - The Coppermind|Eiliz]] and [[Tarma - The Coppermind|Tarma]];[^10] whether they were vassal states to the Silver Kingdoms or independent nations that weren't recorded in history is unclear.

Unlike in modern [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorinism]], writing and reading wasn't restricted to women, as words *men* existed.[^10] Conversely, waging war wasn't restricted to men, as there were female Knights Radiant.[^2]

## Legacy

On Roshar, Heraldic Epochs are fondly remembered as the times that Heralds steered the course of the world and men were bound by honor.[^14] Great men of that era, such as [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]], are revered even in the present day.[^16]

One of the primary tenets of the [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") religion states that at the end of the Heraldic Epochs, the Heralds had pushed the Voidbringers back to the [[Tranquiline Halls - The Coppermind|Tranquiline Halls]] and were fighting to force them out of there as well. According to Vorinism, there were one hundred [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], including [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam").[^1]
