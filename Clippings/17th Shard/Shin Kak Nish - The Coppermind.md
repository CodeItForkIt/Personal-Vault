## Shin Kak Nish

**Shin Kak Nish** was one of the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]] of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^1]

## Geography

Shin Kak Nish was situated on the west coast of the Rosharan continent, bordering the Silver Kingdoms of [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]], [[Sela Tales - The Coppermind|Sela Tales]] and [[Makabakam - The Coppermind|Makabakam]]. The kingdom's territory was essentially the same as the modern country of [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]].[^1] [^2] It was separated from the bordering kingdoms by the [[Misted Mountains - The Coppermind|Misted Mountains]], and it had a long, mountainous coastline on the [Aimian Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aimian_Sea "Aimian Sea").[^1]

The kingdom contained an [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]], but its exact location is not known. It appears to be the only Oathgate that [Nazh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nazh "Nazh") did not narrow down to a particular city in his research.[^3]

## Culture

Shin Kak Nish was the original land given to humans when they arrived on Roshar as refugees from a (possibly self-inflicted) cataclysm on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]].[^4] [^5] [^6] The humans brought non-native flora and fauna with them, giving Shin Kak Nish a unique ecosystem that has persisted into the modern day.[^5] The ground is generally covered in soil and grass, rather than bare stone as in most of Roshar.[^7] Additionally, [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]] are quite weak by the time they reach Shin lands,[^8] and [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] are relatively rare.[^9] The Shin people also have a distinct physical appearance compared to other Rosharans, with pale skin and large, round eyes with no epicanthic folds.[^10] [^11]

Due to their isolation, the Shin people also developed unique food and drink. They are the only culture that makes wine from actual grapes,[^12] as grapes are not otherwise native to Roshar and "wine" is typically made from other fruits.[^13] In one of [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions"), [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]] mentioned that he planned to bake Shin loaf bread, and that it was notably fluffier than bread made from other grains on Roshar.[^10]

The Shin people typically append one of their parent's names to their given name (e.g. [Szeth-son-Neturo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Szeth-son-Neturo "Szeth-son-Neturo")) and this tradition dates to the Silver Kingdoms era, if not earlier.[^14]

Like the other Silver Kingdoms, the name Shin Kak Nish is a palindrome, considered holy in [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorinism]]. The Shin have their own names for each [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty"), such as [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nin-son-God]] [^15] and [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishu-son-God]].[^16] "Kak" may derive from [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]], one of the Heralds, or the number 8.[^17] Modern Shin people have their own religious traditions such as [Stone Shamanism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone_Shamanism "Stone Shamanism"), but it is not clear when these traditions began.

## History

According to the [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele"), the humans who had been accepted into Roshar by the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] later turned on them using the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]]; the singers began calling the humans " [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] ".[^18]

During the [[Heraldic Epochs - The Coppermind|Heraldic Epochs]], Shin Kak Nish seemed to have a working relationship with the other kingdoms. Because of its geographic isolation, use of their [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] was likely key to their interaction with other peoples.[^19] Although records are spotty, some Shin people were either [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] or fought alongside them in the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], such as [Naze-daughter-Kuzodo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Naze-daughter-Kuzodo "Naze-daughter-Kuzodo") [^20] and [Malad-son-Zent](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Malad-son-Zent "Malad-son-Zent").[^14]

Despite its location surrounded by nearly impenetrable mountains,[^21] the kingdom did have some conflicts with its neighbors. [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]] spoke to [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] about an ancient conflict near the Shin Kak Nish border after a Desolation.[^10] At some point, a Shin leader named [Shubreth-son-Mashalan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shubreth-son-Mashalan "Shubreth-son-Mashalan") led a campaign known as the Shin invasion and presumably took over a large portion of the continent behind the might of his cavalry,[^22] [^23] [^24] since horses were among the animals that humans had brought to Shin Kak Nish.[^5] However, Shubreth's empire is thought to have collapsed because it expanded too quickly.[^22]

A Shin man appeared to be the first person to find the abandoned [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]] after the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance") [^14] and most of them remained with the Shin for millenia.[^25] [^26] In the modern day, the Shin people are generally thought of as pacifistic isolationists.[^27] [^7] Because of their secretive nature,[^25] it is not clear how or why Shin society may have changed after significant events in their history such as the Shin invasion or the end of the Heraldic Epochs.[^28]
