## Initiation

**Initiation** is the process by which a person is chosen to gain the ability to access a manifestation of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] (and/or the process of actually gaining such access).[^1] [[Shards|Vessels]] can choose to alter how Initiation for their Shard's magic works to some extent, subject to some limitations determined by the underlying mechanics.[^2] The method of Initiation varies between different manifestations, but there are [[17th Shard|cosmere]] -wide principles underlying the different methods: in order for a person to be Initiated, it is generally necessary for their soul to have some degree of "damage", creating cracks in their [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|spiritweb]] that Investiture can rush into and fill, thereby linking them to the magic.[^3] Some magic systems make it much easier than others to achieve this.[^2]

## First of the Sun

[Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(being\) "Patji (being)") seems to have great control in who can become a [Navigator](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Navigator "Navigator"). Only those that are tested by them, and who survive, can Navigate.[^4] It seems that only trappers qualify, and not every trapper who eats [Luminist](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Luminist "Luminist") can become a Navigator. It is unclear why some trappers can’t Navigate, they are strong and capable too, having proven themselves worthy. At least one former trapper has speculated that they may have access to a different [[Invested Arts|Invested Art]]. Arcanists believe that the reason only trappers can Navigate is because they have lived near the perpendicularity for so long.[^5] Additionally, both the ability to Navigate and the various [Aviar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar") talents can be shared through the Nahel Bond.[^4] Aviar must eat [glowing worms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Glowing_worm "Glowing worm") to gain their talents.[^6]

## Scadrial

The method of Initiation for [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]] is known as [[Snapping - The Coppermind|Snapping]].[^7] A [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrian's]] potential for Allomancy is set at birth according to their [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical]], [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive]], and [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual]] aspects;[^8] sufficient trauma during life (or even during birth itself) [^9] can then provide the necessary damage to the soul to release this latent potential. A large part of the potential is determined genetically, and Allomancy is known in-universe to run through family lines.[^10] [^1]

After [[Sazed - The Coppermind|Sazed]] [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|Ascends]], creating the new Shard [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]], he becomes bothered by how Snapping works and alters it somehow,[^11] making it much easier to reach the necessary threshold of Spiritual damage; however, he is unable to remove the need for the process entirely.[^12]

Initiation for [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemy]] also has a genetic component;[^13] whether it has any further requirements analogous to Snapping is unknown.

[[Hemalurgy - The Coppermind|Hemalurgy]] grants powers which have first been stolen from another person via a metal spike.[^14] [^15] [^16]

## Roshar

[[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] differs from Snapping in that the determination of who is granted access is made on a case-by-case basis by conscious entities (the [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] of the [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]]) depending on the behavior of the person. They are looking for people with broken spirits, as with Snapping,[^17] but each kind of spren also applies to its own specific requirements. Also, perhaps uniquely among magic users, Surgebinders gain access to additional power by swearing [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|oaths]],[^18] [^19] and can lose power or break the bond entirely by violating their oaths or acting contrary to their spirit.[^20] [^21] [^22]

## Nalthis

The [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] are individually chosen by [[Endowment - The Coppermind|Endowment]]; after a [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthian]] dies, she may choose to grant them a [[BioChromatic Breath - The Coppermind|Divine Breath]], bringing them back as a Returned. She is looking for "certain things" when choosing who should Return, but can be erratic in her decisions.[^23] [^24]

[[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]] has the easiest prerequisites of all: anyone who is given [[BioChromatic Breath - The Coppermind|BioChromatic Breath]] can use it for Awakening (even, hypothetically, real-world humans),[^25] and Endowment provides each Nalthian with a Breath automatically.[^26]

## Sel

The moment of a [[Sel - The Coppermind|Selish]] person turning into an [Elantrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantrian "Elantrian") and gaining access to the [[AonDor - The Coppermind|AonDor]] is called the [[Shaod - The Coppermind|Shaod]]. It is distinctive in that it only affects those with sufficient [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to certain geographical areas on [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]], primarily [[Arelon - The Coppermind|Arelon]],[^27] [^28] which happens because the Investiture of [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]] and [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]] is trapped in the Cognitive Realm rather than the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]].[^25] [[Khrissalla - The Coppermind|Khriss]] considers it one of the more mysterious forms of Initiation, as she is unable to find out anything about how Elantrians are chosen beyond the geographical restriction; she guesses that there may simply be an element of randomness involved.[^1]

In order for a person to become a [[Forgery - The Coppermind|Forger]] a few things have to occur. One generally has to be born in [[MaiPon - The Coppermind|MaiPon]];[^29] ancestry there does not seem to be required,[^30] though it is possible that some combination of being of MaiPon descent or being born nearby and living in MaiPon at the time one gains the ability may also fill this prerequisite. However, not everyone who meets the geographical requirements can use the magic system,[^31] despite what some believe.[^32]

Little is known about [[Dakhor - The Coppermind|Dakhor]], but it may only be available to those from [[Fjorden - The Coppermind|Fjordell]], and training in [[Shu-Dereth - The Coppermind|Dakhor Monastery]] somehow enables monks to access it.[^30]

Even less is known about [[ChayShan - The Coppermind|ChayShan]] and [[Bloodsealing - The Coppermind|Bloodsealing]] with the only known perquisite is that the person must be [[JinDo - The Coppermind|JinDo]] or [Dzhamarian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dzhamarian "Dzhamarian").[^33] [^30]

## Taldain

The precise method of Initiation for [[Sand mastery - The Coppermind|sand mastery]] on [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]] is unknown, but it differs from that of AonDor.[^1] Sand mastery must be taught and does not appear spontaneously.[^34]

It is unknown how someone gains [starmarks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Starmarks "Starmarks"), but only a few are chosen to access the power.[^34]

## Vax

Initiation on [[Vax - The Coppermind|Vax]] works differently than it does on Sel, Scadrial, Nalthis, or Taldain.[^1]

## Trivia

- An out-of-universe reason that the magic systems often target broken people is that "characters in pain are the most interesting to write about".[^3]
