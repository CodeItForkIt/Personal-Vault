## Fain

The **fain** or **fainlife** is a class of lifeforms on [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]].[^1] Fain creatures generally have six limbs.[^2]

Two of the three sapient species from Yolen, the [[Sho Del - The Coppermind|Sho Del]] and [dragons](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragon_\(cosmere\) "Dragon (cosmere)"), are fain;[^3] [^2] the Sho Del fill the same niche in the fain ecosystem that humans fill in the normal one.[^4] Another form of fainlife is [[Tamukek - The Coppermind|tamukeks]], bones that are somehow similar to [[Singer - The Coppermind|singer]] [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemhearts]] and can be used for inter- [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realm]] communication in some way.[^5] [^6] Fain flora is bone-white and [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast]] describes fain animals as likely to be timid upon encountering a significant amount of color for the first time.[^7] [^8] The dragon [[Frost - The Coppermind|Frost]] has a throne made of fain wood, which he sits on while taking prayers.[^9]

Though it originated on Yolen, the fain ecosystem has spread beyond that planet in significant ways.[^10] [^11] Knowledge of its existence is not uncommon among [[17th Shard|cosmere]] -aware people after the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering of Adonalsium]]; when discussing how [[Ruin - The Coppermind|Ruin]] and [[Preservation - The Coppermind|Preservation]] used the Yolish ecosystem as a model for [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]], [[Khrissalla - The Coppermind|Khriss]] is able to mention without further explanation that "of course" this means the non-fain parts.[^1]

At some point in history, there were rivalry between the fain and the unfain. [^12]

## Unpublished Works

In *[The Liar of Partinel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Liar_of_Partinel "The Liar of Partinel")*, the fainlife had rapidly grown to cover much of the planet, though humans eventually learned to keep it in check with something known as a **trune ring**;[^13] [^14] **trune** was the name given to the regular, non-fain ecosystem.[^15] A type of fainlife known as **skullmoss** heralded the advance of further fainlife. Its leaves would sometimes drift across a trune ring and some cities would gather them up and burn them, though this was not strictly necessary. Merely interacting with the fainlife was safe and some cities even used fain wood for construction, but eating any amount of fainlife was deadly to humans.[^14]

In [*Dragonsteel* Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragonsteel_Prime "Dragonsteel Prime"), the Yolish version of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] was the only connection between the fain and non-fain parts of the world. At the Shattered Plains, Sho Del fought humans for a material known as Dragonsteel. According to [Jerick](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jerick "Jerick"), at least some Sho Del had also made it past the Shattered Plains and killed civilians, and had attempted to kill his king.[^16] In this draft, Tamu Keks were long, thin bones that came to a point at both ends that could be found on Sho Del; Jerick was able to sense the power coming off them and warned one of his companions not to touch them.[^17]

In the non-canonical work *The Traveler*, fainlife is described as looking like ordinary flora, though the plants and land overtaken by it are bone-white.[^18]

## Trivia

- The Sho Del, dragons, and tamukeks are some of the few things from *Dragonsteel* Prime that are still part of the cosmere.[^19]
- When writing *[Rhythm of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rhythm_of_War "Rhythm of War")*, Brandon debated whether to have the communication box that [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]] gave to [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] contain a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] or a tamukek, but eventually settled on making it a seon because they have personalities whereas tamukeks are just bones.[^6]
- The fainlife ecosystem was built from Brandon thinking about what sort of ecology would likely give rise to dragons and about what other evolutionary strains would exist in such an ecology.[^20]
