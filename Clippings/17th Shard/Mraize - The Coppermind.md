## Mraize

> “ *Morality is as ephemeral as the changing weather.* ”
> 
> —Mraize [^5]

**Betd**, better known by his title of **Mraize**,[^1] is a member of the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. He is a Rosharan born [^6] and is in possession of several artifacts from across the cosmere. His title is not [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") in origin; it could possibly be [Thaylen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen "Thaylen"), as Mraize is from Thaylenah [^4] and refers to [Iyatil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iyatil "Iyatil") as his [babsk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Babsk "Babsk"), a Thaylen title.

### Appearance

Mraize is a tall man with jet black hair and light violet eyes.[^8] His face and hands are both covered in scars, including one which crosses his cheek vertically and deforms his upper lip. He dyes his eyebrows black and cuts them short.[^9] He is almost always sharply dressed in fine suits, wearing white the first time [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] meets him. He wears a golden signet ring on his middle finger with the three-diamond symbol of the Ghostbloods.

When disguised as Gobby, Mraize wears a black and gold guard's uniform from the [Sebarial](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sebarial "Sebarial") princedom. As Gobby, he has a cordial relationship with [[Jushu Davar - The Coppermind|Jushu]], [Eylita](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eylita "Eylita"), and [[Balat Davar - The Coppermind|Balat]].[^3]

### Personality

Mraize has a refined air which seems to conflict with his scarred appearance. He usually speaks quietly and calmly, but boldly, with an accent Shallan cannot recognize.[^8] Despite his cultured manner, he is not always serious, growing warm and friendly when Shallan has impressed him. Something about him reminds Shallan of [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] when she first sees him, though she quickly denies any resemblance when he turns toward her.[^10] He never seems to be upset or surprised by bad news.[^11]

Mraize thinks of himself as "hunter" in several respects. He has trophies from several notable animals in Roshar on display in his hideout, including a [whitespine](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Whitespine "Whitespine") tusk, a [Santhid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Santhid "Santhid") skull and [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemhearts]] from [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshells]]. He speaks with disdain of [Tyn's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tyn "Tyn") supposed fear of meeting with him, stating that the apparent cowardice shows she is acting like "prey", and refers to [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") as his current prey.[^10] Shallan sees him as a hunter, not waiting for opportunities, but going out and finding them.[^12] He dreams of the opportunity to explore the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] and take trophies from the planets he visits.[^13]

Despite his occasional likeable air, he has no problem with killing, or even letting his allies attempt to kill someone he is interested in, as he gives the other Ghostbloods implicit permission to attempt to assassinate Shallan before she becomes a full member. He mentions he likes to learn the local weapons of cultures he wants to understand. He makes use of a blowgun with poisoned darts which is apparently a [Parshendi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshendi "Parshendi") hunting weapon.[^14] He also speaks of torturing Shallan for information as "fun." [^10] He is willing to blackmail, persuade and manipulate people in order to achieve his objectives.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

Following from this moral compass, he believes that morality is ephemeral and ever-changing, and that it changes depending on how you look at it. Therefore, he considers it an axis he has little interest in, and he pays little heed to it.[^5] He occasionally makes moral arguments to manipulate people, such as a utilitarian argument of sacrificing [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] to improve the lives of many, but he shows no signs of truly believing in any moral philosophy beyond his own interests and those of the Ghostbloods.[^15]

He is unforgiving of those that betray him.[^16] [^17] Despite his lack of morals—or perhaps because of it—he would consider himself to be "nice", although not in the same way of any well-adjusted person.[^18]

As a member of the Ghostbloods, Mraize is seeking a way to get [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] off-world through [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"). Until the death of [Ialai Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ialai_Sadeas "Ialai Sadeas"), he hunted the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]] as he feared that they were close to discovering the secret of making this possible. He is not interested in seeing [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] rule Roshar.[^3]

He refers to the Parshendi as the [listeners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Listeners "Listeners"), one of few in the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind#Warcamps|warcamps]] to do so.[^5]

## Attributes and Abilities

Mraize's power resides primarily in his knowledge and possessions; though he has visited Shadesmar, he has never been allowed off-world [^19]. Among his possessions he has [a "green chicken"](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mraize%27s_Aviar "Mraize's Aviar") which is known to be an [Aviar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar"), although its ability is unknown.[^20] [^21] He has a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] from [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]].[^17] He also has [sand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sand_\(Taldain\) "Sand (Taldain)") from [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]],[^22] a [[Yolish Lightweaving - The Coppermind|Yolish]] [[Fain - The Coppermind|fain]] branch with weird scripts,[^23] a silver knife, and an [[Aether - The Coppermind|aether]] he cannot use.[^24] [^25] [^22] He is also believed to have [Tears of Edgli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli").[^10] Many of these possessions and knowledge were gained from trades with beings not of Roshar.[^26] [^20] [^17] [^22] [^23] [^27]

Mraize is skilled with disguises. A frequent disguise of his is "Gobby", an [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") guard. Shallan appreciates his skill, but views it as lacking. She thinks that he wears the persona, rather than the other way around, which causes his true personality to show through.[^28] [^3]

Due to his bond with an [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind#Corruption|Enlightened]] [Cryptic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic"), Mraize has access to the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surge]] of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind#Illumination|Lightweaving]], which he uses to enhance his disguises.[^29] [^19] It is unknown whether Sja-anat's corruption affects his access to [Transformation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Transformation "Transformation").

Mraize also seems to have an uncanny ability to sense the presence of others.[^3] Whether this is due to his having enough [[BioChromatic Breath - The Coppermind|Breath]] to have reached the [[Heightening - The Coppermind#Fourth Heightening|Fourth Heightening]] or to some other, more mundane means is unclear.

## History

### Early Life

Mraize, born as Betd, was born on Roshar in an unknown year in Thaylenah.[^6] [^27] [^4] [^1]

At some point, he became a member of the Ghostbloods. He also at some point bonded with an Aviar.[^5] [^20]

### The Shattered Plains

Mraize, as a member of the Ghostbloods, reported to Iyatil, who reported to Master [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar").[^12] He was confirmed to be behind the assassination attempt on [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] that took place on the *[Wind's Pleasure](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind%27s_Pleasure "Wind's Pleasure")*. He had sent a hunter, [Tyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tyn "Tyn"), to execute the task. When he sent a [spanreed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spanreed "Spanreed") message to Tyn, Shallan, who had killed her, intercepted it and decided to go meet him herself, disguised as Veil. When they met, Shallan kept the pretense of Tyn being alive and managed to get Mraize to trust her with a task. She was to break into Amaram's manor and find the secrets he kept and report them back. Shallan agreed and manages to escape without being followed.[^10]

After that, he was also confirmed to have surveilled the site to supervise the task. He was pleased when he saw Veil again; he initially thought she had not completed her task, but she delivered maps and some texts from Amaram. He was also amazed with her drawing skills and deduced that she had killed Tyn. Mraize offered her the opportunity to be part of the organization, but explicitly said that she was not in yet, and that killing people within the organization was not encouraged at all. On her way out, she decided not to take the coach and hid; she later found that they had killed her coachman during a failed attempt on her life.[^14]

The second task Mraize asked of Veil (Shallan) was to get information from a madman inside [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] ’s warcamp, as well as confirm his allegiance. He was aware that [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]] had learned some information of an extraordinary nature. After Shallan managed to complete her task and deliver information on the madman, who was later revealed to be the [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]], Mraize accepted her into the Ghostbloods. He also said that “the truth destroys more people than it saves”.[^7]

### Urithiru

During the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], Mraize discovered Shallan's true identity. He confronted her days later in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], stating his belief that she was "herself" when she was Veil. He explained that he had her brothers in custody and would deliver them as reward for her services, but wanted to continue working with her. He revealed deep knowledge of the Davar family and confirmed that they owed him a [Soulcaster](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulcaster "Soulcaster").[^30]

Shortly after, he found a position in the inner circle of Ialai Sadeas to keep a close eye on her as she had interests of her own, without allegiances to other humans or listeners.[^5] He was seen wearing a [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sadeas_princedom "Sadeas princedom") uniform,[^5] and was likely the scarred guardsman that carried Ialai's chair to the first meeting of the [Kholin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/House_Kholin "House Kholin") [coalition](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs"), defying [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani's]] order that the participants should carry their own chairs.[^31]

Though his whereabouts and end goals were often unclear, he continued to request more tasks of Shallan. The first was to secure Urithiru and to hunt the source of darkness and expunge it.[^5] Mraize promised information on [[Helaran Davar - The Coppermind|Helaran]] in exchange for the first task,[^5] and later complied. He told her that Helaran was a [Skybreaker](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreaker "Skybreaker") acolyte who probably went to kill Amaram on their behalf.[^32] On the day of Shallan's wedding, he sent her brothers to her with a note, stating that they were her wedding gift, and that he was giving her an additional reward for completing her task in Urithiru; he reduced her family's debt owed from the destruction of the Soulcaster. The note also explained Shallan's next task: to find the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] who seemed willing to break from Odium and persuade her to join the Ghostbloods.[^33]

He also plotted a trap for the Herald [[Shalash - The Coppermind|Shalash]], attracting her with a painting by [Dandos Heraldin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dandos_Heraldin "Dandos Heraldin") that depicted her. When she came to destroy it, he let her know the location of [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]].[^34]

### Pursuit of the Sons of Honor

Mraize and the Ghostbloods hunted and killed the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]], including [Thanadal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thanadal "Thanadal") and [Vamah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vamah "Vamah").[^35] Mraize infiltrated Ialai Sadeas's camp as Gobby.[^3] Shallan, as Radiant, assassinated Ialai, although she did so because she believed the act to be for the good of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), not because Mraize had ordered it.[^35] Mraize believed at this point that the Sons of Honor were defunct.[^3]

> “ *A part that can be that strong. Let that side of you do what needs to be done. Save your [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|husband]], your [kingdom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"), and your world all at once. Become that hunter, Shallan. Become the knife.*”
> 
> —Mraize to Shallan [^15]

Shallan brokered a deal with Sja-anat to have [[Tumi - The Coppermind|Tumi]], an Enlightened [mistspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistspren "Mistspren"), meet with Mraize. Mraize believed the deal included bonding Tumi to a Ghostblood. Mraize expressed interest in bringing Sja-anat to their side, looking to take advantage of her ancient knowledge.[^36]

Mraize met with Shallan at Urithiru as Gobby. He asked her to hunt [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), leader of the Sons of Honor, in [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity"). In return, he offered to tell her everything, and allow her to ask any question. He also offered her the choice to leave the Ghostbloods, and promised he and his babsk (Iyatil) would not hunt her and would discourage other Ghostbloods from doing so. He implied that he had a spy close to her, a practice he experienced with Iyatil.[^3] To aid in this mission, he gives her a rare communication cube containing the seon [Ala](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ala "Ala"), allowing them to communicate across realms.[^17]

While Shallan travelled to Lasting Integrity, she worked to identify Mraize's spy, planting misinformation amongst her [Unseen Court](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unseen_Court "Unseen Court").[^37] Mraize mistakenly confirms the misinformation from [Beryl](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Beryl "Beryl"), though it is later discovered that he obtained this while spying on communications between [[Pattern - The Coppermind|Pattern]] and [Wit](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wit "Wit").[^11]

During this time, Mraize hunted and killed [Gereh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gereh "Gereh") within Urithiru. [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]] tried to interfere, allowing Mraize to capture her in an [[Aluminum - The Coppermind|aluminum]] cage, which was his main goal.[^38] [^39] He gave her as a gift to [Raboniel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel") as part of a request for the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] to negotiate with his master for the right to utilize the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]]. Mraize also gave her a large pouch full of Taldainian sand.[^8]

> “ *Killing Sadeas saved thousands of lives. Delivering Kelek to us, sending the honorspren to bond [Windrunners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunners "Windrunners"), could save millions.*”
> 
> —Mraize to Shallan [^15]

In Lasting Integrity, Shallan sought out Restares, confirming with Mraize for any suspicious humans, and he ruled them out one by one.[^16] Shallan was upset Mraize withheld information, when she discovered Restares was actually the Herald Kelek.[^15] Mraize had hidden a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger within the communication cube. Mraize asked Shallan to collect Kelek's soul, and in return, she'd become a full member of the Ghostbloods. He argued that the Heralds abandoned the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]] and even though they knew the Fused were back, they either hid or helped hasten the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|desolation]]. This would also give Shallan the opportunity to impersonate Kelek to save [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] from the [honorspren's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") judgment.[^15]

Shallan decided not to kill Kelek and informed Mraize that she was quitting the Ghostbloods. With Kelek's help, Shallan freed Ala, who pretended to have the demeanor of abused child from her time with Mraize (although it remained loyal to the Ghostbloods). Kelek believed that [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye") [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] did not exist before the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), and this was why Mraize hunted him. Shallan told him to leave Urithiru and that she'd hunt the gemstone containing [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]]. She also passed on an insult from Hoid for Thaidakar. Mraize warned her that she'd made an enemy of the most powerful organization in the [[17th Shard|cosmere]].[^17] He would later pass on Hoid's message to Thaidakar.[^40]

### Seeking Mishram

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Mraize---The-Coppermind-3.jpg]]

by [botanicaxu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu "Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu")

Mraize and his Aviar

During the ten days before the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|contest of champions]], Mraize and Iyatil sought to enter the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] and find the prison of Ba-Ado-Mishram to obtain leverage against [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].

At the meeting at the Ghostbloods' hideout in [Narak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narak "Narak"), he indicated that a "special contact" had told him of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] and Navani's planned trip to the Spiritual Realm and that he and Iyatil would follow them there. Before leaving, he placed [Zora](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zora "Zora") in charge of the Rosharan Ghostbloods cell.[^41] By the time of the meeting, he had already obtained [anti-Stormlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Stormlight "Anti-Stormlight") and bonded an Enlightened spren. Although he discussed Shallan as a threat with both Iyatil and Sja-anat, he did not notice her presence at the meeting until Iyatil unmasked her.[^42] He briefly confronted her before Iyatil shot her with an anti-Stormlight crossbow bolt and, using Iyatil's [Elsecaller](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elsecaller "Elsecaller") abilities, transferred himself and [Lieke](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lieke "Lieke") to Shadesmar.[^43] [^44]

Once in Shadesmar, the three disguised themselves as guards traveling with some [Windrunners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunners "Windrunners") from Narak to Urithiru (via Oathgate). They accompanied Shallan, [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]], and the two Windrunners to the Cognitive counterpart of where Dalinar and Navani were preparing to cross over to the Spiritual Realm.[^9] As the perpendicularity opened, Shallan realized the ruse, which caused the Ghostbloods to attack the Windrunners. After a short fight resulting in Lieke's death, Iyatil and Mraize lured the Windrunners away by telling them that the guards whose places they'd taken were being held as insurance. Mraize then stabbed the perpendicularity with an anti-Stormlight dagger, collapsing it. This sucked him and the others remaining in Shadesmar, along with [Gavinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor "Gavinor"), into the Spiritual Realm and cut off Navani and Dalinar's way out.[^29]

> “ *Why would I recruit someone I wasn't at least in part frightened of? Why hunt something that can't fight back?*”
> 
> —Mraize to Shallan [^28]

In the Spiritual Realm, Mraize took the form of various characters within the visions to follow Dalinar towards Mishram. In the first, a vision of humankind first fleeing [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], Shallan recognized him pretending to check animals for wounds because he acted with similar bumbling good-naturedness as in his "Gobby" persona. She tried to stab him, but failed, so she instead feinted another attack and hid her knife when he retaliated. This tricked the characters of the vision into restraining him and keeping him away from her.[^28]

In the next vision, which showed the Heralds swearing their oaths to [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], Mraize took the [[Shards|Shard's]] place. He imitated him perfectly until left alone with Shallan, Renarin, and Rlain, at which point he attacked Shallan again. The spren of Rlain and Renarin quickly pulled them out of the vision, leaving Shallan and Mraize to fight alone. As they fought, he pointed out that Mishram remained imprisoned only because Odium wanted her to remain so. He entreated Shallan's help, explaining that Thaidakar feared Odium's escape, and obtaining the prison would provide leverage against the Shard. She disagreed, likening Odium's fear of Mishram to [Adonalsium's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adonalsium "Adonalsium") murder and Mraize's fear of her. She then attacked him one final time, but before she could kill him, Iyatil pretended to be [Formless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Formless "Formless") and disturbed her enough to cause her to pull out of the vision.[^45]

Mraize was largely uninvolved in the next several visions. In one, he took the role of a soldier, but hesitated to attack Shallan after she spoke with Mishram.[^46] However, they did not interact until Shallan entered a vision led by Mraize himself, situated on a beach in Thaylenah.[^47] In that vision, he talked to her about his dreams of traveling the cosmere, and how they had been denied because he chose poorly in attempting to recruit her. She correctly guessed both that his punishment would be reduced if he could kill her, and that part of the point of the vision was to distract her while Iyatil searched for the prison. He ended the conversation by warning her that, one way or the other, their next confrontation would be the end.[^13]

### Death

Mraize and Iyatil followed Shallan, Renarin, and Rlain to Mishram's prison when Shallan found the mindset that allowed the [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to summon the vision.[^48] Mraize wore his own face, while Iyatil maintained that of Formless. Mraize claimed that Iyatil was still watching Dalinar, but Shallan saw through the lie and realized Iyatil's disguise.[^49] Shallan lethally wounded her with her anti-Stormlight knife. Iyatil reflexively breathed in [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]], which killed her. Mraize subtly took an anti-Stormlight knife from Iyatil's corpse, intending that Shallan wouldn't notice it. As the two faced off, Shallan Lightweaved a possible future for Mraize, depicting him as the Unseen Court's agent offworld. He considered, but rejected, the offer, deciding either to kill or recruit Shallan instead.[^19]

While Mraize was distracted by looking at the illusion, Shallan placed illusions on herself and Pattern to make them look like one another. She then, as Pattern, tried to tackle Mraize, who assumed that Shallan was trying to get him to use up his anti-Stormlight on the spren. When she attacked him with a knife, though, he realized the illusion. He thought that Shallan seemed to have switched their knives so that hers was now loaded with anti-Stormlight and his was empty. This was also an illusion, though; she had never switched the knives, she had just placed Lightweavings on both. Mraize dropped his knife and grabbed Shallan's, stabbing her in the chest while she stabbed him in the stomach. Now full of anti-Stormlight, Mraize reflexively pulled in Stormlight to heal, which killed him.[^1]

## Relationships

### Shallan Davar

Mraize is Shallan's direct superior within the Ghostbloods, and he gives her most of her missions for that organization. However, Shallan saw him as an enemy who she needs to work against, and that she only does as he asks in order to discover more about him. Mraize originally knew her by the codename "Veil," and saw her as a valuable and competent asset with many skills. He often calls her "little knife" when speaking with her. By the time the Alethi are starting to occupy Urithiru after its rediscovery, he had discovered her true identity, but simply believed this made her more valuable.

After failing to kill Restares, Shallan leaves the Ghostbloods.[^16] Mraize notes that this places her as an enemy of the Ghostbloods; however, he personally views her simply as an obstacle.[^42] Despite their enmity, Mraize still seeks to convince her to join him up until his death.[^19]

### Iyatil

At first glance, Iyatil seems to be one of Mraize's lackeys and a fellow Ghostbloods member, or perhaps his second-in-command. She is the one who follows Shallan while Mraize is investigating her, and presumably reports back to him. However, with Shallan, Iyatil mentions that he is her student, not her master, making her a viable candidate for his babsk.[^50] [^26] Mraize both respects and dislikes her; he views learning under her as a privilege, but resents her restrictions on his [interplanetary travel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopping "Worldhopping").[^19]

### Restares

Mraize speaks of [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares") as an enemy of the Ghostbloods. He sends Shallan to capture Restares with a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger, believing the Herald to have information that would benefit the Ghostbloods.[^15] After Shallan refuses the mission, he has [Felt](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Felt "Felt") and Ala capture and interrogate Restares more conventionally.[^51]

### Thaidakar

Mraize refers to him as Master [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar"), and sends the information Shallan manages to get from Amaram to him.[^14]

### Taravangian

While Mraize and Taravangian do not interact, [Adrotagia](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adrotagia "Adrotagia"), one of Taravangian's companions, makes a note on the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") wondering if a section of it refers to Mraize. Mraize seems to be speaking of Taravangian when he says "I'm not worried about that one. The old fool sows chaos, but does not reach for the power offered by opportunity. He hides in his insignificant city, listening to its songs, thinking he plays in world events. He has no idea. His is not the position of the hunter." [^14] After Taravangian's Ascension, Mraize shares Thaidakar's wariness around him, especially the potential for his escaping the Roshar system. He therefore seeks to take [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram's]] prison for leverage over the Shard.[^45]

## Trivia

- Mraize was previously assumed to be a [worldhopper](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopper "Worldhopper") from his habit of collecting trophies from foreign planets and general knowledge of the cosmere, and was stated to have been off-world in several interviews.[^27] [^26] However, he flatly states he has never been off-world.[^19]
