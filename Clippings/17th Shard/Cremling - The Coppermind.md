## Cremling

A **cremling** is a type of scavenger crustacean that can be found almost everywhere on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^1] [^2] [^3] [^4] Rather than a single species, the term — derived from [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem"), a type of sediment — encompasses a wide variety of creatures.[^1] Cremlings that live in the ocean are sometimes called **sealings**.[^5]

## Appearance and Anatomy

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Cremling---The-Coppermind-1.png]]

by [Vaigerika](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Vaigerika "Coppermind:Artists/Vaigerika")

As there are many species of cremlings, their appearance is not uniform. They tend to be tiny, multilegged, and thin, with elongated bodies and purple blood.[^3] [^6] [^7] Most have claws and a carapace.[^6] [^8] One known variety is [skrip](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skrip "Skrip"), which has a flat tail, two large claws and long antennae.[^9]

Cremlings come in variety of colors. Ones living in the chasms of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] have a reddish hue with semi-transparent carapace, making their organs visible.[^10] In [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]], there are cremlings shaded like the feelers of [shalebark](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shalebark "Shalebark"), while in the Purelake, they have a multitude of colors.[^2] [^3] Some species, including a variety called "sigs cremlings" by the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]], are poisonous, and indicate so by having vividly red claws or bright coloring on their backs.[^11] [^12] Similarly, the Shattered Plains are home to a crimson and yellow species that has a venomous bite, harmless in small doses but dangerous if a victim is overwhelmed.[^13]

Cremlings can make sounds, including screeching and clicking.[^14] [^15] [Songling](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Songling "Songling") - a small crustacean capable of far wider vocal range - might be a type of cremling.

## Ecology

Cremlings grow from larvae, which mature in stagnant pools of water.[^16] Adults live in burrows often located in cracks in the rock.[^6] Some creatures that Rosharans call cremlings are actually larvae forms of larger animals, like [[Chull - The Coppermind|chulls]] or maybe even [[Tai-na - The Coppermind|Tai-na]].[^17] [^18] Some cremlings are known to make nests, which take the appearance of melon-sized domes of crem-like material.[^19]

Cremlings have two primary food sources: scavenging and plant matter. Typically, they drink blood and eat the flesh of the deceased humans, [Parshendi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshendi "Parshendi"), and other creatures, such as [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]].[^20] [^10] [^21] In the period immediately after [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], they expand their diet to plants, as those will often unfurl to drink in the nutrient-rich rainwater, allowing the cremlings to feed on the newly-exposed parts.[^22] In addition, some cremlings seem to have a symbiotic relationship with [shalebark](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shalebark "Shalebark"). They clean the shalebark by eating anything that has built up on it, and take on coloring that helps them blend with it, disguising them from predators.[^3]

Cremlings are in turn preyed upon by [graspers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Graspers "Graspers"), who will swiftly seize them and pull them into their lairs.[^6] Some cremlings live together in groups, generally called swarms.[^23]

For an unknown reason, some cremlings molt occasionally, shedding their carapace.[^8] [^24] Some cremlings are capable of seeing [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]].[^25]

## Culture

Cremlings are generally reviled on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], being treated as little more than annoying and disgusting pests. Calling someone a cremling is one of Roshar's most common insults, with meaning similar to "rat" or "snake".[^26] In idioms, cremlings are usually synonymous with cautious or cowardly behaviour.[^27] [^28]

Cremlings are edible for humans. While not considered high-quality, their overabundance makes them a reliable food source, and a good cook can turn them into a fine dish.[^29] [^9] They are usually boiled, and the water left afterwards can be drank, although the latter seems to be a habit limited to the [[Horneater Peaks - The Coppermind|Horneaters]].[^29] They taste similarly to crabs.[^30]

Some people collect various species of cremlings, pinning them to boards like butterflies.[^31] Ships have been known to get cremling infestations, likely due to the large amount of food present on a vessel.[^23]

As with " [[Chicken - The Coppermind|chicken]] " and " [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] ", the term "cremling" is often misapplied by Rosharans to refer to creatures that aren't part of the same group, like some non-crustacean hordelings, insects and [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshell]] larvae.[^32] [^33] [^17]

## Sleepless

Some cremlings are part of [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] swarms, and share a common hive mind. Such cremlings, called hordelings, are typically heavily-specialized, with some trained to act as ears or eyes, or to assume humanoid coloring so as to allow the Sleepless to pretend to be human.[^34] [^31] They can operate at a significant distance from the rest of their swarm, making them perfect spies.[^35] Through heavy breeding, over multiple generations, they can be evolved to do almost anything, including mimicking [[Larkin - The Coppermind|larkin]] abilities.[^36]

In the past, hordelings have directly participated in the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], fighting on the side of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^34] Dalinar witnesses a burnt pile of them in one of his dream-visions.[^37]
