## Seon

> “ *You look at things as a human, young master. You see rank and distinction; you try to order the world so that everything has a place either above you or beneath you. To a seon, there is no above or beneath. There are only those we love. And we serve those we love.* ”

**Seons** are sapient magical beings that originate on [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]]. They are [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]] [^2] who serve human masters.

## Appearance and Characteristics

Seons take the form of floating orbs of light, each with a glowing [[Aon - The Coppermind|Aon]] at their core for which they are named.[^3] [^4] They are described as "small" [^3] and "melon sized", or about the size of a "child’s head".[^3] [^5] Seons have control over the level of their brightness,[^6] and they often pulse while speaking or laughing.[^7] The light of seons can be described as a pure white, tinged with a mother-of-pearl iridescence.[^8] [^5] It can be difficult to discern their Aons through the light without close examination.[^3] [^9] The general appearance of seons is similar in the Cognitive and [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realms]];[^10] however, this may not have always been the case,[^11] and is likely related to the unusual history of the [[Dor - The Coppermind|Dor]] on Sel and its effect on Selish magic systems.[^12] [^13] Seons are tangible, although they weigh very little; they cannot pass through solid matter and can therefore be imprisoned.[^14] [^10] Seons need to sleep, though less than humans.[^15]

There is only one seon for each Aon, and they intrinsically know what their Aon means.[^16] [^17] Each seon has an individual personality. Seon personalities are very similar to those of humans and they are able to convey emotions easily; they may even have superstitions.[^3] They are generally agreeable, but can sometimes suffer negative psychological effects from stress or abuse.[^18] [^10] Raoden and Sarene each note that their seons are incredibly wise.[^7] [^19]

Known seons tend to have soothing voices. [Ashe's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashe "Ashe") voice is described as "deep" and "sonorous", with well-enunciated words.[^3] [^20] [Ien's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ien "Ien") voice is "indulgent",[^1] [Dio's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dio "Dio") is "melodious",[^21] and [Riika](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Riika "Riika") needs [Mai](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mai "Mai") to tell her a story when she goes to sleep.[^20] However, [Opa's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Opa "Opa") voice is formal and unemotional, which [[Sarene - The Coppermind|Sarene]] notes as unusual, particularly compared to seons in [[Teod - The Coppermind|Teod]].[^9] It is not clear if seons have a gender, but some of them sound masculine while others sound feminine.[^22]

## Powers and Abilities

As [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of a [[Shards|Shard]] of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]], seons are a sapient form of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] and possess several magical qualities. They are immortal.[^23] They generate their own light and have control over their brightness, to the point that they can become nearly invisible.[^9] They move by floating and seem to be able to change their speed and altitude quickly.[^9] They can even reach speeds that are faster than a galloping horse.[^24] They are able to mimic human speech and mannerisms, which they may have perfected over time.[^7] They are able to shrink in size, becoming small enough to squeeze underneath a closed door.[^25] Their senses are superior to those of humans.[^9] They have an innate sense of direction and can point the way to any place they have ever been.[^9] [^26] They cannot be harmed by conventional means.[^27]

They are able to link to each other over long distances to allow their masters to communicate instantly.[^28] This ability seems to function similarly in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] and the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]], and seons are therefore one of the few ways to instantaneously communicate between the Realms.[^8] [^10] They can temporarily transform into a three-dimensional image of the person speaking on the other end of the link.[^3] [^22] [^25] The image shows only the person's features and not anything else in their surroundings,[^3] although sounds will still transfer to the other end of the line.[^5] It is not clear if they can transform for any other purpose. Seons are able to record these communications and can replay them at different speeds.[^28] Seon communications travel faster than the speed of light, unlike [spanreeds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spanreed "Spanreed").[^29]

Although [[Sel - The Coppermind|Selish]] magic generally does not function properly away from Sel,[^30] seons are capable of traveling to another planet.[^31] Their ability to leave the planet is somehow related to the unusual properties of Investiture on Sel, and does not necessarily apply to similar magical beings.[^13]

### Bonds with Humans

Seons can form bonds with individual humans. The human does not have to be an [Elantrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantrian "Elantrian").[^3] They love their masters and act as loyal companions and advisors.[^1] The bond works through [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual]] [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] [^32] and has similarities to the [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^33] The seons are bound only by love and are not obligated to serve.[^34] The bond typically makes the seon and its master very close. Raoden sees [Ien](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ien "Ien") as more than a servant or even a friend; he sees him as the embodiment of his god's love.[^1] A seon could theoretically abandon its master,[^35] but how and why this could happen is not known. Unlike the Nahel bond, the bond between seons and humans has not been shown to give either party any new abilities.

The bond can be Passed from person to person.[^7] The Passing is initiated when the seon's master instructs it to form a bond with a new person,[^36] but the mechanics of the change in bond is not clear. Passing can occur even if the human is an infant.[^7] It is not uncommon to keep a seon in the same family for hundreds of years.[^7] [^36] Raoden believes that the seons inherently need to serve and that they would seek out a new master if theirs suddenly died.[^36] In some cases, it appears that seons can be assigned by an authority figure; Wyrn gives one to Hrathen [^22] and Raoden gives two seons to [Dashe](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dashe "Dashe").[^20] Unbound seons are quite rare.[^10]

## Cultural Role

[Ashe](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashe "Ashe") notes that Elantris was once "overflowing" with seons [^3] and that nearly all Elantrians had one.[^20] After the Reod, seons become increasingly rare as more of them go "mad".[^7] They remain common enough that some people in larger cities still ignore them,[^37] but Ashe mentions that many people have become wary or even fearful of seons, since they associate them with the fall of Elantris.[^7] This attitude is more prevalent in Arelon, the presumed source of seons, than it is in Teod.[^9] After Elantris is restored and Raoden and Sarene become king and queen, seons are once again seen as desirable in Arelon.[^20]

Many people in [Fjordell](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fjordell "Fjordell") do not believe that seons exist,[^3] or they believe that they are "heathen magic" that should be shunned.[^22]

Outside of Sel, seons are extremely rare and highly valued.[^10] Their services can be sold for high prices.[^28]

## Similarities to Other Splinters

### Spren

Seons have many similarities to the spren that live on Roshar. They are both [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] and essentially "the same thing",[^38] [^39] but there are some differences that would not make them interchangeable.[^40] [^41] Unlike spren, seons can leave Sel and do not change appearance in the Cognitive Realm; these differences are somehow related to the unusual state of the [[Dor - The Coppermind|Dor]] and Sel's "odd" magic systems.[^12] [^13] Seons are not as common as spren, as they are limited to a single individual for each Aon.[^16] Both entities can bond with humans, and the Nahel bond with a spren can theoretically be Passed like a seon bond.[^42] Like spren, seons can turn into [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") if they have something to pull them more into the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]].[^43] They can also not be killed by a Shardblade.[^44] Seons could be used to build a [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrial]], and the nature of the Aon in the seon would influence what kind of fabrial could be made.[^45]

### Skaze

Seons are closely related to the [[Skaze - The Coppermind|skaze]] that are also found on Sel.[^46] [^47] They are both sapient Splinters, with the skaze being of [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]] rather than [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]].[^48] Like seons, skaze could theoretically be used to create a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") or build a [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrial]].[^43] [^45] They each appear as floating balls of light and possess unique humanlike personalities. However, skaze glow with a dark, almost "negative" light and are surrounded by a ring of symbols, rather than having one at their center. They also exhibit personality traits like sarcasm that have not been seen in seons,[^49] and could even be considered "evil".[^46] [^47]

Although the origins of both seons and skaze are unclear, they are categorized together by the scholars of [[Silverlight - The Coppermind|Silverlight]] [^50] and were likely created under similar circumstances. The skaze are influential in [Fjordell](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fjordell "Fjordell") [^51] and are not commonly seen or even mentioned in Arelon or Teod.

## History

### Creation

Seons were created by the Splintering of [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]], a [Shard of Adonalsium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shard_of_Adonalsium "Shard of Adonalsium").[^52] [^53] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] traveled to [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]] and killed both [[Aona - The Coppermind|Aona]] and [[Skai - The Coppermind|Skai]], the [Vessels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel") of Devotion and [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]].[^52] After they were killed, the power of their Shards was left in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]] and formed the [[Dor - The Coppermind|Dor]].[^54] The seons were bits of Devotion's magic that became sapient.[^23] [^50] They had counterparts formed from Dominion's magic known as the [[Skaze - The Coppermind|skaze]], and there were some similarities between the two groups.[^50] The presence of the uncontrolled power of two Shards made the Cognitive Realm on Sel extremely dangerous, as the seons and skaze did not provide an adequate "release valve", unlike the [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^55]

The people of [Opelon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Opelon "Opelon") and [[Teod - The Coppermind|Teod]] did not know the true origin of the seons. The most common assumption was that they were created by Elantrians through AonDor,[^22] [^36] despite a lack of documented proof. Raoden realized that this might be inaccurate, and planned to continue researching the origin of seons.[^56] The seons themselves had some awareness of their origin,[^57] but they were not known to have shared this information with any humans.

### The Reod

The [[Reod - The Coppermind|Reod]] had a significant effect on the magic used in Arelon, including seons. The Reod disrupted the Elantrian connection to the Dor and corrupted the [[Shaod - The Coppermind|Shaod]].[^58] Seons of Elantrians lost their thinking capacity, wandering aimlessly and silently within the walls of Elantris.[^59] These seons usually avoided people [^60] and their movements were lazy and sometimes wobbly.[^19] The change in the seons of Elantrians was caused by the Reod's effect on the Connection to their masters.[^61] [^32] Infrequently, a mad seon might show some unconscious recognition of its Elantrian master, but only through classically conditioned actions, not communication.[^19] [^1] Additionally, if closely inspected, the [[Aon - The Coppermind|Aon]] of a seon who had lost its master to the Shaod would appear patchy and incomplete.[^62]

Seons that were not bound to an Elantrian were unaffected by the Reod. During this time (and possibly earlier) [Wyrn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wyrn_\(title\) "Wyrn (title)") [Wulfden the Fourth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wulfden_the_Fourth "Wulfden the Fourth") used seons to communicate with his [gyorns](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gyorn "Gyorn"), an embarrassing and closely guarded secret.[^22] It is not known how Wyrn and the [Derethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Derethi "Derethi") obtained their seons.

Upon the restoration of Elantris's magic, the mad seons recovered their sanity and returned to their former state.[^56] However, they were unable to remember the time that passed [^56] and they may have been fundamentally changed by the experience.[^63] [^64]

### In the Cosmere

> “ *I'm … supposed to stay. I’m not supposed to talk. To you. To anyone.* ”

Around 1167, Shallan used an unknown seon. However, she did not have it by 1172.[^65] [^66]

The [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]] were able to bring at least two seons to [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] through unknown means. [Ala](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ala "Ala") was kept in captivity and given to [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] inside of a special cube for use as a communication device with [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]], who possessed an unknown seon. Ala, who seemed traumatized by the Ghostbloods, was eventually freed by Shallan with assistance from [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]].[^10] [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]], who was also on Roshar during this time, possessed his [own seon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Olo "Olo") that he used to communicate with [[Pattern - The Coppermind|Pattern]] through Ala.[^67] [^28] After Ala was released, Hoid informed Shallan of the nature of seons and the rarity of finding one that was not bound to someone. Ala then remained with Shallan.[^10] There was also a seon, named [Dao](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dao "Dao"), on Scadrial. This seon was working for the Ghostbloods as well.[^5]

## Trivia

- Seons were inspired by a series of paintings by [Michael Whelan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Michael_Whelan "Coppermind:Artists/Michael Whelan") called *Passage*. Brandon saw the paintings in high school and wrote a story involving balls of light, which later became the seons.[^34] The paintings can be seen on Michael Whelan's [website](https://www.michaelwhelan.com/gallery/personal-visions/passage/).
- In an earlier draft of *Elantris*, seons possessed the ability to 'complete' their Aons a single time, creating the effect that their Aon represents in AonDor while killing the seon. Ien targeted this ability on Dilaf, 'healing' him; since Ien's Aon was still incomplete, this turned Dilaf into a fallen Elantrian.[^68] Brandon used some elements of this mechanism in *[Warbreaker](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Warbreaker "Warbreaker")* with the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned's]] ability to sacrifice their [divine Breath](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Divine_Breath "Divine Breath").[^69]
- The origins of seons and their connection to AonDor will be explored further in a sequel to Elantris.[^14]
