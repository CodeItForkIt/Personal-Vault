## Ghostbloods

For the book trilogy named after the Ghostbloods, see [Mistborn Era 3](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistborn_Era_3 "Mistborn Era 3").

The **Ghostbloods** are a secret organization found on many planets in the [[17th Shard|cosmere]], formed for the purposes of protecting [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]] from all threats.[^1] [^2] [^3]

Members are required to get a tattoo of three overlapping diamonds somewhere on their bodies.[^4] [^5] [^6] The symbol bears a resemblance to the three-petal shape of [Marewill flowers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Marewill_flowers "Marewill flowers").[^7]

## Tenets

The Ghostbloods have three general tenets:[^3]

- Protecting Scadrial.
- Sharing what they know with each other, but not with outsiders.
- Trusting each other.

## Objectives

### Scadrian Ghostbloods

The Scadrian Ghostbloods are devoted to protecting Scadrial at any cost, and due to this came into conflict with the [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set") and [[Autonomy - The Coppermind|Autonomy]].[^3] [^8]

### Rosharan Ghostbloods

> “ *Power, portable, easily contained, renewable. You hold the energy of a storm in your hand, Veil. That raw energy, plucked from the heart of the raging tempest. It is tamed—not only a safe source of light, but of power that those with … particular interests and abilities can access.*”
> 
> —Mraize to Shallan [^9]

The [Rosharan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan "Rosharan") Ghostbloods seek power. They are searching for a way to get [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] ([[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]]) offworld from Roshar through [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"). They hunt [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares") and the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]] as the Ghostbloods fear that the Sons of Honor are close to discovering the secret of making this possible. Stormlight is free, portable, easy-to-access power.[^9] Selling it to worlds with very little or rare Investiture could make a huge profit. To attain this goal, they need control of the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]], which inspired their hunt for [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^10] They seek [perfect gemstones](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Perfect_gemstone "Perfect gemstone") that would allow the Stormlight to last long enough to get offworld, though there is a [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] problem.[^9] They have found ways to circumnavigate this by blanking its [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]].[^11]

The Ghostbloods are obsessed with finding [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]], who has the ability to bind minds of an entire people, as leverage should [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] attempt to escape the [Rosharan subastral](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_subastral "Rosharan subastral").[^12] They are not interested in seeing Odium rule Roshar.[^9] They seek to bond [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], as her ancient knowledge would be invaluable.[^13]

They also seek to imprison [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") in gemstones in order to obtain information. [[Kelsier - The Coppermind|Kelsier]] wants knowledge about an affliction that he shares with the Heralds, an inability to leave the [subastral](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Subastral "Subastral") he is in.[^14] They are also concerned about the maladies of the mind other Cognitive Shadows have shown, and are searching for a way to prevent them in Kelsier.[^15]

Their desire to find a way to carry Stormlight off-world and to help Kelsier learn how to leave Scadrial likely ties into the Ghostbloods' goal of protecting Scadrial.[^3] The Scadrian Ghostbloods, however, view them as running amok.[^8]

## Resources

The Ghostbloods own a tenement building in [[Highprince - The Coppermind|Highprince]] [Sebarial's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sebarial "Sebarial") [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind#warcamps|warcamp]] on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] that has a basement. In it was a display of trophies that [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] saw, including several enormous [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemhearts]], a tusk that was probably from a [whitespine](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Whitespine "Whitespine"), an eye socket that looked like the structure of a [santhid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Santhid "Santhid") ’s skull, a vial of pale [sand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sand_\(Taldain\) "Sand (Taldain)"),[^16] a couple of thick hairpins, a lock of golden hair, a [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolish]] branch of a [[Fain - The Coppermind|fain]] tree with writing on it,[^17] a silver knife, an odd flower preserved in some kind of solution, and a chunk of pale [[Aether - The Coppermind|pink crystal]] that looked like it might be some kind of gemstone.[^18] There were also shells that Shallan vaguely recognized. There were no plaques describing any of these mementos.[^19] The Ghostbloods also have acquired a number of Verdant [[Aether - The Coppermind#Spores|Spores]].[^20]

On Scadrial, they have a safehouse at house one eighty-seven, near Thirty-Third and Finete in the Knightbridge district of [Bilming](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bilming "Bilming").[^21] It is protected with [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] locks and contains maps, a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]], an armory, and three jars of purified [[Dor - The Coppermind|Dor]] in a safe with an [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakened]] lock.[^22] They also own a large estate in [[Elendel - The Coppermind|Elendel]].[^8]

Though there is no evidence to this, they presumably have at least a couple more safe houses.

## Structure

The Ghostbloods operate across multiple planets within the cosmere.[^1] They operate within a strict hierarchy, as a group of hunters without structure would turn on each other too easily. They do not strike against their own.[^3] They do not negotiate upward.[^13] It is common for a master to spy on their pupils.[^9] [Ialai Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ialai_Sadeas "Ialai Sadeas") warns Shallan that the Ghostbloods will watch her to ensure she doesn't rise high enough to learn secrets that could hurt them.[^23]

Joining the Ghostbloods requires being brought in by a member of the organization and the swearing of oaths to the Ghostbloods. It is tradition for the one who offers membership to become the mentor of the one they invited.[^8]

Upon joining the Ghostbloods, they share everything they know and the member can influence decisions. The Ghostbloods do not keep secrets from fellow members, but in most cases members are also not allowed to share Ghostblood secrets with non-members.[^3] Mraize warned Shallan that if she were to break ties with the group, she would be hunted. He and his babsk would not hunt her, but all they could do was discourage others.[^9] Members are not to share secret information with anyone outside of the organization; doing so would result in the assassination of the offender due to the danger it can pose to the organization.[^3] [^8]

Kelsier is not on Roshar and only comes as what they consider an avatar;[^24] [^25] he does show directly to the Scadrian Ghostbloods.[^8] Kelsier does affect the Rosharan Ghostbloods' actions, but they are a little removed from him on Roshar.[^26]

## History

### Formation

The Ghostbloods were formed on Scadrial at some point between the Scadrian year 1 [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|PC]] and the Rosharan year.

### Dealings with Gavilar

Kelsier traded information, including information about the heralds, with [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]] in exchange for handing Kalak over to his agents.[^29]

### Attempted Murder of Jasnah Kholin

The Ghostbloods have made several attempts to murder [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]], and claims that she has had a number of their members assassinated as well. When Jasnah spent time in [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]] to study the [Voidbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Voidbringers "Voidbringers"), a Ghostblood agent named [Kabsal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kabsal "Kabsal") infiltrated the local [Ardentia](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardentia "Ardentia") in order to kill Jasnah. He used her young ward [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] to get close to Jasnah, which led to them forming a relationship. As Kabsal attempted to poison Jasnah with bread, he was inadvertently killed when he ate some of the poisoned bread and Jasnah [Soulcast](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulcast "Soulcast") his antidote, mistakenly believing that to be the poison. Jasnah later found the Ghostblood tattoo on Kabsal's arm.

When Jasnah left Kharbranth, the Ghostbloods planted at least [one operative](https://coppermind.net/wiki/New_kid "New kid") aboard the ship *[Wind's Pleasure](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind%27s_Pleasure "Wind's Pleasure")* on the journey from Kharbranth to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. That operative, along with other men Shallan did not recognize,[^30] attempted to murder Jasnah. They thought they'd succeeded upon stabbing her, but in reality, Jasnah had escaped to [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") using [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind#Transportation|Elsecalling]].[^31] [^32]

Jasnah assumes that she was targeted because of her [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Radiant]] [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind#Immortal Words|oaths]].[^32]

### Recruiting Shallan Davar

A man identified with the Ghostbloods was financing [Lin Davar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lin_Davar "Lin Davar") in his bid to become [[Highprince - The Coppermind|Highprince]] of [Vedenar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vedenar "Vedenar"), [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"). After an unknown Veden [Shardbearer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardbearer "Shardbearer") attacked [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Meridas_Amaram "Meridas Amaram"), Amaram speculated that Kelsier and the Ghostbloods were behind the attack.[^33] The unknown shardbearer turned out to be [[Helaran Davar - The Coppermind|Helaran Davar]], Shallan's brother.[^34]

Mraize as an agent of the Ghostbloods

The Ghostbloods were sought out by Shallan Davar, who, under the disguise of Veil, wanted to investigate them.[^35] Shallan met with Mraize and Iyatil in a Ghostblood hideout located in Sebarial's warcamp, and managed to trick them into believing that she was sent there by [Tyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tyn "Tyn"). Mraize, unaware that Tyn was dead, ordered Shallan to tell Tyn to investigate the quarters of Meridas Amaram.[^19] Shallan successfully performed this task, and brought her findings to Mraize, which led to her accidentally revealing her photographic memory. Mraize then deduced that Tyn was dead, killed by Shallan, but commended her for it. He then told her that she could get a place among the Ghostbloods if she completed tasks for them. However, he also allowed other Ghostbloods to attempt to murder her if they wanted to. Shallan managed to avoid the attempts on her life, and carried out her next task of investigating [Taln's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taln "Taln") presence in the warcamp of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] in a satisfactory manner.[^36]

After the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], and the subsequent discovery of Urithiru, Mraize confronted Shallan, and revealed that he had figured out her real identity as Lin Davar's daughter, Jasnah Kholin's ward, and a Knight Radiant with the ability to Soulcast. Mraize reminded her of her family's debt to the Ghostbloods, and also revealed that Ghostblood agents had rescued her brothers from the civil war in Jah Keved and would bring them safely to Urithiru. The Ghostblood operatives succeeded, and the Davar brothers arrived in Urithiru after the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]].[^37]

Shallan's identities debated their interest in joining the Ghostbloods. Veil was interested in joining, but refused to get the tattoo.[^38] Shallan planned to break ties with the Ghostbloods.[^10] [^9] Formless, an imagined fourth identity for Shallan where she hid her self doubt and selfish ambitions, wanted to join the Ghostbloods.[^39]

Mraize offered Shallan full membership in the Ghostbloods in return for assassinating [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), leader of the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]].[^14] Shallan did not go through with it and broke ties with the group. She threatened Mraize to leave Urithiru and warned him that she would be hunting for Ba-Ado-Mishram. Mraize warned her that she'd moved against the group in the most offensive of ways.[^24]

> “ *This will not end well for you, Shallan. You make an enemy of the most powerful organization in all the cosmere.*”
> 
> —Mraize to Shallan [^24]

### Cleansing Urithiru

Mraize asked Shallan to investigate the wrongness in Urithiru, which she was already sensing.[^40] This led her to find, confront, and expel [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir").[^41]

### Pursuit of the Sons of Honor

The Ghostbloods wished to keep an eye on the activities of [House Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sadeas_princedom "Sadeas princedom"), and Mraize was personally spying on them, acting as a guard to Ialai Sadeas in Urithiru.[^9] They murdered [Thanadal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thanadal "Thanadal") and [Vamah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vamah "Vamah"). They struck at Ialai several times and ultimately succeeded when [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] assassinated her with [Blackbane](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blackbane "Blackbane"), though Shallan claimed she did this for the good of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar").[^23] [^39]

Shallan and the [Unseen Court](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unseen_Court "Unseen Court") searched Ialai's bed chambers and found a notebook containing many Ghostblood secrets, including a map of [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), a route through the [Sea of Lost Lights](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sea_of_Lost_Lights "Sea of Lost Lights"), and [Thaidakar's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar") supposed offworld origins.[^9]

Shallan hunted the [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] under the alias of Restares to [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity"). After an internal struggle, Shallan decided against killing him. She offered to share information about the Ghostbloods. He claimed to be close to getting offworld.[^39]

### Recruiting Sja-anat

During her wedding, Shallan is instructed in a letter from Mraize to recruit Sja-anat into the Ghostbloods.[^37] [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]] requested a bond between a Ghostblood and one of her "enlightened" spren. Shallan only arranged a meeting.[^9] Sja-anat sent [[Tumi - The Coppermind|Tumi]], who ended up bonding [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]].[^42]

### Bringing other forms of Investiture to Roshar

The Ghostbloods have plans to commercialize Investiture on a much larger scale than what happened on [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]]. In these efforts they bring [sand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sand_\(Taldain\) "Sand (Taldain)") from [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]], [[Seon - The Coppermind|Seons]] from [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]], and [Aviars](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar") from [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]] to Roshar. They want to use the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]] to facilitate the trade of these items on Roshar, as they would not need to rely on [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularities]]. Mraize also mentions that perfect gemstones would be a good tool for carrying Stormlight off Roshar;[^9] [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]] has noted that there are nowhere near as many perfect gemstones in circulation as there should be, which leads [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] to theorize that a group that is aware of their importance—like the Ghostbloods—has been secretly collecting them.[^43] The [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") of [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity") have collected several perfect gemstones over millennia,[^44] [^45] though it is unclear how many they have compared to how many Jasnah thinks there should be.

### Searching for Ba-Ado-Mishram

During the ten days before the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|contest of champions]], the Ghostbloods sought to enter the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] and find the prison of Ba-Ado-Mishram to obtain leverage against [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^46] During a meeting at the Ghostbloods' hideout in [Narak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narak "Narak"), Mraize indicated that a contact had told him of Dalinar and Navani's planned trip to the Spiritual Realm and that he and Iyatil would follow them there. Before leaving, he placed [Zora](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zora "Zora") in charge of the Rosharan Ghostbloods cell.[^11] By the time of the meeting, he had already obtained [anti-Stormlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Stormlight "Anti-Stormlight") and he and Iyatil had bonded Enlightened spren. After a brief confrontation with Shallan, Iyatil transferred herself, Mraize, and [Lieke](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lieke "Lieke") to Shadesmar.[^47] [^48]

Once in Shadesmar, the three disguised themselves as guards traveling with some [Windrunners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunners "Windrunners") from Narak to Urithiru. They accompanied Shallan, [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]], and the two Windrunners to the Cognitive counterpart of where Dalinar and Navani were preparing to cross over to the Spiritual Realm.[^49] As the perpendicularity opened, Shallan realized the ruse, which caused the Ghostbloods to attack the Windrunners. After a short fight resulting in Lieke's death, Iyatil and Mraize lured the Windrunners away by telling them that the guards whose places they'd taken were being held as insurance. Mraize then stabbed the perpendicularity with an anti-Stormlight dagger, collapsing it. This sucked him and the others remaining in Shadesmar into the Spiritual Realm and cut off Navani and Dalinar's way out.[^50]

In the Spiritual Realm, Mraize took the form of various characters within the visions to follow Dalinar and Shallan towards Mishram.[^51] Iyatil pretended to be [Formless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Formless "Formless") to stay close to Shallan.[^46] On the ninth day, Mraize talked to Shallan about his dreams of traveling the cosmere, and how they had been denied because he chose poorly in attempting to recruit her. She correctly guessed both that his punishment would be reduced if he could kill her, and that part of the point of the vision was to distract her while Iyatil searched for the prison. He ended the conversation by warning her that, one way or the other, their next confrontation would be the end.[^52]

Mraize and Iyatil followed Shallan, Renarin, and Rlain to Mishram's prison when Shallan found the mindset that allowed the [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to summon the vision.[^53] When Mraize and Iyatil attempted to gain access to the prison, Shallan killed them both, but left their spren alive.[^54] [^55]

### Resistance against Trell

The Ghostbloods on Scadrial worked in order to resist the oncoming threat of [Trell](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trell_\(Trellism\) "Trell (Trellism)").[^2]

On 348 [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|PC]] the Ghostbloods attempted to recruit [[Marasi Colms - The Coppermind|Marasi Colms]] and sent Moonlight to deliver a message to her telling that the Ghostbloods were watching her and were impressed.[^56] [^57]

Several days later the Ghostbloods contacted Kelsier via Seon in their hideout in Bilming informing him that the [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set") had developed a bomb to destroy Elendel.[^22] After Kelsier informed the Ghostbloods that he wouldn't get to Bilming in time, TwinSoul, Moonlight and Marasi discovered and infiltrated the Set's underground [caverns](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wayfarer "Wayfarer").[^58] Since Moonlight and Marasi were discovered and confronted by Set soldiers after Marasi's attempt to save a group of people from execution, Moonlight used a [soulstamp](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulstamp "Soulstamp") to rewrite her history and become an [Elantrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantrian "Elantrian") so Marasi could escape.[^59] [^60]. Ghostblood agents in Elendel were ordered to assist the local authorities with organizing the evacuation, as they too would not reach Bilming in time.[^22] [^61]

After [Wax](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Waxillium_Ladrian "Waxillium Ladrian") and [Wayne](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wayne "Wayne") stopped the bomb that the Set sent to Elendel, Marasi met with Kelsier and the Ghostbloods in Elendel and told them that she wouldn't join with them because of their rule of keeping their information secret. After that, TwinSoul supposedly went on a mission to track down Moonlight.[^8]

- The Ghostbloods find [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] intriguing.[^65]
- The Ghostbloods do not trust the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]]. They are worried about having their organization taken over.[^66]
- The [third Mistborn series](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistborn_\(series\)#Era_3:_Ghostbloods_Trilogy "Mistborn (series)") will be subtitled 'Ghostbloods'.[^67]
