## Sons of Honor

> “ *Lives will be lost. It has ever been our burden as the Sons of Honor. To return the Heralds, to return the dominance of the Church, we had to put the world into a crisis.* ”

The **Sons of Honor** are a secretive group on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. Their goal is to return the dominance of the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] church. They seem to be under the leadership of [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), and are referred to as his cronies by the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]].[^2]

## Beliefs

> “ *No, no, no. I wanted the honor of men to return! I wanted us to explore what made those Radiants so grand. Before things went wrong. Before … I made them … go wrong…* ”
> 
> — [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares") [^3]

The beliefs between the highest leadership of the Sons of Honor and its members are generally at odds with each other.

Like the [Envisagers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Envisagers "Envisagers"),[^4] the Sons of Honor believe that by returning the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] they will cause the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") to return as well. They believe this will usher in a new era of Vorin dominance.[^1] This is the reason for many of their members joining but some leaders such as Gavilar and Restares do not think this is the case and have different motives for their actions.[^5] [^6]

The Sons of Honor appear ruthless and have little regard for the loss of innocent life in the pursuit of their goals.[^7] [^1] Despite their organization being based around Vorinism, their leaders do not appear to strictly follow the gender norms dictated by the church. [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") uses the [stormwarden script](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormwarden_script "Stormwarden script") to get around the prohibitions on writing.[^8] Restares likely knows it as well, given Amaram's letter was written in the script.[^1] The group also identifies itself as highly patriotic and loyal to [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar").[^9] [^10] Despite this, they consider the monarchy of Alethkar to be heretics and seek to overthrow them and replace them with [Ialai](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ialai "Ialai").[^5] [^10] Ialai, however, has no interest in becoming queen and instead seeks to instate [Gavinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor "Gavinor") as king.[^6]

Of the organizations that are aware of the Voidbringers and the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], the Sons of Honor are the most incorrect in their beliefs and actions regarding that knowledge.[^11]

## History

The original founders of the Sons of Honor created the organization based on religious grounds, aiming to restart the Desolations and thus, they hoped, humankind's former glory.[^12] [^13] The Envisagers formed as a splinter group of the Sons of Honor.[^14] Eventually someone managed to recruit [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]] and he quickly rose up the ranks of the organization while greatly increasing their power and getting many new recruits. Notably, he is the individual who allowed Amaram to join their ranks.[^15] Over time the new leaders of the organization, Gavilar and Restares, developed their own goals and plans for the groups unrelated to their stated aim.[^6] [^5] Restares learned of Gavilar's plans and responded favorably, ultimately leading to their collaboration.[^16] To facilitate this, the pair held many secret meetings together.[^17] [^13] Despite this apparent closeness, however, Gavilar still suspected Restares of being behind his assassination.[^18]

Following the death of Gavilar, the organization went through changes. [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] decided to leave the organization and founded the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)").[^19] Restares and Amaram also made more frequent contact with each other, with Amaram seemingly taking over the role of Gavilar.[^5] However, unlike Gavilar, Amaram lacked his own agenda and simply followed Restares orders, often even deferring to him for advice.[^8] [^7] One piece of this advice was to recommend that Amaram take the Shards [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] won and kill any witnesses; Amaram instead decided to spare and enslave Kaladin as a sign of his thanks.[^7]

Amaram continued to maintain the power of the Sons of Honor and was later requested to arrive at the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] so that he could help with the war effort there. While there, he continued trying to restart the Desolations and continued his research into the connection between the [[Listener - The Coppermind|Parshendi]] and Voidbringers as well as trying to find the lost city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^8] Following the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]] and the start of the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], Amaram sent word to Restares that the Sons of Honor were successful in restarting the Desolations, though in truth they had no part in starting the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]]. It is unknown if Amaram actually believed he had helped restart the Desolations.[^1] Before leaving to settle in Urithiru, word about the arrival of a madman claiming to be the Herald [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] reached the ears of the Sons of Honor. Amaram ordered five of his men to investigate the rumors and came to recognize that the madman in Dalinar's warcamp was indeed [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]].[^20] He later went to recover the Herald from his cell. At this time he survived an assassination attempt by the Ghostbloods.[^1]

Two members of the organization, highprinces [Thanadal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thanadal "Thanadal") and [Vamah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Loradar_Vamah "Loradar Vamah") decided to stay at the warcamps.[^21] Thanadal and Vamah seized the opportunity to create their own virtual kingdom in the area and they pushed to control trade in the region.[^22] [^23] Following the death of [Highprince Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") during the settling of Urithiru, Ialai took over her husband's duties. She ultimately decided to name fellow member Amaram as the new acting highprince of House Sadeas.[^24] At this time, the Ghostbloods began actively spying on her and her activities due to her status as an unknown element.[^25] Due to the tensions between house Kholin and house Sadeas, Dalinar Kholin ordered that Amaram and Sadeas be moved to clear up and secure [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City").[^26] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] sensed the growing frustrations of Amaram and his troops and began to manipulate him to his own ends; he used the truth about the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds") to finally convert Amaram to his forces.[^9] Amaram was ultimately killed during the ensuing [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|battle]].[^9]

Following this, House Sadeas was humiliated and Ialai became leader of the Sons of Honor. She immediately moved back to the Shattered Plains.[^27] She did what little she could to regain the previous power of the Sons of Honor, but between the death of Gavilar and the actions taken under Amaram's leadership, there was nothing that she could do.[^5] Ialai spent most of her time trying to get in contact with Restares and also began to investigate references she had heard before to the wider [[17th Shard|cosmere]].[^6] [^28] Seeing their weakened state, the Ghostbloods began a final push to eliminate the Sons of Honor and started the process by assassinating Thanadal.[^6] Seeing this, Vamah attempted to flee the Plains [^29] but was also killed.[^6] Ialai decided the best option for her was to try and hide within her lands. This in the end failed, and she was assassinated by a [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Ghostblood agent]].[^6] With her death, Mraize believed that the Sons of Honor were destroyed. Following its destruction, Shallan was given a mission to locate their ultimate and last surviving leader, Restares.[^30]

## Relationships

The Sons of Honor have an antagonistic relationship with both the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]] and the [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Skybreakers]]. [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]] sends [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] to spy on Amaram in order to discover what secrets the Sons of Honor know.[^31] After receiving Shallan's intelligence, they unsuccessfully try to assassinate Amaram.[^1] After the Battle of Thaylen Field, the Ghostbloods spent the next year successfully assassinating the leadership of the Sons of Honor.[^6] The Ghostbloods also greatly hate and despise Restares for assisting Gavilar.[^16] Despite this however, Mraize acts dismissive towards the Sons of Honor, referring to them simply as Restares's cronies.[^2] The Skybreakers also tried to have Amaram killed as a strike against the Sons of Honor when they send [[Helaran Davar - The Coppermind|Helaran Davar]] to his army with [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] to kill him.[^15] This plan, however, resulted in the Sons of Honor gaining control of another set of shards.[^18] The leader of the Skybreakers, [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] also assisted the [[Listener - The Coppermind|Parshendi]] in assassinating Gavilar Kholin.[^32]

The [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") likely knows about the Sons of Honor, as [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] was a member of Gavilar's trusted inner circle.[^33] It is not clear if the Sons of Honor are aware of the formation and actions of the Diagram. The two organizations have similar origins in their awareness of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] but different philosophies on what should be done about it.[^19] [^11]
