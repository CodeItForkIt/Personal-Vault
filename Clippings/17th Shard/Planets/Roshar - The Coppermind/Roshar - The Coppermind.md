## Roshar

> “ *It is difficult not to feel very small, at times, on this domineering, majestic tempest of a planet.* ”
> 
> — [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") [^9]

**Roshar** is the second planet from the sun in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system"), orbiting between [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] and [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]]. It is also the name of the supercontinent that contains all of the planet's known inhabitants.[^7] Roshar possesses three natural satellites, though it possessed four at the point of creation, however, the fourth moon eventually crashed into Roshar.[^8] [^10]

The planet was directly created by Adonalsium around twelve to thirteen thousand years before the Night of Sorrows.[^11]

People from Roshar are referred to as Rosharans.[^12] The planet is particularly noteworthy due to the activity of several [[Shards]] of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]] in the area; the various forms of magic used by its residents, including [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] through the use of [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]]; and the portability of their local form of Investiture.[^7] [^13] [^14]

## Planetary Characteristics

> “ *Here in this world [[Honor - The Coppermind|I]] found perfection, a relic of the [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|being I had slain for his own good]]. Roshar had been grown entirely from equations, as a grand testament to the divine nature of mathematics—a celebration of the intimate relationship between song, numbers, and art... [[Spren - The Coppermind|They]] weren’t replacing Adonalsium’s work, but expanding upon it. Continuing the equations. Nature could be equations. Nature was equations. So were oaths.*”

Roshar was built by Adonalsium following the fundamental mathematics and equations of nature and the universe. This granted it a preternatural orderliness.[^11] [^15]

There are no oil deposits from mass extinction events on Roshar.[^16]

The rainwater in highstorms is laced with a sort of slurry called [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem"). When crem dries, it hardens into rock. Since the highstorms always sweep from east to west, crem builds up on the eastern side of anything that stays still and isn't regularly cleaned. Over time, the continent is very slowly shifting across the planet as the highstorms cause erosion of the western portion and a build-up on the eastern portion.[^17] This rainwater is full of nutrients, and plants given it fare better than those given water from other sources, such as lake or river water. Various cremlings search out and eat plants as they lap up the stormwater.[^18]

### Physical and Orbital Characteristics

![[Roshar---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

Map of Roshar

Gravitational acceleration on Roshar is notably lower than usual, at 6.86 m/s <sup>2</sup> (70% of the cosmere standard),[^7] though it is still within common ranges.[^19] This is due, in part, to the planet's small size; Roshar has a circumference of approximately 22110 miles (35580 km), giving a radius of 3519 miles (5663 km), and comes in just under 90% of the cosmere standard size.[^7] [^20] These yield a planetary mass of 3.296×10 <sup>24</sup> kilograms, and an average density of 4.33 g/cm <sup>3</sup>.

Roshar has 0° of axial tilt.[^21] As a result, the planet does not have a seasonal cycle.[^22] [^23] This also allows Rosharans to easily tell their location and time by the path and location of the sun.[^24] [^25]

The orbital period of Roshar is 1.1 standard years.[^26] [^27] [^28]

A day on Roshar is 20 Rosharan hours and there are 500 days in a year.[^29] The end of a Rosharan year is marked by the first two weeks of the Weeping.[^30]

Roshar does not have plate tectonics,[^4] though it does have earthquakes [^31] [^32] [^33] and a magnetic field.[^34]

### Surface

Most of Roshar's surface is water. The hydrosphere is separated into several oceans, known as the [Endless Ocean](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Endless_Ocean "Endless Ocean"), the [Ocean of Origins](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ocean_of_Origins "Ocean of Origins"), the [Steamwater Ocean](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Steamwater_Ocean "Steamwater Ocean"), the [Northern Depths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Northern_Depths "Northern Depths"), and the [Southern Depths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Southern_Depths "Southern Depths"). The hydrosphere is also divided into seas, such as the [Aimian Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aimian_Sea "Aimian Sea"), the [Reshi Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi_Sea "Reshi Sea"), and the [Tarat Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tarat_Sea "Tarat Sea").[^35]

At one point in Roshar's past there were no large landmasses.[^36] Like all of the Rosharan system, the Rosharan supercontinent was designed by [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]]; it was constructed following the shape of a Julia set,[^37] for the continent was shaped by the fundamental mathematics and patterns of nature and the universe.[^11] [^15] The continent is not formed by tectonic processes,[^4] as it lacks plate tectonics in their entirety. The continent is instead formed and shaped by Adonalsium, and then by wind and storm erosion, and crem deposition.[^17]

The Rosharan supercontinent is the only continent on the planet, though not necessarily the only landmass, though it is probable that there are no other noteworthy landmasses as [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] could not find the [Uvara](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Uvara "Uvara") islands despite three thousand years of searching.[^38] [^39]

The main continent lies primarily in the southern hemisphere, spanning from just a few degrees north of the equator to about 55° south of the equator, and extends approximately 120° longitude.[^35] [^40] [^41] [^42] It covers roughly 4×10 <sup>7</sup> square kilometers.

### Soul and Tones

Spiritually, Roshar is known to have a [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|Spiritweb]], to which the many spren of Roshar [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connect]].[^43] The planet also possesses a soul.[^44] [^45]

Roshar is stated to have four natural tones, likely reaching back to the planet's creation by Adonalsium.[^46] [^15] [^47] On top of these four tones of the planet, the planet is also associated with the tones of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], and [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], who Invested themselves within it.[^5] [^48] [^4] This likely changed after the birth of [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]] and the exodus of Cultivation.[^49] [^50]

The tones, and Roshar itself, appear to possess some form of agency, for they were stated to have "chosen" [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]].[^51] Furthermore, it has been stated to "adopt" Shards who reside within the system.[^4]

### Time Dilation

Following the combination of Honor and Odium into Retribution, time becomes significantly dilated in the space around Roshar in both the Physical and Cognitive Realms. This effect is strongest on the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows") and diminishes in strength over time, until Roshar realigns with the standard flow of time after about a decade, during which approximately seventy to eighty years pass for the rest of the cosmere.[^52] This had a immense effect on interplanetary trade and communication, due to risks involved with entering an area of such heavy temporal dilation. Some parties, due to this, consider such trade infeasible.[^6] [^53]

## Atmosphere

The level of oxygen in the atmosphere of Roshar is higher than that of most other planets in the cosmere.[^7] [^54] This heightened level of oxygen has been present since its creation and is maintained by the production of oxygen from the ocean.[^54]

### Climate

With the exception of [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] and the occasional well-sheltered [lait](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lait "Lait"), all of Roshar is frequently ravaged by powerful [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], massive hurricanes travelling from east to west. These storms come frequently and, though they do not appear to follow a simple pattern, [stormwardens](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormwarden "Stormwarden") are able to accurately predict their schedule through complex mathematics.[^55] As a result, Shinovar is the only place on Roshar that has soil.[^56] A highstorm consists of a high altitude, wide-spread [cumulonimbus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumulonimbus_cloud "wikipedia:Cumulonimbus cloud"), scraping the top of Roshar's troposphere; they can be [derecho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/derecho "wikipedia:derecho") -like storms bringing heavy rain and strong winds. Though with the death of the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] and the Ascension of Retribution the highstorm was consumed by the Everstorm, which now covers the majority of Roshar, excluding [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") and Urithiru.[^50] [^52]

The one consistent aspect of the Rosharan climate is the [[Weeping - The Coppermind|Weeping]]. The Weeping occurs every five hundred days and consists of four weeks of steady crem-free rain. The middle day of those four weeks is known as the Lightday and alternates between being one clear day in the midst of the rain and being a highstorm.[^57] There are typically fewer highstorms in the weeks leading up to the Weeping.[^58] The seasons following the weeping typically have weaker storms, making them good for farming.[^59] Following the appearance of the Everstorm and before the Night of Sorrows, weather patterns become odder and regular rainfall became far more common.[^60]

Due to having zero obliquity, Roshar does not experience true seasons, with them instead simply being defined by current temperature, climate, and storms, unrelated to irradiance.[^21] These seasons are relatively random, and are often quite short, usually lasting only a few weeks.[^58] [^61] They can be roughly predicted by stormwardens. Seasons are primarily defined by changes in the storms.[^21] Due to not being caused by axial tilt, they can vary greatly even between relatively nearby nations on the same hemisphere.[^62]

The equatorial regions of Roshar, particularly the Reshi Isles, are notable for their intense heat and humidity compared with the rest of the planet.[^63] [^64] [^65]

The far south of Roshar gets cold, freezing near the poles.[^66]

#### Night of Sorrows and the True Desolation

Following the birth of Retribution on, the Highstorm was completely ceased by the new Shard and the Everstorm was spread to cover all of Roshar and continue unabating. This led to constant rainfall and lightning, and Roshar being left in an eternal night-like state. This devastating change in the climate deeply impacted local civilizations, and likely impacted the local ecosystem which had a reliance upon the Highstorm. These environmental changes are classified as the Night of Sorrows.[^6] Whilst Roshar was covered by the Everstorm, Azir and Urithiru, as per the agreements made between Dalinar and Odium, were left free of the storm.[^6]

It is unclear if the True Desolation is still ongoing as of Roshar's space age, or if it ceased sometime before such developments.

## Spren and Ecology

### Spren

![[Roshar---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Sheep](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Sheep "Coppermind:Artists/Sheep")

An [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") in the Physical Realm.

Among many factors that make Roshar unique among other [[17th Shard|cosmere]] worlds is the overabundance of [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]]. Spren are small pieces of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] that have acquired sentience - and, in some cases, sapience - through the influence of Roshar's living creatures.[^67] They are a mix of different [[Shards|Shardic]] Investiture, mainly [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], and exist mostly in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]].[^68] [^69] However, they can and will often become partially visible in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], and some transition there entirely.

Three main groups of spren can be recognized. The nature spren correspond to various natural phenomena, such as [[Spren - The Coppermind#Windspren|windspren]] or [[Spren - The Coppermind#Flamespren|flamespren]], which appear in the presence of wind and fire respectively. Emotion spren are attracted to emotions, such as [[Spren - The Coppermind#Fearspren|fearspren]] appearing when one is afraid or [[Spren - The Coppermind#Gloryspren|gloryspren]] for people who feel intense pride.[^69] However, a natural phenomenon or an emotion will not necessarily summon a spren, as they may not be present in the physical place's Cognitive equivalent. All of or a majority of these spren were made by Adonalsium.

The third group of spren are so-called true spren. Those spren are all sentient, and have an organized society in the Cognitive Realm.[^69] They transition to the Physical Realm only when searching for a prospective [Knight Radiant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knight_Radiant "Knight Radiant") to bond, as the shift robs them of most of their higher functions, which they reclaim through forming a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]].[^59] The Radiant spren seem to represent higher concepts, such as honor for the [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") or truth and lies for [Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic"). All ten varieties of true spren were made by Honor and Cultivation, seven being a mix, [one being of Cultivation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cultivationspren "Cultivationspren"), and [one of Honor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren"). The tenth were made to not be as human and these include the Stormfather, Nightwatcher, and Sibling.[^15]

In addition to those, there is a number of unique spren who are far more powerful than others. Among those is the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]], who can grant boons and curses; the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], who controls the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]]; the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]], who controls the tower-city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]]; and [Cusicesh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cusicesh "Cusicesh"), who announces the beginning of the next step of the Long Trail to the Iriali and opens a perpendicularity for them.[^70]

#### Ancient Spren

> “ *There were echoes, of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|my predecessor]]. Little bits of him left behind. Three powerful incarnations who had his voice, and many smaller ones representing aspects of nature and personality....Shadows of divinity with instructions to protect, to shroud, to nurture. One sang to me, in particular, and that invigorated me, though I did not know why the Wind was chosen to protect. Wind, invisible Wind, so flighty and immaterial.*”
> 
> — [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] Memories [^11]

Early in Roshar's history there were three major spren, the [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), and the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night").[^71] [^72] [^11] These spren predate the Shattering, and are considered to be echoes of Adonalsium, and to possess its voice.[^11] Before the arrival of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], the Wind and Stone were worshipped by the Singers.[^73] [^74] The Night, however, was feared and scorned. Due to this, it eventually abandoned Roshar, but Cultivation made the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]] with some of its remaining essence.[^60] Furthermore, the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] was made using the essence of the [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), and the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] from the essence of the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind").[^60]

Before the creation of the three Bondsmith spren known during the Era of Solitude, [Bondsmiths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") bonded to these three spren.[^60]

Eventually, these primeval spren went silent, with the Night entirely leaving the planet. This silence lasted for thousands of years, until the Wind and Stone began to move and communicate once more in the days leading up to the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|Contest of champions]] and the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows").[^75] [^60]

### Ecology

For a list of Rosharan flora and fauna, see [:Category:Rosharan lifeforms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Category:Rosharan_lifeforms "Category:Rosharan lifeforms").

The shape and form of Rosharan ecology is influenced by four major factors: low gravity, high oxygen content, highstorms, and spren. The high oxygen content and the low gravity permit the existence of megafauna comparable only to the largest [[Sandling - The Coppermind|sandlings]] on [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]], while several species of animals are capable of forming spren bonds, further augmenting their capabilities.[^76] However, the most visually notable feature of Rosharan ecology is the way vast majority of them adapted to highstorms. Multiple species of both flora and fauna have developed shells to protect themselves from extreme weather, withdrawing into them whenever any danger approaches.

Several species are noted as having body parts made of stone, such as the bark of [dalewillow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalewillow "Dalewillow") or hooves of [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadiums]].[^77] As stone doesn't grow naturally, three possibilities must be considered:

- The substance is actually stone, somehow either grown or gathered by the creatures,
- The substance is organic, but highly similar in appearance and texture to stone,
- The substance is hardened crem, deliberately cultivated by those creatures.

#### Flora

Rosharan flora had to adapt to several conditions not found on other worlds, such as the severe weather and scarcity of soil. Due to this, they have developed various idiosyncratic traits and properties indicative of Rosharan flora, such as the use of hardened crem for protection, or the ability to move out of the intense winds of highstorm. Flora in eastern Roshar is most prominent in well-protected laits, which provide defence from the strong winds and rains.

![[Roshar---The-Coppermind-3.jpg]]

by [Ben McSweeney](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ben_McSweeney "Coppermind:Artists/Ben McSweeney")

In highstorm-heavy regions, laits like this are the oases of plant life.

The largest family of plants on Roshar are the [rockbuds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rockbud "Rockbud"). They are characterized by having a thick outer shell from which they take their name, and tendrils, which retract into the shell upon detecting danger. The Rosharan grass, which acts in this way, is likely another subspecies of rockbud, albeit close enough in appearance to Shin grass to retain the name. Similar mechanism has been developed by [vinebuds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vinebud "Vinebud"), which grow from a sturdy, yet elastic stem and, rather than spreading their tendrils along the ground, waft them in the air, akin to anemones.[^78] [Rocklily](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rocklily "Rocklily") leaves are an essential component of the diet of chull larvae.[^79]

Further to the east, where the highstorms are the strongest, even such measures are not enough. There, plants grow mostly in sheltered areas, such as the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|chasms]] and [laits](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lait "Lait"), while remaining sparse in the open air.[^80] More fragile flora, such as vines and weeds, grow and flourish in such terrain, though some, like [knobweed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knobweed "Knobweed"), seem to prefer flatlands, utilizing highstorms deliberately to spread.

Contrarily, in the west where highstorms are weakened by the mountain ranges, plants are much less sensitive to stimulation and retract their leaves more slowly.

Roshar does have larger flora. Some trees, including the aforementioned [dalewillow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalewillow "Dalewillow"), have trunks made of stone while others, such as the [[Jella - The Coppermind|jella]], augment their wooden bark by accumulating crem. Like rockbuds, they withdraw their fronds into the trunks when sensing danger. There are also species such as [makam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makam "Makam") and [cobwood](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cobwood "Cobwood"), which appear to be sturdy enough to sustain highstorms without added protection.[^81] [^82]

Rosharan grass is known to move away from disruptions or vibrations, often fully retracting to protect itself.[^83] [^84] It is type of shallowly burrowed seed pod that has enough room to hold moisture and contain the coiled up leaf. They at most grow to around three feet tall. Seeds emerge from the leaf tip to either be consumed and redeposited as faeces, or carried and redesposited by the wind.[^84] Some reeds produce flowers that entice cremlings, who then feast on the flower petals. During their feasting, these cremlings rub themselves with spores, which are released elsewhere and grow into the next generation of reeds.[^85] Due to the hardness of crem, most Rosharan plants have roots that grow into cracks in the ground.[^86]

Human uses

Some species of native Rosharan flora are edible for humans. This includes a number of fruits as well as a rice-like plant called [tallew](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tallew "Tallew"), and [[Lavis - The Coppermind|lavis]], a type of rockbud that produces grain. Tallew, lavis, and longroot appear to be the major ingredients of Rosharan diet, with massive farmlands devoted to them throughout the continent.[^59] In the West, the same niche appears to be filled by [clema](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Clema "Clema").[^87]

Additionally, there exist a number of plants with known healing properties. This includes antiseptics, such as the sap of [knobweed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knobweed "Knobweed") and [fourleaf](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fourleaf "Fourleaf"), as well as [lister's oil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lister%27s_oil "Lister's oil"); treatments for specific diseases, such as [bitterleaf](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bitterleaf "Bitterleaf") for epilepsy and [bloodivy](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bloodivy "Bloodivy") for the [grindings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Grindings "Grindings"); anesthetics like [winterwort](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Winterwort "Winterwort") and [firemoss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Firemoss "Firemoss"); and other, more specific medicines, such as [denocax](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Denocax_salve "Denocax salve"), which encourages skin healing.[^88] [^89] [^90] [^83] [^13] [^91]

By contrast, several species of plant are noted to have detrimental effect on humans. Firemoss is known to be highly addictive, and [blackbane's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blackbane "Blackbane") leaves, once dried, are extremely poisonous. Eating shells of rockbuds is similarly known to be unhealthy, although the [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki") are capable of eating them without problems, likely thanks to their [[Singer - The Coppermind|singer]] ancestry.[^92] While not all plants are edible, some, like [breachtree](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Breachtree "Breachtree") and [narbin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narbin "Narbin"), are grown to make fabrics.[^58] [^93]

#### Fauna

Roshar contains a wide variety of fauna, most of which appears to be shelled. While most creatures are native to the world, some have been introduced into Roshar during human exodus from [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], among them mammals and birds.[^94]

Like Rosharan flora, the animals of Roshar have also adapted to their environment, such as the lower-than-standard gravity and higher-than-standard oxygen levels.[^9] Following from this, most Rosharan fauna is known to breathe oxygen. The harsh environment and weather placed various pressures upon Rosharaun fauna. Several species, including the largest, grow massive shells which can protect them from highstorms. Others hide in dens or, in case of small [sarpenthyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sarpenthyn "Sarpenthyn"), ooze between the cracks in the rock.[^95]

Spren symbiosis

Multiple native species, as well as at least one imported animal, have formed symbiotic relationships with [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]]. This appears to be faciliated by gemhearts, crystals that grow naturally within the bodies of those creatures.

- Larger animals on Roshar utilize bonds with [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandra "Mandra"), or luckspren, to decrease the pull of gravity on them, allowing creatures such as [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]] to exist without collapsing upon themselves. In the case of [skyeels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skyeel "Skyeel"), the same bond, coupled with smaller size, permits them to fly.
- The native sentient species, the [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singers]], bond with a variety of spren to attain a number of forms, which alter their mental state, biological functions and appearance. While bonded, the spren remain within the gemheart.
- [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]] bond with what appears to be musicspren, attaining higher intelligence and physical strength. It is, however, unclear so far whether or not they have gemhearts.[^9]
- The fish in the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]] gain various medicinal benefits through spren bonds.[^96]

Non-shelled animals

Mammals dwell mostly in Shinovar, though a few species have been successfully exported east. Those include horses, which are widely used as far east as Alethkar, and hogs, which are the primary source of non-Soulcast meat. While most mammals are domesticated, hogs, as well as minks and rats,[^97] can be found in the wild. Several species of birds are known to exist, although they are all known as "chickens" among Eastern Rosharans. This includes actual chickens as well as more colorful birds, such as parrots. In contrast to mammals, they do not seem to do well in the wild, as all examples observed so far are domesticated. Amphibians, such as the [lurg](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lurg "Lurg") can also be found, as well as wide variety of fish, including both fresh- and seawater ones. Cephalopods also exist, albeit with unusual properties -- [zatalef](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zatalef "Zatalef"), for example, is described as bat-like, implying the ability to fly.[^98] Another category is [sarpenthyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sarpenthyn "Sarpenthyn"), which appear to be similar to both mollusks and cephalopods, albeit without shells and with smaller number of arms.[^95]

Shelled animals

The largest known group of animals on Roshar are crustaceans. Those can be of various sizes, from finger-long to four-stories-high. They are all noted as having violet blood and multiple stages of life, with the initial stage being called a larva and the process of switching between stages called pupation.[^79] The creation of shells is aided by the presence of crem in the rain, which the creatures can process into minerals.

One of the largest crustacean family are [[Cremling - The Coppermind|cremlings]], a group of tiny, multi-legged scavengers preying mostly on deceased animals, although they will also feed on uncovered plants after highstorms. Cremlings come in variety of colors, with different features, numbers of legs, and behaviours.[^59] While most are individuals, some are part of the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] swarms, and as such share a sentient hivemind. The [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] cremlings are bred for particular purposes, such as imitating human body parts.[^99] [^100]

A Santhid. Roshar's greatest creatures are far larger than humans.

Other than the cremlings, Roshar also features a number of larger crustaceans that are nonetheless smaller than humans. This group includes predatory species, such as the [[Axehound - The Coppermind|axehound]], herbivores like [axehind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Axehind "Axehind"), and near-cremling sized creatures like [leggers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Legger "Legger") and [[Larkin - The Coppermind|larkin]], as well as species like crabs.[^101] The larkin in particular are unusual for their flight, as well as the ability to pull Stormlight out of other creatures and absorb it.[^102]

The largest family of animals on Roshar are known as [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshells]], which exist both on land and in the seas. All greatshells possess gemhearts and utilize them to attract [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandra "Mandra") to decrease their own weight. The land greatshells include domesticated herbivores known as [[Chull - The Coppermind|chulls]] and two large predatory species, the [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiend]] and the extinct [lanceryn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lanceryn "Lanceryn"). The marine greatshells include the [yu-nerig](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yu-nerig "Yu-nerig"), which comes on land to pupate and is known to feed on humans,[^103] and the [[Tai-na - The Coppermind|Tai-na]], the largest animals known to inhabit the cosmere. Their legs are said to be sixty feet long, while the body itself stretches hundreds of feet tall. The back of Tai-na, which sticks out above water, is an ecosystem of its own, inhabited by multiple species of plants and animals, as well as humans.[^104]

The [santhid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Santhid "Santhid"), a massive marine species with large shell and a number of tendrils, might be considered a greatshell, and houses its own ecosystem, much like the Tai-na; however, as they were considered half-mythical until very recently, it's impossible to state so with any certainty.[^69]

Domestication

A large number of domesticated creatures on Roshar are the imports from Ashyn. Many forms of birds are domesticated: some for food, others for pets. Hogs are kept for meat; minks, like birds, are pets; and horses are common riding mounts -- though the last animal can be worth as much as a village.[^105] Of the local wildlife, axehounds are often kept and bred, similar to dogs, while chulls are the primary mount for hauling cargo or for riders who cannot afford to keep a horse. Another greatshell species called a [[Gumfrem - The Coppermind|gumfrem]] is kept for its gemhearts, which can be used to [Soulcast](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulcast "Soulcast") meat.[^77]

Sapience

Due to the presence of the Shard [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], the creatures on Roshar have higher chance of developing sapience.[^106] So far, behavior indicating sapience has been observed in five species, [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]] horses, [[Tai-na - The Coppermind|Tai-na]], [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|Chasmfiends]], [santhidyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Santhid "Santhid") and [[Larkin - The Coppermind|Larkin]],all of which appear to have intelligence beyond that of regular animals and in some cases even comparable to that of singers and humans.[^107]

Separately from this, Roshar is known to have developed two independent sapient species: [[Siah Aimian - The Coppermind|Siah Aimians]] and [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singers]]. Both of them are humanoid, and capable of breeding with humans.[^108] [^92] Roshar's other sentient species are humans, who immigrated there from [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]]; the Iriali, who have since fully integrated with the population of the planet;[^109] [^110] and the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] (known also as Dysian Aimians), who are not native to the planet.[^111] [^112] [^113]

### Pre-Desolation Roshar

#### Creation and Early Inhabitants

> “ *Blue against a black sky, a globe of infinite potential. This was a new creation, I sensed—one of Adonalsium’s most recent masterworks. It sang to me its name through tones and rhythm, and I told it mine... Here in this world I found perfection, a relic of the being I had slain for his own good.*”
> 
> —Honor on Roshar [^11]

The planet of Roshar predates the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]], and was created by Adonalsium.[^114] It had likely existed for about twelve to thirteen thousand years by the time of the True Desolation.[^115] [^1] It is unknown if Roshar, and the rest of the system, was directly created by the axon, or if it was formed from preexistent primordial material. However, it is known that, from the moment of its creation, it had more oxygen then most other inhabited planets in the cosmere.[^54]

For some period of time, Roshar had no major landmasses, with the Rosharan supercontinent that defines the surface not yet existing, at least not in its modern form.[^36] This supercontinent was then grown by [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]], using fundamental mathematical laws and patterns that govern the cosmere, at an unknown point in time.[^114] [^11] [^116] [^34] Along with the planet, Adonalsium would create three primeval spren, the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), the [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), and the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night"). These spren would watch over and protect Roshar for many millennia.[^60] [^11]

At the point of creation, Roshar possessed four moons. These moons were placed in intentional, albeit unstable orbits around Roshar.[^117] Eventually, the fourth moon crashed into the surface of the planet at an unknown point in time before the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]] and the arrival of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]].[^10] The fragments buried themselves into the area that would come to be known as the Shattered Plains.[^10]

Much of the ecology of the planet existed from this time, including the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], though they were less destructive,[^118] and the [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]].[^119] [^120] Singers lived on Roshar prior to the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]].[^121] The [singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers") were the primary inhabitants of Roshar at this time and are likely the [Dawnsingers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawnsingers "Dawnsingers") spoken of in modern times, though they did not yet have access to the [Surges](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Surges "Surges") as Honor gave those to them after he arrived to Roshar.[^122] [^15] They worshipped Adonalsium [^73] and the Stone and Wind.[^73] [^74] [^123]

#### Arrival of the Shards

Over ten-thousand years before the [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]], shortly after the Shattering, [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]] discovered Roshar and settled there.[^11] Honor felt an immediate attachment to the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), feeling it call to and invigorate him.[^11] Honor granted the Singers the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Stoneshaping]], which allowed the Singers to shape and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connect]] to the [stone of Roshar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone").[^15] He would, however, place various limitations upon it, to prevent the events that happened on [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]] from uncontrolled [Microkinesis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Microkinesis "Microkinesis").[^124] Around two thousand years after their arrival, Honor and Cultivation would make the true spren.[^15]

These Shards, and the ones to come, would come to be an immense motive force on the course of history for both singers and humans. Their arrival, and subsequent influence and relations with sapient and non-sapient entities of the world, would come to shape the societal, cultural, and political arrangements in society was based. This was only intensified with their active role in the existence of the people of Roshar over the millennia.

The Singers would come to worship the Shards that inhabited the planet, though the process was slow and gradual. With some singers continuing to worship Adonalsium even several millennia after the arrival of Honor and Cultivation.[^73]

Due to a lack of love or worship by the people of Roshar, the Night eventually left the planet.[^60] This occurred some time after Honor and Cultivation's arrival on Roshar.[^11]

*Main article:* [[Expulsion - The Coppermind|Expulsion]]

> “ *They came from another world, using powers that we have been forbidden to touch. Dangerous powers, of spren and Surges. They destroyed their lands and have come to us begging* ”
> 
> —From the [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele") [^125]

Humans fled to Roshar from Ashyn, another planet in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system") and then known as Alaswha, seven-thousand one hundred years before the True Desolation.[^125] [^126] [^127] [^111] [^15] They had destroyed the planet using the Surges, though Honor would later imply it was the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshards]], after Odium, who had been contacted by the humans after Hoid told them of the [[Shards]], tricked [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] into experimenting with Surges and had convinced one city state to form an empire.[^128] [^127] [^15] [^39] Honor would back a different state and the warring would led to the destruction of the planet. As the planet burned [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]], the new leader of the empire, would make peace with [Makibak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makibak "Makibak"), the leader of Honor’s faction.[^15] [^129] Honor had spent around nine hundred years helping humanity on Ashyn, and over these centuries, many Singers would come to believe that Honor had abandoned them. These feelings of abandonment by their god left them predisposed to side with Odium when he appeared to them.[^4] [^130] [^131] Odium's arrival in the Rosharan system, and the subsequent movement of him and humanity to Roshar, is the essential cause of much of Rosharan history, and would lay the foundation of its course for the following millennia. [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] states, upon hearing the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind") and [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin's]] rendition of the [Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail "Wandersail"), that it was the notes of that song, the Pure Tones of Roshar, that originally guided the people of Ashyn to Roshar.[^132]

Along with humanity, a large variety of animals from Ashyn would come to Roshar. These include pigs, minks, rats, a variety of parrots, and domestic dogs. The minks would become wild and feral, and rats would prosper despite the small amount that made it through to Roshar. Dogs, however, would go extinct within three-hundred years, due to too small of a breeding population making it through the portal.[^94] [[Axehound - The Coppermind|Axehounds]] were eventually bred and domesticated as a substitute for dogs.[^94]

Following the arrival of humanity on Roshar, Honor and Cultivation commanded the singers to take the humans in despite the forbidden powers they had relating to [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] and [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]],[^125] [^123] and they were given [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] to live in since Shinovar could support the plants and animals they brought and this was where their portal opened by Ishar was.[^128] [^133] [^134] With the humans came [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]], who was in shock at what happened and wondered if he could have changed it.[^135] [^134]

Much like in singer societies, human societies were deeply influenced by the resident Shards of the system. The Shards acted as a fundamental driving influence on various aspects of their societal structure, shaping religion,[^136] culture, morality, institutions, rituals, and so forth. All known religions on Roshar, apart from that of the Iriali, in some way descends from the influence, direct or indirect, of the Shards.

Over time, the Singers began to side with Odium, fueled by dissatisfaction with a god they believed had abandoned them.[^4] [^130] [^131] This switching of sides would increase tensions between the two species, and, along with the natural desire for hegemonic expansion, would lay the foundation for future conflict between them.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

Within fifty years, the humans especially those under Makibak grew dissatisfied with what they were given and with how they were treated by the singers if they went east.[^74] The conflict between the invading humans and the singers living on Roshar became the [First Desolation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/First_Desolation "First Desolation").[^137] At some point during the First Desolation, [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]] attempted to kill Cultivation, but failed in his attempted. Due to this attempt, he would be cursed.[^138] [^139]

The creation of the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] by Odium led to Honor and Ishar to begin to plan the creation of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds").[^140] [^141] Ishar would make the [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connections]] between the Heralds and Honor. Each Herald swore an oath to Honor in the order that they would to be known by (Jezrien, Nale, Chanarach, etc). Hoid was offered a place, but [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]] would take it up.[^138] [^139]

Fifty years before the creation of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds"), Honor, Cultivation, and Odium entered a contract designed by Cultivation and bond by Honor. This deal stipulated how the Shards could interact, restricted the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], and trapped all three Shards to the Roshar system. This caused Roshar to adopt Odium like it had Cultivation and Honor.[^4]

### The Heraldic Epochs

#### The Desolations

*Main article:* [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]]

The [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]] were conflicts between mankind and [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]]. The first Desolation occurred when humans invaded Roshar from the land they were given.[^137] Some of the singers were infused with Odium's [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] and became the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], according to the Heralds, only around four thousand Fused exist in total.[^142] The Fused did not pass into the [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|Beyond]] and were instead reborn into the bodies of singers.

To combat this, the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") made a deal with Honor called the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]]. This deal sealed away the Fused on Braize. However, there was a loophole. The Heralds could choose to allow the Fused to return to Roshar, so they were caught on Braize and tortured. The return of Heralds and Fused and the conflict that occurred became known as the Desolations.[^143] The spren started to mimic the herald's [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]] and the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] were formed. They helped to fight and rebuild society after a Desolation.[^144]

Each Desolation returned humanity to the Stone Age.[^145] [^136] The process of sealing away the Fused is known as the Isolation.[^126] [^146] Each interregnum was a period of progress leading up to the next Desolation.[^147] Thus, human history on Roshar would be defined by a cycle of interregnum and Desolation, where humanity progresses,[^147] and where it regresses.[^145] [^136]

Time during this period was separated into several-century long Epochs, which are further separated into years.[^148] [^91] The exact method for defining when an Epoch starts or ends is currently unknown.[^149] One Desolation began to show signs of occurring in the Eighth Epoch, year 337, when a small village in [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] was attacked by the [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|Midnight Essence]].[^148]

Around six-thousand years before the Night of Sorrows, Honor and Cultivation created the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] out of the essence of the Stone, mixed with their own Investiture.[^60] The Sibling would come to play a vital role in the Desolations, acting as the mind of Urithiru and one of the three Bondsmith Spren.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

At the conclusion of a Desolation fifty years before the Last Desolation, Honor and Odium clashed above [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]] or [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] in general. Within the ground in this region lay the remains of a fourth moon made of a metal unknown to Honor. These remains would react with the shockwave caused by Honor and Odium, liquifying the ground in a pattern causing the creation of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. It was after this event that Honor made a pact with the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] to defeat Odium.[^10]

The time between each Desolation grew shorter with each subsequent Desolation. At first there were centuries between Desolations, which eventually shortened into decades, which finally became a single year between the Last Desolation and the one directly before it.[^91]

#### The False Desolation

*Main article:* [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]]

> “ *Something must be done about the remnants of Odium’s forces. The parsh, as they are now called, continue their war with zeal, even without their masters from Damnation.*”
> 
> —Drawer 30-20, first emerald [^154]

The [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]] began around two-thousand-five-hundred years after the Last Desolation,[^155] around two-thousand years prior to the Night of Sorrows, when [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] absorbed the power of the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control") [^136] and learned how to [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connect]] with the majority of the singers, giving them [[Regal - The Coppermind|forms of power]] and access to [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]].[^156] In response to her accessing of the Well of Control, the Tones of Roshar chose her, and the planet aligned with her.[^51]

The Radiants, unaware of how she did this, sought a way to end the fighting for good, as the Heralds had promised them.[^157] The fighting became particularly intense at this time, as the singers made a push towards [Feverstone Keep](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Feverstone_Keep "Feverstone Keep"), possibly as part of a plan to capture [Rall Elorim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rall_Elorim "Rall Elorim").[^158] [^159]

Working together, the scholars among the Radiants theorized that an [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] could be imprisoned like a regular [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]].[^160] In reality, this method was taught to [[Melishi - The Coppermind|Melishi]] by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] in order to fulfill a promise to Odium. Thus was devised a plan to trap Ba-Ado-Mishram and prevent her from Connecting with the singers and providing them with Voidlight.[^161] This plan would require Melishi, the only [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") of his generation,[^162] and a special prison, a perfect heliodor.[^160] [^163] Although Melishi was confident the plan would succeed in destroying the Voidbringers, [Naze-daughter-Kuzodo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Naze-daughter-Kuzodo "Naze-daughter-Kuzodo") warned of unintended side effects.[^164] Although the plan succeeded, sealing Ba-Ado-Mishram into the Spiritual Realm,[^163] Naze-daughter-Kuzodo's warning was correct.[^165] Because she had been Connected to all the singers and an extent all of Roshar, their Connection and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 4|Identity]] was torn out of them, creating slaveform or parshmen.[^166] The situation was further worsened by how the tones of Roshar and the planet itself had chosen and aligned with Ba-Ado-Mishram.[^51] In the moment when she was sealed away, the planet went still as its rhythms and tones froze. This cessation continued for three seconds.[^163]

This additionally allowed for the creation of the [deadeyes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye"), as when Mishram was released the deadeyes began to heal.[^52] After Mishram was imprisoned, Tanavast began to lose control of Honor and when [Garith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Garith "Garith") reached towards the Shard, all the Radiants were shown a vision of what they would do. This was likely the cause of the Recreance as the Radiants saw what they could do.[^163] [^51]

As Tanavast had made an alliance with Mishram millennia earlier, his betrayal of Mishram caused him to lose control of Honor leading to his demise as it was a broken oath. A part of the mind of Tanavast would live on in the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] and the Shard would not be taken up by another [[Shards|Vessel]] until, when it was briefly taken up by [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]].[^51] [^167]

At the end of the False Desolation, a group of singers known as the [Last Legion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Last_Legion "Last Legion") were allowed to leave the fight by [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]].[^168] They would eventually arrive at the ruins of [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. Seeking freedom, they fled during a highstorm and took on dullform to escape the Fused's control. In order to avoid forgetting their knowledge, they composed songs, including [Song of Listing](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Listing "Song of Listing"), [Song of Secrets](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Secrets "Song of Secrets") and [Song of Histories](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Histories "Song of Histories"). They didn't, however, know how to transform into any forms except dullform and mateform by themselves. They planned to remain living as tribes and refugees, unnoticed and forgotten, as they studied and rediscovered the forms.[^169]

#### The Recreance

*Main article:* [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Day of Recreance]]

During the lead up to the [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Day of Recreance]], many events occurred which demoralized the Knights Radiant. The [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] withdrew from [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] which forced the Knights to abandon their ancestral home.[^170] This was mostly due to Melishi’s experimentation with imprisoning spren, which caused him to withdraw from the Sibling, and the upcoming Recreance. Many Radiants however blamed this on a perceived unworthiness, including increased conflicts between the [Windrunners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunners "Windrunners") and [Skybreakers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreakers "Skybreakers"), but scholars of the time disagreed.[^171] [^172] This concern extended to potential infiltration by [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]].[^173] While this was occurring, the False Desolation was coming to a close. The presumed success in imprisoning Ba-Ado-Mishram leading to the singers having their Connection and Identity ripped out of them likely influenced the Radiants.[^166] In the midst of this turmoil, the Radiants discovered the truth about the origins of humans on Roshar. The singers had been the original inhabitants of Roshar. The humans came as refugees from their [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|homeworld]], which they had destroyed with surges. After being taken in by the singers and given [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], they invaded Roshar and started the Desolations. While Honor had comforted previous generations who learned this, this time he was dying. He raved about the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshards]] and promised the Radiants they would destroy Roshar like they had their homeworld.[^128]

As a result of these revelations, nine of the ten orders decided to abandon their oaths, and in agreement with their spren, kill them. They believed that this would prevent the destruction of Roshar Honor warned them about.[^128] At the locations they left their [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Plate]] and [Blades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), men quickly began fighting over the weapons, leading to much death and destruction.[^174] The Radiants, other than the Skybreakers, were quickly turned on and killed because of the sense of betrayal felt.[^159] Society for both spren and humans drastically changed in the aftermath. Those with Shardblades quickly consolidated their power, including using *[Arts and Majesty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Arts_and_Majesty "Arts and Majesty")* to justify women not wielding them.[^175] It is likely that this is when [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyes]] truly came to power. The genocide decimated the populations of spren societies. Most of the survivors were children forced to spend centuries without nurture or guidance. Some societies took two thousand years to fully recover.[^176]

#### The Scouring of Aimia

*Main article:* [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|Scouring of Aimia]]

What exactly occurred during the [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|Scouring of Aimia]] is unknown but it appears that the vast majority of [[Aimia - The Coppermind|Aimia]] ’s ecosystem collapsed, leading to the extinction of the [lanceryn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lanceryn "Lanceryn") and the near extinction of the [[Larkin - The Coppermind|larkin]].[^177] Most creatures there are quite rare since the scouring.[^178] The island of [Akinah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Akinah "Akinah"), however, was not destroyed and is still protected from outsiders by the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]].[^179] [Hessi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hessi "Hessi") indicates in [Mythica](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mythica "Mythica") that [Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis") may have been responsible for the scouring.[^180]

At some point prior to this event, the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]] of Change was brought to Aimia, and the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] became its guardians due to the decline in the lanceryn and larkin.[^181]

#### Corruption of Shinovar

Around a thousand years before the Night of Sorrows, circa of the Vorin calendar, Ishar let himself be corrupted by the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control").[^182] [^183] Following this, he would begin to corrupt and manipulate the leadership of Shinovar. He would, in part, use them for the purposes of finding proper replacement Heralds in order to reforge the Oathpact.[^184] [^185]

#### The Hierocracy and the War of Loss

*Main articles:* [[Hierocracy - The Coppermind|Hierocracy]] and [War of Loss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/War_of_Loss "War of Loss")

The [[Hierocracy - The Coppermind|Hierocracy]] began when the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin Church]] decided to conquer the world and run the government rather than just provide spiritual guidance.[^177] They claimed their conquering was for the good of the people. Some of the most powerful ardents claimed to have visions from the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty"), which they used to solidify their rule and obfuscate their religion. One of their main goals was to destroy knowledge of the failures of Vorinism, including [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), the Knights Radiant, and the Recreance.[^67] [^93]

They were primarily successful, as even the major libraries on Roshar are missing this information. Around five-hundred years before the Night of Sorrows,<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> before the Hierocracy could spread to the west, [[Sadees - The Coppermind|Sadees]], who would come to be known as the Sunmaker, united the princedoms of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") to drive out the Hierocracy.[^186] [^187] [^188] They fought in various large battles in what came to be known as the [War of Loss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/War_of_Loss "War of Loss").[^174] The Sunmaker successfully cast down the Hierocracy and stated the visions were a fraud.

Sadees's victory in the War of Loss affected much of eastern Roshar. Sadees declared himself king of Alethkar and began a conquest of Roshar.[^189] Although the extent of his empire is not exactly known, [[Herdaz - The Coppermind|Herdaz]] and [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") were both included. He committed genocide in Azir and killed approximately ten percent of the population.[^190] His empire quickly collapsed after his death, however, as he did not choose an heir, and his ten sons divided the kingdom into princedoms and became [[Highprince - The Coppermind|highprinces]].[^191] The effects of the Hierocracy on Vorinism can still be felt centuries later. The central leadership of the church was destroyed and split into mostly independent [Devotaries](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Devotaries "Devotaries").[^81] [^177] The ardents became slaves who could not own land or property.[^93] [^192] Their revisions to historical texts also perpetuate, and it is difficult to find information about pre-Hierocracy history or the Knights Radiant.[^67]

#### Reunification of Alethkar

In the year, House Kholin, led by [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]], led a war in order to reunify Alethkar like Sadees did before him. This conquest was successful, subjugating all ten Alethi princedoms under a singular crown for the first time since the times of the Sunmaker. It was during this war that [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] earned his Shards, including his [Blade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), [Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer_\(Shardblade\) "Oathbringer (Shardblade)").[^193] [^194] The war ended in 1145 with all ten Alethi [[Highprince - The Coppermind|Highprinces]] being unified into a single nation under Gavilar.[^195]

#### Rediscovery of the Listeners

In 1166, Dalinar and Gavilar Kholin went on a hunting trip a little south of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. While Dalinar was scouting an unmapped forest in this region, he stumbled upon [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai]], a young listener with an interest in exploring.[^196] [^93] [^197] She then led Dalinar to her family’s camp.[^198] At first the ruling council decided to let only a select few meet with the humans. The humans would take an interest to the ancient steel weapons of the listeners. Due to a translating mistake, the Alethi would come to believe that the [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]] were the listener’s gods.[^199] When the Alethi left, they gifted Eshonai’s family modern weapons and told them to claim one of the ten cities on the eastern edge of the Plains.[^199] When the humans visited again, Eshonai’s family had claimed a city. It was during this trip that Eshonai’s sister, [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]], was given a gemstone containing the [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|Voidspren]] [Ulim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulim "Ulim").[^200] Some time later Venli, with the help of Ulim, facilitated the rediscovery of warform, just in time for an attack by another listener family. After that confrontation, the listeners began to unite into one nation.[^198]

##### Assassination of Gavilar Kholin

In, during the signing celebration of the Alethi-Listener treaty, Gavilar revealed his plans to return the Fused to Roshar to Eshonai. This led her to warning the Five. Together, the Five decided that he had to be killed before he could go through with his plans. Coming to this decision, they sent [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] as an assassin, and successfully killed the king. It is also said that the Herald Nale pointed Venli in the direction of Szeth's Oathstone. [^151] [^201]

This assassination was traced back to the Listeners, as three Listeners remained to take credit for ordering the assassination,[^202] leading to [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 10|Elhokar Kholin]] and the Ten Highprinces to declare war upon them in the [Vengeance Pact](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vengeance_Pact "Vengeance Pact"), beginning the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]].

At the same time as Gavilar's assassination,[^203] [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] killed her mother, the Herald [[Chanarach - The Coppermind|Chanarach]].[^204] Chanarach was sent to Braize, and was thus once more subjected to torture by the Fused.[^152]

#### The Eighties War

Meanwhile, in the Makabakam region, Emul and Tukar began to engage in a protracted war over ownership of the Dawncity of [[Sesemalex Dar - The Coppermind|Sesemalex Dar]]. The Emuli had the city situated as their capital, and claimed it was their ancestral homeland, granted to them by the Heralds themselves.[^205] [^206] On the other side, the Tukari, led by the Herald Ishar, also claimed rightful ownership of the city for unknown reasons.[^206]

This conflict was not something new, with various wars over Sesemalex Dar occurring over the history of the two nations, with the Eighty's War only being the most recent iteration of these conflicts.[^207]

#### The War of Reckoning

Opening in 1167, the war between the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") and the [Listeners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Listeners "Listeners") started with the [[Assassination of Gavilar - The Coppermind|Assassination of Gavilar]] by [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]], who would come to be known as the Assassin in White, on the orders of the listeners who had learned that [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]] had been trying to bring about the return of the [[Fused - The Coppermind|old listener gods]].[^151] [^201] The Alethi [[Highprince - The Coppermind|Highprinces]] would then head to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] where they fought a war of attrition against the listeners.[^208] The majority of the Alethi armies were on the Plains, including most of their [Shardbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardwielding "Shardwielding").[^209]

As the years went on the fighting devolved into contest between the Highprinces and the listeners over the gemhearts of [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]] which could be used to grow food for the armies.[^208] Throughout the war, [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]] had been manipulated by [Ulim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulim "Ulim") towards finding [[Regal - The Coppermind|forms of power]] in order to bring about the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], though this was under the guise of helping the listeners.[^210] [^100] After 7 years of war, [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] would lead his army in addition to those of [Aladar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aladar "Aladar"), [Roion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Roion "Roion"), and [Sebarial](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sebarial "Sebarial") to the center of the Plains, [Narak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narak "Narak") during the Weeping.[^211] [^212]

During the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], the listeners summon the Everstorm and the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] sent an unexpected [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] to the Plains. Lucky, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] was able to open the [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]] [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] allowing the armies to escape to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^213] [^214] The [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]] would start almost immediately after this battle.

This Desolation was started by the breaking of Chanarach, who was killed by Shallan in, and thus the release of the Fused back to Roshar for the first time in four-thousand-five-hundred years.[^152]

On, during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] was summoned by Odium and a collection of [Listeners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Listeners "Listeners"). This brought an end to the Era of Solitude, and brought forth the True Desolation.[^215] Following the True Desolation, the Alethi side of the Battle of Narak came across an oathgate leading to Urithiru, which they promptly occupied as their base of operations.[^24]

After the rediscovery of the [First Dreams](https://coppermind.net/wiki/First_Dreams "First Dreams"), by a [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylen]] war ship.[^224] At around the say time [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn]] ’s pet [[Larkin - The Coppermind|larkin]], [Chiri-Chiri](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chiri-Chiri "Chiri-Chiri"), was reaching the stage in her growth where she needed to return to the island of [Akinah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Akinah "Akinah").[^225] This prompted Rysn to lead an expedition to [[Aimia - The Coppermind|Aimia]] on her ship the [Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail_\(1174_ship\) "Wandersail (1174 ship)").[^226] The [Coalition](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs") hoped to discover if the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] on the island was operation or not.[^227] The [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] [Nikli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nikliasorm "Nikliasorm") caused various types of sabotage in an effort to stop the expedition.

#### Singer Occupation of Urithiru

In 1175, an invasion of Urithiru was proposed by the newly awakened [Raboniel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel") [^126] To facilitate the plan they set up the [Emuli offensive](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Emuli_offensive "Emuli offensive") and the Veden betrayal as a distraction.[^232] Raboniel and her forces were dropped off by Heavenly Ones several weeks away from the tower and then hiked up to the tower through the caverns that led to the basement.[^233] The singer force would dispatch of the human guards and would enter the pillar room, where Raboniel would start to corrupt the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] causing all the remaining Radiant to fall unconscious, except for [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]] who only lost their powers.[^234] [^235] [^236] Raboniel would have [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]] use his [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] to open the [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] to bring further forces.[^236]

The Sibling would inform [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] about the corruption via [spanreed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spanreed "Spanreed"). Navani would then have Battalionlord [Teofil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teofil "Teofil") lead an attack into the pillar room hoping that they could disable whatever Raboniel had done. This attack would be destroyed when [The Pursuer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lezian "Lezian") joined the fight. Teofil’s men bought Navani the time needed to activate a shield, by using one of the four nodes, around the pillar which would keep Raboniel from further corrupting the Sibling though this did cost them one node which did weaken the shield.[^237] [^238] Navani would soon after be captured by the singers.[^238]

Kaladin would evade capture while transporting an unconscious [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") by hiding in a room that he was led to by the Sibling.[^238] [^239] The next day, the Sibling would inform Navani that another node had been found. Kaladin would then be informed and he would go face Lezian and Raboniel with only a spear and half of his power. The node would end up corrupted and destroyed, which further weakened Kaladin’s powers.[^240] [^241] Due to these events, Navani would have the glove made by [Tomor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tomor "Tomor") left for Kaladin.[^242]

A few days later, the Sibling told Navani about a node in a well in the Breakaway market, which Raboniel overheard and would head towards it.[^48] Frantic, the Sibling told Kaladin that another node had been found in the Breakaway market. Kaladin would go down there and used the glove to simulate his Lashings. After fighting both Lezian and [Leshwi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leshwi "Leshwi"), Kaladin dove into the well and destroyed the node before Raboniel could corrupt it. This was a trap as Lezian placed a lid on the well. Kaladin then had to descend down the well and out of the tower into a highstorm.[^243] Dalinar would then saved Kaladin who informed him of the true situation in the tower.[^244]

After Kaladin’s fight, the human population would begin to rise up.[^245] Kaladin was heavily injured and was not healing, so [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]], [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]], and [Dabbid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dabbid "Dabbid") would save Lift who could heal Kaladin. Lift also reawakened Teft.[^246] In an attempt to free the tower, Kaladin, Teft, Lift, and Dabbid would try to reawaken the captured Radiants. Lift and Teft would go to the infirmary where the Radiants were held, while Kaladin faced Lezian.[^247] In the infirmary, Lift and Teft faced Moash who would cut through Lift’s legs and would injure Teft before using a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger to kill Teft’s spren, [Phendorana](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Phendorana "Phendorana"); the shock of which allowed Moash to kill Teft and destroy the final node.[^247] [^248] Kaladin forced the Pursuer to flee during their fight, titling him as the Defeated One. After becoming insensate from seeing a dead Teft, and the Pursuer coming to kill him, Kaladin stuck the Pursuer in his last body to a wall and used a Reverse Lashing to rip his head off.[^249] Kaladin then chases after the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] holding his father. This chase would lead to them falling towards the base of the mountain. As this was during a highstorm, Dalinar gave him more time, during which Kaladin swore the Fourth Ideal and saved his father.[^249] [^250] [^251] [^252]

During the Occupation, Navani and Raboniel worked together to discover [Anti-Voidlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Voidlight "Anti-Voidlight"), through which they were able to bring permanent death to the Fused. Following this discover, Raboniel was able to discover [Anti-Stormlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Stormlight "Anti-Stormlight"), to be used as a weapon against Spren and the Knights Radiant.[^253]

While the fight near the infirmary was happening, Navani attacks Raboniel using a raysium dagger with some Anti-Voidlight and some [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrials]] she had built during her captivity. #### Contest of Champions

The Contest of Champions between Odium and Dalinar, wherein the fate of Roshar and the Oathpact was to be decided, was planned to occur on.[^266] The deal was struck initially towards the beginning of the war, and the final terms were set around the end of the Occupation of Urithiru using a contract drafted by Hoid and Jasnah.[^267] [^266] Due to a loophole in the contract, Odium sent forces to [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir"), [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylenah]], and the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] in an attempt to capture and claim them before the Contest.[^268]

On the day of the contest, just after his return from the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]], Dalinar met Odium at the top of Urithiru as agreed. It was then that Odium revealed his champion, [Gavinor Kholin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor_Kholin "Gavinor Kholin") who Odium had swapped as [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] left the Spiritual Realm, and then prepared for what appeared to him to be twenty years.[^39] Unable to kill Gavinor, Dalinar would briefly Ascend to Honor, before breaking all oaths made by Honor, which included the one holding Odium to Roshar. This also made him lose the [[Shards|Shard]].

Following this, Odium claimed Honor, becoming [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]] and killed Dalinar, who still arguably won because by freeing Odium, he made the other Shards pay attention to him, as he once again became a threat. Furthermore, Dalinar's actions had removed Odium’s time to prepare for his conquest of the [[17th Shard|cosmere]].[^45] [^39] [^167] [^269] [^50]

#### Second Battle of Narak

Lead Up

The [Second Battle of Narak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Narak "Second Battle of Narak") began when almost a thousand [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] (accounting for nearly half of the battle capable Fused), a force of [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Skybreakers]], and at least one [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclast]] marched on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. At the same time, other forces were heading towards [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir") and [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City").[^270] [^142] This was in an attempt to capture the capital of the Shattered Plains, [Narak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narak "Narak"), so that after the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|Contest of champions]] Odium would hold the land as there was a loophole in the contract that he could exploit. The Coalition would decide to send a force of three hundred [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Radiants]] led by [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]] to the Shattered Plains to counter the Fused.[^271] [^142] Odium would assign [El](https://coppermind.net/wiki/El "El") to lead his army and had him use the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] [Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis") to bring additional forces to the battle.[^272]

The Battle

Sigzil would arrive at the Plains on the fourth of the ten days. The battle was divided across four plateaus: Narak One, which held the city of Narak; Narak Two, which held the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]]; and Naraks Three and Four, which were two adjacent plateaus. Sigzil would suggest that they defend all four plateaus so that the enemy would waste time breaking through the defenses on Naraks Three and Four before they could get to the important Naraks One and Two. This allowed them to predict how the battle would go.[^142]

### Calendar

The Vorin calendar is divided up into ten months, with ten weeks each. Both the months and the weeks are named after the Vorin numerals (which, in turn, are derived from the names of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald")), and are sometimes combined into abbreviations. Such abbreviations have the full name of the month and two suffixes, for week and day. As an example Chachel means it's the third day of the week.[^320] Interestingly, Kaladin, who gets paid once a week, refers to payday as "third pass".[^321] Presumably, this means that another way to talk about days of the week is by saying first, second, third, fourth or fifth pass.

Each week is made up of five days, with a month being made of ten weeks or fifty days, and a year on Roshar is 500 days long, ultimately defined by the time between two [[Weeping - The Coppermind|Weepings]].[^321] [^322] [^57]

Although the Rosharan year is 500 days long, these days are shorter than usual, coming in at 20 Rosharan hours, which are themselves cut down into 50 Rosharan minutes.[^2] [^323] Consequently, a Rosharan year is only 10% longer than the cosmere standard.[^28] [^9] [^26] The sun rises at the fifth hour and sets at the fifteenth. Dates are written in glyphs in order of year, month, week, and day, with each number separated by periods. For example, the third day of Ishi during the month of Ishi in the year 1173 would be written as 1173.10.10.3.[^30]

Other cultures across Roshar have different calendars, which consequently leads to differences in recorded year between nations and cultures. One culture known to make use of a different calendar are the Unkalaki. The Tukari and Emuli, and potentially the Azish in general, in which whose reckoning deems the period from Vorin year and to be in the eighties of an unclear century and millennium.[^206] [^63] [^324] Singers can also measure the passage of time by using the Rhythms. They do this intuitively by attuning to the Rhythm of Peace.[^30]

### Currency

*Main Article: [[Spheres - The Coppermind|Spheres]]*

The primary form of currency found on Roshar are spheres, pieces of gemstone encased in a sphere of glass with one side flattened to prevent rolling.[^322] The gemstones can be any of the ten [[Polestone - The Coppermind|Polestones]] and there are three denominations: chip, mark, and broam. The ten gemstones are organized into five value levels with some gem types having equivalent values.[^325] These value levels are determined by [[Soulcasting - The Coppermind|Soulcasting]] properties, so diamonds, which can create quartz, crystal, and glass, have the lowest value, and emeralds, which can create grain, have the highest.[^97] Spheres, like all gems on Roshar, have the ability to hold and leak Stormlight. Spheres with light are referred to as infused and those without are referred to as dun. Although dun spheres can be used as currency, people are suspicious of them because light is the easiest way to determine a sphere is real.[^90]

Because of their ability to hold Stormlight, spheres are commonly used on Roshar as a source of light. The light from spheres is considered to be steadier and more consistent than traditional light sources such as candles.[^88] It can be expensive, however, as spheres are also used for currency, so the use of spheres as a source of light is often a sign of wealth. Diamonds are considered the best light source because it is both the cheapest and the closest to natural light.[^326]

The denomination of spheres in eastern Roshar is weaker than in the west, with some people drilling holes in spheres to carry them on strings.[^87] Unencased gems can also be used as currency in some places, often with an upcharge for being more difficult to authenticate.[^118]

The [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] use coins made of [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshell]] carapace instead of gemstones.[^327]

Glass beads without gemstones inside are called 'Ends', and are mainly used as gambling tokens.[^328]

### Food and Drink

A wide variety of alcoholic drinks can be found on Roshar.

#### Wines

![[Roshar---The-Coppermind-11.jpg]]

by [Miranda Meeks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Miranda_Meeks "Coppermind:Artists/Miranda Meeks")

Types of Rosharan wine

On Roshar the term Wine refers to a number of alcoholic drinks, only a small subset of which are made of fermented fruit. The use of the term "wine" is the result of linguistic shift, much in the same way that all birds became [[Chicken - The Coppermind|chickens]] and all small crustaceans became [[Cremling - The Coppermind|cremlings]].[^329] The alcohols that fall under the term "wine" are categorized by strength and color, with different colors denoting different alcohol content. Additionally, the stronger "wines" are actually distilled spirits.[^330]

In order from weakest to strongest, those are:

- **Pink**, which typically has a distinct floral taste. It's said to assist alertness. This wine is actually a juice and has no alcoholic content.[^330]
- **Orange**, fruity and with notes of ginger, although apparently it's not made of fruit.
- **Yellow**, which has a deep, "bold" taste while being weak enough to drink casually.
- **Auburn**, spicy and with a earthy aroma - this could be closest to red wine, as it's made of fermented fruit.
- **Red**, said to be "flavourful with a pleasant burn".
- **Sapphire**, with a taste of nuts and honey, made of [[Lavis - The Coppermind|lavis]] grain. It's usually infused, and the closest equivalent to whiskey.
- **Blue**, tasting of berry and lemon.
- **Violet**, the strongest, typically with an aroma of sandalwood.[^331]

Within those colors, a number of different wines exist - for example, among the sapphire varieties are **kimik**, **vari** and **Veden saph**.[^332] While generally the wine colors are standardized, they gain their color by intentional colored infusions, and are occasionally made without them.

#### Other Beverages

![[Roshar---The-Coppermind-12.jpg]]

by [Yen Shu Liao](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Yen_Shu_Liao "Coppermind:Artists/Yen Shu Liao")

Rosharan drinks

Apart from wines, Roshar has a number of other alcoholic drinks. Among those is beer, of which the most popular variety appears to be [mudbeer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mudbeer "Mudbeer"), often drunk to warm up.[^58] Another drink is a liquor called [honu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honu "Honu"), which comes in several varieties that differ in strength.[^333] Both beer and honu are made from [[Lavis - The Coppermind|lavis]] grain.[^221]

The [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki") people have a number of their own alcoholic beverages. This includes a brownish drink called [shiki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shiki "Shiki"), which has to be chilled overnight, and the infamous Horneater lager, which is far stronger than regular beers, with one bartender claiming it melts their cups. Strongest among the Horneater beverages is the **Horneater white**. A clear, distilled moonshine, it is the strongest known alcohol on Roshar (with the possible exception of some violet wines). It is known to strip paint, and can apparently be deadly to non-Unkalaki.[^334] [^332] It is comparable to [Everclear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everclear).[^330]

Other, unspecified alcohols drank across Roshar include a variety of ciders, juices and lagers.[^331]

#### Drinking Culture

[[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] parties typically involve wine of the weakest colors, watered down to prevent the participants from getting drunk too quickly.[^332] Sapphire appears to be the strongest color imbibed casually, and the phrase "to be in (someone)'s violets" refers to a person being heavily, perhaps dangerously drunk.

Beer and [mudbeer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mudbeer "Mudbeer") are typical casual drinks, commonly served in taverns.[^335] Among [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki"), mudbeer is also used for duels - the winner is decided by who can drink the most without passing out.[^322]

Like most processes on Roshar, the act of drinking has its own dedicated [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]]. Winespren, or [[Spren - The Coppermind#Alespren|alespren]], appear near people who are heavily intoxicated, taking form of small bubbles clinging to nearby objects. They are considered quite rare or even nonexistent, as they require a person to be deeply drunk before they appear, which can also lead to the assumption that an alespren sighting was simply a drunken hallucination; however, both [Axies](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Axies "Axies") and [Nazh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nazh "Nazh") attest to their existence.[^336] [^331]

### Languages

The languages on Roshar can be grouped into five main language families. The [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") language family primarily contains languages spoken in the east. [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi"), [Veden](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Veden "Veden"), and [Herdazian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herdazian "Herdazian") are the most closely related, with [Thaylen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen "Thaylen") a bit more distant. [Natan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Natan "Natan"), another Vorin language, is nearly dead.[^337] [^338] This family does include some minor languages like [Bav](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bavland "Bavland"). [Kharbranthian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kharbranthian "Kharbranthian") is not considered its own language and is instead a dialect.[^339]

The next language family, [Makabaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makabaki "Makabaki"), contains around thirty languages, most of which are split off from [Azish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azish "Azish").[^339]

The [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]] language family contains Iriali, [Riran](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Riran "Riran"), [Reshi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi "Reshi"), [Selay](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Selay "Selay") and various [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]] dialects, and other surrounding languages.[^337] [^340] [^339]

The Dawnate language family are languages with roots in the [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]]. These include [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]], [[Listener - The Coppermind|Listener]], and [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki"). They share similar grammar, but the vocabulary is quite different.[^339]

The Aimian language family has two very different languages that get lumped together. Presumably the two languages are related to the [[Siah Aimian - The Coppermind|Siah]] and [Dysian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dysian_Aimian "Dysian Aimian") Aimians.[^339]

Spren in Shadesmar speak their own languages, of which we have little information on.[^339]

Rosharan phrases and curses often have distinct connotations specific to Roshar, such as mentioning Stormfather or storms in some way. [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") and other [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") cultures are also likely to use [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty") or [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds") in their phrases, while [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singer]] / [[Listener - The Coppermind|Listener]] phrases would often use rhythms or tones.

#### Writing

There are at least five Rosharan writing systems, the Alethi [[Women's script - The Coppermind|Women's script]], [Thaylen script](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_script "Thaylen script"), the [[Glyphs - The Coppermind|Glyphs]], the [Azish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") script and the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] warrior script.[^341] [^197] [^326] [^342] [^343]

Vorinism teaches that it is unnatural and not masculine for men to read and write in the Alethi script.[^97]

Although men being able to read Glyphs is permitted, people have a heavy prejudice towards [Stormwardens](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormwarden "Stormwarden"), who have adapted glyphs into a full writing system, although this prejudice is largely due to the Vorin teachings about those who claim to see the future.[^31] [^344]

[[Glyphs - The Coppermind|Glyphs]] are largely logographic, meaning writing symbols or pictures to convey ideas, rather than an alphabet like The Women's script.[^341]

Thaylen is an abjad, meaning it has little to no written vowels, instead, vowels are inferred. An example of this is the name of the slaver [[Tvlakv - The Coppermind|Tvlakv]].[^345]

Thaylen is typically written vertically, but is sometimes written left to right [^345]

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] describes the Azish writing system as looking like the tracks cremlings make.

### Singer Society

Though once the planet's dominant species, in the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] have been subdued by humans. The majority of their species are [parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen"), singers whose [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 4|Identity]] and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]] have been stripped from them by the capture of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]], rendering them mostly docile and wordlessly obedient.[^346] [^156] The singer culture of old has only been preserved by the [Last Legion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Last_Legion "Last Legion"), or listeners, on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] - the last enclave of non- [parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen") singers on Roshar - and the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], spirits of long-dead singers, on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]].

Among the listeners, the Fused are considered gods - however, they are highly dangerous to interact with, and the listeners fled to what became the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] to escape their influence. In the present day, but prior to the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], they were ruled by the Five, a council composed of representatives of each form the listeners could assume. History and knowledge are passed down among them mostly in form of songs, set to the [Rhythms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rhythm "Rhythm") that all singers can attune to.[^169]

Following the Everstorm, the previously lost Identity and Connection were restored to the parshmen, returning them to full sentience.[^77] The listeners, on the other hand, were all but decimated, first by the storm and then by becoming possessed by the Fused, with only a small group escaping west.[^347] The current singer culture, such as it is, is mostly based around following the word of the Fused like gospel; however, the former parshmen retain some of the culture of their states of origin - for example, in [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") they attempted to sue the government for backpay, while in [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylenah]], they took to the sea.[^221] [^348]

### Spren Society

Within [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), the sentient [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] have their own countries.[^69] Among those is a [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") state, with its capital in the city of [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity") and a number of strongholds, as well as a [Highspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highspren "Highspren") country, and a city-state of the [Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic").[^349] [^350] [^67] There are also free cities, such as [Celebrant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Celebrant "Celebrant"), where various spren intermingle. This includes [cultivationspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cultivationspren "Cultivationspren"), which, though ruled by a council called the [Ring](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ring "Ring"), do not have holdings of their own.[^349] [^207] [^351] However, even the "ethnic" spren states do not appear to be strictly isolationist - for example, honorspren military ships are partially crewed by [[Spren - The Coppermind#Mistspren|mistspren]].[^352]

As the inversion of the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] and its supercontinent, Shadesmar is mostly composed of the sea of beads, with a number of narrow peninsulas and islands corresponding to Physical rivers and lakes.[^353] As such, travel is done mostly by boat. Merchant and military ships, pulled by teams of harnessed [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandra "Mandra"), traverse the vast spaces between the cities, ferrying goods, news and travellers.[^354] Trade with lands outside of Roshar isn't unusual - in Celebrant, one may buy canned food (presumably from [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]] or some [worldhopper](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopper "Worldhopper") settlement) and [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthian]] artwork. The price of various items is largely based on their permanence in the Cognitive Realm, with the most persistent objects selling for thousands of broams.[^349]

The relationship between various spren kinds appears to have its own share of political turmoil. Several years prior to the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], the honorspren tried and failed to conquer the rest of Shadesmar, making the other spren unfriendly towards them. By contrast, Cryptics are generally understood to be unwilling to expand beyond their city; however, they are also widely considered to be uncomfortable company.[^176]

After the events of the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), spren have mixed opinions of humans. [Reachers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reachers "Reachers"), [inkspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Inkspren "Inkspren"), and older honorspren are generally opposed to any attempt to restore contact, while cultivationspren, Cryptics, and younger honorspren are supportive of new [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bonds]], albeit cautiously so.[^258]

### Secret Organizations

There are many secret organizations on Roshar, potentially nine.[^355] These include worldhopping organizations with a strong presence on Roshar, such as the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]],[^14] the [[Ire - The Coppermind|Ire]],[^356] the [[Seventeenth Shard - The Coppermind|Seventeenth Shard]],[^340] [^357] and the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]];[^112] [^358] as well as strictly Rosharan organizations such as the [Skybreakers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreakers "Skybreakers"),[^328] the [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]],[^328] the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)"),[^359] and the [Envisagers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Envisagers "Envisagers").[^33]

The Ire are a [Selish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Selish "Selish") group with unknown motivations, although they sell things on Roshar.[^356] The Ghostbloods are a group led by [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar") who are trying to establish an Investiture monopoly by using the [Oathgates](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathgates "Oathgates") and Stormlight.[^14] The Seventeeth Shard is an anti-interventionist worldhopping organization.[^340] [^357] Meanwhile, the Sleepless on Roshar were focused on protecting the Dawnshard underneath Akinah, until it was taken up by [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn]] who they now protect.[^360] [^181]

As for the Rosharan organizations, most of them have goals that in some way relate to [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] and/or the True Desolation. For example, the Sons of Honor want to start the True Desolation to bring back the Heralds, while their splinter group the Envisagers want to bring back Surgebinders.[^33] [^361] [^328] Counter to that, the Skybreakers want to kill the Radiants in order to stop the True Desolation.[^328] The Diagram focuses on saving people from what they see as the inevitable True Desolation.[^359]

### Prejudices

Rosharans would be distrustful of albinos, partially due to their reddish eyes.[^362]

### Moons

For most of its existence, Roshar has possessed three natural satellites. The first and smallest moon is violet **Salas**. The middle moon is **Nomon**, which is a bright pale blue. The final moon is **Mishim**, which is smaller than Nomon and green.

Generally, the moons' colors echo Roshar's [[Shards]], in order: [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]]. The Shin refer to the moons as the Three Sisters, and call each moon by its order, with Salas being the First Sister, Nomon the Second Sister and Mishim the Third Sister, while in [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]], Nomon is considered to be male.[^363] The [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] call Nomon Honor's Moon.[^233]

All three moons rise and fall in the same order every night and have highly unstable orbits on an astronomical timescale. They complete two orbits per day.[^364] These moons are relatively small.[^148] [^3] They orbit relatively close to the surface, and during the night they come closer than their geosynchronous orbit altitude.[^365] [^366] Aside from the darkness of the seventeenth hour (sometimes known as the **hateful hour**), one moon is always visible in the night sky.[^367]

Prior to Honor's arrival in the system, the planet also had a fourth moon, which died and fell. Fragments of this moon are buried underneath the land that would become the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. These fragments hide their surroundings from even the sight of a Shard, and are made of something greater than [[Aluminum - The Coppermind|aluminum]]. When Honor and Odium clashed over [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]], the fragments reacted to their discordant tones and liquefied the surrounding land in a symmetrical pattern, forming the Shattered Plains.[^46] [^10] The [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] are aware of the fourth moon and its fragments, and appear to have used their ability to conceal their surroundings to hide from the Shards of Roshar.[^368] [^10] Even the Shards do not fully understand the nature of the fourth moon.[^303] [^10]

### Stars and Constellations

Other notable features of Rosharan sky are [Taln's Scar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taln%27s_Scar "Taln's Scar") and [Reya's Tear](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tear "Tear"). The Scar is a great swath of red stars that stand out against the white ones.[^83] It is important in Rosharan astrology, particularly its position on one's seventh birthday.[^81] The [Tear](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tear "Tear") is a particularly bright star, named after a single tear shed by a woman named [Reya](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reya "Reya") from Rosharan mythology.[^55]

### Other Planets

There are two other telluric planets in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system"): [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] and [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]].[^7] Both of them are currently inhabited. The remaining celestial bodies in the system are ten gas giants, named after Vorin numerals, which are separated from the telluric planets by an asteroid belt.[^7]

## Trivia

- Although the word "dozen" is often used in the [The Stormlight Archive](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Stormlight_Archive "The Stormlight Archive"), Rosharans actually would say tens instead and Brandon just translated "tens" into "dozens" to make the book easier to read.[^369]
- At some point, the Rosharan supercontinent was originally planned to be shaped like a cymatic pattern, but this idea was scrapped in favor of a Julia Set. In [The Way of Kings Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_Prime "The Way of Kings Prime"), it was laid out in a three-pronged shape.[^40]
- There is no chocolate on Roshar.[^370]
- Roshar is of interest to several powers in the cosmere, as it is a world where pure Investiture is incredibly easy to come by.
- Due to the influence of [Kabbalah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kabbalah "wikipedia:Kabbalah") on Vorin Theology and the Surgebinding chart,[^371] [^372] [^373] [^374] it is likely the importance of ten on Roshar is based on the ten [Sefirot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sefirot "wikipedia:Sefirot") of the [Tree of life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tree_of_life_\(Kabbalah\) "wikipedia:Tree of life (Kabbalah)").
