## Everstorm

> “ *They call for a storm. My opposite. Deadly.* ”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] to [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] during the Battle of Narak.[^1]

The **Everstorm** (Alethi: romanization: *Kalazeras*, lit. *Eternal Storm*) [^2] is a powerful weather pattern on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] similar to a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]], but one that circles the world in the opposite direction. It was summoned by [[Regal - The Coppermind|stormform]] [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]] at the end of the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]].[^3] The period in which it passes over a given area is sometimes referred to as the **Evernight**.[^4]

Following the birth of Retribution, the Everstorm was expanded to cover all of Roshar, and to continue without abating. This event is known as the **[Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows")**. Due to the storm, the planet experienced constant rainfall and lightning, and light from the sun was blocked out, leaving the planet in an eternal night-like state.[^5] The only places free from the storm are [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") and [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], as per Dalinar's agreements with Odium. However, Azir was left within the storm for a period of around a month before it was freed.[^6]

## Description

Where regular [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]] are commanded by the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] and carry with them [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], the Everstorm is of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], and later of [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]]. It travels from west to east, opposite the direction of traditional highstorms.[^3] The Everstorm's clouds are black, and it strikes with red lightning. It lacks a stormwall at the front, and carries neither debris nor [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem"), only billowing smoke and darkness.[^7] It rains far less than the highstorm, and it often comes in short bursts.[^8] [^9] Much like the highstorm, the Everstorm doesn't reach high enough to threaten [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], however, though protected, the storm’s passing is accompanied by a wave of unnatural cold.[^10]

The inside of the Everstorm is completely dark, filled with ash carried by violent winds.[^7] This being said, even within a single passing, the conditions within vary greatly. Sometimes, there is no wind, only lightning; other times, it rains, and yet other, burning embers fall from the skies instead.[^11] Where the Everstorm's lightning hits appears to be under either Odium's or some other entity's control, as it's capable of targeting individual buildings.[^12] The Everstorm is capable of building power while stationary.[^13]

Just what commands the Everstorm is uncertain. [[Pattern - The Coppermind|Pattern]] suggests it's a spren of some kind serving Odium.[^14] Odium himself can exercise a level of control, stopping the storm at will.[^15] [^16] Whether there is some separate entity commanding the Everstorm is left ambiguous, and if such a spren exists, it has yet to make itself known.[^17]

### Frequency

The Everstorm travels at roughly 120 miles per hour,[^18] about a third the speed of a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|Highstorm]]. It takes roughly nine days to circle the entire globe, three of which it spends crossing the Rosharan continent.[^19] [^20] [^21] Its time of arrival does fluctuate slightly, and precise predictions can be inaccurate. Fortunately, its high visibility and lower speed give the people of Roshar plenty of time to seek shelter.[^22] It is not yet clear whether the Everstorm's frequency is affected by the time of the year or other variables.

Odium is capable of altering the Everstorm's speed as he desires.[^23] [^16] He can accelerate it, pushing it to go around the planet in as little as five days. He can also bring it to a full stop and hold it stationary for hours or even days.[^15] [^24] The Everstorm was stopped like this during the Battle of Thaylen Field, and it eventually dissipated, but later returned to round the planet in its usual pattern.[^25] [^26] The Everstorm was also stopped during the Second Battle of Narak for a period of roughly six days.[^24]

Following the formation of Retribution, on, the Everstorm was expanded to cover the entire planet simultaneously. Furthermore, it was made eternal, therefore keeping the world within an eternal, global-scale storm.[^5]

## Influence and Magic

The Everstorm is a paramount threat to [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Rosharans]], mainly because of its destructive power and direction of movement. All structures and houses on the continent are built in a way that protects them from the westward-blowing [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], while allowing for little protection on the leeward side -- the side the Everstorm now strikes from. This makes areas once thought safe, such as [laits](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lait "Lait") and mountainsides, utterly defenseless.[^27] The consequences of this are readily apparent after just a single passing of the Everstorm, with once-wondrous cities like [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") ravaged and turned to rubble.[^12] The advent of the Everstorm also threw off the normal highstorm schedule by about four months.[^18]

When the Everstorm and the highstorm clash the effects are even more destructive, becoming more and more powerful until they pass by and return to normal.[^28] Where the two meet, entire swathes of land can be destroyed, with whole plateaus of rock launched high into the sky.[^29] To be on the ground when the two storms meet is a death sentence.[^10] Fortunately for Rosharans, after the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]] the two storms, either accidentally or by the Stormfather's design, never passed each other over populated areas.[^30]

The Everstorm has numerous magical effects. It attracts [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|stormspren]] and, presumably, other [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]].[^31] It also carries the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], souls of long-dead [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] who serve Odium in numerous lives.[^32] [^33] Through an Everstorm, the Fused can possess any singers who open themselves up to them, killing the original mind and replacing it with themselves while retaining abilities and use of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of the Fused.[^7] In the previous [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], the Fused would be sent back to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] upon their bodies' death and return by an unknown means; during the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] they return to Braize and instead return via the Everstorm, allowing them to be reborn as many times as there are singer bodies available for them.[^32] [^34]

Finally, the Everstorm releases any [[Singer - The Coppermind|parshmen]] who come into contact with it from [[Singer - The Coppermind|slaveform]], restoring their [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] and [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]].[^35] [^33] Such reawakened singers take on a non-slave [[Singer - The Coppermind#Forms|form]]. The seemingly random forms they take on are ordinary ones rather than Odium-fueled [[Regal - The Coppermind|Regal]] ones.[^36]

## History

> “ *The Everstorm. It is a new thing, but old of design.* ”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] [^3]

### Predictions

The Everstorm did not exist prior to the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], although the underlying mechanisms were already in place.[^3] Its arrival was, however, predicted multiple times by the end of the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], both in numerous [Death Rattles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") and by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] in [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions"). On most occasions, it was listed alongside with the True Desolation and the Night of Sorrows.[^37] [^38] [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] likewise had numerous visions about the Everstorm's arrival, though without its name, complete with a precise countdown to its coming.[^39]

The storm first took shape in [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") centuries before its arrival in the Physical Realm, and its appearance was partly what drove some spren to begin searching out bonds to refound the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^40] [^41]

### Summoning

The real possibility of summoning a storm was first put forth by [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]] when researching [[Regal - The Coppermind|stormform]].[^42] After assuming the form, Eshonai confirmed it as a possibility, although she either believed or claimed to believe that what she suggested was an ordinary highstorm. Without consulting with the ruling [[Listener - The Coppermind|Council of Five]], she planned for every able-bodied listener to take on the stormform and call the Everstorm into existence.[^43]

As Alethi forces approached [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Narak]], the listeners awaited them and began a song that would summon the Everstorm. [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]] warned [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] that the song mustn't be allowed to finish under any circumstances, leading Dalinar to order [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] to stop it.[^31] Adolin and his forces launched an attack on the singing listeners, and although they managed to slaughter the singers and defeat Eshonai, they were too late.[^1]

The resulting Everstorm arrived from the west and clashed with the highstorm over Narak, leading to widespread destruction. Although Shallan managed to transport most of the Alethi units to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], sparing them from the worst of the clash, those who did not make it onto the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] platform in time were killed.[^29] [^44]

### True Desolation

The Everstorm then became a new regular fixture of Roshar's weather system, coming in regular nine-day intervals.[^20] [^21] In its first passing, it devastated numerous cities in its wake, with particular focus on [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City"), which Odium was preparing for an upcoming battle.[^11] It also restored [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] and [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] to the [[Singer - The Coppermind|parshmen]], ending [[Singer - The Coppermind|slaveform]].[^35] In subsequent passings, the Everstorm allowed the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] to enter the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]].[^35] [^7]

Odium brought the Everstorm with him to the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], using it to bring his fleet closer to the Thaylen shore before stopping it just outside of the city.[^15] Following the battle, the Everstorm dissipated, although it later returned back to its regular cycle.[^25] [^26]

### The Night of Sorrows

On, Odium and Honor were fused into [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]], and the Everstorm was extended over all of Roshar, transitioning from a local to a global phenomenon. This left the planet under a ceaseless rainfall, and blocked out the sun's light, leaving its inhabitants under an eternal night. This event is known as the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows").[^5] Some time after the Contest of Champions, the Everstorm let up in [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir"), moving exactly to the borders of the country and allowing light to shine on the country once again. This was due to it's capital city [Azimir's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir") status as the only place other than [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] the Coalition was able to keep because to the actions of the [Unoathed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unoathed "Unoathed").[^45] [^6]
