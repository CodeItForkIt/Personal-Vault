## Oathgate

> “ *Within a heartbeat, Alezarv was there, crossing a distance that would have taken more than four months to travel by foot.* ”
> 
> — *[Among the Darkeyed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Among_the_Darkeyed "Among the Darkeyed")*, page 102 [^1]

The **Oathgates** are structures on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that allow people to instantaneously teleport from one location to another. There are twenty in total, with ten located in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], while the other ten sit in various cities across the continent.

## Functionality

Oathgates are [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrials]] that manipulate the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surge]] of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Transportation]]. Together, they form a network of gateways with [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] at its center, allowing for near-instantaneous travel between all corners of the world, and letting people unable to fly access to the tower-city itself. They can only be operated by someone wielding a living [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), such as a [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knight Radiant]],[^2] or with an [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]].[^3] They require [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] to work, although they can be made to use [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]] through methods known to [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium's]] forces.[^4] [^5]

Each Oathgate can hold a small amount of Stormlight in its ten gemstones, enough to transport a single person. Transporting larger groups usually expends 3 marks worth of infused gems for each additional person transported; however,[^6] if the Stormlight in the Oathgate’s gemstones has already been depleted, it requires an additional 50 marks to activate. Notably, Radiants of higher Ideals require less Stormlight to operate these fabrials; a Radiant of the Fourth Ideal needs only 2 marks per person (plus 35 to activate the gate), and a Radiant of the Fifth Ideal needs only 1 mark per person (plus 20 to activate the gate).[^7] A Radiant can use their Shardblade to travel to the Cognitive Realm instead of another Oathgate.[^8] Oathgates function by sending a group or individual between Oathgates through the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]].[^9] [^10]

### Structure and Operation

![[Oathgate---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

The Oathgate platform (purple) can be seen in the bottom left of the city, with a control chamber (yellow) rising from its center.

Oathgates can be found in many of the oldest cities on Roshar, such as [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") and [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]].[^11] Each of them is composed of a large, perfectly circular platform, with a round control building in its center.[^12] The entire Oathgate is typically at a higher altitude than the rest of the city, with a wide ramp leading up to it.[^13] [^14] In some places, the ramp is also accompanied by a staircase, although that could be a later addition to the structure.[^15]

### Control Building

The inside of the control building is richly decorated in murals both on the walls and the floor. The latter is divided into eleven sections, with the ten major ones each dedicated to one of the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]]. Between the first and tenth segments is a smaller, narrow section representing [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^16] The wall above has ten gemstone lamps hanging from it, with each lamp being enclosed in a metal lattice. Once they are infused, the floor begins to glow, and swirls of Stormlight start rising above the platform outside.[^2] [^17]

Along with the lamps, on the wall there is a plate of metal, shaped like a ten-pointed star, with a slot in its center. When a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") is inserted, the slot reshapes to fit its particular form.[^16] This occurs regardless of whether the Blade is living or dead, but only a living Blade or [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] can activate the keyhole, at which point is produces a low, vibrating sound.[^18] [^3] Once the Blade is in the keyhole, the keyhole can be moved along with the entire inner wall to align it with one of the eleven floor sections. There, the Radiant removes the Shardblade from the keyhole. With this, the contents of the control chamber, as well as anything on top of the platform, is swapped with the control chamber and the platform of the Oathgate in the desired location.[^18] The control building can also be swapped by itself, without transporting the platform, taking considerably less Stormlight than moving the entire platform.[^19] Transporting to the Cognitive Realm only allows for what is within the control building, and ignores what is in the outside platform.[^20]

The existence of eleven lamps in each Oathgate control chamber indicates that it might be possible to transport people directly between any two platforms. However, at the present each Oathgate is tied only to one other, connecting each of the ten ancient cities to Urithiru.[^19]

### Locking the Oathgate

Each Oathgate can be locked from either side.[^21] [^22]

All but the Stormseat Oathgate have been locked up by the ancient Knights Radiant when they abandoned the tower city.[^23] As such, over the millennia since the [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Recreance]], their purpose has been forgotten. Many of the Oathgate platforms are covered in buildings, such as the Grand Market in [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir"), or the [Kholinar Palace](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar_Palace "Kholinar Palace") in Kholinar.[^24] [^23] Others, like the one in [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City"), have been transformed into gardens and other religious or ceremonial structures.[^25]

### Shadesmar

In [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), an Oathgate is represented by a flat platform of a material akin to white marble, the size of the control building. When possible -- for example, if the Oathgate is close to the sea shore in the Physical Realm -- the Oathgate is connected with the land by a bridge of matching color.[^26] The platform itself is watched over by a pair of enormous spren, both the size of buildings, each wearing a set of armor and holding a spear. One of the spren is white, while the other is shimmering black.[^27]

Those two spren enable the Oathgate to function properly, and control who can travel, and where.[^27] They can be Enlightened by [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]].[^28] This does not impact their ability to work with spren of the uncorrupted Oathgates.[^29]

In the past, the Oathgates have been capable of transporting people in and out of Shadesmar; however, at some point before their slumbering, the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] forbade the Oathgate spren from performing this service.[^27] [^30] As such, this function of the Oathgate was inaccessible. This being said, the spren corrupted by Sja-anat are not bound by this, and can transport people into Shadesmar, and can bring them back into the Physical Realm.[^28] [^31] Some Oathgate spren have since accepted the authority of the Stormfather in this matter, and once again allow travel to Shadesmar.[^30] This includes some of the spren at Urithiru, though others do not accept that the Stormfather has the authority to release them from the orders of the Sibling.

## Oathgate Locations

### Urithiru

The Oathgate platforms of Urithiru are arranged in a circle on the grand plateau in front of the tower.[^32] [^33] Each Oathgate is connected to a location in each of the ten ancient [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]].[^11] The platforms are 10 feet high, with ramps leading up to them.[^34] The small control buildings in the center of each platform have mosaics on the floor showing Dawnchant glyphs.[^19] In each control room, the keyhole is at the center of a ten-pointed star on a metal plate.[^19] At the present, the plateau in front of the Oathgates are used for storing lumber and training soldiers.[^32] [^35]

In the Cognitive Realm, the platforms are connected by ramps and glowing walkways, which also lead to the main pillar.[^36]

### Shattered Plains

The [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] Oathgate is located in the city of [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Narak]], or Stormseat, in the very center of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. Back when the city was in its prime, the Oathgate was located on a round plaza in its south-western part.[^37] In the present, the platform forms its own plateau adjacent to Narak.[^38]

This Oathgate is the first to have been rediscovered and reactivated in the present day. It's found by [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], and used to transport the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") troops away from the field of battle before they can be killed in the confrontation between the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] and a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]].[^18] Later, it becomes Urithiru's main connection to the outside world, with goods being brought through it from the Plains war camps into the tower-city.[^17]

### Alethkar

![[Oathgate---The-Coppermind-3.png]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

Plans of [Kholinar Palace](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar_Palace "Kholinar Palace"), with the Oathgate on the left

The [[Alethela - The Coppermind|Alethela]] Oathgate is located in the city of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), the capital of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"). It's attached to the [Kholinar Palace](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar_Palace "Kholinar Palace") by a long covered bridge called the Sunwalk, and is technically part of the palatial complex.[^13] The Oathgate platform serves as the home of the palace [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|ardentia]], and is thus covered by numerous buildings.[^28] It's called the Monastery Dais, with the control chamber itself known as the Circle of Memories. The local ardents believe that the ten lamps in the Circle represent the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]], while the keyhole represents the need for the priesthood to ignore borders and boundaries.[^39]

During the [Siege of Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Siege_of_Kholinar "Siege of Kholinar"), the platform becomes home to [Ashertmarn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn"), and as such is the location of the endless Revel organized by the [Cult of Moments](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cult_of_Moments "Cult of Moments").[^40] At the same time, another [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]], [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], corrupts its spren. When [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]], [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and [[Vivenna - The Coppermind|Azure]] attempt to use the Oathgate, they are dumped into Shadesmar instead.[^28] While the Oathgate is later locked from Urithiru side after the news of Kholinar's fall reaches the tower,[^22] [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]] eventually unlocks it, letting the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] attack Urithiru thorugh it.[^29] [^41]

### Thaylenah

![[Oathgate---The-Coppermind-4.jpg]]

by [Marie Seeberger](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Marie_Seeberger "Coppermind:Artists/Marie Seeberger")

[Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") Oathgate as seen from [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar")

The [[Thalath - The Coppermind|Thalath]] Oathgate is located in the Thaylen capital, [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City"). It sits at the city's highest and east-most point, near the cliffs falling down into the sea. Standing high above the other buildings, it provides an excellent view of the entire city.[^19] A solitary path connects it to the Royal Ward. Notably, it's the only known Oathgate so far not to be located within the original borders of the ancient city it's connected to.[^14] In Shadesmar, it's connected to land by a long bridge.[^26]

The Oathgate's original purpose remained in the Thaylen cultural memory, with stories of a "portal of worlds" dazzling children to this day.[^42] However, the platform itself has been transformed into a sculpture garden. [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] fly to Thaylen City to unlock it, while [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]] does the same from Urithiru side, under [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani's]] supervision.[^19] From then on out, it's frequently used to transport both people and cargo.[^43] It's particularly crucial in the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], as Kaladin and his group fight there in an attempt to return from Shadesmar, and the gate itself is used to transport reinforcements to help repel the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]].[^27] [^44]

### Azir

![[Oathgate---The-Coppermind-5.jpg]]

by [Ben McSweeney](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ben_McSweeney "Coppermind:Artists/Ben McSweeney")

Azir's Oathgate platform

The [[Makabakam - The Coppermind|Makabakam]] Oathgate is located in the city of [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir") in [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir"). In the present day, it has been covered up by a bronze dome, with the interior serving as an indoor shopping district, known as the Grand Market. The control building itself is treated as a religious monument of some kind.[^21]

Once the Azish learn that the structure is an Oathgate, they close down the grand market and build an extensive wooden structure within, intended to funnel anyone coming out of the control chamber down a single, highly defensible path.[^21] Although they initially refuse to open the gate out of fear of the Blackthorn, they eventually allow [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]] to do so to let Dalinar visit their city.[^42] [^21] Dalinar intends to inform them that the entire platform can be swapped, rendering their defences meaningless, although it's unknown whether he ended up passing that information along.[^21]

### Jah Keved

The [[Valhav - The Coppermind|Valhav]] Oathgate is located in the city of [Vedenar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vedenar "Vedenar"), the capital of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"). It stands on one of the highest of the grand terraces of the city. Alongside a ramp, a set of stairs leads up to the platform.[^15]

Like the other terraces in Vedenar, the Oathgate platform has been turned into a grand garden, named the Isharest garden,[^45] in the centuries since it's been locked.[^15] After [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] decides to ally with Dalinar, [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]] unlocks the Oathgate.[^32] The garden is then cleaned up, clearing the platform for full use.[^15]

### Akinah

The [[Aimia - The Coppermind|Aimia]] Oathgate is located in the city of [Akinah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Akinah "Akinah"). It was believed to have been destroyed during the [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|Scouring of Aimia]].[^46] In actuality, it survived the Scouring, and fell under the protection of the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]], who had vowed to protect the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]] which also resided on the island.[^47] In an attempt to conceal it, a building was built on top of the Oathgate platform and crem was allowed to cover it.[^48] A fake Oathgate building was built on top, containing a rusted mechanism, a decayed mural, and lacking a slot for a Shardblade.[^48]

When accompanying the *[Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail_\(1174_ship\) "Wandersail (1174 ship)")* to Akinah, the team [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Queen Navani]] sent had the secret mission of determining the location and state of the Akinah Oathgate.[^49] On arriving in Akinah, [Lopen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lopen "Lopen") and [Rushu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rushu "Rushu") found the fake Oathgate building exactly where they expected it. However, due to a number of odd coincidences, and a warning from [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn]], they did not believe they had found the actual Oathgate. After finding a set of stairs, and cutting through the stone with a [[Soulcasting - The Coppermind|Soulcaster]], they discovered the actual Oathgate platform.[^48]

After Rysn brokered a peace agreement with the Sleepless, the *Wandersail* was allowed to leave Akinah with knowledge of the Oathgate.[^50] The Oathgate was not opened, however, since Navani wasn't certain yet if they want to expend the forces needed to secure the location.[^51]

### Iri

The [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iriali]] Oathgate is located in their capital, [Rall Elorim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rall_Elorim "Rall Elorim").[^11] As the Iri leadership allied with [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], the Oathgate is currently under their control.[^3] This Oathgate is located at the heart of Rall Elorim, inside a perfectly circular reservoir that collects water from the [Stormfalls](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormfalls "Stormfalls"). A stone bridge runs to the reservoir’s center, where a weathered temple honoring the One stands on top of the Oathgate. This temple is its control building.[^52]

### Other Oathgates

There are three other Oathgates that are not known to have been reactivated in the present day.

- **Rira**: The [[Rishir - The Coppermind|Rishir]] Oathgate is located in the capital of [Rira](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rira_\(country\) "Rira (country)"), [Kurth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kurth "Kurth").[^11] It's likely come under the control of Odium as the Iriali moved to conquer Rira -- something they've intended to do for a long time.[^53] Because the island that contains Kurth was located in the Silver Kingdom of Rishir and not Iri,[^54] the Kurth Oathgate may sometimes also be referred to as being in the [Reshi Isles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi_Isles "Reshi Isles").[^11]
- **Babatharnam**: The [[Sela Tales - The Coppermind|Sela Tales]] Oathgate sits in the city of [Panatham](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Panatham "Panatham") in [Babatharnam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Babatharnam "Babatharnam"). It has most likely been captured by the Iriali forces.[^46]
- **Shinovar**: The [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin Kak Nish]] Oathgate lies somewhere in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]].[^11] It's exact location is unknown, as there are no cities in Shinovar marked on the world map.[^55] The Map of Oathgates also does not list the city it is located within.

## History

### Creation and the Heraldic Epochs

The origin of the Oathgates is unknown, although they may have been constructed during the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]] epoch, as they link the capitals of those ancient nations.[^56] The Oathgate spren chose this transformation willingly, and were made from inkspren.[^4] [^57] Approximately fifty years before [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]], the city of Stormseat was destroyed in a clash between [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and Odium and its Oathgate was forgotten, although the mechanism remained intact.[^58] [^2] [^56]

Linking far-flung cities with one another and [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], the Oathgates were administered by members of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], whose [Blades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") served as the keys that allowed for their operation.[^59] [^2] Much trade and travel was done via the Oathgates, with the goods traded tariffed by the Radiants.[^60] [^61] Likewise, it seems as though trade with Shadesmar was conducted through the Oathgates.[^27]

### Closing of the Oathgates

As time went on, the tariffs imposed by the Knights Radiant became increasingly steep, which became a source of ire for those taxed.[^61] Later, just before Honor died, the Sibling spoke to the spren of the Oathgates, ordering them to stop allowing for Realmatic transition and only permitting Transportation between locations in the Physical Realm. Being made of Honor's Investiture, the Oathgate spren could not disobey this prohibition.[^27] [^30]

As the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] retreated from mankind and the city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] began to fail, the Radiants closed and locked the active Oathgates and abandoned the city.[^62] In the centuries since, the purpose of the structures was forgotten.[^23] Some became prime real estate for palaces and markets;[^63] [^21] others became religious monuments.[^19] During the [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|scouring of Aimia]], the Oathgate in [Akinah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Akinah "Akinah") was destroyed permanently -- or so it is believed.[^46] However, the Oathgate was instead hidden away, and a fake Oathgate platform constructed above it in an attempt to convince any visitors that the Oathgate no longer functioned. The real Oathgate is still functional, though still left locked.

### Rediscovery

![[Oathgate---The-Coppermind-6.webp]]

by [pekgna](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/pekgna "Coppermind:Artists/pekgna")

[[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] activating the [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]] Oathgate after rediscovering it during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]]

Shortly before the arrival of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] began her research into [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], and alongside it, the Oathgates, hoping they could potentially allow her to find the city.[^64] In the course of her research, she successfully identified the locations of most of them, and may have even made an attempt to activate the Kholinar Oathgate, to no avail.[^65] [^23]

Following in Jasnah's footsteps, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] successfully located the [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]] Oathgate during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]].[^16] She managed to activate it, transferring the Alethi forces safely to Urithiru.[^2] After the battle, this Oathgate was used to ferry people from the warcamps on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] to Urithiru.[^23]

### True Desolation

With the start of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], the Oathgates became central to [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin's]] overall strategy, as they allowed for quick transportation between various corners of the continent.[^66] However, many kingdoms were hesitant to allow the warlike [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") direct access into their heartland.[^42]

King [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved") and [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]] was the first to agree, ordering his Radiant, [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]], to activate the Oathgate.[^32] [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylens]] were the next to agree, and Dalinar sent [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] and [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] to open it from their side.[^67] Finally, the [Azish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azish "Azish") allowed access, after initially denying that the Oathgate was even functional.[^42] [^68]

At the same time, one of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium's]] [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]], [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], suceeded at corrupting the [[Spren - The Coppermind|Oathgate spren]] of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") to bend them to her master's will.[^28] The [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] later used this to invade Urithiru from Kholinar, as a distraction from the attack on [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City").[^69] After [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi"), assisted by [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft"), managed to push them back, they sent reinforcements to Thaylen City through its Oathgate to turn the tide of battle.[^44]

After the Sibling's reawakening, they convinced the majority of the Oathgate spren to work with the [Coalition of monarchs](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs").[^70] Following the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows") and the removal of stormlight from Roshar, the Oathgate spren sensed the end of their era and left the Oathgate platforms, rendering them nonfunctional.[^71]
