## Horneater Peaks

The **Horneater Peaks** are a mountain range in [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved") in eastern [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^1] They are the home of the [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki"), also known as the Horneater people.

## Geography and Ecology

During a dream, [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] has a vision of the peaks and describes them as "majestic", populated at their tips, with villages clustered near vents emitting steam.[^2] [^3] They provide a natural defensive barrier for the nation of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved").[^4] There is a strip of land to the east of the peaks, between them and the border of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), that is strategically important to the conflict during the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^4]

During the [[Heraldic Epochs - The Coppermind|Heraldic Epochs]], the mountains formed most of the eastern border of the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdom]] of [[Valhav - The Coppermind|Valhav]].[^5]

Rocks in the peaks are gray and darker in color than other rocks on Roshar, as the elevation of the peaks offers some protection from the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]] and the associated [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem").[^6]

### Climate

The top of each mountain is collapsed inward, forming a crater, which is filled with a lake of warm water. The heat from each lake is enough to create not only a pocket of livable land, despite the altitude. The [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki") call these bodies of water " [Oceans](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Horneater_Oceans "Horneater Oceans") " or emerald pools.[^7] [^8] The warmth of the Oceans is so great that it is often warmer on the peaks than it is on the rest of the continent.[^7] Their warmth only extends to a point, after which the mountains are covered by ice fields left by highstorms.[^7] [Lunamor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lunamor "Lunamor") refers to his homeland as a "place of extremes" due to the juxtaposition of ice and snow with heat and humidity.[^9]

In the Unkalaki settlements, the air is noticeably thinner than it is at sea level; [Rock](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rock "Rock") regularly refers to other Rosharan people as "airsick lowlanders".[^10] The Horneater Peaks function as a barrier from clouds going east to west, and due to this, the land directly west of the Peaks barely gets any rain, with almost all of it coming from highstorms.[^11]

### Flora

Plants such as grass and fruit trees that would not normally be seen at high elevation are able to grow near the Oceans.[^7] The Unkalaki are able to cultivate crops, as they are known for their distinctive food and drink (such as " [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Horneater white]] " alcohol).[^12]

### Fauna

[[Chull - The Coppermind|Chulls]] breed on the southern slopes of the peaks, and the importance of this cycle is documented by [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]].[^13] Chulls are a food source for the Unkalaki,[^14] and the name "Horneater" comes from the fact that they are able to eat the entirety of chulls and other animals, including horns and shells.[^10]

Geese can be found in the peaks; unlike other Rosharan languages, the Unkalaki language has unique words for different types of birds.[^15]

### Settlements

The tops of the peaks have an unknown number of Unkalaki villages.[^7] [^16] The [Jokasha Monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jokasha_Monastery "Jokasha Monastery") is located on the western slopes of the peaks.[^17] The Horneater Peaks also contain a single spanreed message station, although it is often more then a week’s travel from a village.[^9]

### Notable Features

The Horneater Peaks are the home of [Cultivation's Perpendicularity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cultivation%27s_Perpendicularity "Cultivation's Perpendicularity").[^7] Specifically, the lakes or "Oceans" contain the perpendicularity.[^18] The Unkalaki revere the Oceans and they have a number of unusual qualities. They give off heat, and are emerald in color.[^7] Lunamor maintains that the water is connected to the gods, and that outsiders would be killed if they swam in it.[^7] Bathing in the lakes is part of the reason that some Unkalaki are able to see hidden spren.[^7]

The perpendicularity has been the “door” into Roshar for several worldhoppers, including [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] and Vivenna.[^19]

## History

The story of how the Unkalaki came to live in the Peaks is only known through legend. The Unkalaki are known to be hybrids of [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] and humans.[^20] According to Unkalaki tradition, they became pariahs in Roshar due to their fighting prowess and had to appeal to the gods to create a new place for them to live.[^7]

Over time, the Unkalaki were reintroduced to Rosharan society; at the end of the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]] it was not uncommon to encounter a Horneater in the larger cities of Jah Keved and Alethkar, and many people in the area had partially red hair that indicated some measure of Horneater blood.[^21]

After the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], Lunamor's family traveled to find him and tell him that something was very wrong in their homeland.[^9] Not long after, Kaladin, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]], and Azure traveled to [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") and learned that ships traveling from the area of the peaks to [Celebrant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Celebrant "Celebrant") had not been arriving.[^22] They later learned that [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|Voidspren]] and the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] mysteriously arrived in the southwestern part of Shadesmar (west of the [Nexus of Imagination](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nexus_of_Imagination "Nexus of Imagination")) and traveled to the perpendicularity. The Voidspren then occupied the area, sailing warships and demanding tribute. Other ships in Shadesmar then refused to sail to the perpendicularity.[^23] In the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], [Adrotagia](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adrotagia "Adrotagia") told [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] that the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] [Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach") had settled in the peaks and that [Joshor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Joshor "Joshor") had been sent to investigate.[^4] Rock made the decision to return to the peaks about a year after the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]]; wanting to see his homeland, [Skar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skar "Skar") and [Drehy](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Drehy "Drehy") accompanied him and his family there and returned from the trip after the events of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru's]] occupation and the [coalition's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs") [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emuli]] front.[^24] [^25]

After joining the [Coalition of monarchs](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs"), Jah Keved pulled rank, forcing Unkalaki fourth sons to venture down to the lowlands to enlist with Veden troops. Meanwhile, Unkalaki villagers began crafting arsenals of arrows to contribute to the war efforts, and kaluk’i’iki and their hunters prepared to defend their villages in the absence of Unkalaki warriors.[^26]

## Politics

Although the Unkalaki technically live within the borders of Jah Keved, they are essentially treated as a sovereign nation with the peaks as their territory; they are not referred to as Veden.[^27] They are not mentioned as participants in any of the many skirmishes between Jah Keved and Alethkar. The residents of different peaks occasionally attack each other, but this is rare.[^9] They desperately want to win [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] but have not been able to do so until [Cord](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cord "Cord") claims a set of Plate during the [expedition to Akinah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Expedition_to_Akinah "Expedition to Akinah").[^28]

Worldhoppers have desired to establish trade with Roshar but found the prospect difficult. In control of the only stable entry point to the world, which lies within their peaks, the Horneaters are "unpleasant to deal with." [^29]
