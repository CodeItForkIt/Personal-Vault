## Shinovar

> “ *The world is changing outside, but the Shin seem determined to remain the same.* ”
> 
> — [Vstim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vstim "Vstim") to [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn]] about the Shin [^1]

**Shinovar** is a nation on the western mainland of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^2] Due to the [[Misted Mountains - The Coppermind|mountains]] separating it from the rest of the continent, Shinovar is distinct both ecologically and culturally from the rest of Roshar. Shinovar natives are known as the **Shin**.

## Etymology

*Shinovar* is likely derived from the Silver Kingdom *Shin Kak Nish*, which is derived from [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]].[^3]

## Geography and Ecology

![[Shinovar---The-Coppermind-1.png]]

by Adam J. Marin

A farmland in Shinovar

> “ *The entire landscape had an eerie feel to it, as if it were dead. Nothing moved.* ”
> 
> — [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn's]] opinion of Shinovar [^1]

![[Shinovar---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

A map of Shinovar, focused on depicting the locations of the Shin monastaries

Lying on the western side of continental Roshar, Shinovar is bordered to the east by the [[Misted Mountains - The Coppermind|Misted Mountains]] and to the west by an unnamed mountain range. Multiple rivers flow through Shinovar from the bodies of water to both the north and the south. The Misted Mountains prevent Shinovar from bordering any other nations.[^2] The mountains also protect Shinovar from the worst of the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], so Shinovar experiences far more mild highstorms than the rest of Roshar. Regular storms also occur in Shinovar, particularly during the summer, and it can be difficult to tell the two apart based on strength.[^4] The highstorm still recharges gems with the same amount of [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] despite its lower strength.[^5] [[Spren - The Coppermind|Spren]] rarely manifest in Shinovar, though that is unrelated to highstorm strength.[^6]

Shinovar has a vastly different ecology from the rest of the planet, as the flora and fauna there have not the need of adapting to survival in the highstorms, and in fact, cannot survive outside of Shinovar.[^7] Much of the ecology there is based on the plants and animals brought to Roshar by the [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] refugees.[^8] The ground in Shinovar is coated with a layer of soil, making the ground feel springy to outsiders. The entire land is coated with a thick layer of grass that does not move or react to movement. The trees dotting the landscape are straight upward with leaves that do not withdraw into the trunk.[^1] Plants that are known to be farmed in Shinovar include wheat,[^9] strawberries,[^7] and grapes.[^10] Animals that live in Shinovar include [[Chicken - The Coppermind|chickens]], goats [^11] and horses. Horses are both domesticated and wild.[^12] Foreigners aren’t allowed on farmland.[^13]

Buildings made of clay and earth seem to be common in Shinovar, as they remind [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] of his home in the [Valley of Truth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valley_of_Truth "Valley of Truth"). This is motivated by the Shin's beliefs that stone is sacred and cannot be used as a building material.[^12] The weakened highstorms likely make this form of building viable when it would not be in other parts of Roshar. Shinovar contains an [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] somewhere inside the country, though its exact location is not known.[^14]

## History

### Pre-Expulsion

Little is known about the state of Shinovar before human arrival, except that [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singer]] relationship with the land was as such that it was the land they decided to grant to humanity upon their arrival.[^8] The ecology was very different during this era, not yet introduced to Ashynite flora and fauna. It was covered in soil, much like in the modern day, it, however, had no grass growing within the soil.[^15]

### Heraldic Epochs

#### Human Arrival

When humans first arrived on Roshar from [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] after leaving it inhospitable to human life, around seven thousand one hundred years before the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows"),[^16] they arrived in the area that would come to be known as Shinovar.[^15] Following their arrival, the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] gave them the land to settle in, under the commands of Honor and Cultivation.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> Because it is sheltered from highstorms, humans were able to grow the plants and animals they had brought with them there, and these became a defining aspect of the Shin ecology.

Over time, humanity grew dissatisfied with the territory they were granted, and chose to violently expand it, which begun what came to be known as the First Desolation.[^8]

#### The Silver Kingdoms

![[Shinovar---The-Coppermind-3.jpg]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

Shinovar was founded as [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin Kak Nish]] in the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]] Epoch.[^17]

Due to the Desolations, they were constantly returned back to primitive technological levels, often to the point to making use of stone tools. Though they advanced each time with the help of the Heralds whenever they returned, often helping them to enter into the bronze age, often receiving this assistance from [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] and his [[Order of Willshapers - The Coppermind|Willshapers]].[^18] [^19] [^20]

[[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]] claimed to have been in a war near the Shin border.[^21] Shinovar seems to have been less isolationist during this period, as they had a working [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] and Shin people among the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

### Era of Solitude

After the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], four thousand five hundred years before the Night of Sorrows,[^22] the Shin took control of the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]] and brought them to Shinovar (save for [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln's]] Blade, as it was with him on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]]).[^9] Because of this history, they consider themselves the only ones who remember the truth about Rosharan history, though what they remember might not be entirely accurate.[^23] [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] came back and retrieved his at some point without informing the Shin leaders.[^9] Members of the Shin use and train with the Honorblades and [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|surges]].[^24]

#### Ishar and Shinovar

Around one thousand years before the Night of Sorrows, Ishar absorbed part of the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control") and Connected deeply with the land of Shinovar.[^25] [^26] [^27] He came to have a heavy influence on Shin religion and politics.[^26] <sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> <sup title="This section needs to be expanded with more information"><i>[expand]</i></sup>

He used the people, with their access to the Honorblades, as a way to eventually find replacement [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"). However, he was unable to find anyone who satisfied his expectations for a replacement.[^26]

#### Shin Invasions

During the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], the Shin tried multiple times to invade into the rest of Roshar.[^28] They used Honorblades, Surges,[^29] and cavalry, which was incredibly rare in the rest of the continent. Legends speak of large groups of men on horseback carrying spears.[^30] [Shubreth-son-Mashalan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shubreth-son-Mashalan "Shubreth-son-Mashalan") was likely a leader during one of the invasions.[^31]

#### Lead-up to the Everstorm

Before the arrival of the Everstorm, the Shin coast was repeatedly attacked by [Steen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Steen "Steen") raiders.[^32] The raids happened frequently enough that the monastaries built near the coastline were developed for the purposes of defending against coastal incursions.<sup title="This section needs to be expanded with more information"><i>[expand]</i></sup> [^33]

Approximately eight years before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] announced that the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] were returning.[^34] He was told by the Shin leaders that this was false, made [Truthless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Truthless "Truthless"), and exiled, taking [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] Honorblade with him.[^35] [^36] He was traded to [Vstim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vstim "Vstim"), a [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylen]] merchant who often traded successfully with the Shin, and exiled from Shinovar.[^1]

### Lead-up to the True Desolation

After the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] hit the first time, the Shin sent warnings to other countries, including [Tashikk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tashikk "Tashikk"), to prepare them.[^37] After learning of Talmut’s return, they sent a contingent of Stone Shamans to retrieve the man’s [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]].[^38]

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] sent word to them through a Thaylen merchant asking them to cooperate with his planned coalition.[^39] The Shin were considered particularly important allies to gain because they controlled the westernmost Oathgate on continental Roshar.[^40] When they received his message, the Shin declined to join, sending back only a congratulations to Dalinar.[^41] While the Shin did not join the coalition, they also did not join [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and were considered uncommitted by the end of the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]].[^42]

At some point after the start of the True Desolation, various Unmade settled in Shinovar and the Shin made gods of them.[^43] [^44] [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] claims that he saved the Shin from the Unmade, but the veracity of this statement is unknown.[^43] [^44]

#### Cleansing of Shinovar

In 1175, Kaladin and Szeth went to Shinovar. Their goals included potentially recruiting or getting information from [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]], cleansing the land of Unmade influence, and overthrowing the corrupt Shin leadership. Ishar challenged Szeth to complete a pilgrimage, which Szeth and Kaladin attempted to do. They were eventually told after fighting [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] that the presence they believed to be an Unmade was actually Ishar himself, who they fought at the final monastery on the tenth day. This fight led to both Szeth and Kaladin swearing the Fifth Ideal, and the reforging of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].

## Politics

The way Shinovar is governed does not seem to be well known outside of Shinovar, even to other monarchs like [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]].[^41] When it comes to foreign policy, the Shin tend to be isolationist, declining to join Dalinar's coalition of countries when the Everstorm hit.[^41] [^42] This policy is due to their xenophobic nature.[^45] The Shin view the other peoples of Roshar as barbarians, calling them "stonewalkers" and characterizing them in terms of the raiders who occasionally terrorize Shin villages along the coast.[^46] The Shin believe it is terrible that warfare is so openly practiced in the east, believing their method of separating the unholy soldiers to be superior. They also believe themselves to be superior because of the sacred Truth the Heralds conferred upon them long ago: that the Desolations would begin anew at some point in the future.[^47] In turn, other countries tend to view the Shin as barbarians.[^41] [^42]

Shinovar has a distinct governance style. The ten Monasteries of Truth are the seats of its government, with the Stone Shamans and in particular the Honorbearers playing a large role in making decisions across the country.[^48] [^49] In particular, they are responsible for declaring someone Truthless and dispensing an Oathstone,[^48] [^50] [^51] [^12] as well as selecting the acolytes who will eventually become the next generation of Honorbearers.[^49] [^52] They also involve themselves in more mundane matters, such as overseeing the management of the soldier's camps attached to each monastery [^53] and helping decide what is to be done with unsuitable soldiers.[^49] Each monastery also has a Farmer,[^53] [^52] [^48] who is responsible for much of the day-to-day governance of the surrounding area,[^54] [^53] [^32] [^49] and a Cook,[^55] who ensure that no one goes hungry, and a General, who is responsible for a part of the Shin military.[^56]

These roles are different than many ruling positions on Roshar, in that the rulers holding them perform the dirty work of farming, cooking, etc. in addition to making decisions.[^55] Cities that are big enough to warrant additional bureaucracy also have their own appointed mayor and one or more elected ministers (e.g. of finance).[^49] [^52] [^56] The position of Bondsmith Honorbearer is unique in being passed down through civil tradition, while the other Blades are passed down through religious means.[^57] The Bondsmith Honorbearer is the grand senator of all of Shinovar, and holds great authority.[^58]

[[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] views the leaders of Shinovar as corrupt for falsely making him [Truthless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Truthless "Truthless").[^51]

## Culture

### Peoples

The Shin, natives of Shinovar, have round faces and are one of few ethnicities on Roshar whose eyes lack [epicanthic folds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epicanthic_fold "wikipedia:Epicanthic fold"). These features give them a wide-eyed look that, to outsiders, makes them appear to resemble young children.[^59] Many Shin also have lighter skin than people in other parts of Roshar and are often described as pale,[^60] however, they still possess a wide variety of skin tones, with darker tones being common amongst them.[^55] The average Shin is shorter than the average [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") [^61] and most other humans on Roshar.[^56] Their distinctive features are the result of them being xenophobic and not intermixing with other races very much.[^45] The Herald Ishar appears Shin.[^62]

The Shin have a distinct naming scheme, seemingly unique to their culture. A person is identified first by their given name, then as either son, daughter, or child, and finally their parent -- usually the father for men, and mother for women, though it can be the parent they feel closest to.[^12] [^63] [^13] As such, a Shin would have a name like [Thresh-son-Esan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thresh-son-Esan "Thresh-son-Esan") (Thresh, son of Esan) or [Ali-daughter-Hasweth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ali-daughter-Hasweth "Ali-daughter-Hasweth") (Ali, daughter of Hasweth). In extraordinary circumstances, one can use the name of their grandparent instead, so as to avoid dishonoring their parent. One example of such a case is [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] -son-son- [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Vallano]], meaning "Szeth, son of the son of Vallano".[^12] The same pattern is applied to the names of [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") in the Shin culture, with [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] being considered their parent -- [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] is known as Nin-son-God.[^50]

### Class Structure

Shin citizens divide themselves into "those who add" and "those who subtract," forming a social hierarchy based on contributions to the welfare of others. These roles are signaled by the splashes of color that people with different occupations are allowed to wear.[^13]

High on the social ladder in Shinovar are the farmers, who add to the land by growing plants and are as such considered holy. Each farmer is given the title **he who adds** and is referred to as such by other Shin. Farmers spend their days either working their fields or exercising their power to oversee negotiations, during which they wear fine, multicolored robes that tie in the front. The robes seem designed for horseback riding, as the robes reach almost to the ground from horseback.[^13] Farmers are able to own and trade warriors by way of stones similar to [Oathstones](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathstone "Oathstone").[^64] Because of their holy nature, outsiders are not allowed to visit farming villages or farmland.[^1]

Other people who contribute to society—such as shepherds, musicians, and dancers—wear simpler clothes but can adorn them with a single bright garment, such as a sash or bandana. Meanwhile, merchants and traders are expected to choose splashes that aren’t too ostentatious. These customs vary by region and are different in cities and rural areas.[^13] [^1]

#### Warriors

> “ *Here, warriors are the lowliest of men—kind of like slaves.*”
> 
> — [Vstim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vstim "Vstim") to [[Rysn Ftori - The Coppermind|Rysn]] on Shin culture

Warriors are all viewed extremely poorly in Shin culture. To a Shin, since warriors exist only to destroy, they are far below the farmers and craftsmen who live to create, and even merchants, who trade in other's goods. All warriors are required to obey totally the person who holds their stone, voluntarily subjecting themselves into a state not unlike slavery. While a Shin warrior is able to distinguish his stone, it does not appear to be remarkable in any other way--their subjugation appears to be entirely voluntary. As a rule, Shin warriors cannot be given to outsiders, and the rules regarding the trading of warriors inside Shinovar are unknown. What is known, however, is that any person who picks up a weapon is required to take a stone and live the rest of their life as a warrior. Warriors are recognizable by the simple brown clothing they wear.[^1] Shin warriors function as light infantry, and they are all trained beside Stone Shaman acolytes in the art of the sword and in [kammar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kammar "Kammar").[^56]

The Shin religion accepts that "those who subtract" are a necessary evil of any society, and each monastery commands a force of soldiers. The Farmer and General in each community can assign those soldiers as needed, most frequently to defend homesteads from foreign raiders.

#### Truthless

Only the [Truthless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Truthless "Truthless") are considered lower than warriors in the Shin culture. At present, there is only one Shin Truthless, and there are not likely to be more in the foreseeable future. Truthless are exiled from Shinovar, and required to live the life of a Shin warrior outside of Shinovar. They are required to have a master, but are not allowed to seek or avoid a particular master, freely sharing information about their servitude to any who asks (assuming that a current master has not given contradictory orders). The precise nature of the offense that makes one Truthless is unknown.

### Religion

The Shin follow [Stone Shamanism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone_Shamanism "Stone Shamanism"). They believe stone to be sacred and that their soul is given to the stones upon their death, where they continue to exist.[^12] They consider walking upon stones to be a heresy, and derogatorily refer to those who do as Stonewalkers. The stones of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] are the one exception to this, as Urithiru is a holy site.[^34] Similarly, mining in stone is considered to be a heresy. Because of this, metal is very valuable in Shin territory, with the Shin preferring [[Soulcasting - The Coppermind|Soulcast]] metal above mined metal because of the inherent blasphemy in obtaining the latter. Shin won't trade for fabrials apparently for religious reasons.[^1]

They consider mountains, and likely other geographical features, to have something referred to as a spirit-soul.[^34]

They called the period of darkest night between the first two moons the "hateful hour" and believed it was the only time their gods didn't keep watch over them.[^12] Farm villages and farmland are considered holy, with outsiders not being allowed to visit them.[^1]

### Economy

> “ *Never try to cheat the Shin. Be forthright, tell them the truth, and—if anything—undervalue your goods. They will love you for it. And they’ll pay you for it too.* ”

When it comes to trading, and as a result of their humble nature, haggling with the Shin usually involves both parties devaluing their wares to the other, until an agreement can be reached. The Shin value and reward honesty in negotiations. This unique attitude towards trading means it can be difficult for merchants to understand how to get a good deal from them. [Vstim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vstim "Vstim") is one of the few [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylen]] merchants to understand them, so he often gets good deals. Shin are known for raising and trading [[Chicken - The Coppermind|chickens]], an animal unique to this region of Roshar. They also commonly trade horses, cloth, and dried meats.[^1] They are famed for their horses, and many varieties of fruit, such as strawberries, can only grow in the soil of Shinovar.[^7] At least one Shin trader has visited Urithiru since the beginning of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^11]

The Shin also operate mines where soldiers and criminals work. However, they often use Soulcast metal to make tools and weapons, as it doesn’t require the butchery of stone. The Elsecaller and Lightweaver monasteries produce a small supply of Soulcast metal, but merchants are eager to find other sources from abroad. Soulcast metal is the best currency to use when trading in Shinovar; the Shin consider the Stormlight in gems to be holy, and thus gems are unsuitable for common use as currency or light sources.[^13] Within the country itself, coins minted from [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshell]] carapace are used as currency.[^65]

### Language

> “ *Tan balo ken tala,* ”
> 
> —A presumably Shin greeting [Vstim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vstim "Vstim") gives [Thresh-son-Esan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thresh "Thresh") [^1]

The Shin have their own language, which is part of the Dawnate family derived long ago from the [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]]. It shares grammar with other languages in the Dawnate family, like [[Listener - The Coppermind|listener]] and [[Horneater Peaks - The Coppermind|Horneater]], but the vocabulary is vastly different because of how long ago the languages diverged.[^66] Unlike languages spoken in the east, the Shin have multiple words for different kinds of [[Chicken - The Coppermind|chickens]].[^67] The Shin also don’t ascribe volition to plants, in contrast with peoples in eastern [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^68] The Shin language does not lend itself to simplifying words as well as the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] language family.[^10] There is at least one written form of language, the warrior's script.[^12]

### Food

Meat is not commonly eaten by those who add.[^69] However, it is frequently eaten by those who subtract, such as warriors.[^49] They consume a variety of vegetables, along with bread and soda biscuits.[^55]

When [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] tried Shin bread, he was unimpressed until he was told that he had supplemented it with an unflattering meal. He later tried a simple buttered toast and found it more enjoyable.

They use onions, garlic, and other spices to add flavor to food. These are often crushed and mixed together with a clay mortar and pestle.[^55]

Misir wat is a popular food in Shinovar, it is a thick paste made from red lentils.[^55]

They drink wine, and make it out of grapes, unlike the rest of Roshar.[^70]

## Trivia

- The Shin people are the only Rosharans that would look Caucasian,[^71] with more recent information, this likely refers to the paler Shin individuals.[^55]
- The Shin people are the only group on Roshar who make wine out of grapes.[^70]
- Cats at one point lived in Shinovar.[^72]
- In [The Way of Kings Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_Prime "The Way of Kings Prime"), Shinovar was named Shinavar, and was known as [[Silnasen - The Coppermind|Silnasen]] during the Epoch Kingdoms period. Eventually, Silnasen was made into a city-state in Jah Keved and the modern country was renamed to Shinovar.[^73]
