## Voidbringer

> “ *They are aflame. They burn. They bring the darkness when they come, and so all you can see is that their skin is aflame. Burn, burn, burn....* ”
> 
> — Collected on Palahishev, 1172, 21 seconds pre-death. Subject was a baker's apprentice.[^1]

The **Voidbringers** are historically considered to be a group of creatures that attacked the humans of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] in a series of wars known as [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^2] In the four and a half millennia since [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]],[^3] much of the factual evidence on the Voidbringers has faded into myth and legend, as well as [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] doctrine.[^2] Voidbringers were said to have cast mankind out of the Tranquiline Halls and fought the [Heralds of the Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") and [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] on Roshar.

Despite the fact that the Voidbringers were mostly dismissed as legends and folklore, the scholar [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] feared what was to come and tried to surmise the true nature of Voidbringers, hypothesizing that the singers were the Voidbringers.[^4] In actuality, upon the arrival of the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], the Voidbringers were revealed to be a large coalition of various beings allied with the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] --not solely singers. Furious at their displacement and slavery at the hands of mankind, the Voidbringers are intent on taking Roshar for themselves.[^5]

## History

### Origins of the Voidbringers

> “ *Well were they named Voidbringers, for they brought the void. The empty pit that sucks in emotion. A new god. Their god.* ”
> 
> — [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele") [^6]

> “ *They came from another world, using powers that we have been forbidden to touch. Dangerous powers, of spren and Surges. They destroyed their lands and have come to us begging.*”
> 
> — [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele") [^7]

The first known usage of the term Voidbringer was as a title for [Zoral](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zoral "Zoral") on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] around seven-thousand-one-hundred years before the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows").[^8] The exact context of this title is not currently known, but he was likely one of the major figures to be first contacted by Odium on Ashyn.[^9] [^8]

The term would next be used for the first humans on Roshar.[^10] They came to Roshar fleeing their former homeworld [^10] Ashyn [^9] which they had destroyed with the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshards]],[^11] bringing with them their god, the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^12] Initially confined to [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], the place they had been given that was sheltered from the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], they eventually moved to conquer all of Roshar.[^11] The original inhabitants of Roshar, the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], named them Voidbringers,[^10] for they had brought the void of emotions, Odium.[^12] [^6] At some point in this conflict, mankind instead became aligned with the Shard Honor,[^12] whereas the singers took the aid of Odium, who granted them powers.[^3] When those to whom Odium had granted power died, they refused to move on to the [[Beyond - The Coppermind|Beyond]], instead reincarnating themselves by possessing the bodies of other singers. These were the very first [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]]. Mankind began to lose, unable to defeat enemies that were continually reborn. Ten humans asked Honor for the ability to seal away the spirits of the Fused on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], much as Odium was trapped by Honor. Honor made them into his [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), granting them this ability as well as the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]].

### Trapped in Damnation

While this Oathpact worked for a time, the Heralds could be tortured into betraying their oaths and allowing the spirits to return to Roshar in an event known as a [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]].[^3] Over time the singers and the rest of Odium's forces became known as the Voidbringers by mankind. They fought against mankind to reclaim their homelands on Roshar and occupy them,[^13] destroying human civilization [^14] and killing a huge portion of the population.[^15] In joining with Odium, the singers had given over absolute authority to him, directing the overarching strategy of the Voidbringer attacks and creating plans independently of the Fused.[^16] Mankind repelled them each time, with the help of Honor, the Heralds and the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]]. The cycle of torture and Desolations exhausted the Heralds, causing them to abandon the Oathpact after a few thousand years, leaving behind only [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenelat]], who had been forced to return to Braize when he was killed. Taln had never broken before and, by the strength of his will alone, held back the next Desolation.

> “ *The Ancient of Stones must finally begin to crack It is a wonder that upon his will rested the prosperity and peace of a world for over four millennia.*”
> 
> —The Diagram, Book of the 2nd Ceiling Rotation: pattern 1 [^17]

Millennia later, the remaining singers fought against humans in the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]],[^18] led by the Unmade [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]].[^19] The [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]] [[Melishi - The Coppermind|Melishi]] used his abilities [^20] to imprison the Unmade,[^21] but inadvertently [^22] also damaged the minds of all the singers who had been Connected to her.[^21] This rendered the singers into the servile parshmen who were repurposed to form the backbone of human labor for the next two millennia.[^4] Memory of the Voidbringers faded over the centuries, as they became considered to be myths and legends.[^23] Even several generations after the departure of the Heralds, memory of the Voidbringers was already becoming confused.[^24] Information from the shadowdays was fragmented [^2] and what little knowledge that remained of the Voidbringers was heavily revised and edited by the Vorin church during the days of the [[Hierocracy - The Coppermind|Hierocracy]].[^25] Eventually after four thousand and five hundred years, the Voidspren and the spirits of the Fused were somehow released, even without Taln breaking.[^3] [^26] Voidspren began carving out an empire in Shadesmar,[^27] and as a result some true spren returned to the Physical Realm bond with humans to try to prevent the Desolation.[^28]

### True Desolation

In 1173, a [[Listener - The Coppermind|group of singers]] that had avoided losing their Connection and Identity by abandoning their gods and choosing dullform [^29] fought the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") in the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] [^30] on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]].[^31] A single Voidspren guided one singer, [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]], to the discovery of stormform, a form of power.[^32] Fearful of being driven to extinction by the Alethi,[^33] the singers took stormform and in a final [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|battle with the Alethi]] summoned the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]].[^34] As this storm passed over Roshar in early 1174, it restored Identity and Connection to the parshmen, returning their minds and personalities to them.[^35] The Fused also returned, taking the bodies of unwittingly willing singers.[^36] The Voidspren guided the newly-awakened singers to several different locations, conquering their way through [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]] [^37] and concentrating their forces in [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"),[^37] [Marat](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Marat "Marat"),[^38] and [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]], where humans had agreed to help the Voidbringers.[^39] The Voidbringers in Iri have also moved to capture the Oathgates in [Rall Elorim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rall_Elorim "Rall Elorim"), [Kurth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kurth "Kurth"), and [Panatham](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Panatham "Panatham"), securing most of the territory around the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]].[^40]

> “ *Flame and char. Skin so terrible. Eyes like pits of blackness. Music when they kill.*”
> 
> —A quote from the [Iviad](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iviad "Iviad") [^41]

The Voidbringer forces in Alethkar [laid siege](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Siege_of_Kholinar "Siege of Kholinar") to the capital of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar").[^42] With the help of several Unmade sowing chaos in the city,[^43] the Voidbringers managed to take the city and its Oathgate, despite the best efforts of the returning Knights Radiant.[^44] Several weeks later, Odium directed the Voidbringers to make another push to capture an Oathgate, this time located in [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City"),[^16] as well as force the Bondsmith [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] to submit to Odium and become his champion.[^45] With the help of [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul"), the Voidbringers successfully managed to bond with the men of the [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sadeas_princedom "Sadeas princedom") army.[^16] These corrupted humans, along with [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]],[^46] the Fused, and two thunderclasts, formed the major Voidbringer forces for the battle.[^16] Fortunately Dalinar managed to shake off this temptation and swear his next [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Ideal]], opening [[Honor's Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|Honor's Perpendicularity]] to provide Stormlight for the fight and allow some of his Radiants in Shadesmar to aid in the fight.[^47] The Radiants managed to defend the city by capturing [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") within a [[Polestone - The Coppermind|perfect gemstone]] and freeing the Sadeas soldiers from Odium's influence.[^48] The Voidbringers themselves remain a dangerous enemy with control over vast swathes of Roshar. They have not yet decided what will become of humanity should they achieve total victory, but some among the Fused desire to drive them to extinction to ensure that they are safe from humans and Honor forever.[^49] Odium’s forces were also aggressively laying the groundwork for an empire in the Cognitive Realm.[^50]

## Types of Voidbringers

By the time of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], there are a variety of beings that are considered to be Voidbringers by the modern humans of the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]]. While they are often referred to collectively as Voidbringers when they are discussed as a group, in reality there are several important divisions among the Voidbringers. The word Voidbringers basically serves as a broad term that can refer to any one or all of these disparate groups.

### Fused

> “ *They are the spren of parshmen long dead. They are their kings, their lighteyes, their valiant soldiers from long, long ago.* ”

The group most easily called the Voidbringers, the Fused are [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadows]] of ancient singers that were granted access to the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] by Odium long ago.[^3] Making use of [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]], the Fused are often also referred to as spren instead of Cognitive Shadows,[^3] although either term is applicable. The Fused are among the most dangerous enemy of mankind, as their repeated deaths and resurrections have given them ample opportunity to hone their skills in the art of war against mankind.[^32] With the weakening of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]] and the arrival of the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], the Fused are no longer sent to Damnation upon their death, they are instead reincarnated upon the next Everstorm.[^3] With repeated rebirth, the minds of the Fused gradually degrade, resulting in eventual insanity. The Fused are also responsible for the [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclasts]], enormous skeletal monsters with bodies of stone, inhabiting rock as they would a singer.[^16]

### Voidspren

While the Fused may technically be considered to be Voidspren, there are several other varieties of Voidspren that exist as well. There are several varieties of more intelligent Voidspren,[^51] such as [Ulim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulim "Ulim") [^32] and [Yixli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yixli "Yixli") [^52] that seem capable of sapience in the Physical Realm without a spren bond. Some of these spren are made of golden light, while others appear as red shadows.[^51] Those in the Physical Realm have been guiding groups of the singers to staging points [^52] and administering the singers.[^49] They have also increased their control within [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"),[^27] sailing warships and capturing cities as well as [Cultivation's Perpendicularity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cultivation%27s_Perpendicularity "Cultivation's Perpendicularity").[^51] There are also lesser Voidspren which are mindless,[^53] much like the other subspren common on Roshar. These spren are capable of bonding with regular singers to grant them forms of power, turning them into Regals.[^49]

### Regals

> “ *Qualify yourselves for greater service, and you will be elevated to the place of a Regal, given a form of power.* ”
> 
> — A Fused to a group of common singers [^16]

Regals are singers that have bonded one of the various types of lesser Voidspren.[^49] This grants them one of the forms of power, such as stormform [^53] or envoyform,[^49] which each have various abilities. Common singers that have proven themselves to be particularly effective or loyal to the Fused are granted the privilege of become a Regal.[^16] Regals remain subservient to the Fused,[^49] but they command the respect of the regular singers.[^16] Their relationship to the more intelligent Voidspren remains uncertain.[^49] It is the highest known rank that a non-Fused singer can attain.

### Singers

> “ *That chanting, that singing, those rasping voices.*”
> 
> —One of the [Death Rattles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") [^54]

The singers themselves are merely a species native to Roshar.[^10] However, they have been heavily influenced by their ancestors, the Fused, and typically are supportive of Odium. Most of the singers were damaged by the capture of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] in the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]],[^19] and were robbed of their Connection, Identity, and forms.[^21] With the arrival of the Everstorm, their Connection and Identity were restored and they awoke again.[^35] The singers that awoke feel like citizens of their kingdoms of birth; they practice the same customs and attitudes [^55] and consider those lands their homes.[^5] Many singers are angry about the slavery [^56] and wish to build their own lasting empire and subjugate the humans.[^49] However, some wish that they could avoid conflict and simply find their own place to live.[^16] They feel as though they lack an identity as a people and are struggling to find their path forward.[^12]

### Unmade

The Unmade are a group of nine [^57] ancient, twisted spren [^58] comprised of [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^59] The Unmade were once something else, but they were somehow unmade into their current forms.[^60] They now exist as minions of Odium, doing his bidding.[^57] Each Unmade is endowed with vast and terrible powers,[^61] such as [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|corrupting spren]],[^43] providing [visions of the future](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach"),[^62] or producing [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|evil murderous spren]].[^63] The intelligence of an individual Unmade varies from wise and crafty [^64] to simply mindless forces.[^65] The Unmade are not traditionally counted among the Voidbringers, but the two are often associated and they are among the most powerful of Odium's forces. They may have some relationship with the [Ten Deaths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ten_Deaths "Ten Deaths"), a concept that is poorly understood.[^23]

## Culture

In the thousands of years since the Voidbringers were seen, they have become mythologized in human societies, playing a role in the culture, religion, and folklore of the modern day. They are considered by many to be mythological,[^23] or simple fabrications.[^4] Most people do not believe they exist on Roshar anymore, either destroyed or driven back to the Tranquiline Halls.[^66]

### Myth & Folklore

> “ *They lived out in the wilds, always awaiting the Desolation—or sometimes, a foolish child who took no heed of the night's darkness.* ”
> 
> — A child's tale, yes, but this quote from Shadows Remembered seems to hint at the truth I seek. See page 82, the fourth tale.[^67]

In rural areas, Voidbringers played a wide variety of cultural roles. Oftentimes the Voidbringers were seen as mischievous, rather than evil, punishing foolish people and causing bad luck.[^2] They were are often blamed for small misfortunes, such as a blighted crop [^68] or laundry going missing.[^2] In more urban areas, stories of the Voidbringers are slightly different. They are said to be a type of evil spren that walk the night and enter the hearts of good men [^2] and make them do terrible things.[^69] Some confuse mental illness for possession by a Voidbringer.[^70] Voidbringers are often synonymous with monsters and other superstitions, hiding in corners or under the bed.[^66] The term Voidbringer is even sometimes used as an affectionate nickname for troublemakers,[^71] or a metaphor for difficult past events.[^72]

![[Voidbringer---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Greg Call](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Greg_Call "Coppermind:Artists/Greg Call")

An ancient artist's depiction of a Voidbringer

There are darker rumors of the Voidbringers as well. They are also said to haunt the most violent highstorms [^73] along with the shades of the Lost Radiants, causing the damage left by the storm [^74] and feasting on human flesh [^73] and stealing their hearts.[^30] Other groups claim that the Voidbringers steal souls.[^75] Some peoples, such as the [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki"), totally deny that the Voidbringers ever existed, believing them to be campfire tales.[^30] Any sort of oddity, like the powers associated with [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], may be considered related to the Voidbringers by darkeyes [^76] and lighteyes alike.[^4] In fact, any oddity such as a talking animal,[^77] a shadow that points the wrong way,[^78] or a spren that speaks [^79] may be associated with the Voidbringers. Even only a few generations after Aharietiam, the specifics of what the they looked like were forgotten, with ancient artists drawing pictures of [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]] and labeling them Voidbringers.[^24] They are often associated with the Lost Radiants, who are said to have betrayed mankind to the Voidbringers,[^30] although in Vorin theology this is not believed to be the case. [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] suspected that [his visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") might come from the Voidbringers.[^80]

Tales of the Voidbringers are common among the darkeyes, and are occasionally collected into volumes, such as *[Shadows Remembered](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadows_Remembered "Shadows Remembered")*.[^81] In these, the Voidbringers are often associated with stories of ghosts and spirits;[^81] in some tales, the dead become Voidbringers.[^82] In one such tale, a boy who left his home at night was chased to a cavern near a lake by the Voidbringers.[^81] He escaped by floating a piece of wood carved in a human shape out onto the lake and tricking the Voidbringers into devouring that instead of him. In another story, a traveler shown kindness slaughtered a whole family, drank their blood, and wrote voidish symbols on the walls.[^2] There are other tales of evil men made immortal and tortured over and over again, like the story of [Extes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Extes "Extes").[^83] They also often exist on the periphery of other tales. For instance, Derethil sailed west to discover where the Voidbringers spawned and destroy them there [^84] and [Parasaphi's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parasaphi "Parasaphi") people had been slaughtered by the Voidbringers in the Desolation.[^69]

### Theology

#### Vorinism

According to Vorin theology, the Voidbringers are real creatures,[^2] incarnations of destruction [^42] or death given form.[^85] They were created in Damnation forged from pure hatred.[^2] They are viewed as counterparts to the Almighty,[^2] as well as his mythological enemies.[^66] They are said to have come into existence to oppose the Almighty, as the cosmere needed to balance his ultimate goodness.[^2] They dwell in the hearts of mankind, just as the Almighty once did. The Voidbringers were said to have red eyes [^86] and stand dozens of feet tall with bodies of flame [^87] and stone,[^88] which required [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") to be defeated.[^87] In other tales, the Voidbringers had horrific faces and spindly arms of bone.[^89] Other Voidbringers lacked a form, spectral spirits of the dead or spren from Damnation.[^90]

> “ *A scourge and a plague. A hundred times they came upon mankind. First casting us from the Tranquiline Halls, then trying to destroy us here on Roshar. They weren't just spren that hid under rocks, then came out to steal someone’s laundry. They were creatures of terrible destructive power, forged in Damnation, created from hate.*”
> 
> — [Kabsal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kabsal "Kabsal") on the Voidbringers [^2]

The Voidbringers are said to have attacked humanity in the [[Tranquiline Halls - The Coppermind|Tranquiline Halls]], capturing it and forcing humanity into [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^2] The Voidbringers then tried to force humanity into Damnation itself.[^69] Mankind fought against the Voidbringers, led by the [Heralds of the Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [^91] [^2] and their chosen [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^66] The two groups clashed ninety-nine times,[^2] each time the Voidbringers tried to destroy everything in their path [^42] annihilate mankind [^90] but the humans successfully held Roshar.[^66] Finally in Aharietiam, the Final Desolations, mankind succeeded in throwing back the Voidbringers to the Tranquiline Halls.[^2] The Heralds themselves followed them, intending to reclaim the Tranquiline Halls once and for all. It is believed that the best of mankind will join in the [War for Heaven](https://coppermind.net/wiki/War_for_Heaven "War for Heaven") [^92] and leading men [^93] or fighting against the Voidbringers.[^91] The Unmade were said to be the princes of the Voidbringers,[^94] able to blind the eyes of men and mislead them.[^39] The Voidbringers are also said to flay souls in Damnation, torturing people for eternity.[^95]

In modern Vorinism, the Voidbringers have been relegated to a less prominent place in the theology, as they are associated with the Lost Radiants and the failings of Vorinism.[^2] The Vorin church rejects the tales of the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]], claiming that no Voidbringers survived on Roshar following the Aharietiam.[^18] Even still, precognition or fortune-telling is still considered to be related to the [Voidbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Voidbringers "Voidbringers") [^96] [^97] and [[Voidbinding - The Coppermind|Voidbinding]],[^98] and forbidden in Vorin society.[^99] In fact, the return of the Voidbringers has made the church suspicious of all Radiants and Surgebinding, fearful that their powers come from the Voidbringer.[^100]

#### Stone Shamanism

Little is known about the beliefs of the Shamanate on the Voidbringers. They say that the Voidbringers could hold Stormlight in perfectly, unlike humans.[^87] The Stone Shamans hold that the Voidbringers do not exist anymore. Szeth's claims that the Voidbringers had returned were apparently serious enough to lead to his banishment from Shin society.[^101]

### Scholarship

> “ *The Voidbringers had a natural, real-world correlate, I'm certain of it. Something caused the legends.*”

The well educated don't believe in the Voidbringers oftentimes.[^68] They are considered to be little more than myths to most scholars, similar to the [[Spren - The Coppermind|deathspren]].[^2] Many consider all the tales and folklore to be nonsensical and what few serious histories exist are often self-contradictory and vague, as the Desolations occurred during prehistory.[^2] Some fringe scholars might believe in the Voidbringers, but most respected scholars believe them fabrications [^4] constructed by the Lost Radiants as a justification for their power.[^66] Folklorists are the only ones who make a serious study of the Voidbringers.[^69] [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] began a study into the Voidbringers,[^2] traveling to various libraries across Roshar searching for information on them.[^24] She developed a hypothesis that the Voidbringers and the parshmen were one and the same before the arrival of the Everstorm.[^4] While Jasnah was trapped in Shadesmar and presumed dead, her ward, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]], managed to convince at least some of the Alethi on the Shattered Plains of Jasnah's beliefs.[^102] [^86] Jasnah was ultimately proved at least partially correct,[^103] although she had not anticipated the existence of the Fused.

### Secret Societies

The Voidbringers were important to several different secret societies on Roshar. The [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]] had anticipated that the Voidbringers would return [^104] and are trying to control the situation to their advantage somehow.[^105] The [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Skybreakers]] also predicted that the Voidbringers would return.[^104] They maintained a clandestine presence after the Recreance, hunting down nascent Radiants as [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] believed that they could somehow cause the Voidbringers to return. The [[Sons of Honor - The Coppermind|Sons of Honor]] worked specifically for the return of the Voidbringers, believing that their return would return the Heralds and restore the Knights Radiant and Vorin church to dominance. The [Envisagers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Envisagers "Envisagers") believed that the return of the Voidbringers would return the Radiants as well, but they also tried to manifest Surgebinding by putting themselves in life-threatening danger.[^106]

## Trivia

- [[Pattern - The Coppermind|Pattern]] says that the Voidbringers lack a pattern.[^89] It is uncertain what he meant by this. This may be reference to humans being the true Voidbringers, as they do not have marble patterns on their skin such as that of the singers.
- While humans are not technically considered to be Voidbringers by the modern day definition, they have fought on the side of the Voidbringers in both the past Desolations [^3] and the current Desolation.[^39]
