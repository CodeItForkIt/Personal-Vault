## Unmade

> “ *The Unmade are a deviation, a flair, a conundrum that may not be worth your time. You cannot help but think of them. They are fascinating. Many are mindless. Like the spren of human emotions, only much more nasty. I do believe a few can think, however.* ”
> 
> —From the *[Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(literature\) "Diagram (literature)")*, Book of the 2nd Desk Drawer: paragraph 14 [^1]

The **Unmade** are a group of nine [^2] ancient and twisted [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] [^3] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] comprising [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^4] They are regarded as minions of Odium,[^2] but not all of them continue to do his bidding.[^5] [^6] While they are traditionally depicted as personifications of destruction or forces, some of them are cognizant individuals.[^7] [^8] Each Unmade is endowed with vast and terrible powers,[^9] although the intelligence of the Unmade varies drastically.

[[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]], the most intelligent of the Unmade, has three parts in her compound name,[^10] while [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir"), [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], and [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]] have two parts in a compound name and have displayed a level of awareness.[^7] [^10] [^11] Unmade such as [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") and [Ashertmarn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn"), have shown themselves to be mere creatures of instinct.[^12] [^13]

## Characteristics

### Creation

> “ **The Lady of Pains has the Surge of Transformation and dangerous knowledge. She will infuse my entire heart-the pillar-in the proper order, using her Voidlight. In so doing, she would corrupt me and leave me… leave me as one of the Unmade…** ”
> 
> —The [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] [^14]

The specific process of how [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] created the Unmade is unknown, but they were unmade by his hand.[^5] [^6] They were made after the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], as their more limited nature compared to [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiants]] allowed Odium extra strength to give to the Unmade.[^15] The [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] considers being unmade into something else as equivalent to death.[^16] It is uncertain what the Unmade were before becoming corrupted. Upon her confrontation with [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir"), [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] believes her to have originally been a [creationspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Creationspren "Creationspren"), but later thinks it might in fact have been *human*.[^17] [^18]

One process of unmaking is documented with [Raboniel's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel") attempt to unmake the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]]. While [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Invested]] with [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]], Raboniel placed her hands on the Sibling's pillar. The Sibling reacted by glowing with [Towerlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Towerlight "Towerlight"). The Voidlight moved from Raboniel into the pillar's embedded garnets, starting the process of corruption.[^19] If she had been able to continue, the Sibling says she would infuse the entire pillar with Voidlight in the proper order and utilize the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind#Transformation|Surge of Transformation]].[^14]

[[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] believes Odium could unmake the Unmade again. This would steal their memories and rip them to pieces. She also fears he will unmake her children, both true spren and lesser spren, to the same effect.[^5]

### Functioning

In creating Unmade, Odium trapped them in a halfway existence, many not fully belonging to the Physical or Cognitive Realms. Sja-anat experiences a shadowy version of both Shadesmar and the Physical Realm at the same time, feeling the distance between the two dilate as she moves.[^5]

Some of the Unmade manifest various forms in the Physical Realm if they reside too long in the same place or are pulled through by strong emotions.[^5]

Some are practically mindless, more like emotion spren, others are intelligent, crafty and conniving.[^20] [^5]

Unmade are [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Invested]] enough that they have been able to push through [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru's]] [suppression](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Suppression_fabrial "Suppression fabrial") effect, depending on the circumstances.[^21] [^22] [^23] Although not as Invested as the Stormfather or Nightblood, they are still more Invested than the Vessel of a Shard (though obviously not the Shard itself).[^24]

Buildings under the influence of an Unmade cannot be drawn by [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweavers]].[^25] [^17] The Unmade inherently make those in their proximity feel a strong sense of unease and wrongness. The sensation grows stronger the closer one gets to the Unmade itself.[^26] [^27] [^25]

### Capturing

> “ *You see, it can only be bested by someone who deeply, sincerely, **understands it**.* ”
> 
> — [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] [^13]

An Unmade may be captured in a [[Polestone - The Coppermind|polestone]] like other spren, but to do so seems to require both a [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to them [^17] [^13] [^28] and a perfect gemstone.[^29] Unmade are able to think while in captivity but can not break free.[^21] Unmade do not fade during captivity,[^30] unlike [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds").[^31] If there is a crack in the gemstone, they can speak to others, and if in the Spiritual Realm, may be able to influence the Realm.[^32]

Prior to the [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Recreance]], a [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweaver]] managed to trap [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir"), an act previously thought impossible.[^17] Re-Shephir then escaped her imprisonment by unknown means centuries before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]]. After the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") arrived at [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], she once again encountered a Lightweaver, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]]. Sensing the similarities between Shallan's powers and those of the Lightweaver who had trapped her in the past, Re-Shephir fled rather than risk being imprisoned again.[^17]

[[Melishi - The Coppermind|Melishi]], a [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]], was able to trap [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] in a large heliodor under the orders of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] at the end of the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]], which cut off her [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to the [singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers").[^33] [^34] [^35] [^28] Several Heralds, including [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] and [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]], were also present.[^36] [^32] [^37] [^38] [^28]

During the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] used his intimate knowledge about the Thrill's nature, and the method of capturing spren through coaxing them into a [[Polestone - The Coppermind|gemstone]], to capture [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") in the [King's Drop](https://coppermind.net/wiki/King%27s_Drop "King's Drop").[^29] [^13] It is unclear whether or not he utilized the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]] [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind#Adhesion|Surge of Adhesion]] to aid with his [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to the Thrill and it is also unknown if this is the same method that the ancient Radiants used.

It is also theoretically possible to bind an Unmade to the Physical Realm using a Hemalurgic spike.[^39]

## History

### During the Desolations

The origin of the Unmade is uncertain, but they are believed to have come into existence before the start of human civilization on Roshar.[^40] Each would have been considered to be native to Roshar.[^41] They have been implied to have once been something else before being made into the Unmade,[^42] [^18] but for now there is no indication what they once were. They were active in the Desolations, aiding Odium's troops in the conflict with their various skills.[^40] Urithiru was equipped with some sort of protection against the Unmade.[^43] Between Desolations, the Unmade lose their bond to Odium.[^5] Since [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam"), they have remained subtly active on Roshar, probably operating with diminished capacity.[^40] [^44] [^5] They have also slumbered at times, or entered a period of inactivity.[^45] [^5] About 2,500 years after Aharietiam,[^46] [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] instigated the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]] by [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connecting]] with the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] and granting them forms of power and [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]], until she was captured in a perfect gemstone by a [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] strike team.[^34] [^37] [^35] At some point [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir") was also imprisoned by a Lightweaver,[^17] but she later escaped captivity.[^18]

### True Desolation

About five generations before [Lunamor's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lunamor "Lunamor") time, his great-great-great-grandfather encountered one of the Unmade in the Horneater Peaks.[^47]

The arrival of the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] in [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") revitalized the Unmade.[^5] As the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] continued and the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] returned, the Unmade's influence increased. The death rattles allowed by the presence of [Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach") have been noted in [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]] and the [[Horneater Peaks - The Coppermind|Horneater Peaks]] [^48] [^49] [^50]. [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir") was discovered to be hiding in the core of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] by the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") [^51] and was driven out by [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]].[^17] In the besieged city of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), [Ashertmarn's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn") influence disrupted the leadership in the city [^52] while [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] corrupted the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] spren, turning it into a trap for [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar's]] rescue party.[^42] Queen [Aesudan Kholin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aesudan_Kholin "Aesudan Kholin") consumed a gemstone and worked with [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]], but was eventually consumed by it.[^53] In the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], Yelig-nar joined with [Meridas Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Meridas_Amaram "Meridas Amaram") for the fighting [^53] and [Nergaoul's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") influence helped [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|Voidspren]] bond with [Sadeas's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") soldiers, turning them into warriors for Odium. [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and his men defeated Yelig-nar, while [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] successfully managed to capture Nergaoul in a [perfect gem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/King%27s_Drop "King's Drop"). [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] has been researching the Unmade,[^54] but little factual information was found on them aside from in [Hessi's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hessi "Hessi") *[Mythica](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mythica "Mythica")*.[^40] [^55]

### After the Battle of Thaylen Field

[Nergaoul's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") gemstone prison was put in an aluminum box and sunk to the bottom of the ocean.[^56] [^57]

[Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir") shared information about the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] with the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]].[^58]

[[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] has been using her Enlightened [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] children to influence events according to her designs, while trying to convince [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] she isn't actively trying to undermine him.[^5] This influence, through the lure of corrupted [windspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windspren "Windspren"), allowed [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] to kill Odium's [Vessel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel"), [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]], and take up the [[Shards|Shard]] himself.[^59] [^60] Also, at [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan's]] request, Sja-anat sent [[Tumi - The Coppermind|Tumi]], a corrupted [mistspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistspren "Mistspren"), to investigate bonding a member of the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]], but [[Tumi - The Coppermind|Tumi]] chose to bond [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]] instead, at [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin's]] suggestion.[^61] [^62]

[[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian's]] [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(literature\) "Diagram (literature)") indicates one or more Unmade has set up in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]].[^63] [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] claims that he saved the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] after they accepted the Unmade and tried to make gods of them.[^64] This is not actually true since there were no unmade in Shinovar. Ishar himself was the only thing corrupting them.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

While negotiating the contest of champions with [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]], [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] claims he can demand the Unmade retreat, but not all currently obey his will.[^6]

## Culture

In Rosharan culture, by the time of the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], the Unmade have become mythologized heavily. In fact there are disagreements about the number of Unmade; some stories claim there were ten.[^40] In [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") theology they are spoken of vaguely and are typically combined into one entity.[^3] Even still, [ardents](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardent "Ardent") tell old folktales and stories of the Unmade.[^55] The Unmade are often referred to as spirits of the void or nine shadows that moved in the night.[^9] They are considered to be princes of the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] in story, and feature in elaborate songs, poetry, and epics.[^18] People ascribe dozens of names and powers to each Unmade, making it difficult to determine truth from falsehood centuries later.[^65] There are tales of them corrupting spren and people both.[^40] Some people fear that things like talking chickens will bring the eyes of the Unmade upon them.[^66] However, many in the modern day consider them to be nothing more than simple folklore.[^7] Scholarly works that involve the Unmade are often mocked and dismissed.[^40]

The Unmade feature heavily in the songs of the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]],[^67] [^68] who also fear bringing the eyes of the Unmade upon them.[^69] The songs note the names of all nine Unmade, remembering them as gods the listeners swore to never follow again.[^35]

[Sekeir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sekeir "Sekeir"), the [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") who rules [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity"), claims that humans thrive upon the terrible emotions of the Unmade.[^70]

## Members of the Unmade

### Ashertmarn

> “ *Ashertmarn, the Heart of the Revel, is the final of the three great Mindless Unmade. His gift to men is not prophecy or battle focus, but a lust for indulgence.* ”
> 
> —From Hessi's *[Mythica](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mythica "Mythica")*, page 203 [^71]

**[Ashertmarn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn")** is a mindless Unmade that is also known as the **Heart of the Revel**.[^12] He takes the form of an enormous, black, inhuman heart that grows veins into the ground.[^52] Those who fall under Ashertmarn's influence are driven to debauchery and excess,[^40] in extreme cases to the point of total mindless indulgence.[^52] Ashertmarn was most recently seen in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), grown over the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] there, where he managed to influence many of the Alethi elite to abandon their lives and responsibilities in favor of the revel.[^52] His location is unknown after he left the Oathgate platform.

### Ba-Ado-Mishram

> “ *I find Ba-Ado-Mishram to be the most interesting of the Unmade. She is said to have been keen of mind, a highprincess among the enemy forces, their commander during some of the Desolations. I do not know how this relates to the ancient god of the enemy, named Odium.* ”
> 
> —From Hessi's *Mythica*, page 244 [^12]

**[[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]]** is one of the most intelligent Unmade. She is crafty and conniving.[^5] She served as one of Odium's commanders during the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^12] About 2,500 years after Aharietiam,[^46] she [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connected]] with the vast majority of [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] instigating the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]]. She was able to grant them forms of power and provide [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]].[^34] A [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] strike team captured Ba-Ado-Mishram in a perfect gemstone at the end of the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]], which cut off her Connection to the singers, stealing their minds, and touched the souls of all who belong to [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^33] [^23] [^35] The Sibling's Bondsmith, Melishi, trapped her, while she had been attending a meeting to make peace between the singers and humans, urged on by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], when he hesitated. Her gemstone prison had been located in the Spiritual Realm, in a place resembling Melishi's childhood room. She had looked like a miniature Everstorm in the gemstone.Her animosity towards humans was considerably exacerbated due to this perceived betrayal and the long duration of her incarceration.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] and [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] were present during the capture,[^38] [^37] so they may have clues to its location. Kalak believes Ba-Ado-Mishram should be found and released.[^72] Ba-Ado-Mishram is also being sought by the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]] for unknown purposes, possibly to aid their mission of transporting [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] off [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^37] [^73] Her release would also make it possible for her to usurp the position of Vessel for Odium, this internal strife making the composite faction weaker.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> Rlain and Renarin shattered her gemstone prison and she was released.

### Chemoarish

Little is known of the Unmade **[Chemoarish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chemoarish "Chemoarish")**. By the modern [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], she is known as the **Dustmother** and often is conflated with the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]].[^74] There is a variety of lore associated with her, but all of it is currently unknown. In [Bavland](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bavland "Bavland"), her name is invoked as a curse by the bav people.[^75]. She is presumed to be quite powerful and she is associated with disappearance of the entire cities during desolations.[^76]

According to [Hessi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hessi "Hessi"), she is not one of the three great mindless Unmade.[^71]

### Dai-Gonarthis

> “ *Let me no longer hurt! Let me no longer weep! Dai-Gonarthis! The Black Fisher holds my sorrow and consumes it!* ”
> 
> —Tanatesach 1173, 28 seconds pre-death. A darkeyed female street juggler. Note similarity to sample 1172-89.[^77]

**[Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis")**, also known as the **Black Fisher** is an Unmade of median intelligence.[^77] They have not been recorded as active on Roshar recently, but may have been involved in the [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|Scouring of Aimia]]. Dai-Gonarthis can utilize [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to create [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind#Transportation|Elsegates]], though only between two locations that have a high concentration of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]]. At least prior to the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows"), most places controlled by the [coalition of monarchs](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs") were protected from her touch, though the lands of the former [Silver Kingdom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Silver_Kingdom "Silver Kingdom") of [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] were not.[^78] Her Elsegates manifest as a circle of violet light that extends into the ground like a pit or tunnel. Those traveling through one of her Elsegates appear to jump out of the exit, often with enough momentum that they need someone to help stabilize them when they land.[^79] Using this ability of hers has a cost, though not one that needs to be paid immediately.[^78] Like many of the Unmade, Dai-Gonarthis also appears to have some ability to cause emotional pain and suffering, as [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] wonders if she was responsible for the pain [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] felt after he burned [Rathalas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rathalas "Rathalas") and killed [Evi Kholin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi_Kholin "Evi Kholin").[^80]. There is some uncertainty in the mythology as to whether Dai-Gonarthis is one of the Unmade, but some scholars are convinced.[^81] She is used by [El](https://coppermind.net/wiki/El "El") to bring a second army to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]].

### Moelach

> “ *Moelach is close. I can hear his wheezing, his scratching, his scraping at time like a rat breaking through walls.* ”

**[Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach")** is an Unmade associated with precognition and visions of the future. His presence allows for the [Death Rattles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") that King [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] used to modify the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(literature\) "Diagram (literature)"). Moelach's appearance is unknown. In the year 1166 Moelach was somewhere near Kholinar, then he took up residence in Kharbranth in the year 1167, then relocated for unknown reasons seven years later to the [[Horneater Peaks - The Coppermind|Horneater Peaks]].[^80] [^48] [^82]

### Nergaoul

> “ *Nergaoul was known for driving forces into a battle rage, lending them great ferocity. Curiously, he did this to both sides of a conflict, Voidbringer and human. This seems common of the less self-aware spren.* ”
> 
> —From Hessi's *Mythica*, page 121 [^83]

**[Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul")** is a mindless Unmade that is closely associated with battle rage.[^83] Nergaoul is not very intelligent, taking an animalistic joy in death and killing.[^13] He takes the form of an enormous red mist roiling with the forms of men fighting and dying.[^84] Nergaoul causes the effect known as the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill"), which drives men to fight far beyond when they should have given up and makes them enjoy the killing.[^85] During the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], he was captured within the [King's Drop](https://coppermind.net/wiki/King%27s_Drop "King's Drop") ruby by Dalinar.[^13] Afterwards, this perfect gemstone was sunk to the bottom of the ocean.[^56]

### Re-Shephir

> “ *Re-Shephir, the Midnight Mother, giving birth to abominations with her essence so dark, so terrible, so consuming. She is here! She watches me die!* ”
> 
> —Dated Shashabev, 1173, 8 seconds pre-death. Subject: a darkeyed dock-worker in his forties, father of three.[^86]

**[Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir")** is also known as the **Midnight Mother**,[^86] and **The Spawning Mother**.[^80] She takes the form of a mass of black tarry substance that is capable of flowing over various surfaces and appears to have screaming faces bubbling to the surface.[^51] Her primary ability is to spawn the [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|Midnight Essence]], a type of spren that takes the form of creatures and people they see around them and try to understand humankind by mimicking them. She is a creature of instinct that is drawn to violence and pain.[^11] Before the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), she infiltrated Urithiru and was captured by a [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweaver]].[^17] [^43] After the Recreance, she escaped imprisonment and inhabited [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] for centuries,[^37] until fleeing the tower as a result of a confrontation with [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]].[^17] Her current location is unknown, but at some point she reported to the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], incorrectly, that the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] was dead.[^58] [^23]

### Sja-anat

> “ *You sacrificed some of your children so others could live. It was a law of nature. Humans didn’t understand it. But she did.* ”
> 
> —Sja-anat [^5]

**[[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]]** is known as the **Taker of Secrets**.[^40] She takes the form of a jet black shadow that points the wrong way with white holes for eyes [^52] that often appears in mirrors,[^42] as actually seeing her requires reflected light.[^5] She exists halfway between Shadesmar and the Physical Realm, experiencing both at the same time.[^5] She considers herself intelligent, but not the most clever of the Unmade.[^5] Her primary ability is to corrupt spren,[^40] which she refers to as Enlightening.[^87] [^5] She is capable of corrupting both lesser spren [^87] and true spren,[^88] as well as larger spren, such as the spren of an [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]].[^42] These corrupted spren often act as her servants and messengers.[^87] She is actively working against [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], while trying to convince him that she is not.[^5] While her allegiance to mankind is uncertain, she has taken the following actions against Odium's bidding:

- Sent her children, [[Glys - The Coppermind|Glys]] and [[Tumi - The Coppermind|Tumi]], to bond allies of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]]; she also has more corrupted spren willing to bond.[^89] [^62]
- Saved [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] and her companions from death while traveling through the [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]], whose spren she had corrupted.[^90]
- Sent corrupted [windspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windspren "Windspren") to [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] so that he could use them to lure Odium into the presence of [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]], use the sword to destroy [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]], and take up the [[Shards|Shard]] of Odium.[^59] [^60]

### Yelig-nar

> “ *Yelig-nar, called Blightwind, was one that could speak like a man, though often his voice was accompanied by the wails of those he consumed.* ”
> 
> — [Traxil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Traxil "Traxil"), line 33 - [Jasnah's notebook](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jasnah%27s_notebook "Jasnah's notebook") [^7]

**[[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]]**, also known as **Blightwind**,[^7] differs from the other Unmade. Instead of having a specific physical appearance, he is capable of joining with humans or singers who swallow his gemstone, granting them command of the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]].[^91] [^53] [Hessi's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hessi "Hessi") research indicates this may include all ten Surges, though given the Voidbringers' view of the Surge of [Adhesion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adhesion "Adhesion"),[^92] Yelig-Nar may only grant the other nine. Those he possesses have red eyes, grow crystals from their bodies, and appear to be wreathed in a black wind.[^42] [^13] Those that join with Yelig-nar must control him if they do not wish to be consumed by him.[^53]

## Trivia

- The names of the Unmade are derived from deities of Near Eastern pantheons, and H.P. Lovecraft's [Cthulhu Mythos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cthulhu_Mythos "wikipedia:Cthulhu Mythos"); however, the powers and characteristics of the Unmade do not necessarily match those of their nominal inspirations. Suspected associations are:[^93]
	- [Anat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anat "wikipedia:Anat"), Semitic war goddess, for [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]].
		- [Resheph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resheph "wikipedia:Resheph"), Canaanite plague deity, for [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir").
		- [Nergal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nergal "wikipedia:Nergal"), Mesopotamian god of war and death, for [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul").
		- [Moloch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moloch "wikipedia:Moloch"), a biblical term, commonly interpretated as a Canaanite god associated with fire and child sacrifice, for [Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach").
		- [Dagon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dagon "wikipedia:Dagon"), a Canaanite fisher god and [sea-dwelling entity from the Cthulhu Mythos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dagon_\(short_story\)#Cthulhu_Mythos "wikipedia:Dagon (short story)"), for [Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis").
		- [Asherah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asherah "wikipedia:Asherah"), Sumerian sea goddess, for [Ashertmarn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn").
		- [Chemosh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemosh "wikipedia:Chemosh"), god of Moabites and Ammonites, for [Chemoarish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chemoarish "Chemoarish").
		- Unknown [Cthulhu Mythos deities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cthulhu_Mythos_deities "wikipedia:Cthulhu Mythos deities") for [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] and [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]].[^94]
- [Moloch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moloch "wikipedia:Moloch"), [Dagon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dagon "wikipedia:Dagon"), [Asherah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asherah "wikipedia:Asherah"), and [Chemosh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chemosh "wikipedia:Chemosh") are also named as fallen angels in Hell in Milton's [Paradise Lost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paradise_Lost "wikipedia:Paradise Lost"). They are accompanied by [Thammuz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dumuzid "wikipedia:Dumuzid") who was also known in Canaan as Adon, a possible inspiration for [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]].
- In The Way of Kings Prime, there was an Unmade behind a very ornate door in the First Capital, bound by crystal spikes.[^39]
