## Spren

> “ *Spren appear when something changes—when fear appears, or when it begins to rain. They are the heart of change, and therefore the heart of all things.* ”

**Spren** (Unkalaki: *mafah'liki*) are fragments of the [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|powers of creation]] that have gained sentience through the influence of sapient beings on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^4] They are vastly varied in their appearances, characteristics, and levels of intelligence.[^5] They are deeply connected with the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], as a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] between humans and spren is what allows the former to use [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]].[^6] They are also used to create [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrials]].[^7]

Spren are far more extensive and numerous than the closest comparable entities, [[Seon - The Coppermind|seons]] and [[Skaze - The Coppermind|skaze]], Splinters of [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]] and [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]] respectively. This is one of the reasons Roshar does not have the same problem with travelling to the Cognitive Realm as [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]] -- even though Honor is [Splintered](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Splintering "Splintering") just like the other two Shards, on Roshar, spren act as a "release valve" for the uncontrolled power of Honor.[^8]

## Characteristics

> “ *We don’t sleep; we don’t eat. I think we might feed off humans, actually. Your emotions. Or you thinking about us, maybe. It all seems very complicated. In Shadesmar, we can think on our own, but if we go to your realm, we need a human bond. Otherwise, we’re practically as mindless as those gloryspren.* ”
> 
> — [[Sylphrena - The Coppermind|Sylphrena]] on the nature of spren [^9]

### Definition

For Rosharans, the term "spren" has broad application. Entities such as [[Shards]], [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadows]] and other beings from beyond the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] would be considered spren by the Rosharans.[^10] [^11] Among those are Cognitive aspects of objects. Those Cognitive aspects are based on how the objects are viewed by the surrounding entities and how long they've been the way they currently are.[^3] These "spren" can be spoken to in the Cognitive Realm and bribed with [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]], which forms the basis of [[Soulcasting - The Coppermind|Soulcasting]].[^12] In [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), they usually take the form of small beads, akin to [spheres](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sphere "Sphere") that serve as Roshar's currency. A skilled person can "manifest" a bead, making it take the form of its Physical counterpart.[^13]

In the strictest sense, however, spren are [[Splinter - The Coppermind|splinters]] of one or more Shards, with a single spren being composed of one or more types of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]]. Most spren are splinters of Honor and [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], or a mixture of both that leans towards either one Shard or the other.[^14] There are said to be thousands of different kinds of spren, each named for a different type of phenomenon.[^15] They vary vastly in size, with the smallest being the size of a thumbnail, while the largest can dwarf buildings.[^16] [^17]

### Creation

> “ *Besides, I’m less a force of nature and more one of the raw powers of creation transformed by collective human imagination into a personification of one of their ideals.* ”
> 
> — [[Sylphrena - The Coppermind|Sylphrena]] [^18]

Spren are generally personification of certain cognitive ideals and concepts;[^19] as such, they come into being in the realm of thought, the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]]. In the Cognitive Realm, loose Investiture left behind by the Shards comes alive, shaped and given cognizance by the ideas and concepts of the collective experience of sentient creatures. Over time, those ideas and concepts, imprinted upon scraps of Investiture, become personified as an external entity, becoming spren. All of them are based off of an "Ideal" in the Spiritual Realm; for example flamespren are all based on an "Ideal of Fire." [^20]

Some spren predate the arrival of Honor and Cultivation to Roshar, and perhaps even the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]]. These are Splinters of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]] itself that it left behind intentionally and that have attained sentience on their own since then.[^21] These spren are still very much of Cultivation, however.[^22] Their tendency towards sentience, which can be seen both in Shard-derived spren and Adonalsium-derived spren, is a result of a [[17th Shard|cosmere]] -wide phenomenon of Investiture attaining sentience when left on its own.[^23] Particularly strong concepts can even become personified, creating a sapient spren, a rare event that sometimes occurs quickly and other times spans centuries.[^24] While comparable entities exist on other worlds -- most notably, seons and skaze -- the sheer amount of spren created is unique to Roshar.

The cognitive ideals that create spren are similar to those that determine the appearances of the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] on [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthis]] and the plausibility of [[Forgery - The Coppermind|Forgeries]] on Sel, and are based off of human perception.[^25] [^26]

### Functioning

> “ *Perhaps it was the bond that drew them. The bond to people—like Radiant spren, or the spren of her armor, which kept up with them somehow, rolling across the ground and sometimes flying between gaps. There was something to the bond that drew spren, invigorated them.*”
> 
> — [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] on the nature of spren [^27]

In Shadesmar, spren appear to feed off [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|Highstorms]], growing more lively and energetic when one passes over them.[^28] Spren do not need to sleep.[^29] [^30] They are attracted to the particular phenomena they are named after, popping up around the affected area or person -- for example, someone who is scared will attract fearspren.[^31] However, the causal link between spren and their namesake is not fully understood on Roshar, as some spren, like rotspren, are believed to cause their namesake rather than being attracted by it.[^6]

Larger spren have the ability to shape-shift, often mimicking faces, as well as being able to mimic voices.[^17] They also have the ability to pick up small objects and give small pinches of energy.[^32] They will often appear to a singular person, who they will taunt and play with.[^33] Some of the [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki") have the ability to see these spren, even if the spren in question does not explicitly reveal itself to the viewer. These people are called *[alaii'iku](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sighted "Sighted")* in the Unkalaki language.[^34] The only way to keep a spren divided is to store them in separate halves in gemstones.[^35]

Spren can be combined, similar to Shards.[^36] Some spren are also related to the physical phenomena of [wave-function collapse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/wave-function_collapse "wikipedia:wave-function collapse") -- one can fix certain spren's dimensions simply by the act of measuring it.[^37] [^38] Spren notice when they are being watched and will change based on direct individual perception.[^39] Spren can stay longer in the Physical Realm the more they are personified, such as giving them names and compliments.[^38]

Much like Stormlight itself, it was difficult for Spren to leave Roshar due to their strong [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to the planet, as well as the agreement that Honor and Cultivation had with Odium.[^40] [^41] [^42] [^43] [^44] [^45] This restriction vanished when Cultivation left Roshar.[^41]

#### Death

Spren have multiple definitions of death, and are able to die.[^46] One such way is becoming a [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye"). Spren with a Nahel Bond can die if their bonded [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]] breaks their Oaths to an untenable degree. However, there are stages to spren death, and it's not necessarily one-way. If the Radiant who broke their Oaths is not yet at the point where they would acquire a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), the spren simply seems to disappear.[^47] Such a spren can be brought back to life if the Radiant returns to abiding by the Oaths, and re-swears them. It's possible that swearing the next level of the Ideals is a necessary part of the process.[^48]

By contrast, a spren that has been killed after their Radiant acquires a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") leaves behind a dead Shardblade, and while in Shadesmar, they take the form of a [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye").[^49] [^28] A deadeye is a zombie-like figure with scratched-out eyes, constantly striving to mindlessly reach the location of its Blade.[^28] Deadeye spren can be at least partially revived.[^50] [[Mayalaran - The Coppermind|Mayalaran]], [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin's]] deadeye Shardblade, regains consciousness during Adolin's trial by witness in [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity") while under extreme emotional stress.[^50] The release of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] sent all deadeyes on the resurrection process, though it does not fully heal them.[^41]

Similar to a [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]], a spren can be permanently killed by the use of a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger embedded with a gem of anti-Stormlight (though a spren in Shardblade form would be more resilient to this).[^51] [^52] The wound needs to be fatal, and attacking somewhere else will instead cause that part of their body to be destroyed, but leaving them alive.[^53]

Through unknown methods, a spren can have their cognitive form pulled into the Physical Realm, where the new body will spontaneously form organs and muscles, but the spren will die within minutes. The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] calls this act an abomination, and claims that it should not be possible. Cryptics and Ashspren cannot be brought over properly, because the process of creating bodies for them results in a physical form that collapses upon itself immediately. [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] speculates that their physiology does not conform to the natural laws of the Physical Realm.[^54] These bodies can be made into [[Lifeless - The Coppermind|Lifeless]] and affected by [[Forgery - The Coppermind|Forgery]].[^55] Spren can also be killed by having their essence used for power by a [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]].[^56]

Spren can be cut and will feel pain.[^57] [^58] They can be "killed" by hurting them enough that their minds break, making them something similar to a deadeye. They can also be cut by a Shardblade, but it won't kill them and they will reform in the Cognitive Realm after some time.[^59] [^60] Stormlight will help spren heal faster.[^61] [^62]

## Classification of Spren

![[Spren---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Ben McSweeney](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ben_McSweeney "Coppermind:Artists/Ben McSweeney")

Several types of lesser spren (and a [Reacher](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reacher "Reacher")), as seen in Shadesmar

While their variety is near-infinite, all spren can be classified into several groups.

### Nature and Emotion Spren

Spren naturally found on Roshar are divided into **nature spren** and **emotion spren**.[^4] [^63] Emotion spren are attracted to sensations like anger, joy or passion; they are composed of more Honor than Cultivation. Nature spren are attracted to natural phenomena like wind, decay or rain, and have more Cultivation than Honor in them.[^64] Emotion spren will react to emotional [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]].[^65]

While the lesser spren are often invisible, they are said to be ever present and will pop up around the phenomenon they are named for.[^66] Some spren are easier to find than others, though even the most common will, at times, fail to appear.[^17] In [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] in particular, all types of spren are exceptionally rare.[^67] [^68] Additionally, emotionspren are said to be rare in packed gatherings of people, even when emotions run high.[^69]

### Lesser Spren

The vast majority of all spren are non-sapient; these are called the lesser spren, or subspren.[^10] Although some can speak, talking with them is generally an exercise in futility, which leads to people ignoring them a lot of the time.[^33] Spren of the same sub-type are essentially the same individual, with no differences between any two entities.[^70] They are capable of forming a bond with humans, though it is different from the Nahel bond of true spren.[^71] As creatures of nature, spren are not particularly concerned with good and evil.[^72]

Lesser spren vary in how much they appear in the Physical Realm. Some, like windspren, exist almost entirely in the Physical, having next to no imprint on Shadesmar. By contrast, others exist mostly in Shadesmar, with only parts of them, like tongues or drool, visible in the Physical.[^73] There are also spren like [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandra "Mandra") (luckspren), who will often and easily switch between existing in the Cognitive and the Physical. In Shadesmar, this phenomenon is called "dropping", as the spren appears to drop out of existence, and then drop back in.[^13] A large group of spren gathering can make the air in the Physical Realm glow.[^74]

### True Spren

> “ *There are no foolish oaths. All are the mark of men and true spren over beasts and subspren. The mark of intelligence, free will, and choice.*”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] on what separates sapient from sentient beings [^10]

In contrast, spren that do possess sapience are referred to as true spren.[^10] They seem to all represent higher concepts, such as honor for [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") and truth and lies for [Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic"). Nine known [^75] types of true spren bond with humans to grant them [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bonds]] and thus, the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]].

While true spren can transition into the Physical Realm, they do so rarely, and only when seeking to bond a prospective [Knight Radiant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knight_Radiant "Knight Radiant"). This is because transition between the Realms leaves holes in their memories and impairs their cognitive functions in some ways.[^76] Even after forming a bond, moving too far from their bonded Surgebinder will cause the spren's mental capacity to degrade once more.[^77] [^9] If enough people remember that true spren exist, they can persist in the Physical Realm without a Nahel Bond.[^78] [^79] These spren can see glimpses into the Cognitive Realm.[^80]

Once the true spren make the transition, they cannot affect the Physical Realm in any but the most minor ways.[^76] While in Shadesmar, most sapient spren have a humanoid shape and size, in the Physical Realm they usually take on a smaller and simpler form.[^4]

In [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), true spren have their own culture, politics and nations; though the Cognitive Realm was created by creatures of the Physical, it is spren who shape and rule it.[^5] Some spren, like Cryptics or [highspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highspren "Highspren"), have nation-states, composed wholly of spren of their own kind;[^81] [^82] others, like honorspren, seem to rule a nation of their own, but also allow other types of true spren to live with them.[^83] There are also city-states such as [Celebrant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Celebrant "Celebrant"), where various spren live together.[^84] True spren only need one parent to be born.[^85] [^83] Despite this, some spren do form relationships or get married.[^85]

### Odium's Influence

Lesser spren can be corrupted by [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]], a process which she refers to as "Enlightening".[^86] Such Enlightened spren will appear to be misshapen compared to their regular counterparts, with various parts of them exaggerated to a more painful degree -- for example, a hand becoming more wiry and bony, or petals turning into shards of glass.[^87] [^88]

Separately, there exist **[[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]]**, spren made up of the essence of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]]. The lesser voidspren are, like their native Rosharan counterparts, non-sentient. They can, however, form bonds with [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], granting them [[Regal - The Coppermind|Regal]] forms, which often include supernatural powers and a change in mindset in the singer.[^89]

Some spren of Odium are also sapient, making them true spren. Unlike their Honor- and Cultivation-aligned kin, sapient voidspren are capable of retaining their higher functions in the Physical Realm without bonding a person.[^18] Those spren are usually imprisoned on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], along with the rest of Odium's forces, but they are capable of escaping and traveling to Roshar.[^90] They tend to look like small [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] humans, and have colors such as yellow or red.[^18] [^90] Whether sapient voidspren are capable of granting powers to other creatures is uncertain.

Sja-anat has also gained the ability to Enlighten true spren -- something she was unable to do in the past [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^87] true spren Enlightened by Sja-anat undergo similar physical changes as lesser spren.[^91] They can still form Nahel Bonds, although the bond will behave somewhat differently in a manner that remains poorly-understood. Notably, the Enlightenment does not force the spren to turn to Odium.[^92]

### Unique Spren

There are a small number of spren that are one of a kind, with no other like them in the world. Those include the native spren of Roshar, such as the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]], the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], and the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]], as well as the nine great spren of Odium called the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]]. Each of them appears to have a unique power or ability -- for example, the Nightwatcher can grant boons and banes, [Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach") can induce [Death Rattles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle"), and the Stormfather commands the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]]. The intelligence of unique spren varies greatly -- while some have almost humanlike minds, others are animalistic and act more as forces than they do as people.[^93] The former are capable of communicating with humans, and picking who can and cannot hear them.[^94]

At least some unique spren can be bonded. [Bondsmiths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmiths "Bondsmiths") gain their powers from such bonds, with the Stormfather being one of the three great Bondsmith spren.[^95] [^96] Some Unmade also bond with people -- [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-Nar]] grants the use of nine [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] to whomever swallows a gem containing him, and [Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir") attempts to displace a Nahel Bond.[^97] [^98] Whether an Unmade can become a Bondsmith spren is unknown at this point in time.

## Symbiotic Relationships

Some types of spren exist in symbiotic relationships with various other creatures living on Roshar.[^100] In most cases, such a relationship is facilitated by a [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemheart]], a crystal inside a creature's body that allows them to house the spren.

### Bonds With Animals

Large animals, most notably the [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshells]], utilize spren bonds to grow to enormous sizes that would not be otherwise possible. There seem to be two types of spren facilitating this -- an unknown type living in their gemhearts, and [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandras "Mandras"), which decrease the pull of gravity on their bodies.[^101] [^13] [Skyeels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skyeels "Skyeels") also form bonds with mandras, which allow them to outright fly despite not having wings.[^13]

Non-crustacean creatures forming spren bonds include the [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]], a breed of horses which have evolved to attract some type of spren -- most likely musicspren.[^102] This bond is the possible reason for the Ryshadium's unusually high intelligence, as well as their prodigious strength and other adaptations to Rosharan environment.[^103] [^104] The "magic fish" of the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]] are another example of Rosharan fauna forming symbiotic bonds with spren.[^100] Eating the fish grants numerous benefits, ranging from medicinal, like curing aches, to outright supernatural, like predicting when someone will come to visit or locating missing persons.[^17] Whether or not the Ryshadium and the fish have gemhearts is unknown.

### Singer Forms

[[Singer - The Coppermind|Singers]] can attain different forms by taking a spren into their gemheart during a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]].[^105] Each such form comes with a slightly different physical shape, and even different biology -- for example, only [mateform](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mateform "Mateform"), [slaveform](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Slaveform "Slaveform"), and possibly [dullform](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dullform "Dullform") are capable of reproducing, while [warform](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Warform "Warform") is taller and more muscular, with ridges and natural plating to protect its wearer.[^106] [^107]

Such bonds are formed only with non-sentient spren, and once the singer changes a form, the spren is released from the gemheart, unharmed.[^108] When the bond is formed with a lesser [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]], the singer gains a [[Regal - The Coppermind|Regal]] form.[^89]

### Nahel Bond

Each Order of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] forms a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] with a different type of true spren.[^109] This bond is similar in nature to the bond between a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] and its master, albeit unlike a seon bond, a Nahel bond grants numerous benefits to both parties.[^110] The spren gains the capacity to retain higher mental functions while bonded; their partner gains access to [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]].[^6] Though historically, only humans could become Surgebinders, during the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] some spren have managed to form a Nahel Bond with a singer, although how this changes things is unknown.[^111] [^112] It's theoretically possible for someone to bond multiple spren, although this would require a lot of upkeep on the bondee's side in an effort not to break the rules of either bond.[^113]

Each Nahel Bond grants access to two [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]], with every Surge shared between two orders.[^114] Surgebinders that are organized into proper Orders, rather than acting on their own, are known as [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].

The bond between a Knight and their spren can be strengthened by verbally or mentally stating certain [Ideals](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words") or ideas related to their Order. Once the bond has strengthened to a certain point, the spren gains the ability to become a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") when needed.[^48] A sword is the base form for a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), since they were fashioned after the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]], but they can become any shape their Knight needs, such as a spear, hammer, or shield. While in a Shardblade form, the spren can communicate with their Surgebinder through their mind.[^115]

Additionally, each type of Nahel spren (with the possible exception of the Bondsmith spren) are associated with a type of subspren. These spren are sometimes said to be "cousins" of the corresponding Nahel spren and form a Radiant's [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] when they reach a sufficient Ideal.[^116] [^117] [^118]

[Bondsmiths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmiths "Bondsmiths") powers are granted by three unique spren: the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]], and the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]]. ## Known Subtypes

### Lesser Spren

#### Agonyspren

> “ *Agonyspren, like upside-down faces carved from stone, twisted and faded in and out.* ”
> 
> — [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] [^120]

Agonyspren, a much rarer spren drawn to a person’s agony and suffering, appear as upside down faces carved into stone.[^120]

#### Alespren

> “ *Test successful. Have noted spren who appear only when one is severely intoxicated. Appear as small brown bubbles clinging to objects nearby. Further testing may be needed to prove they were more than a drunken hallucination.* ”

A rare type of spren that appear only in [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]]. They are occasionally seen by people who are under the influence of alcohol. They look like small brown bubbles that cling to objects close to the observer. They were given the name "alespren" by Axies the Collector, a scholar who studies spren, after considering the name "intoxicationspren". The locals refer to them as sudspren.[^17] They may also be called winespren.[^16]

Axies theorized their existence, but was only able to observe them once, leading him to doubt whether they're real.[^17] However, as there is a singer form associated with intoxicationspren, they must exist. For some unknown reason it isn’t a great idea to take this form.[^121]

#### Angerspren

> “ *Vamah was watching them stand there, and his expression was as thunderous as a highstorm, angerspren boiling up from the ground around him like small pools of bubbling blood.* ”
> 
> —Adolin Kholin [^122]

A common type of spren that appear around people experiencing anger. They generally, have the appearance of pools of boiling blood bubbling up from the ground around the feet of the angry individual.[^122]

Among the listeners, angerspren have the appearance of bolts of lightning that radiate out from the feet of the angry individual.[^123] [^124]

In the Cognitive Realm, they are lithe, six legged predators with whip-like tails and spines.[^125] They have toothy maws, and hunt in packs, making them even more dangerous.[^126] They make a loud screeching noise similar to sheets of steel grinding against each other when attracted.[^127] Angerspren are said to be "nasty" and very dangerous even to other spren. The pools of boiling blood seen in the Physical Realm are actually saliva drooling from their mouths.[^73]

#### Angstspren

A type of spren drawn by anxiety, appear as green water bugs encircling a person's head.[^1] [^128]

#### Anguishspren

> “ *Certainly there were painspren appearing in droves in the room, even a few rare anguishspren, like teeth growing from the walls.* ”
> 
> — [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani Kholin]] [^129]

A rare type of spren drawn by anguish and extreme loss. They appear similar to human teeth.[^129]

#### Anticipationspren

> “ *A few anticipationspren—like red streamers, growing from the ground and whipping in the wind—began to sprout from the rock and wave among the soldiers.* ”
> 
> —The Way of Kings: Chapter Six [^32]

A common type of spren that appear around people looking forward to something, or who are nervous about something. They appear as red streamers that grow out from the ground and blow in the wind. They often appear around soldiers directly before a battle.[^32]

The streamer that appears in the Physical Realm is actually the tongue of the anticipationspren. The spren itself has a round bulbous body and is about the size of a human toddler. It will open its mouth and stick its tongue in the air when near feelings of anticipation.[^86] [^130]

#### Anxietyspren

> “ *A few anxietyspren, like twisting black cross shapes, appeared around Rysn.* ”
>

The [[Tai-na - The Coppermind|Tai-na]] of the [Reshi Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi_Sea "Reshi Sea") bond an unknown spren, referred to as their "soul" by their [inhabitants](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi "Reshi"), that allow these greatshells to grow to gargantuan sizes.[^235] These "souls" will sometimes show themselves to people, and seem to be able to interact with the world and magic in some ways, such as slowing a person's fall.[^236] It is uncertain whether these are the [same spren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Luckspren "Luckspren") bonded by other greatshells or not, but [Axies the Collector](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Axies_the_Collector "Axies the Collector") believes the "souls" must be more "spectacular" than the others.

#### Vaporspren

Vaporspren are a type of spren that sometimes appear around bodies of water. They are mentioned as looking iridescent,[^237] gliding around on gossamer wings. The Iriali celebrate a festival named after these spren; the Vaporlight Festival.[^162]

#### Wavespren

> “ *Wavespren danced atop the tips of whited caps across a field of shimmering blue.* ”
> 
> —Dawnshard: Chapter Five [^238]

These spren are four-legged with smooth skin and large eyes.[^239] They are found in the ocean, and have been seen splashing into the air after the collision of two waves [^240] as well as riding waves on the beach.[^239]

#### Windspren

> “ *Windspren were devious spirits who had a penchant for staying where they weren't wanted...* ”
> 
> —The Way of Kings: Chapter Two [^33]

> “ *The windspren themselves were entranced by you, Brightness Shallan, and led us here!* ”
> 
> — [Captain Tozbek](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tozbek "Tozbek") [^189]

A common type of spren that appear in the presence of wind. As one of the larger types of spren, windspren are able to change their appearance at will, though they often appear as ribbons of light.[^33] The leading edge of a highstorm is home to thousands of them.[^241] They are also common at the tail of a highstorm, as if chasing after it.[^242] They are a rare sight in Shadesmar, as they spend almost all their time in the Physical Realm. A windspren in Shadesmar is described as a pinprick of light trailing a long luminescense behind it, moving much like it would in the Physical Realm.[^83] Windspren also seem attracted to Radiants moving particularly fast, such as Windrunners and Edgedancers utilizing Gravitation and Abrasion.[^117] [^243]

Windspren are closely related to [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren"), such as Sylphrena.[^244] This is evidenced by their ability to stick things together,[^33] making use of a rudimentary version of the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surge]] of Adhesion.[^245] They generally use this ability to play pranks on people, doing things like sticking shoes to stone or sticking shoelaces together to cause someone to trip.[^87] Additionally, windspren form [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Plate]].[^118] Once windspren become armor spren, they appear as pinpricks of light, but normally remain invisible to all but their Radiant.[^246] [^247] Kaladin Stormblessed occasionally attracts windspren, and has been able to control them-and by extension, the wind itself-on at least one occasion; it is unclear if this is unique to him or if any Windrunner can do it.[^18]

Singers bond to windspren to enter nimbleform.[^248] [^192]

### True Spren

The first seven varieties of true spren were created by both Honor and Cultivation, after which Cultivation created the cultivationspren on her own and Honor created the honorspren "almost entirely" on his own.[^249]

#### Ashspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Dustbringers - The Coppermind|Order of Dustbringers]], granting them their powers.[^250] Ashspren grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Division and Abrasion. In Shadesmar, they have a humanoid form with ash-white skin that falls away when the ashspren moves, revealing bones underneath before quickly growing back.[^84] In the Physical Realm, they manifest as branching patterns of fire that appear to burn through the air or across the surfaces of objects, leaving behind illusionary cracks but not actually damaging their medium. They seem to have the ability to hide from both other humans and other spren.[^250]

They seem to be fond of destroying the things around them.[^251]

#### Cryptics

Also known as liespren, Cryptics are a type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Order of Lightweavers]], granting them their powers.[^4] Cryptics grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Transformation and Illumination.[^114] They represent fundamental mathematical laws of nature, and truths that explain the fabric of existence.[^86]

In [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), they have the appearance of figures in robes that hang stiffly, as if made of glass rather than fabric. Instead of heads, they have a floating symbol full of impossible angles and geometries.[^252] There, Cryptics reside in a single city-state, and do not wish to expand.[^82] In the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], Cryptics take the shape of a complex and constantly shifting geometric pattern slightly raised off a surface or object.[^4] They can also manifest as a floating three-dimensional mass of twisting lines.[^253]

#### Cultivationspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knight Radiant [[Order of Edgedancers - The Coppermind|Order of Edgedancers]], granting them their powers, which include the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Abrasion and Progression.[^254] They appear in the Physical Realm as a growing vine with crystals, while in Shadesmar they take a form of a humanoid seemingly made out of thickly-bound, thin vines and pieces of crystal.[^76] They are generally considered the spren that express the purest form of Cultivation.[^255]

Cultivationspren are ruled by a group of them called the [Ring](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ring "Ring"), which, among others, dictates which spren are to bond whom.[^243] They have the capacity to let their kinsmen travel into the Physical Realm without the usual accompanying loss of mental functions.[^76]

#### Highspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Order of Skybreakers]], granting them their powers. Highspren grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Gravitation and Division.[^114]

In the Physical Realm, highspren are described as looking like black slits in reality, with white glowing stars shining through.[^256] They seem to have their own settlements in Shadesmar and appear rather isolationistic, with none to be seen in places like [Celebrant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Celebrant "Celebrant").[^81] [^84] Other spren consider them to be strict and rule-bound.[^250]

#### Honorspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Windrunners - The Coppermind|Order of Windrunners]], granting them their powers. Honorspren grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Gravitation and Adhesion.[^114] They are said to be quite "discerning" in deciding who they would bond with.[^257] They are generally considered the spren that express the purest form of Honor.[^255]

In the Physical Realm, they can change their forms, although in any shape, they are blue-white with a matching glow.[^33] In Shadesmar, they look like humans with blue-white skin and clothes that are part of them.[^169] They are noted to be highly warlike, with numerous cities and fortresses, as well as their own fleet.[^250]

#### Inkspren

A type of sapient spren that form Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Elsecallers - The Coppermind|Order of Elsecallers]], granting them their powers. Inkspren grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Transformation and Transportation.[^114] They look like small, human-shaped figures with ink-black skin and clothing, coated in what seems to be oil, with a mother-of-pearl or prismatic quality to their look.[^258] They are capable of blending into shadows.[^258] [^4] In addition, they can change their size, but not their shape.[^82]

##### Oathgate Spren

> “ *We are apologetic. We have been… alone very long. We would enjoy granting passage to men again. But we cannot do that which was forbidden.* ”
> 
> —Spren of [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") Oathgate [^259]

Oathgate spren are transformed inkspren that reside over the [Oathgates](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathgates "Oathgates").[^151] They call themselves the gatekeepers.[^259] They take the form of enormous, statue-like humanoids, many times taller than a person, wearing armor and holding fittingly large spears. They always appear in pairs, with one being mother-of-pearl shimmering white, while the other is shimmering black.[^260] They act and speak in concert, with no visible differences in their opinions or demeanor.[^259]

The Oathgate spren facilitate transfer between Oathgates, with any pair being able to transport anything on the Oathgate's physical platform and in its control chamber between themselves and a matching Oathgate in either [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] or one other city.[^261] Their existence is a crucial element of the Oathgates' functionality, as an Oathgate can be rebuilt so long as the spren are left uncompromised. It appears they are bound to the eleven gemstones that are used to operate the Oathgate.[^92] While the Oathgate spren can be Enlightened, this does not impact their ability to send travelers to unEnlightened ones.[^262]

In the past, those spren also allowed people without the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surge]] of Transportation to travel to and from [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"); however, the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] forbade it to them shortly before their departure. While the Oathgate spren are highly regretful about it, they are physically incapable of disobeying their orders.[^259] Although many of the gatekeepers uphold the Siblings order, some of them have listened to the Stormfather's request to allow people to use the Oathgates to travel between the Physical Realm and Shadesmar once again.[^263]

#### Mistspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Truthwatchers - The Coppermind|Order of Truthwatchers]], granting them their powers.[^263] In Shadesmar, they take the form of humanoid figures made out of swirling, indistinct fog. Their faces look like porcelain masks, and they wear gloves, trousers and vests that adhere to their bodies' shape despite their seeming incorporeality,[^83] [^263] and take the form of light reflected through the mirror while in the Physical Realm.[^264]

They have at times been affiliated with [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren"), working as common sailors on their ships.[^83]

#### Peakspren

A type of sapient spren that forms Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Stonewards - The Coppermind|Order of Stonewards]], granting them their powers.[^265] In Shadesmar, they take the form of a humanoid figure with skin of cracked stone and molten light glowing from within.[^84]

#### Reachers

A type of sapient spren that formed Nahel Bonds with the Knights Radiant [[Order of Willshapers - The Coppermind|Order of Willshapers]], granting them their powers. Reachers grant the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Transportation and Cohesion. In the Physical Realm, they appear as glowing balls of light.[^112] In Shadesmar, they appear as humans with metallic bronze skin and holes for pupils, like living statues. There, they are also known as Reachers.[^13]

Reachers are often guides in Shadesmar, as they like to travel to different places, trading and selling goods.[^127] They have ships pulled by teams of [mandras](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mandra "Mandra"), inlaid with lines of copper that may serve as a medium of communication.[^13]

## Enlightened Spren

The Unmade [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] has the ability to corrupt spren by injecting them with Voidlight, which she calls Enlightening.[^87] [^266] In the past, she could only Enlighten lesser spren; presently, even Radiant spren and [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] spren are possible for her to Enlighten.[^86] After being Enlightened, the spren's essence changes to be a mixture of the Shards it was previously and Odium.[^267] [^268] This also allows the spren, and those they are bonded to, to hear the rhythms of Odium.[^269] They work similarly to normal spren in [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrials]], although they were unaffected by the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling's]] [suppression fabrial](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Suppression_fabrial "Suppression fabrial") when its effect was inverted.[^270] A singer would adopt a different form if an Enlightened spren entered their gemheart.[^271]

These Enlightened spren are drawn to the same phenomena, but are different in appearance from the typical spren.[^272] [^273] [Syl](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Syl "Syl") believes that Enlightened spren can be recognized by vividly red color, though not all spren fit this description.[^169] Overall, Enlightened spren seem to appear more painful, twisted and more intense -- the fire is blue-hot rather than red, streamers turn into whips, edges grow sharper and more jagged.[^164] [^273] Enlightened spren are hated by Honor, Cultivation, and Odium.[^274]

Sja-anat refers to the Enlightened spren as her children.[^275] [^276]

### True spren

Sja-anat does not usually Enlighten true spren without their consent.[^283] Enlightening has "dire ramifications" for existing Nahel Bonds, so Sja-Anat usually Enlightens unbonded spren.[^284] It is theoretically possible for Sja-Anat to Enlighten deadeyes.[^285] Radiants who have bonded Enlightened spren have different abilities from standard members of their Order. While some Surges can remain unaffected,[^286] [^287] [^288] others can have vastly different effects.[^289] [^112] An Enlightened Radiant will have different oaths from a standard one.[^290] They are also capable of hearing Odium's [Rhythms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rhythm "Rhythm") and using Voidlight for Surgebinding.[^269] [^291]

Enlightened true spren have extra affinity and abilities in the Spiritual Realm that normal true spren lack.[^292] [^27] Enlightened spren are easily able to navigate the Spiritual Realm, as well as find and move other people or things inside of it.[^267] [^292] [^293] They can also track down visions that are created.[^294] They are able to make other people appear as the person they are taking over in visions, though they can't also choose which person they get.[^295] [^296] [^139] They can also alter a vision, removing or resetting certain elements.[^297] If noticed by a Shard, Enlightened spren have difficulty using their abilities.[^298]

They can also create visions from the memories of their Radiant, which can keep them safe from even the notice of Shards.[^274] [^294] [^267] These visions are slightly off, bleeding color and are not as real as other visions.[^274] [^267] This is due to them creating the vision from the Radiant's memories, instead of the Connections and tones of the Spiritual Realm.[^274] Using these visions, they, and others, can hover outside of other visions and watch from outside of it undetected.[^295] [^209] If their Radiant is inside of a vision, they are able to view what actually happened at the original event the vision is mimicking.[^299] [^300]

- **Mistspren**: Rather than looking like a reflected light, Enlightened [Mistspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistspren "Mistspren") appear as a crystalline structure dripping red glow upwards.[^91]
- **Cryptic**: Instead of usually dark coloration, Enlightened [Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic") appears bone-white, with their head patterns reminiscent of scribbles instead of geometrical shapes.[^268]
- **Inkspren**: [Inkspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Inkspren "Inkspren") appear as a human-like form, but instead of its usual black, Enlightened inkspren shine with colors.[^268]
- **Oathgate Spren**: Enlightened [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] spren look like stretched-out versions of people, 30 feet tall, hovering in the air. One is pitch-black and the other is red.[^301] [^151]

## Known unique spren

Unique spren are spren that are one of a kind, or at least exceedingly rare and powerful. Most of them appear to have been created, or at least heavily affected, by direct [[Shards|Shardic]] influence. They include:

### Bondsmith Spren

Bondsmiths form a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] with one of three unique spren: the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]],[^95] the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]],[^302] or the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]].[^303] Historically, Bondsmiths bonded to the primeval spren of Roshar, the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), and [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night").[^99]

#### Nightwatcher

The Nightwatcher is a [[Splinter - The Coppermind|splinter]] of [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]],[^304] and has been raised by Cultivation akin to a child.[^305] She holds court in an overgrown, fertile region known as the [Valley](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valley "Valley") of the Nightwatcher, slightly south of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], in the mountains of [Greater Hexi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Greater_Hexi "Greater Hexi").[^306] Any visitor there can ask for a boon; she might bestow something that strives to fulfill that boon. However, each boon is also associated with a curse, which is not necessarily matching.[^307] Boons can range from mental alterations, such as renown or skill, or physical gifts, like wealth or magical objects. However, the Nightwatcher has some problems understanding more abstract requests, like forgiveness or unchanging. For this reason, Cultivation monitors her closely, intervening if need be.[^305] Additionally she is one of the three [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") spren.[^308] She was created from the essence of the Night, one of the primeval spren of Roshar.[^99]

The boon and curse system is known to Rosharans as [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]].[^307]

#### Stormfather

The Stormfather is one of the oldest entities on Roshar, having existed in some form prior to the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]].[^309] He is the Cognitive manifestation of peoples' impression of the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]].[^18] He exists mostly in the Physical Realm, with only a minor presence in Shadesmar.[^250] He commands the highstorms that circle the planet, with the ability to choose when to send one.[^310] While he generally accompanies storms, his level of power allows him to be much more omnipresent.[^311] He's one of the three [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") spren, granting the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [Adhesion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adhesion "Adhesion") and [Tension](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tension "Tension").[^95]

To the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], the Stormfather is known as the Rider of Storms. At some point, he has switched sides to humans, leading to [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] feeling betrayed by him.[^105] He was also changed by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] shortly before the latter's death, providing him with more awareness, and partly merging him with [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast's]] [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]].[^312] [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar's]] ability to open [[Honor's Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|Honor's Perpendicularity]] might have something to do with it.[^313]

#### Sibling

The Sibling is one of the three Bondsmith spren and inhabits and powers the tower-city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]]. They have pretended to be dormant since the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), after a prolonged decline that saw many of the tower's life support systems failing, eventually forcing the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] to abandon the city.[^314] [^315] Stormfather asserts that the Sibling's withdrawal is tied to humankind hurting them in the past in some manner.[^316] They were created from the essence of the Stone, one of the primeval spren of Roshar.[^99]

Unlike most known true spren, the Sibling did not view themselves as male or female, and in fact found the spren's adherence to physical creatures' gender norms to be bizarre.[^317]

### Primeval Spren

There are three especially old and powerful Spren created by Adonalsium along with Roshar. These are known as the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), the [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), and the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night").[^99] [^63]

#### Wind

The Wind is one of the primeval spren of Roshar. The Wind was originally constituted the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]], and their impact on Roshar has fluctuated with time.[^208] Being the embodiment of Roshar's wind, the Wind is nearly omnipresent. She watches much of what goes on throughout the world, though her ability to respond to and influence it is limited.[^174]

#### Stone

The Stone is one of the primeval spren of Roshar. The Stone was originally constituted the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]], and their impact on Roshar has fluctuated with time.[^208] Being the embodiment of Roshar's stone, the Stone is nearly omnipresent. It watches much of what goes on throughout the world, and remembers all that happens within it.[^318]

#### Night

The Night is one of the primeval spren of Roshar. The Night was originally constituted the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]], and their impact on Roshar has fluctuated with time.[^208] Even in the earliest days of Roshar, few people loved or even spoke of the Night.[^99] Instead, the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] claimed her siblings Wind and Stone, as well as the spren, as their gods.[^319] [^320] As a result, the Night eventually left Roshar, though how this affected the planet or where she went are unclear.

### The Unmade

> “ *We were made, then unmade.* ”
> 
> — [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] [^321]

The Unmade are a group of nine ancient spren of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^261] Each of them are exceedingly massive and powerful, and each has a unique form and effect on the surrounding environment.[^322] They vary vastly in not only their appearance, but also their level of intelligence, with some being mindless, while others are capable of complex thought.[^112] [^321] Unlike most servants of Odium, many Umade have remained on Roshar after the [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam"), and dwell there to this day.[^323]

The Unmade are:

- **[Ashertmarn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashertmarn "Ashertmarn")** - also called Heart of the Revel. Causes people in its sphere of influence to give into mindless debauchery and excess. Last seen in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), although it withdrew to lure Shallan into using the local [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]], making its current location uncertain.[^87]
- **[[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]]** - one of the intelligent Unmade. Can connect with the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], granting them access to [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]] and [[Regal - The Coppermind|Regal]] forms.[^324] She has been trapped at the end of the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]], and is yet to be seen in the present day. She has been released from her gemstone prison, where she had been trapped by Melishi, the Sibling's Bondsmith, by Rlain and Renarin. She seemed compassionate towards the singers and was amenable to the suggestion of making peace with the humans in the past. She looked like a miniature Evestorm in her prison.
- **[Chemoarish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chemoarish "Chemoarish")** - also known as the Dustmother.[^325] Very little is known about her, as she is yet to be seen.
- **[Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis")** - sometimes called the Black Fisher. As with Chemoarish, her true nature is unknown at this point in time.[^326] She grants the ability to create Elsegates, but her release appears to be distasteful for Odium.
- **[Moelach](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moelach "Moelach")** - doesn't appear to have a presence in the Physical Realm, but causes [Death Rattles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") in some people who die within his sphere of influence.[^327] Last seen in the environs of [Vedenar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vedenar "Vedenar").[^328]
- **[Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul")** - an animalistic Unmade; usually doesn't have a presence in the Physical Realm, but can take the form of a cloud of red mist with various shapes within in. Its presence causes a lust for battle known as the Thrill.[^91] At the present, it has been captured and imprisoned in the [King's Drop](https://coppermind.net/wiki/King%27s_Drop "King's Drop") by [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]].[^112]
- **[Re-Shephir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Re-Shephir "Re-Shephir")** - known as the Midnight Mother. She can create [[Midnight Essence - The Coppermind|Midnight Essence]], monsters that mimic the creatures around them. Has been nesting in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] until [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] banishes her; her current whereabouts are unknown.[^18]
- **[[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]]** - one of the intelligent Unmade, once called the Taker of Secrets. She can "enlighten" spren, corrupting them with Odium's Investiture. In the present, she appears to be unwilling to continue working with Odium, and is looking for a way to switch sides.[^321]
- **[[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]]** - sometimes called the Blightwind.[^329] He's capable of bonding with people who swallow a gemstone containing him, granting them the use nine [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]]. Was present at the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], although the Fused presumably took him back when they withdrew.[^112]

### Grey Remnant

The Grey Remnant resides underneath [Rathalas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rathalas "Rathalas"), and has slumbered there for thousands of years.[^330] [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] priests hid his existence to prevent people from seeking his knowledge, as they considered it to be to close to predicting the future. The Grey Remnant appears as dozens of emaciated hands and he has a low, raspy voice.[^331]

He is related to the Nightwatcher, and refers to her as his mother.[^332] [^330] Due to being mostly forgotten, he is much weaker and less coherent than he was in the past.[^331] To try and counteract this, he will exchange information for a painful truth, which he hopes will [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connect]] him with others.

## Myths and Lore

Apart from the real spren, Rosharan folklore has a small number of creatures whose existence is questionable, but which are also referred to as spren. Chief among them are the **[Dawnsingers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawnsingers "Dawnsingers")**, which the [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") theology teaches were servants of the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty"), sent by him to care for mankind on Roshar.[^159] In the lore, Dawnsingers are credited with bestowing numerous gifts upon humanity, including the [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]], humanity's original written language, and [Dawncities](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawncities "Dawncities"), a group of ancient settlements following [cymatic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cymatics "Cymatics") patterns.[^205] [^333] The [ardents](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardent "Ardent") of [Palanaeum](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Palanaeum "Palanaeum") claim that their library was constructed by the Dawnsingers.[^66] It's eventually revealed, however, that the Dawnsingers aren't spren, but rather the ancient [singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers"), the original inhabitants of Roshar.[^334]

Standing in opposition to the Dawnsingers in the folklore are the **[[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]]**, which in the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]] are believed to be a type of evil spren.[^159] They are said to have existed in ancient times, walking the land with bodies of stone.[^31] Other stories claim that the Voidbringers stalk the most violent [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], or that they live out in the wilderness, coming out only at night. Most tales agree that Voidbringers would eat human flesh.[^242] [^335] Those stories appear to be inspired by different types of Odium's servants, who likely merged together into a single, nebulous entity in the public consciousness over the millennia.

Finally, there are several types of spren that the common knowledge doesn't align with Odium, but which are considered malicious nonetheless. Those include the **raspings**, the **stormwhispers**, and the **nightspren**, of which the last are considered to be the most fearsome and dangerous. Those spren are mostly believed in by the more traditional, rural followers of Vorinism. The more educated segment of the populace considers them to be ancient fabrications, created by the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] past to support their dominance.[^336]
