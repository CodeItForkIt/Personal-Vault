## Dawnsinger

> “ *The Dawnsingers weren’t spren, as theology has often postulated. Nor were they Heralds. They were parshmen.* ”
> 
> — [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] [^1]

The **Dawnsingers** are the original native people of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], ancient [singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers") that predated the arrival of humans and the [[Shards]] on the planet.[^2] [^1]

## Legends

Like other ancient Rosharan concepts, [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") legends obfuscated the true nature of the Dawnsingers for centuries, if not millennia. The ardentia believe them to be healing [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] sent by the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty") to care for humans.[^3] They are also said to be the origin of [[Glyphs - The Coppermind|glyphs]] and letters.[^3]

Some [lighteyes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lighteyed "Lighteyed") believe that certain cities known as " [Dawncities](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawncities "Dawncities") " can trace their origins back to the Dawnsingers. These claims seem to stem from the spectacular organic architecture that mirrors specific [cymatic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cymatics "Cymatics") patterns in cities such as [[Sesemalex Dar - The Coppermind|Sesemalex Dar]] and surpasses known human capabilities, but there is no direct evidence of the origin of these cities.[^4]

## History

Roshar is known to have been designed and created by [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]] [^5] [^6] [^7] and the Dawnsingers were the planet's earliest inhabitants. The specifics of their creation are not known. The Dawnsingers began worshipping Adonalsium as the **Origin of Songs**. After the Shattering, the Shards of Honor and Cultivation arrived on the planet. The Dawnsingers realised that they were foreign, and debated whether or not they should be worshipped. Honor, feeling horrible for killing their god, granted them [the power to shape stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stoneshaping "Stoneshaping") using their songs.[^8] [^9]

The discovery of the [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele") refuted many of the legends surrounding the Dawnsingers. The stele contained the oldest known text on Roshar, written by a Dawnsinger in their native language known as [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]].[^1] [^10] It recounted the arrival of humans to Roshar after they destroyed their home planet of [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]],[^1] [^11] possibly using a [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]] in some way.[^12] The Dawnsingers took pity on the humans and gave them the land that became [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]].[^12] [^11]

After the humans arrived, they betrayed the Dawnsingers and even turned their gods "of spren, stone, and wind" against them; this presumably referred to the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] being granted to humans, which motivated the spren to begin forming [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bonds]] with them.[^13] [^1] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] was involved in these events, and the Dawnsingers considered him a god of the humans.[^14] The Dawnsingers believed that Odium sucked in all emotion, and therefore named the humans the " [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] ".[^1] Relations between singers and humans were peaceful at first, but Odium’s influence exacerbated growing tensions between the two peoples. Humans didn’t easily adapt to Roshar, struggling to grow plants from Ashyn in the crem-laced mud of their new home. Tensions were further exacerbated by the Dawnsingers refusing to allow humans to spread eastward unless they agreed to be second-class citizens under the singers. Eventually, a group of humans stole from and killed a singer, and singers killed the humans in reprisal.[^15] Despite efforts by [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] and the singer envoy [Elodi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elodi "Elodi"), conflict continued to escalate.[^16]

The First Desolation wasn’t a continent-spanning war, but a series of bloody skirmishes as humans sought to steal land from the Dawnsingers. Wearing furs and wielding weapons of stone or carapace, the humans aggressively attacked the singers. It was then that Odium saw an opportunity: manipulating certain singers, he encouraged their mistrust of humans, turning anger at their encroachment onto singer lands into a desire for vengeance and retribution, eventually offering them immortality and making them into his [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]]. After the creation of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), the Fused were locked away and the Dawnsingers lost the war.[^16] [^17]

After the revelations of the Eila Stele, some members of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Radiant]] [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Order of Skybreakers]] led by [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] determined that the Dawnsingers remained the rightful rulers of Roshar. Because Skybreakers were obligated to enforce the prevailing law, Nale and his followers planned to swear to the Dawnsingers and their laws instead of the local codes put in place by humans.[^18]

## Legacy

The Dawnsingers are the ancestors of modern singers, including the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]]. Some of the songs of the listeners—including the [Song of Spren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Spren "Song of Spren"), the [Song of Secrets](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Secrets "Song of Secrets"), and the [Song of Histories](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Song_of_Histories "Song of Histories") —mention the ancient history of the singers. They refer to the perceived betrayal perpetrated by humans and spren, and go on to mention the adoption of [[Regal - The Coppermind|forms of power]] to help fight back (and some unintended consequences).[^19] [^20] [^21] However, this history is "fraught" and the songs are not entirely consistent.[^22] They also taught early humans to speak the Dawnchant, influencing the development of human languages on Roshar.[^23]
