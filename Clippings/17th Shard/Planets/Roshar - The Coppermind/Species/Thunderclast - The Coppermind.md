## Thunderclast

> “ *Those aren’t stones. They’re creatures.* ”
> 
> — [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] [^2]

**Thunderclasts** are three giant stone monsters from [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that serve [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] during the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^3] [^4] [^5]

## Appearance and Nature

![[Thunderclast---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [botanicaxu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu "Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu")

[[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] confronting a thunderclast at the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]]

> “ *Even after all these centuries, seeing a thunderclast up close made Kalak shiver.* ”
> 
> — [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] [^3]

Thunderclasts are enormous stone beasts, easily five or six times larger than a person, approximately 30 feet tall, with their hands alone being six feet long.[^2] [^3] They take a skeletal, humanoid form, with long limbs that end with spiky, clawed fingers. On their chests, they have rib-like protrusions, and there are ridges running along their backs.[^6] Their heads are narrow and arrow-like, with glowing red eyes.[^6] [^7]

The thunderclast proper, however, is a [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]], significantly larger and more twisted than a [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] soul.[^6] [^1] They appear as a large shadow with red eyes.[^8] When they desire to take on a physical form, they sink into the stone and animate it, ripping themselves out in the humanoid form and leaving behind them a weirdly shaped hollow in the stone.[^4] [^3] As such, they retain some qualities of the material they take on, like the color or a covering of moss or fungus.[^6]

## Abilities and Attributes

> “ *Thunderclast! Hammers! Ready hammers!* ”
> 
> —Soldiers hunting thunderclasts on the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]] [^6]

Thunderclasts are able to sink into the stone and animate it, ripping themselves out in the humanoid form and leaving behind them a weirdly shaped hollow in the stone.[^4] [^3] They have trouble forming from worked or covered stone.[^9]

Despite their enormous size, thunderclasts have no problems staying upright, and can swing their body around with incredible force, easily toppling regular fortifications.[^10] Though brute force is their greatest strength, they're also capable of precise actions such as throwing boulders,[^11] or carrying regular [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] in their hands without crushing them.[^10]

Despite being made of the same material as their targets, they do not crack, and it seems they cannot be destroyed by ordinary means, although hammers are given as a viable option for combating them.[^10] [^6] Another viable way to deal with them is to cut them up with [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") to the point where they're no longer mobile, although this carries significant risk to both people and their surroundings. Indeed, [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] theorizes that killing thunderclasts was in fact the original purpose of Shardblades, an idea mirrored by Szeth's thoughts on them.[^12] [^13]

Thunderclast can be sent back to the Cognitive Realm by severing their spine where the neck meets the head.[^14] It can take them weeks of healing before they can reenter the Physical Realm. If they willingly leave their stone body, they can make another one in just a few days.[^1] [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] can kill them for good.[^15]

Although warped and twisted, thunderclasts seem to retain some level of personality, enough so to answer to names and have a concrete sense of their gender. While they can obey commands, however, they do not seem capable of communicating themselves.[^15] They are capable of making earth-shaking roars and trumpeting sounds to express their emotions, though.[^13]

## History

The thunderclasts were created by Odium thousands of years before the True Desolation, and date back to at least [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon's]] times.[^16] [^2] They were created from the souls of ancient singers.[^16]

People back then knew to look out for them, and sent out hunting parties, although only [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] were capable of fighting them.[^6] They were powerful enough to pose dangers even to the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), with [Kelek](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kelek "Kelek") in particular dying to them several times.[^3] After the [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam"), however, thunderclasts passed on into myths. By the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], there were numerous stories about [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] with bodies of stone, likely as a cultural memory of the thunderclasts.[^17] [^18]

Thunderclasts appeared a few times in [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions"). When he met [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]], he saw numerous corpses of them littering the battlefield and, in a separate vision in which he participated in a hunt for a corrupted spren on the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]], he witnessed one rip itself out of the ground.[^2] [^6] However, they didn't make their proper debut in the modern era until the [Siege of Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Siege_of_Kholinar "Siege of Kholinar"), when at least one thunderclast participated in the main attack, easily breaking down the city wall and decimating numerous buildings in its wake.[^11] [^19]

In the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], Odium summoned two thunderclasts -- [Kai-garnis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kai-garnis "Kai-garnis") and [Yushah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yushah "Yushah"). After Yushah broke through the wall, he sent her after the [Thaylen Gemstone Reserve](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_Gemstone_Reserve "Thaylen Gemstone Reserve") while Kai-garnis went to assist the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] in taking the [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]]. Both were eventually defeated; Yushah was destroyed permanently by [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] with [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]],[^20] while Kai-garnis was immobilized by [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] and fled her stone body.[^15] [^13]

### Azimir and Narak

In the lead-up to the contest of champions, Odium deploys both remaining thunderclasts, Kai-garnis and [Terushal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Terushal "Terushal"). The former participated in the initial assault in the Second Battle of Narak, while the latter was sent through Shadesmar to attack Azimir.

Kai-garnis was used by the Fused army to get through the walls defending Narak Four. While [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]] and the Stonewards eventually defeated her, the damage was done, with the thunderclast punching a massive hole through the wall and the corpse forming a bridge through the wall for the armies to get through.[^21]

Terushal was deployed at Azimir on the same day the Fused forces broke through and took the city. Adolin and his Honor Guard made a valiant attempt to stop the thunderclast, but they were all but decimated, with [Neziham](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Neziham "Neziham") being crushed. Adolin attempted to trip the thunderclast but was stopped, his chain snapped in half.[^9] Adolin got on his horse despite his injuries and went over to the thunderclast, defeating it.[^22] However, in the process his leg was crushed, becoming a stump after it was healed by [Rahel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rahel "Rahel"), who didn't have the experience to heal the full foot.[^23]
