## Old Magic

> “ *When this world was created, long before [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], or [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] arrived, [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]] left something behind on it. Sometimes it’s called the Old Magic.* ”
> 
> — [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] [^1]

The **Old Magic** is a manifestation of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that is known to predate spren bonds.[^2] It has a deep connection to and association with the old gods of Roshar, the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind"), the [Stone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone "Stone"), and the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night"), who are said to embody the Old Magic, but it has since become synonymous with the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]].[^1] [^3]

While there are other unknown elements to the Old Magic, no one on Roshar has been confirmed to know or make use of it.[^4] As such, the most prevalent form of Old Magic comes in the form of boons and curses from the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]]: a person can gain a single unique ability from the Old Magic, but are also given a unique curse along with their boon, which may or may not be related.[^5] [Khriss's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") writings in the [Ars Arcanum](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ars_Arcanum "Ars Arcanum") states that it is cousin to [[Voidbinding - The Coppermind|Voidbinding]].[^6]

## History

> “ *There were echoes, of my predecessor. Little bits of him left behind. Three powerful incarnations who had his voice, and many smaller ones representing aspects of nature and personality....Shadows of divinity with instructions to protect, to shroud, to nurture. One sang to me, in particular, and that invigorated me, though I did not know why the Wind was chosen to protect. Wind, invisible Wind, so flighty and immaterial.*”
> 
> — [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] Memories [^7]

The Old Magic was left by Adonalsium on Roshar after creating the planet prior to the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]].[^1] It is embodied in the Wind, the Stone, and the Night, and from it were the eldest spren of Roshar born.[^3] [^7] When they were created by Adonalsium, they were given instructions to protect, shroud, and nurture the life of Roshar.[^7]

Over time, the Singers began to worship the Stone and Wind,[^8] [^9] [^10] though they did not worship the Night.[^9] This would eventually lead to the Night leaving Roshar.[^11]

Bondsmiths, in the days before the modern Bondsmith spren, bonded to these old gods.[^11]

At some point, the Shards would make the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] from the Wind, and the Nightwatcher from the Night. The [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] would be made from the Stone intermixed with the Investiture of Honor and Cultivation around six thousand years before the Night of Sorrows.[^11]

Eventually, the Old Magic became culturally synonymous with the Nightwatcher, who was created from the Night.[^1] [^3]

## Mechanics

To gain a boon and a curse from the Old Magic, a person has to seek the Nightwatcher. In very rare cases, a boon and curse can be given by [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]].[^12] [^13]

Anyone is allowed to seek a single boon from the Nightwatcher. To do this, the person must first travel to the [Valley](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valley "Valley") in the west, where the Nightwatcher resides. After finding the Nightwatcher, the person may request a boon. The precise phrasing of the request does not appear to make a difference, however, since the Nightwatcher bestows a boon that she feels the person deserves.[^5] The Old Magic can be given to any sapient species, including ones not from Roshar.[^14] A person's boon and curse stay with them for life and will stay with them if they leave Roshar.[^8] [^14] The boons and curses are limited in a particular manner.[^15] According to [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]], those who have the Old Magic have a certain "smell".[^16] Many view the Old Magic as inscrutable.[^17]

There is more to Old Magic than just boons and curses; however, there is no person currently known to make use of that.[^4]

Cultivation will sometimes intervene, and grant her own boons and curses in a far more calculated manner, and may use the Old Magic to distract from her own interference, possibly even creating the phenomenon.[^18] [^19]

### Boon

The Nightwatcher claims to have the ability to grant almost any physical request one might ask of her, though just because she can does not mean she will give what is asked for. She claims to be able to give many things such as renown, wealth, skill, the ability to swing a sword and never tire, beauty, followers, even glory. She even claims she can give a person physical possessions such as spheres, gemstones, [Shards](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardwielding "Shardwielding"), or a [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Blade that bleeds darkness and cannot be defeated]].[^13] The Nightwatcher does have trouble with extremely nebulous requests relating to human emotion.[^13] The Nightwatcher is also capable of changing a person's species or eye color,[^20] [^21] and can alter a person's DNA or [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|spiritweb]].[^22]

### Curse

Contrary to popular belief, it is not possible to avoid the curse by carefully phrasing a request -- or even to predict the curse that the Nightwatcher will bestow.[^5] Interestingly, it does not appear that balance is carefully maintained through an individual blessing and curse -- often, highly visible curses are the result of relatively minor boons. The curses can be quite thorough, however -- in one case, a man lost all feeling in both of his hands until the day he died. Whether these curses follow some cosmic justice, or are merely the result of a capricious spren, is unknown. Many people throughout centuries have tried to find ways of dealing with their curse and a cure for them, with no successful attempts.[^17] The only person we know of who was able to get rid of their curse is Dalinar, but that was part of the curse itself, that he would have to deal with Evi's loss eventually.[^23] [^13]

## Development

Brandon built the Old Magic into *[The Stormlight Archive](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Stormlight_Archive "The Stormlight Archive")* because he felt that at a certain point he wanted there to be a contrast to rule-based magic systems. He wanted there to be a primal magic that didn't really adhere to the rules, one that couldn't be anticipated. Brandon felt that it was vital to include so that he didn't overexplain everything in the books. Characters, in some ways, can be given wish fulfillment, but even that can turn on its head because of the Old Magic as it doesn't give people what they think should have gotten.[^24]

## Trivia

- It has been confirmed that Dalinar, Lift, and Taravangian all received their boons and curses directly from Cultivation, rather than from the Nightwatcher as is the norm.[^18] Brandon has also stated that this is of particular note, but has yet to say how.
