## Oathpact

> “ *And thus we had an agreement. They swore to me, and I gave them the largest portion of myself that I would ever grant.*”
> 
> —Honor, explaining the Oathpact to [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]].[^1]

The **Oathpact** is an ancient agreement sworn to [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] by the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of [The Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Almighty "The Almighty") which grants them [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] and access to [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] to protect [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] and trap the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] in between [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^2]

## History

### Founding the Oathpact

At the time of the [First Desolation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/First_Desolation "First Desolation"), while weakened from previous battles with [[Shards]] and caged to the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system") by an agreement of accommodation with [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]],[^3] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] moved to increase the strength of his followers on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. Granting immortality and frighteningly destructive power to a number of [Singers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Singers "Singers") who had fallen in his service, Odium created the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], soldiers that could be reborn when killed and could manipulate the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]], empowered by his own [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]]. He inspired the Fused to cause destruction and overtake the planet, in an effort to provoke direct action against him by his captors.

Driven by the suffering of the people of Roshar and fearful of what Odium would do once he had fully conquered, Honor knew he had to intervene, though he refused to endanger the planet in a destructive battle between the two Shards themselves. Failing to secure assistance from other Shards (who were uniformly loath to act against Odium while he was bound on Roshar, and thus prevented from wreaking havoc across the entire [[17th Shard|cosmere]]),[^1] Honor chose to offer a portion of his power to humans as he had once before, desperate to find a way to prevent Odium's followers from destroying this planet like he had destroyed [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]].

Forty-three years into the First Desolation,[^4] [^5] Honor placed his hopes in the leaders of the humans who had escaped from Ashyn nearly a hundred years before, meeting them in their warcamp east of the [[Misted Mountains - The Coppermind|Misted Mountains]]. [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]], one of these leaders, presented them with a plan. Having already been [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] of Odium on that planet, most of them shared a [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] that could be exploited to bind the Shard's power even further, and prevent his followers from continuing to overrun Roshar. An oath spoken to Honor could be used to grant the Surges again to a number of willing humans. This new force, now directly Connected to Honor, could use his power and the well of souls located on the nearby planet of [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] to bind the souls of the Fused when they were killed, preventing them from being reborn on Roshar.[^6]

> “ *With this Oathpact, we will create a method to lock them away on another world. We will close a loophole that has allowed Odium to meddle, and we will provide champions to stand against the tide of darkness.*”
> 
> —Honor, describing the Oathpact to Taln [^7]

Alongside Ishar, Honor met with [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]], [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]], [[Chanarach - The Coppermind|Chanarach]], [[Vedel - The Coppermind|Vedel]], [[Pralla - The Coppermind|Pralla]], [[Shalash - The Coppermind|Shalash]], [[Battar - The Coppermind|Battar]], [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]], and, eventually, [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]], convincing each in turn that, though he was not perfect and certainly shared responsibility for the events that led to the terrible destruction of their home world, together they could use his power to safeguard their new home on Roshar and prevent another cataclysm from occurring.

Five men and five women entered into the Oathpact with Honor, numbered to his design. To each, he gave a piece of himself, a splinter of [Light](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Light "Light") that formed into an [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]], bonding them to his power and granting access to the Surges, making them [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of the Almighty.[^7]

Using Honor's Connection to the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]], the Herald Vedel then sealed their souls at their current ages, granting them immortality.[^6]

### The Desolations

*Main article:* [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]]

Armed with Honorblades and in command of the Surges, the Heralds led the humans of Roshar in war against Odium's Fused, driving them back and depleting their numbers. Once enough of the Fused were eliminated, or if they themselves fell in battle, each of the Heralds travelled to Braize where, gathering together, they could use the power of the Oathpact to bind the souls of the dead and prevent any further resurrections. With the Fused jailed far away, the rest of humanity could put an end to the conflict.

After a time trapped on that prison planet, however, the Fused discovered that their jailers were also the key to their freedom. If one or more of the Heralds could be captured and put to torture, their resolve could be weakened, just like any human's, until they eventually broke, no longer able to maintain their part of the soul binding. The Fused could then break free of their captivity and return to Roshar, bent on destruction.

Thus the Heralds and the Fused found themselves locked in a cycle of terrible, escalating war, commonly referred to as the Desolations. Each time a Herald would break under torture, the Fused would be reborn into the bodies of willing Singers, gathering armies to destroy the nations and settlements that had been established in their absence. The Heralds would return as well, attempting to quickly rebuild civilization and lead the battle until the Fused could be locked away again, closing the cycle.

In the beginning, the Heralds held strong and hundreds of years passed between Desolations, granting the populations of humans and singers time to recover.[^8] Leaning on their hundreds of years of experience, the Heralds would use the brief time after each return to teach the fledgling human populations necessary skills, such as metalworking, medicine, and how to make war.[^9] But after millennia of nearly constant battle on Roshar and torture on Braize,[^8] [^10] the Heralds began to lose their will, breaking faster each time. In the end, there was less than a year between Desolations, leaving no time to rebuild society before the forces of Odium came to destroy again.[^8]

### Aharietiam

*Main article:* [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]]

The weight of these thousands of years of fighting took its toll on the Heralds, who could no longer bear to continue as they were. Though the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] had formed and aided humanity in the Desolations, Odium had not been idle. Continuing to escalate the war, he granted more and more terrible forms of power to his subjects and perverted powerful [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] to create the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] and [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclasts]], who rampaged across Roshar, killing soldier and civilian alike. Meanwhile Honor, their god, had begun to grow distant and unreliable. To meet these new challenges, the Heralds were forced to draw upon even more of his power, eventually Connecting themselves to the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]] of Roshar itself.[^11]

> “ *It has been decided, Kalak. We will go our ways, and we will not seek out one another. Our Blades must be left. The Oathpact ends now.*”
> 
> —Jezrien, commanding Kalak to relinquish his Honorblade.[^2]

Though they had had little time to prepare for it, by chance a Desolation came to a close with victory over Odium's forces leaving all but one of the Heralds still standing on Roshar. Unwilling to let this opportunity slip away, the remaining Heralds quickly came to a decision, petitioning Honor himself to release them from the Oathpact. They pointed out the worst of its effects, begging for him to modify their responsibilities or at least find a way to lessen their nearly constant torment. Recoiling at their explicit desire to break their oaths, Honor begrudgingly admitted the possibility that the Oathpact could be bent to continue holding the Fused on Braize if at least one Herald would be willing to stand in place of them all. Turning his back on their weakness, Honor declared that he would no longer intervene. They could do as they pleased.[^12]

End of the Oathpact

To their shame, nine Heralds abandoned their Honorblades, rammed through stone into a ring of swords. Taln, the single Herald who had fallen in the battle, was left on Braize to uphold the Oathpact, alone.[^2]

### The True Desolation

*Main article:* [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]]

For over four thousand years, the worst of Odium's forces were confined to Braize by Taln's singular refusal to break, seemingly ending the Desolations and allowing humanity to rebuild and conquer over the Singers. As the years passed, the Heralds eventually fell into legend, deified in the eyes of the burgeoning [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] religion and venerated by their apparent victory over the so-called [Voidbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Voidbringers "Voidbringers") so long ago.

Still immortal, the Heralds stayed out of public view, falling deeper into madness and suffering in obscurity. Though they could fall in battle, the years had not aged their bodies. Only their minds betrayed the passing of time, becoming more and more fragile.[^13]

After the Herald Chana eventually settled in the [Valam princedom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valam_princedom "Valam princedom") of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"), her particular insanity led to tragedy, when she was killed in self-defense by her eleven-year-old daughter, [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]].[^14] Returning to Braize for the first time in millennia, Chana attempted to evade capture by the Fused, but was caught and broken,[^15] allowing the beginning of the True Desolation.

During the [Alethi's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]], Taln was also released from his torture, appearing suddenly outside the city gates of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar").[^16] Though he came with word of an imminent Desolation and calls to prepare, his warnings fell on deaf ears and he was taken for a madman. At the same time, [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|Voidspren]] that had escaped Braize were able to convince modern [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] to summon the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]], granting themselves a permanent, reliable method of returning to Roshar and greatly weakening the Oathpact's effectiveness.[^17] [^18] Another Desolation had come, and with it armies of Fused bent on conquering once again.

When Kholinar itself was besieged, the traitor [Vyre](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vyre "Vyre") assassinated the Herald Jezrien using a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") [knife](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vyre%27s_knife "Vyre's knife") fitted with a large sapphire in the hilt,[^19] a weapon that had been designed specifically to kill a Herald. This death, the first time one of them had been truly killed, was felt by each of the remaining Heralds as though a part of their own soul had been ripped away.[^19] [^20] The Oathpact was broken.

### Reforging the Oathpact

> “ *I have the answer, a way to fix the problems that beset us. Come to me in Shinovar. I can reset the Oathpact, though I must be sane to do it. I must... have help... to...*”
> 
> —Ishar, in a brief moment of lucidity, asking Dalinar to help reforge the Oathpact.[^20]

As the war against Odium's forces pressed on, [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] worked from the newly recovered city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] to unite the kingdoms of humanity in an alliance led by the re-emergent Knights Radiant, and defend Roshar. Soon, Dalinar discovered that the mad, god-priest [Tezim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tezim "Tezim"), ruler of [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar"), was in truth the Herald Ishar,[^21] now back in possession of his Honorblade and demanding delivery of the ancient city of Radiants into his control. Traveling to Tukar to face him personally, Dalinar was humbled by the sheer power wielded by the Herald, nearly losing his [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] to the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] in the confrontation.[^20] Witnessing Ishar's insanity break long enough to learn that the Oathpact could be restored, Dalinar enlisted the Radiants [Kaladin Stormblessed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kaladin_Stormblessed "Kaladin Stormblessed") and [Szeth-son-son-Vallano](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Szeth-son-son-Vallano "Szeth-son-son-Vallano") to travel to [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] and see it done.[^22]

As the two made their way through the land of Szeth's past, it became clear that they had both been long expected. Per Ishar's design, Szeth had been prepared for this moment all his life, freed of his personal connections and ready to be forced to take up Jezrien's former position in the Oathpact.[^23] [^24] Kaladin, meanwhile, was called there by the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind") itself,[^25] one of the ancient powers of Roshar that dated back to the time of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]].[^26]

Confronting Ishar together, Szeth used [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] to defeat the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] [Honorbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorbearers "Honorbearers") Ishar had corrupted [^27] while Kaladin, having been Connected to Ishar during the fray,[^28] spoke the [Windrunners'](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") [Fifth Ideal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knights_Radiant/Immortal_Words "Knights Radiant/Immortal Words"),[^29] dispelling the dark power the ancient Herald had long ago absorbed into himself from the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control").[^30] Removing this darkness from Ishar finally began the healing of the Heralds, who had all been tainted by their Connection to him via the Oathpact.[^31]

> “ *Kaladin distinctly thought he heard, echoing through that failing bond, the gasps of eight other people as an unacknowledged darkness left them. An oppressive cloud that Ishar thought he'd been holding back, but had in reality been infecting every Herald. The blackness he'd absorbed from Odium centuries ago, by finding his pool of power.* ”
> 
> —Kaladin, on the beginning of the healing of the Heralds [^31]

After the death of the Stormfather and the merging of the Shards of Honor and Odium into [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]], all of Honor's expressed power on the planet began to be reclaimed by the new, more powerful deity.[^24] To save themselves and all of the spren of Roshar from being consumed or fashioned into Unmade, Ishar and Nale followed the Wind's urging to draw on their deep Connection to Honor to attempt to bind Retribution from doing so, just as they had been able to bind Odium in the past. His mind finally clear and seeing no other choice available, Ishar reforged the Oathpact with this new purpose, joined by all of the remaining, living Heralds and Kaladin, who stepped before the wounded and comatose Szeth, volunteering as a new Herald and taking the place of Jezrien.[^24]

## Mechanics

> “ *I will take back your Surges, then grant them anew, and together you will become a force that both protects Roshar and binds the enemy away from it* ”
> 
> —Honor, offering the Oathpact.[^6]

### Oaths Sworn

The Oathpact is a magically binding covenant founded by [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] and spoken between the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), ten Rosharan humans, most of whom were originally refugees of the planet [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]].[^6] Willingly giving up the unchecked, dangerous [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] abilities previously granted to them on their former homeworld, the Heralds swear to protect the planet of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] by holding back the darkness.[^6] This oath allows the Heralds direct access to Honor's [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] and the ability to use a near-infinite supply of his [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]].[^32] [^6]

Their special connection to Honor's power allows the Heralds to use a method devised by [[Vedel - The Coppermind|Vedel]] to seal their souls to their current age, making them effectively immortal.[^6] Though they can be killed, their [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadows]] are reborn in bodies made entirely of Investiture.[^33]

To seal the Oathpact, each of the Heralds are granted an [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]]. Formed out of [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of Honor's own [Light](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Light "Light"),[^7] these blades provide the Heralds new, controlled access to the Surges, now governed by Honor's will to prevent the destruction of Roshar and following a set of rules crafted by the Shard [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]].[^6]

The Oathpact leverages the Heralds' pre-existing Connection to the Shard [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] —a product of their dealings with him on Ashyn— to enable them to bind the cognitive shadows of his [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] killed in battle to the well of souls on the planet [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], and prevent their immediate return to Roshar after death.[^6] [^34]

> “ *We form a binding through our willpower, holding the others we are Connected to - our replacements in a way - to Braize. Impossible, save for the strange nature of that planet lending our ten oaths a multiplicative strength.*”
> 
> —Jezrien, explaining the Oathpact's binding of the Fused to Dalinar, in a vision of the past.[^12]

The Heralds are able to bind the Fused to Braize by a special act of will that they perform, making use of Honor's expressed power to enforce their combined oaths to trap the souls of Odium's most Invested followers. For this to work, it is necessary for the [number](https://coppermind.net/edit/Numerology?redlink=1 "Numerology (page does not exist)") of these oaths to be closely aligned with the Shard of Honor itself; to be most effective, the participants of the Oathpact must be numbered at sixteen (for the number of Shards of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]]), ten (for Honor's own number), four (for the number of Adonalsium's aspects), or one (for the number of Adonalsium itself).[^12]

Once the Fused trapped on Braize succeed in capturing a Herald and forcing them to break under torture, the soul binding that the Heralds are performing can no longer be maintained. The Fused are then free to begin to be reborn on Roshar once again, and another [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]] begins.[^8]

To account for Odium's forces continually gaining strength and escalating their attacks during the Desolations, Honor pushes the Heralds to draw ever-increasing amounts of his own power, eventually leading them to Connect to the ancient [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]] of Roshar itself, left over from the days of Adonalsium. Driven by an increasing desperation as the wars drag on for millennia, the Heralds begin to develop abilities Honor does not intend or fully understand, though he finds this acceptable as it allows for him to continue taking a hands-off approach to the conflict.[^11]

### Honor Broken

When the majority of the Heralds ultimately make a decision to forsake their place in the Oathpact and relinquish a small part of their Honor,[^12] the Oathpact is heavily damaged but not fully broken. Leaving their Honorblades behind causes the Heralds to lose command of the Surges, but they still continue to retain their immortality, as well as their Connection to Honor's power and to each other.[^35] [^36]

Living for many thousands of years, the Heralds endure maladies of the mind unique to their ancient life experience,[^13] tortured by private pains that affect the way they see reality.[^36] Often, they are gripped by periods of particularly strong madness or weakness,[^19] [^37] causing them to act out irrationally or behave in ways that make them appear simple or insane.[^38] [^13] Though moments of relative calm and surprising lucidity can also be observed, especially when the Heralds find themselves nearby a [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knight Radiant]] swearing one of the [Immortal Words](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knights_Radiant/Immortal_Words "Knights Radiant/Immortal Words").[^39] [^22]

The Oathpact is still visible to a [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith"), even in its broken state, evidenced by lines of Connection networking the Heralds together. Though the lines are weak and impotent for most of the Heralds, one powerful, vibrant line still Connects [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]],[^36] as seen by [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] when he is confronted by [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] during a battle in [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir").[^36] This Connection allows the Heralds to both magically aid one another, such as bolstering each other's ability to withstand physical pain and mental anguish,[^28] as well as to harm one another, such as when the Heralds find their private insanities greatly increased after Ishar absorbs some of Odium's power from the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control").[^30] [^31]

[[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] death is the event that finally fractures the Oathpact, preventing the Heralds from successfully binding Odium's forces and ensuring that the Fused can leave Braize at will, via the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]].[^40] [^41] Even if the Oathpact was fully functioning, the Everstorm would have rendered it useless regardless.[^18] [^42] [^43]

### Circle Reforged

> “ *If the spren are to be preserved, if a Splinter of Honor is to be kept from Retribution's touch, we must stand tall again. Reaffirm our oaths, exploit that weakness he made in himself for us.* ”
> 
> —Ishar, founder of the Oathpact.[^24]

[[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin's]] successful removal of the influence of Odium's Investiture that has been infecting the Heralds [^31] coupled with the Shards of Honor and Odium being temporarily diverted by changing [Vessels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel"),[^27] finally sets Ishar free to reset the Oathpact and improve upon its original design. Devising a plan with the urging of the Wind, Ishar recruits the assistance of [[Shalash - The Coppermind|Ash]] and [[Pralla - The Coppermind|Pralla]] to remove the minds of the Heralds into a [vision](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Visions "Visions") when they return to Braize. Mimicking the process that the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] uses to grant visions, this method allows time for the Heralds to begin healing their madness and avoid the worst of any torture their souls may have to endure.

[[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution's]] subsequent attempt to absorb all of the spren on Roshar made up of Honor or Odium's Investiture is prevented by the new focus of the Oathpact, sworn by the surviving Heralds mere moments after his [Ascension](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ascension "Ascension").

This protection of the spren is made possible by the power created through a circle of ten combined oaths, including the newly elevated Kaladin, as Jezrien's replacement in the Oathpact. The Heralds make use of their unbroken, potent Connection to Honor to bind Retribution from acting against the spren, just as they had bound some of Odium's actions with the strength of their prior oaths.[^24]
