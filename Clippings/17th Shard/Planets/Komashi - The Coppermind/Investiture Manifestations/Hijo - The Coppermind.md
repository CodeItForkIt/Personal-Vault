## Hijo

The **primal hijo**, also known as **primal spirits**, or simply as **hijo**, are [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]] of [[Virtuosity - The Coppermind|Virtuosity]] found on [[Komashi - The Coppermind|Komashi]].[^1] They naturally reside underground, and are attracted by thoughts, emotions, and artistic expressions.[^1] They can be bound by a [[Yoki-hijo - The Coppermind|yoki-hijo]] into useful tools and devices,[^2] or be utilized to produce and supply [[Hion - The Coppermind|hion]].[^3]

## Appearance

Hijo typically take on a teardrop-shaped form roughly the size of a person's head.[^2] They glow brightly like molten metal with a swirl of red and blue colors.[^2] [^4] They have no eyes or other discernible features, though they do seem to sense their surroundings and are able to speak audibly and telepathically.[^2] Bound hijo do not speak.[^5] The spirits which are bound to the [[Father machine - The Coppermind|father machine]] have an ice-like appearance, and undulate to an inaudible rhythm.[^6]

## Behavior

![[Hijo---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [TheNerdyAlchemist](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/TheNerdyAlchemist "Coppermind:Artists/TheNerdyAlchemist")

The hijo are related to Komashi's strange geothermal activity, apparently being directly responsible for the intense heating of the ground in the region of [[Torio - The Coppermind|Torio]].[^2] Their level of activity seems to vary somewhat. They are especially active every thirteenth year during the [Festival of Reveals](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Festival_of_Reveals "Festival of Reveals").[^7]

Primal spirits are attracted to human thoughts and emotions expressed through art, in accordance with the Intent of Virtuosity.[^1] [^8] Many different forms of art can attract their attention, though not all are known to do so.[^9] Though painting appears to be one of them, it could indeed draw Hijo, and it is simply the commodification of the art through [[Painter - The Coppermind|Painters]], which minimizes the pure artistry of painting, which causes it to fail to draw Hijo.[^10] A musical performance such as a rock concert, for example, could summon spirits.[^11] In Torio, a yoki-hijo stacking stones was known to be the best way to attract the spirits.[^9] Following their freedom from the [[Father machine - The Coppermind|father machine]], the hijo have also shown interest in historical dramas and perhaps other [hion viewer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hion_viewer "Hion viewer") programs.[^3] Some locations prove better for attracting the spirits than others, such as a [place of ritual](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Place_of_ritual "Place of ritual"), but they can be attracted elsewhere.[^9]

According to [[Yumi - The Coppermind|Yumi]], yoki-hijo must typically create twenty stacks of approximately thirty stones each in order to summon the spirits, though smaller stacks are also sufficient if they have increased complexity.[^12]

### Boons

Stories speak of the spirits granting boons, which are always associated with adventure. In practice there is little direct contact between spirits and humans aside from the yoki-hijo and so the truth of these stories is difficult to discern. One spirit is able to create a unique [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] between Yumi and [[Nikaro - The Coppermind|Nikaro]], however, suggesting the spirits are perhaps capable of these mythological abilities.[^13]

Body Swapping

Hijo are able to form a Spiritual Connection between two individuals and have them swap bodies with each other.[^14] [^6] [^15] Those linked will swap bodies every day, where one is in control of the other's body while the other is a Cognitive Shadow.[^16] [^17] Despite seeing themselves as normal, those around them will see and feel the other person's body.[^18] If a person is highly Invested, they can instead rewrite the other's body to match their form instead.[^17] [^1] While linked, they will understand each other's languages.[^19] [^20] They may also share certain abilities, such as the abilities of a yoki-hijo.[^21] [^22]

The process takes a strong physical toll, and requires them to sleep much more than a normal human would.[^23] [^24] While the two are with one body, the other will sleep.[^25] The amount of sleep needed lessens with time before averaging to roughly twelve hours asleep per day.[^25] [^26] [^14] When the two make physical contact, they will feel a rush of emotions and heat from each other, further Connecting their souls.[^19] [^27] [^28]

When a Cognitive Shadow, they cannot normally be seen or heard, and will pass through most objects and people.[^16] This is affected by their perception, and they may be able to interact with certain objects or the souls of objects with practice.[^18] [^25] [^9] They are also protected from having their memories manipulated.[^15] The Cognitive Shadow cannot move more than a few yards away from the other person, but this can be extended.[^19] [^29] [^9]

This Connection can be forcibly severed, causing the two to no longer swap bodies.[^14] Despite this, a Connection between them will still exist, and they will be able to speak telepathically and feel each other's emotions.[^15] [^20]

## Uses

The people of Komashi rely on the hijo for their ways of life, and are able to use these spirits in several ways.

### Bound Spirits

When a yoki-hijo summons a spirit, they may request that the spirit be bound into some device or tool. They do this by offering a measure of their own Investiture along with their artwork and mentally projecting an idea of what they wish the spirit to become. If this is not done properly, the spirit may become confused or be frightened off. If successful, the stack of stones becomes strengthened, and the spirit transforms.[^2] The scholars of the [Institute of Mechanical Solutions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Institute_of_Mechanical_Solutions "Institute of Mechanical Solutions") are able to bind spirits by showing them pictures of the things they wish them to become.[^4]

Bound spirits typically take on either a stone or metal form, and always seem to be divided into two parts.[^2] [^1] Spirits typically remain bound for five to ten years.[^2] In general, the more spirits that are bound in a session, the longer all of them will last.[^2] Spirits bound by the prototype machine are generally smaller, seem to have operational issues much sooner, and may not remain bound as long.[^21] This is likely due to the machine consuming a portion of the spirit's own Investiture in order to operate.[^21]

Common types of bound spirits include:

Repelling statues

Involves two statues, one female and one male, with grotesque features.[^7] These statues behave similarly to [[Fabrial - The Coppermind#Reversers|reverser fabrials]], with movement and forces of one affecting the other in an opposite manner. These are most often used to levitate vehicles or structures above the hot ground in Torio.[^2] In some cases, however, they may even simply be used for religious purposes--to be put on display to give people comfort by reminding them that the spirits are watching over them.[^27]

Spirit lights

The spirit divides into two balls that fit into the palm of a hand: one a bright orange and the other a dull blue.[^2] When the balls are separate, they glow, and are commonly used as a light source in Torio.[^2] Touching the balls together turns the lights off.[^13]

Flyers

These devices include the flyer itself--a hovering device approximately two feet across with insectile wings--and a handheld controller portion.[^2] These are commonly used in Torio to corral plants and to drive threats away.[^16]

Spirit saw

Presumably a simple device for sawing. It is unclear what the two halves of the device are.[^2]

### Hion

With some measure of human intervention, the spirits are able to produce and power the hion lines which sustain modern life on Komashi.[^6] The original hion lines are produced by the machine, which harvests the Investiture of the hijo to create hion lines emerging from the ground. After the father machine is destroyed, some hijo voluntarily continue to power these hion lines.[^3]

## History

In Yumi's time, the hijo were considered essential for human civilization in Torio because the bound spirits were vital tools necessary for survival. Accordingly, the spirits, as well as the yoki-hijo who summoned them and bound them, were greatly revered.[^2]

When the father machine was activated, all of the known spirits on Komashi were bound to that device. They congregated around the machine and were unable to leave it for approximately 1700 years.[^15] During this time, the surface of the planet cooled and the machine used their energy to produce hion. At one point, some spirits managed to escape and contact residents of [Futinoro](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Futinoro "Futinoro"), in an event which would come to be known among the scholars as "the [Incident](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Incident "Incident") ".[^30] [^31] A few decades later, Yumi was able to free another spirit, which granted her Connection to Nikaro and ultimately enabled the yoki-hijo to destroy the machine and free the hijo.[^15] [^13]

Freedom of the hijo caused the surface of Komashi to heat up once more, but only to a lesser extent as the spirits voluntarily supplied some of their power to continue supplying hion in response to hion viewer dramas.[^3]
