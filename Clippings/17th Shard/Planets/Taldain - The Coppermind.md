## Taldain

For the planetary system that includes the planet Taldain, see [Taldain system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taldain_system "Taldain system").

> “ *Taldain is one of the most bizarre planets in the cosmere, a fact that, in turn, feels bizarre to me.* ”
> 
> — [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") [^5]

**Taldain** is a planet in the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] located within the [Taldain system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taldain_system "Taldain system") and is the current residence of the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Autonomy - The Coppermind|Autonomy]].[^6] [^7] [^5] It is the home world of [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss"). The demonym of Taldain is Taldain.[^8]

## Geography and Ecology

Taldain is a tidally locked planet; one side of the world experiences perpetual day while the other side is locked in a type of endless night. This caused the ecology and culture on both sides of the planet to develop separately from one another. The two prominent cultures on Taldain are known as Dayside and Darkside.[^5] The individuals pertaining to each culture are known as 'Daysiders' and 'Darksiders' respectively. There is a temperature difference between the two halves, though not enough for it to snow on Darkside.[^9]

The area between both sides is known as the [Border Ocean](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Border_Ocean "Border Ocean") which has the **Terminal Storm** at the center, a perpetual storm making it dangerous to cross.[^10] [^11]

### Dayside

![[Taldain---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Ben McSweeney](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ben_McSweeney "Coppermind:Artists/Ben McSweeney")

Kerla Landscape, as sketched by Duchess Khrissalla.

Most of the planet’s Investiture is found on Dayside.[^12] Much of Dayside is covered in [sand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sand_\(Taldain\) "Sand (Taldain)") and rock, the sand itself being covered by lichen. This lichen is where the sand's striking white color comes from as well as the reason it changes between white and black in the process of sand mastery and other forms of Investiture.[^13]

**Dayside** is home to one large circular landmass that is surrounded by ocean on all sides. On this landmass there are five nations; [[Kerzta - The Coppermind|Kerzta]], [[Lossand - The Coppermind|Lossand]], [Denka](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Denka "Denka"), [Seevis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Seevis "Seevis"), and [Tallon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tallon "Tallon"). There is a mountain range on the eastern side of the landmass, which marks the borders of Seevis and Tallon and separates the [Rim Kingdoms](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rim_Kingdoms "Rim Kingdoms") from Kerzta and Lossand. A mountain named [KraeDa](https://coppermind.net/wiki/KraeDa "KraeDa") lies at the center of Dayside.[^11] A wide variety of Flora and Fauna make their home on this part of the planet. But due to the harshness of the sunlight and environment, most of the flora and fauna live beneath the surface of the sand.[^5] In the [[Kerla - The Coppermind|Kerla]], where the water table is high, the majority of life is found in a unique ecosystem beneath the sand that has been described as more like a lake of sand than a desert.[^10]

#### Flora

In the Kerla, most plants, including farms of vegetables, exist beneath the sand.[^10] The [[Dorim vine - The Coppermind|dorim vine]] is a plant used as a water source on Dayside by its inhabitants, and areas where they grow are not considered to be a desert. The plant stores water primarily as a defensive measure against other creatures as water dissolves the shells of many sand creatures. Because of this, any creature attempting to eat the plant would find its mouth beginning to melt.[^14] As a result, the growth of dorim vines limits how large sandlings can grow in these regions.[^15]

The banks of the [Ry'do Ali](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ry%27Do_Ali_River "Ry'Do Ali River") in Lossand is cultivated for farming despite the area being less fertile than the Kerla.[^16] [^17] [^10] Plants in this area of rocky low sands are described as being noticeably shorter and "more stumpy" than Darkside flora.[^10] The [Darkside quarter](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lonzare "Lonzare") of [[Kezare - The Coppermind|Kezare]] has flowers native to Darkside growing within it.[^18]

#### Fauna

[[Sandling - The Coppermind|Sandlings]] inhabit all areas of the continent, ranging in size from insectile sandlings found everywhere to gargantuan deep sandlings. It is rare for a deep sandling to venture far from the [deep sands](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deep_sand "Deep sand").[^19] Most sandlings live by eating the layer of sand exposed to the sun, and digesting the lichen that covers the sand. The sand turns dull after being digested, but the lichen from undigested sand will spread back onto it, given time.[^10] Some sandlings, like the [DelRakin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/DelRak "DelRak") are predators that eat other sandlings.[^15]

There are multiple breeds of deep sandling. Some of them, such as [KaRakin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/KaRak "KaRak"), are hunted for sport. Another breed consists of thousands of small sandlings which act as a group in the deep sands; when they move they agitate the sand above them causing them to be mistaken for a much larger creature forcing wanderers to retreat. Delrak Naisha are another breed found in the deep sands. They are creatures that wait just beneath the sand; if the sand above them is disturbed, they emerge and consume whatever is above them.[^10] [^15]

[[Tonk - The Coppermind|Tonks]] are a type of domesticated sandling on Dayside used as beasts of burden; they are primarily used as mounts.[^10] [^14] They are scared of water, however, and refuse to cross it. Travelling with tonks is also frustrating because of their natural instinct to bury themselves underneath sand during a sandstorm and their tendency to mistake many things for sandstorms.[^16]

[Terhan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Terha "Terha") are another beast of burden, larger than a tonk and often used by soldiers as beasts of war. They may be treated by [DoKall](https://coppermind.net/wiki/DoKall "DoKall") to make their carapace immune to water when used in this role.[^10] [^15] [Rezalin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rezal "Rezal") may be used for faster long-distance transportation beasts, as their oversized rear legs allow them to move quickly from dune to dune.[^10] [^1]

### Darkside

**Darkside**, also known as **Starside** in Elis,[^11] is illuminated by the Eye of [Ridos](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ridos "Ridos") with a spectrum composed primarily by UV light, with most visible light filtered out by the particulate cloud.[^10] The result is that the sky is perpetually in a state of twilight as if the sun had just set.[^5] This causes **Darksiders** to have illuminated teeth, eyes, and nails.[^20] The plant and animal life also glow under UV light.[^9] [^5] Visitors to the region often describe it as looking somewhere between striking and garish.[^5] It also accounts for why Darksiders have dark skin when they don't live in a traditional climate where a high melanin content is common. When visiting Dayside, Darksiders must wear dark lenses to protect their eyes. They can go without, however, if the angle of the sun is not directly above them or they force themselves to adapt.[^21] [^2]

The landmass of Darkside is of an unknown size and shape. There are many countries, though most have been subordinated to the [Dynastic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dynastic "Dynastic") Empire. Elis is a landlocked country on Darkside.[^11] Nimmyn is a geographical location whose flora has been imported to Lraezare, but it is not known if it is a country or region.[^18]

Unlike what was once assumed, Darkside is Invested by [[Autonomy - The Coppermind|Autonomy]], though the effects of this aren't as noticeable.[^5] [^22] [^12] The particulate cloud around Ridos's Eye emits a pulse every week that Invests [Starmarks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Starmarks "Starmarks") on Darkside, even if only some people can access that Investiture.[^10]

#### Flora

On Darkside, the flora has adapted to utilize the UV section of the electromagnetic spectrum.[^23] Lichen is a common growth across the entire Darkside continent,[^13] but flowers and crops also grow well.[^5] Grapes have been exported to Dayside for the making of wine, and some flowering plants have been imported for the [[Lraezare - The Coppermind|Lraezare]] district of [[Kezare - The Coppermind|Kezare]] to help [Lonsha](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lonsha "Lonsha") expats feel more at-home.[^16] [^24] [^10]

#### Fauna

Fauna on Darkside is "remarkable" are often referenced as proverbs, usually with reference to Starmarks.[^5] [Wombears](https://coppermind.net/edit/Wombear?redlink=1 "Wombear (page does not exist)") are a saddle-able beast of burden on Darkside.[^10] The diversity of animal life extends at least from invertibrates, like the [venomleech](https://coppermind.net/edit/Venomleech?redlink=1 "Venomleech (page does not exist)"), to apex predators, like the [devil-tiger](https://coppermind.net/edit/Devil-tiger?redlink=1 "Devil-tiger (page does not exist)").[^11] [^25]

## History

At an unknown date the planet was created and purposefully placed in its current location in the [Taldain System](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taldain_System "Taldain System").[^26] Following the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]], Autonomy settled into the system as the sole Shard present.[^6] [^7] At another stage in its early history, the planet was settled by humans; due to how the genetics of the settlers played out, Daysiders ended up with paler skin.[^27]

Over time those on Dayside began to notice the influence of one of Autonomy's Avatars which became known as the [[Sand Lord - The Coppermind|Sand Lord]].[^22] This entity eventually would form the basis of the [[Ker'reen - The Coppermind|Ker'reen]] and [Los'seen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Los%27seen "Los'seen") religions and became the dominant object of worship for the people of [[Kerzta - The Coppermind|Kerzta]]. Differing religious beliefs were formed in the [[Diem - The Coppermind|Diem]], an important part of the government of [[Lossand - The Coppermind|Lossand]]. This difference of faith led to frequent war between Lossand and Kerzta. Peace was eventually gained when the high priest government of Kerzta was replaced by a merchant nobility.[^14]

After 200 years, there was a push again by the A'kar, the head priest of Ker'reen, to take control by democratic means. They made contact with a [recent convert](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elorin "Elorin") to Ker'reen within the Diem's ranks and persuaded them to remain a member of the Diem and assist with a plan to wipe the organization out. It was hoped that by doing so the A'kar would win favor with the public, under the pretense that the destruction of the Diem was the Sandlord's whim.[^19] [^28] Their efforts nearly succeeded but ultimately the remnants of the Diem managed to organize themselves and the [[Kenton - The Coppermind|new Lord Mastrell]] succeeded in persuading the Lossand government to maintain the Diem. This allowed the Diem to grow more powerful than before. As a result, support for the A'kar crumbled and they failed to take control of the Kerztian government.[^29]

At a later point, for reasons unknown, the planet became closed off to visitors.[^30] [^5] [^31] The planet also stopped progressing technologically around this time, a sudden shift from their previous status as one of the most advanced planets in the Cosmere.[^30] [^31] [^32] It was still isolated from the rest of the cosmere at the start of the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows") circa on the Vorin Calendar.[^33]

At a later date it became easy to visit Taldain again.[^34]

Taldain's history with the [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] is mostly unknown, but at some point, it became legal on the planet to kill the beings on sight.[^32]

According to [Starling](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Starling "Starling"), Taldain is one of the most technologically advanced planets in the Cosmere along with Scadrial and Invention's planets. [^35]

## Culture

The culture of Taldain is split between the Dayside and Darkside due to the planet's unique geography.[^5]

### Dayside

Because the sun does not set on **Dayside**, individuals living there have a variety of issues that need to be resolved. In regards to keeping time, an hour on Dayside is 90 minutes long.[^10] Because of its stationary nature many Daysiders find it easy to navigate by using the sun as a reference point.[^14] Due to its ever present nature many Daysiders feel a spiritual bond with their sun, and some, such as the Kerztians, worship it.[^14] Most Daysiders are afraid of the dark as well being unused to it.[^36] [^2]

On Dayside there is a large circular continent that can be found. On this landmass there are a variety of countries. The largest and most powerful nations is [[Kerzta - The Coppermind|Kerzta]] which is located on the western portion of the Dayside continent. Its people worship the [[Sand Lord - The Coppermind|Sand Lord]] and abide by the [[Ker'reen - The Coppermind|Ker'reen]] religion. [[Lossand - The Coppermind|Lossand]] is a country that is immediately next to it and stretches in a wedge from [KraeDa](https://coppermind.net/wiki/KraeDa "KraeDa") in the center of the continent to the southern coast around the [Ry'DoAli River](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ry%27DoAli_River "Ry'DoAli River"). It is ruled by the Council of [[Taishin - The Coppermind|Taishin]], the heads of the eight Professions vital to their life and culture. Both of these nations have an enmity with one another due to Kerzta accepting Sand mastery, although they have not been in direct conflict in centuries.

In addition to these countries are the Rim Kingdoms, which are a series of smaller nations on the eastern and north-eastern coast of the Dayside continent. They consist of; [Denka](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Denka "Denka"), [Seevis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Seevis "Seevis"), and [Tallon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tallon "Tallon").

### Darkside

On **Darkside** or **Starside**, the most powerful and influential force in regards to culture, economics and politics is the [[Dynasty - The Coppermind|Dynasty]]. This force is ruled by [Skathan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skathan "Skathan").[^16] Its dominance is so strong that the language Darksiders speak is called Dynastic after the country.[^14] It also is the most technologically advanced country on the planet with weapons such as guns and explosives being common creations within its borders.[^16] They also had instant noodles, which Hoid enjoyed, and wants [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]] to replicate.[^37] [^38] Despite its dominance, some minor nations such as [[Elis - The Coppermind|Elis]] resist the Dynasty's push to grow and expand its influence even further.[^39] Despite this, the nation's power runs deep within Darkside, with them being able to send their agents to infiltrate different groups throughout the continent.[^39]

The Kerztian word for Darksider is **Lonsha**.[^14]

## Astronomy

![[Taldain---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Ben McSweeney](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ben_McSweeney "Coppermind:Artists/Ben McSweeney")

Top-down view of Taldain's Orbit.

Taldain is tidally locked around two suns, this makes it unique among other planets in the Cosmere.[^5] It is located between the two suns in the system which orbit one another, because of a layer of particulates around one of these suns one half of the world is trapped in endless sunlight and the other half in night.[^5] This placement was done purposefully by its creator.[^10] [^26] It is the only planet within the system and a single moon, **Nizh Da**, has a polar orbit around the world.[^5]

The orbit of Nizh Da is what defines the length of a day on Taldain.[^10] The exact precession and movement of the moon is controlled so that it remains in the same position at the same hour, at any point in the year. This is due to the influence of Autonomy.[^10]

An hour on Dayside is ninety minutes long and each day is separated into fifteen hours. On Darkside, an hour is sixty minutes long, and a day is separated into twenty-four hours.[^11] [^10] Due to this, ninety Dayside minutes are equal to ninety-six Darkside minutes, with one dayside minute being equal to approximately 1.066 Darkside minutes.[^10]

It is kept in an unstable orbit around AisDa by the gravitational pull of Ridos, and by some other, unknown force, presumably Autonomy. This astronomically unstable orbit is known as "Wombear's Saddle".[^10]

Both of Taldain's suns are Invested, with AisDa constantly bombarding Dayside with Invested light, and the Eye of Ridos releasing a pulse of Investiture every seven orbits of Nizh Da.[^10]

## Magic System

The world of Taldain has been settled by Autonomy and as a result her Investiture can be found throughout the planet, primarily in the atmosphere.[^22] Autonomy has allowed it to spread unusually, making it in theory easy to access; a [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] for instance would have little difficulty living on Taldain [^31] and one could also theoretically access the world's Investiture on another planet so long as they can see the star system.[^40] For a time this was the only way to access its magic system as the world was shut off from the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]],[^22] but eventually it became possible to visit the world again.[^34]

The most dramatic form of Investiture that can be found on the planet is [[Sand mastery - The Coppermind|Sand mastery]] which is located on Dayside. Despite its flashy appearance, it uses very little Investiture however and primarily works through subtle manipulation with [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]] between the user, Shard, and White Sand. It is believed by residents of Taldain that only Dayside was Invested by the Shard, however, this is incorrect and in fact both parts of the world are Invested, with the majority of it falling on Dayside.[^5]

Darkside magic primarily functions through tattoos,[^41] [^3] but its effects are not nearly as noticeable.[^12] [^22] This is seemingly the system referred to as [Starmarks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Starmarks "Starmarks").[^10] [^11]
