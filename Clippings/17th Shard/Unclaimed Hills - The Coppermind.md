## Unclaimed Hills

The **Unclaimed Hills** is a geographical region in eastern [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that contains the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]].[^1]

## Location

The Unclaimed Hills lie in the east of Roshar, covering the northern part of the greater region of the [Frostlands](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Frostlands "Frostlands"). They form the eastern border of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") and stretch all the way to the shores of the [Ocean of Origins](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ocean_of_Origins "Ocean of Origins"), while to the south, they gradually flatten out. Their most notable geographical feature is the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]], which occupy the southern portion of the area.[^1] Another location, several days away from the Shattered Plains, is the [Covenant Stone Crossroads](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Covenant_Stone_Crossroads "Covenant Stone Crossroads").[^2]

## Geography and Ecology

by [Marie Seeberger](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Marie_Seeberger "Coppermind:Artists/Marie Seeberger")

A slave caravan riding into the Hills

The Unclaimed Hills get their name from their uneven terrain. Some hills are little more than mounds; others are far taller.[^1] They are rocky, covered with tough, retractable grass stretching as far as the eye can see. On occasion, this green monotony is broken by spiny plants such as wild [blackbane](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blackbane "Blackbane") and an unnamed shrub with thick stalks and spiny green needles, but there are no trees or any taller vegetation.[^3] [^4]

The whole region is highly exposed to the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], making the plants skittish. At any sign of movement, they retract, leaving behind only brown stone.[^3] Most of the Hills' fauna stays hidden, only revealing their presence after a highstorm. Then, one can find numerous [[Cremling - The Coppermind|cremlings]], [leggers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Legger "Legger") and slugs coming out to drink the water.[^4] Though the land is arable, it would be difficult to farm it properly -- rockbuds grow small, and crops give little yields.[^5]

## Inhabitants

The Unclaimed Hills are, as their name indicates, held by no nation, and sparsely inhabited. [[Listener - The Coppermind|Listeners]] once lived in the greater Unclaimed Hills, but they have since congregated deep within the Shattered Plains,[^6] and during the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]], their presence is reduced to the plateau of [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Narak]], while the edges are taken over by the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") military.[^7] [^8] The Hills themselves are home to a small population of the [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natan]] people.[^9]

What cities there are lie mostly on the ocean coast.[^4] Most of them are small and independent, and nominally [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]], although their adherence to the doctrine -- particularly in terms of the prohibition on slave trade -- is far looser than in the western nations.[^3] The largest among them are the city-state [[New Natanan - The Coppermind|New Natanan]] and a settlement called [Dawn's Shadow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawn%27s_Shadow "Dawn's Shadow").[^1]

After the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") move onto the Plains, several plans are made to reinhabit the land. Initially, [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] proposes homesteading the area and adding it to the Alethi territory, though that plan is stopped before it can be fully put in place by the coming of the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]].[^10] Later, [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani Kholin]] suggests building a new kingdom there after Alethkar falls to the voidbringers, but the idea is discarded on the account of how difficult the region is to farm.[^5]

## History

### Ancient History and Abandonment

Back during the [[Heraldic Epochs - The Coppermind|Heraldic Epochs]], the territory now known as the Unclaimed Hills was split between the [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind|Silver Kingdoms]] of [[Alethela - The Coppermind|Alethela]] and [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]].[^11] It's possible that the final battle of the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]] was fought in a canyon within the Hills -- [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] would later bring [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] into a memory of it through a [vision](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions").[^12]

Following the splintering of Alethela and the destruction of Natanatan, the land slowly dropped in importance. It's possible there was another kingdom there at some point; however, by the late [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]], the region was essentially uninhabited, traversed only by travellers, hunters, bandits and, on occasion, [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] venturing out of the Shattered Plains.[^13] In the absence of people, beasts, including the [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]], flourished.[^9]

### Rediscovery and the War of Reckoning

Several expeditions were sent out into the Unclaimed Hills in search of a river passage to [[New Natanan - The Coppermind|New Natanan]].[^14] Following the unification of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]] and [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] went on their own hunting trip there, whereupon they encountered the listeners and discovered the Shattered Plains -- and on them, chasmfiends, the source of the listeners' gems.[^15] Initially, the relations between the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") and the listeners, dubbed Parshendi by the humans, went well, but on the night of an important treaty between the two, the listeners chose to assassinate Gavilar to stop him from releasing their gods.[^16] As a result, the Alethi moved onto the Shattered Plains and began to wage the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] against the listeners.

The return of human population to the Hills brought with it an increase in both trade and banditry. Slavers and criminals flourished in the difficult, uninhabited wasteland, and the [[Highprince - The Coppermind|highprinces]] were either uninterested or incapable of stopping it.[^17] Though the Alethi had plans for reinhabiting the Shattered Plains fully, those were put on hold, first by the war and then by the beginning of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^10] [^18]

### True Desolation

As the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] began circling the globe, the Alethi focused on the defending the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]], in particular the area around the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] in their center.[^5] The Hills remained simply a place people travelled through, whether to escape or on their way to and from the Plains.[^19]
