## Honorblade

> “ *Honor gave these to men, and those men gained powers from them. Spren figured out what He'd done, and we imitated it.* ”
> 
> — [[Sylphrena - The Coppermind|Syl]] [^1]

The **Honorblades** are the magical swords carried by the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") to fight the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] during the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]]. They were pieces of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] that gave their wielders access to his essence.[^2] [^3]

## Mechanics

> “ *With this sword, someone can do what you can, but without the... checks a spren requires.* ”
> 
> — [[Sylphrena - The Coppermind|Syl]] [^1]

There are ten Honorblades total, one for each Herald.[^4] Every Honorblade is unique, covered in various glyphs and patterns.[^5] Honorblades do not need to be shaped as a sword, with Kaladin's Honorblade taking the form of a spear.[^6] However, the Honorblades of the original ten Heralds took the shape of a sword.[^7] If a new Herald is made, an Honorblade is created at the same time and appears before them.[^4] [^6]

They are technically a type of [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"); they were prototypes of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knight Radiant]] Shardblades, and share many qualities with them.[^8] [^9] Among others, they can be bonded, summoned and dismissed much like a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") can.[^10] Like Radiant Shardblades, they can be used to operate [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]],[^11] and can dull their edges.[^12] Unlike Radiant Shardblades, they cannot change shape.[^13] It's unclear if they require ten heartbeats to be summoned, like dead Blades, or if they can appear instantly; this is likely tied to their wielders' perception.[^13] [^14] [^15] They are near-indestructible, with the only thing known to be capable of damaging them being [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]].[^15] Honorblades are made out of the [God Metal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/God_Metal "God Metal") of Honor, and they are a physical and solid manifestation to his essence.[^2]

Unlike Shardblades, Honorblades do not manifest as [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]], and will transition normally when brought into the Realm.[^9] They are made of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor's]] own [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], which he deliberately splintered off himself and crystallized into metallic form.[^11] [^16] Honorblades are self-aware, but it is currently unknown to what extent.[^17] [^9] They are able to have conversations with Nightblood.[^12] [^9] In this sense, Honorblades are comparable to the blade wielded by [[Vivenna - The Coppermind|Azure]], which is also known to posses an unknown level of sapience.[^18] [^19]

One does not need to be a Herald in order to use an Honorblade.[^20] [^10] Likewise, a Herald dying does not cause the Honorblade to be destroyed or vanish.[^21] Honorblades can be corrupted if the Herald dies permanently.[^22] The Honorblades do go with the Heralds when they travel to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], and they appear to have a crucial role in the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], as nine of the Heralds leave them behind in the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]].[^5] They are considered an anchor for the Oathpact.[^23] A Herald seems to have a special [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to their Blade, as [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] is still able to feel and locate his Blade despite someone else having bonded it.[^24]

### Surgebinding

Holding an Honorblade inherently grants the ability to [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebind]] without a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]].[^25] [^26] This ability is similar to that of [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind#Unsealed Metalminds|unsealed metalminds]], though they rely on different magic systems.[^27]

Honorblade Surgebinding is in many ways inferior to Radiant Surgebinding; among others, the Surgebinding healing bestowed by Honorblades is less efficient than Radiant healing.[^10] [^9] Additionally, [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] require more [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] as fuel to power them, up to dangerous levels.[^28] [^29] While the latter still applies to the Heralds, they were once powered directly by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], which made the issue of Stormlight consumption irrelevant.[^3]

If a Surgebinder uses an Honorblade that matches the power they already have, their powers will be slightly enhanced.[^30]

### Heralds and Shinovar

> “ *Our Blades must be left. The Oathpact ends now.* ”
> 
> — [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] [^5]

The Heralds received the Honorblades from Honor during the creation of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].[^2] They used them throughout the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]] until the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]], whereupon nine of them left them at the site of the final battle before walking away from the Oathpact, feeling that as they had broken it, they no longer deserved the Honorblades.[^31] [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel's]] Honorblade disappeared back to Braize along with him.[^5] It is possible that these blades were left behind as more than just a symbol.[^7]

After the Heralds departed, one of the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] soldiers who participated in the battle stumbled upon them. Likely because of this, the nine Honorblades ended up in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]].[^32] [^33] Over the millennia, they retained the Blades, and even occasionally used them, most notably during the Shin invasions.[^34] Even after the invasions failed, they would still train some of their people in the use of the Blades and the associated Surges.[^28] Those selected to bear the Blades were known as [Honorbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorbearers "Honorbearers"), and held one of the highest stations within Shin society.[^35]

Sometime between and,[^36] [^37] the Herald [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] reclaimed his own Blade from the Shin, leaving them with eight.[^33]

In, [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] was chosen by [Pozen-son-Nash](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Pozen-son-Nash "Pozen-son-Nash"), the current bearer of [[Battar - The Coppermind|Battar's]] Blade, to replace [Tuko-son-Tuko](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tuko-son-Tuko "Tuko-son-Tuko") as the Windrunner Honorbearer.[^38] [^36] After completing his pilgrimage, he was given [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] Honorblade.[^37] Around the same time, his father [Neturo-son-Vallano](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Neturo-son-Vallano "Neturo-son-Vallano") was training to become the bearer of [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar's]];[^39] [^40] and in time Szeth's sister [Elid-daughter-Zeenid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elid-daughter-Zeenid "Elid-daughter-Zeenid") became the bearer of [[Chanarach - The Coppermind|Chanarach's]].[^41]

### War of Reckoning

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Honorblade---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Kelley King](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Kelley_King "Coppermind:Artists/Kelley King")

The ten original Honorblades

When Szeth was declared [Truthless](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Truthless "Truthless") by the Honorbearers in, the voice of Ishar directed them to let Szeth keep Jezrien's Blade, an unusual arrangement for a Truthless.[^10] [^42] [^39] [^43] The Blade would go on to cut a swath through the [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Rosharan]] leadership as Szeth became the infamous Assassin in White. Among others, [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]], [Hanavanar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hanavanar "Hanavanar") of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved") and two [Prime Aqasixes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Prime_Aqasix "Prime Aqasix") of [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir") fell to Jezrien's Honorblade between and.[^10] [^44] [^45]

At roughly the same time, the Herald Talenel returned from [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], carrying his own Honorblade. He dropped it upon entering [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), but for unknown reasons, it didn't vanish.[^46] Talenel was later collected by one of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin's]] soldiers, [Bordin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bordin "Bordin"), to be brought to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. The Blade was taken along with them; however, at some point during the transit, it was swapped out for a regular [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade").[^47] [Stone Shamans](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stone_Shamanism "Stone Shamanism") from [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], under [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar's]] influence, sent [Taszo-son-Clutio](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taszo-son-Clutio "Taszo-son-Clutio") to recover Taln's Honorblade. Though he died in the attempt, the companions he entrusted his task to eventually succeeded.[^48] [^49] [Rit-daughter-Clutio](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rit-daughter-Clutio "Rit-daughter-Clutio"), as the head of Taln's monastery (or Talmut, as the Shin call him), received the blade from them and became an Honorbearer.[^50]

During the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], Szeth used Jezrien's Honorblade to battle [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] above the Plains. At the end of the fight, Szeth unbonded it and it ended up in Kaladin's hands.[^1]

### The True Desolation

Kaladin later passed the Honorblade to Dalinar, who hid it in one of the vents in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^11] After a while, he gave it to [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]], to use for training and Oathgate operation in absence of their leader.[^51] The bridgemen would swap the Blade among themselves every once in a while in order to hide who currently had it and remain unpredictable.[^52]

Despite that, agents of the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") managed to figure out the time when [Eth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eth "Eth") held the Honorblade and attacked him. They snuck up to him with [Teft's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") sold-off uniform coat, then killed him with a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and stole the Honorblade before running off.[^53] [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] later gave the Blade to [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] at the latter's demands. The Blade was later given to [Leshwi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leshwi "Leshwi"), who passed it onto [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]] to allow him to join the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] in the skies.[^21]

At some point very recently, Ishar killed Neturo-son-Vallano and took back his Blade.[^54] [^15] Several of the Honorblades were (slightly) damaged by [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] during Szeth’s fight with Ishar’s Fused.[^55]
