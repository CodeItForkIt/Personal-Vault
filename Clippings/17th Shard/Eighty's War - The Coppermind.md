## Eighty's War

The **Eighty's War** was a military conflict on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] between the bordering nations of [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]] and [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar") during the time of the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]]. The war began circa [^1] and lasted until.[^2] The conflict arose over the city of [[Sesemalex Dar - The Coppermind|Sesemalex Dar]], which is situated on the border between the kingdoms. Emul claimed the city as their capital, but the Tukari wanted it for themselves.[^1]

## Cause

The Emuli believed that Sesemalex Dar was their ancestral homeland and a gift from the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), because the city's patterned layout was central to their religion.[^3] The city was believed to be one of the [Dawncities](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dawncities "Dawncities") [^3] and named by [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] himself,[^4] increasing its religious significance. The Tukari were led by their "god-priest" [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Tezim]],[^1] who was described as a "warlord" [^5] and "tyrant".[^6] Tezim also claimed that he was "an aspect of the [[Honor - The Coppermind|Almighty]] ",[^5] and rumors swirled about him.[^7] Many observers assumed that the war was religious in nature, although the city was also a major port and a center of trade. [Au-nak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Au-nak "Au-nak") believed that the Tukari were using religion as a front for their economic aspirations.[^1]

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] had suspicions about Tezim and confirmed with the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] that he was actually [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]], one of the ten ancient [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of the Almighty. However, when Dalinar asked why Ishar would attack Emul, the Stormfather simply said that he was insane and that there was no logic in his actions.[^8] He believed that Ishar sought his own death, and possibly the death of all humankind.[^8]

## Fighting

The war seemed to be well-known across Roshar. [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] wondered if the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] was enough to distract the two nations from fighting.[^5] [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]] believed that Sesemalex Dar had been a war zone for "basically a billion years".[^9] Although the fighting lasted for years, it was not as intense or destructive as the brief [Veden civil war](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Veden_civil_war "Veden civil war").[^1]

After the [[Singer - The Coppermind|parshmen]] were awakened, Emul was attacked by [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringer]] armies that were leaving the area of [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir") on their way to [Marat](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Marat "Marat").[^10] [^11] Emul lost control of most of its territory.[^10] The Emuli leader, [Vexil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vexil "Vexil") the Wise, was desperate for assistance, although he still talked about conquering Tukar.[^2] It is unclear how Tezim and the Tukari reacted to the True Desolation, but it seemed to bring an end to the Eighty's War.[^2]
