## Grand Apparatus

The **Grand Apparatus** is a planetary [^1] [^2] megastructure comprising cities made of thousands of communal moving rooms controlled by a giant centralized [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Invested]] mechanism.[^3] [^4] Though its denizens consider themselves to be [[Invention - The Coppermind|Invention's]] people,[^5] it is thought that the structure was built before the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]] and thus not constructed by Invention directly.[^3]

The Apparatus is home to a multitudinous array of peoples including [Lawnarks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lawnark "Lawnark") who are enslaved by a tyrannical ruling class of [[Sleepless - The Coppermind|Sleepless]] who run experiments on the mortal occupants.[^3] [^6] There are no birds on the Grand Apparatus.[^3]

## Planetary Characteristics

The Grand Apparatus is composed of a vast number of rooms all atop and around one another and constantly in motion. It is unknown if there is a planetary surface beyond these rooms as the ruling Sleepless have made leaving the city all but impossible. If a denizen needs or wants a room with a particular function such as a bedroom or bathroom, they can make a request to the Apparatus itself and the appropriate room will be brought to adjoin the one currently occupied. Because of the Apparatus' nature, access to sunlight is infrequent and is only accessed by requesting a room with windows which is moved to the structure's perimeter upon transferring. Personal possessions are kept in trunks which are moved along with each resident as they transfer from room to room. Because all rooms are shared, there is no concept of ownership of space.[^3]

## Culture and Society

The mortal residents of the Apparatus are overseen, observed, and experimented on by the ruling Sleepless. Much of the resident peoples' culture is thus formed in relation to this dynamic, such as their propensity toward verbosity and their pride in their technological capability which they acquired through centuries of fighting for freedom.[^3] [^5] Few crews take work near the Grand Apparatus—likely due to the perceived danger of the Sleepless—which is why what little work there is typically pays well.[^1] Many people, knowing of the experiments happening on the Apparatus, expect the people from there to be a kind of simple, addlepated people, awed by the outside world. They often don’t realise that the people from the Apparatus are more advanced than most planets.[^5]

Residents of the Apparatus believe that, just as they only temporarily occupy any given room, their spirits are only temporary occupants of their bodies rather than owners. There is a myth of a place called "the gravelands", where rooms that have broken and worn down are discarded by the Apparatus, taking with it any occupants who are unfortunate enough to be inside at the time.[^3]

## Trivia

- [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] has visited the Grand Apparatus and claims to have solved a murder there, though the veracity of his involvement is unknown.[^3]
- In both the 2023 and 2024 States of Sanderson, Brandon mentioned wanting to write something called [*The Grand Apparatus*](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unpublished_works#Potential_Works "Unpublished works"), presumably located on this planet. Nothing else about this work is known.[^7] [^8]
