## Nahel bond

> “ *The Nahel bond is far more intimate a relationship. The linking of spirits. This is not a thing that should be done lightly, unsupervised.* ”
> 
> — [Notum](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Notum "Notum") [^1]

The **Nahel bond** was originally discovered as a bond between a [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] and a being from the Physical Realm.[^2] It's a symbiotic relationship, granting the spren the ability to manifest into the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] without losing their mind. In the case of a sapient individual in the Physical Realm, it grants the person the power of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] as well. It's also a bond formed by, for example, [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshells]] and [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]].[^2] It is now also known throughout the cosmere as a specific linking of spiritwebs where at least one of the beings is invested. [Aviar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar") are a great example of a non-rosharan Nahel Bond. It is identified by an ability to understand the others thoughts and share the ability to use Investiture, such as how [Sak](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sak "Sak") can share her precognition with [Dusk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dusk "Dusk") and how when Dusk [Navigates](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Navigator "Navigator"), his Aviar can sense the Current as well.[^3] This is also seen in spren bonds by the gain in investiture of the mortal.[^4]

Originally, the Nahel bond granted power to individuals with no affiliation with one another. Eventually, the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] were formed and the Surgebinders were gathered into ten different Orders of Knights, each Order's members bonded to the same type of spren.

## Spren Bonding Mechanics

[Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic") observing [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] prior to her bond

> “ *The responsibility of what we've been given — whether it be the crown or the Nahel bond — needs to make *us* better.* ”
> 
> — [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]] [^5]

### Formation

When a spren decides to form a bond, they fade away from the Cognitive Realm to transition to the Physical Realm.[^6] The process is described as traumatic, as the spren will forget themselves briefly once in the Physical Realm.[^7] The transition can be made easier by going in groups, or by more people remembering the spren.[^6] [^8] [^9]

Spren seem to bond with people who share their perspective or personality; however, they also seem to seek partners that would complement them.[^10] The bonded spren is more likely to be of the opposite gender to their bond-mate, but this is by no means a hard rule,[^11] and there are numerous Surgebinders with same-gender spren, such as [[Malata - The Coppermind|Malata]] with [[Spark - The Coppermind|Spark]], or [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] with [[Glys - The Coppermind|Glys]]. Surgebinders are able to instinctively tell their spren apart from others.[^12] [^13]

A person is able to bond with two different spren, though most spren aren't going to like it.[^14] A person can also bond with two spren of the same order, though this will not increase their powers or strength.[^15] The [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] describes such a double bond as being imbricated. He also says that an imbricated Nahel bond can cause strange effects and that he believes there were rules against it prior to the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^6] Spren can theoretically bond with another spren,[^16] or with two different people at the same time.[^17] They could also have a bond with a [Vessel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel").[^18]

How exactly the bond is decided on varies, though it requires volition.[^19] Some spren are highly discerning, while others do not pay much attention to the morality of their prospective partner.[^5] Some spren choose the people they bond with individually, while others are more organized and make the decision by committee, then send one of their number to bond.[^20]

Forming a bond utilizes the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] -wide properties of [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] and [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] in some manner; lessening those attributes, such as by storing them in a [metalmind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Metalmind "Metalmind"), would affect the bond.[^21] It is also harder to form a bond with a heavily Invested being, such as a [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]], due to Investiture resisting Investiture.[^22] [^23] Spren are also less likely to bond with a person using a [[Hemalurgy - The Coppermind|Hemalurgic]] spike.[^24] This can be contrasted with the [[Luhel bond - The Coppermind|Luhel Bond]], which primarily utilizes physical matter.[^25]

### Progression

The Nahel bond is forged and progressed by the partner stating [Immortal Words](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words"), also known as Oaths or Ideals, which align with their Order's general outlook and attitude. The first Ideal is identical for all Orders; the latter ones are Order-specific, with [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweavers]] in particular stating deep, intimate Truths about themselves rather than Ideals.[^26]

As the Radiant states further Oaths, they are brought closer to the spren they're bonded to. The spren's mental capacity in the Physical Realm increases, while the bondholder gains the ability to use [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] more effectively.[^27] At some point, the bond also grants the use of [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]].[^4] [^28] However, saying the words - at the very least, in one's mind, though out loud is preferred - is necessary to progress it.[^29]

As the bond progresses, many spren gain the ability to speak directly into their Radiant's mind, and vice versa.[^30] [^31] This was discouraged by Honor, who considered it an invasion of privacy.[^32]

### Breaking

There are some ways to break a Nahel bond. The best-known is when the Surgebinder acts in a manner contrary to the Oaths that they have sworn. This initially manifests by limiting the spren's intelligence and the Surgebinder's capacity to draw in Stormlight.[^33] [^34] Eventually, it can kill the spren, sending it back to the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]].[^35] Revival is possible, albeit difficult, and typically requires the Surgebinder who broke the Oaths to re-swear them, possibly along with progressing to a new Ideal of the Oaths.[^4] [^36] Another way to break the Nahel bond is to deliberately forswear the First Ideal; this, too, will kill the spren.[^37] Before the release of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]], if the spren is at a point where it can become a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), breaking the bond will turn it into a [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye").[^38] If a spren is permanently killed by the use of a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger embedded with a gem of anti-Stormlight, the bond will break, leaving the Radiant traumatized.[^39]

> “ *Some breaks are messy. A slice with a sharpened knife is clean; a slice with a dull one is ragged. Your break, done by a child without full Intent, is ragged. In some ways that makes it worse, but it does mean that some Connection between you two persists.*”
> 
> —Pattern to Shallan [^40]

Breaking the bond without full Intent can leave the spren damaged (and before the release of Ba-Ado-Mishram, a deadeye), but still Connected to the Radiant.[^40] This leaves the Radiant with access to some of their powers and their Dead Shardblade.[^41] [^42]

There are some ways to break the bond without killing the spren. Dying while upholding the Oaths -- for example, of old age, or in battle -- will send the spren back to [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), and severely traumatize it, but the spren will survive and can bond again.[^43] The spren can also not allow their bond to become strong enough to hurt them, leaving them safe from becoming a deadeye even at the Fifth Ideal.[^44] [^45] The spren can safely break the bond on their own, though this requires a great deal of caution.[^46] [^47] This will cause the Radiant pain, but will not be destructive to them like it would be a spren.[^48]

A Nahel bond can also be passed from one person to another, similar to a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]] bond. This has happened a few times in the past,[^49] though the mechanics behind it are unknown. A Radiant revived as a [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] would not have their Nahel Bond.[^50] Spren are able to retain their sapience in the Physical Realm for a time after the death of their Radiant, but will need to bond again in order to keep it.[^12]

Individuals who have already formed a Nahel bond are also able to become a [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]], however, this does pose risks for the Surgebinder's spren, as the Dawnshard can use the spren's substance to fuel the Radiant's abilities.[^51] [^52] This can cause the spren to lose their body in the Cognitive Realm, leaving them as a fragment of themselves.[^53] [^54] After this, they mainly exist in their Surgebinder's mind and must use the Radiant's body to sense the world around them.[^55] [^56] [^57] They are also stripped of most vocal expression, giving them a monotoned voice.[^58]

The pair also loses many of their abilities, including Surgebinding and the Radiant being able to communicate with their mind.[^30] They are still able to send feelings and emotions to their spren.[^53] Even after the Radiant has given up the Dawnshard, they can still further burn away the mind and personality of their spren for a short burst of Surgebinding.[^59]

If the Nahel bond has progressed to the point of being able to summon Shardplate and Shardblade, the Shardblade and Shardplate remain unaffected, even if the spren is completely consumed.[^60] [^61]

## Powers Granted by the Nahel Bond

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Nahel-bond---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by Paul Canavan

An [[Order of Edgedancers - The Coppermind|Edgedancer]] using her powers

> “ *“The Radiant bond is a symbiosis. Which means the bond gives something to both parties. Like … a contract. The human gets access to the Surges that are an innate aspect of the spren. In return, the spren gets stability in this realm. A human mind and soul to anchor them to the physical world, without which spren have trouble thinking and functioning.* ”
> 
> — [May Aladar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/May_Aladar "May Aladar") [^62]

### Surgebinding

The Nahel bond makes a person a [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]]. This allows them to breathe in [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]], which grants numerous benefits, such as increased reflexes and agility, as well as incredible healing abilities. Moreover, Surgebinders can use Stormlight to fuel their usage of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]]. Each type of Radiant spren grants its Surgebinder two different Surges, with each Surge shared between two Orders of Surgebinders.[^63]

### Resonances

Some Orders have additional, unique abilities known as resonances that occur due to the combination of their Surges. Examples of such resonances are the Windrunners' increased number of squires, and the Lightweavers' mnemonic abilities.[^64] [^65] Rather than being directly caused by the Nahel bond, this is a result of the two Surges interacting to produce an effect together. While some [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") benefit from the same resonances, others do not - for example, [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] will not have a large number of squires, because Heralds do not get squires.[^66]

It's possible that the bond also perfects innate skills of the user, without granting additional skills; for example, an expert spearman would become a spearman without equal, but wouldn't become a master swordsman.[^67] While this effect has been observed with [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]], it's uncertain whether it holds true for other Surgebinders or orders.

### Shardblade

When the Nahel bond is at a sufficient strength, the bonded spren can instantly manifest itself as metal, in any shape the radiant can imagine. This includes becoming a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), which, unlike dead Blades, is much more versatile. Known forms range from strictly militant, such as a shield or a spear,[^68] to utilitarian, like forks,[^69] or plain rods. More complex shapes such as clocks can be formed, but only if the radiant understands it fully. A gun can be created, but needs extra energy to actually shoot.[^29] Moreover, while a human using a dead Shardblade must wait ten heartbeats before the summoning occurs, a living Blade will manifest instantenously.[^70]

While in Shardblade form, a spren can communicate with its partner telepathically.[^70] The exception to that is the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], who, while refusing to become a Blade, can communicate with his Surgebinder regardless of the physical distance, directly in their mind.[^71] [^72]

### Benefits for the spren

While a spren can cross over into the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] without forming a Nahel bond, the transition leaves them only semi-sapient, with loss of self-awareness, memory and intelligence. There are precautions spren can take to try and minimize the loss of self, but they are not very effective.[^73] By forming a Nahel bond, spren are capable of regaining all those things, growing more like themselves the stronger the bond is. However, with a few exceptions (such as the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]]), the spren must remain in close physical proximity to their partner to retain their mind. The distance can vary, but some can go upwards of a few miles.[^74] The more that people are thinking about and remembering true spren in general, the faster a spren can regain themselves.[^9] [^62]

Spren can also anchor themselves to a Radiant while looking for a squire to bond, granting them some level of stability in the Physical Realm.[^75] Therefore, unlike the [[Luhel bond - The Coppermind|Luhel bond]] which trades in physical matter, the Nahel bond trades in consciousness and anchoring to the Physical Realm.[^25]

If a spren is somehow pushed back into the Cognitive Realm, their Radiant may be able to pull them back into the Physical Realm.[^76] [^77] Spren are able to hide inside of their Surgebinder, which helps them hide from Shards.[^78] [^79]

## Trivia

- The word "Nahel" is a Rosharan word that means "the bond to divinity", while the word "bond" was added to the term to avoid confusing readers.[^80] Assuming the Luhel bond comes from the same root, it can be assumed that the root *\-hel* indicates a bond to something, and *na-* relates to divinity.
