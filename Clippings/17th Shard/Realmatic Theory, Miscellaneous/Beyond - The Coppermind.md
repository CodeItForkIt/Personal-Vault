## Beyond

> “ *\[The Cognitive Realm\] is the in-between step. After death and before... the Beyond. The Somewhere Else. Where souls must go.* ”
> 
> — [[Leras - The Coppermind|Leras]] explaining the relationship between the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]] and the Beyond to [[Kelsier - The Coppermind|Kelsier]] [^1]

The **Beyond** is an unknown location that the people of the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] are drawn to after they die. [[Leras - The Coppermind|Leras]] occasionally refers to the Beyond as the **Somewhere Else**.[^1] The [[Iri - The Coppermind|One]] teaches that the Beyond is the same as the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]], though most other philosophers would disagree.[^2]

## Transportation

![[Beyond---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Kyle Pearson](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Kyle_Pearson "Coppermind:Artists/Kyle Pearson")

[[Leras - The Coppermind|Leras]] comforting someone before they go to the Beyond

When a person dies, their mind is transported to the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]],[^1] though they are not always visible to other people in the Cognitive Realm.[^3] This includes near-sapient lifeforms like [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]]; less sapient lifeforms may also experience this process, though they would transition very quickly and that question is a matter of debate among philosophers.[^4] For most people, this time in the Cognitive Realm is nothing more than an "in between" step, as their mind is soon pulled into the Beyond.[^1] When they go to the Beyond, they appear to stretch toward an unseen, distant point that defies geometry and has also been described as "nothingness" and "eternity." [^5] [^6] [^7] People on [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]] appear to turn into white mist as they stretch.[^1] Once someone transitions to the Beyond, their [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] returns to the [[Shards]] and the cosmere at large.[^8] [^9] The pull of the Beyond is part of why shadows point the wrong way in the Cognitive Realm.[^10]

Before someone moves on to the Beyond, their Cognitive aspect can be returned to their physical body with the proper [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], both by use of an Invested Art [^11] [^9] [^12] [^13] or by the act of a [[Shards|Shard]].[^14] [^15] It is also possible to escape the pull of the Beyond by entering a [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]] and becoming a [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 1|Cognitive Shadow]],[^1] [^16] [^17] though this method does not restore a person's ties to the Physical Realm.[^18] A Shard can also similarly Invest a Cognitive Shadow to keep it from the Beyond without returning it to the Physical Realm.[^19] Being killed by an unsheathed [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] [^20] or [[Lights|anti-Investiture]] [^21] [^22] or consumed by [[Yelig-nar - The Coppermind|Yelig-nar]] [^23] prevents a person's mind from being preserved, though not from reaching the Beyond.[^24] If a person was saved from the Beyond by the direct act of a Shard, the Shard may later choose to withdraw their Investiture, sending the person to the Beyond.[^25] [^26] If someone uses [[Forgery - The Coppermind|Forgery]] to make themself die, they will not come back from the Beyond when the seal breaks,[^27] and it seems unlikely that there is any way for someone to return from the Beyond.

[[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancers]], [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemists]], and people who had a [[Hemalurgy - The Coppermind|Hemalurgic]] spike in life can remain in the Cognitive Realm longer before being pulled to the Beyond,[^1] [^28] [^4] while those who were the victim of a Hemalurgic spiking and less sapient lifeforms cannot remain as long.[^4] [^29] [[Sliver - The Coppermind|Slivers]] can resist the pull of the Beyond indefinitely, though they can still choose to go and still feel the pull of the Beyond when seriously wounded.[^30] [^5] From this, it is possible to infer that the amount of Investiture a person has or had in life determines the length of time for which they can resist the pull of the Beyond.[^1] [^26] [^28]

What happens in the Beyond itself is unknown in the cosmere and is the question [[Khrissalla - The Coppermind|Khriss]] most wants answered.[^31]

## Shards and the Beyond

While the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] is not the same as the Beyond, that distinction isn't necessarily immediately obvious to [[Shards]].[^2] It does not seem exceedingly difficult to at least grasp the basic premise, however, as [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] came to understand the difference within moments of [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|his Ascension]].[^32] Learning information about the Beyond itself, however, is at least exceedingly difficult and may be impossible, even for Shards.[^33] [^34] [^35] Harmony, at least, still has not learned anything about it, though he considers going to it a kind of adventure.[^9] [[Ruin - The Coppermind|Ruin]] claims that [Mare](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mare "Mare") "longed for Kelsier to join her in the Beyond," but it seems likely that he was simply lying to manipulate Kelsier and does not actually have knowledge of what occurs in the Beyond.[^36] [^26] The Beyond is stated to be beyond the reach of Shards.[^37]

While trapped in the [[Well of Ascension - The Coppermind|Well of Ascension]], Kelsier refers to Ruin as "the thing Beyond," [^18] but this is likely a misnomer as a Shard's power is concentrated in the Spiritual Realm.[^38]

## Development

While [Brandon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Brandon_Sanderson "Brandon Sanderson") initially indicated that Shards might be able to learn to access the Beyond,[^41] [^42] he has since indicated otherwise, particularly after the nature of death in the cosmere was more fully explored in *[Mistborn: Secret History](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mistborn:_Secret_History "Mistborn: Secret History")*.[^33] [^34] [^35] [^43] When elaborating on this, Brandon has said that he doesn't want to ever give a definite answer as to whether or not there is an afterlife in the cosmere, as he feels it is important that characters can believe either way without being canonically wrong. As a result, questions about the nature of the Beyond will be [RAFO'd](https://coppermind.net/wiki/RAFO "RAFO").[^43] For the same reason, Brandon has also RAFO'd questions about whether there is a capital-G God in the cosmere [^44] and the relationship between the Beyond and the [God Beyond](https://coppermind.net/wiki/God_Beyond "God Beyond").[^45]
