## Cognitive Realm

This article is about the entire Cognitive Realm, informally known as Shadesmar. For the region that coined this term, see [Rosharan subastral](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_subastral "Rosharan subastral").

> “ *All things have three components: the soul, the body, and the mind. That place you saw, Shadesmar, is what we call the Cognitive Realm—the place of the mind.* ”

The **Cognitive Realm**, known colloquially as **Shadesmar**, is the realm of thought or of the mind.[^3] It contains the Cognitive aspects of both living and non-living things and can be accessed via perpendicularity. It is where minds exist and project their impressions.[^4] On at least one planet, the deceased appear in the Cognitive Realm before passing on to the Beyond. The Cognitive Realm, along with its counterparts, the Spiritual Realm and the Physical Realm are studied together as part of Realmatic Theory.

The informal name, Shadesmar, originates from the Rosharan subastral [^5] and also the Rosharan word for "Cognitive Realm".[^6] [^7] Although other worlds have their own words for "Cognitive Realm", when these get magically translated to another language, it results in the name "Shadesmar", even if not in reference to the Rosharan subastral.[^8] Shadesmar, as coined and used by Rosharans, is also used in [[Silverlight - The Coppermind|Silverlight]] as the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] standard.[^8]

## Locations

The Cognitive Realm exists as a connected plane that is heavily distorted based on the presence of cognitive thought.[^9] [^10] [^11] The Cognitive Realm is split into sections known as subastrals that reflect to corresponding planets in the cosmere separated by the emberdark. These subastrals can be reached from their neighbors, making worldhopping and interworld trade possible.

### Emberdark

> “ *Infinity in three shades, each one more unnerving than the one before.* ”
> 
> — [Dusk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dusk "Dusk"), about to explore the Emberdark, observing the sky, the horizon, and the unsea.[^12]

"Emberdark" is the Rosharan term for the vast expanse between planetary subastrals.[^13] Since the emberdark is mostly unexplored, there is too little cognitive activity to shape it. This leaves its surface—the unsea—incorporeal, consisting of transparent yet quite viscuous, liquid-like nothing.[^14] Some subastrals are nearly indistinguishable from the surrounding emberdark. Upon nearing a subastral where this isn't the case, the unsea solidifies into a featureless plain, which gains individuality with increasing proximity to the planet.[^11]

### Subastrals

Regions of the Cognitive Realm that correspond to planets are formally known as **subastrals** (e.g. Scadrian subastral or Selish subastral),[^3] but are also sometimes—more poetically—referred to as **expanses** (e.g. Expanse of the Vapors, or Expanse of the Densities).[^15] [^16] [^17] Subastrals generally differ from each other as they are shaped by the perceptions of cultures living in the corresponding area of the Physical Realm.[^11] Common features are obsidian ground and a land-sea inversion but this is not ubiquitous.

#### Rosharan Subastral

*Main article:* [Rosharan subastral](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_subastral "Rosharan subastral")

Shadesmar is a bustling land and home to the spren. Shadesmar is composed of an inverted ocean made of beads that represent the souls of Physical Realm objects, while living souls from the Physical Realm are represented by flames. There is a thriving society that lives in the area with both spren specific and mixed spren cities being found in the region. Humans can also be found living in Shadesmar however they are rarer and are more commonly travelers. Trade is common between Rosharans and off worlders, with goods being bought and sold using the local Investiture, Stormlight. Relations, however, between spren and humans are not always positive with many spren treating humans poorly due to the lingering effects of the Recreance on their peoples. As well as this, several factions, including Odium's Forces and the Ghostbloods became active in the area c. 1174. Odium's forces have worked to gain influence over the Realm, while the Ghostbloods have sought to build a network for Stormlight trade off world.

It is known as Makari Sin to the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]].[^18]

#### Scadrian Subastral

The Scadrian subastral is also known as the [Expanse of the Vapors](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Expanse_of_the_Vapors "Expanse of the Vapors") [^15] [^19] or, in popular accounts, the 'ghostly dimension'.[^20] This region is characterized by its misty environment, with all objects from the Physical Realm appearing as misty figures. Like with the Rosharan subastral, bodies of water in the Physical Realm reflect as rocky shores, while the ground appears as a misty liquid. Living beings, as well as all metal, glow brightly in the Cognitive Realm, to the point that they can mask objects in the Physical Realm if concentrated enough.[^21]

Worldhoppers have been known to travel by boat across the misty fluid of the Scadrian Cognitive Realm,[^22] but [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive shadows]], [[Shards]], and the souls of the dead appear capable of walking on the fluid. It is not known whether such beings can also walk on the Cognitive seas of other worlds.

#### Selish Subastral

The Selish subastral is also known as the [Expanse of the Densities](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Expanse_of_the_Densities "Expanse of the Densities").[^19] [^16] [^17] Due to the Splintering of the local Shards, and subsequent locking of those Splintered bodies in the Cognitive Realm, the Selish subastral is uninhabitable, as the Shardic substance has formed into a highly pressurized plasma-like storm known as the Dor.[^23] [^24] [^25] This makes the Cognitive Realm on Sel extremely dangerous as anyone who comes there unprepared would be ripped apart by the power of the Shards.[^26] It is possible to pass through the Dor and survive, though by what means it is unclear.[^27] [^28] [^29] [^30] There are people who guide others safely through the subastral.[^31]

The Selish subastral appears different based on where it is linked in the Physical Realm.[^32]

#### Nalthian Subastral

The Nalthian subastral is also known as the [Expanse of Vibrance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Expanse_of_Vibrance "Expanse of Vibrance").[^19] Little is known about the Nalthian Subastral besides the fact that the surface is painted colors and wet with pigment. Additionally, the subastral has a number of flowers, which drip ink.[^11]

#### Taldain Subastral

The Taldain subastral is also known as the [Expanse of the Broken Sky](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Expanse_of_the_Broken_Sky "Expanse of the Broken Sky").[^19] [^15]

#### Lumaran Subastral

Little is known about the Lumaran Subastral, however, the solid-fluid inversion is weirder than normal in comparison to the other subastrals shown.[^33]

#### Canticle Subastral

Little is known about the Canticle Subastral besides the fact that it has constant and dangerous shakings and tremors.[^34]

#### Drominad Subastral

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Cognitive-Realm---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Esther Hi'ilani Candari](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Esther_Hi%27ilani_Candari "Coppermind:Artists/Esther Hi'ilani Candari")

Due to the sea-faring culture of the [Eelakin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eelakin "Eelakin"), the Drominad subastral strongly resembles the unsea of the emberdark, even where the planet's ocean would normally have solidified. The unsea is a little thicker here, better visible.[^11] The ground in this subastral is rocky and black, but not like the glassy type found elsewhere in Shadesmar, rather, the ground looks more like coral, with bulbous sections full of texture.[^35] There are a few small islands, and the sky contains blue smoke-like streaks.[^36] The areas surrounding these islands are inhabitated by Invested lifeforms such as shoals of [glowing fish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Glowing_fish "Glowing fish") and [skullsnakes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skullsnake "Skullsnake") who prey on them.[^37]

The cognitive aspects of people and [Aviars](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aviar "Aviar") appear in the subastral as floating lights in the shape of fishhooks, with the hooks of those that are bonded being intertwined.[^36]

## Geography and Ecology

### Environments

The Cognitive Realm looks different on every planet, with its traits partially influenced by perception,[^43] and although the composition of each subastral differs, there are certain shared characteristics between the different parts of the realm. The landscape is also not fixed - as it is a reflection of the Physical Realm, changes in that Realm, such as a body of water getting filled in with dirt, will be reflected in the Cognitive Realm, albeit with a delay.[^44] The Cognitive Realm is flat, and does not have a curvature; it is unknown which projection it uses.[^45] [^46] [^47] [^1]

The Cognitive Realm overlays the Physical Realm,[^2] however, as it is more compact, locations do not map one to one between the two realms.[^48] [^49] As the realm forms based on people’s perception of objects and space, the vertical dimension,[^9] space between planets,[^50] [^51] and unknown locations in the Physical Realm,[^52] [^50] are particularly compressed. Areas, such as the middle of oceans or in between planets, where thought does not exist are shrunk in the Cognitive Realm to be little to no distance at all, though the Cognitive Realm does still exist in these spaces.[^53] [^54] Cognitive Realm geography does not need to be based off naturally occurring Physical Realm geology, and can also form as a reflection of man-made land or water sources.[^55]

As time passed and humanity developed a greater understanding of the scale of space in the Physical Realm, the distances between planets in the Cognitive Realm expanded dramatically. However, due to humanity's inability to truly grasp the scale of space, the distances would never become truly equal.[^56]

The sky in the Cognitive Realm is pitch black [^57] with no moon or stars.[^58] [^9] On the horizon of the Rosharan SubAstral sits a small, frail,[^9] white sun,[^59] [^2] that provides enough light to illuminate the lands but not the sky, despite appearing almost too weak to achieve even this.[^9] The sun does not move or change regardless of time of day or distance traveled - because of this, the Cognitive Realm does not have any natural day and night cycle.[^60] [^58] [^61] This “sun” is not present on the Drominad subastral, as well as possibly others. [^62] On some worlds, clouds create certain formations which they strictly hold to, however this is not the case for all worlds.[^63] [^64] It has a natural, breathable atmosphere due to air permeating into the Cognitive from the Physical Realm.[^65] Shadesmar also has several aquifers, and water has been thought to travel through a perpendicularity into the Cognitive Realm, although how an object crosses the Realms without the necessary Intent is as of yet unknown.[^66]

On most planets, the "water bodies" and lands of the Cognitive Realm are an inverse reflection of the Physical Realm. The Cognitive Realm's non-solid part corresponds to land in the Physical Realm, and Cognitive Realm land is the reflection of oceans, rivers, and lakes. This inversion does not extend to all subastrals in the cosmere.[^67] [^62] Each subastral manifests these bodies of "water" differently, reflecting the perception of local people.[^68] [^21]

Shadows behave strangely in the Cognitive Realm, depending on the source of light that is creating them. Shadows cast by the sun will point towards it instead of away,[^57] [^9] while those cast by manifested fire will point away from the light source as would occur in the Physical Realm.[^27] Backward shadows occur on rare occasions in the Physical Realm, and are related in some way to the Cognitive Realm.[^69] [^63] [^70] While most places appear illuminated without a particular light source, places perceived as "inside", such as a cave, can still be dark.[^71]

### Flora, Fauna, and Beings

There is a variety of plant life that blooms in the Cognitive Realm, although its substance differs between planets. Plant life blooms on the inverted ground of the Cognitive Realm as it corresponds to bodies of water. On some subastrals, water in a cup in the Physical Realm is enough to allow the plants to bloom.[^72]

Living beings reflect in the Cognitive Realm in some manner, with this manifestation varying between subastral.[^57] [^64] If someone is to die in the Physical Realm, their soul will disappear. In some subastrals they may appear briefly in the Cognitive Realm before passing onto the Beyond, although this does not appear to be the case on all planets.[^73] [^74] Through [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], a soul can last longer in the Cognitive Realm, and some even hold on long enough to be considered [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadows]], souls that fail to pass on and are stuck in the Cognitive Realm. Similar to objects, touching a person will give the impression of the person it represents and it is possible to feel their emotions and general thoughts.[^73]

## Trivia

- Although Brandon plans for Shadesmar to be used in reference to the whole cosmere and not just the Rosharan subastral, this may shift to the more scientific term ‘Cognitive Realm’ once Brandon reaches Era 4.[^8]
- The Cognitive Realm is, at least in part, inspired by [Idealism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idealism "wikipedia:Idealism").[^75]
