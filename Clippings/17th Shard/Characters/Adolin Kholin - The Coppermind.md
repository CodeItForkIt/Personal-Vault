## Adolin Kholin

> “ *My father thinks I'm a better man than he is. Unfortunately for you, he's wrong.* ”
> 
> —Adolin to Sadeas [^3]

**Adolin Kholin** is a [lighteyed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lighteyed "Lighteyed") [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") prince and third in line to the throne of Alethkar.[^4] He is considered to be one of the best swordsmen in all of Alethkar.[^5] Adolin is the son of [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]], the elder brother of [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin Kholin]] and husband of [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]] [^6] who are members of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^3] He is the current [[Highprince - The Coppermind|Highprince]] of the [Kholin princedom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholin_princedom "Kholin princedom").

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

Adolin's hair is mostly blond, with streaks of Alethi black running through it;[^7] the blond color comes from [his mother](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi_Kholin "Evi Kholin") and is seen as a sign of his bloodline being impure by some Alethi.[^8] His hair has been described as an unruly mop [^9] [^10] and a stylish mess.[^11] Adolin prefers to remain clean-shaven, but he does grow a beard after throwing himself in jail in protest of Kaladin's imprisonment. His beard was also blond speckled with black.[^12] His eyes are blue [^7] and have an [epicanthic fold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epicanthic_fold "wikipedia:Epicanthic fold").[^13] He has been described as handsome on numerous occasions [^7] [^14] [^15] and, as he is Alethi, it can be surmised that his skin is tan in color. However, because he is half- [Riran](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Riran "Riran"), his skin tone is lighter than his father's.[^16] There is some inconsistency as to Adolin's height; he is described as both shorter [^7] and the same height [^10] as Dalinar. Shardplate adds a few inches to his height but still doesn't make him as tall as Kaladin.[^17] He has a birthmark on the back of his left thigh [^18] and is right-handed.[^3] [^19]

During the [Battle of Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_Azimir "Battle of Azimir"), Adolin loses his right leg below the knee.[^20]

### Personality

Adolin is incredibly loyal to his father, though he doesn't always see eye-to-eye with Dalinar and is unafraid to voice his opinions.[^21] His skepticism surrounding the Codes eventually changes and Adolin begins to understand their purpose. He grows to become a better leader as a result and at the same time, he grows further away from the people he once considered to be friends.[^11]

He deeply cares about his family, and one of the few times Adolin admits to being frightened is seeing his father looking fragile and worn down.[^9] Adolin even allows the four-on-one duel to continue when [Relis Ruthar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relis_Ruthar "Relis Ruthar") threatens to have [Abrobadar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Abrobadar "Abrobadar") "accidentally" kill [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]].[^22] He is also quick to defend Renarin when [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") insults his younger brother.[^7] Adolin seems to have had a good relationship with his deceased mother as well, carrying a chain that once belonged to her for good luck during duels.[^23]

He is impulsive, often having to be held back by Renarin or rebuked by his father.[^24] [^7] Sadeas even refers to him as a "firebrand".[^7] This impulsiveness of his has led to him throwing Kaladin across the practice grounds while sparring,[^25] accidentally allowing Relis Ruthar to set him up in a one-on-four duel [^26] and contributed in some part to his snapping and murdering Sadeas.[^3] His impulsive behavior is often also beneficial, as seen in his rescue of Notum [^27] and how his offer to stand trial gets the envoy into Lasting Integrity.[^28]

Adolin has a strong sense of right and wrong, instilled in him by his mother.[^29] In his first encounter with Kaladin, he saves a prostitute from being beaten by one of Sadeas's officers.[^14] He also throws himself in jail as a show of solidarity with Kaladin.[^12] He is willing to kill those that he considers morally wrong and a threat to those he loves, such as Sadeas. This arises from a mixture of his strong sense of morality, justice, and his hotheaded nature.[^3] [^30] [^31] [^32] He is also willing to hide evidence of his involvement in a crime if he feels justified in his actions.[^3]

He is shown to be very confident, charismatic and friendly, being on good terms with both [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyes]] and [[Darkeyes - The Coppermind|darkeyes]].[^33] While Adolin likes to be familiar with a large number of people, calling them friends and acquaintances, he is not close with many of them.[^11] Before he marries [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]], he's also infamous for his numerous, short-lived courtships.[^34] Adolin admits to Kaladin that while he knows how to get women, he is unsure of how to keep them.[^33]

Adolin is observant, which has led to him being suspicious of those he does not trust. He absolutely does not trust Sadeas, even when his father asks him to, and is eventually proven right at the [Battle of the Tower](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_the_Tower "Battle of the Tower").[^35] He is suspicious of Kaladin for a long time, being the only one to notice that the arm Szeth cut with his [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] miraculously healed itself.[^36] He is the first person outside of [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]] to believe Kaladin's claims about [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram"), stating that he always felt the man was hiding something.[^12]

Adolin is very tactically minded. Shallan notes that he is not as "brilliant" as Kaladin,[^37] but this does not mean he is unintelligent. He even admits to sometimes feigning being dense.[^15] He applies his knowledge in a more direct manner to win duels and battles and isn't quite as skilled in wit or rhetoric.[^33] Adolin is compared to his father on various occasions, with Dalinar himself saying that Adolin is a better man than he is,[^38] though Adolin believes differently.[^3]

He doesn't seem to be particularly religious, something Dalinar points out.[^21]

With the rise of the Radiants and the fall of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), the young highprince is struggling to find his place in the world. He feels that others are often better placed to meet the challenges and that without access to surgebinding he doesn't have much to contribute to the leading of the Alethi or the coalition.[^39] His conversation with [Azure](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azure "Azure") in Shadesmar on royalty and duty [^40] helps evaluate his own roles and skills. Later it leads him to decline the Alethi crown after Elhokar's death.[^6] He continues to struggle when he takes on the mantle of highprince. His desire to prove himself makes him very determined to succeed in his diplomatic mission to the honorspren, as he doesn't want to let his father down, and he is motivated by his desire to show he is still good for something.[^41]

Adolin is also a very caring friend to those around him. He sees it when his friends need support, and he is always there for them. An example is when he takes Kaladin out when the Windrunner is depressed after his retirement. During the conversation, Adolin explained how he was looking for ways to help Kaladin and Shallan with their mental struggles.[^42]

Adolin is possibly bicurious.[^43]

## Attributes and Abilities

### Ryshadium

He had a white stallion, a [[Ryshadium - The Coppermind|Ryshadium]] horse named [Sureblood](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sureblood "Sureblood"),[^44] one of only a dozen Ryshadium in all the Alethi warcamps on the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]]. Adolin believed there was an undefinable bond between him and Sureblood, that he could read the Ryshadium's emotions, though it was more delicate than the bond between a man and a sword.[^45]

Sureblood died during the Battle of Narak.[^46] Adolin felt regret for not having spent more time with the Ryshadium due to being distracted by the duels and courting Shallan.[^45]

Later around 1175, Adolin started to visit and take care of Dalinar's Ryshadium, [Gallant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gallant "Gallant"), as his father had less time for this.[^45] Later it chose Adolin as his second rider.[^47] Over time their bond deepened, and Gallant helped by carrying Maya on the expedition to Shadesmar.[^48] When Adolin ran to save Notum, Gallant came running to his aid without being summoned.[^49]

### Shardbearer

Adolin is one of the few full [Shardbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardbearer "Shardbearer") in the Alethi warcamps.

#### Shardplate

Adolin inherited his [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] from his mother's side of the family on his sixteenth birthday.[^1] [Evi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi "Evi") and her brother, [Toh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Toh "Toh"), brought the Plate with them as a bargaining chip and surrendered it to Dalinar when he and Evi married.[^50] The [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iriali]] Queen considers the Kholins to be thieves and wants the plate returned to Iri.[^51]

#### Mayalaran

Adolin won a then unnamed [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") from [Tinalar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tinalar "Tinalar") in a duel during his youth. From the age of five Adolin had insisted on winning his own.[^52]

Adolin is proficient with Blade commands, a set of mental commands that allow him to keep the Blade summoned even after letting go of it.[^53] This allows him to perform such feats as using his Blade as a foothold on a sheer surface.[^54] After Szeth's first attempt on Dalinar's life, he practices being able to throw his Shardblade into a target.[^53] This appears to be a rather extraordinary feat given [Hrdalm's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hrdalm "Hrdalm") amazement upon being saved by the technique.[^55]

Adolin now knows [[Mayalaran - The Coppermind|Mayalaran's]] name and can summon her in seven heartbeats rather than the typical ten heartbeats required of a dead Shardblade. He is even able to sense her resigned agreement at being loaned to another Shardbearer,[^55] hinting at a stronger bond between a human and the bonded [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye") spren than usual. During his second trip in Shadesmar, Adolin develops his bond with Maya. They exercise every morning with a joint kata.[^39] She also starts helping him take care of Gallant.[^56] Later, when Adolin fought to defend Notum, Maya joined him and stood back to back with him.[^49] Her waking up came to a culmination at the trial where she spoke for the first time, deciding the trial.[^57]

### Dueling

> “ *This might be our last fight together \[...\] I appreciate what you've done for me. I know you'd do it for anyone who held you, but I still appreciate it.* ”
> 
> —Adolin to his Shardblade, Maya [^23]

Adolin's [Calling](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Calling "Calling") is dueling and he frequently resorts to challenging other lighteyes to settle disputes. Since arriving at the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]], he has been unable to duel as much as he had previously due to his father's insistence that he follow the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War"), which state that needless duels should be avoided during times of war so that commanding officers are not wounded.[^58] Once Dalinar becomes [Highprince of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highprince_of_War "Highprince of War"), he uses Adolin and his dueling as a key part in his plan to discipline the Highprinces by taking away their Shards in duels.[^59]

Adolin has a series of traditions he performs before any duel he fights. While in the preparation room, which is used primarily for meditation, he talks to his unnamed Blade before continuing on to the staging room where his armorers help him into his Shardplate. [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] normally paints a glyphward for him, though by his final duel he has changed to having [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] paint them for him. Finally, [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] will check to make sure Adolin has eaten chicken at some point before the duel and that he has their mother's chain on his person.[^23] Navani passes these off as superstitions, which Adolin denies. Before his final duel he forgot his mother's chain; this does seem to affect him psychologically, although by the time his armorers have finished, he seems mostly recovered.[^22]

Adolin has employed various Shardblade Stances in his duels, though he relies most heavily on [Windstance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windstance "Windstance") and diverse tactics. In the duel against [Resi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Resi "Resi"), which is disguised as a friendly match, Adolin implements the defensive and offensive forms of Windstance, his style described as dancing.[^11] Against [Salinor Eved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Salinor_Eved "Salinor Eved") he switches to using [Ironstance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ironstance "Ironstance") as well as employing elements of hand to hand combat for a rather brutal display, much to the ire of Highjudge [Istow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Istow "Istow").[^23] While fighting against [Eranniv](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eranniv "Eranniv"), [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") notes that Adolin is fighting far below his own capacity,[^60] making it seem like a close fight. Against [Elit Ruthar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elit_Ruthar "Elit Ruthar"), Adolin employs Windstance again, focusing on precision while implementing a tactic of exhausting Elit's plate of stormlight until it can no longer move.[^26]

In the final duel against Elit, [Relis Ruthar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relis_Ruthar "Relis Ruthar"), [Abrobadar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Abrobadar "Abrobadar") and [Jakamav](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jakamav "Jakamav") Adolin relies most heavily on Windstance, as it is the most effective against multiple opponents. At one point he backs himself up against a wall to protect his back.[^22] Once [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] joins the duel, he remarks that two opponents actually seem like an even match for Adolin.[^61] By the end of the duel, Adolin is pinned beneath Jakamav and holding him in a wrestling grip.

It can be argued that Adolin is actually the most skilled duelist on the Shattered Plains, given that he is both capable of winning a fight while hiding his true level of skill and being an even match against two men. Sadeas remarks that Adolin is growing into the Blackthorn, a man who conquered [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") with his skill and power, and that thought terrifies him.[^62]

### Tactician

Adolin has been trained in battlefield tactics since a young age [^63] and has employed these skills during the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], the Battle of Kholinar and the Battle of Thaylen City.

### Early Life (1150-1167)

#### Childhood

Adolin was born earlier than expected, on [^1] [^2], with a full head of blonde and black hair. Dalinar, joyous of having been given a son, named him, using the components "Adoda", meaning "light", and the suffix "lin", meaning "born unto". He hoped that Adolin would have his father's strength and his mother's compassion.[^64] On the same day Gavilar informed Dalinar that he would be put to use in the border skirmishes. Dalinar proceeded to spend much of Adolin's childhood away from Kholinar and his family.[^65]

Adolin's early sense of morality was instilled in him by his mother.[^29] Evi was fond of hugs and physical affection and this translated to Adolin easily reacquainting himself with personal displays of affection while betrothed to Shallan later in life.[^66]

At age five, Adolin visited Dalinar on the battlefront with his mother and a very young Renarin. Dalinar had last seen Adolin a little over a year before. Already Adolin was showing an energetic personality, playing with wooden swords, crafting armor from [rockbud](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rockbud "Rockbud") shells and string, and terrorizing the chulls. When presented to his father he saluted him but quickly shied away afterwards, and had to have confirmed Dalinar was his father. He told Dalinar that he and his mother offered prayers for him every night so that he would be safe. Adolin showed a desire to please his father even at such a young age. He told his father he didn't want Dalinar to give him a shardblade, he wanted to win one himself.[^52]

Adolin started training with the sword at the age of six. Renarin mentioned that this made him different in some way, but there is no elaboration.[^54]

#### Adolescence

He eventually started training with [Zahel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zahel "Zahel") after stubbornly refusing to at first [^25] some number of years before the present.[^53] There are indications he was trained by his father in using Shardplate [^25] which he inherited from his mother's side of the family.[^23] When Adolin was young, Dalinar also made him serve in a spearman squad for two months with the intention of teaching him what it was like to "serve in the shoes of his men." [^67]

Adolin mentioned that he became a soldier by circumstance rather than choice.[^23]

Dalinar believed that Adolin was also brought up and trained to be a [[Highprince - The Coppermind|highprince]] in a manner that he himself was not,[^9] and that this would make him a fine highprince.

### War of Reckoning (1167-1173)

#### The Chasmfiend Hunt

Adolin and his brother Renarin accompanied King [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] and the Highprinces [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]], [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas"), and [Vamah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vamah "Vamah") on a chasmfiend hunt, and Adolin spent the early part of the trip worrying about the state of his father's mind and his reputation. He worried that Sadeas would make a move against his father now that Dalinar was appearing weak to the eyes of Alethi society. Adolin had been given effective command of the Kholin army by his father and restrictions had been placed on his dueling as per the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War"). To make matters worse Dalinar revealed he was considering leaving the Shattered Plains, a plan Adolin knew would ruin his father's reputation.[^1]

Adolin with his family on the hunt

When the [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiend]] appeared on the wrong plateau, threatening unarmed attendants, guests, and scribes, Adolin helped his father and Elhokar kill it. During the fight, Adolin was momentarily awed by seeing his father display the prowess that earned him the title of Blackthorn.[^68] Afterwards Adolin oversaw the cleanup, happy to be doing something, while the other lighteyes on the trip relaxed leisurely. When Sadeas insulted his father, Adolin was quick to defend him, though his manner in doing so was brash and led to a rebuke from his father and a gentle restraint from Renarin. Sadeas then turned his attention to his brother, and Adolin nearly summoned his Shardblade but stopped when he was ordered to by Dalinar.[^7]

Later, Dalinar took Adolin aside to inspect the king's saddle as Elhokar had requested of them. Adolin quickly realized that Elhokar believed the girth was cut deliberately and, annoyed, pointed out his cousin's worsening paranoia and that he and his father seemed to have become Elhokar's bodyguards. Despite their misgivings, both he and Dalinar agreed that if the girth was cut there may have been a plot to kill the king, as Alethi lighteyes were fond of their overly complicated plots. Dalinar assigned Adolin the task of investigating this further. Adolin wondered if Sadeas may have been behind it, but Dalinar shut down the suggestion. He explained to Adolin that they and Sadeas both had a vested interest and desire to keep Elhokar safe and would work together to manipulate other highprinces for the King's benefit. Dalinar then revealed [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar's]] final words. Adolin connected this to his father's presumed delusions and assumed they were a product of guilt over Gavilar's death. Dalinar hoped Adolin would learn to see the importance of the Codes. He then asked Adolin to at least respect Sadeas. Adolin reluctantly agreed and at his insistence Dalinar agreed to consider that Sadeas might be playing them.[^7]

Elhokar later queried them about their opinions on the strap. When Dalinar told him that while it may have been cut there was no definitive proof, the king insisted that there was a plot to kill him and that perhaps someone on the hunt had planned it. Adolin caught the king giving them a suspicious look, and when Elhokar left he asked his father if he had seen it too. Dalinar cut him off and told Adolin he would talk to Elhokar about it later. When the bridges finally arrived, Adolin resumed control of the army, directing its movements back towards the camps.[^7]

#### The Investigation

Adolin used a disastrous date with [Janala](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Janala "Janala") to visit different leatherworkers (including [Yis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yis "Yis") and [Avaran](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Avaran "Avaran")) with the King's saddle strap. They all agreed it had been sliced, but did not agree on whether it was cut or an accident. During the date, they visited the temple where Adolin had a talk with [Kadash](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kadash "Kadash"), one of his father's Ardents, about Dalinar's visions. Kadash was disturbed by the visions and dismissed them coming from the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty"). He said the only one who could help Dalinar would be Adolin, as he was family.[^58]

Two days later Adolin informed his father of his findings. Dalinar and Adolin agreed that the best action would be for Dalinar to tell Elhokar that they had found nothing. Adolin brought up the visions, but Dalinar ended the conversation as they arrived at one of the king's feasts. Before they parted ways, Dalinar ordered Adolin and Renarin to restrict their drinking.[^34]

During the feast, Adolin found his father once more and informed him that he had dueled three different men who had accused both Dalinar and House Kholin of being cowards and of asking the king to abandon the [Vengeance Pact](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vengeance_Pact "Vengeance Pact"). Dalinar told Adolin to ignore what others said or thought. He added that the rumors were true. Dalinar had told the king in confidence they needed to end the war and return to [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"). Dalinar asked for Adolin's trust and promised to talk later, but Adolin replied he was worried that the visions may be delusions. His father then acknowledged Adolin was right to be worried about their House's reputation. Dalinar thought that Elhokar would keep quiet about their conversation. He explained to Adolin he had suggested winning the war by striking the Parshendi directly.[^58] Once the Vengeance Pact was fulfilled they would be able to go back home and stabilize Alethkar.[^34] Adolin agreed with his father's plan without objection, to Dalinar's surprise. Adolin explained he could support a strike, but that they would need a solid plan to get everyone on board with it. They planned to slowly get the highprinces to start working together with them to build trust. As far as responding to the gossip, they disagreed when Adolin wanted to defend the house's honor. Dalinar countered by saying that dueling others wouldn't change how they thought about the Kholins. He would publish an official refutation, which Adolin admitted was better than nothing.[^34]

Just as Adolin was about to leave, his father spotted his aunt [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] and Adolin hugged her affectionately. She asked him how his courting was going, which Dalinar likened to a fast dance with changing partners. She told Adolin that it was only old people who should have to be boring. Adolin thanked her and then left to tell Renarin that their aunt had returned. Later that evening Elhokar announced that he had named Sadeas as [Highprince of Information](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highprince_of_Information "Highprince of Information") and the investigation was no longer in House Kholin's hands.[^34]

#### A House Divided

> “ *I know it's hard to accept, but sometimes, people get old. Sometimes, the mind stops working right.*”
> 
> —Adolin to Dalinar [^69]

Days later, Adolin went to see his father at the [Gallery of Maps](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gallery_of_Maps "Gallery of Maps"). Adolin revealed that he was now courting [Malasha Seveks](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Malasha_Seveks "Malasha Seveks"). They discussed Dalinar's meeting with Highprince [Roion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Roion "Roion"), who declined to work together. So, Dalinar planned to approach the others. Adolin brought up that Sadeas asked permission to enter their warcamp for his investigation. Adolin was worried about Sadeas' motives, but Dalinar pointed out that denying Sadeas's authority would undermine the king's as well besides making the Kholins look guilty.

To Adolin's dismay, Dalinar stated he was trusting Sadeas because of the visions. Adolin then accused his father of betting their house's future on the visions. Dalinar attempted to reassure Adolin by stating he would not stand by idle to see their house fall, and dismissed Adolin.[^69]

> “ *What you see is not real. Your life now is a rationalization, a way of trying to pretend that what's happening isn't happening. But I'll go to Damnation itself before I'll let you drag the entire house down without speaking my mind on it!*”
> 
> —Adolin to Dalinar [^69]

Dalinar and Adolin spoke to one another some days later when Dalinar called for the army to mobilize for a plateau skirmish. Adolin apologized for where and how he said his grievances to his father, though he was not sorry for voicing them. Just as they were about to set off, Sadeas demanded admittance to the war camp. Dalinar allowed him to accompany the plateau run to conduct the investigation, while Adolin was very suspicious of Sadeas' intent.[^44] Adolin, along with Dalinar, led the charge against the Parshendi. During the battle, when the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill") left Dalinar, Adolin was quick to help and protect his father. When the battle was won, Adolin harvested the [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemheart]].[^44]

Later, Adolin and his father inspected the army while discussing several topics like the purpose of Plate, the Thrill, the faith of the war camps, and the gemheart economy. During the conversation, Adolin was worried about his father's state of mind. At some point, he got distracted and thought about Malasha and the new woman he was interested in, [Danlan Morakotha](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Danlan_Morakotha "Danlan Morakotha"). Dalinar made Adolin inspect the Fifth Battalion for him, and afterwards his father praised him for his leadership. They went on to interview the men that were investigated by Sadeas. The men directly made it clear they were loyal to Kholin and hadn't told anything to Sadeas. Adolin noted that if they had acted this way around Sadeas, it would have seemed incriminating.[^9]

After the interview, Adolin confronted Dalinar about how he had been pushing him into a leadership position during the day. In response, Dalinar reminded Adolin of his comments earlier pertaining to his mental health. Moreover, Dalinar told him he had a difficult decision to make. At that moment, they were informed that Highprince [Thanadal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thanadal "Thanadal"), who was the last one Dalinar had to approach except Sadeas, had canceled their meeting. Thanadal had no intention of running a joint plateau assault, from which Dalinar inferred the other Highprinces had never liked his influence over the king. They would rather wait and see Dalinar fall, than let him regain his footing. Dalinar then left the rest of the inspections for Adolin to complete.[^9]

In the evening, before their talk with [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]], Adolin tried to talk to his father, but Dalinar wasn't ready to discuss things. Adolin then introduced Dalinar and Navani to Danlan, whom he had helped get a position as one of Dalinar's clerks. Dalinar expressed incredulity that Adolin had a new woman calling on him even though she had only been in camp for a day. The Parshendi tried to immobilize Adolin by lassoing him with ropes, but he managed to cut through them and fended the Parshendi off. The Parshendi were engaging Adolin in teams to keep him out of the battle where he'd be the most deadly. While jogging across the bridge to find out what was going on with the rest of the army, Adolin grabbed the arm of a man in a thick cloak whom he assumed was delivering something and asked where his father was. When the hood fell off, Adolin discovered the man was the Assassin in White.[^98]

The group split in Celebrant; Adolin went with Kaladin and Syl to change money and buy supplies. The group soon discovered that the city was under the control of the Fused. They narrowly escaped when Syl surrendered herself to a ship of honorspren.[^118] The honorspren sailed southwest, letting the group move freely on deck with the exception of Syl, who was kept captive. During the trip, Adolin had a long talk with Shallan, and they both revealed some of their secrets; Adolin confessed to her that he was the one who had killed Sadeas, and Shallan talked about her problems with her other identities. Shortly thereafter, they spotted eight Fused in pursuit of the ship.[^119] They were close to [Longbrow's Straits](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Longbrow%27s_Straits "Longbrow's Straits"), here a land area, with Thaylen City on the other side. Using the bead of a room, Adolin, Shallan, Kaladin, and their spren snuck into the bead ocean while the honorspren and Azure held off the Fused. Although he does so once Kaladin promises to aid Dalinar against [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai]],[^78] he is bothered by the interaction and doesn't trust Kaladin for a while longer, even after the bridgemen become Dalinar's bodyguards.[^10] [^17] Similarly, Kaladin holds on to his disdain and mentally mocks Adolin's apparent foppishness and arrogance.[^59] [^17] The tensions come to a head on the sparring grounds, when Kaladin loses his temper and attacks Adolin, who in turn carelessly throws Kaladin through the air with the strength of Shardplate.[^25]

After Szeth first attacks Dalinar and Adolin, Kaladin, and Dalinar manage to fight him off,[^82] Adolin begins to respect Kaladin in earnest. Though he remains suspicious of Kaladin's unexplained combat prowess, he keeps his suspicions to himself and refrains from mockery the next time they cross paths on the sparring grounds.[^36] [^53] Their interactions begin to be less openly hostile, if still somewhat awkward.[^84] [^88]

Their relationship really starts to turn around when Kaladin saves Adolin and Renarin in the [disadvantaged duel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adolin_Kholin#The_Duel "Adolin Kholin").[^61] Adolin realizes that Kaladin has shown himself to be trustworthy no matter what secrets he's keeping, so he voluntarily confines himself to prison for the duration of Kaladin's sentence out of thanks and in protest at Elhokar's unfair treatment, and even makes sure Bridge Four is gathered to meet Kaladin upon his release. Even though Adolin's cell is much nicer, Kaladin is deeply moved by the gesture.[^12] Adolin, for his part, doesn't hold Kaladin accountable for interfering with the plan to duel Sadeas, and he instantly believes Kaladin's accusations about Amaram, contrary to everyone else's skepticism.[^12]

After that, they begin to show real rapport as they work together to plan for Szeth's reappearance; Kaladin maintains a lingering grudge out of habit,[^33] but soon realizes that he truly does like Adolin and think of him as a good man.[^149] [^93] By the time of the [expedition to Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Siege_of_Kholinar#Siege "Siege of Kholinar"), they frequently joke around with each other and their mockery has a friendly tone rather than an antagonistic one.[^110] [^150] After Elhokar's death, when Kaladin begins to go into shock, Adolin is the one who makes sure to keep him moving and talking.[^112] [^113] [^115] Kaladin, in his depression, almost slips back into mocking Adolin in his head, but their friendship is strong enough by then that stops himself.[^151]

> “ *You don’t have to smile. You don’t have to talk. But if you’re going to be miserable, you might as well do it with friends.* ”
> 
> —Adolin [^42]

Adolin continues to watch out for Kaladin's mental health in Urithiru, dragging him out to socialize,[^42] helping plan his love life, and checking on him in Lirin's clinic.[^42] [^47] [^152] In fact, for a long time, practically the only times Kaladin goes out for fun are when Adolin gets him to, and he finds himself missing Adolin and Shallan when they are not with him.[^152] Without Adolin's support after Teft's death, Kaladin almost gives up on everything; only saying the [Fourth Ideal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words") with Dalinar's help allows him to pull through.[^153] [^154]

### Mayalaran

Adolin wins [[Mayalaran - The Coppermind|Mayalaran's]] Shardblade form in a duel well before becoming aware of her nature as a spren. Even without that knowledge, he treats his Blade with reverence and respect, speaking to it before every duel and thinking of himself as only borrowing it.[^23] Like many duelists, he thinks of the Blade as an extension of his soul and sometimes even alive in some sense, though it is not clear whether that is superstition or an instinctual understanding of Shardblades' true nature.[^105]

When he encounters Mayalaran's spren form in Shadesmar after the [Battle of Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_Kholinar "Battle of Kholinar"), he is initially unnerved by her deadeye appearance and behavior,[^115] but he soon overcomes that feeling, calling her a friend and wishing she didn't have to be locked up for her own safety.[^40] During the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]], he can feel her eagerness to fight a thunderclast, she speaks her name directly into his mind, and he is able to summon her in less than the usual ten heartbeats -- signs of the unusual bond they have been forming and Maya's partial revival.[^55] Even though gaining a Nahel bond would give him access to a live Shardblade, he refuses to entertain the idea, since it would require him to stop carrying Maya.[^42]

Their bond develops further during Adolin's next trip to Shadesmar. He takes care of Maya and treats her as a normal spren, despite her muteness and the skepticism of other spren about the point of interacting with a deadeye. She begins to show more and more responsiveness and initiative, such as by brushing Gallant and participating in Adolin's morning katas.[^56] [^39] She eventually even comes to his aid while he is rescuing Notum by performing a kata with a sword to keep the attackers off Adolin's back.[^49]

When the honorspren in Lasting Integrity attempt to use Maya as a sham witness in their trial, she surprises them by speaking up powerfully in defense of Adolin and the ancient Radiants, making it undeniable to all that Adolin's consistent care for Maya has caused something to happen that no one thought was possible -- restoration of a deadeye's consciousness, at least in a partial form. From then on, Maya is able to talk with some coherence, and explains a little about the Recreance to Adolin. Blended confirms that some kind of bond is forming between the two of them, but doesn't seem to know exactly what is happening.[^57]

## Development

> “ *I brought out \[Dalinar's\] son, who hero-worships his father, who could then say 'my father is going mad. I love this man, I have to protect him. But he's also going mad, what do I do?'* ”
> 
> —Brandon Sanderson [^155]

In the first draft of *[[The Way of Kings (in-world) - The Coppermind|The Way of Kings]]* (known as *[The Way of Kings Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_Prime "The Way of Kings Prime")*), Adolin is named **Aredor**, and is Dalinar's second son after Sheneras (since removed from the plot). At the start of the book, Sheneras dies while protecting [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]], making Aredor the heir.[^156] Throughout the book, Aredor serves a guide to Merin ([[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]]), introducing him to the world of the nobility.[^157] He is executed by Elhokar toward the end of the story,[^158] which is one of the reasons Dalinar ends up killing Elhokar. However, his death was not considered particularly relevant, as [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] was always the most important of the three siblings.[^159]

When Brandon began writing *The Way of Kings* proper, Aredor was retooled as Adolin. Originally, he did not have any viewpoints; this changed when Brandon shifted Dalinar's doubts about his own sanity to his son. This gave Adolin an internal conflict of what he should do about his father, a man he highly respects and who seems to be going insane, which in turn made both of them stronger characters.[^155]

## Trivia

- Adolin's name is derived from Alethi word *adoda*, "light", with the suffix *\-lin*, meaning "born unto".[^64] His name likely means "born unto light."
- Were they to exist on Roshar, he'd prefer onion rings over french fries.[^160]
- Brandon has said that Adolin and Shallan could be comfortable being in a polyamorous triad with Kaladin, indicating that he may have some level of interest in men. However, this is not further explored in the text.[^43]
- Adolin is the best swordsman Brandon has written, with the exception of Lan Mandragoran.[^161] This does not count immortals like the Heralds or [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Vasher]], since they have an unfair amount of experience.[^162]
- Adolin is a better dancer than Shallan or Kaladin.[^163]
- Adolin would not make a good Ringbearer.[^164]
