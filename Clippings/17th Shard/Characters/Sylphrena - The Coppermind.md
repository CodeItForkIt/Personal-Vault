## Sylphrena

> “ *I bind things, Kaladin. I am honorspren. Spirit of oaths. Of promises. And of nobility.* ”
> 
> —Sylphrena speaks to [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] about her nature [^1]

**Sylphrena**, or **Syl**, is an [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] who has formed a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] with [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]].[^1] A [[Splinter - The Coppermind|splinter]] of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]],[^2] she was created by the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] and is referred to among her kinsmen as the **Ancient Daughter**.[^3] She was bonded to [Relador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relador "Relador") prior to the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance").[^4]

Syl defies the wishes of both the Stormfather and the other honorspren when she decides to search for another worthy human to bond.[^5] [^6] [^7] Once bonded to Kaladin, she discovers anew what it is to be the [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] of a [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knight Radiant]], with the awakening of her mind attendant to that position.[^8] [^9] [^5] Kaladin struggles at times with the oaths required by their bond, and for a time his conflicting oaths and the confusion in his mind regarding what is honorable nearly kills Syl.[^10] [^11] However, Syl helps him through such times and eagerly explores the powers of a [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") with him so they can use them to their fullest.[^9] [^12] [^13] The bond with Kaladin becomes so integral a part of Sylphrena that a large part of her motivation is seeking to understand and help him.[^14]

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

In the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], Syl can take on a variety of appearances, including a ribbon of light,[^15] a flurry of leaves,[^16] flame,[^17] a small cloud.[^18] However, she prefers the form of a young woman about a handspan tall, with an angular face and flowing hair that fades into mist behind her head.[^19] Her size as a young lady can change, such as becoming smaller when afraid,[^20] or becoming human sized as she prefers.[^21] She usually wears a long, flowing dress, of a girlish cut.[^22] She will often augment it with various other articles of clothing, like a cap, a jacket or an umbrella, or swap it out for similar attire, like a [havah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Havah "Havah") with a [safehand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Safehand "Safehand").[^23] [^15] During the final ten days, she wore a [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]] uniform,[^24] though she switched this to a more regal attire after the death of the Stormfather.[^25] Regardless of her clothing, however, she always goes barefoot.[^26] In any form she takes, her entire form has a uniform, blue-white color, and glows with faint blue light. While [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and [Lunamor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lunamor "Lunamor") can always see her, to others, she's invisible unless she wills herself to be seen.[^27] As Kaladin and Syl's bond grows stronger, Syl is able to manifest her clothes in varying shades of blue; while she can even change her clothing to other colors, she is not able to change the color of her "skin." [^4] [^28]

Syl is also able to appear as a shape-shifting [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"). She occasionally appears as a sword or shield, but most often manifests as a spear.[^29] Common between all these forms are a physical makeup of glowing, silvery metal and glyphs engraved on the surface.[^29] [^30] As a Shardspear, the glyphs are swirling ones engraved along the spearhead.[^29] She is able to appear in varying lengths, but typically appears with a cylindrical grip, unadorned save for a foot long helical swirl of metal right before the tip. The spearhead has a single serration at the base where it connects to the grip.[^31] [^32] [^33] [^34] As a Shardblade, Syl typically appears sleek and beautiful, with a Windrunner glyph centered on the blade. Lines running off the glyph towards the hilt are reminiscent of her hair.[^30]

In [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), Syl is incapable of changing forms or of becoming invisible.[^35] There, she is human-sized, looking like a pretty girl with blue-white skin.[^36] She wears a dress but, like with her Physical Realm form, she is barefoot.[^26] This being said, while she is humanoid, she does not share human biology -- her eyes are decorative rather than functional, and she sees the world in the same way spren without human features, like the [Cryptics](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic"), do.[^35]

### Personality

> “ *I know this because I am intelligent and articulate. You should compliment me now.* ”
> 
> —Syl [^5]

Syl is generally cheerful and mischievous, often making jokes and playing pranks on those around her. She's highly irreverent, and can be forgetful at times.[^30] She's unflinchingly optimistic, always trying to see the best in people, and always believing that there's something good waiting in the future.[^37] She does have a bit of a prideful streak, although she often plays it for laughs, calling herself a "tiny piece of god" and trying to trick [Lunamor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lunamor "Lunamor") into building a shrine for her.[^5] She's constantly curious about the world around her, particularly about people and how they look and act, often using her invisibility to spy on them at an uncomfortably close distance.[^38] She has little sense of propriety in this regard -- at some point, she even sneaks in to observe people being intimate with each other.[^30]

She has a very strict code of ethics, despising deception and lies, although she eventually becomes more willing to be cunning.[^39] [^20] Syl believes that being a [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") is not about finding justice, and that the use of their power is only justified when protecting others.[^40] [^41] She can be very assertive and even angry when she perceives someone as acting in a wrong manner.[^41] [^42] However, she also believes in the "us versus them" duality in the war against [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], and becomes uneasy and confused when the lines grow blurry.[^43] She can be rather forceful in her attempts to do what she thinks is right, even when it could hurt the emotions of others. She despises people who wield dead [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade").[^30]

Syl is particularly focused on keeping [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] in a healthy mental state. She always tries to push him to keep going even when he's at his worst, and encourages him to let himself be happy.[^37] [^29] When he's in the depths of depression, she looks after him and tries to comfort him, often acting like his sounding board when he's unwilling to speak to others.[^26] However, while her efforts are always well-meaning, she can sometimes try and force him to act her way, even when he's perfectly capable of disagreeing.[^30] Eventually she turns her efforts towards her desire to understand Kaladin's feelings. After she asks [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] to help her, the [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] that results brings back Syl's past and causes her to sink into bouts of both grief and the depression naturally attendant to it.[^44] This gloominess is exacerbated by the corruption in the tower at the time but persists after the tower's functionality is restored.[^45]

Syl wants to be viewed as a person, rather than just as a spren. She begins being visible to more people than just Kaladin, appearing at a human size instead of small, and seeking to do things for her own sake.[^46] She struggles to find a balance between wanting to help Kaladin, but not having that be her sole motivation in life.[^47] [^21] One of the things she does just for herself is being a scribe [^48], and keeping a journal [^46].

## Abilities and Attributes

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-2.webp]]

by [Antti Hakosaari](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Antti_Hakosaari "Coppermind:Artists/Antti Hakosaari")

As a Shardspear

Like other larger spren, Syl can change her form and choose who to show herself to, making her an excellent spy.[^35] She is capable of moving and carrying small or light things, such as a leaf or a pen.[^37] [^49] She can float or fly through the air, as well as walk sideways, and is particularly good at finding things.[^50] She can also use [Adhesion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adhesion "Adhesion") to stick things together. She generally uses this to play pranks on people, though she has also applied this ability to assist people.[^19] [^51] She is capable of giving small "pinches of energy." She's also, like all [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren"), able to sense an oncoming [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] days prior.[^52] Following the death of the Stormfather, she is able to accept Words.[^25]

### Nahel Bond

> “ *I'm behind what is happening to you. I'm doing it. It's both of us. But without me, nothing would be changing in you. I'm... taking something from you. And giving something in return.* ”

As an honorspren, Syl has the capacity to bond with people, gaining higher sentience in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] in exchange for granting her partner -- currently [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] -- [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") abilities, including the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of Adhesion and [Gravitation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gravitation "Gravitation").[^9] Beyond that, once her Surgebinder reaches the Third Oath, she is able to transform into a living, shape-shifting Shardblade. In this form, she's capable of communicating with [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] telepathically.[^29]

However, there is a downside to this bond. When Kaladin strays from his Oaths and break them, Syl begins to lose her higher mental functions, to the point where she essentially dies.[^53] [^54] However, she is brought back once Kaladin returns to the path of the Windrunner and swears the Third Oath.[^13]

Syl also protects Kaladin from the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill").[^55]

## History

### Creation and Early Life

> “ *Sylphrena is both old and young. Old of form, but young of mind.* ”
> 
> — [Notum](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Notum "Notum") [^3]

Sylphrena was born some time before the [[Day of Recreance - The Coppermind|Day of Recreance]].[^3] She was created by the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] at the [Godforge](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Godforge "Godforge"),[^56] and was one of the first [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") to be created by the Stormfather rather than [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]].[^3] Although she was too young to form a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]], she did so anyway with a man named [Relador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relador "Relador").[^6] [^4] He took part in only one battle before dying, which sent Syl into a state of deep slumber. This led to everyone assuming she had passed away, but it also saved her from becoming a [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye") during the Recreance, as befell with so many of her siblings.[^3]

Over a thousand years later, she was found and reawakened. The Stormfather, not willing to let her come to any more harm, tasked [Notum](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Notum "Notum") with keeping her safe in one of honorspren cities.[^3] [^57] However, she wasn't happy with staying put. Sometime around the year, the winds drew her to [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]], a soldier in [Amaram's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") army.[^22] [^58] Against the wishes of other honorspren, she ran away to bond with him. This led to her kinsmen posting a huge bounty for bringing her back to their capital, [Lasting Integrity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lasting_Integrity "Lasting Integrity").[^7] [^3]

### Forming a Bond (1168 - 1173)

#### Meeting Kaladin

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-3.jpg]]

by [ThomasW](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/ThomasW "Coppermind:Artists/ThomasW")

Syl speaking with Kaladin for the first time

> “ *It seems that if you're worried about hurting people, you shouldn’t be afraid to help the bridgemen. What more could you do to them?* ”
> 
> —Encouraging [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] [^37]

Leaving [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") made Syl lose most of her cognitive ability. She became mostly mindless, acting much like her cousins, [windspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windspren "Windspren").[^19] [^59] However, she didn't forget her desire to accompany Kaladin, and followed him closely throughout his time in Amaram's army, during his battle with [[Helaran Davar - The Coppermind|Helaran Davar]], and, later, as he was a slave. Initially, she only played pranks on him, but in time his proximity began to return her mind and intelligence.[^19] [^9]

She did not speak to Kaladin until he found and kept the leaves of [blackbane](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blackbane "Blackbane"), a strong poison. Kaladin was initially unwilling to respond, thinking he was going insane, but eventually gave in.[^19] [^60] From then on out, she would accompany him everywhere. During his first bridge run, she urged him to get up and move, and as he spiraled further down, beaten by the constant battles, she tried to keep up his spirits.[^61] Eventually, however, she stated that she was unable to see him in that state any longer and departed.[^22]

Reminded of how Kaladin felt hope when he found blackbane, and unaware of its purpose, Syl left to look for more of the plant. By the time she returned, Kaladin was on the verge of suicide, and she found him standing on the edge of the [Honor Chasm](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honor_Chasm "Honor Chasm"). When she finally managed, with significant difficulty, to bring him the leaf, Kaladin was initially amused that she'd thought a poison would make him feel better. In the ensuing conversation, she managed to convince him to give life another try.[^37]

#### Bridge Four

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-4.png]]

by [Ashley Coad](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ashley_Coad "Coppermind:Artists/Ashley Coad")

Keeping Kaladin safe from the [deathspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deathspren "Deathspren")

> “ *Soon you'll hardly be a spren at all. You’ll be a little translucent philosopher. We’ll have to send you off to a monastery to spend your time in deep, important thoughts.* ”

Though Kaladin's initial attempts to sway over [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]] weren't very successful, Syl continued to support him. He started using her as a spy -- initially, to keep an eye on [Gaz](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gaz "Gaz"), though she eventually began looking after the other bridgemen as well.[^8] [^27] She continued to discuss various topics with Kaladin in hopes of understanding humans. It was during that time that she first noticed [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]], and immediately believed him to be an honorable man, despite her partner's misgivings.[^27]

Shortly afterwards, Syl and Kaladin discovered that [Rock](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rock "Rock"), one of the other bridgemen, could see her.[^27] From then on out, when Bridge Four was given chasm duty, she assisted both him and Kaladin in searching for valuable items.[^16] [^18] She also continued spying on [Gaz](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gaz "Gaz").[^62]

After the catastrophe brought about by Bridge Four's side carry, Syl stayed with Kaladin when he was left out in the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] as a punishment and protected him, first by trying to hold back the storm, then by fighting off the [deathspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deathspren "Deathspren") attracted to him with a sword.[^63] [^64] Afterwards, she began to remember a little of her past, and though they were only vague memories, they were enough for her to be happy at hearing the [First Ideal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words#First_Ideal "Immortal Words") of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] and to react with hissing at the mention of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^65] [^66]

As Bridge Four continued with their chasm duty, Syl eventually revealed herself to all of them at Kaladin's urging as she continued to assist them all.[^67] As Kaladin began to notice his budding [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], she admitted that she was no ordinary windspren; although she still didn't remember too much, she knew her bond with Kaladin was responsible for what was happening to them both.[^9] From then on out, she encouraged Kaladin to train his abilities.[^68]

Things came to a head during the [Battle of the Tower](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_the_Tower "Battle of the Tower"). There, Syl manifested as a full-sized figure and remembered her true nature. She encouraged Kaladin to say the [Immortal Words](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words") and accompanied him during the battle.[^1] Afterwards, she pushed Kaladin further to trust Dalinar, leading to Bridge Four becoming the Kholin bodyguards.[^69]

#### Training a Windrunner

> “ *Something dangerous is coming.* ”

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-5.jpg]]

by [Connor Chamberlain](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Connor_Chamberlain "Coppermind:Artists/Connor Chamberlain")

Syl Human Sized

Syl's respect for Dalinar only grew after he discarded [Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer_\(Shardblade\) "Oathbringer (Shardblade)") to free the companies of [bridgemen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bridgemen "Bridgemen"), and she continued to push Kaladin to trust him more.[^5] She also told Kaladin about the possibility of other Surgebinders being out there and insisted that he become what Dalinar needed, as she began to notice [stormspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormspren "Stormspren"), Odium's servants, out and about.[^5] When [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") arrived, causing Kaladin's opinion of Dalinar to drop by his association, Syl insisted that he speak with Dalinar about his experience at Amaram's hands and be frank with him; he did so, though it was seemingly to no avail.[^42]

It was around this time that Kaladin began backsliding on his oaths, causing Syl to worry about him.[^40] Nonetheless, she stuck by him and gave him a warning when [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] was coming to assassinate Dalinar. She also immediately recognized that Szeth was not a Surgebinder, despite his abilities.[^70] Despite her increasing knowledge, though, she was still surprised at finding out that Kaladin could heal Shardblade wounds.[^71]

Even as Kaladin's mental state continued to deteriorate, Syl still insisted that Dalinar could be trusted and that Kaladin should open up to him, especially after learning about [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash's]] plot to kill [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]].[^72] [^73] Eventually, after a successful training in the chasms that saw Kaladin learn how to fly, and her sharing more information about Surgebinding, he was almost convinced, but his motivation evaporated when he saw that Dalinar made Amaram the head of the new Knights Radiant.[^58] [^74] Syl still managed to convince him that things would be different during [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin's]] four-on-one duel. Unfortunately, she was wrong, and when Kaladin asked to challenge Amaram as a [boon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/King%27s_Boon "King's Boon") he was arrested.[^75]

#### Near-Death and Return

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-6.jpg]]

by [botanicaxu](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu "Coppermind:Artists/botanicaxu")

Kaladin summoning Syl as a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade")

After Kaladin was arrested, Syl was disappointed and subdued.[^75] Soon, Kaladin began to abandon his Oaths in his mind, causing her to begin losing her sapience while in the Physical Realm.[^53] She became more childlike and easily distracted, losing her bond with Kaladin to the point where he could not draw upon [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]]. Nonetheless, she retained enough presence of mind to remind him to say the words, and to provide him with one last breath of Stormlight when he fell into the chasms.[^76] [^54] After that, Kaladin did not see or hear her for some time, except for a distant weeping,[^77] and the Stormfather claimed she had died.[^10] An honorspren at Lasting Integrity later claimed she had *almost* died.[^11]

As Kaladin began to slowly return to his Oaths, growing wary of Moash's plan, his connection to her deepened again.[^78] Finally, he took a stand to protect [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] from the would-be assassins. Syl attempted to return to Kaladin, but the Stormfather prevented her and complained of her disobedience,[^79] revealing he had been sheltering her, in an attempt to protect her from actually dying. However, the Nahel bond had regained enough strength for her to speak in Kaladin's mind, encouraging him to say the Words. He did so, and once he swore the Third Ideal, the Stormfather could no longer prevent Syl from returning to the Physical Realm. She returned as a ribbon of light and then as a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade"), and Kaladin regained his powers.[^13]

> “ *Kaladin! Stretch forth thy hand!*”
> 
> —Just before becoming a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") [^13]

After scaring off Moash and [Graves](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Graves "Graves"), the two set out to protect [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] from [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]], who was attacking in the chaos of the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]].[^80] In the ensuing fight, she showcased her ability to transform into many different weapons, culminating with Kaladin using her to slice through Szeth's wrist, breaking his tie to the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]]. After making sure that Kaladin took the Blade with him, Syl demanded -- and received -- a smile from him.[^81]

### The True Desolation (1174)

#### Hearthstone and Kholinar

![[Sylphrena---The-Coppermind-7.png]]

by [Ashley Coad](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Ashley_Coad "Coppermind:Artists/Ashley Coad")

Syl coming to [Hearthstone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hearthstone "Hearthstone") with Kaladin

> “ *The bond isn't about what’s right and wrong, is it, Syl. It’s about what **you see** as right and wrong.* ”

Immediately after the Battle of Narak, Syl explained to Kaladin that other Shardblades were dead spren, and that [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]] were something different.[^81] A few days later, the two left for [Hearthstone](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hearthstone "Hearthstone"), Kaladin's hometown, to make sure that his family was safe.[^82] After they confirmed that [Lirin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lirin "Lirin") and [Hesina](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hesina "Hesina") --along with the newest addition to the family, [Oroden](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oroden "Oroden") --were fine, the journey turned into a hunt for the [Parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen") who departed in the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]].[^83] [^30]

During that time, Syl began to worry about Kaladin's happiness, eventually growing to believe that he needed to find himself a romantic partner. Though she lightly teased him about some girls and [ardents](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardent "Ardent") met on the way, and revealed that she had been spying on people in their intimate moments, her true goal was to get Kaladin together with [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], as she believed that the [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweaver]] was making him happy.[^30]

This fun ended when Syl and Kaladin came across a group of former Parshmen and Kaladin willingly gave himself up to see where the encounter would go.[^84] Noticing that there was a [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]] accompanying the group, Syl began to act like a windspren again in order to fool her.[^20] This being said, as [Yixli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yixli "Yixli") wasn't always present with the newly-minted [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], she did have the time to discuss matters with Kaladin. During one such conversation, she admitted that she didn't know why killing singers didn't feel wrong to her when killing humans did.[^52]

The two eventually departed in a [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] after seeing a [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] in [Rathalas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rathalas "Rathalas").[^43] Once back in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], Kaladin focused on training his squires, with Syl accompanying him. At one point, she noticed some of her old [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") acquaintances, and noted that they were not very fond of her, even though they had also begun searching for bondmates.[^85]

After briefly going to [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") to open its [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]], she and Kaladin departed for the besieged [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), accompanying [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]], [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]], Shallan and a few squires and guards.[^86] Once in the city, Syl was the first to notice the [[Sja-anat - The Coppermind|Sja-anat]] -corrupted spren.[^87] While Kaladin was working with the [Kholinar Wall Guard](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar_Wall_Guard "Kholinar Wall Guard"), Syl assisted him in various minor ways.[^51] During one battle in particular, she managed to point him towards destroying a Fused [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemheart]], which was the quickest way to kill it.[^88]

During the battle for [Kholinar Palace](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar_Palace "Kholinar Palace"), Syl accompanied Kaladin as a weapon. There, he managed to kill the voidspren toying with [Gavinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor "Gavinor"), which left her shaken by the impossibility of the deed.[^89] Still, she continued to fight, although despite everyone's best efforts, she, along with Adolin, Kaladin, Shallan, [[Pattern - The Coppermind|Pattern]], and the Wall Guard commander, [Azure](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azure "Azure"), ended up dropped into [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") after trying to unlock the Oathgate.[^90]

#### Journey Through Shadesmar

> “ *You'd better catch me before I scamper away! Wow! I’m feeling capricious today. I might just vanish again, off to where nobody can find me!* ”
> 
> —Talking to the crew of *[Honor's Path](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honor%27s_Path "Honor's Path")* [^7]

Once in Shadesmar, Syl took on a humanoid and human-sized form and began acting more like a physical creature.[^90] Early on, her focus was on keeping Kaladin functioning in the face of his ongoing mental breakdown caused by the events of the palace battle.[^82] Eventually, he felt good enough to ask her if she could carry a message to Dalinar, and she explained that she would lose her mind if she strayed too far from him into the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]].[^26]

Crossing the Perpendicularity, Syl returned to her semi-corporeal form and accompanied Kaladin as a weapon in the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]].[^96] After the battle, the two discussed once more the righteousness of fighting [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], who were only trying to regain their home; Kaladin decided that he would still fight for humanity's survival and reassured Syl that he would not kill her, or any other sapient spren, ever again.[^97] Eventually, the two went to rescue [Skar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skar "Skar") and [Drehy](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Drehy "Drehy"), two of Kaladin's squires who had managed to get Gavinor out of Kholinar.[^98]

After Lyn reported an incoming group of [Heavenly Ones](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heavenly_Ones "Heavenly Ones"), the Windrunners spread out and prepared to engage them. Kaladin and Syl fought [Leshwi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leshwi "Leshwi") for a while, Syl shouting a parting insult as the Fused outpaced them. When [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") came to check on Kal, the encounter roused his suspicions that Syl had asked Bridge Four to watch out for him. They made for a very even match, and just as they both dealt serious blows to each other, Leshwi gave Kaladin and Syl leave to go after some civilians at the manor.[^101]

Syl warned Kaladin that the setup felt like a trap designed for a Windrunner. Indeed, when one of the soldiers activated a strange [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]] fabrial, Syl was forced into her form as a spren and Kaladin lost his ability to Surgebind. He managed to kill Lezian anyway, and then Syl encouraged him to go after Roshone.[^102] In the cellar, Syl warned Kal when she saw [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]] behind him. In the form of a spear, she was with Kal while Moash brought up his most painful memories and tried to talk him into killing himself; Syl tried to speak to Kal, but he was to embroiled in his own emotions and couldn't hear her. They were saved when [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] created an illusion of Moash and chased him away.[^103] Back on the ship, Syl informed Kal that [Laran](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Laran "Laran") had sworn the Third Ideal and suggested that he go congratulate her. Watching him withdraw into himself again, she told him she didn't know how to help him. She told him that he had been getting worse, and though he had to admit to himself that she was right, he didn't know what to do about it either.[^104]

As the Windrunners approached the Shattered Plains, Syl informed Kaladin that [Yunfah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yunfah "Yunfah") was in the area and wished to speak with him. Syl acted as an intermediary, as the wind from flying kept Kaladin from hearing anyone except her. Yunfah was angry that Kaladin kept wanting him to bond [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]], but Syl suggested that he keep pushing the spren. Kaladin gave him ten days to try to work with Rlain before he could look for someone else, and he reluctantly left. They landed at the new fort being built at Narak and spoke with Dalinar, to whom Syl apologized on behalf of her honorspren relatives. She remained silent, only looking on in concern as Dalinar relieved Kaladin of duty and confirming that he wasn't abandoning his oaths to think of serving the war effort in some other way.[^105] When Kaladin and the Windrunners returned to Urithiru, Syl went after Adolin. She brought him to Kaladin's room, where Adolin badgered Kal into going out with him for the evening. As they left, Kaladin thanked Syl. They went to [Jez's Duty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jez%27s_Duty "Jez's Duty"), where Syl flitted around the room and enjoyed looking at the people, riding on [Jor's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jor "Jor") shoulder for a while and only returning to the others briefly.[^106]

#### Invasion

A few days later, Syl flew into Jez's Duty just ahead of [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]]. She sat on Kaladin's shoulder while they talked about war, their search for [Dabbid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dabbid "Dabbid"), and Kaladin's new group therapy sessions. She was pleased when Kaladin admitted that his work with the others with mental illness was helping him and agreed to attend a group session himself.[^113] As [Raboniel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel") and her strike team began corrupting the crystal pillar far away in the basement, Syl suddenly found herself separated from Kaladin. It took her the duration of Kaladin's climb up to Lirin's clinic until she found a way to pull herself back to Kaladin with his help. She could no longer become a Blade, and when she tried to go to Navani to report on the invasion, she could only go a little ways from Kaladin before she began losing herself. She brought him news that Navani knew about what was going on and was sending out messengers, but there were more and more enemy forces arriving all the time through the Oathgates.[^114]

They continued to the [Devotary of Mercy](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Devotary_of_Mercy "Devotary of Mercy"), where Kaladin gained [Kuno's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kuno "Kuno") promise of future help and supplies before returning to their hideout.[^120]

#### Nodes

As Kaladin took care of Teft with their new supplies, he finally convinced Syl to open up to him about the cause of her recent melancholy moods. She explained about remembering [Relador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relador "Relador"), and they discussed the best way to deal with feelings of sadness and depression. Syl practiced changing colors until they were startled by a noise at the door, which turned out to be [Dabbid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dabbid "Dabbid"). Leaving him with Teft, Syl accompanied Kaladin as he went to investigate the Oathgates. She scouted for him as he climbed the outside of the tower, and when a group of scouts began packing up their supplies, she realized they must have had a functioning [spanreed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spanreed "Spanreed") with them.[^4] Knowing of Navani's desire to discover how the invading force had functioning [[Fabrial - The Coppermind|fabrials]], Syl and Kaladin gave chase to the group. Having looked ahead, Syl brought Kaladin news of a spanreed hub and also pointed out to him that spren were more visible to everyone than usual because of the corruption in the tower. Deciding it was worth it to proceed despite the added danger of discovery, Kaladin came up with the idea of Syl pretending to be a Voidspren to fool the guards. She wrapped herself in red cloth and successfully distracted the guards while Kaladin snuck past. Approaching the spanreed room via a ventilation shaft, Kaladin snared some fabrials with a rope and relied on Syl to check that the hallway was clear before exiting the shaft. As Syl was preparing to distract the guards again in the guise of a Voidspren, however, the Sibling contacted Kaladin and asked for his help defending one of their nodes.[^28]

Kaladin and Syl raced for the node, guided by the Sibling's light. The Pursuer immediately engaged Kaladin, and Syl used her new trick of imitating a Voidspren to distract him for a moment, giving Kaladin a chance to get free. They managed to trap the Pursuer in a room, then returned to the node and destroyed it.[^121] After a night's rest, Kaladin was able to give Navani a report on the preceding day; he also reported to her Syl's findings from her studies of the spanreeds they had acquired. Syl determined that the spren inside were corrupted in a similar manner to [[Glys - The Coppermind|Glys]], allowing them to operate with [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]].[^122] As Kal continued to have nightmares, Syl helped by trying to wake him. He tended to Teft after one such time, and she told him about asking Dalinar to help her understand his feelings. Dabbid came in and delivered Tomor's fabrial to them, and after a half hour Syl reminded Kaladin of his meeting with Navani. After the queen explained to him about placing the conjoined rubies, Syl accompanied him to the ninth floor to do so and then supervised as he practiced with the fabrial.[^44]

During another of Kaladin's nightmares, Syl attempted to wake him to tell him that the enemy had found another node in the [Breakaway](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Breakaway "Breakaway"). When he finally woke, she quickly apprised him of the situation. They caught up to Dabbid, who had taken Kal's spear with the intention of dealing with the problem himself, and rushed onwards. Syl warned Kaladin of the Pursuer's approach; as he fought with the Fused, she was able to help him by turning the dial on his fabrial for him. She scouted out the location of the node, noting its position in the well. Kaladin broke away from the fight at her signal, and she guided him into the water and to the sapphire. Though Raboniel was already there and managed to injure Kaladin, he successfully destroyed the node. Syl led him upwards again, but as she shot out of the well, the Pursuer maneuvered the lid into place and weighted it with his troops. Panicking and terrified, Syl realized that Kaladin was stuck below.[^123] As Kaladin swam away, Syl likely lost her ability to stay manifested in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] but stayed nearby out of instinct.[^124] She entered a state similar to that after the death of Relador, but when Kaladin called out to her the next time he went near the Breakaway, she heard him and broke through back to the Physical Realm.[^125]

Kaladin explained to Syl that the enemy had found the Sibling's final node and were planning on executing the Radiants, so she accompanied him straight to the atrium.[^125] Syl flew around the struggling pair as Kaladin and the Pursuer fought.[^126] When they saw Teft's body, though, both Syl and Kaladin felt crushed by the loss. One of the Fused took off with Lirin, and the two of them followed to the top of the tower. Syl cried out to Kaladin that she had forgotten the words to the [[Order of Windrunners - The Coppermind|Fourth Ideal]], but an unheeding Kaladin jumped from the roof.[^127] As he fell, Syl continued to weep over forgetting the words.[^128] Suddenly, however, Kaladin returned from his vision and reached out for her again.[^129] Together, they flew downwards to save Lirin [^130] and then returned to the fight in Urithiru.[^131]

Syl encouraged Kaladin to ask Dalinar for permission to keep doing his work with those with mental illnesses. After granting it, Dalinar revealed to them their new time limit regarding the contest of champions. ### The Final Ten Days

On the first of the final ten days, Syl and Kaladin talked about Syl's decision to manifest in a human size in the Physical Realm, and she explained that this size made people treat her more like a person.[^133] [^24] They also discussed Kaladin's improved mental state, their shared grief over Teft, and the upcoming battles. Syl sensed something on the wind, and Kaladin heard a voice talking to him, though Syl could not hear this.[^133] Syl played with Oroden and chatted to Hesina before she and Kaladin left to meet with Dalinar.[^24]

> “ *I felt... more like a person. Less like a force of nature. I'm finding I missed that.*”
> 
> —Syl on being in Shadesmar [^24]

Syl flew off to find Navani, and returned to find Wit teaching Kaladin to play the flute. Wit warned them that they might never return to Urithiru after this mission, and left.[^134] Dalinar explained that Szeth would be accompanying them to [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], and the three of them flew to a mountain peak near Urithiru to talk. Syl was fully visible to Dalinar during this conversation and added her own input, which Dalinar found odd, as the other honorspren typically only appeared to their Radiants. Dalinar asked Kaladin to be his heir, and Syl assured him that being royalty wasn't all that bad.[^135]

Syl and Kaladin went together to say their goodbyes to the various members of Bridge Four. While talking with Leyten, Syl spotten the honorspren [Lusintia](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lusintia "Lusintia") trailing after Dabbid.[^136] They visited Skar,[^48] and then Syl led Kaladin to the library to collect a copy of [The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)"), as well as some stationery. The librarians were dismissive of Syl's newfound writing ability, causing Kaladin to berate them.[^49] Syl went with Kaladin to say goodbye to all the rest of Bridge Four, and they left for Shinovar with Szeth.[^137] They stopped in [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir") along the way, and said their farewells to Adolin and Shallan. They promised that at the end of the Desolation, they'd meet up for drinks. Syl worried that Kaladin had had a sense of finality about him all day, but Kaladin reassured her that they would survive the coming days.[^138]

They arrived in Shinovar the next day, and both Syl and Kaladin were bemused by the plantlife there.[^139] Szeth was refusing to open up to Kaladin about his past, and Syl comforted him when he grew frustrated. The [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind") then spoke to Syl and Kaladin to warn them of Odium. Ishar approached them as well, but they couldn't convince him to help them and Dalinar before he left. As part of this discussion, Ishar mentioned that Szeth was his servant, which confused Syl and Kaladin.[^140]

Syl bantered with [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] and Kaladin, before they arrived at Szeth's family's farmhouse. Inside, they found a terrified [lifespren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lifespren "Lifespren"), and Syl explained that the spren could all sense that their potential end was drawing near, and that there was something wrong in Shinovar. Kaladin asked her why she had been more withdrawn recently, and she told him that she'd realized she needed to start living for herself, instead of just for him. He agreed, and said they would figure it out together.[^141]

They arrived at the [Bondsmith monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith_monastery "Bondsmith monastery"), and Syl translated for Kaladin as Szeth spoke with the locals.[^142] Syl and Kaladin stood by and watched as Szeth fought the Honorbearer, and went with him to check on the locals.[^142] They then continued on to the [Willshaper monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Willshaper_monastery "Willshaper monastery").[^143] Along the way, Syl encourage Szeth to let Kaladin help him, and Szeth seemed to consider this. After finding [Sivi-daughter-Sivi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sivi-daughter-Sivi "Sivi-daughter-Sivi") dead, they moved on towards the next monastery.[^144] They stopped to camp that night, and Syl sat with Kaladin as he practiced the flute. She read to him from *The Way of Kings* and scribed a report back to Urithiru.[^46]

> “ *I returned to the Physical Realm because I enjoy it here(...) I remind myself of that. I'm a person, and I chose.*”
> 
> —On bonding Kaladin [^46]

Syl and Kaladin talked again about trying to live for themselves instead of other people, which prompted Kaladin to ask Syl if she wanted to do a kata with him. She agreed, and the two danced, Syl switching from spear to humanoid and back. The Wind joined them, eventually prompting Kaladin to return to his flute, while Syl listened.[^46]

In the next monastery,Syl and Kaladin find Szeth crying over a toy sheep. Kaladin assumed that Szeth was mourning a child he could not protect, but Syl helps Kaladin realize that Szeth *was* the child. While Kaladin talked with Szeth, Syl retrieved the sheep and gave it to Kaladin to carry. [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] then arrived, fetched by [12124](https://coppermind.net/wiki/12124 "12124") to supervise Szeth.[^145]

Syl scouted ahead as they journeyed to the [Lightweaver monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lightweaver_monastery "Lightweaver monastery").[^146] Szeth was faced with many illusions of [[Shalash - The Coppermind|Shalash]], and his task was to determine which was the Honorbearer he had to kill. He would only be given one attempt, and his spren was not allowed to help him. Instead, Syl secretly followed him, giving him advice and keeping him calm. Szeth figured it out, and stabbed his Shardblade through a small gap in the floor, killing [Moss-son-Farrier](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moss-son-Farrier "Moss-son-Farrier").[^147] Afterwards, Syl still felt uneasy, and realized that one of the acolytes was also going to try to kill Szeth. She warned Kaladin, and he summoned her as a Shardspear just in time to prevent the Truthwatcher Honorbearer from killing Szeth.[^21]

That night, Syl noticed that something had changed in her bond with Kaladin, and he seemed more peaceful than before. 12124 approached to ask for Kaladin's help, and Syl was skeptical, due to her distrust of highspren. After they talked, Syl found she could empathize a bit more with the other spren. The Wind spoke to Kaladin and Syl again, saying that she would need to ask much more of them in the days to come. The Wind showed them a few fragments of visions of Nale saving people during the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], and asked Kaladin to help the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"). They then spotted all the spren of Shinovar moving together in a giant group, headed towards the final monasteries.[^148]

After Szeth faced [Elid-daughter-Zeenid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elid-daughter-Zeenid "Elid-daughter-Zeenid"), Nale admitted that Szeth's pilgrimage was an audition for him to take [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] place in the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]]. Syl argued with Nale's logic, pointing out that reforming the Oathpact wouldn't necessarily seal the Fused, and that Nale had previously been fighting on the side of the singers anyway. Nale ignored her.[^149]

The day before the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|Contest of champions]], Syl mentioned her dislike of Nale to Kaladin, who agreed. The Wind spoke to them again, exhorting them to help the Herald. They left with Szeth to the [Skybreaker monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skybreaker_monastery "Skybreaker monastery").[^150] Nale waited for them there, and asked Szeth to vow to follow Nale's orders and become a Herald. Szeth refused, and Nale moved to kill Szeth, but Kaladin blocked the blow with Syl as a Shardspear. Syl expressed her worry for Kaladin, but agreed that a fight with Nale was likely the only way forward.[^151] The duel went on for quite some time, with Kaladin barely managing to defend himself. Syl spoke in Kaladin's mind, reminding him of Nale's previous quest to find and kill all budding Radiants. Eventually, Nale beat Kaladin against the wall and threw him across the room, and went to deal the killing blow. Syl stood in his way, trying to remind him of his morals, but Nale stepped through her.[^152]

The Wind interrupted him, playing the melody of the Wandersail. Kaladin drew in some Stormlight to heal, and summoned Syl as a flute. Syl warned Kaladin that he should run from Nale, but Kaladin refused. He played the first few notes of the melody, and began telling the story of Derethil to Nale. The Wind continued to play the song through Syl as Kaladin spoke. Nale realized that he had forgotten his convictions, and broke down crying. Syl reappeared in her humanoid form beside him.[^153]

Nale explained to the others that there was no [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] in Shinovar, and Syl worked out that 'the Voice' Szeth had heard was in fact [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]].[^154]

#### Night of Sorrows

> “ *The last remnants of Honor lie here, in the hands of a broken circle of broken men and broken women. I see. Damnation, Kaladin. I see.* ”
> 
> —Syl before the refounding of the Oathpact [^25]

Syl, Kaladin, Szeth and Nale chatted along the way to the final monastery,[^56] and Nale told them what he and Ishar had been planning.[^155] They tried to figure out how to return Ishar to his senses, but found nothing conclusive.[^156] Szeth convinced Kaladin and Syl to stand by as he fought the Honorbearers, and try to talk to Ishar.[^157]

Joining the Oathpact

Kaladin summoned Syl as a flute again and began to play Wandersail, but Ishar immediately dismissed this, as he was the one who had written the story.[^158] Syl tried to convince Ishar to accept their help, and grew worried for the spren when he refused.[^159] Ishar then Connected everyone present to the mental anguish of the Heralds that he had taken on,[^160] and Syl collapsed. Kaladin fought it, and Syl tried to get up as well. Kaladin swore his Fifth Ideal,[^161] allowing him to free the other from Ishar's attack.[^162]

After the deaths of Dalinar and the Stormfather, [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]] was going to consume all spren, as they were now in his power. Syl began to cry, having realized what Kaladin would need to do, but he reassured her that he was willing. Kaladin spoke the Words to join the Oathpact, and it was Syl's voice that accepted them, in lieu of the Stormfather. Together, Kaladin and Syl reached their hands into the circle of Honorblades, and drew out a Shardspear.[^25] Syl held Kaladin's hand as his soul was ripped from his body, and left Roshar.[^163]

Although their souls still went to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], the minds of the Heralds entered a vision of [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] in the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]], and Syl and [[121 - The Coppermind|121]] went as well.[^25] [^164]

## Relationships

### Kaladin

> “ *It is my solemn and important duty to bring **happiness**, **light**, and **joy** into your world when you're being a dour idiot.* ”
> 
> —Syl to Kaladin [^30]

Syl first begins watching [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] during his time in [Amaram's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Meridas_Amaram "Meridas Amaram") army.[^22] As she follows him during his slavery and to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]], she develops more and more awareness.[^8] She shows her care for Kaladin early on, both when she stands before him when he is strung up in the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]] and when she brings him [blackbane](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Blackbane "Blackbane") leaves and talks him out of jumping at the [Honor Chasm](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honor_Chasm "Honor Chasm").[^63] [^37] The two of them form a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]], and Kaladin finally says the words to become a [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") of the Second Ideal at the [Battle of the Tower](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_the_Tower "Battle of the Tower") after Syl convinces him that it is the right choice to go back for the Kholin troops.[^1]

Kaladin's sense of honor becomes confused for a time when [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]] talks to him about helping assassinate [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]]. This causes Syl to regress closer and closer to her state like a [windspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windspren "Windspren") and eventually "kills" her.[^76] [^10] When Kaladin confronts Moash with the intent of keeping him from the king, however, Syl is able to make her way back to Kaladin. They restore their bond by swearing the Third Ideal.[^13] After this experience, the two think more deeply about what makes killing right and discuss how the ethics of that question relates to their bond.[^43]

Because she wants Kaladin to be happy, Syl begins talking to him about relationships. She sees that Kaladin enjoys being around [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], so she tries to encourage him in that direction even though Shallan is spoken for.[^30] This idea does not pan out, as Shallan definitively chooses [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] during the time of the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]].[^97] However, Syl only consults Adolin for advice next, and together they convince Kaladin to date [Lyn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lyn "Lyn") for a time; this relationship doesn't work out either.[^113] [^99]

Seeing that Kaladin's mood continues to worsen, Syl tries to talk to him about it but is rebuffed.[^99] The problem is exacerbated when [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] informs Kaladin of his decision to retire him from active duty. Kal crashes, and Syl goes to the only person she knows of who can pull him out of such a state: Adolin.[^106] Perplexed and desperately wanting to help Kaladin, Syl goes to Dalinar and asks him if there is anything he can do to help her understand Kal's emotions. Somehow in this encounter Dalinar [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connects]] Syl to her past and she begins to remember and to grieve over [Relador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relador "Relador").[^14] [^44] Kaladin notices her gloomy moods, and they have the opportunity to talk more freely about how to deal with such times, Syl with more empathy and experience than ever before.[^117] [^44] [^45] Kaladin and Syl work together to help Syl find purpose outside of just supporting Kaladin.[^47]

### The Stormfather

> “ *You do not exist for \[the humans\]. You exist for you. You exist to choose.* ”
> 
> —The Stormfather to Syl [^14]

Syl is one of the first [honorspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorspren "Honorspren") that the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], rather than [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], created. Because she is the only one to survive the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), he feels very protective of her.[^3] The Stormfather commands her return and either approves of or instigates a bounty after Syl sneaks away to bond Kaladin. This changes with time, however, as he both accepts their bond and bonds a human himself.[^7] [^165] After the Stormfather dies, Syl is distraught, wondering if she ever truly knew him.[^25]

### Relador

> “ *He went to fight, despite his age. He shouldn't have, and when he was killed, it hurt.* ”
> 
> —Syl telling [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] about Relador [^14]

[Relador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Relador "Relador") is the first man Syl bonded as a [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner").[^14] He died shortly before the events of the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"), sending Syl into a catatonic state. Because of this, she avoided the fate of the majority of her kinsmen, who became [deadeyes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye").[^166] When [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connects]] her to her past, Syl begins experiencing grief over this loss. This helps her understand Kaladin better.[^44]

## Trivia

- Sylphrena is one of the few characters who have spoken in small-caps.[^13]
- In an earlier version of *The Way of Kings*, Syl was named "The East Wind." [^167]
- Sylphrena originally came from an unpublished Cosmere work called *Climb the Sky*.[^168]
- Sylphrena's name is inspired by the word "sylph", or air spirit, and "sylphlike" can be used to describe a slender young woman, both of which fit Syl to a certain degree.[^169]
- Syl was written with [ADHD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ADHD "wikipedia:ADHD") -like mannerisms, though it is hard to tell if she can be properly diagnosed with it because she is a spren and not human, and therefore has a different psychology.[^170]
- Syl’s favorite color would be blue, but she would probably say sparkles or sequins.[^171]
