## Chanarach

> “ *Chana help the man or woman who stood between [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]] and one of her own.* ”
> 
> — [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] [^3]

**Chanarach**, known as **Chanaranach'Elin** in Vorinism and nicknamed **Chana**, is one of the ten [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of the [[Honor - The Coppermind|Almighty]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], and the patron of the [[Order of Dustbringers - The Coppermind|Order of Dustbringers]].[^4] [^5] In more recent times, she married [Lin Davar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lin_Davar "Lin Davar") and lived with him on the Davar Estate with their four children and Lin's eldest son whom he had with another woman.[^6]

## Appearance and Personality

Chana was born on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], and left it for [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] when the planet was razed by human [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]], making her likely of similar age to the other [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald").[^7] [^8] [^9] Vorin artwork tends to present her as a young woman.[^10] Vorin depictions also show her wearing blue armor and holding a straight sword with a bifurcated blade. Chana's [safehand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Safehand "Safehand") is not covered in these depictions,[^11] while other female Heralds do have their safehand covered.[^12] [^13] [^14] [^15]

During the early years of their time on Roshar, Chana had the disposition of a soldier. In the present, Shallan's memories of Chana describe her as being sweet and overtly feminine. The reality was that Chana was an erratic individual, often getting into shouting arguments with her husband [Lin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lin "Lin") and was delusional that her daughter Shallan had inherited the Oathpact from her, freeing her from immortality.[^16] Despite being severely mentally unwell, she genuinely cares for her children; calling herself selfish for wanting a family despite [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] warning her she would ultimately hurt them.[^6]

## Attributes and Abilities

![[Chanarach---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Shuravf](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Shuravf "Coppermind:Artists/Shuravf")

As a [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), Chanaranach is immortal and capable of returning from death, though at the cost of a Desolation.[^17] She was a capable fighter, and wielded an [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] that made her a [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinder]], granting her the use of the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Division]] and [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Abrasion]].[^18] She has discarded her Blade, however, leaving her without Surgebinding.[^19]

Perhaps due to her power over Abrasion, Chanarach is known for being incredibly fast, as [Fleet](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fleet "Fleet") beating her in a foot race is considered a noteworthy achievement.[^2]

## History

She lived in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] for a period, before humanity began to expand outward, inciting a war between the humans and the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]].[^17] She was [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] bodyguard during the early years of their time on Roshar and perhaps when they lived on Ashyn as well.[^20] She and Ishar were among Jezrien's inner circle, even among the other Heralds.[^21]

### Herald of the Almighty

When humans and singers went to war, she and nine other people were made Heralds by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], forming the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]]. Following every [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]], she would travel to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] and be tortured there until either she or one of the other Heralds broke, sending them back to Roshar and starting a new Desolation. This cycle went on for thousands of years, gradually wearing on her sanity, along with that of all the other Heralds.[^17]

At [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]], the Last Desolation, Chanarach, like the rest, abandoned the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], leaving her [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] at the location of the final battle and departing for parts unknown.[^19] What happened to her afterwards is unknown, though she has been seen alive.[^22]

### Homemaker

At some point, Chana married Lin Davar and took his last name. She had four children with him: [[Balat Davar - The Coppermind|Balat]], [[Wikim Davar - The Coppermind|Wikim]], [[Jushu Davar - The Coppermind|Jushu]] and [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]], the oldest Davar son [[Helaran Davar - The Coppermind|Helaran]] having been a bastard of Lin's with another woman.[^16] Despite this, it seems Chana raised Helaran as her own, as he called her mother and was upset at her passing.

During this time, Chana had frequent visits from a man named Dreder. The servants gossiped that Chana was having a secret affair with him, but in reality he was a [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Skybreaker]] acolyte in the service of the Herald [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]].[^23]

### The Night of King Gavilar's Assassination

Nale contacted Chana and Dreder at the Davar estate while at the Kholinar Palace via a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]], requesting that she join him and [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] to assist them with their work. When Chana didn't reply, Nale continued to chastise Chana about her desire for a family. He was aware that her daughter Shallan had bonded a spren, and threatened that if Chana herself didn't kill Shallan then he would have Dreder kill her instead.[^16]

Chana was convinced that if she killed Shallan, Shallan would take her place in the Oathpact, ending her immortality and freeing her. Dreder recognized this as a delusion of Chana's and asked her to prove it by killing Shallan.[^16]

Shallan, who had overheard the conversation, attempted to flee, but was found by her father. He brought her into his chamber where he was confronted by Chana and Dreder. Lin was subdued by Dreder, who handed a knife to Chana. Pinning Shallan down, Chana raised the knife up high but hesitated, and in that moment Shallan summoned [[Testament - The Coppermind|Testament]] and killed both her mother and Dreder.[^16] Chana was sent to Braize, where she was tortured until she broke around the year 1174.[^6]

### Shallan's Wedding

Wanting to see her daughter again, Chana went to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] after hearing that the Radiants had returned and that Shallan had become one. She found her way to Shallan and [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin's]] wedding, but did not confront Shallan.[^24]

### Spiritual Realm

Some months prior to the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|contest of champions]], Chana had once more died and returned to Braize. Upon Shallan's trip into the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]] three days away from the Contest, Shallan decided it was time to recover memories she had suppressed about her mother's true identity and what her death meant. By doing this, Chana felt Shallan calling to her from the Spiritual Realm, allowing part of herself to be pulled into a vision with Shallan. There, she admitted she was the one who broke, starting a new Desolation. She apologized to Shallan, who forgave her and told her the Desolation wasn't her fault. Chana thought she didn't deserve her daughter’s forgiveness, but Shallan forgave her anyway.[^6]

Not long after mother and daughter reconciled, Chana was found by the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] due to part of her still being in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]]. She embraced Shallan and promised she would try not to break so easily this time, before disappearing from the vision in a puff of smoke.[^6]

After the formation of the new Oathpact, Chana, like the other Heralds, ended up in a vision where her mind was preserved while her soul was tortured on Braize.[^25]

## Culture

In [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorinism]], Chanarach is known under the symmetrical name **Chanaranach**, though the diminutive Chana is also used.[^4] [^2] **Chan-a-rach** is a far rarer spelling, only seen used in a song.[^2] She is associated with the number 3 (chach), the [Essence](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ten_Essences "Ten Essences") ‘Spark’, and the divine attributes of ‘Brave’ and ‘Obedient’. Her body focus is the soul.[^18] Phrases such as "Chana knows..." and "Chana help (somebody)" are common, and typically related to threat or protectiveness.[^2] [^3] The name [Chanada](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chanada "Chanada") is likely derived from Chana.[^26] And the name [Chanasha](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chanasha "Chanasha") definitely is.[^1]

As with the other Heralds, there is a temple to her in every modern city.[^27] [^28] This tradition appears to go back to the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], as there was once a temple of Chanaranach in [[Stormseat - The Coppermind|Stormseat]].[^4]

She is thought of as the Herald of the Common Man and is believed to be "fine and reputable".[^1]
