## Nightwatcher

The **Nightwatcher** is a powerful [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. She is a [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinter]] of [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]] and grants boons and curses to mortals through the [[Old Magic - The Coppermind|Old Magic]].[^2] She is one of the three potential [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") spren.[^3] She was created directly from the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night"), one of the primeval spren of Roshar, created by [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]].[^4]

The Nightwatcher would bless those who sought her out with one blessing of her choosing. In exchange for this blessing, she also bestows a single curse, although this curse very rarely has any obvious relation to the blessing that was gained.[^5] Because of the Nightwatcher's ethereal nature and ties to a single location, the [Valley](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valley "Valley"), she is considered by many to be a powerful [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]].[^6] [^7] [^8] [^9] It is surprisingly common for devoutly Vorin [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyes]] to go to the Nightwatcher at one point or another over the course of their lives.[^10]

She does not always appear to those seeking her, having a tendency to avoid groups and likely [foreigners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopping "Worldhopping") like [Felt](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Felt "Felt"). Supposedly, the best time of day to visit the Nightwatcher is right after the sun sets.[^2]

## Appearance and Personality

The Nightwatcher manifests in the Physical Realm as a dark green mist shaped vaguely like a person. Described as crawling on the ground with too-long arms reaching out and pulling her along as she floats above the ground. She appears to leave a trail of her essence behind her, like a river of twisting vapor curling through the trees. The only part of her that has any detail is her smooth distinctly feminine face with silken black eyes. She seems to be able to create extra limbs like smaller hands to interact with people.[^2] Cultivation made the Nightwatcher in such a way that she is not shaped by people's perception or belief of her, unlike most spren. This is why she behaves less human.[^11]

She is sometimes mistakenly conflated with the [Chemoarish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chemoarish "Chemoarish"), an [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]].[^12]

## Attributes and Abilities

### Nahel Bond

The Nightwatcher is one of the three great [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith") spren.[^13] [^1] Forming a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] with her grants one the power over the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [Tension](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tension "Tension") and [Adhesion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adhesion "Adhesion").[^14] While a Bondsmith can use Adhesion the way a [Windrunner](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Windrunner "Windrunner") would,[^15] they can also apply their powers in different manner, using them more in the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual]]. Using Spiritual Adhesion, a Bondsmith can form a [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] between themselves and another person, allowing them to speak their language.[^16] They also seem capable of hearing the spiritual aspects of objects and using this to mend broken things back whole.[^17] Each Bondsmith seems to make use of a different variety of Light to fuel their abilities, the Stormfather's Bondsmith uses Stormlight,[^18] the Sibling's [Towerlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Towerlight "Towerlight"),[^19] and the Nightwatcher grants her Bondsmith [Lifelight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lifelight "Lifelight").[^20]

### Old Magic

Blessing

The Nightwatcher claims to have the ability to grant almost any physical request one might ask of her, though just because she can does not mean she will give what is asked for. She claims to be able to give many things such as renown, wealth, skill, the ability to swing a sword and never tire, beauty, followers, and glory, as well as physical possessions such as spheres, gemstones, Shardblades and Plate, or even a unique and highly Invested item like [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]]. The Nightwatcher does have trouble with extremely nebulous requests relating to human emotion.[^2] The Nightwatcher is also capable of changing a person's species.[^21] She can also change a person's eye color, and has done so in the past.[^22]

Anyone is allowed to seek a single boon from the Nightwatcher. To do this, the supplicant must first travel to the [Valley](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Valley "Valley") in the west, where the Nightwatcher resides. After finding the Nightwatcher, the supplicant may request a boon. There are no clear restrictions on what the Nightwatcher has the power to grant--like most things regarding the Old Magic, the few known facts are surrounded by a web of myths and legends. The precise phrasing of the request does not appear to make a difference, however, since the Nightwatcher bestows a boon that she feels the supplicant deserves. In most cases, the continued pain of the curse appears to outweigh the blessing of the boon, even if the supplicant receives the boon they desired. A notable exception was [Av's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Av "Av") father, who received enough cloth to keep his family from starving during a famine. His curse, seeing the world upside down, apparently stopped troubling him after he acclimated to the situation.[^5]

Curse

Contrary to popular belief, it is not possible to avoid the curse by carefully phrasing a request--or even to predict the curse that the Nightwatcher will bestow.[^5] Interestingly, it does not appear that balance is carefully maintained through an individual blessing and curse--often, highly visible curses are the result of relatively minor boons. The curses can be quite thorough, however--in one case, a man lost all feeling in both of his hands until the day he died.[^5] Whether these curses follow some cosmic justice, or are merely the result of a capricious spren, is unknown.

The curse, as well as the boon, is permanent. Throughout history many visitors have tried to get rid of their curse, but there are no known cases of success.[^23]

## History

### Origin

Called "child" by [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], it is likely that the Nightwatcher was created by [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]] for a specific purpose from a different spren much like the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] was by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]]. The Nightwatcher is one of three [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] that are shadows of a god, for she was created from the essence of the [Night](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night "Night").[^1] [^4]

The Nightwatcher was deliberately created by Cultivation to be un-Connected to humans and is unaffected by the way they think about her.[^11] This was done so she could grow more organically.

Over time, the Nightwatcher would come to be seen as synonymous with the Old Magic.[^24] [^25] Furthermore, she is often conflated with Chemoarish, one of the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]].[^12]

### Known Visitors

Dalinar Kholin

**Boon:** Loss of Spiritual whispers and guilt for the murders he had committed

**Curse:** Loss of all memories relating to Evi

[[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] sought the Nightwatcher to end the pain he was feeling after accidentally causing the death of his wife, [Evi Kholin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi_Kholin "Evi Kholin"). When he finally visited the Valley, he asked to be "forgiven", much to the Nightwatcher's confusion. Eventually, Cultivation decided to intervene. She erased his memories of Evi, calling it a "pruning" of his memory so he could grow and heal, but made it so that the memories would eventually return.[^2] After this event he has no memory of killing her or anything involving her, although he is aware she existed. Additionally, when someone mentions her name, all that he hears is " *Shshshsh* ".[^10] Dalinar keeps his request, boon, and curse very private, and very few people know of his visit to the Nightwatcher. The memory came back gradually before Odium attempted to make Dalinar into his champion and failed, and Dalinar does not remember that Cultivation gave the boon until the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]].[^26]

Taravangian

**Boon:** The intelligence to keep Kharbranth intact while Rayse was Odium and the capacity to become the Shard's new Vessel

**Curse:** The inability to have both intelligence and compassion at the same time

[[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] claims to have asked the Nightwatcher for, "Capacity. Capacity to stop what was coming. The capacity to save humankind." [^27] Cultivation personally intervened when he asked for his boon.[^28] [^29] As a result, Taravangian's level of intelligence varies from day to day. This enabled him to write the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(literature\) "Diagram (literature)") on a day of particularly notable intelligence. He believes that his curse is a level of compassion inversely proportional to his intelligence; he feels little to no compassion on days of high intelligence, contrasted with days of low intelligence and great compassion "to feel pain for what he had done." [^27] Some days he is as dull-minded as a [parshman](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshman "Parshman") and brought easily to tears as he realizes decisions he makes may bring death to innocents, while on others he is more intelligent and able-minded as the greatest scholar but regards everything he is ordering done with cold logic and an eye for the bigger picture of Roshar.[^27] The change in intelligence and compassion will still happen even if Taravangian is awake and conscious in the middle of the night.[^30] On a day similar to the day he wrote the Diagram, he was cursed with low intelligence, but high emotion, which attracted one of the [[Shards]], [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], to him and Connected him to the Shard, allowing him to Ascend.

Lift

**Boon:** Produce [Lifelight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lifelight "Lifelight") from mass; using Lifelight to Surgebind, being partially stuck within the Cognitive Realm, vision manipulation

**Curse:** Unknown

The precise wording of [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift's]] request is unknown, but she believes that "she was supposed to stay the same, and the world was supposed to change around her." [^31] Lift interprets this to mean that she shouldn't age, which does not appear to be the case. Cultivation intervened and made it so that Lift's Surgebinding would be powered by Lifelight, rather than Stormlight, though the specifics of Lift's curse and boon are not known.[^11] [^32] Lift's abilities to physically touch [[Wyndle - The Coppermind|Wyndle]] and to digest food into [Lifelight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lifelight "Lifelight") are apparently related to one or both of these.[^33] Lift's spiritweb has somehow changed to allow her to convert her mass to Investiture directly, though she can only convert what she is able to metabolize for nutrients.[^34] [^35] Because of this, Lift is unable to get [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] from [[Spheres - The Coppermind|spheres]].[^36] Wyndle also supposes that she is partially stuck in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]].[^37] Lift's boon also allows her a degree of control over the [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") entrusted to the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]]. She is able to enter the visions without the Stormfather's help, leaving on her own and taking [Yanagawn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yanagawn "Yanagawn") with her.[^38]

Baxil

**Boon:** Unknown

**Curse:** Unknown

[Baxil's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Baxil "Baxil") transformation after visiting the Nightwatcher seems to have limited his physical form and removed some of his senses, as well as making him fascinating to [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]]. People can only see and touch him in specific circumstances, but he is still able to interact with objects. He can no longer consume food, though he can taste it to some extent in a place where many people are thinking about it. He also feels that his body is constantly close to dissipating like smoke, and needs to be wrapped in specially prepared red bandages to survive.[^39]

Ylt

**Boon:** A [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") knife

**Curse:** The inability to touch the raysium knife

[Ylt](https://coppermind.net/edit/Ylt?redlink=1 "Ylt (page does not exist)") asked for the power to unseat and kill a [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald").[^40] As his boon, Ylt was given a golden, rayisum knife, with an emerald set in the pommel. As a curse, he was unable to touch it, and instead needed to convince another to use it.[^41]

Av's father

**Boon:** A heap of good cloth

**Curse:** Seeing the world upside down

As his boon, [Av's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Av "Av") father received a heap of good cloth, which he sold to keep his family from starving during the lurnip famine.[^5] As a curse, he sees the world upside down. The rest of Av's brothers also went, as well as his mother. He is the only member of the family who did not regret his request.

Av's brother

**Boon:** Unknown

**Curse:** Numb hands

Av's brother's boon is unknown, as he was too embarrassed to share what he asked for. As a curse, both his hands became numb.[^5]

Shubalai

[Shubalai](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shubalai "Shubalai") visited and spoke with the Nightwatcher, recording their entire conversation for posterity.[^7]

Hoid

At some point [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] visited and spoke with the Nightwatcher.[^42]

Axies The Collector

[Axies](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Axies "Axies") visited the Valley several dozen times, unsuccessfully trying to find the Nightwatcher for his research.[^43]

Others

There is a rumor about a man who made every person he touched fall upwards ever since he visited the Old Magic.[^2]
