## Dalinar Kholin

> “ *I see beauty in you, Dalinar Kholin. I see a great man struggling against a terrible one.* ”

**Dalinar Kholin**, also known as the **Blackthorn**, is an [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") member of the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Order of Bondsmiths]], bonded to the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]]; he briefly held the position as the Vessel of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], before renouncing his oaths and thus renouncing the Shard itself.[^6] He is the King of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] and the de facto leader of the reformed [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^7] He is the younger brother of [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]], and uncle of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 10|Elhokar Kholin]] and the current Queen of Alethkar, [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]]. He is currently married to Brightness [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani Kholin]], Gavilar's widow. He has two sons, [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] and [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], from [Evi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi "Evi"), his first wife. Before he renounced the title, letting Adolin take over in his place, Dalinar was the [[Highprince - The Coppermind|highprince]] of the [Kholin princedom](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholin_princedom "Kholin princedom"). Before his death, he was also the King of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

Dalinar is an aging, but powerfully-built man in his fifties, with a warrior's face which he keeps clean-shaven.[^8] He has black hair that is gray in the temples, and blue eyes.[^9] He is often described as the opposite of handsome, with numerous bruises and an "unfortunate" face. His nose was broken at some point during his youth, and it has been crooked ever since.[^10] He has numerous old scars on his chest and arms, a remnant of hundreds of battles fought, to the extent where doctors are worried about his ability to use his sword arm.[^11] When he acquired Shardplate, he chose to leave it unpainted, which gave it a slate grey color. He also removed any unnecessary decoration or adornment from his plate.[^9] He wears a sapphire signet ring with his house's glyphpair on it.[^12]

### Personality

#### The Blackthorn

> “ *I'm a man of extremes, Navani. I discovered that when I was a youth. I’ve learned, repeatedly, that the only way to control those extremes is to dedicate my life to something.* ”
> 
> —Dalinar [^13]

In his youth, Dalinar was heavily addicted to the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill"), to the point of turning to other drugs, like [firemoss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Firemoss "Firemoss"), when it wasn't possible.[^14] Back then, he cared for little other than killing, often ignoring strategy and tactics in favor of rushing into battle.[^15] While initially, he sought equal combat, over time the Thrill corrupted that desire, to the point where eventually Dalinar relished in the murder of common soldiers and found fighting other Shardbearers to be a chore.[^16] [^17] Over time, Dalinar managed to temper that hot-headedness to become a keen strategist and logistician, although he retains his desire to be at the forefront of a fight to this day.[^18] At the time, he also had an overblown sense of personal pride, willing to punish thousands of people for a small subset of them ambushing him.[^19]

His violent and wrathful nature, while often intensified by the Thrill, was not entirely based on in it. He did genuinely feel bloodlust and hatred.[^20]

Dalinar blindly followed orders given to him by Gavilar, even if he didn't understand the reason for the orders. For instance, he attacked Yezriar's princedom simply due to the fact that Gavilar ordered it, even though he personally had no idea why.[^16]

The death of his wife, [Evi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi "Evi"), changed everything. His overwhelming guilt led to Dalinar beginning to drink heavily, often ignoring his family or acting with outright hostility towards them, and doing only the most perfunctory of social functions.[^21] Though he was somewhat aware of how horridly he acted, it seemed he was unable to temper himself, even after making a conscious effort.[^22] [^23] It wasn't until the death of his brother and a visit to the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]], where his memories of Evi and the events at the [Rift](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rift "Rift") were pruned by [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|Cultivation]], that he managed to get past this.[^24]

#### The Bondsmith

> “ *I have spent too much of my time worrying about what people think, Navani. When I thought my time had arrived, I realized that all my worrying had been wasted. In the end, I was pleased with how I had lived my life.* ”
> 
> —Dalinar [^25]

In his last few years, Dalinar Kholin was a man defined by duty, striving to act in an honorable and righteous manner.[^26] He was, however, keenly aware of his own failings in this regard, and his tendency towards the extreme. He seeked causes that he could believe in and which he can dedicate himself to, such as the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War") or *[The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)")*.[^13] He considers the latter in particular to be a particularly important part of his current self, as it was the book that began to heal him following his brother's death, and it is this book he constantly refers to for advice.[^23] [^27]

Dalinar considers himself wholly a soldier and general with no talent for back-room politicking.[^25] He also does not believe himself to be an intelligent man, and openly admits to his own foolishness.[^28] [^29] He is unflinchingly honest and straightforward, preferring to speak plainly rather than engage in any delicate diplomatic dance.[^30] He has a bad habit of trusting people, such as [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") and [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram"), based on prior association, to the point of ignoring even the most obvious alarm signs.[^31] [^32]

Dalinar cares little for social propriety, considering the opinions of others to be wholly irrelevant to himself so long as they don't actually impede his actions.[^30] [^33] Furthermore, he began to completely shirk the gender norms of society as he grew, learning how to read and write; and defending Renarin from mockery for his own failure to live up to the norms expected of him, and giving him solidarity.[^26] [^34] [^35] He also was, after a while, comfortable with marrying Navani due to their mutual love, despite what Vorin tradition dictated about marrying one's brother's widow.[^36] He is fully aware that people see him as a godless heretic for this, among other things, but doesn't care and sticks by his choices and beliefs. He is also aware of how his breaking of the rules enforced upon him by the system makes people feel threatened, but he continues regardless.[^37] [^38] [^39]

He doesn't, however, deal well with not being in control; he finds the idea of events important to him being impossible for him to influence distressing, and has a bad habit of going over regular chain of command to claim power for himself.[^40] [^36]

As an extension of this, like his brother, he has a tendency to treat people, even those he loves, like tools.[^41] He tends to believe that he knows what is best for people, and through this, can get rather commanding and controlling. An example of this controlling nature can be seen in how he forces Kaladin to retire as a soldier as he felt that it was best for him given his increasing trauma, refusing to take no for an answer.[^42] It can also be seen in how he believes that some people, such as various Alethi Highprinces, need to be forced into doing what is right, that they cannot be trusted to make their own decisions.[^25] [^43]

His complete opposition to the concept of democracy is likely a reflection of this controlling nature of his, his distrust of other people to make the right decisions or get things done, and in his views on what the role of a leader is in society.[^44] [^25] [^43] This nature of his can be found rooted in the Way of Kings, that which he uses as a foundation of his morality and worldview.[^9] [^23] [^27] It teaches that a monarch, or any ruler, provides stability and strength to his people, that he and his orders are the firm foundation upon which society stands.[^45] [^10]

Another aspect of his controlling nature arises from his interpretation of his behavior as the Blackthorn as being rooted in a lack of control, which led him to try to overcompensate through always being in control. He does this as he fears returning to the person that he used to be.[^44] His tendency to fully and single-mindedly dedicate himself towards things and ideals is another method for keeping himself from returning to what he was.[^13]

He can be somewhat egalitarian in his assessment of others, in particular soldiers, and will often promote men based not on eye color or social status, but rather on the ability they display.[^16] This extends beyond the [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") ranks system, as Dalinar is willing to give a chance to people he doesn't normally associate with fighting, like [Parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen") or women, if he considers their skills valuable or if they are recommended to him by someone trustworthy.[^46] [^47] This doesn't mean he's entirely free of bias, however, and when the worlds of the court and the military collide, he can often end up referring back to the established social order, possibly without fully realizing it.[^48] His respect for [Parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen") and [Parshendi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshendi "Parshendi") likely has some root in his childhood of being raised by a Parshman nurse, due to his mother dying and his father never being fit to raise him.[^12]

Despite his egalitarian assessment of others, he tends to be racist and judgmental towards foreign nations and cultures. Such as [Tu Bayla](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tu_Bayla "Tu Bayla"), which he views as a group of lowly nomads, and becomes angry at being described in similar terms to them.[^49]

He feels extreme guilt and self-hatred for various aspects of his past life; such as the burning of Rathalas and killing of his wife, which mentally broke him enough that it laid the foundation of his change in character.[^23] [^27] The memories of this haunted him enough that he shook and cried in bed every night once they began to return to him.[^50] Another thing that continues to haunt him is the fact that he didn't properly follow the Codes of War on the night of Gavilar's assassination, leading to him blaming himself for his death.[^9] He feels that he must honestly admit his past mistakes, and take full responsibility for them, for he believes that taking responsibility is the first step towards becoming a better man.[^51] [^52] [^53] [^54] He also sees the shame and the pain he experiences due to his actions to be paramount, as he refuses to give them up under any circumstances.[^55] [^54]

Due to this, he feels great shame at how he went to the Nightwatcher to remove his memories so that he would stop feeling pain. He believes that the person he became due to that was a falsehood, a mere pretense of being a better person.[^50] His loss of memory, and its return, is a major motivating factor in his views on the importance of pain and taking responsibility, for he learned what it was like to lose them, and therefore learned of their value.[^55] [^54]

He admits that he is a hypocrite, but believes that sometimes a hypocrite is simply a person in the process of changing, and feels that that interpretation applies to himself.[^56] He believes that he is still going through important growth and change, and has yet to reach his complete self.[^56]

He follows the Alethi Codes of War with an ardent zeal, due to a variety of reasons; he does not believe that one gains from following the codes, but that it stops oneself from becoming that which they loathe; he also feels a deep guilt for not following them on the night of Gavilar's assassination, and blames his death on himself.[^57] These codes have deeply influence the way that he leads, such as his belief that a leader should never order his followers to do anything that he would not himself do.[^58] His belief in the inherent goodness of the Codes was enough that, once he became the Highprince of War, he attempted to enforce the Codes on all warcamps on the Shattered Plains.[^25]

> “ *So today, you and your men sacrificed to buy me twenty-six hundred priceless lives. And all I had to repay you with was a single priceless sword. I call that a bargain.*”
> 
> —Dalinar to Kaladin [^25]

He deeply values human life, far more than anything else. This growing value of human life in his heart led to his taste for battle waning away, with his thirst being redirected into a love for [[Greatshell - The Coppermind|greatshell]] hunts.[^9] Due to this belief in the intrinsic value of human life, he traded Oathbringer for a group of darkeyed slaves, and considered it to be a bargain.[^25] [^59] The value that he ascribes to human life, even to that of darkeyed slaves, has led him to refuse to use bridge crews in his army during the War of Reckoning.[^60] It also leads to the safety of others being one of the first things to cross his mind when danger arises, even before his own well-being.[^61] Despite enjoying the hunt, he feels a great melancholy whenever killing larger and more majestic animals such as chasmfiends, and he believes that there is an intrinsic connection between a man and the beasts he hunts.[^62]

He is willing to sacrifice his own life for what he deems as a worthy cause, such as protecting Elhokar Kholin, the only thing he has left of his brother, from being crushed by a chasmfiend in a hunt gone wrong.[^61]

He is a very practical man that dislikes fancy outfits of lace and silk, much preferring more practical uniform and armour.[^62] He finds such outfits to look like ridiculous costumes on others, and laments at their popularity in the modern day. He also finds that Plate looks better on others as well.[^62] His desire for practicality over substance has also left him to choose grey Shardplate over Shardplate painted flashy colours.[^61]

At some point after learning of Honor's death, and being excommunicated by the Vorin church, he came to become an adherent of the God Beyond.[^42] [^63]

## Attributes and Abilities

Dalinar was raised in the Vorin religion; his [Calling](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Calling "Calling") is to be a leader, and his chosen [Glory](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Glory "Glory") is determination.[^64] At some point in his life, he joined a lay devotary called the Order of [Talenelat](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Talenelat "Talenelat").[^65] However, over time his faith wavers, and his visions lead him to conclude that the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty") is dead. This ends with his excommunication from the [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") church.[^50]

He has an excellent memory and can quote entire chapters of *[The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)")* without pause.[^66] Unlike most Vorin men, he has been taught how to write and read by [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]].[^67]

### Combat

In his younger years, Dalinar Kholin has been considered one of the greatest warriors of the modern world, and though in the present day his reputation has faded somewhat, he is still incredibly dangerous.[^9] Throughout his life, Dalinar has seen every aspect of war, from open-field combat to logistics and sieges.[^16] [^15] His only blank spot appears to be naval combat, as though they are not landlocked, the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") do not typically war on sea.[^8]

For most of his life, Dalinar possessed a set of [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]], as well as a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") named [Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer_\(Shardblade\) "Oathbringer (Shardblade)"), with the latter coming into his hands after he killed highlord [Tanalan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tanalan%27s_father "Tanalan's father") in the first siege of the [Rift](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rift "Rift").[^15] He was considered extremely skilled at using both.[^61] This being said, near the end of the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] he gave them up. Oathbringer was given to [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas"), in exchange for all of his enslaved bridgemen, while the Plate was given to [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] shortly afterward.[^25] [^68]

He is not able to summon the Stormfather as a Shardblade in normal circumstances, as he is forbidden from doing so.[^7] In extreme and desperate circumstances, Dalinar is able to summon the Stormfather as a blade for a very short period, but doing this damages their Bond and appears to cause the Stormfather great pain.[^50] [^69]

### Leadership

Dalinar is the head of the Kholin household, and as such holds authority over all other members of his family. His nephew [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] and Elhokar’s son [Gavinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor "Gavinor") are exceptions, as they are in the royal line and thus above and outside Dalinar's authority. The Kholin family is made up of his sons [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] and [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], his sister-in-law-turned-wife [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]], and his niece [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]]. Besides these members Dalinar has a large extended family, but it is unknown how much authority he has over them.[^33] As the Kholin highprince, Dalinar owns all of the land belonging to his princedom.

He also commands the [Kholin Army](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Category:Kholin_army "Category:Kholin army"), though, in battles, he often passes the command to his eldest son. The army is large and well-trained, although it takes significant losses in the [Battle of the Tower](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_the_Tower "Battle of the Tower").[^70] The [Cobalt Guard](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cobalt_Guard "Cobalt Guard") and [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]], two units of honor guard dedicated to protecting the king and the Kholins, are also part of the army. He also once had a group of soldiers who accompanied him into battle, [Dalinar's elites](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_elites "Dalinar's elites"), but they seem to have been dissolved by the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]], though some of their members still fight for him.

Dalinar’s presence is often regarded as incredibly powerful, perhaps even unconsciously so. At the feast before Gavilar’s assassination, Navani notes that Dalinar seems to attract his own cluster of lighteyes, likening the arrangement to planets orbiting around a star, at once hungry for and fearful of the fire.[^71] When meeting with the highprinces for a council on the Shattered Plains, Shallan likens him to a boulder as he stands, causing everyone to stop arguing and listen to what he has to say.[^10]

### Visions

> “ *I assumed I was dreaming myself, when I saw the first vision. When they kept happening, I was forced to acknowledge that no dream is this crisp, this logical. In no dream could we be having this conversation.* ”
> 
> —Dalinar to Fen during the [Starfalls vision](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions#Starfalls "Dalinar's visions") [^72]

Since Dalinar began reading *[The Way of Kings](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)")*, he has been experiencing [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") during every [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]]. These visions feel real to him.[^73] He experiences them as himself, but other people in the visions see him as someone else, not as an outsider. At the end of every one of them, Dalinar hears the voice of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] speaking to him, commanding him to "unite them". As the visions are pre-recorded, Dalinar cannot communicate with Honor.[^74]

While Dalinar is having the visions, his body attempts to act them out in reality, leading to him appearing to have fits and speak gibberish while to his eyes, he's moving around and speaking the native language.[^73] Eventually, the blabbering is revealed to be [[Dawnchant - The Coppermind|Dawnchant]], an ancient, long-dead language, proving the visions to be real.[^75]

At the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] informs Dalinar that he would receive no more new visions.[^7] However, after becoming the Stormfather's bonded [Knight Radiant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knight_Radiant "Knight Radiant"), Dalinar can replay the previous ones at will during [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]]. He can even bring in other people to share the vision with him, as long as they are inside a highstorm at the time or they are physically touching him.[^76]

As he gets closer to becoming a Bondsmith, Dalinar begins to be able to shape his visions and has had two vision-like experiences that the Stormfather disclaims any responsibility for.[^77] [^7] [^8] The first happens just before he formally bonds the Stormfather and shows his childhood home, followed by an enveloping warmth.[^7] The second happens after he flees the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill") at [Vedenar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vedenar "Vedenar") and shows [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]] go shopping, followed by a [[Thunderclast - The Coppermind|thunderclast]] attack.[^78]

### Surgebinding

> “ *Yours is the power Ishar once held. Before he was Herald of Luck, they called him Binder of Gods. He was the founder of the Oathpact. No Radiant is capable of more than you. Yours is the power of Connection, of joining men and worlds, minds and souls. Your Surges are the greatest of all, though they will be impotent if you seek to wield them for mere battle.* ”
> 
> —The Stormfather on Dalinar's abilities [^79]

Dalinar has a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] with the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]], the personification of storms and the divine. This makes him a [Knight Radiant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Knight_Radiant "Knight Radiant") of the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Order of Bondsmiths]]. His [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] are Tension and Adhesion. Adhesion allows him to bind objects together, and Tension grants the Surgebinder power over Soft Axial Connections, though what precisely this entails is yet to be revealed.

Like all Surgebinders, Dalinar can draw or breathe in [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]], which gives him superhuman strength, speed, and healing capabilities.

As a [Bondsmith](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith "Bondsmith"), Dalinar can bind inanimate objects, allowing him to restore broken things from pieces to their original shape. The ability seems to manifest as Dalinar hearing the broken pieces talk to him, though it's uncertain whether this happens every time.[^80]

He can also Connect to people - by touching them, he can acquire the ability to speak and understand their native language, though it wears off after a time.[^40] This ability has other implications, but he is unable to pick up other people's abilities with them.[^81]

#### Honor's Perpendicularity

Dalinar has other abilities, seemingly connected to the Stormfather's particular relation to [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]]. He can summon [[Honor's Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|Honor's Perpendicularity]], which provides a temporary way to move between Roshar and [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), as well as a near-infinite amount of [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] while open.[^54] [^82] Outside of it, he can also refill [spheres](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sphere "Sphere") with Stormlight and supercharge other [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] with it, although it's uncertain whether this is part of the standard Bondsmiths' toolset, or an ability unique to him.[^26] [^83]

## History

### Early Life and the War to Unify Alethkar (1120 – 1166)

#### Childhood

Dalinar was born in about,[^1] the second son to the Kholin princedom in Alethkar. His mother died when he was young and he was primarily raised by a parshman nurse.[^84] [^12] Dalinar got along better with his grandparents than his father, who died at some point prior to 1141.[^85] In his youth, Dalinar deeply respected his elder brother [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar]], and although envious of him at times,[^64] he followed his brother into a war to unite Alethkar under the Kholin banner. Dalinar tried women’s food as a boy, but he found it distastefully sweet. Sometime during his youth, Dalinar visited [[Sela Tales - The Coppermind|Sela Tales]] and the [[Purelake - The Coppermind|Purelake]].[^86]

In his youth, Dalinar had feelings for [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]], but he realized that Gavilar was also interested in her. For Gavilar's sake, he stepped aside. Later, Navani admitted to having an interest in Dalinar during this time, but since Gavilar was the one who chased her, she chose to accept his proposal.

#### Unifying Alethkar

> “ *We looked at this place here, this kingdom, and we realized, 'Hey, all these people have stuff.' And we figured... hey, maybe we should have that stuff. So we took it.* ”
> 
> —Dalinar's view of the war [^87]

Dalinar, Gavilar, and their friend, [Torol Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas"), sought to unite the scattered Alethi princedoms and have a true kingdom once more. Each highprince historically ruled over their lands individually, and conflicts between the princedoms were common. This strife between the warring highprinces had lasted for centuries. Gavilar's vision was to put an end to that.

The Kholins started by conquering their neighbors by force. From the beginning, Dalinar was an unstoppable terror on the battlefield. In some part due to his close connection with the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill") and Dalinar's own callous bloodlust, he quickly created a reputation for being able to win any engagement. Dalinar by himself could clear a battlefield of opponents, losing himself in the ease of killing.

In the early days of the unification war, Dalinar hand-picked his own elite force of soldiers, sometimes even selecting enemy combatants and winning them over. One notable encounter of this was when fighting Brightlord Yezriar, an enemy soldier shot Dalinar with massive black arrows. The wounds were very serious, but Dalinar was so impressed at the distance the archer shot from that he chased him down. This archer was [Teleb](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teleb "Teleb"), and Dalinar recruited him on the spot. Those black arrows with which Teleb shot Dalinar mark the incident that gave Dalinar his famous moniker of The Blackthorn.[^16]

Over the next year, Dalinar won his own Shards. His Plate he won in combat that involved kicking a man off of a cliff. The Plate was still new to him when the war took them to [Rathalas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rathalas "Rathalas"), otherwise known as the Rift, where Brightlord Tanalan ruled. Dalinar knew that Tanalan had a Shardblade, and in the ensuing battle, he went straight to Tanalan to kill him and win the Blade. He defeated Tanalan but Tanalan's body was taken away by his men before the Shard appeared. Dalinar tracked down the place where Tanalan was taken and found Tanalan's widow, and Tanalan's young son holding the Shardblade. Dalinar took the Blade but couldn’t find it within himself to kill Tanalan's family. After the battle, Gavilar told Dalinar that the Blade he won was [Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer_\(Shardblade\) "Oathbringer (Shardblade)"), the sword that belonged to the legendary Alethi conqueror, [[Sadees - The Coppermind|Sadees]] the Sunmaker.[^15]

A year later, the war had started to slow down and the Kholins were looking for allies. They turned to [Rira](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rira_\(country\) "Rira (country)") and [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]]. They negotiated with [Toh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Toh "Toh") and [Evi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evi "Evi"), a Westerner Brightlord, and his sister. During one of their initial meetings, Dalinar impressed them by walking about in a highstorm and stopping an assassin from killing Gavilar in a notably casual manner.[^87]

Two years after that, the war was still ongoing. Dalinar and Evi were betrothed, not yet married. Dalinar led the attack against Highprince [Kalanor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kalanor "Kalanor"), intent on winning the Shards that Kalanor held. While in the battle, Dalinar got so caught up in the Thrill that he didn't realize he killed Kholin men during the assault. Dalinar killed Kalanor in single combat, winning his Shards, but the Thrill was so strong in him that when Gavilar approached him afterward, Dalinar went after his own brother with the intent to kill him. Only when Gavilar took off his helm and smiled at him did Dalinar snap out of it. Deeply ashamed of himself, Dalinar gifted Gavilar the Shards he had just won, swearing to himself that he must never be king.[^17]

#### Married Life

The day before Dalinar’s wedding Sadeas organised a feast in his name, Dalinar would remember this feast with fondness.[^70] Sometime later, five years after his fight with Kalanor, the war ended, although the fighting didn’t, and Evi got pregnant with their first child. Even though the couple loved eachother, their relationship was strained by Dalinar's willingness to kill and Evi's horror at it. Gavilar received a demand from [Rathalas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rathalas "Rathalas"), telling Dalinar to return the Blade to their heir. Though Gavilar was upset that Dalinar didn't deal with the Tanalan heir, Evi was happy to find out that Dalinar had spared the Tanalan boy so many years ago. Dalinar was happy both to have pleased Evi and also at the news of a Rift rebellion, for if they were going to rebel, he would have the opportunity to fight.[^88]

But a year later, Dalinar was still safe at home in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"). His restlessness turned to using [firemoss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Firemoss "Firemoss") and permanently maiming people in tavern fights. At a wrestling match, Dalinar was summoned away with the news that Evi was in labor. He ran to see her, and found her afterward, with his son already born. Dalinar was so overjoyed when he saw his son the first time that he was overtaken with [gloryspren](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gloryspren "Gloryspren"). He named him [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]], "born unto light", and spoke a hope for him that he would have his father's strength, but also his mother's compassion.[^14]

Gavilar was present at Adolin's birth but brought Dalinar some bad news. He'd been unable to contain the situation at Rathalas. He'd decided not to send Dalinar to fight there, however, opting to send him instead to fight the Herdazians and Vedens on Alethkar's border as a gesture of Kholin strength.[^14] Dalinar fought on the border against the Herdazians for four years, ensuring the security of Alethkar's border and gaining leadership experience and capability to match his personal prowess in combat, becoming known as his brother's best general. Unfortunately, for all that time, the princedom of Rathalas still refused to submit. Evi brought Adolin and their second son, [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], to the battlefront. It was the first time Dalinar met Renarin, and he wasn't pleased to learn the meaning of Renarin's chosen name: "Like one who was born unto himself." Evi reminded Dalinar that he didn't answer many of her [spanreed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spanreed "Spanreed") messages and he could have had input into Renarin's name if he'd wanted to. It bothered Dalinar that even so long after battle, even while interacting with his family, that the Thrill takes so long to fade.[^18]

Dalinar spent more years fighting the Vedens. As Adolin grew into his adolescence, Dalinar was pleased to have his oldest accompany him on the years-long campaign and became a somewhat wiser and kinder man, attempting to be better now that he wished to be a role model for his son. Dalinar was coaching Adolin on battlefield strategy when Evi arrived with a letter from Gavilar. Dalinar guessed the contents before Evi could even finish the letter: Gavilar was sending him to the Rift. The Rathalas rebels were more entrenched than ever, and Gavilar was out of options. Dalinar, pleased to hear the news, suggested that they might never settle in Kholinar again. Evi was distraught to hear it and cried at the thought that she might never again have a life free from war and death. Both upset at each other, Dalinar promised Evi at least a year in Kholinar after he put down the rebellion at the Rift.[^89]

#### The Rift

After some soul-searching and wishing to spare his wife pain, Dalinar approached the Rift's leaders in person, and Dalinar spoke with [Tanalan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tanalan "Tanalan") the younger, the boy he spared who had grown up into the Brightlord of the Rift. Though initially hostile, Tanalan listened to Dalinar's plain-spoken assessment that the rebels would lose, and even considered Dalinar's offer of a personal duel between themselves. Tanalan informed Dalinar that Sadeas was working against him, and Dalinar promised him the rank of Highprince in return as the two concocted a cover story that would allow both sides to save face and call it a political victory. Dalinar left him with Tanalan's directions to where Sadeas' men were hiding, pleased that he was able to find a peaceful solution, knowing that it would make Evi happy.[^5]

Dalinar and his men approached the cavern where the Sadeas men were working, but realized that it made no sense for traitors to be wearing their own colors. He recognized that it was a trap just as it sprung. Tanalan had lied to him. The Rathalas rebels then triggered a landslide that killed the Kholin men accompanying him; but, though seriously wounded, Dalinar survived.

> “ *I intend to so thoroughly ruin this place that for ten generations, nobody will dare build here for fear of the spirits who will haunt it. We will make a pyre of this city, and there shall be no weeping for its passing, for none will remain to weep.*”
> 
> —Dalinar planning his revenge on Rathalas [^90]

Despite his injuries, Dalinar's rage drove him forward in revenge against Tanalan's betrayal, the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill") burning in him like never before. He slaughtered the rebels who ambushed him, unconscious of how savagely he killed them. Half dead, he dragged himself back to the Kholin camp in such a state of rage that his appearance provoked fearspren from Evi when she first saw him again. Though she maintained that they should take a few days to find a peaceful solution, Sadeas said what Dalinar felt: the Rift needed to pay. Dalinar had the Soulcasters put to use making oil and sent Evi away. He promised to destroy Rathalas so thoroughly that nobody would rebuild there for ten generations, to make it a pyre, that nobody would cry over the city because everyone in it will be dead.[^90]

The Thrill didn't let Dalinar sleep until the assault on the city of Rathalas. A group of messengers from the city came out with a flag of truce, but Dalinar ordered them shot dead before they could approach the camp. Dalinar ordered the attack to take place at night. With the oil that he had ordered Soulcast, Dalinar, Sadeas, and their combined forces began to burn Rathalas. Only when much of the Rift was up in flames did Dalinar order his forces in. All the while Dalinar made a genuine attempt to hold the Thrill at bay, knowing that enjoying this act of revenge would take away the last sliver of decency he had left.

After making his way through the city, Dalinar finally came face to face with Tanalan. Tanalan begged for his family's lives, but Dalinar's response was, "I am an animal." He explained that, like an animal, he'd been pushed to being feral, and once an animal goes feral, there's no coming back. He refused to spare Tanalan's family. As Tanalan wept at the destruction of the palace and the city, knowing his family just died, Dalinar wondered at how the Thrill still wasn't satisfied with the destruction.

> “ *She came to us, to plead. How could you have missed her? Do you track your own family so poorly? The hole you burned … we don't hide there anymore. Everyone knows about it. Now it’s a prison.*”
> 
> —Tanalan's last words to Dalinar.[^19]

His anger re-kindled at Tanalan, Dalinar said at least he didn't hide away in the secret hiding spot the Tanalan family used before, because they burned it. Tanalan started laughing hysterically at this and told Dalinar that Evi had been in there. He explained how Evi had snuck into the city to beg Tanalan to surrender so that they might be spared. Tanalan had captured Evi to use her as a hostage, and sent messengers to notify Dalinar of this--the same messengers Dalinar had ordered killed without even meeting them. Evi had died there. At this, Dalinar strangled Tanalan to death, but Tanalan died smiling. Looking at the raging inferno that was Rathalas, Dalinar's words about a feral animal being impossible to control came back to him. It was impossible to go into the city, and his own men had to pull him away.

Six hours later, they recovered Evi's body where Tanalan said it would be. Though it looked like Evi had gone to defect, Dalinar made sure nobody called her a traitor. To himself, Dalinar refused to admit he was responsible for this. He told his people at the warcamp that Evi died a hero, and he had burned the Rift in retribution.[^19]

Years later, Dalinar's reaction to his memories of what happened at the Rift was to drink. Immediately after and even years later, Dalinar could hear the screams of Evi and the people of Rathalas, burning.[^21] Dalinar's dependence on alcohol was very serious, to the point that his family sometimes hid his alcohol from him, and he was reduced to asking it from strangers.

#### Gavilar's Change and the Discovery of the Parshendi

In the years after the reunification of Alethkar, Gavilar began to lose his thirst for battle. He had started reading a book called [*The Way of Kings*](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_\(in-world\) "The Way of Kings (in-world)"), trying to convince Dalinar and his other Highprinces to be peaceful and to follow the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War").[^64] Like the other Highprinces, Dalinar resisted his brother's ideas, finding them unpalatable and strange – even un-Alethi. To try and change Gavilar's mind, Dalinar convinced him to go on an expedition, a hunt for a chasmfiend. He suggested this hoping to remind Gavilar of the good times in their youth, to reawaken his brother's fighting spirit.[^45]

In, Dalinar and Gavilar set out on their expedition. In an unmapped forest south of the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]], Dalinar met a [Parshendi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshendi "Parshendi") patrol. At first, disbelieving what he thought were simple [parshmen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Parshmen "Parshmen") acting individually in a civilized society, Dalinar quickly accepted that the Parshendi were intelligent and organized, unlike their parshmen cousins. Dalinar brought Gavilar to meet them, and within days the Alethi and Parshendi could communicate well.[^45]

Meeting the Parshendi was ten years after the events of the Rift, and Dalinar was still an alcoholic. After returning to Kholinar, Dalinar realized he felt more himself on the expedition than in the decade leading up to it. His alcoholism led to fits of anger and emotional distance from his sons, Adolin and Renarin, who had grown up a lot in that time. Though he'd felt especially a lack of connection from Adolin, it's his second son who connected with him here. Renarin broke through with his unfailing love for Dalinar, and this was what made Dalinar realize he needed to stop hating his sons for making him miss Evi.[^22]

### War of Reckoning (1167 – 1173)

#### Gavilar's Death and Dalinar's Transformation

> “ *Brother, follow the Codes tonight. There is something strange upon the winds.* ”
> 
> —Gavilar's last words [^23]

A year later, in, the Parshendi signed a formal treaty with Alethkar in the Alethi capital of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"). Within hours of signing the treaty, the Parshendi sent [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]], the Assassin in White, to assassinate Gavilar. While the Alethi king was assassinated in the middle of the feast, Dalinar was in a drunken stupor, completely unaware and unable to help.[^91] [^62] Even after this time, Dalinar was still struggling with his alcoholism, though he hadn't lost his temper with his sons since the time that Renarin reached out.[^23] He would go to great lengths to find alcohol despite the best efforts of those around him.[^71] Gavilar's last words were written beside his body, addressed to Dalinar, saying that he must find the most important words a man can say. Dalinar recognized the quote from *The Way of Kings*.[^23]

> “ *Evi had said the Old Magic could transform a man. It was about time he started trusting her.*”
> 
> —Dalinar deciding to travel to the Valley [^23]

At Gavilar's funeral, Elhokar swore revenge against the Parshendi on behalf of all the Alethi. Dalinar was pleased that this could be something to unite the Alethi people in spirit, finally. While he told Adolin to go swear the Vengeance Pact on behalf of the Kholin house, he wandered off to drink. He was on his way when he heard the words from *The Way of Kings* in the hallways. When he followed the voice, he found his niece [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]] reading the book. Though he was still desperate for a drink, he sat down and requested that she continue. She read the book to him, start to finish. When she finished, she and Dalinar embraced, and she left.[^23]

Adolin found Dalinar the moment after, to ask about the Kholin forces that they will send to the Shattered Plains. Dalinar decided then and there that he needed to be a better man. He told Adolin that he had been a poor father, but that he was so proud of his sons. Dalinar created a plan so that he could leave for the Shattered Plains separate from the other forces, and that a delay would not be considered unusual by anyone else.[^23]

> “
> 
> *In doing this, I provide for him a weapon. Dangerous, very dangerous. Yet, all things must be cultivated. What I take from you will grow back eventually. This is part of the cost.

>

>

On his separate way to the Shattered Plains, he took a detour and, travelling with a caravan through [Greater Hexi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Greater_Hexi "Greater Hexi"), visited the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]]. The Thrill was notably absent. As he approached, he again experienced visions of Rathalas burning, and its people screaming, more real than it had ever been since that night. More visions came of killing the Parshendi, of killing Elhokar and taking the throne for himself, invading other lands, and becoming emperor over all. He knew that this was his future.[^24]

Finally, the Nightwatcher came to him and asked what the Son of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] and the Son of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] wanted from her. He asked her whether he could ever be forgiven. The Nightwatcher grew aggravated, as she could not grant this request, but another [[Cultivation - The Coppermind|being]] approached. A matronly woman with brown skin and a brown dress, calling the Nightwatcher ‘child', commanded Dalinar to attend her instead.[^24]

Though at first, this woman seemed like she would reject him, telling him to seek out Honor instead, Dalinar insisted. She told him she would not change him, but she would prune him. The cost would be high. She warned him she would also take Evi. Dalinar agreed to her terms.[^24]

Afterward, Dalinar found himself outside, unable to remember the specifics of his agreement. He could not fathom that he would ask for forgiveness, of all things, and found it silly in hindsight that he would come to the Nightwatcher. He assumed that he had asked for and been given the boon of peace. He resolved then and there to stop drinking, to stop moping. He decided at this moment to start following the [Alethi Codes of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi_Codes_of_War "Alethi Codes of War") - he had failed to follow the Codes and it had cost his brother his life. He asked for a copy of *The Way of Kings* to be brought to him along the way to the Shattered Plains.[^24]

#### The War Against the Parshendi

At first, Dalinar threw himself into the fighting against the Parshendi with abandon, his guilt driving him. But as his grief faded, so did his thirst for battle and war. Dalinar and the Kholin warcamp followed the Codes when no other Alethi highprince would. He studied the words of [[Nohadon - The Coppermind|Nohadon]], the author of *The Way of Kings*, convinced that he must now become an example to Alethkar and lead them towards peace and honor.

At the same time, Dalinar began having fits during every [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]]. During these fits, Dalinar lost awareness of his surroundings, experiencing visions of the past as though he were living them himself. The visions seemed to be from the [Almighty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Almighty "Almighty"),[^9] leading Dalinar to believe that God was telling him to unite the Alethi Highprinces. In most of these visions, the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] were present. This loss of awareness was frightening by itself, but seeing the Radiants as heroes was disturbing to Dalinar and his family. The Kholins kept Dalinar separate from others during highstorms, not willing to let others see him during these fits.

Men began to speak of Dalinar the same way they'd spoken of Gavilar later in his life, namely that he was becoming weak and feeble despite Dalinar's history as a terrifying force on the battlefield as the Blackthorn. The rumors of his madness during the storms combined with his strange obsession with the teachings of *The Way of Kings* lost Dalinar a lot of respect and influence among his peers.

#### The War Changes

The battle against the Parshendi was the first time Dalinar lost the [Thrill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thrill "Thrill") in his memory.[^12] This loss of the Thrill during battle caused Dalinar to question why he was fighting this war and whether alternative methods of settling the Alethi dispute with the Parshendi would yield better results. The Highprinces saw Dalinar's wish for a more peaceful resolution as a sign of weakness, rather than wisdom, so he kept these questions to himself.

Dalinar was deeply frustrated by the war at the Shattered Plains, as the Highprinces' priorities had changed from seeking vengeance on the Parshendi to competing for [[Gemheart - The Coppermind|gemhearts]] to gain wealth for themselves. Dalinar realized the war on the Parshendi had become nothing but a game to the Highprinces and he strived to unite them. He wanted to be the [Highprince of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highprince_of_War "Highprince of War") to realize this.[^64] Despite his ambivalent feelings about staying at the Shattered Plains, Dalinar was devoted to Elhokar and the nation of Alethkar above all. Though Dalinar rarely went on hunts, in he decided to accompany Elhokar and Sadeas.

Dalinar then confided his wish for the Alethi to return to Alethkar. Adolin was shocked and upset at this suggestion, even when Dalinar shared his concern about the state of affairs back in their home Adolin was already worried about his father's sanity,[^92] but when Dalinar began to make important decisions based on the visions – like wanting to leave the Shattered Plains – Adolin questioned the wisdom in trusting dreams, especially blasphemous ones. Dalinar maintained that the visions were real and grew further determined to unite the Alethi highprinces.

A chasmfiend surprised them on top of the plateau, and Dalinar saved Elhokar from being killed by it. It served as a reminder of Dalinar's strength to their armies, but it did not last. After the battle, Elhokar showed Dalinar a cut strap on his saddle and asked him to look into it. Elhokar suspected the work of assassins but Dalinar believed Elhokar to be overly paranoid; he agreed to investigate to allay Elhokar's fears. He took Adolin to check on the cut strap but the results were inconclusive. Dalinar told Elhokar that it was unclear and he was overreacting, but Elhokar staunchly believed that someone was trying to kill him.

#### Dalinar Reaches Out

Dalinar and Sadeas worked in tandem to strongarm another Highprince, [Vamah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vamah "Vamah"), into using the king's [Soulcasters](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulcaster "Soulcaster"), ensuring that Vamah must depend on Elhokar's resources to continue in the war. Though Adolin was suspicious of Sadeas, who could be unkind at times, Dalinar maintained his belief that Sadeas held the good of Alethkar as his top priority as much as he did. It was situations like this that Dalinar knew that he could trust Sadeas in his goal of uniting the highprinces. Dalinar continued to try and use other means to convince - or manipulate - the other highprinces to follow Elhokar. Dalinar promised Renarin that he would give him a set of [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] as soon as he had one to spare.

### Refounding of the Knights Radiant (1173 - 1174)

> “ *I'll have us be what we were before, son. A kingdom that can stand through storms, a kingdom that is a light and not a darkness. I will have a truly unified Alethkar, with highprinces who are loyal and just. I'll have more than that. I'm going to refound the Knights Radiant.* ”

#### The Start

Dalinar started planning, in his capacity as [Highprince of War](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Highprince_of_War "Highprince of War"), to unify Alethkar and refound the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^98] However, soon after his next vision, he found glyphs scratched onto his wall: *Sixty-two days. Death follows.*

When prompted on his endgame, Dalinar stated his intention to refound the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Radiants]]. He wasn't sure why exactly he needed to refound the Radiants, but he knew he only had about sixty days to do it.

#### Bondsmith

As Dalinar shaved and dressed the next morning he reflected on the events of the night before, both the strange dream and the memories he had recovered. #### Terms of the Contest

Several months later, Dalinar was brought into a vision by Odium, in which he set the terms of their contest of champions: On the tenth day of the new month, they would each appoint a willing champion, who would be allowed to the top of Urithiru—unharmed by either side—to fight to the death. #### Adolin

Though he neglected both of his sons at first, Dalinar soon begins to love Adolin dearly. Adolin has always been a born fighter, and as a young boy would participate in Dalniar’s meetings to learn, and Dalinar enjoyed having him around. Between the burning of Rathalas and shortly after the discovery of the Parshendi, he despised Adolin due to how he reminded him of Evi.[^22]

After visting the Nightwatcher and forgetting about Evi, Dalinar grooms Adolin as his eventual successor, drilling the Codes into him from his youth. They fight side by side on multiple occasions, and rely on each other. When Dalinar starts having visions from the Stormfather, Adolin initially doubts him, but soon believes his father and becomes one of his strongest supporters.

Dalinar has difficulty keeping up with his son's tumultuous love life, never being able to keep track of who he is with at any given time.[^9]

Adolin, feels a complex mix of hero-worship and exhaustion with Dalinar. He believed that he was a deeply wise man, and felt ire towards those that mocked him and didn't show him respect.[^9] At the same time, he judges his father's adherence to the Alethi Codes of War, and feels that he fell from the warrior that he once was. Though he still follows these orders without question, despite his feelings on the matter.[^9] Eventually, however, Adolin came to understand the point of the Codes and appreciate his father's adherence to them.[^66]

He blames all of this on his delusions, but he doesn't hate him for them; he simply believes that he is troubled, and was going mad and losing his wits.[^9] One of the reasons for his anger when people insulted him came from his belief that he was losing his mind, unfair and unconscionable to be taking shots at him when he was in such a state.[^9] He was deeply jealous of those that were able to see Dalinar at his prime,[^9] though these feelings likely changed upon learning what he had done as the Blackthorn.[^135]

Later, when Elhokar died, Dalinar wants Adolin to become the new king. Adolin refuses as he doesn't believe he is the right person to take the crown. His father could not understand this, and is furious when Adolin admits to killing Sadeas.[^26] The publishing of the Blackthorn's memoir, including the admission of Dalinar's role in Evi's death, caused a further rift between Adolin and his father. Adolin still loves and respects his father but can not forgive him for killing his mother. The young highprince is slowly realizing he has a different moral code than his father.[^135] Nevertheless, Adolin stays loyal and doesn't want to let his father down by failing the mission to the honorspren.[^146]

Dalinar gives Renarin plate and blade, wanting him to be able to defend himself if the need arises.[^113] [^64] After Renarin becomes a Radiant, Dalinar learns to see his healing abilities as invaluable, and sees that Renarin is indeed strong, though in a different way than he had thought. Dalinar feels shame for his treatment of Renarin, and soon treats him with respect.[^30] [^35]

He tries to protect Renarin from various forms of insults and mockery, be they malicious or in good faith.[^9] [^35] This in part comes from thinking of Renarin as overly fragile, something Wit actively called him out on.[^9] He is enraged by people referring to Renarin as useless for his inability to properly fight in the war, and gets sent into a nigh-murderous rage by such claims.[^62] [^64] Despite his respect for him, he does doubt his capability to fight due to his epilepsy, and tends to still think of him as simply "the boy", even after he has long since become a man.[^64]

When Renarin goes to the meetings of the scholars in Urithiru, Dalinar sometimes joins the meetings to show support for his son and to dispel the notion that he is acting unmasculine.[^35] Dalinar and Renarin share a bond over the fact that both of them experience visions against their will. After he learns about what happened to his mother Renarin chooses to blame Odium for her death instead of Dalinar so he would not lose him as well.[^147]

Renarin himself was hurt upon discovering that his father was the one who killed Evi. But, not wanting to lose another person important to him, he choose to blame Odium for her death instead.[^147]

#### Gavilar

> “ *"My brother was a liar, Sebarial"* ”
> 
> —Dalinar Kholin to Highprince Sebarial

[^148]

He deeply loved and respected his brother, enough so that he refused to allow criticism of him,[^149] and introduced him to people that he felt that he should know.[^13] While he did deeply respect him, he did feel anger at how, in his eyes, he took Navani from him; once he was angered enough to consider killing Gavilar so that he could be with her, though he did not go through with the attempt.[^17] He loved him, but at the same time felt a deep and bitter jealousy towards him, and he always felt, and still feels, overshadowed by him.[^64]

Gavilar's death left him deeply shaken.[^23] He blamed himself for his death, feeling that if he followed the Alethi Codes of War he would have been able to be there to protect him.[^9] This guilt was a large motivator in him choosing to finally go to the Nightwatcher, and helped spark his change into a better person.[^23] One of his motivators in being so protective of Elhokar, and trying so hard to guide him to become a better king, is rooted in his guilt, and in him seeing Elhokar as a remaining piece of his brother.[^62]

He wants to retain his legacy, not wanting to let the power of the Kholin House over Alethkar that him and his brother built up collapse due to infighting and Elhokar's incompetence.[^9] He has now been disillusioned regarding Gavilar's honourable facade.[^150]

Gavilar himself felt an affection for Dalinar and after their mother died and their father suffered a brain injury and was left unable to take care of them, he took on a sort of paternal role over Dalinar.[^8] However, despite this affection, he ultimately treated him as little more than a tool.[^151]

#### Elhokar

Dalinar grew highly protective of [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] following Gavilar's death, having sworn to himself that he would not let the boy come to harm.[^61] Over time, however, his tendency to take control begins to take precedence. However non-deliberately, Dalinar starts to pull the kingdom out of Elhokar's hands. Though Dalinar isn't happy about it, he does little to stop himself once he realizes it, and often forces his will on his nephew.[^30] [^25] He does see traces of Gavilar in Elhokar; this is, however, rare.[^152]

#### Jasnah

Dalinar's relationship with [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]] is perhaps the best among his relatives. The two bond together over *The Way of Kings* after Gavilar's assassination, and respect each other's knowledge and opinions.[^23] They consoled each other over Gavilar's death, finding comfort in one another.[^23]

Dalinar considers Jasnah to be the most trustworthy person in his family, often wishing that she would come to the Shattered Plains with him.[^86] Jasnah likewise is willing to aid her uncle, offering heartfelt advice when needed.[^76] After their campaign in Emul, Dalinar asks Jasnah to write the under text of Oathbringer. She agrees, though she promises to hold nothing back.[^153]

Dalinar and Navani

### Navani

> “ *You have my heart. Always.* ”
> 
> —Dalinar [^154]

Dalinar and Navani are deeply in love with each other. They have been for decades now; however, initially, Navani chose Gavilar over his brother. While it is possible this was because Gavilar was a more advantageous match, Navani claims that young Dalinar scared her with his intensity.[^45] Dalinar never stops carrying the torch for her, even after his own marriage, and at one point nearly killed his brother for a chance of being with Navani.[^17] The two part ways shortly after Gavilar's death, with Navani remaining in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") to keep an eye on [Aesudan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aesudan "Aesudan").[^155]

When the two meet again, Dalinar is a changed man and strives to keep to the Vorin tradition, which states that his sister-in-law should be like a sister to him. Navani, however, has none of it, and begins to flirt with him almost immediately, to his mild panic.[^155] Eventually, he does give in, and the two begin a proper relationship, with Dalinar himself making it public by telling Elhokar about it.[^149] [^25] From then on, the two form an effective team, working together both on [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") and on warcamp politics.[^98] While their relationship is generally considered scandalous, Sadeas' betrayal overshadows it until it's accepted as the new normal.[^99] Eventually, the two decide to take it further, and when no [Vorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vorin "Vorin") [ardent](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardent "Ardent") is willing to help, they have the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] himself marry them.[^36]

Dalinar is often willing to share his secrets with Navani; notably, she's one of the very few people he's told about not remembering Evi, and the one he turns to when he seeks someone to take notes on his visions.[^36] [^18] He seems unwilling to tell her everything, though; he doesn't share the details of the events at the Rift once he remembers them, possibly out of fear that it would push Navani away.[^124]

While Dalinar is incapacitated Navani leads the coalition and covers for him while he is unable to do his job.[^156] [^124]

### Torol Sadeas

His relationship with Sadeas is a very complicated mixture of respect, hatred, mutual goals, and memories of their formal friendship.

Before Gavilar's Assassination, they are very close friends who worked to conquer Alethkar together. Dalinar, a cruel man himself, did not consider Sadeas in the wrong for his bloodthirst and complete disregard for the lives of his men.[^62] [^64] [^16]

Following the assassination in, their relationship fractured and left them as bitter enemies. He despises Sadeas for his cruelty, and also blames him for not being with Gavilar when he was assassinated.[^62] [^64] At the same time, they are both united under a common goal of protecting Elhokar, seeing him as the only memory left of Gavilar, although their methods in doing so starkly differ.[^64] Despite the fact that they try to work together, he is willing to break off any remaining positive relations to protect Renarin's honor and dignity when Sadeas insults him.[^62]

Dalinar greatly enjoys seeing Wit torment Sadeas.[^62]

### Wit

He rather dislikes Wit, and finds him very odd.[^9] [^62] Though he does find himself feeling rather fond of him when he mocks Sadeas.[^62]

Wit is well aware of this and tends to purposely agitate him as a joke.[^9] Despite this, he doesn't seem to hate him, and happily gives him advice.[^9] Wit himself believed that Dalinar was likely part of the Sons of Honor, and that Gavilar had confided in him his plans and knowledge of the cosmere. Although these suspicions were later proven incorrect.[^65] [^157]

## Development

> “ *I will say Dalinar is my oldest character, followed by [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]]. Those two have been around since I was 15 and so, there is some favoritism for them, perhaps, just in longevity sense.* ”
> 
> —Brandon, *[Shadows of Self](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadows_of_Self "Shadows of Self")* signing [^158]

Dalinar is one of Brandon's oldest characters. He appeared in Brandon's first book, written when he was fifteen; there, he was named **Jared**. Like Dalinar, Jared was a younger brother to a well-liked king who was eventually assassinated; unlike Dalinar, Jared takes over the reins of the kingdom and takes his time to mold his nephew into a proper ruler, as the proto- [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] character (alongside proto- [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]]) is initially kidnapped by the same assassins.[^159] While the story itself was canned, Brandon took Jared with him further, into *[Dragonsteel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragonsteel_Prime "Dragonsteel Prime")*, where he participated in the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] plot that would eventually be moved to *[The Stormlight Archive](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Stormlight_Archive "The Stormlight Archive")*.[^160]

The next known incarnation of Dalinar is **Dalenar**, in [*The Way of Kings* Prime](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Way_of_Kings_Prime "The Way of Kings Prime"). Like Jared before him, Dalenar loses his older brother to assassins, although it seems he doesn't become the next king himself. Dalenar has three sons: Sheneres, Aredor (who would eventually become [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]]), and [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], with Sheneres dying at the start of the book while trying to protect the king.[^161] At the end of the *Way of Kings* Prime, Dalenar is forced to kill Elhokar for the greater good (after Elhokar had started a civil war and executed Aredor); Brandon eventually took that out, having decided that Dalenar trying to help his nephew would make the plot more interesting.[^162]

Brandon eventually rewrote the entire story from scratch, creating *[[The Way of Kings (in-world) - The Coppermind|The Way of Kings]]* we have today. It's then that Dalenar became Dalinar, and, according to Brandon, it was then that he finally got the character "right".[^163]

## Trivia

- When pressed to give his favorite character, Brandon often points towards Dalinar, although he also notes that this might be because of how long ago he was created.[^164] [^165]
- Early on in the writing of *[Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer "Oathbringer")*, Brandon intended for it to be [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth's]] book, with Dalinar's being the fifth. The two were eventually swapped.[^166]
- Dalinar is somewhat based on [Subutai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subutai "wikipedia:Subutai"), a Mongolian strategist famous for a long and illustrious military career in service to [Genghis Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genghis_Khan "wikipedia:Genghis Khan") and his son, [Ögedei Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%96gedei_Khan "wikipedia:Ögedei Khan").[^167]
- Dalinar is a Gryffindor.[^168]
- There was a [Feruchemist](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gereh "Gereh") keeping watch on Dalinar in his house.[^169]
- Dalinar is one of the few characters who have spoken in small-caps.[^170] [^171]
