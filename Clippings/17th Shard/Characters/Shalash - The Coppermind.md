## Shalash

> “ *A woman sits and scratches out her own eyes. Daughter of kings and winds, the vandal.* ”

**Shalash**, also known as **Ash**, is one of the ten [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of [[Honor - The Coppermind|The Almighty]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^3] She's commonly titled the **Herald of Beauty**;[^4] she's the patron of the [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Order of Lightweavers]] and the daughter of another Herald, [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]].[^5] Prior to the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], she was traveling the world destroying any depiction of herself she could find.[^6] Currently, she resides with the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] in [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^7] She had been little more than a child when the exodus of humans from Ashyn to Roshar occured, and did not appreciate Jezrien treating her like a child for a long time afterward.

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

Shalash has dark skin and long black hair. Her eyes are a faint violet, so faint as to be almost white, and lack epicanthic folds. She is also described as being tall and lean as well as exceptionally beautiful.[^9] [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Rosharans]] who see her think she's [Makabaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makabak "Makabak") with some [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] heritage.[^10] In recent years, she tends to wear practical clothing over anything particularly feminine. She carries around a thin-bladed sword, though it's not a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade").[^9]

### Personality

She dislikes thievery, though she has no issues with breaking into the dwellings of the rich to destroy art, even if she doesn't want the destruction to be too easy. This appears to be a perversion of her divine attribute of Creative, and may be a symptom of her forsaking of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].[^9] It's focused on depictions of herself - whenever she sees her own face, whether as a painting, statue or tapestry, she is compelled to destroy it. She seems unable to resist the urge even when pressed for time. She is, however, aware of this, and when doing something important, she actively avoids looking anywhere one may find artwork of the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald").[^6] She hates people worshipping her at all, and sneers at those who consider her divine.[^10] She thinks that all people should stop worshipping, talking about, and painting Heralds.[^11]

She isn't interested much in scholarly matters, such as [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realmatic Theory]], which implies that she wasn't a scholar.[^10] Despite this, as the patron of Lightweavers, she understands the symbolic power of imagery and its implications for Cognitive Shadows.[^12]

Shalash is very private and does not appreciate being asked prying questions.[^9] She seems to be highly regretful of abandoning the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]]. Upon meeting [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] again, she's extremely apologetic for leaving him behind, and expresses dismay that he doesn't hate her for it.[^8] Despite that, she no longer cares about the fate of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] and its people.[^6] She also seems to have periods of losing touch with reality, as she mentions missing decades, if not centuries of memories.[^8]

## Abilities and Attributes

### Immortality

Shalash is a [[Cognitive Shadow - The Coppermind|Cognitive Shadow]] capable of returning to life, though the exact mechanics of how are unclear. Following every death, she travels to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]]; whenever she or another [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") gives in, she returns to life on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^13] While alive, she's ageless, and will likely live forever unless killed.[^6] She also appears to have no need of sleep; at the very least, it seems she cannot easily lose consciousness.[^7]

Even prior to becoming a Herald, her access to the Surges slowed her aging by a tremendous degree, such that she showed minimal signs of aging over a period of several decades.[^14] [^15] [^16]

### Surgebinding

As part of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], she was given one of the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]], granting her access to [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]]. Her Blade allowed her to use the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Illumination]] and [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Transformation]]. However, she has left her [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Blade]] behind at [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]], and hasn't recovered it since.[^17] [^9]

She possessed some form of Surgebinding before becoming a Herald,[^15] [^16] this form of Surgebinding was unbound by many of the rules and restrictions that applied to her Surgebinding granted by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], and was associated with [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^18]

### Other

Considering her well-known attributes, it's likely Shalash was once an artist of some sort, or at least artistically inclined.[^19] Her hearing is noted as exceptionally good.[^9] She's also fairly capable of spying and infiltration, as she has no problems mixing herself into a new city or finding her way into a military camp.[^20] [^6] Though her obsession with depictions of herself is compulsive, she is very covert about it, breaking into houses and leaving without any trace of her presence other than missing or destroyed art.[^9] [^21]

## History

### Heraldic Epochs

![[Shalash---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Katie Payne](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Katie_Payne "Coppermind:Artists/Katie Payne")

End of the Oathpact

Shalash was born on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], then known as Alaswha, to [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] and an unknown mother over seven-thousand years before the [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows").[^22] She was born during a period of political upheaval on Ashyn, as wars occurred between the myriad city-states that covered the planet, as a result of the intervention of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]].[^18] At some point, she gained the power of Surgebinding, which slowed down her rate of aging.[^14] [^15] [^16]

She lived through the decades of peace with the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]], and survived into the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]] when humanity began to turn against their benefactors and expand their hegemony.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> Due to the power of the Surges, she aged little during these decades.[^15] [^16]

When the Desolations started, she was chosen by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] to be one of his Heralds. This formed the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], which included Shalash receiving one of the [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblades]] to aid her in her duties.[^13] The precise circumstances of how this came to be are unknown, but there are stories about [Proving Day](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Proving_Day "Proving Day"), during which the would-be Heralds faced difficult, if not impossible, tasks to prove themselves.[^23]

Following the formation of the Oathpact, Shalash and the other Heralds spent several thousand years helping the people of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] fight the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] in subsequent Desolations. Preceding each Desolation, the Heralds would return to Roshar and have a short time to prepare them for said Desolation, teaching them any necessary technology they may have lost in the intervening time. The level of technology the Heralds returned to varied with each Desolation, in some cases the Rosharans had not progressed past the Stone Age.[^24] [^25] At the end of each Desolation, if Shalash did not "die", she, along with any other surviving Heralds, was required to leave Roshar and return to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]]. There, she and the others would be tortured to get them to give in and return, whereupon a new Desolation would begin.[^13]

At [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]], around three-thousand years after becoming a Herald, Shalash and all Heralds other than [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] -- who was dead at the time -- decided to forsake the Oathpact and abandon their Honorblades. They lied to the people of Roshar, telling them they had "won" and went their separate ways.[^17] They were, however, not as successful in their breaking as they'd thought.[^26]

### Era of Solitude

> “ *I'm worried about Ash. She’s getting worse.* ”
> 
> — [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] [^27]

At an unknown point in time during the [[Era of Solitude - The Coppermind|Era of Solitude]] she lived in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], where the Shin tried to kill her, which she wasn't particularly against. She only left after they started worshipping her.[^10]

Shalash was present during the [Challenge of Stormhold](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Challenge_of_Stormhold "Challenge of Stormhold"), which she recounts to [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] that King [Iyalid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iyalid "Iyalid") had been drunk during the Challenge, settling a debate between her and [Jochi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jochi "Jochi").[^28]

Roughly four and a half thousand years after Aharietiam Shalash was traveling the world destroying any depictions of herself. Sometime prior to the [[Assassination of Gavilar - The Coppermind|assassination]] of King [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]] of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") in she infiltrated the royal palace in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") and destroyed her own statue.[^29] [^3] This pattern of behavior seemed to have gotten much worse at the time, as her fellow Heralds noted that she was getting worse.[^27]

Five years later, in 1173, Shalash, alongside with hired help [Baxil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Baxil "Baxil") and [Av](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Av "Av"), infiltrated the palace of [Ashno of Sages](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ashno_of_Sages "Ashno of Sages"), the Prime of [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]], and destroyed a great deal of artwork depicting herself, including a canvas painting and a statue.[^9] Either during or prior to that time, she also infiltrated the [Bronze Palace](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bronze_Palace "Bronze Palace") in [Azimir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azimir "Azimir"), where she defaced the mural of the Heralds, scratching out her own face, along with destroying many of the statues in her temple in [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylenah]].[^21] [^30]

### True Desolation

Following the [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved") civil war, at the start of the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]], Ash became a washerwoman under the assumed name Pom to track down a painting of herself by [Dandos the Oilsworn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dandos "Dandos"). This turned out to be a trap set up by [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]], who wished to tell her where [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] was.[^20] This set her on the path to [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City"), where she arrived just in time for the [[Battle of Thaylen Field - The Coppermind|Battle of Thaylen Field]]. There, she found Taln.[^6]

During the battle, Taln was next to catatonic, requiring Ash to pull him along until [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] managed to open [[Honor's Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|Honor's Perpendicularity]], whereupon the two Heralds managed to have a short conversation.[^8] After the battle, with Taln once more uncommunicative, Ash decided to take him to [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]]. This was, however, not to be, as at the same time, [[Moash - The Coppermind|Vyre]] managed to kill [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]], sending her into deep shock. The resulting commotion made Jasnah notice them and come to confront them, as she was watching the line to the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]] for the two Heralds by comparing faces to drawings she had received from [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]]. Shalash fell unconscious before the two could talk.[^7]

After being discovered at the Battle of Thaylen Field, Ash and Taln moved to [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], where they were often interviewed by Jasnah. Ash provided some information about [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmiths]] and the Oathpact to Dalinar, but claimed that Ishar was the real expert on the subject.[^10] The two Heralds were brought along to the offensive in Emul, and Ash was eventually again questioned by Dalinar about Ishar.[^11]

### Battle of Azimir

After the offensive in Emul, Ash and Taln were brought to and kept in an Azish hospital. During the battle of Azimir, [Abidi the Monarch](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Abidi "Abidi") enters and orders his Fused to kill those in the hospital. After Taln launches into battle with the Fused, she joins him, wielding a sword. They manage to kill almost every Fused in the city, but both Ash and Taln die of their wounds.

### Reforging of the Oathpact

On, she and the other eight living Heralds reforged the Oathpact alongside [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]], who replaced Jezrien's place.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> As opposed to the original Oathpact that forced the Heralds to experience their torture on Braize, the new Oathpact sent their minds into a paradisical vision, such that they would not experience said torture.[^31] <sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> <sup title="This section needs to be expanded with more information"><i>[expand]</i></sup>

## Relationships

### Other Heralds

Shalash is the daughter of [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]], a fellow [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"). It doesn't seem as though the two are very close; the only time she's seen thinking about him is when she feels the pain of his death.[^7]

She considers all the Heralds --except herself and maybe [[Ishar - The Coppermind|Ishar]] -- to be mad from thousands years of torture.[^32] She also considers the Herald of Mysteries a man willing to risk his life, soul, and work for the better of humankind.[^33]

She views [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] as a brother and was very close with him;[^34] she finds herself drawn to his hands, and remembers the sensation of them even after four thousand years. He's also the only person to whom she's apologetic about abandoning the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], and she nearly breaks down when he refuses to hate her for it.[^8] She treats Taln very gently and leads him like a disabled man when they walk together.[^10]

Other Heralds -- [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] and possibly [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]] -- appear to have been keeping tabs on her activities, though it's unknown whether they've met with her directly in recent years.[^27]

### Hoid

She has met [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]], and knows him well enough to be able to recognize his art style, though she refers to him as Midius.[^7] Hoid is particularly tender and reverent when he deals with her and Taln.[^10]

## Culture

> “ *Ash's eyes!* ”
> 
> — [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] [^35]

In [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorinism]], Shalash is known as **Shalash'Elin**, the Herald of Beauty, though the nickname Ash is also used.[^4] Tradition associates her with the number 6 (shash), the [Essence](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ten_Essences "Ten Essences") of Blood, and the divine attributes of Creative and Honest.[^19] Like all other Heralds, she has temples to herself in all major cities.[^30] [^36] There are numerous pieces of artwork devoted to her, and depictions of her can be found decorating even utilitarian items like braziers.[^6]

"Ash's eyes" and "Ash's mask" are extremely common phrases, often used to express shock or surprise.[^37] [^13] [^38] "Shalashian temperament" is an idiom with uncertain meaning, though it may refer to being honest or impulsive.[^39] The name [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] is derived from Shalash.[^37] "Ash's brush of endless paint" is a phrase likely said by artists when they see something beautiful and astonishing.[^40]

Further west, in [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]] and, likely, other [Makabaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makabak "Makabak") countries, Shalash is known as **Epan** and titled the **Lady of Dreams**.[^9] In [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], she is called **Shush-daughter-God**.[^1]

- Shalash will be the flashback character in the eighth book of the Stormlight Archive.[^41]
- She's one of only two Heralds, alongside [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kalak]], whose names are naturally symmetric under [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] rules.[^42]
- "Shalash" bears a certain resemblance to [[Aon - The Coppermind|Aons]] Shao (Transformation), Ala (Beauty), and Ashe (Light). While this isn't coincidental, Shalash herself may be unaware of the linguistics of her name.[^43]
- Her name and [Shashara's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shashara "Shashara") are similar, but this isn't significant.[^44]
- Shalash's portrait used in the chapter arches is called the Artist by the [Dragonsteel Entertainment](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragonsteel_Entertainment "Dragonsteel Entertainment") staff.[^45]
- In Syrian mythology, Shalash was the wife of the god Dagon.[^46]
