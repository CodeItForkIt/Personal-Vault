## Moash

> “ *He couldn't be blamed. He was a product of his culture.* ”
> 
> —Moash about himself [^2]

**Moash**, also known as **Vyre**, is an [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") man on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. Originally a [bridgeman](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bridgeman "Bridgeman") in [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]],[^3] he becomes a soldier and bodyguard under [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]]; eventually, he leaves Bridge Four and switches sides to the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)"), then the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], serving them as a slave and warrior. He's the current holder of [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]].

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

Moash is lean, though strong, with a narrow hawkish face and brown hair speckled with black.[^4] [^3] [^5] He is tall, has a prominent nose, and has a scar on his chin.[^6] [^7] Born [darkeyed](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Darkeyed "Darkeyed"), Moash has brown eyes which turn tan after he receives a full set of Shards.[^8] When using [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]], Moash's eyes glow blue.[^9] Moash is a young man, potentially close to Kaladin's age.[^3] He is Alethi, and thus has tan skin and an [epicanthic fold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epicanthic_fold "wikipedia:Epicanthic fold").[^10]

Unlike many other members of Bridge Four, Moash does not have slave brands on his forehead. Due to this, he gets his Bridge Four tattoo on his left upper arm.[^6] [^11]

Following being named Vyre, Moash wears a custom uniform in the same style as the Bridge Four uniform, but dyed black and with no shoulder patch.[^12] He also appears to have lost weight, with his cheeks appearing sunken in.[^7]

After the Occupation of Urithiru, Moash gets impaled with crystal eye spikes through his empty eye sockets after his eyes get burned away.[^13] The crystal eyes glow violet, possibly with Voidlight, and jut from the back of his skull and the sides of his head, resembling a crown of crystals. Moash still has eyelids around his eye spikes, and can blink.[^14] [^15]

### Personality

Moash comes off as standoffish and grim, holding people at arm's length when he first meets them. Though once he does warm up to someone, he gets attached extremely quickly.[^3] [^4] Once he joins a group, he's friendly and amicable, and often the driving force behind more relaxed, off-duty activities.[^16] He values his friendships, and tries to salvage them when he can.[^17] [^18] He's eager to gain power and he admires those who do have it.[^19] At the same time, he's highly suspicious of those above him in the social order.[^6] He has a strong sense of justice,[^2] originating with the injustice visited on his grandparents earlier in his life.

Moash tends to view people's actions by the external factors that motivate said actions, external factors such as one's position as a lighteyes or darkeyes in the Vorin caste system.[^2] As he becomes more involved in the Fused army and pushes deeper into Odium's emotional voiding, his old priorities slip away.[^20] [^21]

His need to see [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]] and then all [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyes]] face retribution consumes him and pushes him further and further away from friends and into the waiting arms of the [Voidbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Voidbringers "Voidbringers").[^22] Eventually, as he gives in to Odium's power completely, he seems to lose any drive altogether, telling himself he feels no emotions even as he accomplishes his vengeance. In this period, he follows the commandments to seek out passion and follow the orders of the Fused.[^23] [^24] Although he continues to care for Kaladin, he thinks that suicide is the only way that Kaladin can be saved from Odium's torment, and in his own despair, urges Kaladin to kill himself rather than risk Kaladin joining Odium.[^25]

## Attributes and Abilities

Moash is a skilled spearman, enough so to kill an attacking [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], and was one of the best fighters while he was in Bridge Four.[^6] [^26] A fast learner, Moash tends to pick up new skills quickly and works diligently at them.[^27] [^28] He's also a decent spear teacher and commander, able to train a group of [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] before the attack on [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar").[^21]

For a time, Moash possessed a set of [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]]. While he was able to move naturally in his Plate after less than a week, he is less skilled with the Shardblade, finding it too clunky, and still defaulted to the spear when his life was on the line.[^29] [^26] Eventually, he's given [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien's]] [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]], granting him control over the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [Gravitation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gravitation "Gravitation") and [Adhesion](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Adhesion "Adhesion"). He appears to be natural at using it, as it takes him no time at all to drink in [[Stormlight - The Coppermind|Stormlight]] and use Lashings.[^24]

Following the Occupation of Urithiru, he was left blind until Odium and [Battah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battah "Battah") impaled [crystal spikes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crystal_spike "Crystal spike") into his eyes, allowing Moash to see [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] as outlines of light - seeing living things, infused gemstones, and [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]], but not color. Moash is able to distinguish different "rhythms" of light. This procedure appears to have granted Moash sight similar to [[Steel Inquisitor - The Coppermind|Steel Inquisitor]] Steel Sight, although focused on Rosharan Investiture. It is currently not known if this is due to the material used, nature of Investiture on Roshar, or the intent present when performing the Hemalurgy like procedure.[^30] [^13]

## History

### Early Life

Both of Moash's parents died when he was a child. He was raised by his aging grandparents, [Ana](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ana "Ana") and [Da](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Da "Da"), who both worked as silversmiths in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar").[^31] It was his grandfather who named him. Later, Moash would discover that his name was [[Singer - The Coppermind|singer]] in origin.[^32] Moash was generally met with hostility and distrust by other people, and likely had a troubled childhood because of it. This was one of the reasons Moash joined the caravans for work, which his grandparents encouraged.[^11] He did not want to enter the family business, preferring jobs that were more physically active.[^31] Moash worked the caravans with his uncle when he was young.[^2] Later during his time as a caravaneer, Moash would also work with [Rill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rill "Rill"), a mercenary, and [Guff](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Guff "Guff"), a wheelwright.[^16] [^11]

While he was working on a caravan for a few months, his grandparents became victims in the [Roshone Affair](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Roshone#Kholinar_and_the_Roshone_Affair "Roshone") which led to them dying while incarcerated in the palace dungeons. Moash ultimately blamed [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar Kholin]], who was then a royal prince but would later become the king of Alethkar, for their deaths. Ana and Da had been dead for two months by the time Moash returned and was told of their passing by a neighbor after returning to an empty home. The pain of losing them helped shape Moash's personality.[^31]

> “ *He’d always been met with hostility, no matter where he storming went. A youth like him, too big and obviously too confident for a darkeyes, had been considered a threat. \[...\] He’d spent his life putting up with looks like that.*”
> 
> —Moash about himself.[^11]

### Bridge Four

#### Sent to the Bridgecrews

Moash joined the war voluntarily with the hope of winning a Shardblade, and traveled to the Shattered Plains in [Jam's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jam_\(caravaneer\) "Jam (caravaneer)") caravan.[^11] Assuming he would be made a spearman, Moash instead got sent to the bridge crews along with most of the other people who joined with him, ending up in [[Bridge Four - The Coppermind|Bridge Four]]. Moash initially tried to run away from the bridge crews, but he was caught and beaten for it, with the threat of receiving a slave brand if he tried to run again.[^33]

When [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] first began his attempts to reform Bridge Four, Moash was singled out to be made an example due to his strong appearance and since he was one of the men who ignored Kaladin's command to get up, instead rolling back over to sleep more. Kaladin pulled Moash out of bed and punched him in the stomach, picking him up over his shoulder and taking him outside the barracks while he was stunned. Irritated at this treatment, Moash called out to [Gaz](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gaz "Gaz"), asking if they needed to follow Kaladin's orders. Receiving a negative from Gaz, Moash told Kaladin to storm off unless he planned on beating all of them up, and he and the other bridgemen dispersed.[^3]

Moash continued to distrust Kaladin's intentions and openly disliked him. While many of the other bridgemen also disliked Kaladin, he was one of the most vocal.[^34] When Kaladin chose to stand instead of resting on a bridge run, Moash swore at him.[^35] He was the first one to laugh at the idea of pooling their money and food to help the wounded bridgemen Kaladin brought back, claiming that if it were him, he'd rather die quickly on a bridge run than slowly in the barracks.[^36] Moash, along with [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]], refused to look at Kaladin when he tried to pull the bridgemen into conversation on chasm duty.[^34] Moash did let Kaladin use his flint and steel to cauterize wounds, however, which he likely had due to it being standard equipment that caravaneers carried with them.[^35] [^11]

When Kaladin picked up and stared at a spear during chasm duty, Moash taunted him, calling Kaladin "lordling," which he picked up from talking with Gaz. Moash shared with [Narm](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Narm "Narm") what he learned from Gaz, claiming that Kaladin was lying about being a previous squadleader. This triggered the other bridgemen to start complaining about Kaladin with each other, until they were stunned into silence by Kaladin's kata that he performed. Moash himself dropped the spheres he was holding in shock. That night, Moash accepted [Rock's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rock "Rock") stew, though Moash still refused to rise the next morning to train when Kaladin called for it.[^34]

Later, Kaladin named Moash a subsquad commander for Bridge Four, along with Rock, [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft"), and [Skar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skar "Skar"). Kaladin told the four to go gather their men, but Moash lingered behind to ask Kaladin why he chose him to be one of the subsquad leaders. He explained that it was because Moash was so vocally resistant to Kaladin's efforts for so long, and that in addition to being capable and intelligent, it showed Moash was strong-willed, which Kaladin could use. Moash accepted this, but claimed that he was not like the others who revered Kaladin, and he still didn't trust him. When asked why Moash was choosing to obey him then, he claimed he was curious before moving off to follow Kaladin's previous orders.[^4]

Despite his efforts to distrust Kaladin, Moash joined Teft and Rock in telling Kaladin they won't forget him and will carry on his legacy after Kaladin is beaten and strung up for attempting the side carry maneuver on a bridge run. Moash commented that this way they would only lose one member thanks to what Kaladin did, and that he did it for them.[^37] Moash was awed by the side carry maneuver at the time, not realizing the harm it had done to the greater battle.[^38]

Moash shared with Kaladin his desire to win a Shardblade and how he was sent to the bridge crews after Sigzil told them about how the kingdom [Babatharnam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Babatharnam "Babatharnam") decides who gets placed in power. Unlike Kaladin, Moash said that he'd join the lighteyes if given the opportunity and that he still planned to win a Shardblade to change the world so abuses wouldn't happen to anyone. Even though Moash said he would make lighteyes work the difficult and dangerous jobs instead of darkeyes, he denied Sigzil's warning that the world would stay the same.[^33]

> **Sigzil**: " *If you were to overthrow the lighteyes and place yourselves in power, Moash, I doubt that the world would be a very different place. The abuses would still happen. Simply to other people.*"  
> **Moash**: " *No. I’d change the world, Sigzil. And I mean to.*"
> 
> ―Conversation between [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]] and Moash [^33]

#### Learning to Fight and Trust

> “ *You’re a fool and an instigator. But you’re an honest one. If you get us killed, it won’t be on purpose. Can’t say that for some I’ve served under.*”

Moash was eager to begin training with spears in the chasms.[^40] When Bridge Four started their training, Moash was one of the first who changed into a fighting man and only needed two hours to start exercises Kaladin usually wouldn't start until the second or third day of training. Only Skar and Drehy were nearly as talented as Moash was.[^41] Later, on the bridgerun when [Dunny](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dunny "Dunny") died, Moash used one of the moves Kaladin taught him to knock Kaladin to the ground and prevent him from running onto the bridge in an attempt to try and reach Dunny's body, and probably saved Kaladin's life. After Kaladin tended to the wounded bridgemen, Kaladin told Moash that he was right to stop him, and Moash then told Kaladin that he was wrong about him and they shook hands.[^39]

Once Moash fully accepted putting his trust in Kaladin, he later defended him when Skar questioned how Kaladin would get the spears out of the chasms once it was time to run, much to Kaladin's surprise. During that same chasm duty shift Kaladin found an emerald broam, which Moash became infatuated with and insisted he could swallow so they could keep it to spend on medical supplies. Moash was crestfallen when Kaladin repeatedly told him it would be too suspicious for a bridgeman to spend a broam, despite Bridge Four being able to keep an equal amount of other spheres in smaller denominations.[^42]

Bridge Four continued to get more disciplined and loyal to Kaladin. Moash and the other bridgemen always chose to stand beside Kaladin at parade rest while the army crossed their bridge, despite passing soldiers jeering and throwing overripe [pile-vine](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Pilevine "Pilevine") fruits at them, one of which hit Moash in the face. After they returned from that bridge run, Moash led Bridge Four in doing their first salute to Kaladin, which Teft had taught them.[^43]

The situation for Bridge Four became more dire after [Hashal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hashal "Hashal") informed them they would be required to go on every bridge run while continuing to perform chasm duty every night.[^44] Exhausted after barely getting any sleep, Moash and the other bridgemen felt discouraged on their chances of survival. Moash suggested charging the soldiers and [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") as he passed, and said that if they were to die anyway they could take out a few lighteyes before they were killed. Some of the bridgemen liked this idea, but Kaladin dismissed it and said he had another plan, which satisfied Moash. Kaladin's plan, which was to wear armor adorned in singer bones and carapace to draw arrow fire to himself instead of the bridgemen, worked, much to the joy of Bridge Four. Moash was excited by the idea and immediately began thinking about expanding on it. Later, Moash commented that their luck that day was almost enough to make Moash buy his own prayer armband despite his criticism of Kaladin's purchase of one before the bridge run.[^45]

Following Kaladin's success, Bridge Four's scavenging quota in the chasms was decreased, giving them more time to train secretly. Moash was "surprisingly good" with the spear, and often kept training while the other men took breaks. Kaladin recognized the dedication in Moash and related to how he felt after [[Tien - The Coppermind|Tien]] died, and advised Moash not to overwork himself. Moash told Kaladin when asked that he worked so hard because of vengeance, but declined telling Kaladin who he wanted to kill. Moash then told Kaladin that it was because of him that Moash's plans were still possible, and swore to guard Kaladin with his life. Kaladin felt more comfortable with Moash because he didn't seem to revere him in the same way the others did.[^28]

#### Battle of the Tower

On Tanatashev 1173, Bridge Four participated in the [Battle of the Tower](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battle_of_the_Tower "Battle of the Tower"), where the combined armies of Highprinces [Sadeas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Torol_Sadeas "Torol Sadeas") and [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] assaulted the Tower. Moash was one of the four decoys wearing carapace armor, along with Kaladin, used to attract Parshendi arrows. Teft was injured in the approach with an arrow to the shoulder, and Moash carried Teft urgently over to Kaladin to be tended to once their bridge was set.[^28] [^46]

Over an hour later, Kaladin called Moash to stay by him when he noticed Sadeas retreating from the battle, and followed Kaladin through the crowd to watch Sadeas, unharmed, cross back over bridge ten.[^47] Bridge Four left with the rest of Sadeas' army, falling slowly behind due to carrying their two wounded members. Despite the opportunity to escape once they got far enough behind, Moash and the rest of Bridge Four agreed with Kaladin to go back and help Dalinar's army.[^48]

After Kaladin exhausted himself from using too much Stormlight in order to protect Bridge Four on the approach, Moash commanded Bridge Four and directed them to lift the bridge back up to run it into place.[^48] Once Kaladin said the Second Ideal and recovered to fight on the other side of the bridge, Moash led a group to defend the bridge from Parshendi that had jumped the chasm to attack. Though all three dead were on Moash's side of the battle, he felt energized by the hard fight instead of weakened. Moash stayed with Kaladin when he went to find someone in command once the Alethi soldiers broke through to the bridge. As Kaladin fought through Parshendi to get to [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin Kholin]] to tell him to retreat, Moash followed close. He reluctantly went back with Adolin when told to do so by Kaladin.[^49]

When the battle was over, Dalinar traded his Shardblade, [Oathbringer](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Oathbringer_\(Shardblade\) "Oathbringer (Shardblade)"), for the bridgemen.[^50] Moash and the rest of Bridge Four celebrated with stew in the Kholin warcamp that night, and Moash expressed eagerness to try and learn how to use Radiant powers like Kaladin.[^19]

### Kholin Bodyguard

> “ *I’m not going to pretend I don’t hate them. But I’ll be civil. We have a duty. We’ll do it well. Better than anyone expects. We’re Bridge Four.* ”
> 
> —Moash to [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] in regards to lighteyes.[^51]

Moash decided to stay to become a bodyguard for [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] underneath [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]], who was now appointed as a captain. Kaladin led Moash, [Skar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Skar "Skar"), and [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") through the camp the next morning and explained the plan to train the rest of the bridgemen. Moash was skeptical that the hundreds of worn down bridgemen could be turned into fighting men, and was also reluctant to put his trust in Dalinar. However, he was excited to be further trained by Kaladin and hoped to train with swords despite being darkeyed. Kaladin named Moash a lieutenant as second in command behind Kaladin, along with Skar, Teft, [Rock](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rock "Rock"), and [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]]. Skar and Moash were specifically chosen to focus on guarding Dalinar, as they were the most proficient and dangerous of Bridge Four.[^6]

Later that day, Bridge Four received tattoos over their slave brands, marking them as free men. Moash felt conflicted on getting the tattoo, since he did not have a slave brand, and decided to get the tattoo on his upper arm to Kaladin's approval. He was the only member - even of those without a slave brand - to get the tattoo placed on his arm instead of his forehead. Kaladin then lead Bridge Four to the quartermaster, [Rind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rind "Rind"), to receive their uniforms. Moash received his uniform first, and was skeptical of how it would look on him. However, after he saw himself in the mirror and was complimented by the quartermaster, Moash blushed and accepted that the uniforms looked good, which cued the rest of Bridge Four to put on theirs.[^6]

Shortly after becoming a bodyguard, Moash was approached by [Rill](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rill "Rill"), a mercenary who Moash worked with when he was a caravaneer. Rill introduced Moash to [Graves](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Graves "Graves"), who brought Moash into his plans for assassinating King [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar Kholin]].[^16]

A bit under two Rosharan weeks later, Kaladin took Moash, [Drehy](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Drehy "Drehy"), [Mart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Mart "Mart"), and [Eth](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eth "Eth") to guard Dalinar during a meeting with Elhokar at the palace. Moash tried to threaten the low ranking lighteyed guard at the palace gates after the guard doubted Kaladin, but Kaladin cut Moash off and chastised him, much to Moash's annoyance. After having a moment to think and cool down, Moash apologized to Kaladin and agreed that Bridge Four is above provoking fights and that he'll do better. Once at the meeting, Drehy and Moash took watch on the balcony. Dalinar, realizing that their plans would put more than just him at risk, expanded Kaladin's team to guarding [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]], [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]], and [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]] as well as having them slowly replace the King's Guard.[^51]

The first time Moash guarded Adolin and Renarin while they trained, he was underwhelmed by how ordinary the lighteyed training grounds looked. After Kaladin spoke disrespectfully to an [ardent](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ardent "Ardent") about the King's Blades, Moash reminded Kaladin that he had previously told him that they should avoid provoking lighteyes. Before moving off to his guard position, Moash questioned Kaladin about who [Amaram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Amaram "Amaram") was, having noticed Kaladin's distress since he arrived at the warcamps. Kaladin told Moash that Amaram was the one that had given Kaladin his slave brands, and Moash confirmed with Kaladin that they would somehow get back at the lighteyes who had wronged them.[^52]

#### Assassination Plot Begins

> **Moash**: " *You have to get the man into a duel, Kaladin. Bring him down, man against man. Until you do it, something’s going to feel wrong to you, deep down in your gut.*"  
> **Kaladin**: " *You sound like you know what this feels like.*"  
> **Moash**: " *Yeah. I have some Voidbringers in my past too. Maybe that’s why I understand you. Maybe that’s why you understand me.*"
> 
> ―Conversation about Amaram [^53]

That night, Moash had a shift on the King's Guard during the highstorm. Kaladin expressed concern at Moash working two shifts, Moash in turn encouraging Kaladin to get some sleep as well, as he and the rest of Bridge Four were worried about him.[^54] It was at the end of this shift that Moash enacted his first assassination attempt with Graves on Elhokar's life. The railing on Elhokar's balcony was cut with a Shardblade, coming free when he leaned on it and nearly sending him over the edge.[^55] Likely, Moash had smuggled a rope out onto the balcony, letting Graves up to slice the railing.[^53]

While later learning to ride horses, Kaladin questioned [Natam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Natam "Natam") if he saw anything. Natam told Kaladin that Moash stayed past the end of his shift and kept watch on the balcony. Moash was eager to learn how to ride, and Kaladin found him intently listening to [Jenet's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jenet "Jenet") teachings. Kaladin wondered if Moash had something to do with the assassination attempt, but quickly brushed it off as ridiculous.[^27]

During the next highstorm, when [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] attacked, Moash acted as the rear guard with [Ralinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ralinor "Ralinor"). When told to by Kaladin, Moash retreated to protect the king and grabbed Renarin to haul him back with them.[^56]

Sigzil later explained the idea of smuggling out a rope onto the balcony to Kaladin. Unsettled by how it implicated Moash, Kaladin told Sigzil to not speak to anyone else about it, wanting to investigate on his own. That night, Kaladin talked to Moash, but did not successfully get any information. Moash first brought up Amaram, asking what they were going to do about him, as Moash knew what Kaladin's situation felt like. Kaladin probed for what had happened in Moash's past, but Moash declined to answer that night. Moash tried to encourage Kaladin to go out with him and Bridge Four to some taverns, but Kaladin refused.[^53]

#### Past Revealed

> “ *And what else am I supposed to do? What kind of justice can a man like me get on a *king*, Kaladin? Tell me.* ”
> 
> —Moash to Kaladin.[^31]

Kaladin accepted [Zahel's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Zahel "Zahel") previous offer to train him in using a Shardblade, and brought Moash, Teft and [Yake](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yake "Yake") with him to the lighteyed training grounds. Moash was eager to hold a Shardblade, and grabbed for it first before Teft could, drawing a gloryspren as he held it. The Blade was heavier than Moash expected, as the stories made him think it would have no weight.[^31]

[Ivis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ivis "Ivis") took over training Moash and the others in sword stances, sparring moves, and facing a Shardblade while Zahel trained Renarin. When the others took a water break, Moash kept practicing his sword forms. Moash did not flinch while facing the Shardblade, so Ivis let Moash go and he joined Kaladin at the water barrel. It was there that Moash, unprompted, told Kaladin about how King Elhokar had his grandparents, [Ana](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ana "Ana") and [Da](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Da "Da"), thrown in jail and left to die. Moash described the event as execution, but Kaladin was not sure it was the same as murder.[^31]

With his suspicions of a motive confirmed, Kaladin confronted Moash about sabotaging Elhokar's railing and trying to assassinate him. After some argument, Moash promised to pause his plans as long as Kaladin promised to meet with Graves and listen to his ideas. Kaladin agreed, and Moash relaxed, returning to sword practice.[^31]

#### Shardbearer

When [[Adolin Kholin - The Coppermind|Adolin]] tried to give [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] the gift of [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]], Kaladin instead gave them to Moash, thereby making him a lighteyes in rank.[^57] The Shardblade had a twisting vine pattern along the center of the blade, and the Shardplate, previously belonging to [Abrobadar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Abrobadar "Abrobadar"), was painted orange.[^57] Moash later had the Plate repainted to be blue with red accents.[^29] His eyes started to lighten a week after receiving the Shards,[^8] though he still did not consider himself lighteyes.

### Attempted Regicide and Desertion

> **Kaladin**: " *Sometimes lives must be spent for the greater good.*"  
> **Moash**: " *Exactly!*"  
> **Kaladin**: " *That's what Amaram said. In regards to my men, whom he killed to cover up his theft.*"
> 
> ―Conversation between [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] and Moash on the topic of assassinating [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]]

Moash became involved in a plot with [Graves](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Graves "Graves") to assassinate King Elhokar Kholin, which was ultimately thwarted by Kaladin. Moash took his Plate and Blade and fled the warcamps with Graves after their failed attempt.[^20]

They head out into the wilderness with a caravan of stolen goods. Graves intended to introduce Moash to the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)"), hoping that bringing back a full Shardbearer could compensate for his plan's failure. A short while into the trip, they were attacked by a group of [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]]. Graves was killed, and Moash attempted to fight with his Shardblade but deemed it too unwieldy. He got hold of a spear and was able to kill [Leshwi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leshwi "Leshwi"), one of the Fused.

Upon seeing this, the other Fused gave Moash a choice, he could give up his weapons and come with them, or die at their hands.[^26] They did this because they thought that Leshwi would want to have revenge once she was reborn. Leshwi did not want revenge however, considering Moash to have great passion.[^32]

### Servant of the Fused

![[Moash---The-Coppermind-3.jpg]]

by [taratjah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/taratjah "Coppermind:Artists/taratjah")

As a slave, he pulled supply sledges across the country. On one of his routes, he saw a group of singers pulling sledges like the other slaves. He stood up for the group, gaining more respect among the Fused and the singers.[^2] Eventually, he joined the assault on Kholinar as a warrior for the Fused's army.

In Kholinar, he and his singer companions fought with Kholinar's Wall Guard. At the climax of the fight, Moash found his way to King [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar]], and stabbed him through the chest. He then stabbed the king again in the eye, just before Elhokar could finish swearing the [First Ideal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words#The_First_Ideal "Immortal Words"). His vengeance was fulfilled, and he looked up to see Kaladin, who was in Kholinar with the king on a mission to save the city. Moash saluted his former captain, who had frozen in shock at what happened, and left.[^22]

Following the siege, he was put to work as a laborer clearing out the debris. There he was approached by a Fused, [Hnanan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hnanan "Hnanan"), who gave him a [bright golden knife](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vyre%27s_knife "Vyre's knife") with a sapphire attached to it. The Fused then sent him to assassinate the [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]], whom he killed with the knife.[^23] Some time after the successful assassination, the Fused, [Leshwi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leshwi "Leshwi"), offered Moash Jezrien's [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]] as well as a new identity. He accepted, took the singer name **Vyre**, which meant *He Who Quiets*, and used the Honorblade to join the Fused in the air.[^24]

A year later, Moash took part in the Fused assault on Hearthstone, where he murdered Roshone in front of Kaladin, who went into a state of battle shock. Moash, now no longer capable of reasoning in a conventional way,[^25] then attempted to persuade Kaladin to commit suicide. He was stopped by Renarin, who showed Moash a vision of the man he could have been had he not betrayed Bridge Four, a Windrunner of the Third Ideal, causing Moash to flee.[^58]

In Kholinar, he sat in on a session of the Nine, where he warns them that Kaladin will foil their plan to occupy Urithiru.[^59] Later, he was approached by Odium, who intended to break Kaladin and questioned Moash on the best ways of tormenting his former captain.[^1]

### The Occupation of Urithiru

> **Moash**: " *I am unchained.*"  
> **Odium**: " *And yet, you think so often of Kaladin.*"  
> **Moash**: " *I am … mostly unchained.*"
> 
> ―Moash speaking with [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] [^1]

During the occupation of Urithiru, Vyre used his Honorblade to operate the [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] between Kholinar and the tower for the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]].[^60] When the uprising to take Urithiru back began, he set a trap for Kaladin in his family's clinic, but the trap was instead sprung by [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") and [[Lift - The Coppermind|Lift]]. Moash injured Lift, then used a [raysium](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raysium "Raysium") dagger containing [anti-Stormlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Stormlight "Anti-Stormlight") to permanently kill Teft's spren, [Phendorana](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Phendorana "Phendorana"), before killing Teft.[^61] [^62] He handed [Lirin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lirin "Lirin") to the Fused as insurance against Kaladin, ordering them to kill Lirin in front of his son should Kaladin fight back.[^63]

Moash then descended to the basement of Urithiru to kill Queen [[Navani Kholin - The Coppermind|Navani]]. Nearly succeeding, he was ambushed by an injured [Raboniel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Raboniel "Raboniel"), which gave Navani time to bond the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]]. After restoring the tower, she used its Light to incapacitate Moash and forcibly return his pain to him.[^64] Moash fled, hating himself for what he had done to Teft and realizing that he did not truly regret the murder, only the guilt. He eventually broke his spine in a fall and nearly died before being rescued by a few Heavenly Ones and given Stormlight. When he recovered, he found that he was blind.[^30]

### Transformation

![[Moash---The-Coppermind-4.jpg]]

by [ghostfellow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/ghostfellow "Coppermind:Artists/ghostfellow")

After being brought to [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), Moash found that Odium was no longer feeding on his emotions, and began to dwell on his actions in Urithiru. Moash was certain he had seen Teft's spirit in the glow behind Navani, the last thing he saw before he was blinded. Laying in bed, Moash clawed at his face and begged to have his pain taken away again, the protection from his guilt being the reason Moash followed Odium in the first place. Odium appeared and told Moash that unlike [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]], he didn't think taking emotions was right and refused Moash's pleas. He insisted Moash was not a monster, but rather a hero for his actions, and explained that he needed to be able to handle the price of what he had done if he wanted to be worthy of his title and [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]].

Odium then placed a vision into Moash's mind of "every wrong being righted" - the Fused armies bringing unity and peace to every world, lead by the Blackthorn, with working class people getting retribution against kings. He told Moash that if he joined him, he would have a part in that, and his pain would be worth something. Moash asked how, and Odium stepped back to allow [Battah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Battah "Battah") to strap Moash down and impale [crystal spikes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crystal_spike "Crystal spike") into Moash's empty eye sockets with a mallet. While Moash screamed, in his thoughts he claimed to reject his guilt and condemned Kaladin for serving lighteyes despite claiming to protect.

When the procedure was finished, Moash found that he could see [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] as outlines of light - seeing living things, gemstones, and spren, but not color. Odium appeared to him as a blinding source of light, while Battah appeared as a different "rhythm" of light. When asked if he was ready to serve, Moash confirmed that he will "do what must be done".[^13]

### Siege of the Shattered Plains

> “ *This will hurt me. It is the pain of building a new empire.* ”
> 
> —Moash to Sigzil.[^65]

Five days before the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|Contest of champions]], Moash arrived on the Shattered Plains with [El](https://coppermind.net/wiki/El "El") and the other reinforcements through the Elsegate created by [Dai-Gonarthis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dai-Gonarthis "Dai-Gonarthis").[^14] Moash did not join the battle until two days later when [Leyten](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Leyten "Leyten") and his squires found Moash. Leyten sent a code over spanreed to [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]] that they had found him, and intended to wait for Sigzil to arrive to engage but was caught by the fabrial Moash held that negated Radiant abilities. When Sigzil rushed over to help, he was also caught by the fabrial and he and his squires fell to the ground. Since Moash was using an Honorblade, his abilities still worked.

As Sigzil recovered, Moash killed one of Leyten's squires with a Shardblade strike to the head and kicked Leyten away. Sigzil sent his squires to retreat and Leyten used himself to distract Moash while his squires ran, Sigzil finally arriving to in turn distract Moash from Leyten. Moash didn't flinch after being stabbed in the back by Sigzil, and Sigzil then noticed Moash's crystal eyes for the first time, unnerved.

Knowing he was outmatched without his Radiant abilities, Sigzil attempted to get Moash talking and asked why he's fighting them. Moash responded that Sigzil chose the lighteyes over him despite everything they'd done to them, and that he welcomes his emotions now. Able to see Radiant spren, Moash suddenly Lashed upward when Sigzil trailed off and used his [anti-Stormlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Anti-Stormlight "Anti-Stormlight") dagger to kill [Ethenia](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ethenia "Ethenia"), Leyten's spren. Afterwards, Moash stabbed Leyten in the chest with the same knife before giving a Bridge Four salute and retreating. Leyten died in Sigzil's arms after shouting a [Death Rattle](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") that prophesied Moash killing Sigzil and [[Vienta - The Coppermind|Vienta]].[^15]

Two days later, the final day before the Contest of Champions, Moash appeared again on the battlefield to prevent Sigzil from retreating with the rest of the army. Moash activated his fabrial, causing Sigzil to drop from the sky from a height that broke his legs and made him unable to move them. Landing next to him, Moash explained to Sigzil that he demanded the fabrials be given to him, despite their rarity and the Fused fearing the power of them. Sigzil tried to get out his knife to fight back, but Moash stepped on Sigzil's wrist and caused him to drop it, and said Sigzil was in the way of what he was going to build with the new Odium.

Moash then attacked Vienta and nicked her arm, destroying it, but did not land a direct enough hit to kill her. Before Moash could land a killing blow, Sigzil renounced his oaths as a Windrunner, which caused Vienta to disappear before Moash's attack hit. Once Vienta appeared on the ground as a [deadeye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye") Shardblade, Sigzil used it to fend Moash off. Distracted, Moash did not notice [Lopen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lopen "Lopen"), who tackled him to the ground and destroyed the fabrial, restoring Radiant powers in the area. The rest of Bridge Four then appeared to defend Sigzil and Lopen, and deciding he was outmatched, Moash retreated.[^65]

## Relationships

### Ana and Da

> “ *He murdered them, Kaladin. That sorry excuse for a king killed the only family I ever had.* ”
> 
> —Moash [^17]

The death of Moash's grandparents, [Ana](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ana "Ana") and [Da](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Da "Da"), influenced him greatly. Ana and Da raised Moash after the death of Moash's parents when he was a child.[^31] They were kind, and the ones that encouraged Moash to join the caravans for work.[^11] Moash considered them the only family he had ever had, despite also having an uncle that he worked on the caravans with.[^17] [^2]

### Kaladin

> “ *Moash … had been his friend. They’d spent hours by the fire, talking about their lives. Kaladin had opened his heart to this man, in ways he hadn’t to most of the others. \[...\] Kaladin had failed Moash. As soundly as he’d failed Dunny, Mart, and Jaks. And of them all, losing Moash hurt the most. Because in those callous eyes, Kaladin saw himself.* ”
> 
> —Kaladin's thoughts about Moash.[^58]

Moash and [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] have a complex relationship. During their first moments in Bridge Four, Moash was Kaladin's biggest detractor, and only later trusted him after Kaladin proved he was genuine in his efforts.[^39] From then on, Moash supported Kaladin and they became close friends and confidants.[^42] [^51] [^58] Moash and Kaladin both saw themselves in each other, and they have similar histories with lighteyes, including both having their lives significantly changed by the [Roshone Affair](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Roshone#Kholinar_and_the_Roshone_Affair "Roshone").[^28] [^53] [^66]

However, Moash's desire for vengeance against King [[Elhokar Kholin - The Coppermind|Elhokar Kholin]] split them apart. Moash simultaneously resented Kaladin for having defended the person who killed his grandparents, and felt guilty for having tried to kill Kaladin to get to Elhokar.[^32] [^26] After he finally succeeded in killing Elhokar during the siege of Kholinar, it was partially Moash's guilt in seeing how Kaladin reacted that caused him to agree to have his pain taken away by [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^23]

Once Moash had his pain taken away and became Vyre, Kaladin was the only thing that regularly caused his painful emotions to return briefly. Moash became fixated on getting Kaladin to admit Moash was right so that he could become truly painless, and believed convincing Kaladin to kill himself was a mercy compared to Odium potentially taking Kaladin as Odium had taken Moash. Moash refused to physically kill Kaladin himself, however, and believed it to be impossible.[^1] Though Kaladin had initially felt guilty and hurt over what had happened between him and Moash, after these actions by Moash - including killing [Teft](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Teft "Teft") - Kaladin no longer missed him.[^58] [^67]

## Trivia

- Though Moash's name is similar to [Moshe Feder](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moshe_Feder "Moshe Feder") (Brandon's editor) it is not actually a cameo.[^68]
- Moash's eye color is initially (and erroneously) written as dark green in *The Way of Kings*. In *Words of Radiance*, his eyes are said to be brown. Peter Ahlstrom has confirmed that Moash's eyes are brown and that the first book is incorrect.[^69]
- The name Moash was originally a singer name.[^32]
- Had he not begun killing for Odium, Moash could have become a Windrunner of at least the Third Ideal.[^58]
- Initial concept art of Moash's black Bridge Four uniform included a Bridge Four patch that was distorted and upside down, however, it was deemed too dramatic even for Moash.[^70]
