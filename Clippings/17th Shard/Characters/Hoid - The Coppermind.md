## Hoid

> “ *And while I am your friend, please understand that our goals do not completely align. You must not trust yourself with me. If I have to watch this world crumble and burn to get what I need, I will do so. With tears, yes, but I would let it happen.* ”

**Hoid** is a [worldhopper](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopper "Worldhopper") and [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]] [^7] that travels the cosmere, originally born on the planet [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]] before the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]].[^8] He was one of the major figures in the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering of Adonalsium]], but avoided taking up a [[Shards|Shard]] himself,[^9] instead choosing to wander the cosmere pursuing his unknown goals, often in the guise of a fool [^10] or storyteller.[^11] He will be guided at times by a mysterious sense about where he should go, but not what he is to accomplish there.[^12] One of his major goals is the defeat of [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]], the original [Vessel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel") of the [[Shards|Shard]] [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^13] While Hoid has generally avoided direct involvement in the major events of the cosmere, he is oftentimes present in the periphery, influencing in large ways and small the people who will go on to alter the course of history, as well as occasionally taking on an apprentice to train at his side.[^14] Hoid has been present for events such as the restoration of the city of [Elantris](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantris_\(city\) "Elantris (city)") on [[Sel - The Coppermind|Sel]],[^15] the [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|Catacendre]] of [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]],[^16] and the [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]] of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^17] Along the way he has gained access to a prodigious number of powerful [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 4|Invested Arts]] and arcana, including [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]],[^18] [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 6|AonDor]],[^19] [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]],[^20] and [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]],[^21] among others.

## Appearance & Personality

### Appearance

Hoid's appearance varies dramatically from book to book. He's often in disguise, which makes his true appearance difficult to ascertain. Couple this with the fact that he can Lightweave his appearance in order to look however he wants, it makes it nearly impossible to know for sure what he looks like. To the best of our knowledge, his face is lined,[^22] hawk-like and arrow shaped. Hoid is tall and thin [^10] and also has a sharp nose and jaw with delicate eyebrows.[^22] He tends to cock his head to one side [^23] and has keen blue eyes.[^10] Hoid's hair is white, though he was not born with that color [^24] and he occasionally dyes it.[^25] There is a particular reason for his hair being white.[^26]

When he is on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] during the [[Clippings/17th Shard/Events/War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]], serving as King [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 10|Elhokar Kholin's]], and later Queen [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin's]] Wit, Hoid has the appearance of an Alethi man in his mid-30s [^27] with deep onyx hair. He wears a stiff black coat and carries a long thin sword at his waist.[^10] This blade is not magically Invested but has personal value to Hoid.[^28] It is a symbol of office for the King/Queen's Wit, though Hoid himself is not a skilled swordfighter.[^29]

### Personality

Hoid's true personality, while also usually disguised, is more easily seen as he occasionally drops whatever part he is playing. One of his overall most noticeable traits is his sharp wit and intelligence. The characters he plays often have a penchant for jokes and mockery but when he removes the facade his personality often seems mysterious and grave.[^30] [^23] Hoid would not consider himself a good man.[^31] Hoid is not always serious when he should be, due to how much normal human interaction bores him.[^32]

Hoid cannot directly physically harm people; this is an involuntary trait that is the result of changes to his spirit made during his time as a [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]].[^33] [^34] [[Lunamor - The Coppermind|Lunamor]] says he cannot hurt man because it is forbidden by other gods.[^35] He also tells a young [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]], "You needn't fear me. I'm terribly ineffective at hurting people. I blame my upbringing." [^36] Hoid apologizes to [[Kelsier - The Coppermind|Kelsier]] after beating him in a fight, stating he doesn't have the inclination to do real harm to him.[^37] This aversion causes him to be nauseated when thinking about physically harming someone, and is sometimes bad enough to incapacitate him. However, this only extends to physical trauma- Hoid has no issues with mentally hurting someone or through inaction.[^38] Hoid also has issues with harming himself physically, such as when he has to manipulate another man into punching him when he wants a tooth knocked out, rather than just doing it himself.[^18] [^39] If he had the capacity to, he would be willing to kill to achieve his goals, he would also let even those he cares about perish if it meant the furtherance of his goals.[^6] [^40]

Hoid is a very kind person to those he takes a liking to, doing his best to emotionally console them and give them the will to keep going. This can be seen in the various times he has told [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] stories in order to comfort him and teach him a lesson that he believes he needs to learn.[^23] [^41] He has done the same to Shallan, most notably when he met her as a child traumatized by her experiences and the abuses she went through during the [[Middlefest Fair - The Coppermind|Middlefest Fair]]. Here, he consoles her and gave her motivation to keep moving forward.[^36] He did the same later, when speaking with her in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar").[^42] He occasionally uses [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomancy]] to help Soothe people.[^36] [^43]

While he is very comforting to those he likes, and can sometimes be open about some parts of his past; he does make sure that they never come to trust him too much, and that they understand what kind of person he is, and that they understand the dangers involved with getting too close to or trusting him.[^6] [^44]

On the other end, he finds great joy in tormenting those he hates, such as [Ruthar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ruthar "Ruthar"), [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]], or Kelsier.[^45] [^46] [^47] [^37] This is likely a large part in his enjoyment of playing the role of court jesters like the King's Wit or the Imperial Fool. This behavior of his tends to rub many people the wrong way, making him despised by many people he interacts with.[^37] [^17] He sometimes bickers even with those he likes, such as [[Design - The Coppermind|Design]], though this takes the form of an exasperated fondness rather than bitter mockery.[^46] [^48]

He deeply enjoys storytelling, and has learned many stories over his long life. The stories he tells often have a personal or thematic relevance to those he is telling them to.[^11] [^23] [^42] [^41] Though sometimes, he simply tells stories for the sake of telling them, as he feels that as stories exist, they must be told.[^11] [^48] [^49] As a storyteller, he deeply hates when people spoil his stories.[^18] He also enjoys pontificating on the nature of art and storytelling to whoever would listen.[^30] [^17] [^18] [^46] [^50] [^48] He also enjoys asking people questions of philosophy and ideology, and would do so even with complete strangers. He uses these questions in order to use the answers of others as a jumping off point to share his own philosophy on the subject in question.[^30]

He also finds joy in getting away with things.[^51] As a child, he often stared at the stars and wondered about what they were and what it would be like to visit them.[^8] For unknown reasons, he was willing to turn down the immense power of a [[Shards|Shard]] when it was offered to him.[^52] [^53]

He has a very negative outlook on himself, often believing that he has no answers for others or even any answers at all,[^23] believes that he should never be trusted due to what he is willing to do to achieve his goals,[^6] [^44] he thinks himself arrogant and regrets many of his actions and inactions throughout his life,[^54] [^23] and he does not consider himself a good man.[^31] He claims that the [First Gem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/First_Gem "First Gem") became completely worthless in his wearing of it.[^23] His ultimate goal is to "make that which once was," though the exact details are currently unknown.[^55]

#### Philosophy

He believes that the main thing that people value in creation is not the creation itself, but the novelty, timeliness, and originality of it. Observing that if two people make the same discovery independently, the one who reveals their discovery first is the one given credit and lauded for their innovation, while the second is considered a forger. That if two artists create similar works of comparable pulchritude independent of each other, one would be lauded and the other would be bemoaned as derivative. He claims that if it was truly a matter of the aesthetic beauty of a work, it would not matter which came first. From this, he claims that the idea that what is valued most is creative ability, wisdom, and invention to be nothing more than a lie that humanity tells itself. He admits that it is a rather cynical viewpoint, but holds to it regardless.[^30]

He feels that art and aesthetic beauty is not some eternal idea outside of humanity, but rather that it inherently relies upon and is defined by humanity. That art produced with low audience expectations can gain much love and loyal following from those given more then they expect; but conversely, an artist held in very high esteem can have their works torn apart and considered a failure for falling even slightly short from expectations. Aesthetic beauty is given definition through audience expectation and interpretation, it relies deeply upon audience participation, and therefore is not eternal and independent. He claims that this aspect of art makes it fundamentally unfair. He furthers this idea to say that the concept of expectation defining value extends to all parts of life, such as in financial gains.[^17]

He believes that all great art is necessarily hated by some due to the variety in human tastes. To make it so nobody would hate a work of art, one must remove everything that makes it special, therefore making it so that it is loved by nobody. Being hated is not proof that art is great, but not being hated is proof that it is not.[^18] He believes that art is about emotion, examination and going places people have never gone before to discover and investigate new things; it is from this definition of art, and his recognition of the subjective nature of aesthetic taste, that he argues that all great art is necessarily hated by somebody.[^18] When questioning himself on how many people need to love a work of art to outweigh the hate it inevitably inspires, and balance out the risk. Thus, if even one person loves a work, no matter how many people hate it, it is worthwhile.[^18]

He believes that stories don't live until they are imagined in someone's mind, and that everyone's experience of the story is individual and unique. He believes that the meaning of a story is up to the audience themselves to decide; that it is not meant to tell them how to think, but instead to give them questions to think upon.[^23] He thinks that the interpretations people have of stories are based upon what they themselves are looking for, as are the answers they find in them.[^23] His belief in how stories take on life in the minds of an audience is likely a reason for his feeling of obligation to tell the stories that he learns to whoever will listen.[^11]

He sees purpose as the spiritual conjunction that glues together human existence and volition. Thus, he sees it as a deeply important part of one's existence as a sapient being.[^56]

## Attributes and Abilities

Hoid does not yet have the [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 4|abilities]] of every planet he's visited.[^63] Some of his powers predate the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]], but none of them are gained through [[Hemalurgy - The Coppermind|Hemalurgy]].[^64] Hoid is concerned about opening himself up to the influence of Shards, so he only gains access to manifestations of Investiture through more conventional, but more difficult ways.[^65] Hoid has access to multiple Invested Arts, but not an incredibly large number.[^66] He is likely the most powerful non-Shard entity in the cosmere,[^67] although a few of the older dragons and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 17|aethers]] might rival him.[^68] He is considered to be one of the most dangerous and knowledgeable beings in the cosmere.[^69]

After [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] stole some of his memories, Hoid instituted protection protocols to defend himself if something or someone tried to interact with his soul.[^70] This causes his body to be frozen in time, while his mind remains unaffected.[^71] In this state, he is still able to make [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connections]] with others, and may even see and hear from those he is Connected with.[^72] The physical stasis was not what Hoid intended to happen and it will last until the person stops trying to interact with his soul.[^70]

There are only a few things in the cosmere that are capable of hurting him, one of which is likely [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 19|Nightblood]], and he stays far away from all of them.[^73] Hoid also works with a [[Seon - The Coppermind|seon]], [Olo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Olo "Olo").[^74] [^47] [^4] Hoid owns [sand](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sand_\(Taldain\) "Sand (Taldain)") from [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]],[^75] and a [[Tamukek - The Coppermind|tamukek]].[^76] [^4]

### Illusions

> “ *I see. You do not yet understand the nature of lies. I had that trouble myself, long ago. The Shards here are very strict. You will have to see the truth, child, before you can expand upon it. Just as a man should know the law before he breaks it.* ”
> 
> —Hoid on Lightweaving to Shallan [^36]

Hoid has access to an Invested Art known as [[Yolish Lightweaving - The Coppermind|Lightweaving]] that originates from Yolen prior to the Shattering of Adonalsium.[^77] Lightweaving allows for the creation of optical and auditory illusions.[^78] This Lightweaving is much less restrictive than the later [Rosharan variant](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Illumination "Illumination"),[^36] and does not require a spren or Stormlight to function.[^77] Hoid sometimes makes use of this ability in his disguises [^79] although he has several ways of changing how he looks [^80] including practical effects.[^81] He may also make use of it in some of his storytelling,[^82] such as the pictures of [Derethil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Derethil "Derethil") and the *[Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail "Wandersail")* that Kaladin sees in the smoke when Hoid is telling him a story.[^23]

Hoid's Yolish Lightweaving does not always seem to function perfectly on Roshar;[^83] in fact, he needs help from Shallan to fully craft an illusion.[^42] His power is somehow limited, and he has trouble making some aspects of it work, even after multiple attempts.[^83] It is uncertain why this occurs and whether or not this issue is unique to Roshar. Hoid's bonding of a Cryptic, of the name Design,[^41] provides abilities that supplement his own Lightweaving [^84] and allows him to overcome his issues crafting illusions, although further impacts or resonances between the two are unknown.[^85] [^41] Hoid's access to Yolish Lightweaving does allow him to guide another Lightweaver's power, although this is not something he would be able to do with just any Invested Art.[^83]

### Worldhopping

Hoid travels between various planets within the cosmere by making use of [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularities]],[^86] places usually created by the presence of a Shard on a planet where the sheer power of Investiture causes the borders between the realms to be weakened.[^87] A knowledgeable person such as Hoid is able to transition from the Physical Realm to the Cognitive Realm.[^86] Hoid can then travel to a different world's subastral, as the distances between worlds in the Cognitive Realm are much smaller than in the Physical Realm.[^88] Hoid is very proficient at traveling in the Cognitive Realm.[^89] He can easily travel to any world that has an active [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]], even those worlds where the perpendicularities are dangerous to pass through such as the [[Well of Ascension - The Coppermind|Well of Ascension]].[^37] Hoid is known to be aware of perpendicularities on Scadrial,[^37] Sel,[^90] and Roshar,[^35] and likely knows of Nalthis's perpendicularity as well.[^11] [^91] While Hoid typically makes use of natural perpendicularities in his travels, later in life he may also have access to an ability that essentially allows him to generate his own personal perpendicularity, much like the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surge]] of Transportation.[^92] Following the invention of space travel, he also makes use of spaceships to travel between planets.[^70]

### Dawnshard

Hoid held the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]], Exist, with an Intent diametrically opposed to the concepts of harm and violence for a long period of time.[^93] [^7] [^34] His time holding it caused changes to his spiritweb in a similar manner to [[Savant - The Coppermind|savantism]]. These changes meant that long-term, Hoid found himself unable to inflict harm on himself or any others,[^18] [^36] as well as unable to eat meat he believes was naturally produced (i.e., he could eat meat he believes was made in a lab or by magic).[^94] His Dawnshard is in some way connected to the [First Gem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/First_Gem "First Gem"),[^95] his eponymous topaz. There were other side effects of the Dawnshard. Hoid held it for so long that not only is he ageless,[^96] [^97] [^34] it has become essentially impossible for him to die.[^14] While other people who have held this Dawnshard have gained the ability to Skip and teleport from planet to planet, Hoid has not for most of his life had access to this ability, and it is uncertain if he ever gained it.[^98]

#### Time-Dilation

In addition to his agelessness, Hoid has not actually experienced all those years,[^99] instead making use of some time-dilation technique that may render him somehow unconscious or unaware.[^100] However, Hoid does not have the ability to travel backwards in time.[^101]

### Healing Factor

> “ *I'd be surprised if that little knife of yours poses me any real threat, Kholin.* ”
> 
> —Hoid to Jasnah on her Shardblade [^17]

Hoid has access to some sort of healing factor that is capable of repairing both the body and the [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|soul]].[^102] It will regenerate from the largest piece of flesh he has remaining.[^4] He is able to regenerate any damaged body parts at will, including his head. He can also heal from being stabbed [[Hemalurgy - The Coppermind|hemalurgically]].[^103] Hoid is able to repress his healing factor in some way, preventing instantaneous healing, an ability that he makes use of in some of his disguises.[^18] This healing factor makes it very difficult to kill Hoid,[^104] although he is still at least somewhat vulnerable.[^18] The name of the manifestation of Investiture responsible for Hoid's healing is unknown, but it is ancient,[^39] likely originating from some Pre-Shattering source. It is uncertain whether or not this healing factor is at all related to Hoid's apparent agelessness, which is known to originate from a remnant of a weapon built to kill Adonalsium.[^97] Hoid is unafraid of Shardblades for multiple reasons, one such reason is likely to be the effective healing factor that he possesses.[^105]

Hoid keeps excised portions of his body upon other planets as a contingency plan, in the circumstance that his body be destroyed to such an extent as to prevent healing, such as through it being entirely sublimed. One such instance is when he left a section of flesh behind on Scadrial before travelling to Roshar, so that he would be able to regenerate there if he were to be killed by one of the Shards on the planet. This came to pass upon his sublimation by [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]], and his subsequent regeneration upon Scadrial.[^4]

### Fortune

> “ *I have discovered a place that I must be, though to be honest I'm not exactly sure why I need to be there. This doesn't always work as well as I'd like it to.*”
> 
> —Hoid to Dalinar [^6]

Hoid has access to an ability that allows him to make use of the Spiritual attribute of [[Fortune - The Coppermind|Fortune]],[^12] which grants him a limited amount of foresight.[^106] Hoid's Fortune ability gives him an innate knowledge of where and when he needs to be,[^106] [^107] and whatever magic system he accesses Fortune through is his favorite.[^108] While this ability may grant him knowledge of what he is to do,[^12] [^109] usually Hoid does not know why his ability has brought him to a specific place.[^12] Hoid's Fortune ability is not infallible; occasionally it directs him to go somewhere, but either nothing notable is happening or he is unable to discover the reason why he has been led there.[^110] While he is good at figuring out where he needs to go, he's a lot worse at his ability to read the future. In fact, he tends to severely overestimate his ability to know what is going to happen next.[^111] Hoid's fortune ability has let him know about instant noodles,[^112] although he has had prior experience with them on Taldain.[^113]

### Connection

Hoid has access to some manifestation of Investiture that allows him to manipulate the Spiritual attribute of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]]. The full extent of this ability is unknown, but its primary known use is to help Hoid speak the various languages of the worlds of the cosmere that he visits. The ability allows him to Connect to whatever land he is in, temporarily altering his Spiritual aspect to believe that he was raised in that place, allowing him to speak as a native.[^114] While Hoid has learned naturally how to speak some foreign languages,[^115] and is decent with languages, he desires to pass as a local oftentimes, which requires something beyond natural learning.[^116] Generally he makes use of his Connection ability to communicate in foreign languages.[^115] This ability is not without fault though, as when Hoid speaks naturally it will fill in local idioms and phrases on its own, but if Hoid is careless and focuses on a specific word or phrase the ability may fail and he will say the word verbatim in his own tongue [^114] or it may be said in an odd way.[^117] Hoid is an expert in using his Connection ability, but even he sometimes makes mistakes. This is the reason he occasionally slips up and may say words that confuse others,[^118] such as "coin" [^17] and "bunny" [^20] on Roshar. As Hoid has demonstrated the ability to fluently speak foreign languages before the knowledge of unsealed metalminds became widespread,[^15] it is unlikely that he is making use of Feruchemical [[Duralumin - The Coppermind|duralumin]] to manipulate Connection.

#### Visions

Hoid, via unknown means, is able to enter and exit certain [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Visions "Visions"), as well as bring those he is bonded with, like Design.[^41] While doing so, he creates a large, pure white bubble of light, which can shelter those who have been brought into the vision naturally. While in the vision, Hoid is able to create whatever objects he wants. Hoid is only able to do this for roughly twenty minutes before being detected. Hoid is also able to hear, but not see, what is happening inside of visions near him without entering them.[^119] He can access the [Spirit Shores](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Spirit_Shores "Spirit Shores"), which exist in the Spiritual Realm, despite not being a dragon. This is likely due to the same ability.[^60]

Visions have difficulty replicating Hoid properly, as the vision replica of him knows it is an imitation.[^120] [^61]

### Metallic Arts

Hoid has also gained access to Allomancy,[^21] by burning a bead of lerasium stolen from the Well of Ascension.[^37] [^121] [^122] This grants him power equivalent to that of the ancient Allomancers.[^123] It is uncertain how practiced he is with these powers, but he does make use of Allomancy occasionally.[^43] [^36] [^75] He also has access to [[Atium - The Coppermind|atium]].[^124] It is unknown whether or not Hoid has access to [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemy]].[^125] However, he does have access to some unkeyed metalminds,[^126] which would not be useful to him without Feruchemical abilities or unsealed metalminds. The particular attributes that are stored in these metalminds are unknown. Hoid does not make use of Hemalurgy, to gain access to the Metallic Arts or any other manifestation of Investiture, as he is concerned about opening himself up to the influence of Shards.[^65] He does, however, own many Hemalurgic spikes.[^127] [^128]

### Awakening

> “ *Perfect pitch makes this all so much easier than it once was.* ”
> 
> —Hoid to Kaladin on making music [^20]

As a Yolish man, Hoid was not himself born with any [[BioChromatic Breath - The Coppermind|BioChromatic Breath]].[^129] However at some point Hoid has gained access to enough BioChromatic Breath to bring him to at least the Second Heightening.[^46] [^20] This means Hoid has at least two hundred Breaths, which grant him the ability to recognize unsupressed Breath auras and judge about how much breath another Awakener has, as well as perfect pitch.[^130] [^46] That is not to say that Hoid may not have reached a higher Heightening, just that the second Heightening is his minimum power level.[^131] Hoid's level of skill with [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]] is still unknown, as he has only made use of it on one known occasion, when he Awakened a doll, causing it to walk.[^18] This makes it difficult to ascertain his knowledge of Commands or complex Awakenings. Hoid's level of control over Investiture does allow him to suppress his Breath aura, making it difficult to be noticed by others. However, there are various methods of detecting Investiture that could circumvent this suppression.[^132] Hoid's perfect pitch does aid him in tuning instruments and making music.[^20] Additionally, holding Breath has allowed Hoid to outsource centuries of his memories to said Breath.[^133] After [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium's]] destruction of some of these Breaths in order to wipe his short-term memory, his level of Breath was reduced to just under the Second Heightening, resulting in a loss of perfect pitch.[^46]

### Surgebinder

> “ *It's either go with me now or wait it out and get captured. I honestly don't even know if you've the mind to listen. But if you do, know this: I will give you truths. And I know some juicy ones.* ”
> 
> —Hoid to his [Cryptic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic"), Design [^18]

Hoid has bonded the [Cryptic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic") [[Design - The Coppermind|Design]], which grants him access to Surgebinding.[^18] [^46] [^134] While the enhanced reflexes and healing permitted by Stormlight will likely be less useful to Hoid, the [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]] allows him to access the surges of [Illumination](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Illumination "Illumination") and [Transformation](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Transformation "Transformation").[^18] This allows Hoid to [Soulcast](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Soulcast "Soulcast"), transforming one type of matter to another by persuading its Cognitive aspect as well as Lightweaving, which grants him the ability to create visual and auditory illusions. This Rosharan Lightweaving seems to have a compounding effect with his Yolish Lightweaving, and allows him to circumvent the problems that he has experienced with his own illusions.[^41] Although Hoid's Ideals are unknown, he has admitted some truths.[^135] This may eventually grant him a [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]], and he has access to at least a [Shardblade](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") from their bond.[^136] [^4]

Prior to the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|contest of champions]], Hoid set up unspecified protections on Design which allowed them to keep their bond despite being on different planets. While at this distance, Hoid can still use Surgebinding, but cannot summon Design as a Shardblade.[^4]

Out of all of the orders of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], [[Order of Lightweavers - The Coppermind|Lightweaver]] was his first choice, as Lightweaving matches him very well and he's quite familiar and experienced with it. ### Storytelling Method

Hoid tells stories with smoke [^23] and sand.[^11]

#### Sand

On Nalthis, [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]] calls Hoid to tell the story of [[Hallandren - The Coppermind|Hallandren]] history to him and [Siri](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Siri "Siri"). As he told the story, he dropped sands, dusts, and small objects, mixing them together to produce various effects.

He began by trailing sand out of his hand, then he added a powdery black sand, when both trails mixed it became a white sand. He began to scatter fern leaves and then threw the handful up with blue and deep red dust making it seem like a minor explosion. He sprinkled silver glitter in front of him, then trailed powdery brown dirt. Black dust, and then [flower petals](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli"). He then sent out a spray of half a dozen colors from both hands. Then bright yellow dust from one hand and black from the other. Torn writing on paper, followed by metal bits, black charcoal dust from both hands, he crumbled clay, sprinkled golden dust around, bits of grass and earth, a white powder and then a spray of water that turned into mist.

#### Smoke

While telling stories with smoke he uses a flute, blowing the smoke, it may be [[Yolish Lightweaving - The Coppermind|Yolish Lightweaving]] that causes patterns and images to appear in smoke.

On Roshar, the chasms echoed the music allowing him to speak and play, without the music ever seeming to stop. It is unclear how the flute could be used without chasms or canyons.

## History

### Origins

> “ *Other men... other men, as they age, merely grow stranger. I fear that I am one of those. I am the bones of a foreign species left drying on the plain that was once, long ago, a sea. A curiosity, perhaps a reminder, that all has not always been as it is now.*”
> 
> —Hoid on his age [^44]

The individual who would later become known as Hoid was born on [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]] sometime prior to the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering of Adonalsium]].[^8] [^129] [^141] He was born naturally and his parents were married.[^142] [^37] From an early age he developed a fascination with the topic of whether life existed on other planets and often listened to local philosophers debate the topic.[^8]

During this period before the Shattering, Hoid learned [[Yolish Lightweaving - The Coppermind|Yolish Lightweaving]], which sometimes got him into trouble.[^77] [^143] Some of his oldest aliases, one of which likely came from a former master of his,[^144] also date back to this period; among these are Hoid, which he says he stole from someone he should have loved,[^23] and Cephandrius Maxtori.[^145] [^146]

Eventually, Hoid met the [dragon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragon_\(cosmere\) "Dragon (cosmere)") [[Frost - The Coppermind|Frost]], who is his elder by a small amount, though Hoid may have already started aging strangely by that time.[^147] [^148] [^149] Shortly thereafter, Hoid also met the people who would become the sixteen original Vessels.[^147] His early relationships with the future Vessels seem to have been amicable; he went out for drinks with [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast]] at least once,[^17] considered [[Ati - The Coppermind|Ati]] to be a kind and generous man,[^150] and became friends with [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]].[^151] He even dated the dragon [[Medelantorius - The Coppermind|Medelantorius]].[^152]

He had some connection to the king of a Yolish state. As part of a contest between members of high society on the capabilities of the lower classes, he was chosen to judge the creativity of a poem written by the son of a lumberman, named [Jerick](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jerick "Jerick").[^61] [^62]

#### The Shattering

At some point, for a variety of reasons, the future Vessels resolved to kill [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]];[^153] Hoid considered doing so to be somewhat necessary and was only loosely involved in their plan,[^154] [^155] though he was present for the actual Shattering.[^156] At the Shattering, he was offered one of the newly-created [[Shards]], which he refused;[^157] even millennia later, he regards this as having been a wise decision because it allows him a greater degree of freedom.[^9]

In his conversations with the new Vessels, he implied that his rejection of a Shard meant he would not interfere with their affairs,[^37] though given his interest in being able to do as he pleased it seems unlikely he actually intended to leave them alone. The Shattering appears to have been a turning point in his relationships with many of the Vessels, as he seems to look down on them for being so eager to Ascend,[^9] [^158] while [[Edgli - The Coppermind|Edgli]] at least looks down on him continuing to involve himself in the affairs of the cosmere after refusing a Shard;[^52] [^53] additionally, the Shattering may be the origin of his grudge against Rayse and [[Bavadin - The Coppermind|Bavadin]].[^159] [^160]

At some point early in the history of the cosmere, Hoid unwittingly held a [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshard]];[^34] [^61] this Dawnshard appears to have been associated with a piece of topaz.[^161] [^95] Hoid apparently attempted to do something more with the gemstone for a long time,[^144] but ultimately was forced to admit that it had become worthless because he wore it.[^23] His association with this gem earned him the name Topaz and the title Bearer of the First Gem.[^162] [^95] Holding a Dawnshard also permanently altered Hoid's [[Spiritweb - The Coppermind|spiritweb]]; among other things, these alterations make him unable to harm others or consume meat,[^34] allow him to heal from nearly any injury,[^39] [^73] and are likely the cause of his immortality.[^97]

Hoid's involvement in important events early in the history of the cosmere has had significant effects on his relationship to Investiture. The fact that he comes from before the Shattering, when magic worked much differently, makes Investiture sometimes act oddly with regard to him;[^129] the Shattering also appears to have changed magic such that his Yolish Lightweaving is no longer fully functional.[^163] Additionally, his connection to certain events involving the Dawnshards makes some uses of Investiture particularly dangerous for him.[^164]

He was present on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], then known as Alaswha, during its scouring and went with its inhabitants to Roshar.[^120]

He was acquainted with several of the beings that would come to be the Heralds, and they recognize him by the name Midius. He was the individual that taught them how they could use Investiture to enhance their memories.[^165] [^166] He was present at the founding of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]] and was asked to join in it; his refusal resulted in the selection of [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]] as a Herald.[^167] [^168]

At some point, Hoid danced with the ancient [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]]. He grew to be close with one singer known as [Vatwha](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vatwha "Vatwha").[^18] Hoid came to be worshipped by the [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki") as *Lunu'anaki*, the god of travel and mischief who cannot hurt man.[^35] Furthermore, he came to be known to the Iriali as the "Trickster Aspect." [^169]

Hoid founded the [[Worldsingers - The Coppermind|Worldsingers]], and has the rank of Master Worldsinger, and at some point in the early Sixth Epoch, he took [[Senne Khald - The Coppermind|Senne Khald]] as his apprentice, and assigned her with the task of writing a treatise on all the Radiant Orders.[^170] [^171] [^107]

He and Jasnah worked together in order to write up a contract for the contest of champions with Odium.[^9]

Some time after Taravangian took up Odium on, Hoid was visited by the new Vessel while he was telling stories at the Shattered Plains. #### Final Ten Days

The day Kaladin planned to leave for Shinovar, Hoid teased him about the flute he had lost while relocating from Sadeas’s camp to Dalinar’s. Kaladin asked Hoid if he wanted it back, but Hoid declined, encouraging him to try playing it instead. Kaladin attempts to play the story of the [Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail "Wandersail"), which Wit had performed for him back on the Shattered Plains.[^23] Hoid revealed that the story of the Wandersail was based on one of Roshar’s ancient rhythms, belonging to one of Roshar’s ancient gods. It was this rhythm that ancient humans used to guide them to Roshar when their planet was destroyed. Kaladin guessed that the [wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind") might be one of Roshar’s ancient gods, predating even the [[Shards]] on Roshar. Hoid encouraged Kaladin to listen to the wind, noting it might be weaker than it once was but was still worth heeding.

Kaladin admitted that the wind had been asking him for help, and Hoid urged him to follow its guidance. Kaladin asked Hoid for an encouraging story, but Hoid told him he already had everything he needed to prepare for what was ahead. Kaladin noticed a shift in Hoid’s tone, sensing an ominous note as if one of them might not survive what was coming. However, Hoid quickly lightens up, and says goodbye to Kaladin.[^215]

The following day, Hoid woke abruptly, sensing something wrong with his Breaths.[^75] He concluded that his memories stored in Breath were incomplete, and that Odium tricked him into thinking that he won when they met in the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]],[^46] something Rayse would never do. He planned to research it, but Jasnah pointed out that a different trick could arise if he turned another blind eye, so he decided to contact Frost and his sister for help.[^75] Later, Hoid showed up at the Coalition meeting and unveiled that there was a new Vessel for Odium who was digging deep into Alethi law to take over "seats of power", therefore taking over entire countries at a time.[^76]

When the Bondsmiths decided to take a test journey to the [[Spiritual Realm - The Coppermind|Spiritual Realm]], Hoid helped them set up a test run.[^119] When [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]] used anti-Stormlight to sever Dalinar and Navani's grounding in the Physical Realm, it causes the Bondsmiths, along with everyone in the vicinity, to begin to be forcefully sucked into the Spiritual Realm. Hoid managed to avoid this, noticing Lift at the last second and protecting her. However, he failed to notice [Gavinor](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Gavinor "Gavinor"), who is sucked in and becomes trapped in the Spiritual Realm along with Dalinar, Navani, [[Mraize - The Coppermind|Mraize]], [Iyatil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Iyatil "Iyatil"), Shallan, [[Renarin Kholin - The Coppermind|Renarin]], and [[Rlain - The Coppermind|Rlain]].[^216] In an effort to avoid panic in Urithiru at the sudden absence of the king and queen, Hoid lightweaved disguises of the Bondsmiths that he and Lift wore.

During Rysn's meeting with representatives of the Thaylen Patent Office, Hoid entered the room disguised as Dalinar. After everyone except for Hoid, [Nikli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nikli "Nikli"), [Alalhawithador](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alalhawithador "Alalhawithador"), [Chiri-Chiri](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chiri-Chiri "Chiri-Chiri"), and herself had left the room, Hoid dropped his lightweaving, and they discussed their powers. Hoid stopped the effect and left, promising that they would never meet again. Rysn quickly made a plan for how to go into hiding, and her Sleepless companions agreed to it.[^217]

### Bet with the Sorceress of Lumar

Hoid would later go on to visit the planet of [[Lumar - The Coppermind|Lumar]],[^249] [^2] a relative backwater in the cosmere.[^252]

At some point he informed his friend, the [[Kandra - The Coppermind|kandra]] [Ulaam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulaam "Ulaam"), of his intentions to visit the planet via letter.[^255] Still hoping to gain access to AonDor, he met with [[Riina - The Coppermind|Riina]], who was living on the planet in the guise of the Sorceress of the Midnight Sea.[^255] While visiting her island, Hoid agreed to a bet with her. Riina would be allowed to place him under a curse, and in exchange, if he could successfully break it, he would be granted admission to the Elantrians and the ability to use AonDor. The curse placed on him stripped him of his sense of humor, sense of style, sense of decorum, sense of purpose, intelligence, and sense of self.[^251] In order to break the curse, he would need to return to the central room of the Sorceress' ship on her island.[^19] As a part of the curse, he was also forbidden from telling anyone any information about his curse, how he had been given it, or how it could be broken. Riina finished her casting by sending Hoid far away from the Midnight Sea. During his time here, he likely came into possession of some Midnight spores.[^256]

> “ *I once ate an entire watermelon in one sitting. And it gave me diarrhea.*”
> 
> —Hoid to [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 16|Tress]] while under the effects of [[Riina - The Coppermind|Riina's]] curse [^5]

While under the effects of this curse, Hoid's behavior bore little resemblance to his normal personality. He dressed quite oddly, trying at times to wear every shade of orange he could find,[^257] or his red-sequined briefs.[^258] He spent time doing things like inventing the card game Kings, in which you hold your cards backwards and can see everyone's hand but your own, an invention that lost him many of his possessions. Bad jokes and nonsensical ideas filled basically all his time, and he was only very rarely able to think clearly enough to communicate anything meaningful or work to accomplish his goals. He was left with some dim awareness of his situation though, a cruel decision on the part of Riina who wanted him aware enough to be horrified by his situation. Any attempt to share information about his curse would only start him stuttering and send him back to his normal befuddled state.[^5]

Hoid spent a long time at sea, wandering without apparent progress at breaking his curse. Ulaam eventually arrived on the planet and found Hoid, but was uninterested in working too hard to free him from his curse, preferring to simply stick close and wait for the problem to resolve itself.[^5] The two of them spent at least a year traveling together in this way.[^252] Eventually Hoid would become the cabin boy of the *[Whistlebow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Whistlebow "Whistlebow")*.[^259] He came into contact with [[Charlie - The Coppermind|Charlie]], son of the duke of [Diggen's Point](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diggen%27s_Point "Diggen's Point") and returned there to give [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 16|Tress]] a ceramic cup and Charlie's first letter to her.[^249] Later, he was traded for by [Fort](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fort "Fort") for a large sum on the orders of Captain [Crow](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crow "Crow") who was interested in Hoid once she had heard that he had been cursed by the Sorceress.[^260] Hoid settled onto a routine onto the *[[Crow's Song - The Coppermind|Crow's Song]]*, working as a cabin boy there as well,[^251] and kept fed despite his near-uselessness by Fort.[^261] Ulaam continued as his companion, and the two were suffered by the crew despite their oddities, due in part to Ulaam's skill with medicine.[^262] During his time on the *Crow's Song*, Hoid gave midnight spores to [Weev](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Weev "Weev"), the ship's [sprouter](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Sprouter "Sprouter"), believing them to be licorice.[^256]

Another of his shipmates, Tress, became interested in Hoid's past after Ulaam explained to her that Hoid had been cursed,[^255] and approached him on the deck of the ship to try to obtain information about the Sorceress despite his curse.[^5] Hoid was unable to say much of note, but he dimly realized she was trying to help him and was able to offer some cryptic clues, asking Tress to bring him to her planet so he could save her,[^5] meaning the map of Lumar present on the Sorceress's ship.[^263] He also told her to look for a grouping of six stars,[^5] a carving under her bed, which would lead her to Weev's secret supply of midnight aether.[^5] After Tress recovered the midnight aether, Ulaam confronted Hoid, asking if he had given the aether to her. Ulaam and Tress continued to press Hoid for information and he was able to tell them to look for a person that is not a person and could talk when they should not, which they realized referred to Charlie in his guise of Huck the rat.[^256]

### The Dynamic

Hoid spent a number of years travelling the cosmere aboard the [Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dynamic_Storyteller%27s_Incredible_Conveyance "Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance"), a small spaceship. He was the navigator on the crew.[^270] The *Dynamic* was crewed by misfits he had found all across the cosmere. His apprentice, [Illistandrista](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Illistandrista "Illistandrista"), also called Starling, was among these misfits.[^271] Notably, Starling is the niece of Frost.[^272] Hoid took her on as an apprentice after she challenged draconic authorities, as her sense of justice impressed him.[^60] Hoid and Starling got into a lot of tight scrapes together, some of these separate from the crew of the *Dynamic*. At some point, he dropped her into a volcano, although she doesn’t blame him for it.[^273] He eventually left the *Dynamic*, as he believed Starling could lead the crew without him.

Shortly before the discovery of the [Drominad perpendicularity](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji%27s_Eye "Patji's Eye"), Hoid was imprisoned in a vault. Starling wondered if Hoid's wife would be able to help him escape the vault, but he said she could not remember him, and that 'the twins' would be more helpful. It is unclear who Hoid's wife is, but the twins are his children.[^60] [^274]

By this era, Hoid was one of the most famous people in the entire cosmere, and was an outlaw in [[Silverlight - The Coppermind|Silverlight]].[^275] He also owed significant debts to [[Xisisrefliel - The Coppermind|Xisisrefliel]].[^276]

## Relationships

Due to his nature as a worldhopper, Hoid has met most of the main characters in the cosmere, as well as a large quantity of side characters.

### The Shards

Hoid's relationships with the various [Vessels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel") are varied, to say the least, ranging from hate and terror to respect and camaraderie.

Ati

Hoid considers Ati to once have been a kind and generous man before his Ascension, though he thinks very differently of what he became.[^150]

Bavadin

Hoid has a long-standing grudge against [[Bavadin - The Coppermind|Bavadin]] for unknown reasons.[^159] [^160] She has instilled in many of her avatars, including the one on [[Obrodai - The Coppermind|Obrodai]], a deep dislike of him;[^197] however, he and Bavadin herself had a relationship at some point.[^220]

Edgli

Hoid and [[Edgli - The Coppermind|Edgli]] mutually detest each other. However, their conversations are far more polite than Hoid's relations with Bavadin or Rayse.[^219] [^53]

Koravellium Avast

Hoid, while not *enemies* with [[Koravellium Avast - The Coppermind|Koravellium]], has never really gotten along with her. However, he recognizes the fact that she poses a threat to Odium, and respects that.[^203]

Rayse

Hoid was once friends with [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]],[^46] [^151] though considered him to have always been a very loathsome, crafty, and dangerous individual.[^150] He is now terrified of the Shard, and takes many precautions against him. However, he is not above taunting the Shard, as he feels him to be the most detestable person he has ever met, albeit preferably from a position of superiority. The last time Hoid spoke to Rayse in person was more than a thousand years before the Vessel's death.[^46] [^150]

Sazed

[[Sazed - The Coppermind|Sazed]] has a benevolent feeling towards Hoid.[^222] Despite this, Hoid has hidden from the Shard whenever he visits [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]],[^188] a decision of which Sazed disapproves and questions [^277] but by which he is also fascinated.[^189] He wishes to work with Hoid to keep Odium contained, as he is highly disturbed by the wound upon the Spiritual Realm where Ambition was Splintered. They disagree on whether it is the Vessel or the power that is more dangerous, with Sazed believing that the Investiture will make any Vessel of Odium the same as their predecessor, but they agree that it would be the combination of a Vessel's craftiness and the power's Intent that would be most fearsome--coincidentally, a very good description of Taravangian.

Tanavast

Hoid was acquainted with [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast]] before the Shattering. Tanavast did like Hoid enough to buy him drinks once.[^17]

Taravangian

Prior to [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian's]] Ascension, Hoid had never notably interacted with him. However, after the occupation of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]], when going to challenge Rayse, he realized that a new Vessel had Ascended, and was completely terrified, as Rayse was controlled by the power, but a new Vessel might have more control. However, Taravangian destroyed the Breaths holding his short-term memory, so he did not believe anything to be amiss.[^46]

Medelantorius

[[Medelantorius - The Coppermind|Medelantorius]] appears rather fond of Hoid, and believes it has been too long since their last conversation as of Harmony's letter.[^278] The exact nature of their relationship, and his feelings concerning her, are currently unknown. However, they have dated at some point in the past.[^152]

### Cosmere-Wide

Khrissalla

Hoid thinks well of Khriss.[^279] Although it is unknown how much information she has shared with him, she knows far more about the cosmere than he, as she pursues the knowledge for the sake of scholarship, while he uses it to sound smart and achieve his goals.[^280] If he were to ask her about something that she knew, though, and she perceived that he was asking for a morally good reason, she would tell him.[^281]

The Seventeenth Shard

Hoid is quite dismissive of the [[Seventeenth Shard - The Coppermind|Seventeenth Shard]]. Although they fear that his actions will exacerbate Shardic conflict due to their non-interventionist policy, he easily evades those whom they assign to pursue him.[^25] [^282] [^283]

Frost

Hoid and [[Frost - The Coppermind|Frost]] know each other well, having worked together in the past, and have a method of communicating with one another besides the [Silverlight Mercantile](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Silverlight_Mercantile "Silverlight Mercantile") interplanetary postal service. Despite this, they are completely opposed on the courses of action that would be best for the cosmere. Frost, as a member of the Seventeenth Shard,[^283] believes that the best course of action is to let events play out as they will, especially in regard to [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium's]] growing power and ambition. However, as Hoid is a worldhopper and Frost is not, the former believes that he has a more informed stance, and plans to take more active action against the Shard.[^144] [^284] [^285]

Starling

[Starling](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Illistandrista "Illistandrista") is one of Hoid's apprentices. The two are quite close, and Starling looks up to him a lot.[^271] They are quite affectionate towards each other. Hoid taught Starling many skills related to speech and storytelling, as well as leadership.[^276] Her empathy and sense of justice impresses him, and he believes she has a lot of potential as captain of the *Dynamic*. Starling loves listening to Hoid's stories, especially those about Roshar.[^60]

Starling's uncle Frost did not completely approve of Starling's apprenticeship with Hoid, as he worried Hoid would put Starling in danger. Starling made the decision to go with Hoid anyway, in defiance of Frost.[^60]

The Ghostbloods

Hoid has a very different opinion of the Ghostbloods than he does of their leader, viewing them as generally not a threat.[^214] [^286]

Wan ShaiLu

It is unclear how Hoid feels about Wan ShaiLu, despite him betraying her without hesitation in order to get his hands on the Moon Scepter.[^287] [^288]

Wan ShaiLu, on the other hand, despises him for his betrayal.[^287] After escaping from the Rose Empire, she began to hunt after him to enact her revenge, an act that she eventually succeeded at.[^178] [^289] Their current relationship is unclear, but she does wonder to herself if she was keeping watch over him, or he over her, but she knows that they are in reality just keeping watch over the same third parties.[^178]

Nightblood

Hoid is terrified of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 19|Nightblood]] and tries to stay very far away from it, as he believes that it has a chance of killing him.[^73] [^290]

Riina

Hoid and [[Riina - The Coppermind|Riina]] hold a generally low opinion of one another. He views her as being easy to manipulate, and thus chooses her as the member of the [[Ire - The Coppermind|Ire]] to approach with the goal of becoming an [Elantrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elantrian "Elantrian"). Riina views Hoid with some wariness; she actively surveils him even while he is cursed, and she chooses to leave [[Lumar - The Coppermind|Lumar]] rather than risk fighting him.[^264] [^19]

Ulaam

> “ *Hoid has too many issues to count. I wouldn’t trouble yourself with his situation. He’s nearly as deft at untying knots as he is at creating them.* ”
> 
> —Ulaam to [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 16|Tress]] on Hoid [^255]

Hoid and [Ulaam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulaam "Ulaam") consider one another friends. The kandra enjoys insulting and humiliating him (albeit in a friendly way); Ulaam records what Hoid says during his curse from Riina and sends transcripts to their mutual friends.[^262] Before the kandra were released from Scadrial, Ulaam maintained a small cell culture of Hoid's cells. This meant that if Hoid's body was completely destroyed, he would regenerate from these cells.[^4]

### Roshar

In his position as King's, and later, Queen's Wit, Hoid has had opportunities to interact with many notable Rosharans, and has taken full advantage of such chances.

Design

Hoid has a fondly exasperated view towards [[Design - The Coppermind|Design]]. Though, as a pair who share a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]], they are extremely close, he is consistently frustrated by her habit of spoiling stories that he's telling, as he sees it.[^41] [^46]

Despite this amicable relationship, they are not the best for each other; both exacerbate certain features of one another in a way that isn't good for either of them.[^211]

Jasnah

Hoid was romantically involved with [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah]].[^212] He has immense respect for her, both due to her sheer intelligence and the fact that, when all else fails, she is, above all, paranoid. Jasnah notes that he seems to think such things as kissing her safehand would spark passion in her, though it is her view that mental stimulation is far superior.[^9] Before she left for Thaylen City, she ended their relationship by leaving him a letter,[^291] because she disliked how he kept secrets from her.[^75] Hoid agrees with and accepts this decision.[^292] [^293]

Kaladin

Hoid feels paternally to [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]]. In such situations as when Kaladin is terrified at the discovery of his Radiant powers,[^23] languishing in prison,[^20] or trapped in a vision concocted by Odium and [[Moash - The Coppermind|Moash]] in order to try and break him,[^41] Hoid is always there with a story to bring him back from the brink. At one point, he gave Kaladin his [Trailman's flute](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trailman%27s_flute "Trailman's flute").[^23] [^203]

Senne Khald

Hoid took [[Senne Khald - The Coppermind|Senne Khald]] as his apprentice in the Sixth Epoch. His exact relationship with her is unclear.[^171]

Shallan Davar

Hoid feels similarly to [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] as he does to Kaladin. Visiting her in times of need, such as when her father is growing more and more abusive or when she's sitting in a dark building in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"), racked with guilt about [Grund's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Grund "Grund") death, he guides her to a path of self-forgiveness.

Sigzil

Hoid's relationship with [[Sigzil - The Coppermind|Sigzil]] seems similar to that of Jasnah and Shallan. When in Azir, Hoid found Sigzil to be in some sort of trouble, and, after rescuing him, took the young [Azish](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azish "Azish") man under his wing and began teaching him as a [Worldsinger](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldsinger "Worldsinger").[^23] The two traveled [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]] together, at one point ending up in [Babatharnam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Babatharnam "Babatharnam"), where Hoid had to pay for Sigzil to be freed three separate times, though Sigzil suspected that they mainly arrested him because Hoid had a lot of money.[^35] Hoid graduated him as a full Worldsinger in.[^23]

Hoid feels shame for how he affected Sigzil by giving him the Dawnshard, and for abandoning him to slavery.[^8]

For a while, Sigzil believed that Hoid thought him dead, but was filled with rage upon learning that he knew he was alive all through his slavery at the Shattered Plains.[^8] He is further angered by the burden placed upon him by Hoid giving him the Dawnshard.[^8]

Torol Sadeas

Hoid dislikes Sadeas, insulting him at any chance he gets. Though at first he tries to get Sadeas to attempt to kill him in order to remove the man as a threat, he makes fun of Sadeas after his death just for the fun of it.[^294] [^44] <sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

### Scadrial

Kelsier

[[Kelsier - The Coppermind|Kelsier]] and Hoid mutually detest one another. Hoid considers him extremely dangerous, though he doesn't think much of his organization.[^214] [^286] He disapproves of how Kelsier interferes with Roshar through the Ghostbloods.[^47] He therefore works against them, supporting Shallan in her efforts against them and giving [Wax](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wax "Wax") an unsealed [coppermind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind "Coppermind") containing knowledge that Kelsier would have preferred to have stayed hidden.[^239]

Wax

Hoid thinks positively of Wax, acting as his coachman and helping him on multiple occasions, such as when he gives him a coppermind containing information on Kelsier,[^224] [^239] and saves him from the ocean after the destruction of the [Pewternaut](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Pewternaut "Pewternaut").[^245]

Wayne

Hoid has a rather cordial relationship with Wayne, although he finds him bizarre. He once tried to convince Wayne to stop being so hard on himself and accept that he is a good person.[^247] Wayne also likes Hoid and enjoys talking with him, though he doesn't know his true identity.[^236] [^237]

While relatively cordial, Hoid does seem to get annoyed by Wayne's kleptomania. Even though he wanted his harmonica back, he did ultimately accept his trade of it for a rat.[^247]

Joshin Yomen and Lady Mi'chelle Ostlin

Hoid is good friends with Joshin Yomen and Lady Mi'chelle Ostlin, with their wedding being a big part of why he returned to Scadrial, wanting to come to congratulate his friends.[^230] [^232] [^233]

### Lumar

Tress

Hoid holds significant respect for Tress. He sees her as very mature and talented for her age,[^138] and he remembers her adventure and tells her tale years to decades after they part.[^138] [^265] Although he felt that she didn't have the right to give him commands, he ultimately obliged due to her being his captain.[^19] He kept tabs on her and the rest of the crew of the *Crow's Song* for several years after his curse was broken.[^138]

Tress knew Hoid as a cabin boy on Diggen's Point, and a member of the *Crow's Song*. For much of their relationship, she held little respect for him due to the effects of the curse Riina put on him.[^255] [^260] She eventually came to see him as a means to an end for finding Charlie.[^255] Her opinion on him greatly changed after he saved her from Riina after his curse was broken, though she still viewed him as a member of her crew and thus under her command.[^19]

## Speculation

### Worldsingers & Worldbringers

The names of the [[Worldsingers - The Coppermind|Worldsingers]], a group of storytellers on Roshar, and the Conclave of [Worldbringers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldbringers "Worldbringers"), the leading body of the original [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 6|Terris]] religion, are strikingly similar. Brandon has stated that this is not a coincidence.[^300] Some believe that Hoid is a common root to both of these organizations, one of which, the Worldbringers, was very [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realmatically]] aware.[^301] In addition to this he is known to be a founder of the Worldsingers and a member of the Worldbringers.[^170] [^5]

## Trivia

- Hoid is one of the few characters who have spoken in small-caps.[^217]
- The character of Hoid existed before the concept of Adonalsium was developed. He first appeared in a short story Brandon wrote long ago, going by the name Kamp.[^302] He was on a planet trying to figure out how the magical system of the area worked.[^303]
- Brandon has stated that Hoid is one of his favorite characters, alongside [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]], by the virtue of being one of the oldest.[^304]
- Brandon may eventually write a parallel novel to the Mistborn series that chronicles what Hoid was up to behind the scenes.[^305]
- In 2018, Brandon did a reading of an uncanonical scene he had written several years prior, called "The Traveler." In it, two men, likely Hoid and Frost, have a conversation shortly after the deaths of [[Leras - The Coppermind|Leras]] and [[Ati - The Coppermind|Ati]]. A recording and a transcription of the reading can be found [here](https://wob.coppermind.net/entry/9518 "wob:9518").
- Hoid, under the name Topaz, was Brandon's first [Dungeons & Dragons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons "wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons") character.[^306]
- Brandon does not believe he would aid Hoid's cause if he were in the cosmere.[^307]
- Hoid fears the Beyond.[^308]
- He has a good opinion of [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss"), and they have met at least once.[^279]
- Hoid has spoken with the [[Nightwatcher - The Coppermind|Nightwatcher]].[^309]
- For several books, Hoid's best friend was somebody named Bat'Chor. This may no longer be the case as of Mistborn Era 2.[^310]
- Hoid has had a romantic interest in the past.[^311] In fact, there were several,[^312] and he has been married at least once.
- He is the happiest when "getting away with something". When asked in what published book that was, Brandon responded with *[The Bands of Mourning](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Bands_of_Mourning "The Bands of Mourning")*.[^51]
- Hoid's quest may be "To make that which once was." [^55]
- Hoid is unimpressed with the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]].[^286]
- Hoid loathes the [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set"), and the members who know of him loathe him in return.[^313]
- Though he once (extremely accurately) said to Kaladin that he began life as words on a page, this was not in fact a break of the fourth wall.[^314]
- He has dressed in drag before, "multiple times." [^315]
- Prior to the events of *Words of Radiance*, Hoid had not been hugged in "a long time." [^316]
- His favorite type of spren is a [Cryptic](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cryptic "Cryptic").[^317]
- Hoid does not pay taxes.[^318]
- Hoid's favorite color is black.[^319]
- Hoid loves bacon.[^320]
- Hoid likes milkshakes.[^321]
- Hoid is interested in having a nose that can't smell cheese.[^253]
- Hoid is a fan of ramen,[^322] and co-owned a restaurant called the [[Noodle Pupil - The Coppermind|Noodle Pupil]] on [[Komashi - The Coppermind|Komashi]] with [[Design - The Coppermind|Design]] [^71] before Design gave it away to [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 15|Yumi]] and [[Nikaro - The Coppermind|Nikaro]].[^70]
- Hoid says he has been to a planet full of pancake shaped people who speak using flatulence.[^323] Brandon has left it up to readers if he was joking or not.[^324]
