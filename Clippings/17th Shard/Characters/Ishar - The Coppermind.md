## Ishar

> “ *I… have seen Ishar. He curses me at night, even as he names himself a god. He seeks death. His own. Perhaps that of every man.* ”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] [^5]

**Ishar**, also known as **Ishi'Elin**, **Tashi**, **Tezim** and **Ishu-son-God**, is one of the ten [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") of the [[Honor - The Coppermind|Almighty]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], who served as a protector of humanity against the Desolations from 7000 to 4500 Rosharan years before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]],[^6] [^7] [^4] and as a leader of [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar") starting circa in the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] calendar under the guise of Tezim.[^5] He has been influencing the [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]] [Honorbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorbearer "Honorbearer") for generations.[^8] He serves as a major religious figure in many world religions.[^5] [^9] He's known as the Herald of Luck, and serves as the patron of the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Order of Bondsmiths]].

He is the current bearer of his own [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]].[^4]

## Appearance and Personality

> —Ishar, writing as Tezim [^10]

### Appearance

Ishar is a strong-looking man with the look of a warrior, outfitted in robes and wearing an ardent's beard and bald head.[^4] [^11] Rosharan artwork typically presents him as looking much older than other Heralds, with a full beard and grey hair.[^12] [^13] Modern Rosharans would consider him to look [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin]], suggesting he's pale-skinned and lacks the epicanthic folds common to most people on the planet.[^14] [^15] [^4]

### Personality

He is known to be incredibly charismatic, capable of swaying entire nations to follow him, as well as to convince the other Heralds of his opinions and his sanity.[^9] [^5] He also seems to be scholarly, and deeply interested in the inner working of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]] and the mechanics surrounding it.[^6] In addition, Ishar is an excellent duelist, easily defeating five Windrunners of the Third Ideal with minimal use of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]]. The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] notes that among the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Heralds "Heralds") he was only average in skill, though he was not a warrior at the beginning of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].[^4]

In the present day Ishar, like other Heralds, is insane. His particular brand of madness seems to reflect his nature as a spiritual leader. He also bore part of the pain of the other Heralds and was partly corrupted by the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control").[^16] [^17] He alternately believes himself to be above all other men, and hates who he is and wishes to die.[^5] [^10] The latter does not stop him from presenting himself as the ultimate authority and demanding utter respect as the god-priest of [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar").[^10] Ishar tells Dalinar that his insanity could be temporarily cured by a Radiant swearing an Ideal near him.[^4]

This megalomania and charisma together make for a highly dangerous combination, as even now the other Heralds hold Ishar in particularly high esteem. [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] claims that Ishar has always been the wisest of them all, and [[Shalash - The Coppermind|Shalash]] believes that he would know how to cure [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln's]] madness.[^18] Additionally, Shalash presents him as a confident, eager man; energetic, and more of a battlefield commander than a wise scholar. He is bold rather than crafty, a man who pulled others after him on seemingly crazed ideas that ended up working.[^4]

He wishes to combine together the Shards and become [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]] reborn.[^4] He sees himself as an aspect of the Almighty.[^19] [^4]

## Attributes and Abilities

### Herald of the Almighty

As a [Herald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald"), Ishar is immortal. When he dies, he travels to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] and can return to Roshar, though at the cost of triggering a [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]].[^5] He wields an [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]], having retrieved it from the Shin after abandoning it at [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Aharietiam]].[^4] It grants him [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] of the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmiths]], namely the [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surges]] of [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Adhesion]] and [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Tension]].

Ishar has mastered his fighting skills throughout the ages, and is capable of fighting multiple Shardbearers with ease.[^4] He is capable of summoning Honor's perpendicularity, draining Stormlight from Radiants by bonding them to the ground and even stealing away a Radiant bond. The Stormfather implies that without Honor to restrict him, the extent of his abilities is unknown.[^4]

### Binder of Gods

> “ *But take care; Ishar's skill as a duelist is a lesser danger. He has recovered his Honorblade. He is a Bondsmith unchained.* ”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] to Dalinar [^4]

Ishar possessed powers even before he became known as a Herald, and like most of the other future Heralds had access to the Surges.[^20] [^21] [^22] His particular powers are seemingly tied to the [[17th Shard|cosmere]] -wide phenomenon of [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] and at one point had access to the powers akin to Elsecalling.[^23] [^20] He is often described as the founder of the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]], and is believed to have been the founder of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], forcing them to organize and adopt precepts to live by, to prevent another [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] -like catastrophe.[^24] [^5] Even among the other Heralds, the Radiants were sometimes known as "Ishar's Knights".[^25]

With the help of [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], Ishar would develop the idea of the Oathpact and would be the one to Connect each Herald to Honor and would be the last to join the bond. He also had the power to add people to the Oathpact, as seen when he makes [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] a Herald.[^26] [^27]

Perhaps due to his abilities, he appears to have been the Heralds' expert on Connection and bonds. He studied the Oathpact, and was the one who realized that one Herald was enough to keep it working.[^6] He also sought a way to preserve information between the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], and in the present day, convinced Nale to hunt down budding [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]], claiming it would prevent a new invasion.[^28] [^9]

When he was visited by Dalinar after the end of the Emuli campaign, in his duel against five Windrunners, he used his powers to Connect them to the ground, causing their Stormlight to drain away. He also demonstrated the ability to steal Dalinar's bond with the Stormfather, against both Dalinar's and the Stormfather's wills, as well as take Dalinar's Connection as Odium's opponent in the contest of champions, although neither taking was completed.[^4]

### Well of Control

Ishar gained some of the powers of Odium after absorbing a fraction of the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control").[^17] This allows him to Connect to [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] and become a god to the people there.[^2] Ishar is able to communicate to those Connected to Shinovar telepathically, and can even read their minds after a certain amount of time.[^29] [^30] He can also heal those he has Connected with.[^29] Ishar does not need to stay in Shinovar to stay Connected to those in Shinovar.[^31] If a person rejects him, he loses the ability to speak with them telepathically and to see what they are doing.[^32]

The powers partly corrupted Ishar,[^16] [^17] and a darkness could be felt around him.[^31] This eventually spread to Shinovar, causing the spren to leave.[^33] It also infected the other Heralds via their Connection to Ishar and the Oathpact.[^34]

#### Human Fused

Using the powers from the Well, Ishar was able to create his own version of [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]].[^35] [^3] [^36] Like regular Fused, only willing participants can be used.[^37] [^35] When they die, their bodies turn to a black smoke and disappear, and can be reborn.[^38] To be reborn, human Fused take over a body. Without a storm, the process takes days and is far more painful.[^35] They can be permanently killed by [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]].[^39]

Ishar has some degree of control over the human Fused, and can even control their movements.[^40] Ishar can choose to kill his Fused whenever he wishes.[^41] Human Fused will react to Ishar's emotions, and act as if they are experiencing them.[^39]

### God-Priest Tezim

> “ *Tezim the Great, last and first man, Herald of Heralds and bearer of the Oathpact. His grandness, immortality, and power be praised.* ”
> 
> —Ishar's letter to [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] [^10]

In the current era, Ishar acts as the leader of [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar"), calling himself the god-priest Tezim. The Tukari are zealously religious, and indeed seem to believe him to be a god.[^10] For the past six years, they've been at war with the neighboring [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]] over the port city of [[Sesemalex Dar - The Coppermind|Sesemalex Dar]], in the conflict known as [[Eighty's War - The Coppermind|Eighty's War]].[^42] Ishar's reasons for leading the Tukari on Sesemalex Dar are unclear, though the two countries have always been embroiled in conflict.[^43] It's also uncertain how long Ishar's been influencing Tukari politics, though his official rise to power occurred somewhere around the start of Eighty's War.[^44]

As Tezim, Ishar refuses to participate in [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar's]] alliance and demands that the [Alethi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethi "Alethi") turn [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] over to him.[^10] Whether he has the power to back up his claim remains to be seen.

## History

### Life on Ashyn and the Expulsion

Like most of the Heralds, Ishar was born on [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]] prior to mankind's arrival on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], over seven-thousand Rosharan years before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^7] [^45] He was either a king, scholar, or general.[^46]

Ishar was the first to be tricked by Odium into experimenting with the Surges,[^47] [^4] that eventually left Ashyn uninhabitable and desolate outside of a few hospitable areas, and forced the population to flee to Roshar,[^2] [^22] [^4] and forcing those that remained on Ashyn into floating cities.[^48] [^49] He was also the first human on Ashyn to discover how to [[17th Shard|worldhop]], enabling humanity to leave the planet when they needed to flee.[^50] [^51] [^22] He used these abilities to open a portal to Roshar, following the tones of the planet.[^23] [^51]

### Herald of the Almighty

> “ *Before he was Herald of Luck, they called him Binder of Gods.* ”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] [^5]

He, along with the rest of humanity, were granted modern-day [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] as territory by the native [[Singer - The Coppermind|singer]] population. When relations with the singers broke down, due to humanity trying to push eastward for more fertile land, and the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]] started to appear, Ishar, along with nine other people, was raised to a Herald by [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], creating the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]].[^46] [^20] Ishar's part in the Oathpact was larger than that of the other Heralds, as he worked with Honor to design it and he would be the one to connect each Herald to Honor using his powers over [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]].[^5] [^52] [^27]

The Oathpact demanded that to stop the [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]], the Heralds had to travel to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], the planet that collected souls, after each one ended. This led to them being tortured until at least one broke, sending them all -- and a Desolation -- back to Roshar.[^53] [^46] It was during one of those cycles that [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] began appearing. Ishar was instrumental in transforming this scattered group into the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], although what precisely he used, other than threats of death and destruction upon those who wouldn't obey, is unknown.[^24] During that time, he also sought a way to preserve information between the Desolations.[^28]

> “ *We defeat them each time, we drive them back to Braize. Then we follow… we follow….*”
> 
> —Ishar to Honor [^54]

After around two thousand five hundred years, however, the Heralds had had enough of their endless torture and Ishar was worried that they would lose their nerve before the enemy did. When Ishar voiced these concerns to Honor, the Shard realized what immortality was doing to them and let them chose if they wanted to abandon the fight.[^4] [^54] After this Desolation in which only [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Taln]] died in battle, Ishar suggested that one person may be enough to keep the following Desolations at bay. And so, the nine agreed to abandon the Oathpact, leaving Taln to suffer alone for over four and a half thousand years.[^55] [^54] This Desolation came to be known as the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], or Aharietiam.

### The Era of Solitude

> “ *Tashi doesn't care much for what you do here. In fact, I'd pray that he doesn't reach your city, as I doubt you'd like the consequences.* ”
> 
> — [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] [^56]

Ishar's activities after the Aharietiam are largely unknown. Some three thousand years before the True Desolation, Ishar met with an elderly [Derethil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Derethil "Derethil") and wrote down the story of the [Wandersail](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wandersail "Wandersail") and tried to find the islands mentioned by Derethil.[^57] Some thousand years before the Desolation, Ishar took up the power of the [Well of Control](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Well_of_Control "Well of Control"), which affected both Ishar’s sanity and the other Heralds. Using the Well of Control, he Connected to the land of Shinovar.[^17] [^35]

At some point, he met with Nale and convinced him to hunt down and destroy all nascent [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]], believing that the return of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] would unleash a new Desolation.[^58] [^59] He would begin to command the [Honorbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Honorbearers "Honorbearers") of Shinovar hoping to find replacement Heralds, though only [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]] would be acceptable to him.[^8] [^60] He would try to make an army of [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] brought to the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] and would make the most of the Honorbearers into Fused.[^35] This caused the spren to leave Shinovar [^33] During this time, he also insinuated himself into the politics of [Tukar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tukar "Tukar") and became its leader, the god-priest, under the assumed identity of Tezim.[^5] As Tezim, he waged [[Eighty's War - The Coppermind|Eighty's War]] against [[Emul - The Coppermind|Emul]].[^42]

### Lead-up to The True Desolation

After the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]] began near the end of, [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] contacted Tukar around the middle of, hoping for it to join the fledgling alliance. Ishar refused, and instead demanded ownership of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]] and fealty from the newly refounded [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]]. He was denied.[^10] Shortly after, [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] visited him again, hoping to gain more understanding as to why the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] still happened, despite his efforts. What they talked about is unknown, but Nale later decided to abandon mankind and side with the singers.[^18]

#### Campaign in Emul

During the [coalition of monarchs'](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs") campaign in Emul in, Dalinar had a crew of [[Order of Windrunners - The Coppermind|Windrunners]] fly himself, [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]], and [[Nightblood - The Coppermind|Nightblood]] to Ishar's camp in an attempt to learn more about his own [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmith]] abilities. While still in the air, Dalinar noted that he already had a [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to the Herald, allowing him to identify him on sight. Ishar accused Dalinar of being [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium's]] champion, and refused his pleas for aid, though he did acknowledge that his "lies" were reasonable.[^4]

He summoned his own [[Honorblade - The Coppermind|Honorblade]], which he claimed to have received from Szeth's father before he thanked Ishar for letting him die. Ishar attacked Dalinar, easily defeating the five Windrunners. As Dalinar opened a [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]], Ishar approached him and created a Connection with Dalinar, meaning to steal his bond with the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] as well as Dalinar's Connection to Odium, in which he sensed Odium's and Dalinar's opposition to each other.[^4] At the last moment, Szeth used Nightblood to sever Ishar's new Connection and then collapse the perpendicularity, which Ishar had been holding open against Dalinar's will. Nightblood was then able to chip a piece from Ishar's Honorblade. Shocked by the damage to his Blade, Ishar opened his own perpendicularity, which the Stormfather was unable to sense, and fled into [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar") with his men. Once Ishar was gone, Dalinar heard the Herald's voice through the perpendicularity. Sounding much more sane, he informed Dalinar that he could be restored temporarily if a Radiant swore an oath near him. Ishar claimed to know a way of resetting the Oathpact, and asked Dalinar to meet him in Shinovar, before reverting to madness.[^4]

Ishar had been experimenting on Radiant spren, somehow bringing their cognitive forms into the physical realm and measuring how long they were able to survive—usually mere seconds—and then performing autopsies. The Stormfather was extremely distraught at this revelation and called it an abomination.[^4]

#### The Cleansing of Shinovar

He first appeared to Szeth and [[Kaladin - The Coppermind|Kaladin]] on their first night in Shinovar and told them to go on a pilgrimage to the various monasteries across Shinovar.[^11]

Ishar next encountered Szeth and Kaladin, who were traveling with [[Nale - The Coppermind|Nale]] on the day of the [[Contest of champions - The Coppermind|Contest of champions]], at the [Bondsmith monastery](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bondsmith_monastery "Bondsmith monastery").[^2] He had Szeth fight all of the Fused Honorbearers all at once.[^33] During the fight, he ignored Kaladin who was trying to help Ishar overcome his insanity, and stop his plans with Szeth.[^39] Szeth would swear the Fifth Ideal near him, hoping for him to gain his sanity back due to the release of power on swearing,[^2] but it did not work as Ishar had placed protections against being healed by sworn Ideals.

He made Kaladin feel the darkness of the Heralds to explain what he had been enduring for the others.[^40] [^61] [^62] [^63] Kaladin managed to convince Ishar that he would feel better eventually and swore the Fifth Ideal of the Windrunners unexpectedly to Ishar, forcing the power of Odium out of Ishar.[^63] [^34]

Ishar remade the Oathpact in order to protect the spren from [[Retribution - The Coppermind|Retribution]] at the request of the [Wind](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wind "Wind") who had been pushing towards this.[^26] The Wind suggested that they create a place for their minds to go separate to their bodies, like the visions from the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]]. Kaladin choose to become a Herald instead of Szeth to facilitate it with Jezrien no longer being around to fulfil his position.[^26] Ishar used his abilities to send the Heralds, including himself and Kaladin, to Braize.[^64]

When Szeth awoke, he believed that Ishar had killed Kaladin, as Kaladin's body was left behind.[^65] Ishar, like the other Heralds, ended up in a vision where their minds were preserved while their souls were tortured on Braize.[^66]

## Relationships

### Nale

Ishar is greatly respected by Nale, who believes him to be the only one who hasn't succumbed to madness. It was likely at Ishar's suggestion that Nale began his crusade against [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]], and when the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] comes, Nale goes to Ishar for comfort and advice.[^9] Ishar believes that Nale is weak.[^33]

### The Stormfather

Ishar has a negative view of the Stormfather, cursing him at night.[^5]

### Szeth

Ishar spent decades grooming Szeth to become a Herald by talking to him since his youth.[^33]

## Culture

![[Ishar---The-Coppermind-5.webp]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Isaac_Stewart "Isaac Stewart")

Ishar's chapter capstone

As one of the Heralds, Ishar is venerated throughout [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]]. In the [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorin]] tradition, he is known as **Ishi'Elin**, and associated with number 10 (Ishi), the [Essence](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ten_Essences "Ten Essences") of Sinew, and the divine attributes of Pious and Guiding.[^67] He is often listed alongside [[Jezrien - The Coppermind|Jezrien]] and [[Kalak - The Coppermind|Kelek]] as one of the most important Heralds,[^68] and is even thought to be the leader of the Heralds in the west of the continent.[^69]

"Ishi be praised" is a common phrase.[^70] However, unlike with most Heralds, Ishar's true name remained in use among the Vorins, with places like [Ishar's Field](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ishar%27s_Field "Ishar's Field"), and the curse of "Ishar's soul".[^71] [^72] Names such as [Ishal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ishal "Ishal"), [Ishnah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ishnah "Ishnah") and [Ishikk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ishikk "Ishikk") are likely derived from his.

Unlike in the East, where Jezrien is placed as the most important, in the West Ishar is often considered the leader of the Heralds.[^56] In [Makabaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Makabak "Makabak") nations, he's called **Tashi**, and is titled the God of Gods and the Binder of the World.[^73] The people of [Tashikk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tashikk "Tashikk") in particular -- which itself may be named after Tashi -- place him as equal, though opposed to [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], or Nun Raylisi, and believe that as long as they stay in their homeland, Tashi protects them from evil.[^44] In [[Yeddaw - The Coppermind|Yeddaw]], the tenth of the traditional ten pancakes of the [[Weeping - The Coppermind|Weeping]] is dedicated to Tashi, and is imagined rather than actually made.[^74] [Tashi's Light Orphanage](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tashi%27s_Light_Orphanage "Tashi's Light Orphanage") is named after him, and "Tashi above" is a commonly-used phrase.[^44] [^75]

Further west, in [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]], he's known as **Ishu-son-God**.[^9]
