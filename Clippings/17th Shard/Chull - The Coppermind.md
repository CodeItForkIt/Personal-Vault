## Chull

The **chull** is a species of domesticated Rosharan animal. There are differing breeds of chulls with a vast array of phenotypes. They are found all across [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]].[^1] They have been domesticated for over seven thousand years, being herded by [[Singer - The Coppermind|Singers]] before the arrival of humanity on Roshar.[^2]

## Appearance and Anatomy

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Chull---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Isaac Stewart](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Isaac_Stewart "Coppermind:Artists/Isaac Stewart")

A depiction of the five stages of life for a chull: Larva (chull cremling), first Pupation, Adult, second Pupation, and Senescence.

Mature chulls are massive hexapodal crustaceans with thick legs with large claws and a stone-like shell. Their carapaces are typically red.[^3] Chulls have whip-like antennae that grow in pace with their shell as they age, so that the chull can sense out spaces to determine if they're large enough to pass through,[^4] and beady fist-size black eyes.[^5]

They are known to excrete undigested matter as feces, colloquially referred to as "chull dung." [^6] [^7] [^8] Their mouth opens horizontally, and is strong enough to crush rockbuds and other such plants.[^1] [^3] Their two large claws are strong enough to break bones.[^9]

### Shell

Chull shells are a light, porous stone created from minerals the chull consumes in its food and water and then excretes through channels in the back and legs, particularly rock and [crem](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Crem "Crem").[^10] [^11] Since the shell is not alive, and contains no known nerves, chulls are not hurt when their shells are cracked, shaped, or smoothed.[^12] [^11] These shells are hard, enough so to deflect arrows, thus they provide the chull with efficient protection.[^13]

Their shell naturally grows in clusters of Fibonacci spirals, comparable to [Romanesco broccoli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanesco_broccoli "wikipedia:Romanesco broccoli"). This creates spaces for various plants to take root and cremlings to find homes, allowing for the creation of complex symbiotic ecosystems to form on the backs of wild chulls.[^1] [^14] The smooth shells of domestic chulls are due to domestic care, with owners sanding down and shaping the shell for whatever purpose they are needed for, be it practical or decorative.[^14]

The shell of a Chull is not only used for protection, but also as a means of camouflage.[^6]

Chulls pull their legs and head into their shell when they sleep, and as a result sleeping chulls look like large boulders or small hills.[^6]

The [Reshi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi "Reshi"), and likely others, paint their chulls' shells. One can identify what island a Reshi chull handler is from by the colors and patterns on their chulls' shells.[^5]

### Reproduction

They reproduce in a similar manner to [[Chasmfiend - The Coppermind|chasmfiends]],[^15] and are known to, in some manner, produce eggs.[^16] They are known to breed with other chulls, therefore they engage in sexual reproduction, with the breeding cycle on the southern slopes of the [[Horneater Peaks - The Coppermind|Horneater Peaks]] being considered especially important.[^17] Outside of chull reproduction in the natural world, humans are known to breed chulls in batches for their use as beasts of burden.[^18]

### Life Cycle

Chulls begin life as an egg,[^16] which eventually hatches into larvae known as a **chull cremling** before eventually undergoing a process known as the **First Pupation**, in which they surround themselves with a shell and metamorphize into an adult chull. First Pupation can be encouraged by feeding the chull cremling a consistent diet of [rocklily](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rocklily "Rocklily") leaves.[^19] The chull loses its memory of its larval state after its metamorphosis, and training must resume as if with a new chull. Their behaviour can also potentially change after pupation.[^19]

Their **Second Pupation** has some correlation with [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorms]], and one can help prevent it by keeping a chull sheltered during highstorms. Second Pupation can also be delayed by feeding a chull crushed [shelltick](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shelltick "Shelltick") and mixing [shalebark](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shalebark "Shalebark") oil into their water.[^19]

## Behavior

Chulls are generally docile creatures. While known to happen, it is very unusual for Chulls to behave aggressively.[^3] They tend to move with a slow, lumbering gait and are generally slower than humans.[^20] [^21] Chulls are considered slow to react.[^22]

The behavior of an individual chull can change dramatically following First Pupation.[^19]

They sometimes clack their mandibles and look about their surroundings, they also often twitch their antennae. What these behaviors mean is as of yet unknown.[^5] They are also known to engage in behavior referred to as fidgeting, this is observed when they are stopped after pulling a wagon for a prolonged period.[^3]

In many cases, they are considered to be incredibly stubborn.[^23] [^9]

### Diet

Chulls are omnivorous creatures with a varied diet. They eat everything from [rockbuds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rockbud "Rockbud") to slugs to smaller crustaceans.[^3] [^11] [^14] [^24] [^25] Domestic chulls are often fed lavis grain;[^26] chulls are also often fed rocklily leaves, or crushed shelltick and shalebark oil mixed into their water, which are all used in order to prevent metamorphosis at various stages of the chull lifecycle.[^19]

### Grooming

Chulls are known to groom one another, removing various rockbuds that grow upon their shell.[^14]

### Herding

Chulls are often herded in large groups by their owners.[^27]

### Intelligence

Chulls are considered less intelligent than horses,[^28] they are able to memorize various rhythms and associate them with commands and actions, allowing them to be effective beasts of burden.[^29] However, this requires training.[^19] [^30] Comparing someone's intelligence to a chull is sometimes used as an insult.[^31] [^32]

### Pack Behavior

Wild chulls are known to gather together in packs, indicating that they are social animals.[^33] [^34] They are known to groom each other, likely other members of their pack.[^14]

### Predation

They are known to be prey to chasmfiends, these larges creatures are able to crush a chull's shell in their strong jaws, making for easy consumption.[^35] [^36]

### Sleep

Chulls appear to be diurnal creatures, sleeping during the night and spending the day awake.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> When they sleep, they pull their legs into grooves the shell, disguising themselves as boulders in order to protect themselves while they sleep.[^6] [^4]

### Vocalizations

They are capable of making a wide range of noises, including a gravelly bleating,[^37] [^35] [^38] trumpeting,[^39] and snorting.[^40]

## Training

In order for chulls to learn to how follow human orders, and thus serve as beasts of burden, they must be trained by a professional trainer. Chulls must be trained twice, once as a chull cremling, and once as a mature chull following the memory loss after First Pupation.[^19] [^30]

There are several different methods of training a chull.

- In the rural areas of [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"), chulls are harnessed and controlled with complex leather bridles.[^1]
- In the coastal region around [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]], chull drivers often sit directly on the chull's shell and direct them by tapping on their antennae with long poles.[^1]
- Some chulls are trained to respond to different rhythms tapped out on their shells with a guiding reed. These reeds are often as long as a person is tall and allow the driver to drive from the wagon or cart rather than riding on the chull itself.[^29]

## Use

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Chull---The-Coppermind-2.png]]

by [creativityinajar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/creativityinajar "Coppermind:Artists/creativityinajar")

Chull are highly valued as work animals and are considered to be worth more than the average slave.[^41] Some people maintain chull herds and rent their labor out on a contract basis.[^42] They are slow but strong and are used to pull everything up to and including the large complex bridges [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar]] uses for chasm crossings.[^42] Since their shells can be carved without harming the animal, some people sand flat places on top of a chull to ride and many carts are hooked to fittings drilled directly into their shells. Shelves and racks can also be carved into or attached directly to chull shells.[^1]

Since it is lightweight and durable, chull shell is used as a crafting material. [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan]] had a [havah](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Havah "Havah") with chull-shell buttons.[^43]

Chull meat is edible. It can be dried and stored as jerky.[^33] In some cultures, including [[Herdaz - The Coppermind|Herdazian]] and [Unkalaki](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Unkalaki "Unkalaki"), chull head is considered a delicacy.[^44]

During the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]], [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] used them to pull his bridges, rather than put the lives of [bridgemen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bridgemen "Bridgemen") at risk.[^42]
