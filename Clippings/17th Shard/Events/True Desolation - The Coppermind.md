## True Desolation

> “ *I have seen the end, and have heard it named. The Night of Sorrows, the True Desolation. The Everstorm.* ”
> 
> —Collected on, 15 seconds pre-death. Subject was a darkeyed youth of unknown origin.[^1]

The **True Desolation** is a global conflict on Roshar between humans and [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]]. Nearly all of the world's countries participated in some capacity. [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and his servants wage war against the rest of Roshar, hoping to eventually allow the [[Shards|Shard]] to break free of the ties bonding him to the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system").[^2] The term " [Night of Sorrows](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Night_of_Sorrows "Night of Sorrows") " is often used in conjunction with the True Desolation, but after [Stormfall](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormfall "Stormfall") it becomes clear that it is one aspect of the True Desolation rather than a synonym.[^3]

The causes of the True Desolation are complex and rooted in events that occurred many millennia before the conflict. Such as long standing racial and ethnic tensions between humans and the Fused rooted in the human colonization of Roshar, and the sealing away of Odium in the Rosharan system to halt his crusade against the [[Shards|Shards of Adonalsium]].<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

## Lead-up

The causes of the True Desolation can be traced back to Odium's crusade across the cosmere, attempting to kill every Vessel he could in order to become the sole god of the cosmere. Eventually, in his attempt to kill Honor and Cultivation, he settled on Ashyn, wherein he convinced humanity to experiment with the Dawnshard and Surges, leading to the destruction of the planet. Due to the destruction of Ashyn, mankind fled to Roshar, where they were accepted by the native Singers and given the land that would come to be [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]] as their territory. Within a generation, mankind became dissatisfied with what territory they were given, and violently expanded and colonized Roshar, bringing about the First Desolation.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup>

At some point during the First Desolation, Odium sided with the Singers as Honor and Cultivation came to side with humanity. Furthermore, Odium was sealed away in the Rosharan System by Honor and Cultivation.[^4] [^5] Honor also chose the Heralds and granted them great strength, while Odium turned a large population of Singers into the Fused. Eventually, humanity won and sealed the Fused away on [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]], along with themselves as part of the spell sealing them upon the planet.<sup><i><a href="https://coppermind.net/wiki/Help:Cite" title="Help:Cite">[citation needed]</a></i></sup> Whenever a Herald broke from the torture, the Fused would rush back to Roshar, starting another Desolation. This happened several times over the seven-thousand year history of humanity's existence on Roshar.

After the [[Last Desolation - The Coppermind|Last Desolation]], nine of the ten Heralds could no longer bear to return to Braize to be tortured by the [[Fused - The Coppermind|Fused]], and instead gave up the [[Oathpact - The Coppermind|Oathpact]]. One Herald, [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]], had been killed in the final battle and had automatically returned to Braize; with him there, the Oathpact remained intact and the Fused were sealed away. They also left behind the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]] to continue to protect the people of Roshar.[^6]

Around the time of the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]], the newest generation of Radiants learned that humans were not native to Roshar and had essentially been taking the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers']] land for millennia. Around this time, something—possibly [[Melishi - The Coppermind|Melishi]] trapping the [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]] [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]] —caused the singers to be stripped of their [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]] and [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 4|Identity]], leaving them mostly unable to act for themselves. [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] did not support the Radiants through these events, instead raving about how they would destroy Roshar. As a result, all of the Knights Radiant except the [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Order of Skybreakers]] broke their [oaths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Immortal_Words "Immortal Words"), killing their [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]] in an event known as the [Recreance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Recreance "Recreance"). Some time after this, Odium finally [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splintered]] Honor, though not before Honor was able to make some arrangements to guide the people of Roshar through the coming fight with Odium.[^7]

> “ *Unite them. The sun approaches the horizon. The Everstorm comes. The True Desolation. The Night of Sorrows.*”
> 
> — [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]] during one of Dalinar's visions [^8]

Sometime prior to the reunification of [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar"), the [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather]] began sending the [visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions") that Honor had recorded to potential [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmiths]] to warn them of Odium's coming, guide them to refound the Knights Radiant, and encourage them to unify Roshar against the threat of Odium and his armies. [[Gavilar Kholin - The Coppermind|Gavilar Kholin]] received these visions prior to his death, and he got in contact with [Thaidakar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaidakar "Thaidakar") and [Restares](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Restares "Restares"), somehow getting his hands upon Voidlight and Anti-Voidlight, and forming a plan to bring back the Fused.[^9] Shortly after Gavilar’s death in, [[Dalinar Kholin - The Coppermind|Dalinar Kholin]] began to receive these visions as well.[^10]

On the same night as Gavilar's assassination, the Herald [[Chanarach - The Coppermind|Chanarach]] was killed by her daughter [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]], sending her back to [[Braize - The Coppermind|Braize]] to endure torture alongside [Taln](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taln "Taln").[^11]

The spren were also aware of Odium's impending return as they saw the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]] beginning to take shape in Shadesmar as early as centuries before the start of the True Desolation.[^12] [^13] Motivated by this, a few Radiant spren began to return to the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]] to bond with potential Radiants as early as. The earliest known examples being [[Ivory - The Coppermind|Ivory]] with [[Jasnah Kholin - The Coppermind|Jasnah Kholin]] and [[Testament - The Coppermind|Testament]] with [[Shallan Davar - The Coppermind|Shallan Davar]].[^14] [^15] [^16] [^17]

The [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") collected a [Death Rattle](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Death_Rattle "Death Rattle") that predicted the coming of the True Desolation.[^1] When confronted about his knowledge, [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] told Dalinar that the information came from the Herald [[Battar - The Coppermind|Battar]].[^18]

## Beginning

> “ *Who am I? I... I am Talenel'Elin, Stonesinew, Herald of the Almighty. The Desolation has come. Oh, God... it has come. And I have failed.* ”
> 
> —Talenel upon arriving in [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar") [^19]

The True Desolation formally began when the Herald [[Chanarach - The Coppermind|Chanarach]] broke,[^11] four and a half millennia after the [Aharietiam](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Aharietiam "Aharietiam"), allowing the Fused to return to Roshar. The Herald [[Talenel - The Coppermind|Talenel]] then returned to Roshar to warn the people of their coming and prepare them to fight, arriving outside of [Kholinar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kholinar "Kholinar"). Upon entering the city, he proclaims that the Desolation has come and that he has failed, before falling to the ground, unconscious.[^19] Talenel is later brought to the [[Shattered Plains - The Coppermind|Shattered Plains]] where he is widely viewed as a madman. He repeats the same mantra warning of the coming desolation.[^20]

Meanwhile, some [[Voidspren - The Coppermind|voidspren]] were laying the groundwork for the Fused to return. One, [Ulim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Ulim "Ulim"), successfully made contact with the singer [[Venli - The Coppermind|Venli]], who was researching new forms to help the [[Listener - The Coppermind|listeners]] fight the [[War of Reckoning - The Coppermind|War of Reckoning]] against the Alethi. With significant help from Ulim, Venli discovered [[Regal - The Coppermind#stormform|stormform]].[^21] [^22] Though [[Eshonai - The Coppermind|Eshonai]] worried that stormform would open the listeners up to their gods, she volunteered to take the form as a test of its nature and abilities.[^23] She is unable, however, to control the form and, with Venli's assistance, convinced most of the rest of their people to adopt it as well.[^24]

### The Battle of Narak

During the [[Battle of Narak - The Coppermind|Battle of Narak]], on, the stormform singers used its power to summon a new storm of Odium, known as the [[Everstorm - The Coppermind|Everstorm]]. Much like the [[Highstorm - The Coppermind|highstorm]], it continually rounds the planet, though in the opposite direction.[^25] <sup title="This section needs to be expanded with more information"><i>[expand]</i></sup>

The Everstorm restored Connection and Identity to the singers and allows the Fused to be reborn in the body of a singer as it passes over a region.[^26] [^5]

## Forging the Coalition

Following the Battle of Narak and the human occupation of Urithiru, some time in, Dalinar Kholin forged the [Coalition of monarchs](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coalition_of_monarchs "Coalition of monarchs"),[^27] he began to contact monarchs from all across Roshar to try and get them to join the offensive against the Fused armies.[^28] The primary focus was upon nations that contained Oathgates, due to their strategeic importance.[^29] It was also around this point that the war between Emul and Tukar came to a halt as the new, greater threat diverted their focus and brought an end to their fighting.[^30]

The first leader Dalinar and his scribes reached out to was [Yanagawn](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yanagawn "Yanagawn") of Azir, however, they rebuked his advances due to a distrust of him and Alethkar as a whole. They completely broke off communications after he became desperate and begun to speak to them more forcefully. This distrust was ultimately sourced in the genocide perpetrated against the Azish people by Sadees the Sunmaker, and by Dalinar's own history of brutality and conquest.

Following their failure to ally with the Makabaki, they reached out to [Fen Rnamdi](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fen_Rnamdi "Fen Rnamdi") of Thaylenah. However, she too distrusted the Alethi, with her flatly refusing to meet with that at Urithiru, or allow them to send troops to [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") to help repair it after the destruction wrought by the Everstorm.[^31] He tried to convince her several times, but each time was met with rejection.[^32]

They also contacted [Yezier](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yezier "Yezier") and [Tashikk](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tashikk "Tashikk"), but were rejected by them as well; however, [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]] accepted his invitation and informed Dalinar that he would be arriving with one of his radiants via the Oathgate in Jah Keved.[^31]

## Battle of Thaylen Field

### Lead-up

Leading up to the Battle of Thaylen Field, the Fused occupied Marat, cutting through and conquering Emul on the way. They wished to use Marat's access to the Southern Depths to give them a military advantage against various coastal nations, such as Thaylenah, along with giving them an easy avenue of attack towards Jah Keved.[^33] [^33]

Shortly before the confrontation, [[Taravangian - The Coppermind|Taravangian]], who was working for Odium, manipulated the government in order to motivate them to send off a large portion of the city's defense force, greatly reducing their military prowess and defensive capabilities. This left them reliant upon military aide from the Alethi in order to protect themselves from the naval attack by the Fused offensive.[^34]

Thousands of Singers arrived at Thaylenah masked by the Everstorm, which was spawned by Odium. This allowed them to catch the human defenders off guard.[^35] <sup title="This section needs to be expanded with more information"><i>[expand]</i></sup> At the same time, ten-thousand Singers gathered around the [Thaylen City](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Thaylen_City "Thaylen City") [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgate]] in [Shadesmar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shadesmar "Shadesmar"), using the passing of the Everstorm to pass into the Physical Realm.[^36] [Nergaoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nergaoul "Nergaoul") also gathered at Thaylenah.

As the battle looms, two Thunderclasts rip themselves free of the ground, one of which goes to attack the Gemstone Reserve. As the defenders watch, helpless, [[Szeth - The Coppermind|Szeth]], wielding [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 19|Nightblood]], crashes down from the sky, cleaving the second Thunderclast in two, and permanently destroying it, so it shall never rise again.
