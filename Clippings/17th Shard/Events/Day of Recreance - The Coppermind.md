## Day of Recreance

> “ *That they responded immediately and with great consternation is undeniable, as these were primary among those who would forswear and abandon their oaths. The term Recreance was not then applied but has since become a popular title by which this event is named.*”
> 
> — *[Words of Radiance](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Words_of_Radiance_\(in-world\) "Words of Radiance (in-world)")* chapter 38, page 6 [^1]

The **Day of Recreance** was the final act of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]], around two-thousand Rosharan years before the [[True Desolation - The Coppermind|True Desolation]].[^2] [^3] Nine of the ten orders abandoned their [Shardblades](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardblade "Shardblade") and [[Shardplate - The Coppermind|Shardplate]] and broke their oaths, killing their [[Spren - The Coppermind|spren]]. The [[Order of Skybreakers - The Coppermind|Order of Skybreakers]] was the one order that did not participate; instead, the Skybreakers hid themselves away.[^4] For around two thousand years, the reason for the Recreance remained unknown until it was rediscovered in the [Eila Stele](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eila_Stele "Eila Stele") during the True Desolation.

## Lead Up

Before the Recreance, the Radiants had been fighting in the [[False Desolation - The Coppermind|False Desolation]]. [[Ba-Ado-Mishram - The Coppermind|Ba-Ado-Mishram]], one of the nine [[Unmade - The Coppermind|Unmade]], had learned how to [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connect]] to the [[Singer - The Coppermind|singers]] to give them forms of power and [[Voidlight - The Coppermind|Voidlight]]. The fighting was particularly intense at this time.[^5] In an attempt to stop the fighting, the Radiants sent a strike team to try and imprison Ba-Ado-Mishram using the unique powers of the [[Order of Bondsmiths - The Coppermind|Bondsmiths]].[^6] [^7] This strike team succeeded, causing the singers to lose their [[Identity - The Coppermind|Identity]] and Connection and become unable to think or act independently, transforming them into what are known as [[Singer - The Coppermind|parshmen]].

While this was occurring, the Knights Radiant were forced to abandon [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]]. Due to Mishram's imprisonment the [[Sibling - The Coppermind|Sibling]] withdrew from the tower.[^8] People blamed this withdrawal on increased conflicts among the Radiants and claimed the Sibling withdrew intentionally, but scholars of the time disagreed.[^9] [^10] Some of the tower's protections failed, leading to wilting plants and cooling air, but other functions, such as increased air pressure, continued to work.[^11] Some of the Radiants seemed concerned about the Unmade infiltrating the tower because of the failing protections when they left.[^12]

## Cause

> “ *Regardless, I... understand now as I never did before. The ancient Radiants didn’t abandon their oaths out of pettiness. They tried to protect the world. I blame them for their weakness, their broken oaths. But I also understand.*”
> 
> —The [[Stormfather - The Coppermind|Stormfather's]] thoughts on the Recreance [^13]

By means of a vision from [[Honor - The Coppermind|Honor]], the Knights Radiant discovered that [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinding]] had destroyed [[Ashyn - The Coppermind|Ashyn]], the world mankind first inhabited, and that humans then invaded [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], the [[Dawnsinger - The Coppermind|Dawnsingers']] land. When previous generations had learned this fact, Honor had counseled them that their cause was just. During the generation of the Recreance, Honor was deteriorating under the onslaught of [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] and the growing division between the power of the Shard and its [[Shards|Vessel]] [[Tanavast - The Coppermind|Tanavast]] and did not do so. Instead, he raved about the [[Dawnshard - The Coppermind|Dawnshards]], ancient weapons responsible for the destruction of Ashyn, and promised the Radiants they would also destroy Roshar. In order to save the world, nine orders decided to give up their powers so that Roshar wouldn't be destroyed like their first world was.[^13]

> “ *Please... Understand... I do not want this. We cannot...*”
> 
> —The power of Honor to its Vessel [^14]

There were also other motivations for the Recreance, which have to do with the imprisoning of Ba-Ado-Mishram during the False Desolation.[^15] [^16] [^17] [^18] Tanavast, in direct opposition to the will of the power of Honor, betrayed the "demigod" Unmade Mishram, to whom he had pledged no aggression from his side earlier if she could supplant Odium. He had done this as an agreement between him and Odium, whose [[Intent - The Coppermind|Intent]] refused him the ability to strike at her himself. He instructed the then-Bondsmith [[Melishi - The Coppermind|Melishi]] of the Sibling to trap Mishram in a large [[Polestone - The Coppermind|heliodor]] and to imprison her in an ambush staged during what was supposed and promised to be a peaceful meeting between the singers and the humans that Mishram attended. A dissonant note entered the pure tones of Roshar--of which Mishram had been an integral part--and every Radiant and oath made by Honor was tainted.[^19]

> “ *I projected the future to them. And to them, I did not explain it was only a POSSIBLE future. They saw the world destroyed at their hands.*”
> 
> —The Stormfather's memories [^14]

All Radiants of that time witnessed a vision of Roshar's destruction at their hands owing to Honor, who intensely feared what Surges unbound--which they would be if he failed to survive the contention between Intent and Vessel--would do to Roshar.

Radiants of that era believed Tanavast's vision as the only outcome their existence would lead to, and renounced their oaths near [Feverstone Keep](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Feverstone_Keep "Feverstone Keep"). Admittedly, they would not have known of the creation of [deadeyes](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Deadeye "Deadeye") on such an occurrence, yet they, Knight and Bonded spren, decided together of their own volition.[^14]

## Feverstone Keep

![[Day-of-Recreance---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Caio Santos](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Caio_Santos "Coppermind:Artists/Caio Santos")

The Recreance at Feverstone Keep

According to [Dalinar's visions](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dalinar%27s_visions "Dalinar's visions"), Feverstone Keep was one of the locations where the Radiants dropped their Plate and Blades. It was a fort near [Rall Elorim](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rall_Elorim "Rall Elorim") that the parsh were pushing towards.[^20] The Orders of [[Order of Stonewards - The Coppermind|Stonewards]] and [[Order of Windrunners - The Coppermind|Windrunners]] were on the front lines fighting.

The majority of them walked back to the keep. A scout sent out to meet them reported that they were friendly, but the men inside were confused as to why the Radiants were coming. While wearing their Shardplate, the Radiants charged forward in a line. When they stopped just within bowshot, more Windrunners dropped from the sky to join them. One man in a blue cape stepped forward, drove his Blade into the ground, removed his Plate, and turned to walk away. As he did so, the glow of the weapons began to fade. The other Knights followed his lead. There were soldiers waiting for the Radiants to return from the keep.

Men inside the keep came out and examined the discarded weapons. Initially, some officers yelled for the Blades to be left alone. They were quickly overwhelmed, however, as men rushed to claim a weapon. It did not take long for the scramble to deteriorate into a fight, with men using their newly obtained Blades to kill one another.[^21]

## Aftermath and Legacy

> “ *This act of great villainy went beyond the impudence which had hitherto been ascribed to the orders; as the fighting was particularly intense at this time, many attributed this act to a sense of inherent betrayal; and after they withdrew, about two thousand made assault upon them, destroying much of the membership* ”
> 
> — *Words of Radiance*, Chapter 38, page 20 [^5]

The Knights' actions during the Recreance led not only to the destruction of the orders as a whole but also to the deaths of the former members. The sense of betrayal many felt at the act of the Radiants led many people to attack and kill them. The Skybreakers chose to hide their order and take up a mission of preventing the Knights Radiant from rising again, believing it would lead to the return of the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]] and [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolations]].[^5]

Society quickly changed after the Recreance and began to form the culture seen in modern Roshar. Those in power wanted control of the Shardblades. To prevent women from wielding them, they encouraged following [Arts and Majesty](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Arts_and_Majesty "Arts and Majesty"), a philosophical book claiming masculine arts are two-handed and feminine arts are one-handed. In retaliation, a movement of women claimed the ability to read and write for themselves.[^22] It is also likely that the division between [[Lighteyes - The Coppermind|lighteyes]] and [[Darkeyes - The Coppermind|darkeyes]] was formed during this time. Eye color is connected to the memory of the Radiants.[^23] [^24] The lighteyes' authority likely extends from those memories. The majority of lighteyes are descended from people whose eyes changed color, which would mean their ancestors were either Radiants or [Shardbearers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shardbearer "Shardbearer").[^25]

Spren societies were completely decimated. The majority of the surviving spren were mere children at the time and were forced to spend centuries without nurture and guidance. It took some spren societies two thousand years to regain the culture they had lost.[^26] Many spren feel a mistrust and dislike of humans as a result. This causes some spren to bond singers or work with the [Diagram](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Diagram_\(group\) "Diagram (group)") rather than support the reformation of the Knights Radiant and trust humans again.[^27] [^28] Some would even choose to kill humans before allowing the formation of a [[Nahel bond - The Coppermind|Nahel bond]].[^26]

Within two hundred years of the Recreance, knowledge of the Radiants was already beginning to be lost. It was difficult to distinguish facts, superstition, and lore.[^29] At some point many Shardblades were lost as hundreds were dropped during the Recreance and only around eighty are known in the modern world.[^21]
