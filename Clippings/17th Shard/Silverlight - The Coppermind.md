## Silverlight

**Silverlight** is a city state in the [[17th Shard|cosmere]], located in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]].[^1] [^2] It is an ancient city that existed in some form long before notable cosmere events such as the [[Reod - The Coppermind|Reod]] and the [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|Catacendre]].[^3] The city was once a collection of [dragon](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragon_\(cosmere\) "Dragon (cosmere)") palaces and many dragons still have a home base there, including [[Xisisrefliel - The Coppermind|Xisisrefliel]].[^4] [^5] People from Silverlight are called Lighters.[^4]

It is a meeting point for [[17th Shard|worldhoppers]] and [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realmatically aware]] experts,[^6] and it is the base of operations for the [[Seventeenth Shard - The Coppermind|Seventeenth Shard]].[^7]

## Etymology

The name *Silverlight* is derived from the *Silverlight Nexus*, the city’s greatest landmark.[^4] Similarly, the demonym *Lighters* seems to be derived from the city’s name.

## Geography and Appearance

Silverlight is located entirely in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]]. Not much is known about its location, but the ground near the city is glassy and is known to have plants growing on it.[^4] [^8]

### Universities

Silverlight is famous for its universities. Their Arcanists are aware of many esoteric facts about various planetary systems in the cosmere. This includes manifestations of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], the locations of [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularities]], the existence of [[Splinter - The Coppermind|Splinters]], and the history and current status of [[Shards]].[^9] [^10] [^11] They seek to learn more about cosmere mysteries that they cannot fully explain, such as the existence of a perpendicularity on [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]] [^12] or the nature of the [Evil](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Evil "Evil") on [[Threnody - The Coppermind|Threnody]].[^13] As part of their research, they travel throughout the cosmere investigating unusual events and situations,[^12] although some people prefer to avoid the more dangerous fields of study.[^14] The universities are in contact with the [[Ire - The Coppermind|Ire]], which was for a long time one of the most cosmere-knowledgeable groups in existence, but the Ire are not always willing to collaborate with Silverlight's scholars.[^11] Khriss was involved in the founding of at least one of them.[^15]

Silverlight has produced a number of guide books for the various worlds in the cosmere. These were often written with great difficulty due to how hard it was to get from world-to-world before space travel. Among these is a survey book that has the descriptions of a number of worlds in one volume. One such survey book was in the possession of a [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylen]] worldhopper who was killed by the [Cinder King](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cinder_King "Cinder King") on [[Canticle - The Coppermind|Canticle]].[^16] Arcanists sign a contract with the universities, which makes their research property of the university.[^4] Meanwhile, non-researchers have also been known to sign a contract with the universities. They fund them, and in turn, they get first rights to everything created or discovered by the Arcanists.[^17]

### Silverlight Nexus

Silverlight has its own [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]], known as the Silverlight Nexus. In the Cognitive Realm, the perpendicularity appears like a sun. It is colored a frosty white, and hangs in the void beneath the city, lighting everything from underneath with a calm, white glow. The Nexus is about as large as the city itself, making it the largest perpendicularity by far. It is also unique among perpendicularities in that it leads to three different places in the Physical Realm, rather then one. One of these is the greatstar of [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]]. The powerful Nexus of Silverlight is also the cause of the time dilation in Silverlight. Each day spent in the city passes as ten for those on the outside, which can cause problems with relationships if one stays in Silverlight too long. The perpendicularity is available for travelers through holes in the glassy ground.[^4]

## Culture

### Lighters

The inhabitants of Silverlight are called *Lighters*. Most Lighters have lived in Silverlight for generations, but many of them still retain some part of their ancestry. The city is home to people from dozens of planets, and many of them aren’t human.[^4] [^17] [^18] Residents of Silverlight are known to use [[Silver Kingdoms - The Coppermind#Threnodite Chains|silver chains]] that originate on Threnody,[^19] possibly to anchor themselves through [Cognitive anomalies](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cognitive_anomaly "Cognitive anomaly") while traveling to different planetary systems.[^20] Due to the diverse knowledge of its residents, Silverlight has the best parties in the cosmere.[^21] Silverlight has its own recognizable accent.[^22]

### Architecture

Like any settlement that has existed as long as it has, Silverlight has experienced many different architectural styles. By the Space Age, the city’s architectural aesthetic is to build tall instead of wide: the city at this time contains many willowy skyscrapers of a limber design, using ultralight and extremely strong supports of [dragonsteel](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragonsteel_\(metal\) "Dragonsteel (metal)"), allowing for constructions that go much higher than otherwise would have been possible. Walkways crisscross them, and many have hovering pods as rooms, hanging out over the expanse.[^4] Many of the buildings owned by [dragons](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dragon_\(cosmere\) "Dragon (cosmere)") started as dens, then became palaces and ended up as skyscrapers.[^17]

### Laws

Silverlight appears to have its own jurisdiction and laws. They are known to have played a large role in the development of interplanetary laws and treaties, such as the [Silverlight Codes of Interplanetary Conduct](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Silverlight_Codes_of_Interplanetary_Conduct "Silverlight Codes of Interplanetary Conduct"). These codes include the Refugee and Lost Expatriate Bill, which forces interplanetary travelers to grant asylum under unspecified circumstances.[^23] The Silverlight courts are considered to be just. [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Cephandrius Maxtori]] is a recognized outlaw by Silverlight laws.[^18] Slavery is outlawed in Silverlight.[^17] There are also a number of accords about the treatment of prisoners called the *Silverlight Accords*. They do not protect the dead.[^24] By law some artifacts, like those of the Iriali describing their travels, belong to the people who created them themselves, and any sale or transfer of such an artifact is forbidden. It can only be directly gifted to another.[^4]

### Economy

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Silverlight---The-Coppermind-1.svg]]

Silverlight has a large and well running economy. They have a mercantile company, known as [Silverlight Mercantile](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Silverlight_Mercantile "Silverlight Mercantile"), that is known to act as an interplanetary postal service, making use of mysterious [messengers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Messengers "Messengers") that can trace [[Connection - The Coppermind|Connection]] to find anyone, no matter where they are.[^25] Little more is known about it beyond this function. [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] used them in order to send [MeLaan](https://coppermind.net/wiki/MeLaan "MeLaan") a message concerning [Wayne's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wayne "Wayne") passing.[^25]

Silverlight also has its own film industry which uses false facades to imitate building fronts.[^26] The city is also a hub for the trading of information.[^17] Silverlight is relatively safe, but it is still a big city and docking fees include only minimal security. For that reason, many people choose to leave a guard behind on their ship.[^4] Agriculture is a little different in Silverlight. Plants here grow by the light of the Silverlight Nexus, the city’s perpendicularity, so they need to be exposed to the Nexus’s light and tones.[^8] The city is a metropolis, and traffic can be a mess. Generally though, flight control keeps it organized.[^4]

## History

Silverlight has a long history, going back millennia before the Space Age. The city started out as a bunch of dragon palaces. However, it soon became one of the first multicultural settlements in the cosmere. A few decades before the discovery of [Patji's Eye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji%27s_Eye "Patji's Eye"), the dragons revealed that Yolen was the homeworld of the [[Shards]], and that it was accessible via Silverlight’s perpendicularity.[^4] This caused people to want to visit the place, and soon there were long waitlists.[^27] Silverlight is a politically strong and important power, and even the Malwish don’t dare bully them.[^28]

## Trivia

- Brandon plans to write a novella set on Silverlight.[^29] [^30] Khriss would be a viewpoint character [^31] and it would include the Seventeenth Shard.[^32]
- Brandon was hesitant about namedropping this city because for a time he thought he might have to change the name due to the release of [Microsoft Silverlight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Silverlight "wikipedia:Microsoft Silverlight").[^33]
