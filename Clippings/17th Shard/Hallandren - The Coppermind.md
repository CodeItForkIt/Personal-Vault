## Hallandren

> “ *This, however, was Hallandren: land of Returned gods, Lifeless servants, BioChromatic research, and—of course—color.* ”
> 
> — [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Vasher]] [^1]

**Hallandren** is the most powerful kingdom on [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthis]].[^2] Its capital is [[T'Telir - The Coppermind|T'Telir]].[^3] It is unique for its jungle environment and the [Tears of Edgli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli"), which grow nowhere else. It is home to the Hallandren [[Iridescent Tones - The Coppermind|Iridescent Tones]], the religion that supports and is led by the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]].[^4]

## Etymology

The meaning of the name *Hallandren* is unknown, but it likely stems from its predecessor kingdom, [Hanald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hanald "Hanald"). The kingdom is also known as *The Kingdom of Iridescence*, likely due to the abundance of bright colors.[^5] People from Hallandren are called Hallandren.[^6]

## Geography and Ecology

> “ *A paradise hidden between the mountains, a land with pleasant rains that never grew cold, a land where succulent food grew spontaneously.* ”
> 
> — [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] telling what the [Chedesh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chedesh "Chedesh") thought when they first landed on Hallandren's beaches [^7]

Hallandren is located in the jungle valley on Nalthis. It is unique in the known world of Nalthis as being the only place that [Tears of Edgli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli") will grow, as well as being the only known jungle climate.[^7] The growth of plants in this region is partly helped by [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], much like the [Elendel Basin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elendel_Basin "Elendel Basin").[^8] The jungle is inhabited by panthers, parrots, monkeys, snakes, and crocodiles.[^7] [^9] [^10] [^11] Parts of the jungle are cleared to make room for cropland, where food and dyes are produced.[^11]

To Hallandren's east,[^12] there is an inland [sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bright_Sea "Bright Sea") that features several islands.[^13] At least a third of the coastline is controlled by Hallandren, which has its [[T'Telir - The Coppermind|capital city]] built abutting the water.[^14] The area suffers frequent earthquakes.[^15] There are mountains north of Hallandren, as well as toward the sunrise. There is a perpendicularity located in the Hallandren jungle.[^8]

Elephants and zebras are said to be exotic creatures from "the south." Whether this means they come from southern Hallandren or south of Hallandren is unclear.[^10]

The Hallandren people have access to fine wines, sweet potatoes, figs, nuts, and garlic, among other foods; it is unknown, however, how much grows in the area and how much is imported.[^16] [^17] The Bright Sea is full of life, including many sorts of mussels, fish, and tentacled creatures.[^11] [^18] [^19]

## Government

> “ *The Hallandren theocracy is a top-heavy structure laden with bureaucratic foolishness and inertia.* ”
> 
> — [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Vasher]] [^20]

### Theocracy

Hallandren is a theocracy ruled by the [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind|Pantheon]] of the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]], headed by the [[Susebron - The Coppermind|God King]] [^4] The religion behind this theocracy is the Iridescent Tones. A religion that worships the Returned as gods,[^4] and believes they returned to guide and bless their people.[^4]

The [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] have duties and votes on specific matters in the city and the empire.[^21] The task of the priesthood is generally to inform the gods of the people’s opinions.[^21] The Returned can then make informed decisions, which the priests enforce. In reality the priests handle most of the minor decisions, so that the Returned only vote on the major decisions.[^22] This method of government, waiting on debates that must be brought to the gods (who cannot leave the court to be among the people of the city) by the priests, operates notoriously slowly.

The government loans out [[Lifeless - The Coppermind|Lifeless]] to help citizens with menial labor such as clearing land of trees in order to create farmland. Terms of such a loan stipulate that in war time any food produced on the landowner's land will be seized by the Returned. While this is specific to the policy for lending out the Lifeless, the government retains the right to seize any land during war time regardless. Most of the land in Hallandren is private property.[^11]

#### Court Assembly

The Court Assembly is a standing commission of the Court of Gods that provides decisions to Hallandren's citizens.[^23] It meets daily to make small judgments, but important discussions are reserved for weekly meetings of the full Assembly.[^23] The Assembly is made up primarily of priests and priestesses, and meetings are held in a large arena at the back of the Court of Gods.[^23] [^16] The Returned do not sit on the Assembly,[^23] but many of them regularly attend the weekly meetings to watch from ornate stone observation boxes,[^16] including Susebron.[^24]

Some Returned do not concern themselves with politics and rarely attend,[^2] while others find the meetings boring because they believe their opinions do not matter.[^25] During Assembly meetings, arguments are brought forth regarding various topics such as relations with Idris; [[Sisirinah - The Coppermind|Siri]] was also presented to the Court at the Assembly.[^26] The Assembly's discussions are often full of rhetoric [^27] and politically savvy Returned try to influence the direction of the Assembly.[^21] Attending the Court Assembly is thought of as a privilege by the people of Hallandren; there are only four benches in the arena for citizens to attend, so only people who are rich, influential, favored by a god, or have at least fifty Breaths attend regularly.[^26] Average citizens may enter a lottery to win a token to attend the Assembly.[^26] [^22]

#### Main Assembly

The regular Court Assembly is not empowered to make major decisions such as a declaration of war; instead, all of the Returned must attend a gathering known as a "main" or "general" assembly so they can all cast a vote.[^28] [^5] The outcome of a Main Assembly becomes the Will of the Pantheon. All the gods have the right to refuse the Will of the Pantheon, although it is frowned upon. These special assemblies are rare and often very crowded, as citizens who win lottery tokens to attend an Assembly often save them for a main assembly so they can see all of the gods together.[^22]

#### Territories

Hallandren controls the entire jungle valley, and is bordered by [[Idris - The Coppermind|mountains]] to the north [^29] and east.[^30] It is also bordered by the [Bright Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bright_Sea "Bright Sea") [^3] and controls about one third of it’s coastline. They also control multiple islands in the Bright Sea.[^13]

#### Administrative Districts

Hallandren is made up of a number of provinces. The only known province is [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]], a quiet and peaceful province in the south of Hallandren.[^30] This province is home to an ethnic group called [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind#Culture|Pahn Kahl]],[^30] although this isn’t the only place in Hallandren where these people can be found.[^7]

### The Monarchy

#### The God King

![[Clippings/17th Shard/attachments/Hallandren---The-Coppermind-1.jpg]]

by [Shuravf](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Shuravf "Coppermind:Artists/Shuravf")

[[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]], the current God King of Hallandren

Hallandren has had a monarchy for its entire history, some three hundred years. All of their kings were stillborn, after which they immediately [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] to rule the empire. With the exception of their first king, who is called [Peacegiver](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Peacegiver "Peacegiver"), all of them are named Susebron, with the current king being [[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]] the fifth.[^7] Due to their nature as Returned they carry the title of God King.

The God King has absolute power in Hallandren,[^31] although in reality he acts more like a figurehead for the priesthood.[^32] The priests also cut out his tongue to prevent him from using his [Breaths](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Light_of_Peace "Light of Peace"),[^32] as their god [Peacegiver](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Peacegiver "Peacegiver") commanded them to do.[^18] The God King resides in a [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind#God king.E2.80.99s palace|large black palace]] in the [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind|Court of Gods]].[^3] He can also veto any decision made by the Pantheon of Gods.[^22]

Not all Hallandren consider [Peacegiver](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Peacegiver "Peacegiver") to have been their first God King, some instead consider Susebron the First as their first king. At least one of the God Kings ruled for a century. The God Kings are believed to have received life from Peacegiver.[^7]

#### The Queen

For most of a God King’s rule the Hallandren are without a queen.[^31] This is because a queen is only selected after an infant Returns.[^13]

> “ *The dynasty of Hallandren was founded at the conclusion of the Manywar, the first one gaining his Breath and life from Peacegiver himself, who was revered for dispelling Kalad’s Phantoms and bringing a peaceful end to the Manywar. Since that day, each God King has fathered a stillborn son who then Returned and took his place.*”
> 
> —Hoid on the Hallandren Kings [^7]

The Queen is formally addressed with ‘Your Majesty’ or ‘Your Excellency’.[^7] [^30] She is also sometimes addressed as ‘Vessel’ as she is considered to be a Vessel for the God King.[^30]

The Queen has only one duty, to provide the kingdom with an heir.[^23] She spends the rest of her time being idle, enjoying the same luxuries as the Returned.[^23] She is not allowed to leave the [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind|Court of Gods]].[^23] She is also one of the few people in the Court of Gods who are allowed to touch the God King.[^33]

#### Succession

The God King’s priests take an infant Returning as a sign that it’s time to change Kings.[^30] They then choose a wife for the God King and hope she has a child, as they would rather have the God King's literal child.[^13] Sometimes the queen is the actual mother of the child, while other times a marriage is arranged. Retired God Kings are proclaimed dead from healing those in need and are taken to one of the isles in the middle of the Inner Sea.[^10] They maintain a lavish lifestyle until they die.[^34]

### Foreign Policy and International Relationships

#### Idris

> “ *They were called rebels, but those “rebels” were the true Hallandren royal family. Mortal men though they might be, their bloodline was a challenge to the Court of Gods.*”
> 
> —Vasher thinking about the Idrians

Hallandren and [[Idris - The Coppermind|Idris]] have a mostly antagonistic relationship. This is because of a number of reasons, most of which can find their way back to the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]]. One of these reasons is the splintering of [Hanald](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Hanald "Hanald"), with both kingdoms claiming the other’s territories as their own.[^21] Another reason is the Idrian religion of Austrism. This religion, banished after the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]], began teaching against everything that had to do with Hallandren, such as ostentation [^31] and awakening.[^35] Due to these reasons, along with the false stories told by ramblemen, the hatred of Hallandren became deeply rooted in the Idrian culture.

Many Idrians in the lower mountains were forced out of their homes through famine or lack of money,[^36] these Idrians, unwilling to make the long and hard journey to [Bevalis](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bevalis "Bevalis") to petition the king, traveled south into Hallandren and started working here.[^36] These people are looked down upon by both the Idrians and the Hallandren.

After the [[Pahn Kahl rebellion - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl rebellion]] the God King [[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]] the fifth, a friend of the Idrians, took more direct control of Hallandren politics and because of this the relations with Idris likely improved.

#### Tedradel

Since the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]], which saw Hanald, the predecessor of Hallandren, and Tedradel go to war with each other [^7] Tedradel’s opinion of Hallandren hasn’t improved much.[^35] Their opinion of Hallandren is so low that [Yarda](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Yarda "Yarda") considers it possible that Tedradel would go to war again, should Idris be attacked.[^35]

Tedradel still has many traders in T’Telir,[^14] and likely other Hallandren cities as well.

#### The Northern Kingdoms

Hallandren doesn’t have a very great relationship with the Northern Kingdoms. If Hallandren had decided to attack Idris a number of these kingdoms would have aided Idris.[^29] Dozens of these kingdoms do not share Hallandren’s views of Idris as a rebel faction.[^29]

Hallandren trades extensively with the Northern kingdoms.[^29]

## Military

### The Lifeless Army

Hallandren has a large lifeless army, forty thousand strong.[^35] They have been adding to this army for several centuries before the [[Pahn Kahl rebellion - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl rebellion]].[^28]

The Hallandren Lifeless army is split into four sections of ten thousand troops, each commanded by a different Returned in the Pantheon.[^28] The commanders prior to the [[Pahn Kahl rebellion - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl rebellion]] were [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]], [[Hopefinder - The Coppermind|Hopefinder]], [[Allmother - The Coppermind|Allmother]] and [[Mercystar - The Coppermind|Mercystar]].[^28] Some of these Returned are bored by their duty, reasoning that they never have to actually do anything.[^25]

While the bulk of the army is Lifeless,[^2] there is quite a large support structure for the army proper which is composed of humans.[^37]

The [[Lifeless - The Coppermind|lifeless]] army resides in the lifeless barracks, located at the bottom of the Court of Gods in T’Telir..[^2] These barracks are heavily fortified,[^2] and the lifeless stationed here have orders to kill anyone who attacks the lifeless.[^37] The lifeless are kept in the dark.[^2]

### Kalad’s Phantoms

After the events of the [[Pahn Kahl rebellion - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl rebellion]] Peacegiver, under the alias of [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Vasher]], granted the security phrase of Kalad’s Phantoms to the God King Susebron.[^38] This army includes some thousand soldiers which still function.[^38]

### Armament

#### Bladed weapons

Most Hallandren use a ‘Dueling blade’, which are a long and thin type of blade.[^37] The Hallandren also use daggers and spears.[^37] [^3]

#### Awakened clothing

It is not uncommon for an awakener to [[Awakening - The Coppermind|awaken]] their clothing, helping them fight.[^39] There are even specialized sets of clothing made for this very purpose.[^39]

## Culture

### People

Hallandren contains many different peoples and cultures,[^14] the majority of the population is ethnic Hallandren, although the kingdom also has a number of [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]] [^7] and [[Idris - The Coppermind|Idrians]] [^14] living inside it’s borders. The people at the edges of the kingdom of Hallandren have dark skin.[^16] The natural hair colors of the Hallandren people include black, brown and blond.[^11]

Hallandren has no nobility apart from the Returned,[^3] instead it has a small but very rich class which is almost completely compromised of the most successful merchants, a middle class, and a large peasant class. Hallandren is the only nation on [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthis]] to have a middle class, instead of only a high and low class.[^29]

Pride is very important to the Hallandren people, and priests are considered to be even more prideful.[^22] The Hallandren are also fond of carriages.[^6] Although the Returned and their Queen prefer to use palanquins.[^16] The Hallandren keep their dead in charnel houses.[^37]

#### Athleticism

Hallandren's society places value in athleticism.[^10] Duels for sport are common enough that someone talented with a blade could gain a wealth of prizes, notable fame, and a sizable following of students in their lifetime.[^6] The Court of Gods’ arena is often used as a location for athletic individuals to engage in contests of physical prowess.[^10]

Some arena competitions mentioned are shot put, javelin toss, archery, leaping contests, and races.[^10] However, special accolades are not given to the winner, but to all the contestants, for possessing the physical prowess required to participate.[^10]

#### Arts and Games

The Hallandren people enjoy music, painting, dance, poetry, sculpture, puppetry, play performance, sandpainting and lots of other ‘lesser’ arts.[^23] The Hallandren also have fireworks, and use them to make large fireshows.[^40]

By the time of the [[Pahn Kahl rebellion - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl rebellion]] the abstract painting style is popular,[^4] especially in paintings sent to [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]] The Bold.[^4] There are a few [artists](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nenefra "Nenefra") who can make a special type of painting,[^17] invested people, such as the Returned, can see things in these paintings which aren’t actually there.[^17] While normal people see the actual strokes of paint.[^41] [^17] [^42]

Some wealthy Hallandren play [Tarachin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tarachin "Tarachin"),[^11] a notoriously difficult game.[^11] Tarachin requires strength to throw the spheres correctly,[^11] keen wit to understand where to place them,[^11] coordination to do so with the necessary precision,[^11] and a great understanding of strategy to pick the proper sphere and dominate the game field.[^11]

### Fashion and Beauty

> “ *a hundred different colors and as many different clothing styles were represented. It was good to see some variety again, even if that variety was somewhat garish.*”
> 
> —Siri thinking about the Hallandren [^16]

The Hallandren wear flamboyant and brightly coloured clothing.[^16] They wear a wide variety of styles,[^16] with each person trying to stand out.[^14] However, because of this nobody stands out.[^16] Modesty in both design and color is unfashionable, but not nonexistent.[^16]

Hallandren clothing is revealing,[^14] with blouses that are open well below the neck, skirts well above the knees etc.[^14] Clothing in Hallandren ranges from vests and shorts to long robes and gowns. Some ancient Returned wore robes that hung from only one shoulder with a wrap around their waist that came down to mid-thigh.[^11] Ornate hats are popular, both on men and women.[^16] Some Hallandren carry umbrellas for when it is raining.[^16] Many Hallandren also dye their hair.[^16] Clothing is often made from leather,[^14] although silk is also used for the rich.[^33] A number of women also wear trousers, considered to be utilitarian.[^14]

Hallandren women wear perfumes,[^33] either taking perfumed baths [^33] or having someone else rub perfume onto the body. [^33] They also have many kinds of make-up, such as lipstick and eyeliner.[^33] Some women have a sort of paint on their thighs.[^40] Long hair is seen as a sign of beauty.[^33]

Glossy black hair, pale skin and a lusciously curvaceous body are considered by some to be the epitome of female beauty.[^40] The Hallandren find both thin and heavy women to be attractive.[^43] The epitome of male beauty is large bulging muscles and square jawed features.[^11]

### BioChroma and Awakening

Hallandren is known for it’s research in BioChroma.[^1]

#### Breaths and Awakening

The Hallandren believes that losing your [[BioChromatic Breath - The Coppermind|Breath]] doesn’t change you much.[^4] There is also a very strict breath market.[^44] Due to these reasons many people sell their breaths when they need some money. These breaths are often bought by rich people.[^29] Most people stop at 50 breaths,[^29] at this point they have attained the first heightening,[^29] their lifespan is also improved by a good decade [^29] or so and their immune system is a lot more effective. They can also do a little bit of [[Awakening - The Coppermind|awakening]] themselves,[^29] a useful skill to have. People with more then a thousand breaths are uncommon, but not unheard of.[^29]

Awakened objects performing tasks aren’t uncommon.[^29]

#### The Returned

The [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] also need one breath per week to stay alive.[^4] These breaths are usually provided by children.[^4] The families who sell their breaths to the Returned are blessed and gain enough money to live a whole year on.[^6]

#### Lifeless

[[Lifeless - The Coppermind|Lifeless]] are fairly common in Hallandren,[^6] although most people still give them a wide berth.[^11] It is common to dress lifeless in grey clothing,[^11] matching their skin and marking them as a lifeless.

People in Hallandren can pay an awakener to make a lifeless.[^37] Families can also sell corpses to these people,[^37] with the more skilled the person was the higher the cost is going to be.

### Language and Writing

The Hallandren speak a language called Hallandren.

The Hallandren have their own Alphabet as well. The first letter of this alphabet is called shash.[^32] The Hallandren also use a second script called the [artisan's script](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Artisan%27s_script "Artisan's script").[^4] This script is a form of writing based on color, each hue representing a different sound. Skilled artists of at least the Third Heightening often write poetry using this script.[^4]

### Architecture

The Hallandren have a very diverse architectural style.[^3] This style consists of many domes and spires, while pyramids are also, rarely, used. Buildings are generally painted in a wide array of, often very bright, colours.[^14] Even furniture and other objects are painted.[^11] Due to this there are very few dull things in a Hallandren city, and most of these are located in the slums, which often contain objects and buildings with faded colours.[^11]

The Hallandren love greenery and often design their cities with many gardens and open spaces to make place for greenery.[^3] [^6] Some buildings are painted for someone of the [[Heightening - The Coppermind#Third Heightening|Third Heightening]], with very minute changes in color. Examples of this are the Returned palaces.[^16]

### Cuisine

#### Foods

The Hallandren enjoy a wide variety of foods, including seafoods and sea fruits [^18] as well as regular plants and animals. Known sealife includes mussels, fish, and octopi.[^11] [^14] [^18] The Hallandren also like many foreign foods.[^29] The Hallandren upper class enjoy eating [tinkfans](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tinkfan "Tinkfan"), a [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]] delicacy.[^45] They like their food spicy.[^3]

#### Drinks

The Hallandren also drink hot tea.[^29] A known variety is citrus tea.[^29] They also enjoy a wide variety of juices.[^36]

### Calendar

The Hallandren may have a calendar which views the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]] as the year zero, as Hoid mentions that the time before the Manywar was before time itself began.[^7]

## Religions

Many different religions are worshipped in Hallandren, although most people follow the state religion, the [[Iridescent Tones - The Coppermind|Iridescent Tones]]. Other religions include [[Austrism - The Coppermind|Austrism]] and the [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind#Culture|Pahn Kahl]] faith.

### The Iridescent Tones

The majority of the Hallandren people follow the [[Iridescent Tones - The Coppermind|Iridescent Tones]].[^26] This religion worships the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] as gods [^4] and considers [Peacegiver](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Peacegiver "Peacegiver") to be a god amongst gods.[^18]

The [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind|Court of Gods]] is located in Hallandren, in the city of T’Telir.[^3] The Returned gods of Hallandren reside in this Court, supposedly blessing the Hallandren with guidance and good fortune.[^4]

The Iridescent Tones were established in Hallandren shortly after the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]]. After this war the Hallandren felt that they had been forsaken by Austre and saved by Peacegiver,[^18] and so abandoned Austrism and began worshipping the Returned.[^7]

### Austrism

Most of the Idrians living in Hallandren follow Austrism.[^46] This is a monotheistic religion centred around the worship of Austre and the Five Visions.[^47] [^48] Austrism was practised in Hallandren before the Manywar.[^26] Idrians who convert to the Iridescent Tones are called ‘scrapes’ by other Idrians.[^49] The term is an epithet that refers to a scratch on a person’s arm revealing the colorful blood underneath.

### The Pahn Kahl Faith

Most [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]] living in Hallandren follow their own religion.[^26] This religion is a kind of nature worship where the storms of the [Bright Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bright_Sea "Bright Sea") are revered as manifestations of the unity of their five gods.[^50] Their religion also focuses more on the Voice that sends back the Returned.[^51]

Many people in Hallandren are unaware of the Pahn Kahl religion, instead assuming them to worship the Returned as well.[^16]

## Economics

### The Dye Trade

Hallandren’s main source of income are the dyes made from the [Tears of Edgli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli").[^7] These flowers can only be grown in Hallandren, and as such the Hallandren people have a monopoly on them. The Tears of Edgli make excellent dyes, and are far cheaper to produce then other dyes.[^7] For these reasons, as well as their use in awakening, they are very desired, far more so then other dyes.[^7]

### Trade Routes

#### The Bright Sea

Hallandren is located on the western shore of the [Bright Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bright_Sea "Bright Sea").[^3] This sea contains many trade routes, which are a boon to the kingdoms located on its shores. Hallandren profits a lot from trade with these kingdoms, such as Tedradel.[^14]

Hallandren imports fresh foodstuffs from a number of cities on the coast,[^14] and they also import oil. There are many merchants from Tedradel in Hallandren, selling a wide variety of products such as clothing.[^14] Hallandren has multiple [Stormrunners](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Stormrunners "Stormrunners").[^52]

#### Idris and the Northern Kingdoms

Hallandren also trades with many other kingdoms, such as the Northern Kingdoms,[^4] beyond the mountains.[^29] [^4] These trade routes run through Idris,[^29] making this country very important to Hallandren, one of the reasons they want to control it. [Bebid](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bebid "Bebid") reasons that should trade with these kingdoms stop, Hallandren would suffer a full economic collapse.[^29]

Hallandren imports carpets from these nations.[^4]

#### Perpendicularity and the Cognitive Realm

There is a trade route running through the Hallandren [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]].[^53] This route also runs through the Nalthian [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]], which is located somewhere in Hallandren. It is unknown if the Hallandren government is involved in this trade.

### Politics

Priests control much of the trade in the city. Being wealthy individuals with notable political power in Hallandren's theocracy, they own and organize a large sum of the traded goods in Hallandren.[^37] There are strict rules around buying and selling Breath.[^1]

[Truthcall](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Truthcall "Truthcall") is one of the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] given a vote over issues of trade with other kingdoms. As such, his decisions hold sway over the economics of both Hallandren and its trade partners.[^11]

### Currency

Coins in Hallandren are called marks, with a smaller variety called bits.[^27] [^54] Five thousand marks is considered to be a fortune,[^27] while a single bit would not buy more than a mouthful of food.[^54]

Hallandren coins, likely marks, look like small metal discs about an inch wide, coated with a colored enamel. One side is engraved with the picture of a divine figure in robes, likely a [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]], while the other side has that same figure in battle gear. Both sides have writing on them.[^55]

### Agriculture and Animal Husbandry

Hallandren is an agricultural society, with slash and burn techniques.[^11] There are a number of Hallandren who clear these forests and then rent them to growers.[^11]

The Hallandren herd livestock such as sheep and cows.[^35] [^21] They also fish the sea for fish, octopi, mussels and squid.[^11] [^14] [^18]

## History

### Hanald

The region that became Hanald was originally populated by the [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]] people.[^7] When the people of [Chedesh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chedesh "Chedesh"), who dominated the world at the time,[^7] began exploring other parts of the [Inner Sea's](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Inner_Sea "Inner Sea") coastline, a group of their ships discovered this valley filled with jungles.[^7] One of the explorers, [Vo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vo "Vo"), died and became the first [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]] while on one of the ships in the bay.[^7] He then died again a week later.

Dismissive of the native Pahn Kahl, his crewmates founded a kingdom on the site and named it Hanald.[^7] This kingdom would grow and expand to become one of the most powerful kingdoms of its age.[^7]

### The Manywar and Founding

After [[Manywar - The Coppermind#Roots Of The Conflict|Tensions]] between the Nalthian kingdoms grew [Kalad](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kalad "Kalad"), then known as [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Strifelover]], overthrew the Royal Family and seized control of Hanald. Shortly after this he attacked the northern kingdoms of [[Kuth - The Coppermind|Kuth]] and [[Huth - The Coppermind|Huth]], starting the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]].

After a while Kalad had a change of heart and recalled his troops from their fighting.[^7] He used these troops to seize control of his own country and stopped the Manywar through diplomacy. Due to this he got the name Peacegiver.[^7]

During this time the Royal Family fled T’Telir,[^7] heading to the northern mountains, and established the kingdom of Idris.[^35] After their departure the Cult of the Returned took control of the new nation, renaming it Hallandren.[^7] Their religion eventually evolved into the [[Iridescent Tones - The Coppermind|Iridescent Tones]] and became the norm within Hallandren's territory. The only notable exception was Pahn Kahl, which was absorbed into the new nation of Hallandren as a province,[^56] where the people retained their own religion and culture.[^51] The conclusion of the Manywar was also the foundation for Hallandren's power in the region of the [Bright Sea](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bright_Sea "Bright Sea") in the modern post-war era.[^57] Peacegiver also gave the first [[Susebron - The Coppermind|God King]] a treasure of fifty thousand Breaths and entrusted his priests to see that it was cared for until he needed it again.[^18]

### The God Kings

#### The Treaty With Idris

The king [Dedelin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dedelin "Dedelin") negotiated a treaty with [[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]], promising to send his daughter (ostensibly [[Vivenna - The Coppermind|Vivenna]],[^35] though this was not stated specifically) to the [[Court of Gods - The Coppermind|Court of Gods]] to marry him when Vivenna turned twenty-two, thereby reintroducing the bloodline of the royals into Hallandren's own ruling line.[^35]

He couldn’t bring himself to send [[Vivenna - The Coppermind|Vivenna]] to Hallandren though, so instead he sent [[Sisirinah - The Coppermind|Sisirinah]],[^35] [^31] who would go on to become queen of the Hallandren.[^3]

#### The Pahn Kahl Rebellion

Three hundred years after the [[Manywar - The Coppermind|Manywar]], a Pahn Kahl named [Vahr](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vahr "Vahr") made an attempt at stirring up his people.[^27] He spent over a decade working to that that end, but was ultimately captured and imprisoned due to [[Havarseth - The Coppermind|Bluefingers']] information leaks and killed by Vasher.[^58] [^1]

[[Havarseth - The Coppermind|Bluefingers]] himself planned an extensive subversive campaign centered around starting a war between Hallandren and Idris: he brought mercenaries and Pahn Kahl Awakeners into the tunnel system below the court,[^59] manipulated [[Sisirinah - The Coppermind|Siri]] into distrusting the Hallandren priests,[^60] and hired [Denth's crew](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Denth%27s_crew "Denth's crew") to increase tensions in the city.[^61] When his plans were nearing completion, he tortured [[Blushweaver - The Coppermind|Blushweaver]] and [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]] in order to get the Command phrases for Hallandren's Lifeless army, changed the Command phrase, and sent the army towards Idris.[^30] The attempted rebellion was defeated when the [[Susebron - The Coppermind|God King]], with his newly healed tongue, used [Peacegiver's Treasure](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Peacegiver%27s_Treasure "Peacegiver's Treasure") to storm his palace with an army of Awakened cloths. Vasher decided to give the God King the Command phrase to Kalad's Phantoms, which he then sent after the Lifeless army.[^62]
