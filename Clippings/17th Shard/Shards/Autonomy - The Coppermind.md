## Autonomy

> “ *It is when the soul is pushed to the limit that true exceptionalism manifests. And so, there must be a consequence—as final and terrible as death—for failure.* ”
> 
> —Autonomy [^4]

**Autonomy** is a [[Shards|Shard]] of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]], currently held by [[Bavadin - The Coppermind|Bavadin]].[^1] It's presently located on [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]], Invested in at least one of the local suns, [AisDa](https://coppermind.net/wiki/AisDa "AisDa"), though it possesses a number of [avatars](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Avatar "Avatar") in other worlds throughout the [[17th Shard|cosmere]].[^5] [^6] [^7] [^8]

## Intent

> “ *Autonomy is driven to divide off from the rest of us, go her own way. She pushes her followers to prove themselves, and she rewards those who are bold, who survive against the odds. She respects big plans and big accomplishments.* ”
> 
> — [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] [^1]

Autonomy is ultimately an Intent about individualism, self-actualization, and creative self-expression.[^4] [^9] Furthermore, she promotes survival of the fittest and the competitive exclusion principle; she also supports the application of these ideas not just to nature, but to the social structures of sapient beings.[^4] [^10] [^11] [^12] As a believer in survival of the fittest, she believes that it is through struggle and defying the odds that a person, or a people, reach their full potential.[^1] [^4]

Autonomy wants to remain alone; she does not wish to be corrupted by other Shards and thinks that all Shards are better off on their own. Due to this, she is driven to divide off from the other Shards.[^1] [^13] She strives to become the most powerful, most untouchable Shard; to achieve this, she spreads her influence throughout the cosmere.[^9] Under Autonomy's world order, one must earn their right to keep existing.[^10] Her followers are those who are capable of surviving on their own and defying the odds.[^1] Due to her Intent, she rewards even those that go against her, if they are successful.[^9]

However, this doesn't stop Autonomy from meddling in affairs of others.[^6] She prefers individuality and distinctiveness to be centrally coordinated so that they nicely fit together in a greater plan.[^9] She also seeks to extend her influence across the cosmere and often intervenes in the affairs of her fellow Shards, up to and including indirectly attacking them and plotting invasions of their worlds.[^1] [^9] [^14]

It is unknown if her heteronomous inclinations are an aspect that arises purely from her Intent, or if it arises from how she personally manifests the Intent.

## Associated Magics

### Sand mastery

Sand mastery is the magic system of the [Dayside](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Dayside "Dayside") of [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]]. Autonomy's [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] travels through the atmosphere there and is absorbed by a small lichen living on the local sand; this has the effect of turning the sand white.[^6] Sand masters can use this to command the sand, making it move around. Sand is controlled in ribbons; the strength of a sand master is defined by how many ribbons they are capable of calling forth.[^15] [^8] They do so at the cost of their internal stores of water,[^6] an aspect of the magic which is implied to be intentional mimicry of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 17|Aether]] magic.[^16]

It's unclear what the conditions for becoming a sand master are. The ability seems to be a combination of nature and nurture,[^17] and must be encouraged to appear in a person by another Sand master;[^8] it can take weeks of training before one can control sand. At the same time, it appears to be in-born to at least some extent, as different people have different strength, and in some, the power will not appear at all.[^15] [^17]

The same [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] used in sand mastery can be also utilized by people in other star systems, if Taldain's sun is visible from their location.[^18] [[Surgebinding - The Coppermind|Surgebinders]] could draw Investiture from the sun's light.[^19]

### Starmarks

**Starmarks** are a form of Invested Art on Darkside, charged by weekly pulses from [Ridos](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taldain_system "Taldain system"). Those who use this art are called **Starcarved**. There are many similarities between starmarks and sand mastery.[^8]

### Navigation

**Navigation** is a form of Invested Art on [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]], granted to those who have survived working on the Pantheon and spent time near [Patji's Eye](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji%27s_Eye "Patji's Eye").[^20] [^21] [^22]. It is given by Autonomy's avatar, [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(being\) "Patji (being)").[^20] Those who use this art are called **Navigators**. Navigators are able to sense pulses in the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]] from the [Grand Knell](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Grand_Knell "Grand Knell"), and can feel disturbances from when things get in its way.[^23] Unlike a [Seeker](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Seeker "Seeker"), who can only get a bearing to the Grand Knell, a Navigator can sense subastral obstructions to the Current, allowing them to chart a more accurate course through the Emberdark.[^21]

### Aviar

Aviar are a type of birds on the planet of [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]]. They are known for their supernatural abilities; every species of Aviar has their own, specific gift. They gain those talents by feeding on fruits of [Cakoban's fingers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Cakoban%27s_finger "Cakoban's finger"), a tree native to the island of [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(island\) "Patji (island)"). This fruit contains worms, which bond with Aviar to grant them their powers. Aviar abilities include hiding one's mind from those capable of perceiving it, and showing others their possible deaths.[^24] As the mind of Patji is an avatar of Autonomy, it's highly likely that Aviar make use of Autonomy's Investiture.[^25]

### Avatars

> “ *Autonomy is trying to outcompete the others by filling the cosmere with versions of herself. Crowd out the competition, so to speak.* ”
> 
> — [Moonlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moonlight "Moonlight") [^9]

Like all Shards,[^26] Autonomy is able to create avatars—semi-autonomous entities that wield a portion of Autonomy's power without being wholly separate from Autonomy herself. Autonomy creates two types of avatars. One type are pieces of Autonomy that have been allowed to gain their own sentience, becoming effectively alternate identities of herself. The other are Physical beings that have been granted the use of a portion of Autonomy's power, and becoming a [[Sliver - The Coppermind|Sliver]] of her.[^1] [^27] Physical avatars benefit from becoming Invested, granting them immortality, as well as incredible healing and resilience.[^28] However, they require a [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]] to Autonomy herself in order to benefit from their status as an avatar; interrupting and cutting off that Connection leads them to lose the power.[^27] While they are connected and begin to become a sliver, they also begin to take on Autonomy's Intent, similar to full [Vessels](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vessel "Vessel").[^27] [^4]

Avatars, both made and Invested, can be of any gender, race and species, including fully inhuman ones.[^29] They are considered to be Shards of Adonalsium, in a way.[^7] Autonomy can create avatars on different [[17th Shard|planets]] in the cosmere without physically visiting them.[^3] Avatars can also form without a direct and conscious decision on Autonomy's part, but she will always be aware of this happening.[^30]

Through this process, Autonomy has created several personas, both male and female; she has more female personas than male ones, though the latter are also quite popular. She prefers to make her avatars rather than herself the center of attention.[^29]

- **[[Sand Lord - The Coppermind|Sand Lord]]**, on Taldain; Taldain is also home to many other, unnamed personas of Autonomy, though it is unclear if they are all avatars or if they are all different "faces" of the Sand Lord.[^31] [^29] He apparently manifested himself at some point to [Elorin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elorin "Elorin"), leading to an attempt to destroy the [[Diem - The Coppermind|sand masters]], though whether driving sand mastery to extinction was his goal remains uncertain.[^32] The Sand Lord may have also been responsible for the schism between Kerzta and Lossand, as the siblings who founded both nations, [Kerzt](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Kerzt "Kerzt") and [Lossa](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Lossa "Lossa") both claimed to have received visits from the Sand Lord, but the content of those visits were very different. Lossa was gifted sand mastery, while Kerzt was led to believe sand mastery was a curse.[^8]
- **[Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(being\) "Patji (being)")**, located on [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]]; the origin and amount of its [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] is unknown.[^25] [^7] It wrote a letter to Hoid where it dismisses his concerns over [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]].[^33] [^34] [^7]
- **[[Obrodai - The Coppermind|Obrodai]] avatar**, younger than either of the above. She has been instilled with a great dislike of [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] in order to prevent him from returning there.[^35]
- **[Telsin Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Telsin_Ladrian "Telsin Ladrian")**, the leader of the [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set"). Telsin was Invested by Autonomy in order to facilitate either control or destruction of [Scadrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Scadrian "Scadrian") society. In this function, both Telsin and Autonomy herself are occassionally referred to as [Trell](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trell_\(Trellism\) "Trell (Trellism)").[^9] [^4]

### Trellium

[[Trellium - The Coppermind|Trellium]], more rarely called **bavadinium**, is the [God Metal](https://coppermind.net/wiki/God_Metal "God Metal") of Autonomy.[^36] While its [[Allomancy - The Coppermind|Allomantic]] and [[Feruchemy - The Coppermind|Feruchemical]] properties are unknown, when used as a [[Hemalurgic chimera - The Coppermind|Hemalurgic]] spike, trellium can grant other Allomantic or Feruchemical abilities, and hide the bearer of the spike from [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony's]] vision and control.[^37] [^38] Additionally, trellium repells other forms of [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]], particularly [[Harmonium - The Coppermind|harmonium]]. [^39]

When trellium is brought into contact with superheated harmonium, it causes a massive, exceptionally destructive explosion, capable of levelling entire cities.[^39] Moreover, in the aftermath of the blast, small quantities of [[Lerasium - The Coppermind|lerasium]] and [[Atium - The Coppermind|atium]] can be found.[^36] [^40]

### Faceless Immortals

> “ *They didn't understand, couldn’t understand. The Set had Faceless Immortals of its own.* ”
> 
> — [Edwarn Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Edwarn_Ladrian "Edwarn Ladrian") [^41]

Autonomy appears to have the ability to take control of living bodies, even as far as on [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]]. It is unclear what her limitations are, or if there is any requirement the person must fulfill before Autonomy can use them; it is likely that it requires [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 18|Connection]] to Autonomy, and possibly the presence of trellium. When people are under Autonomy's control, she can make them move to their wish and speak what she wants; they can be recognized as hers by red glow in their eyes.[^4] The [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set") calls them their "Faceless Immortals"; they do not appear aware of the Immortals' true nature.[^41] It's unclear if Autonomy can return the body under the control of its original owner, or if the person is still aware of themselves while Autonomy is in control.

### Perpendicularity Control

> “ *There are planets where Autonomy has created such portals unexpectedly, and against all understood mechanics.* ”
> 
> — [Moonlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moonlight "Moonlight") [^42]

Autonomy seems to have mastered control over [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularities]]. She has barred access to [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]] through the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]], locking any native worldhoppers out of their own homeland.[^6] On other worlds, she has shown the ability to create artificial, temporary perpendicularities; the methods by which she does so are unknown to cosmere scholars and appear to break the known laws of [[Realmatic Theory, Miscellaneous|Realmatic Theory]].[^42]

However, unlike natural perpendicularities, the portals created by Autonomy have strict constraints, requiring specific time and place to form. What affects those conditions is unclear, though it appears that large quantities of Invested individuals and liquid Investiture are key aspects. Whatever the case may be, those constraints means that for Autonomy to use the portals effectively, she requires a great deal of coordination and timing.[^43] Additionally, once made, those perpedicularities can be destroyed by spending the Investiture they are comprised of; a small group of [Allomancers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Allomancer "Allomancer") can destroy a portal over the course of several minutes, by using the Investiture to power their Allomancy, in order to quickly burn it away.[^44]

## History

For the history of Bavadin prior to taking up the Shard, see [[Bavadin - The Coppermind|Bavadin#History]].

### Early History and Isolation of Taldain

> “ *Autonomy's policy of isolationism in recent times (in direct contrast to her interference with other planets, I might add) has prevented travel to and from Taldain for many, many years.* ”
> 
> — [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") [^6]

Autonomy was created in the aftermath of the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering of Adonalsium]], and taken up by [[Bavadin - The Coppermind|Bavadin]], who carried it away from [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]].[^45] Eventually, she settled in [Taldain system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Taldain_system "Taldain system").[^6] Someone altered the system into its current, unnatural shape, though whether this was Autonomy or some other force is unknown.[^46]

Eventually, Autonomy began to enforce a policy of strict isolationism for the planet, preventing people from leaving and travellers such as [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") from returning.[^6] She also began to take a more active role in Taldain's affairs, teaching her people about advanced physics and technologies she has forseen with her powers, leading to the planet's meteoric rise as a technological powerhouse.[^36] Throughout this time, however, Taldain remained isolated from the rest of the cosmere.

Even as Taldain grew inaccessible, Autonomy started to nurture avatars of herself on various worlds, including [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]].[^47] At some point during this time, she has seeded the religion of [Trelagism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trelagism "Trelagism") on [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]], presumably with the intent of later activating it and raising her own avatar on the planet.[^9]

It is possible that Autonomy aided [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]] in killing [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]] and [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]] in an unknown way.[^48] The two Shards appear to have some form of alliance or pact preventing them from attacking each other. Odium intended to eventually break it and double-cross Autonomy, killing her off; however, he was trapped in the [Rosharan system](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rosharan_system "Rosharan system") before that could happen.[^49]

Millennia later, [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] sent a message to Autonomy asking for aid with Odium and the ongoing [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]]. The message was intercepted by her avatar on First of the Sun and the request denied, although [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(being\) "Patji (being)") indicated that other avatars may be more inclined to assist.[^50] [^51] At the same time, Autonomy was manifesting another avatar on [[Obrodai - The Coppermind|Obrodai]], and Patji demanded that Hoid does not return there.[^35]

### Trell

#### The Set

> “ *One day, the men of gold and red, bearers of the final metal, will come to you. And you will be ruled by them. Worship. Worship Trell and wait…* ”
> 
> — [Miles Hundredlives](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Miles_Hundredlives "Miles Hundredlives") [^52]

At some point following the [[Catacendre - The Coppermind|Catacendre]], Autonomy decided that [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial's]] advancing technological development would be detrimental to her own goals.[^1] Autonomy's initial plan for dealing with this was the creation of [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set"), a secret society that resurrected the long-dead religion of [Trelagism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trelagism "Trelagism") as [Trellism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trellism "Trellism"). The leaders of the Set were not aware of Autonomy's true nature and intentions, knowing her as the male Trell and interacting with her through Trell's "Faceless Immortals".[^41]

While this was happening in the [[Physical Realm - The Coppermind|Physical Realm]], Autonomy began the process of Investing herself into Scadrial, taking advantage of [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony's]] conflicting intents to infiltrate the planet. Harmony could not understand it at the time, albeit he knew enough to visualize it to [Wax](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wax "Wax") as a choking, pressing red haze.[^53]

Set's original purpose appears to have been a takeover of the [Elendel Basin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Elendel_Basin "Elendel Basin"), installing themselves—and, by extension, Trell—as the rulers of Scadrial.[^54] However, the discovery of [[Southern Scadrian - The Coppermind|Southern Scadrians]] and their vastly more advanced technology led them to attempt to accelerate the timeline. Autonomy, on the other hand, decided to forego the plan to control Scadrial and opt for its complete destruction instead.[^41] For this reason, she eliminated Set's then-leader, [Edwarn Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Edwarn_Ladrian "Edwarn Ladrian"), and began to move her men of gold and red into Scadrian subastral of the [[Cognitive Realm - The Coppermind|Cognitive Realm]], intending a full-blown invasion.[^55]

Before this could happen, however, [Telsin Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Telsin_Ladrian "Telsin Ladrian") somehow managed to convince Autonomy to give the Set a chance to take over the planet again. Autonomy, ever appreciative of bold, ambitious actions, responded by Investing Telsin as her avatar. However, she also granted Telsin a deadline before which the world would have to find itself under Set's control, as on a specific date, the artificial [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]] she was creating to let her armies in would be opened.[^43]

At some point during this time, Autonomy's actions came under attention of the [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]], who began their work to stop her.[^56]

#### Bilming

> “ *It is not the bomb you should worry about. It is the destruction I have sent if that bomb fails.* ”
> 
> —Autonomy [^4]

Over the six years following the death of Edwarn Ladrian, the Set fully took control of the city of [Bilming](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bilming "Bilming") on behalf of Autonomy, up to developing it with accordance to Autonomy's philosophy and using Taldain architecture, particularly the [brutalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brutalist_architecture "wikipedia:Brutalist architecture") style.[^9] At the same time, they conducted underground tests in order to create a [[Trellium - The Coppermind|trellium]] - [[Harmonium - The Coppermind|harmonium]] bomb.[^1] The key part of the plan—and the part of it meant to impress Autonomy—was the delivery device: a self-propelled rocket, something that Autonomy herself was yet to figure out.[^27]

During this time, Autonomy finished the process of Investing herself into the planet. About a year before her deadline, she communicated with [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]], attempting a compromise: she would let Harmony peacefully depart Scadrial and Invest himself elsewhere, while she took control of Scadrial. Harmony rejected her offer, and was subsequently bound by Autonomy, blinding his future-sight. In response, Harmony sent his agents—primarily [Waxillium Ladrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Waxillium_Ladrian "Waxillium Ladrian") —to Bilming.[^1]

While in Bilming, Wax and his team, as well as a group of [[Ghostbloods - The Coppermind|Ghostbloods]], began to unravel Set's plan. Autonomy eventually chose to speak with Wax through a dying body, warning him about the impending invasion and encouraging him to prove his desire to protect Scadrial to her.[^4] Later that day, when Wax confronted Telsin, Harmony briefly disrupted Autonomy's link to her, causing Telsin to fall unconscious.[^27] When Telsin came to, Autonomy abandoned her willingly, having apparently given up on her and her plans.[^28]

After Wax and [Wayne](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Wayne "Wayne") destroyed the Set's bomb, while [[Marasi Colms - The Coppermind|Marasi]] destroyed the perpendicularity intended for the [men of gold and red](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Men_of_gold_and_red "Men of gold and red"), Autonomy begrudgingly withdrew from Scadrial, acknowledging their victory.[^57] However, this was only temporary—she fully intended to regroup and return in the future.[^36]

## Influence

### Religions

![[Autonomy---The-Coppermind-2.jpg]]

by [Fritz Casas](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Fritz_Casas "Fritz Casas")

The Sand Lord of [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]]

Autonomy is widely worshipped by many of [[17th Shard|cosmere's]] worlds and cultures. She—or, sometimes, he or they—appears in various forms and under various names throughout her many religions. In some places, there are entire pantheons of gods where every member is actually a different persona of her.[^29] [^9] Religions worshipping Autonomy include [[Ker'reen - The Coppermind|Ker'reen]] and [Los'seen](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Los%27seen "Los'seen") of [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]], which worships her under the male persona of [[Sand Lord - The Coppermind|Sand Lord]], the [Eelakin](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Eelakin "Eelakin") faith, which considers the island of [Patji](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Patji_\(island\) "Patji (island)"), the site of the Patji avatar, one of its gods, and the [Scadrian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Scadrian "Scadrian") faiths of [Trelagism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trelagism "Trelagism") and later [Trellism](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Trellism "Trellism"), in which she is known as the god Trell.[^58] [^8] [^59] [^9]

Autonomy will often seed these religions on various planets—including those already Invested by other [[Shards]]. These are seeds of influence she can later reactivate in order to exert her influence on the planet; for example, reactivating Trelagism as Trellism with the [Set](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Set "Set").[^9]

### Technological and Cultural Control

> “ *She claims she wants everyone to be individual. Gives them each a little house that is distinctive from the others, but only in a way that fits her plan, her desires. It's fake individualism. A corporate uniqueness. Like an advertisement telling people to go their own way, be their own person—by buying this product like everyone else.* ”
> 
> — [Moonlight](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Moonlight "Moonlight") [^9]

Autonomy has consistently shared her Shardic knowledge of advanced physics and technology with her people. This has led to [[Taldain - The Coppermind|Taldain]] becoming one of cosmere's most technologically-advanced worlds, and the fastest to advance.[^36] However, Autonomy has at the same time insisted on stopping Taldain from developing further than she desires, and has meddled in the planet's cultural and political development from ancient times, including manipulating specific individuals in an attempt to divide faith in the Sand Lord and destroy the [[Diem - The Coppermind|Diem]] should they prove unworthy to survive.[^8] [^32]

Presently, Autonomy has effectively isolated Taldain from the rest of the cosmere. As such, off-world travel to and from the planet has all but ceased.[^60] [^61] This also had the result of cutting Taldain [worldhoppers](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Worldhopper "Worldhopper") off from their own homeland; people like [Khriss](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Khriss "Khriss") and [[Baon - The Coppermind|Baon]] are virtual exiles.[^45] Autonomy is not, however, opposed to exporting Taldain's culture—or rather, the version of it she approves of—to other worlds. Places under Autonomy's control, like [Bilming](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Bilming "Bilming"), will eventually come to look similar to cities of Taldain, mimicking aspects such as layout and architectural style.[^9]

### Interplanetary Conflicts

> “ *Autonomy has access to … some very specialized troops. Hard to control. Dangerous to unleash.* ”
> 
> — [TwinSoul](https://coppermind.net/wiki/TwinSoul "TwinSoul") [^42]

Autonomy herself interferes directly in a number of cosmere's worlds.[^62] She might have aided Odium in killing and splintering [[Devotion - The Coppermind|Devotion]] and [[Dominion - The Coppermind|Dominion]] among other [[Shards]].[^63] [^48] She has been taking over multiple worlds through her avatars, with [[First of the Sun - The Coppermind|First of the Sun]], [[Obrodai - The Coppermind|Obrodai]] and [[Scadrial - The Coppermind|Scadrial]] being known to host her avatars at one point or another.[^35] [^64] On Scadrial in particular, through her persona of Trell, Autonomy can outright blind [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] and attempt to bring destruction to the planet as part of her plan to prevent the rise of Scadrial as an interstellar power.[^9] She appears to be influencing Roshar to an unknown degree.[^65]

When subterfuge proves insufficient, Autonomy has access to more forceful methods, as she commands an army known only as the [men of gold and red](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Men_of_gold_and_red "Men of gold and red").[^14] They are humanoid figures with golden skin and red eyes that give them their appellation, equipped with advanced firearms.[^55] [^10] Their full capabilities are unknown; while they specialize in widespread destruction, they are reportedly difficult to control, making unleashing them an irreversible act.[^42]
