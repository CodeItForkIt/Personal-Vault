## Endowment

> “ *It was agreed that we would not interfere with one another, and it disappoints me that so few of the Shards have kept to this original agreement.* ”
> 
> —Endowment [^2]

**Endowment** is one of the sixteen [[Shards|Shards of Adonalsium]]. The Shard's Vessel is [[Edgli - The Coppermind|Edgli]].[^1] She is located on [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthis]], and is the only Shard present there.[^3]

## Intent

Endowment's [[Shards|Intent]] is not entirely clear; it's likely related to bestowing gifts or talents upon others. The Intent does not seem to influence [[Edgli - The Coppermind|Edgli]] in all matters, as she is still capable of refusing giving aid to [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]].[^4] The prime examples of the Intent's influence are the creation of the Returned, via resurrection and a Returned Breath, and the magic system of [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]], as [Breath](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Breath "Breath") can only be given, and cannot be forcefully taken.[^5] Furthermore, it is said to be Endowment's nature to give gifts without strings attached.[^6]

The way in which she manifests her Intent is notably erratic and inconsistent.[^7] [^8]

Endowment is the Shard Hoid might have taken, had he wanted to become a Vessel.[^9]

## Associated Magic

### Breath and Awakening

Nalthis' only known magic system is [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]] -- the use of [Breath](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Breath "Breath"), Endowment's [[Investiture - The Coppermind|Investiture]] in its gaseous form, to enact a variety of effects, typically tied to animating inanimate objects. Every Nalthian is born with a single Breath, and can bestow it onto other people or objects. Giving away one's Breath makes one into a Drab, which makes a person prone to depression and sickness.[^10] By contrast, holding more Breath grants a variety of benefits, the number of which increases with the number of Breaths held; the tiers of acquiring new abilities are called [[Heightening - The Coppermind|Heightenings]].[^11]

Breaths are bestowed upon others with [Commands](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Command "Command"). The primary command is "My life to yours. My Breath become yours." However, hundreds of other Commands can be used to bestow Breath onto inanimate objects or dead bodies, Awakening them. Awakened objects perform a specific task, dictated by their Commands, which can range from simple, such as "hold when thrown" to complex, such as "fetch keys".[^12] The more complex the Command, the more Breath it requires.[^13] Additionally, animating objects with Breath requires color; a nearby source of vivid color is required, and will be bleached, in order for the Command to take effect.[^13]

Breaths can also be used to manipulate and store memories, although specifics of how are unclear.[^14] [^15]

### Divine Breath and the Returned

> “ *He remembered a voice, calm and comforting, offering him an opportunity. To Return.* ”
> 
> — [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]] [^16]

Endowment can create [[Splinter - The Coppermind|splinters]] of herself called divine Breath, and bestow them upon the recently deceased.[^17] This brings them back to life; such people are known as the [[Returned - The Coppermind|Returned]]. The Returned have little to no memory of their previous life, but they do retain any skills they've had while alive.[^18] [^19] Their appearance also changes, in accordance to how they perceive themselves and the influence of local culture on their image of a "god". With some skill, the Returned can change their appearance to some extent, though it is not true shapeshifting.[^20]

The divine Breath immediately grants the Returned the effects of the Fifth [[Heightening - The Coppermind|Heightening]]. However, the Returned must consume at least one Breath a week; otherwise, after their divine Breath is consumed and, with nothing to support their existence, they die.[^21] Endowment does not discourage this, and is fine with her Breath being used to feed the Returned.[^22]

They can also choose to bestow their divine Breath upon others, which typically results in a massive feat of power, often tied to healing, though it will kill the Returned.[^23] Giving up their divine Breath is often the reason they were Returned in the first place -- a potential Returned is shown a vision of the future by Endowment, and chooses to come back based on that, although the memory of it fades away until it is needed.[^16]

### The Tears of Edgli

The [Tears of Edgli](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Tears_of_Edgli "Tears of Edgli") are a special flower that grows only in [[Hallandren - The Coppermind|Hallandren]], near Endowment's [[Perpendicularity - The Coppermind|perpendicularity]].[^24] [^25] They come in a variety of colors and make for extremely good dyes; they are also more efficient when used as a source of color for [[Awakening - The Coppermind|Awakening]].[^26] This is because, having grown near the perpendicularity, the Tears contain some of Endowment's Investiture seeping out of the ground.[^25]

### Other

Endowment has some capacity to perceive the future and bestow those visions upon others; she communes with people as a disembodied voice to do so.[^16] She also has the ability to smite people on her planet.[^27] Perhaps related to this, she appears to have keen awareness of who enters and leaves Nalthis; she can detect [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid's]] presence while other [[Shards]] have trouble spotting him on their own worlds.[^28]

## History

> “ *If Rayse becomes an issue, he will be dealt with. And so will you.* ”

Like all [[Shards]], Endowment was created at the [[Shattering - The Coppermind|Shattering]] of [[Adonalsium - The Coppermind|Adonalsium]], and contains one sixteenth of him.[^29] The people present at the Shattering agreed to go separate ways after taking their Shards.[^2] And so, when Endowment was picked up by [[Edgli - The Coppermind|Edgli]], she took it from [[Yolen - The Coppermind|Yolen]] and eventually settled on [[Nalthis - The Coppermind|Nalthis]].[^1] [^30] There, she would hold to the Shards' pact of isolation, content in granting blessings and divine power to the people of Nalthis -- something her mortal self was long denied.[^31]

Millennia later, the people of [Chedesh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Chedesh "Chedesh") landed on the shores of the land that now houses [[Hallandren - The Coppermind|Hallandren]]. It was then that Endowment first created a Returned in [Vo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vo "Vo"), who managed to have five Visions and father a child before dying a week later. From then on out, Returned began appearing in other parts of the world.[^32] Among others, Endowment Returned the [[Five Scholars - The Coppermind|Five Scholars]], including [[Vasher - The Coppermind|Vasher]] and [Shashara](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shashara "Shashara"), who together created [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 19|Nightblood]], and [[Lightsong - The Coppermind|Lightsong]], who saved the Hallandren from a continent-wide war by restoring the power of [[Susebron - The Coppermind|Susebron]], the god-king.[^33] [^16]

At an unknown point in time, Endowment received a request from [[Valor - The Coppermind|Valor]] for aid in an unknown matter, to which Endowment acquiesced and provided aid without meeting Valor in person.[^34] [^35]

Around the time of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 2|True Desolation]] on [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], [[Hoid - The Coppermind|Hoid]] began contacting various Shards in an attempt to build a resistance to [[Odium - The Coppermind|Odium]], which was then held by [[Rayse - The Coppermind|Rayse]]. Endowment, still annoyed at the Vessels who chose to settle down in pairs in violation of the original pact, declined.[^2] Believing Rayse to be of little concern, she chided Hoid for dabbling in matters far outside his scope, and suggested that if Odium were to become an issue, he would be dealt with promptly -- as would Hoid himself.[^36]

Some time later, [[Harmony - The Coppermind|Harmony]] contacted Endowment at Hoid's behest. She was willing to communicate, though it's unknown what she told him.[^37] Hoid would later contact Endowment again, seeking information on Valor's location and her dealings with Endowment.[^34] [^38] Endowment refused, maintaining that she had her own plans to deal with Odium.[^31] [^39] She further claimed that Valor had no desire to speak to Hoid again despite Harmony's claims, and that she and Valor are the only Shards who still keep to isolation despite their past dealings.[^40]

## Influence

> “ *By the Colors!* ”
> 
> — [Nalthian](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Nalthian "Nalthian") expression [^19]

![[Endowment---The-Coppermind-1.png]]

by [Heatherly](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Coppermind:Artists/Heatherly "Coppermind:Artists/Heatherly")

Despite being Nalthis' only Shard, the planet's inhabitants seem by and large unaware of her existence, even through religion. Of the known cultures, only the [[Pahn Kahl - The Coppermind|Pahn Kahl]] have any knowledge of her; and even then, she is known only as the voice that brings back the Returned.[^41] While the people of [[Idris - The Coppermind|Idris]] are monotheistic, their god, [Austre](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Austre "Austre"), is not based on Endowment.[^42]

Regardless, she is capable of influencing Nalthis through her Returned. She communicates with select few dead as a disembodied voice, and presents them with chosen visions of the future before asking them to Return and act on this vision. Although the Returned come back amnesiac, their memories do come back when needed.[^16] Through this, Endowment can indirectly steer the course of history; most notably, Returning [Vo](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Vo "Vo") caused the creation of [[Hallandren - The Coppermind|Hallandren]] and Returning [Shashara](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Shashara "Shashara") led to the creation of [[The Coppermind Wiki - 17th Shard, the Official Brandon Sanderson Fansite 19|Nightblood]]. Endowment also had some additional level of involvement in Nightblood's creation, above simply her Investiture being used.[^43]

Endowment appears to be uninterested in reaching beyond the planet and meddling with the greater [[17th Shard|cosmere]], being content in keeping to Nalthis and granting its people her divine blessings.[^44] [^31] Despite this, she was willing to give aid to [[Valor - The Coppermind|Valor]] when asked, in a manner that did not require her to go to Valor in person, thus technically maintaining isolation.[^35]

## Trivia

- If Brandon were to become a Vessel, Endowment is one of the Shards he has said he might choose.[^45]
- In *[The Well of Ascension](https://coppermind.net/wiki/The_Well_of_Ascension "The Well of Ascension")* 's original ending, Endowment was one of many Shards which saved [[Sazed - The Coppermind|Sazed]] from [Marsh](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Marsh "Marsh").[^46]
- In *[Magic: The Gathering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic:_The_Gathering "wikipedia:Magic: The Gathering")* colors, Endowment would be mono-green.[^47]
