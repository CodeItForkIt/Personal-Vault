## Silver Kingdoms

The **Silver Kingdoms**, or the **Epoch Kingdoms**, are an ancient coalition of nations on the planet of [[Roshar - The Coppermind|Roshar]], dating back to the era of the [[Knights Radiant - The Coppermind|Knights Radiant]].[^1] [^2]

## Overview

The era when the Silver Kingdoms existed is called the **Silver Kingdoms Epoch** [^2] or the **Heraldic Epochs**. There were ten kingdoms, each of which had a perfectly palindromic name - a reflection of the holiness of symmetry that can be found in [[Vorinism - The Coppermind|Vorinism]]. However, it's more than likely that those names predate the rise of Vorin religion.[^1]

Each country had its own purpose within the war against the [[Voidbringer - The Coppermind|Voidbringers]], likely preordained by the [Heralds](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Herald "Herald") - for example, [[Alethela - The Coppermind|Alethela]] was the "protector state", set up to preserve the arts of war and train people in case of a [[Desolation - The Coppermind|Desolation]].[^1] All ten states had their own [[Oathgate - The Coppermind|Oathgates]] within their capital cities, connecting them to the Knight Radiant tower city of [[Urithiru - The Coppermind|Urithiru]].[^3] Within Urithiru, each of the states is said to have had a throne set up for its ruler.[^4]

In the modern era, most of the Silver Kingdoms have splintered, evolved into successor states that lack continuity of government with their ancient antecedents, or ceased to exist entirely, with [[Shin Kak Nish - The Coppermind|Shin Kak Nish]] (now called [[Shinovar - The Coppermind|Shinovar]]) and perhaps [[Aimia - The Coppermind|Aimia]] being the only known exceptions (although the [[Scouring of Aimia - The Coppermind|Scouring of Aimia]] makes its current status unclear). Most, like [[Makabakam - The Coppermind|Makabakam]], have splintered into a number of smaller states, while [[Natanatan - The Coppermind|Natanatan]] has fallen completely, having been reduced to a small number of stateless settlements and a lone city-state of [[New Natanan - The Coppermind|New Natanan]]. However, the modern cultural, ethnic and linguistic make-up of Roshar still reflects that of those ancient kingdoms, and many current borders lie along similar lines to those of old. Many (though not all) of the splinter states that were once Makabakam give nominal fealty to the ruler of [Azir](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Azir "Azir"), but these states are not truly united in a political sense. Alethela splintered at some time in the past before eventually being reunited into [Alethkar](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Alethkar "Alethkar") under the [[Sadees - The Coppermind|Sunmaker]]. [[Valhav - The Coppermind|Valhav]] appears to have undergone a similar process, ultimately succeeded in modern times by [Jah Keved](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Jah_Keved "Jah Keved"). The Silver Kingdom of Iri has been divided into two nations, modern [[Iri - The Coppermind|Iri]] and [Rira](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Rira_\(country\) "Rira (country)"); it is unknown whether one or both of these states is continuous with the ancient kingdom. [[Rishir - The Coppermind|Rishir]] has been divided into [[Herdaz - The Coppermind|Herdaz]] and the many kingdoms of the [Reshi Isles](https://coppermind.net/wiki/Reshi_Isles "Reshi Isles"), while its capital of Kurth is now part of Rira. [[Thalath - The Coppermind|Thalath's]] continental territory is largely ungoverned in the modern age, though it does contain the city state of [[Kharbranth - The Coppermind|Kharbranth]], but the island nation of [[Thaylenah - The Coppermind|Thaylenah]] appears to be a successor state of some sort. Like Makabakam, the ancient kingdom of [[Sela Tales - The Coppermind|Sela Tales]] is divided in modern times into several nations; little is known about their relationships to each other or to the old kingdom.[^5]
