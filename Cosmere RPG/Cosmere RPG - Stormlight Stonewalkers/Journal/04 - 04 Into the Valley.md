# 04: Into the Valley

## Chapter Summary

In Rathalas, the Gray Remnant revealed that Ylt and his acolytes are sailing for the Valley of the Nightwatcher to ask a boon of the legendary spren. In this chapter, Ylt reveals his true desire in the ancient grove: to supplant the Herald Pailiah. The Nightwatcher responds by providing a dangerous artifact the party must wrest from Ylt.

## Running This Chapter

The chapter opens with the party sailing to Greater Hexi, chasing Ylt and his band of zealots. First, the PCs encounter the Everstorm as it sweeps across Roshar. Then they engage in a longer-term endeavor that ultimately determines the difficulty of the fight with Ylt’s rearguard.

Before the party follows Ylt’s trail into the Valley of the Nightwatcher, familiarize yourself with the [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Visions of Tomorrow|Visions of Tomorrow]] section. In this scene, each PC experiences a different prophetic vision induced by the Valley, either foreshadowing future events or addressing their personal regrets, fears, or obstacles.

In the center of the Nightwatcher’s grove, a pitched battle between Ylt, Kaiana, and the party unfolds to determine the fate of a raysium knife—a weapon capable of killing a Herald. Ylt plans to supplant the Herald Pailiah, but the PCs can thwart him either by force or by winning Kaiana as an ally.

After the battle, the party has the chance to make a deal with the enigmatic Nightwatcher. Her curses and boons are tailored to each individual, so familiarize yourself with the [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#A Boon and a Curse|A Boon and a Curse]] section and the suggested examples in the Boons and Curses table at the end of this chapter.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 4th level and advance to 5th level after leaving the Valley.

The following decisions offer opportunities to introduce spren, strengthen spren bonds, or swear Ideals:

- A PC’s actions to save their ship and its crew during the Everstorm.

- Whether the PCs save Axoq.

- Whether the PCs convince Kaiana to switch sides.

- How the PCs confront Ylt and speak with the Nightwatcher.

Characters with goals related to learning more about the Heralds, Honorblades, or Radiants have opportunities to do so when experiencing the Nightwatcher’s visions, or when speaking with the Nightwatcher, Kaiana, and Ylt.

## Setting Sail

Now free of Ylt’s grip, Teryn’s remaining loyalist bandits are keen to help the party stop Ylt. Teryn or Ubo takes the characters to a hidden harbor on the banks of the Sea of Spears, accessible from the carpenter’s yard. Although Ylt’s remaining zealots left with the newer of the harbor’s two ships, the remaining ship—affectionately dubbed the *Storm’s Jest*—is more seaworthy than she looks. Teryn offers to lend the Storm’s Jest and its small crew to the party so they can apprehend Ylt.

## New Allies

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Ubo|Ubo]]** is the first to volunteer to help the PCs crew the ship. He knows these waterways well from his time as a ship’s quartermaster. If the party approaches any other NPCs to join them at sea, most are easy to convince. However, Teryn wishes to stay and lead the bandits, and Axies plans to continue learning from the Gray Remnant.

Ubo grabs additional ship hands by inviting carpentry yard workers. The final crew should include Ubo, four additional NPCs—either invited by the party or Ubo’s workers—and two enslaved singers called **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Tam|Tam]]** (commoner, he/him) and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Veda|Veda]]** (commoner, she/her). Like most singers in Roshar at this time, Tam and Veda are trapped in slaveform; the bandits put them to work after capturing them during a raid on a merchant caravan.

Before leaving, Teryn takes the party aside to share her suspicion that Kaiana, Ylt’s second-in-command, might not be as loyal to Ylt as he believes.

## The Everstorm

The PCs know that Ylt is traveling to Hexi to request a boon from the Nightwatcher, a legendary spren who grants boons but always tempers them with a curse. With an older ship and lost time, it’ll be no easy feat to catch up to Ylt and his Eyes of Pala acolytes. However, Po’ahu urges them to try. With Ubo’s sailing prowess, the party may be able to catch Ylt and interrupt his plans, even if it takes weeks. Unbeknownst to the party, the Everstorm—a new storm wrought by Odium’s ascendance in Roshar—threatens to throw them off course.

## Writhing Clouds

Several days into the journey, on the wide river between the Sea of Spears and the city of Karanak, the characters notice dark clouds on the horizon. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A clap of thunder booms across the gray sky, echoing from the west. Ubo calls a few sharp commands to the crew.\
>      “Furl the sails! A storm is coming!” His shouts turn to a whisper, and confusionspren expand around him. “A storm… from the west?!”\
>      A wet thud makes Ubo jump as something falls from the sky to the deck, landing at his feet.\
>      “What in the storming world—” It’s a gasping, three-foot-long skyeel.

The PCs have a few moments to react. If they don’t look around at the horizon, the ship’s lookout shouts, pointing to the west.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> From a distance, what looked like a cloud now surges into view, moving fast toward the ship. Thousands of skyeels twist and weave over each other, forming a mass of writhing bodies and gnashing teeth. The swarm of bodies is moments away from reaching the ship.

All PCs not behind cover must succeed on a DC 10 Agility test or take 2 (`1d4`) impact damage as the brunt of the swarm sweeps across the deck.

A PC who succeeds on a DC 12 Survival test notices that the flying skyeels’ movements are erratic and driven as if fleeing something from the west.

The panic makes the largest skyeels especially violent. Two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Greater Skyeel|greater skyeels]]** and two normal **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Skyeel|skyeels]]** attack. On their first turn, they Dive Bomb (2) the deck, their air pouches deflating. Once on the deck, they fight as cornered prey.

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Ubo|Ubo]]** (bandit) joins the PCs in their fight against the skyeels, and he warns the PCs about the ship’s deteriorating condition as it’s battered by the greater skyeels (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Battlefield Effects|Battlefield Effects]]). The other crew seek refuge belowdecks, unprepared for the intensity of this attack.

### Battlefield Effects

Fighting on board a ship in rough waters is no easy feat. The following effects are active during the combat:

- **Skyeel Panic.** The greater skyeels thrash during their attack, rending woodwork and threatening to break clean through the deck. Whenever a greater skyeel uses Bite (1) on deck, including their initial Dive Bomb, they deal 9 (`1d6 + 5`) impact damage to the ship. Describe the damage the skyeels wreak and keep track of the ship’s damage total, as this affects its ability to survive the Everstorm in the next section.

- **Swaying Deck.** At the end of each round, all characters on the deck of the ship must make a DC 10 Agility test. On a failed test, a character is knocked Prone and falls 10 feet to the left or right side of the ship (alternating directions each round). Characters can choose to fail this test, such as a skyeel choosing to fail in order to remain within reach of their target. The railings on the deck’s edge prevent anyone from falling off, but hitting the railing deals 2 (`1d4`) impact damage to both the character and the ship.

- **Skyeel Swarm.** A new skyeel arrives at the end of each round of combat.

### Aftermath

The skyeels continue to sweep across the deck until both greater skyeels are killed. The ship may have sustained significant damage during combat, but the Everstorm approaches, and the sailors have no chance to make repairs. Immediately proceed to [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#A New Storm|A New Storm]].

## A New Storm

In defiance of all known laws of nature in Roshar, a cruel storm approaches from the west rather than the east. There is no moment to take a breath, and no time to plan. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The western sky plunges into shadow as a black storm seethes eastward. Unlike the highstorms you’re accustomed to, this roiling mass of clouds is streaked with violent red lightning. Though the stormwall carries no debris or crem as it surges across the river, it descends with a biting chill, a sea of smoke, and choking ash.\
>      Confused and panicked by the incoming calamity, the sailors have no time to prepare before the storm crashes against the ship.

![[pg078_everstorm at sea_JEFF BROWN.webp]]

**As the Storm’s Jest sets sail, the Everstorm strikes.** **-** **JEFF BROWN**

Run the scene with the Everstorm as an endeavor with life-and-death stakes (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). The characters must fight the winds and lightning to save themselves, their ship, and the sailors aboard the *Storm’s Jest*.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Emerging Powers
> During the Everstorm endeavor, budding Radiants might try to use their new Surgebinding skills to save the crew or one another. Set DCs based on the circumstances, giving an advantage on tests that are aligned with that Radiant’s Ideals.

Depending on how much damage the ship took during the skyeel battle, this endeavor might begin with 1 or more failures, as shown on the Ship Integrity table.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Ship Integrity]]

### Everstorm Hazards

The party’s approaches to this endeavor should be guided by the hazards of the storm. Before each contribution, present one of the following hazards for the PCs to respond to:

- **Lightning Strike.** Red lightning tears through the clouds. A PC might avoid an area likely to be struck (DC 14 Deduction), dive for cover (DC 15 Agility), or build a lightning rod (DC 12 Crafting).

- **Powerful Winds.** Heavy gusts threaten to blow the crew off deck. A PC might tie themself to the ship (DC 13 Thievery), hold on for dear life (DC 14 Athletics), or watch for a calm pocket in the squall (DC 15 Survival).

- **Sailors Overboard.** The crew are in danger of being lost in the swell. A PC might steer the ship to pick them up (DC 13 Agility), organize the crew to tie and brace a rope line (DC 13 Leadership), or swim out to save them (DC 15 Athletics).

- **Capsizing Ship.** A vast wave threatens to turn over the Storm’s Jest. A PC might heave the ship’s helm toward the wave (DC 14 Athletics), release the snagged mainsail (DC 14 Thievery), or order the crew to gather all weight on the stormward side (DC 13 Leadership). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

- **Breaking Mast.** A lightning strike shatters part of the mast, threatening to bring it down. A character might quickly reinforce it (DC 15 Crafting), throw a barrel of water over the burning wood (DC 13 Athletics), or bring the mast down in a controlled way (DC 14 Lore). If a PC has trouble deciding what to do, the mast might break apart, forcing them to dive to safety (DC 15 Agility).

During these hazards, the characters might notice wails of agony from the singers Tam and Veda—a reaction to the enormity of their plight as they awaken. See the upcoming [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Awakening|Awakening]] section.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Everstorm Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Everstorm Opportunities and Complications]]

### Voidspren

Singer characters are approached by a strange spren who moves through the air like lightning, but who coalesces into a red humanoid figure while at rest. Roil (she/her) is an impulsive Voidspren seeking singers to bless with forms of power while attempting to turn them toward Odium’s influence. If a singer PC expresses interest, they can add the goal “Achieve a form of power.” If they do so, Roil follows them after the storm, appearing only to that PC and encouraging their more spontaneous instincts. See the “Singer Ancestry” section of chapter 2 in the *Stormlight Handbook* for more information on forms of power.

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 6 successes before 4 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective. Once the PCs achieve their final success or failure, the worst of the Everstorm finally passes.

**Success.** Though the lives of the sailors hang in the balance, the Everstorm’s fury lessens and finally passes. The waters calm and the skies open once more. However, even in success, the Everstorm may have taken its toll. For each failure accrued during the endeavor, one of the crewmembers dies, likely crushed by debris or drowned.

**Failure.** Everyone aboard the Storm’s Jest suffers greatly during the unexpected storm. They’re crushed against the ship, nearly drowned in churning waves, or even struck by lightning. Ubo dies along with three human crew (however, both singers should survive, as they appear in the next scene). All PCs suffer an injury and must make an injury roll to determine its duration. To reflect that each PC may have suffered in a different way, generate random injuries using the Injury Effects table in chapter 9 of the *Stormlight Handbook*.

**Catastrophic Failure.** If the endeavor fails with no successes at all, you might consider an even worse outcome: The Storm’s Jest is destroyed and its crew is lost. The PCs can wash up near Karanak, adding days to their journey and adding 2 failures to their next endeavor. Although this outcome is not detailed here, you could follow with an endeavor focusing on the party’s survival in the wilderness before moving onward. A budding Elsecaller or Lightweaver might need to have a frank discussion with a stick…

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> A PC who bravely faces the storm and risks their life to help the crew (rather than themself) attracts the attention of an honorspren.

## Awakening

Whether or not the previous endeavor succeeded, it soon becomes clear that some members of the ship’s crew have changed.

Across Roshar, the passing of the Everstorm brings with it an awakening for the singer people, and the dawning of a new age. All at once, the singers finally cast off what they know as slaveform and reconnect with their suppressed identity. On the *Storm’s Jest*, the singers **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Tam|Tam]]** and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Veda|Veda]]** are visited by that same gift. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the harsh winds and waves of the storm finally pass, you find the two parshmen of the ship’s crew in a close embrace, laughing, crying, and speaking to one another with quick, hushed tones. The human crew stare in confusion at their unexpected expressiveness.\
>      One of the pair leans down to lift a cudgel left discarded on the deck. Her hands tremble as she levels it toward the other crew. The other calls out so all the crew can hear him:\
>      “We’re taking the life raft. That’s all. We’re leaving.”

Both Tam and Veda have adopted workform.

If all the PCs are human, they must succeed on a DC 12 Persuasion test to speak at length with the singer pair. On a failed test, Tam and Veda remain nervous given their precarious position, wary of how the crew of the *Storm’s Jest* will react to their awakening. They try to leave immediately, using the chaos of the storm’s aftermath to slip away before anyone can muster the strength to object. On a successful test, Tam and Veda trust the characters enough to talk about what’s happened. They introduce themselves, express anguish at their years of enslavement, and describe their reconnection to their identities after a lifetime of dreamlike awareness.

If any of the PCs are singers, Tam and Veda invite them to an intimate celebration of their new selves. This might include sharing a meal together or singing with rediscovered rhythms. Singers who are moved by Tam and Veda’s awakening gain the Determined condition.

Regardless of how well any of the crew or PCs try to convince them, Tam and Veda soon leave the *Storm’s Jest* to find freedom on their own terms.

## Pursuit of Truth

With the passing of the first Everstorm, the remaining crew members regroup, frightened. They’re unsure what the arrival of the deadly new storm means and are intent on reaching the next port, Karanak.

If characters suffered injuries, flesh wounds heal before the ship reaches Karanak. All other injuries last through the journey across the Tarat Sea.

## Karanak

It takes fifteen days to reach Karanak, a port city in the Bethab princedom of Alethkar. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Thin trails of smoke stretch into the sky as the port city of Karanak comes into view. Even from a distance, the storm’s destruction is clear. The buildings on the entire western side of the city are ripped in two, many little more than rubble. Near the city’s center, the cracked pillars of a large Vorin temple point at odd angles upward, like exposed bones through its collapsed roof. Thankfully, as the docks come into view, it’s clear the city is still inhabited and abuzz with activity.

The city, usually rich from trade with Jah Keved and Kharbranth, was badly damaged in the Everstorm. Karanak’s citizens are recovering from the disaster and are wary of strangers. The ship’s bandit crew is similarly cautious about staying in the settlement for too long.

If the party stays for more than a day in Karanak, each additional day counts as 1 failure on the upcoming Following Ylt endeavor (maximum of 2 failures). Po’ahu becomes increasingly agitated about stopping Ylt if the party doesn’t leave the city quickly.

The crew informs the party that the *Storm’s Jest* is in dire need of repairs. Depending on how many of the ship’s crew were lost in the Everstorm, the ship also might need up to four more sailors. If the PCs choose to leave Karanak without a repaired ship or a full crew, this makes the [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Following Ylt|Following Ylt]] endeavor more difficult.

### Singers in Karanak

Much like Tam and Veda, the enslaved singer population awoke during the Everstorm and left the city. The Karanak populace—now all human—are deeply suspicious of singers, and while a singer PC is present, all social tests gain a disadvantage.

### Repairs

If the Everstorm endeavor succeeded, then the repairs needed are minor and can be completed by the bandit crew. If the Everstorm endeavor failed, either the party must pay 200 marks for additional labor and supplies, or a PC must spend a day in Karanak repairing the ship and succeed on a DC 15 Crafting test.

### Hiring Sailors

A PC can hire a sailor for 30 marks by succeeding on a DC 15 Leadership test (making a separate test for each sailor). The DC is reduced by 5 for every 30 marks extra they agree to pay for the journey to Hexi.

### Shopping

PCs can purchase equipment and non-special weapons found in chapter 7 of the *Stormlight Handbook*. No other businesses are open as citizens rebuild after the storm.

### Asking Questions

The locals are wary, but if the PCs have the Alethi expertise, treat them kindly, or grease the conversation with marks, any local can answer the following questions:

- **What is known about the strange storm?** “The stormwardens are as confused as us lot, saying it’s a new storm. Spanreeds from elsewhere say all of Roshar has seen it, and some have had it worse. It’s destroyed whole cities.”

- **What happened to the singers?** “The parshmen all fled on the night of that unholy storm, eyes glowing like Voidbringers. We haven’t seen any since.”

- **Have you seen individuals with green glyphs or a boat like ours?** “Yes, just a couple of days ago, there was a boat with a torn sail that was fixed in the harbor. The crew, ’round about nine of them, all had their faces painted green. They were a strange lot, but they paid well, and storms know we need the marks.”

## Following Ylt

Once the characters are finished in Karanak, run the race to catch Ylt in Greater Hexi as an endeavor, abridging the weeks-long journey across the Tarat Sea, from Karanak to the highlands of Greater Hexi. The goal of the endeavor is to make the journey as fast as possible and catch Ylt’s Eyes of Pala acolytes unprepared (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]).

![[pg082_Map for Stonewalkers_ISAAC STEWART.webp]]

**The party’s route from Rathalas to Greater Hexi** **-** **ISAAC STEWART**

Treat the endeavor as a montage and briefly narrate each test’s result, advancing westward after each success. If the *Storm’s Jest* is still damaged or doesn’t have a full crew, PCs gain a disadvantage on tests related to sailing or other activities on the ship. Potential approaches vary with each stage of the journey:

- **Tarat Sea.** Out on the open seas, a PC might chart a course following documented currents (DC 15 Lore), read the winds and weather (DC 14 Perception), or bolster supplies by fishing and hunting marine life (DC 14 Survival). They also might survey the decks to make a list of concerns (DC 13 Deduction), make the necessary repairs (DC 13 Crafting), or organize the party into a shipshape crew (DC 13 Leadership).

- **Storm in Triax.** As the *Storm’s Jest* swings by the coast of Triax, the crew are forced to shelter from a highstorm. The best refuge is a sailors’ stormshelter, but the tiny outpost is a den for pirates and deserters. Here, PCs might ward off unspoken threats from others in the shelter (DC 16 Intimidation), carouse to make dubious friends (DC 15 Persuasion), or creep about for signs of Ylt’s spies (DC 15 Stealth). PCs can recharge their spheres during the highstorm, and singers can change forms (see “Singer Ancestry” in chapter 2 of the *Stormlight Handbook*).

- **Shores of Hexi.** Finally, the party must make their landing at the rainswept shores of Greater Hexi. A PC might discover where Ylt docked his ship (DC 15 Perception), quickly scout ahead for useful information (DC 15 Athletics), or follow the Eyes of Pala’s tracks (DC 15 Survival).

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 3 successes before 3 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective. With the final success or failure, the PCs finds themselves in Greater Hexi, hot on Ylt’s trail.

- **Success.** The party makes swift progress on their journey and catches Ylt’s rearguard unprepared.

- **Failure.** The party makes slow progress across Hexi, and Ylt’s rearguard ambushes the characters.

## Rearguard

Regardless of how the previous endeavor concluded, the party’s long journey is over, and they finally catch up to the Eyes of Pala in the frozen trenches of Hexi (see [[Scenes Index|map 4.1]]). Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stone, gravel, and a thin layer of snow crunch underfoot as the rains of the riddens freeze in the cool southern air. Coldspren occasionally grip nearby surfaces like pristine spikes. Here, the desolate lowlands are cut with wrinkles. Some of the trenches are only inches deep, while others rise and fall over several feet. Slippery lichen clings to their stormward sides, while frosted grass adorns the leeward edges.

Ylt has left a rearguard force of his loyal Eyes of Pala acolytes behind while he and Kaiana search the Valley. The squad consists of three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Agent|Eyes of Pala agents]]**, two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]**, and two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]**. The soldiers are aware they are likely to be followed and are ready to turn their weapons on anyone they meet—but their readiness depends on how the party fared on the [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Following Ylt|Following Ylt]] endeavor.

![[pg083_Map 4-1_Hexi Trenches_FERNANDO SALVATERRA.webp]]

**Map 4.1: Hexi Trenches** **-** **FERNANDO SALVATERRA**

### Successful Endeavor

If the previous endeavor succeeded, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Within the deepest trench of the undulating landscape, the steady light of spheres peeks between the folds of hogshide tents. Several green-clad soldiers are hunched into the leeward side, sharing a meal of grain rations. Their faces are painted with the green glyph of Ylt’s contingent. They haven’t yet noticed your approach.

The Eyes of Pala acolytes are distracted and the party can make their move. A PC who succeeds on a DC 14 Stealth test can approach the camp undetected.

### Failed Endeavor

If the previous endeavor failed, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A sudden war cry sounds across the lowlands, and a squad of soldiers burst from hiding places in the deepest trenches, their blades raised in attack. Their faces are painted with a familiar green glyph.

The party is ambushed, and each PC gains the Surprised condition until the end of their first turn.

### Axoq

The Eyes of Pala have imprisoned an Emuli medic named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Axoq|Axoq]]** (expert, he/him) in their camp. He is in the westmost tent. If the PCs approach undetected, see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Saving Axoq|Saving Axoq]].

![[pg084_Axoq_ESTHER SCHRADER.webp]]

**ESTHER SCHRADER**

Otherwise, at the start of the second fast NPC turn, Axoq emerges from the tent, using the fighting as a distraction while he flees north. He is Slowed due to being bound. If the PCs failed the Following Ylt endeavor, Axoq is also Exhausted [-2] from his imprisonment. While the rearguard is most concerned with fighting the PCs, they attempt to violently apprehend Axoq.

> [!example] Roleplaying Axoq
> **Characteristics:** Nostalgic, botanically knowledgeable, soft-spoken.
>
> **Goal:** Do his part for the Emuli war effort.
>
> **Appearance:** Axoq (“AX-ock”; he/him) is a willowy Emuli human with dark skin and a colorful wrap tied around his grey hair.
>
> Axoq is quick to identify various plants while traveling and explains their applications, occasionally pocketing a clipping. He is deeply superstitious of the Valley, as the plants there “grow wrong.” In quiet moments, he shares that he’s homesick for a little house by a peca orchard, a house that the Tukari destroyed.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> If a PC makes every effort to save the life of the Emuli medic Axoq, they may attract the attention of a cultivationspren.

### Battlefield Effects

The Eyes of Pala acolytes have camped (or set their ambush) in a place where the wrinkles of the Hexi landscape are particularly deep. The following effects are active during this combat:

**Deep Trenches.** Characters who squat in the trenches have enough cover to use the Brace (1) action.

**Slippery Lichen.** The east-facing sides of the trenches are difficult to cross. While moving across the algae, a character must succeed on a DC 10 Agility test or their movement rate is reduced by half until the end of that turn, and they fall Prone after completing their movement.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Hexi Trenches Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Hexi Trenches Opportunities and Complications]]

### Aftermath

When the rearguard is defeated, the party can search the camp, speak with Axoq, and question any survivors. A PC can interrogate one of Ylt’s rearguard by succeeding on an Intimidation or Persuasion test against the acolyte’s Spiritual defense. On a success, the acolyte reveals that Ylt and Kaiana have headed onwards through the foothills to the Valley. They also reveal the direction in which the two went.

If the party doesn’t glean this information from the rearguard, Axoq previously overheard it and can direct the PCs.

## Saving Axoq

If Axoq survives the skirmish, he thanks the PCs and explains he was a medic for an Emuli patrol embroiled in the Emuli–Tukari conflict (described in [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|The Eighty’s War]] in chapter 5). He crossed the mountains with his comrades in search of medicinal ingredients. Ylt’s rearguard killed most of the Emuli soldiers and pressed Axoq for information about the Valley’s location.

Axoq offers the PCs some of the medicines he has gathered: five doses of the ***anesthetic*** winterwort.

Axoq also explains that his patrol was en route to an important fort called Ashiqqil Keep that the Tukari recently captured. He asks to accompany the PCs in that direction.

## The Valley of the Nightwatcher

Once the party leaves the Eyes of Pala’s rearguard camp, they can head northwest into the foothills of the mountains between Greater Hexi and Emul. Here lies the Valley where the Nightwatcher makes her home. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Among the snowy foothills of the Hexi mountains lies the Valley of the Nightwatcher. The land can barely contain the wildlife within; twisting vines and gnarled trees tangle up to a thick canopy while ferns and grasses war for space underneath. Moss and snaking vines choke the surface, and lifespren dance weightless along the wilds.\
>      Within the wall of foliage is a dark passage into the forest. The air here is hot and thick compared to the chill of the mountains. Intermittent cracks make it sound as though the Valley is actively growing—or breathing.

If Axoq is traveling with the PCs, he refuses to enter the tunnel of foliage that leads to the Valley, claiming that those who enter leave changed. He encourages tired-looking PCs to rest before entering, and as the party enters, he waits outside.

## Visions of Tomorrow

When the party enters the Valley, they wander for some time, though it’s difficult to tell how long. Their trek leads them through curtains of thick vines and moss that block their vision. The tunnels through the foliage subtly shift, separating the PCs from one another. With the characters lost and alone, the Nightwatcher shows each a different one of the following visions.

### Vision: Lost Shadows

Behind every sheltered bough of the forest, figures of shadow watch, imploring the PC to find them. The air fills with a discordant drone, which a singer PC recognizes as the Rhythm of Pleading. Whenever the PC tries to approach the figures, the shadows evaporate like smoke. A singer PC notices that the shadows have singer carapaces; a human PC must succeed on a DC 14 Perception test to notice this detail.

The shadows whisper among themselves, cursing the PC’s failures. One speaks more kindly, but with no less urgency: “You have to take it! Take it!”

(This vision alludes to the shadowform singers in [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]]. If one of the PCs is a singer, this is an excellent vision to choose for them.)

### Vision: Odium’s Pawn

Through the spaces between roots and vines, a light shines. If the PC seeks the source, they come upon Ylt, a painful radiance effusing his golden skin. He cuts his way through the foliage with grim determination.

Ylt doesn’t react to the PC unless they attack or touch him. If they do, he turns sharply toward them, and his form flashes away suddenly and terribly. In his place is a being who stretches into infinity. To humans, the being appears as an old human man in golden robes with a scepter. To singers, the being appears as a malen singer with a golden carapace.

Just before the vision ends, the figure’s hatred consumes the PC in molten, red-hot light.

### Vision: Power of Regrowth

As the PC fights their way through the shifting foliage, they must make a DC 14 Agility test.

On a failure, the PC falls through an unseen hole in twisted roots below, grievously injuring themself. They lie in a pool of their own blood in a pitch-black cave. A voice calls for them, searching for them. If the PC responds, a woman appears: the Truthwatcher Kaiana, wreathed in Stormlight. She gives the PC a warm smile and offers a hand to heal their mortal wounds.

On a success, the PC catches themself, controlling their fall. In this case, they hear Kaiana calling for help and find her in the dark, wounded but faintly glowing with Stormlight. “I knew he was wrong… I knew…” she cries, inconsolable.

### Vision: Custom Visions

All PCs should receive a unique vision, so if there are four PCs in your party, you’ll need to create a vision customized for the fourth PC. If they have a regret, desire, or unsolved question related to Taszo or the promise they made him, the vision should relate to those. Otherwise, the vision should address part of their backstory—especially their purpose, obstacle, or goals. For example, they may relive a time they failed to save a life, their last goodbye, or their greatest fear.

### After the Visions

Each vision ends with the PC noticing a thick carpet of mist forming, making it impossible to sense distance or space. As the PCs find themselves in this cloudy white space, they must each make a DC 12 Discipline test. On a failure, a PC is shaken by their visions and must either spend 1 focus or be Disoriented during the first round of the next combat (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Truthwatcher’s Folly|Truthwatcher’s Folly]]).

When the visions are over, any aid the PCs received or harm they sustained within the vision disappears. With the party reunited, progress to the next section.

## Truthwatcher’s Folly

As the characters’ individual visions clear, they experience a final shared vision of Ylt and Kaiana in a jungle grove. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The haze shifts to reveal a vision of Ylt standing before a serpentine coil of emerald mists which form a feminine face: the spren known as the Nightwatcher. The Reshi woman, Kaiana, stands beside him.\
>      “For my boon,” Ylt bellows, “I seek the power to unseat a Herald. Pala is not fit to lead. Let me take her place!”\
>      Vines grow at Ylt’s feet, rising in a mass until they part, revealing a golden knife with an emerald set in its pommel.\
>      “This blade can slay a Herald,” the Nightwatcher whispers. “But…”\
>      Ylt reaches hungrily for the knife—and his hand passes through it. He growls, “You dare mock me with an illusion?”\
>      The spren answers. “Your boon is this weapon. Your curse is that you must persuade another to wield it.”\
>      Beside him, Kaiana regards Ylt and the knife with shock.

![[pg086_Beseeching the nightwatcher_OLIVIA HINTZ.webp]]

**OLIVIA HINTZ**

The Nightwatcher fulfills Ylt’s request by giving him a raysium knife capable of extracting Pailiah’s soul and thus severing her connection to the Oathpact. His curse is that he can’t touch the knife—he must instead convince an ally to do his bidding.

### Starting the Battle

The mists disperse, and the PCs find themselves at the edge of a grove (see [[Scenes Index|map 4.2]]), physically present in the scene they have just witnessed. In the middle of the grove, 50 feet away from the PCs, stand **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt]]** and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]]**, though the Nightwatcher has withdrawn and is nowhere to be seen. Between them is the ***[[Items/Stonewalkers Unique Items/Raysium Knife|raysium knife]]***; you can find rules for wielding this knife in [[Journal/10 - Appendix B Items|appendix B]].

Just before combat begins, Ylt notices the party’s arrival. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ylt turns the Honorblade toward you, settling into a combat stance even as he speaks to Kaiana in soothing tones.\
>      “This is what my visions were trying to tell us, Kaiana. You’ve seen that Pala is lost. We’ve already claimed the power of a Herald’s Blade. If you have the strength to strike her down, I will take on her burden.”

On the first round of combat, Ylt and Kaiana both take fast turns, with Ylt acting before Kaiana. On Ylt’s first turn, he uses the Ready action to prepare a Strike (2total) against any PC who approaches the knife.

Ylt and Kaiana have the following goals during this scene.

### Ylt’s Goals

Ylt is furious about the party’s arrival and requires no provocation to attack them, seeing them as a clear threat to his plans.

With the Nightwatcher proffering the raysium knife, Ylt is entirely convinced he must slay Pailiah to take her place as a Herald. Beyond killing the PCs, his goal is to leave the grove with Kaiana carrying the raysium knife. Throughout the scene, Ylt entreats Kaiana to take the knife and join him. He begins with confidence that Kaiana will obey him, but becomes increasingly agitated if Kaiana refuses.

In combat, Ylt does his best to prevent PCs from taking the knife. If one does, Ylt focuses his attacks on that character.

If Ylt loses Taln’s Honorblade at any point, he resummons it as 1 using the Shardblade’s Summoning / Dismissing trait. When he does, the Blade immediately disappears, then reappears in his hand at the start of his next fast turn.

### Kaiana’s Goals

Kaiana is conflicted and begins by fighting only in self-defense. If no PC has claimed the raysium knife when her first turn begins, Kaiana takes it, attempting to keep the powerful knife out of the party’s hands (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Kaiana Takes the Knife|Kaiana Takes the Knife]]).

Kaiana can be convinced by either Ylt or the party to fight for that side. Even so, Kaiana is loyal to Pailiah and refuses to kill Pailiah or leave the grove with the knife.

![[pg088_raysium knife_STEVEN SHAN.webp]]

**Raysium Knife** **-** **STEVEN SHAN**

### Event: Kaiana Aids Ylt

This scene is a combat—but if the PCs choose, it can be a conversational battle over Kaiana’s allegiance. To reflect this, create a negative event, Kaiana Aids Ylt. This event has three slots.

![[pg086_event card_Kaiana Aids Ylt.webp]]

#### **Progressing the Event**

At the end of each round, if no PCs attempted to sway Kaiana during that round (see Swaying Kaiana), the event automatically progresses by one.

#### **Triggering the Event**

If Kaiana hasn’t yet sided with the party when all the event slots fill, Kaiana aids Ylt. She remains resolute that she won’t betray Pailiah, but she sees the PCs as a threat and attacks them.

#### **Swaying Kaiana**

As long as the Kaiana Aids Ylt event hasn’t been triggered, a PC can attempt to convince Kaiana to join their cause. To do so, the PC can Use a Skill (1) to make an opposed Leadership test against Ylt (Leadership +6). If the party convinced Kaiana to question Ylt in chapter 3, this test gains an advantage. Raise the stakes on this test, and always spend Complications from it to progress the Kaiana Aids Ylt event.

If a PC attacks Kaiana or attempts to disarm her (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Kaiana Takes the Knife|Kaiana Takes the Knife]]), subsequent tests to influence her gain a disadvantage.

Each time a PC succeeds on this Leadership test against Ylt, Kaiana can spend 2 focus to resist influence. If a PC succeeds and Kaiana doesn’t have enough focus to resist, she’s fully convinced and sides with the party.

If the party convinces Kaiana to side with them, she refrains from attacking Ylt but refuses to go with him. Ylt then realizes the futility of the fight; proceed to [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Resolving the Combat|Resolving the Combat]].

### Kaiana Takes the Knife

If Kaiana makes it to the raysium knife before any of the PCs, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Kaiana grabs the golden knife, her expression full of doubt. She makes no move to attack, focusing instead on Ylt.\
>      “I never agreed to this. Pala may be unwell, but she’s still the Herald of our order.”\
>      Without lowering his guard, Ylt gazes imploringly at her with his bright green eyes. “Look how my visions have brought us to this point. Pala has done what she can, but a new Desolation is here. Only I have the strength to lead us now!”

#### **Disarming Kaiana**

If a PC wants to disarm Kaiana, they can Use a Skill (1) with a heavy or light melee weapon against Kaiana’s Cognitive defense (13), raising the stakes. A PC who does so with a light weapon gains a disadvantage on the test, and a character who does so with a heavy weapon gains two disadvantages.

### Battlefield Effects

![[pg089_Map 4-2_Cultivation's Valley_DALLIN BIFANO.webp]]

**Map 4.2: Cultivation’s Valley** **-** **DALLIN BIFANO**

The following effect is active during this combat:

- **Shifting Vines.** At the start of each round, the thick wall of foliage visibly closes, then opens four different tunnels in locations of your choice. Whenever a character steps through these tunnels beyond the perimeters of the grove, they are transported according to the Nightwatcher’s whims. An affected character must immediately make a DC 12 Discipline test. On a success, the character instantly reappears in any other tunnel of their choice on the perimeter. On a failure, they reappear in a tunnel of the GM’s choice (if they’re a PC) or of the players’ choice (if they’re opposing the PCs).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Grove Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Resolving the Combat

This combat ends when one of these conditions are met:

- Kaiana dies or sides with the party.

- Ylt is reduced to 40 health or below, and Kaiana holds the knife.

- Ylt is reduced to 40 health or below, but Kaiana doesn’t hold the knife.

#### **Kaiana Dies or Sides With the Party**

If Kaiana dies or sides with the party, Ylt realizes he can’t leave with the knife. If Kaiana sides with the party, Ylt also uses a final action to attack her before fleeing.

In either case, the Nightwatcher reappears, silently observing the scene. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ylt’s green eyes flash in anger as he shouts at the Nightwatcher. “I see you’ve deceived me, spren of Cultivation. But despite your efforts, I’ve learned the truth: A Herald can be slain. I will find a way to take Pala’s place!”\
>      He cuts through the vines closest to him, opening a tunnel into the grove, then steps into the twisting foliage.

#### **Ylt Is Wounded and Kaiana Holds the Knife**

If Ylt is reduced to 40 health or below and Kaiana holds the knife (but hasn’t sided with the PCs), Ylt begins to flee, but Kaiana is conflicted. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “I’ll follow you, I’ll always follow you—but the future of our order can’t begin with murder.” She drops the knife.\
>      “No more killing. Let’s find another path. Let’s make another future.”\
>      Ylt regards her, rage contorting his face as he stares at the discarded knife… then he breathes, his expression softening. “You’re right, we’ll find another way.”\
>      Hearing his words, golden gloryspren swirl around Kaiana, intermingling with the lifespren motes of the Valley. She smiles, stepping forward.\
>      Then Ylt moves, lightning fast, and brutally stabs the Honorblade through her shoulder. “I’ll find another way,” he growls.

Kaiana is unprepared for the attack. Consider it a critical hit with Taln’s Honorblade, immediately dealing her 25 spirit damage. If this reduces Kaiana to 0 health, she dies. Otherwise, she’s grievously injured and murmurs in shock, “I knew he was wrong… I knew…”

#### **Ylt is Wounded but Kaiana Doesn’t Hold the Knife**

If Ylt is reduced to 40 health or below but Kaiana doesn’t hold the knife (and hasn’t sided with the PCs), both Ylt and Kaiana flee.

> [!tip] What if Someone Dies?
> Though this scene assumes that nobody dies, it’s possible that either Ylt, Kaiana, or a PC could meet their end.
>
> With a stroke of luck and exceptional skill, the party might be able to kill Ylt, allowing them to recover the Honorblade earlier than the adventure intends. If you wish to run the remaining chapters, the party needs a new reason to head to Rall Elorim. In this case, have Kaiana or the Nightwatcher encourage the characters to find Pailiah and free the Eyes of Pala acolytes from Ylt’s odious influence. Even without their figurehead and the Honorblade, Ylt’s zealots pose a threat to the people of Rall Elorim—most notably by releasing the trapped shadowform singers detailed in [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]]. Perhaps even Pailiah herself has fallen under Odium’s spell.
>
> If Kaiana dies, see the [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#Horizons-Ever-Drifting|Path to Radiance: Kaiana’s Spren]] sidebar.
>
> If a PC dies, another PC might ask the Nightwatcher to restore them (see [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley#A Boon and a Curse|A Boon and a Curse]]). However, the Nightwatcher can’t grant this boon if the PC has been dead for more than a minute—so you may wish to hasten the Nightwatcher’s return to the grove and have her immediately mention the death, rather than approaching the conversation playfully. This potentially gives the party a chance to request this boon before time runs out.

### Aftermath

The combat concludes after Ylt flees. If a PC pursues him, Ylt quickly loses them in the shifting forest, and the PC finds themself transported back to the grove.

If Kaiana switches sides and is still alive, she becomes despondent as she grapples with Ylt’s plan to betray Pailiah. Despite this, she wordlessly approaches injured characters and, if they allow, she treats their wounds using her Regrowth ability. If the PCs debate what to do next or ask for her guidance, she grows passionate, entreating the PCs to help her free the Eyes of Pala from Ylt’s tyranny and pledging to work with the party. She is certain Ylt now heads to Pailiah’s base in Rall Elorim.

### Horizons-Ever-Drifting

The mistspren **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Horizons-Ever-Drifting|Horizons-Ever-Drifting]]** (she/her) is bonded with Kaiana and adores her. When the mistspren moves, she looks like light refracted through crystal, and when she’s still, her light twists into kelp- like shapes, sprouting upward and waving as if in invisible waters.

Horizons loves travel and seeing new locations, especially cities. She becomes bored and antsy if Kaiana stays somewhere for more than a few days.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Kaiana’s Spren
> If Kaiana dies, her bonded mistspren **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Horizons-Ever-Drifting|Horizons-Ever-Drifting]]** stays in the Physical Realm to mourn her lost friend. If a PC shows determination to seek the truth behind the Eyes of Pala and Ylt’s betrayal, Horizons may seek a new bond with them.

## A Boon and a Curse

Once the party has a moment of peace, the Nightwatcher returns to the grove. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “You’ve done well, as I knew you would.”\
>      The Nightwatcher flows from the mists surrounding the grove, smiling widely. She reaches out with myriad tendrils, teasing the grasses at your feet. After a moment, her playful demeanor gives way to sternness.\
>      “Ylt must be stopped, and the Honorblade must find its way to Shinovar. Take the knife I have given and present it to the Herald of Justice. It will help him see the truth and aid you in your journey.”

![[Nightwatcher_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Nightwatcher
> **Characteristics:** Alien, generous, mystical.
>
> **Goal:** Ensure the raysium knife is presented to Nale.
>
> **Appearance:** The Nightwatcher (she/her) manifests as a crawling, many-armed serpentine coil of emerald mists with a feminine face. She has pure black eyes and a voice like wind through a forest.
>
> The Nightwatcher is an enigmatic and legendary spren who grants boons and curses from within the Valley of the Nightwatcher. While she occasionally tilts the scales in the affairs of Roshar, her aims are inscrutable.

![[pg091_Cultivation's Valley Scene_DAVID FRASHESKI.webp]]

**DAVID FRASHESKI**

The Nightwatcher is willing to answer the following questions:

**What’s special about this knife?** “Its blade is forged from raysium—Odium’s God Metal. Against a mortal foe, it can draw away some Investiture. But if used to strike the killing blow against a Herald, it’s far more deadly, as it can capture their soul and sever them from the Oathpact.”

**How can we find the Honorblade?** “Ylt now takes a path you will not easily follow. This has become a matter of Heralds, and you must seek a Herald’s aid.”

**Where do we find the Herald of Justice?** “You must take the raysium knife west across the mountains to Ashiqqil Keep. There, you will find a battle and people in need of aid. Within the keep, seek Nale, a Makabaki man marked by a crescent. Deliver this blade to him and he may aid you in hunting Odium’s servant.” (Nale is the Herald of Justice; see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|The Eighty’s War]] and [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]] in chapter 5 for more information.)

**What’s your motivation in all this?** “You would have to ask my mother. She moves the pieces into place so that all transpires as it must, and she is not to be questioned.” (The Nightwatcher’s mother is Cultivation, one of the Shards of Adonalsium described in chapter 3 of the Stormlight World Guide.)

**What’s in it for us?** “I’ve given freely of my gifts. I’ve shown you your fate and brought you here to face Odium’s knight. But I offer you each another boon, if you wish, and a cost to balance.”

### Seeking a Boon

Each PC who wishes can make their own deal with the Nightwatcher. However, Po’ahu warns that the Old Magic isn’t always simple or predictable.

The Nightwatcher asks what each character wants, then offers a boon related to their desire—along with a curse of her choosing. Use the examples on the upcoming Boons and Curses table, or create boons tailored to each PC. A curse need not be directly related to its boon—some may seem quite arbitrary.

The PC can accept or reject her offer, but they only get one offer and can’t barter. If they accept the offer, vines cover the character and the deal is done.

If Kaiana is with the party, she refrains from asking a boon of the Nightwatcher.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Boons and Curses]]

### Leaving the Valley

After all deals are made, the Nightwatcher and her grove disappear, leaving the party alone. Po’ahu urges the PCs to travel west across the mountains, following the Nightwatcher’s request.

If Kaiana is alive, she assures the PCs that Ylt will have fled west to seek passage to Rall Elorim from Emul. However, she doesn’t know if he would then head north overland or south by sea.

If Axoq followed the party into the mountains, he rejoins them as they leave the Valley.

> [!tip] Refusing the Call
> If the PCs refuse to take the raysium knife to the judge as the Nightwatcher requested, Nale comes to them instead. See [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]] in chapter 5 for guidance on running that tense scene—though depending on the party’s path to Rall Elorim, the circumstances may differ.

## Journeying Onward

After Ylt is thwarted and Kaiana’s fate is decided, the party can proceed from the Valley, head overland to Emul, and pursue the Nightwatcher’s quest alongside their ongoing mission to retrieve Taln’s Honorblade.

After surviving the confrontation with Ylt, the characters advance to 5th level.
