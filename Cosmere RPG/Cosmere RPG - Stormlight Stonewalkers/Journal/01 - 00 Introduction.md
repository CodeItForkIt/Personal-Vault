# 00: Introduction

![[Stonewalkers Book Cover.webp]]

Roshar is on the cusp of upheaval. Unknown to most, the Everstorm nears, while dangerous forces grapple for power.

As conflict rises between Odium’s forces and the Knights Radiant, an important Blade is lost in the calm before the storm—or is it?

The *Stonewalkers* adventure weaves the tale of Taln’s Honorblade, its tumultuous journey to Shinovar, and the heroes who decide its fate. As these heroes hunt a heretical thief, bargain with the Nightwatcher, and negotiate with Nale, they change the course of history. This introduction provides an overview of the adventure, guidance for running it, and suggestions for tying the characters into the story.

## About This Book

Stonewalkers is an adventure optimized for four player characters (PCs), the heroes who stop a corrupt Radiant and return Taln’s Honorblade to Shinovar.

This adventure spans seven chapters, bringing characters from level 1 to level 7, with the option to continue beyond the adventure to level 8. This book describes the challenges the characters must overcome, the individuals they meet, and the locations they explore during their quest for the Honorblade.

Besides this book, you’ll need a copy of the *Stormlight Handbook* to play *Stonewalkers*.

## Welcome to Roshar

Stonewalkers is set on Roshar, the world of Brandon Sanderson’s series, The Stormlight Archive. The adventure starts in the Unclaimed Hills, but the characters ultimately travel to the Shattered Plains’ warcamps, the ruins of Rathalas, Greater Hexi, Sesemalex Dar, and Rall Elorim. While significant details are covered in this adventure, the *Stormlight World* Guide further details the wider world, cultures, and customs of Roshar. The following sections describe the world and history pertinent to this adventure.

## A World Ravaged by Storms

Roshar is shaped by highstorms: massive hurricanes that constantly travel east to west across Roshar. The highstorms’ influence is everpresent, resulting in shelled flora and carapaced fauna, and deeply influencing the world’s architecture and culture.

### Stormlight

The highstorms carry Stormlight, Roshar’s primary source of Investiture a divine power that some characters and artifacts can use to enable miraculous abilities.

Gems can hold Stormlight from highstorms, causing them to glow. Captured Stormlight is used for light, to power fabrials—devices akin to magical technology—and to empower Surgebinding. Surgebinding was the magical art of the Lost Radiants (see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#The Knights Radiant|The Knights Radiant]] later in this introduction); each Radiant could wield two of ten surges that manipulate nature’s fundamental forces.

### Shards

Shardblades and Shardplate are fantastically powerful swords and armor. A Shardblade can annihilate an entire squad of regular enemies with a single swing, and Shardplate makes its wearer impervious to mundane strikes. Both are exceedingly rare and militarily important items, with nations owning a couple dozen at most.

This adventure features Taln’s Honorblade, which is a legendary Shardblade once owned by a Herald, as described in the later [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#The Heraldic Epochs|The Heraldic Epochs]] section.

### Spren

Another unique element of Roshar is the existence of spren: tiny visible fragments of Investiture who personify emotions, concepts, and natural phenomena. An angry person might attract angerspren, which manifest as boiling pools of blood. High winds attract windspren, appearing as ribbons of light. Meanwhile, a powerful lie might attract a Cryptic (also known as a liespren), a sapient spren who appears as a complex shifting pattern. A Cryptic is an example of a Radiant spren (see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#The Knights Radiant|The Knights Radiant]]).

![[pg002_Angstspren_MARIE SEEBERGER.webp]]

**The Merchant Svalka Attracts Angstpren** **-** **MARIE SEEBERGER**

#### **Legendary Spren**

In addition to common spren, two legendary unique spren appear in Stonewalkers: the Gray Remnant and the Nightwatcher. Both are sapient, powerful demigodlike characters with their own motivations and agendas for the future of Roshar. They are described in more detail in the [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#History of Roshar|History of Roshar]] and [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] sections.

### Odium

Roshar is influenced by Cultivation, Honor, and Odium. These three Shards each bear an aspect of the deific power of creation called Adonalsium. Odium is a god of hatred who has operated covertly throughout the millennia. *Stonewalkers* occurs during a period of upheaval where Odium once again gathers his forces and threatens Roshar. In particular, Odium influences Ylt, the adventure’s main antagonist.

### Singers

The singers are the native people of Roshar, a species of carapaced humanoids who evolved to thrive amid the world’s highstorms. Singers communicate through both speech and humming rhythms. Each singer has a gemstone in their body called a gemheart, and they can change form by entering a highstorm and willingly taking a spren into their gemheart.

During the events of *Stonewalkers*, singers transition from enslavement to a challenging emancipation, with many factions aligning themselves with Odium for protection. Singers are a playable ancestry in *Stonewalkers;* more information about including singers in your campaign can be found in the upcoming [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#Playing With Singers|Playing With Singers]] section.

## History of Roshar

*Stonewalkers* is set at the end of a period in Roshar known as the Era of Solitude, coinciding with events that take place during the novel *Words of Radiance*. The following historical events are relevant to the *Stonewalkers* adventure. A more comprehensive timeline appears in chapter 4 of the *Stormlight World Guide*.

### The Heraldic Epochs

The events of *Stonewalkers* start during the Heraldic Epochs, over seven thousand years before this adventure’s heroes meet on a dusty road in the Unclaimed Hills. Long ago, to fight against Odium, ten humans made the Oathpact: In return for vowing to eternally protect humanity, these ten humans were each blessed with immortality and an Honorblade, a legendary sword that enabled Surgebinding. These people became known as the Heralds, saint-like figures in the human history of Roshar.

![[pg003_Ten Heralds statues 1_MARIE SEEBERGER.webp.webp]]

**Jezrien, Nale, Chanaranach, Vedel, and Pailiah** **-** **MARIE SEEBERGER**

For centuries, the Heralds led the fight in cataclysmic battles called the Desolations. At the end of each Desolation, the Heralds would seal Odium’s forces, known as the Voidbringers, on a hellish planet called Braize. The Heralds would remain trapped with Odium’s forces on Braize, enduring torture until a Herald broke, thus allowing the Voidbringers to return anew to Roshar and begin a new Desolation. The Heralds table provides each Herald’s name, their Radiant order, the surges they wield, their Essence, and their attributes, listed in order of the Vorin number they’re associated with.

### The Knights Radiant

During the Desolations, other humans unlocked the powers of Surgebinding by bonding with special types of sapient spren, known as Radiant spren. Those who spoke oaths of binding were called the Knights Radiant. These individuals each gained two surges, mirroring the Heralds, and organized themselves into ten orders, each with its patron Herald.

### The End of Heralds and Radiants

The endless cycles of fighting and torture on Braize took their toll on the Heralds. In a final act of desperation, the other nine Heralds agreed to abandon the Herald Taln on Braize to endure alone, believing this would keep the Voidbringers from returning to Roshar.

After this betrayal, known as the Last Desolation, the nine remaining immortal Heralds walked away from their roles as Roshar’s leaders and protectors.

The Knights Radiant remained for another two thousand years, protecting Roshar until an event called the Day of Recreance. The prevailing historical interpretation is that on that day, the Knights Radiant laid down their weapons and abandoned their duties as protectors.

### The Shadowform Singers

During the centuries between the Last Desolation and the start of the adventure, singers and humans clashed in bloody conflicts. Ba-Ado-Mishram, an ambitious spren empowered by Odium’s Investiture, led the singer people. She made many calculated risks during that time, including the creation of shadowform.

Shadowform was a theorized singer form, which Ba-Ado-Mishram hoped would create an army of deadly half-corporeal assassins. She attempted to unlock the form by binding singers with shadowspren— but this attempt failed. Instead, those singers’ bodies became permanent shadows, with only their gemhearts remaining solid. The singers were mourned and their gemhearts were buried in Rall Elorim.

The shadowform singers feature in [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|chapter 6]] and [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]] as part of a plot to unseat the Herald Pailiah.

## Present Day in the Era of Solitude

*Stonewalkers* takes place in the last days of the Era of Solitude, long after the disappearance of the Heralds and Knights Radiant.

### Common Knowledge

Due to the loss of historical knowledge—and at times, the re-writing of it, largely by the Vorin church— Roshar’s common folk know few details of Roshar’s ancient history. The following beliefs are commonplace on Roshar today:

- **Heralds and the Knights Radiant.** The Heralds are mythical religious figures who once protected Roshar and now fight in the Tranquiline Halls after defeating the Voidbringers on Roshar. Specifics of the Radiant orders and descriptions of Surgebinding are the realm of folklore scholars, ardents, and storytellers. Taln’s sacrifice and the details of the Oathpact are lost knowledge.

- **Bonding Spren and Radiant Powers.** The acts of bonding spren, breathing in Stormlight, and swearing Radiant Ideals are lost knowledge. Radiant abilities such as regrowing limbs, flying, or creating illusions are regarded as legendary myths and would be shocking to witness.

- **Odium and Ba-Ado-Mishram.** Odium operates from the shadows, his existence largely unknown except as an evil opposing “the Almighty,” as Honor is known across most of Roshar. The story of Ba-Ado-Mishram and her shadowform singers is also lost to history.

### Present-Day Heralds

In the millennia since the Last Desolation, the Heralds have continued their immortal lives, existing in seclusion or incognito. Three Heralds feature in *Stonewalkers*.

Ylt, the adventure’s antagonist, is a disciple of the scholarly Herald Pailiah. Pailiah, known as Pala in the Iriali culture, secretly leads a small sect of mundane acolytes. These acolytes call themselves the Eyes of Pala and operate out of the vaults below Rall Elorim.

At the start of the adventure, the characters meet the Herald Taln, also called Talenelat by the Alethi and Talmut by the Shin. He never broke under torture, and he has returned to Roshar because another Herald’s death led to a new Desolation.

Nale, Herald of Justice, acts as a lawkeeper. He leads a small, secret force of Radiant Skybreakers— the only group of Radiants operating during the Era of Solitude. Though he was once intent on hunting down and eliminating other nascent Radiants, his agenda changes as the Everstorm transforms Roshar.

By the time the party meets him in [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|chapter 5]], he is working to align with the singers and navigate a new future for Roshar.

### Shinovar and the Honorblades

Unknown to most, after the Era of Desolation, the isolationist nation of Shinovar took nine of the ten Heralds’ Honorblades. The Stone Shamans, Shinovar’s religious order, have spent centuries training monks in the Blades’ use. Called Honorbearers, these monks do so to prepare for Odium’s eventual return.

This adventure features the fate of the tenth Honorblade, Taln’s Honorblade, which the Stone Shamans seek.

### Singers and the War of Reckoning

The end of the Era of Solitude is a particularly brutal time for singers. Most are trapped in a spiritually and mentally shackled singer form known as “slaveform,” and they have been captured to serve human masters who call them parshmen. The majority of human societies during this time period practice singer enslavement. The few free singers call themselves the listeners, though humans know them as Parshendi. On the Shattered Plains, these listeners war against the human nation of Alethkar. Known as the War of Reckoning, this confl ict is seemingly endless and unwinnable for the listeners.

## The Everstorm and the Rise of Odium

During *Stonewalkers*, the characters witness the modern-day rise of Odium and the fi rst Everstorm. Empowered by Odium, the Everstorm is a black storm filled with deadly red lightning. Within it, singers can change into any form—including Odium-influenced forms of power—or they can sacrifice themselves to become vessels for the Fused, ancient powerful Surgebinding beings who are loyal to Odium.

The arrival of the Everstorm has another profound impact: It restores former “slaveform” singers to full autonomy.

### The Everstorm in Stonewalkers

The Everstorm is a major event in *Stonewalkers*. The Stone Shamans seek to prepare for the Everstorm through their mission to retrieve Taln’s Honorblade; while the Shin don’t know Odium’s exact plans, the ill omens referenced in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]] through [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|chapter 3]] refer to the Everstorm and should be used to foreshadow Odium’s rise.

In [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]], the characters experience the Everstorm and the singers’ awakening fi rsthand. Across Roshar, a great political and cultural shift occurs, with the heroes witnessing the Everstorm’s impact in Karanak, Sesemalex Dar, and Rall Elorim. The Everstorm emboldens Ylt, and in [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]], he stages a coup against the Herald Pailiah.

## Handling Canon

Stonewalkers touches on several canon characters, objects, and events. The “Canon and Continuity” section in chapter 13 of the *Stormlight Handbook* discusses different methods of handling canon. If your group wishes to maintain continuity with the books, Stonewalkers touches on the following canon events of the Stormlight Archive:

- Bordin transports the Herald Taln and a Shardblade to the Kholin warcamp. However, the Shardblade that Bordin delivers to Dalinar isn’t Taln’s Honorblade. The characters accompany this caravan on their way to the Shattered Plains and witness the true Honorblade’s theft in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]].

- Liss is operating out of the warcamps. The characters meet Liss in [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]].

- Taln is held at the ardentia monastery and Amaram suspects Taln is a Herald. The characters infiltrate the monastery, speaking with Taln (and potentially Amaram) at the end of [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]].

- The Everstorm is summoned during the Battle of Narak. Shortly after, the Everstorm overtakes the characters while they travel by boat to Karanak in [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]].

- Taln’s Honorblade is taken to Shinovar. The end of Stonewalkers offers characters the option of delivering the Blade to the Shin.

## Playing With Singers

*Stonewalkers* was designed with both human and singer characters in mind, and players who want to create characters with singer ancestry are encouraged to do so.

The Era of Solitude was a deeply traumatic time for singers. The GM and players should work together to determine how they want to handle singer characters at the table, including PCs and NPCs. As the GM, be prepared to facilitate this conversation. To aid you, refer to the “Safety Guide” section in chapter 13 of the *Stormlight Handbook*; this provides guidance for how to check in with players and determine what topics everyone wants to include in your game. The section “Including Marginalization Responsibly” offers broad advice on how to address marginalization dynamics while roleplaying.

The following sections provide *Stonewalkers*-specific guidance for integrating singer PCs into your campaign.

### Singers in *Stonewalkers*

In general, this adventure strives to present scenarios where a character’s ancestry adds texture and nuance to the story. Playing a singer or human changes gameplay, but neither ancestry should significantly limit a character’s overall ability to act or impact the game.

The adventure notes scenes where singer ancestry can provide a benefit or additional nuance to a situation. Use these as jumping-off points to highlight singer characters.

Keep the following guidance in mind as you progress through each chapter.

#### **Singers in Chapters 1–2**

[[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Chapter 1]] and [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]] take place in Alethi human society. Most humans on Roshar are used to seeing enslaved singers. It is suggested that GMs treat dullform, workform, nimbleform, and scholarform as physically similar enough to slaveform that singer PCs can choose these forms without humans recognizing they’re not in slaveform.

However, warform is readily identifiable by Alethi soldiers who are used to fighting singers on the Shattered Plains. If you’re following canon, most Alethi would react to a warform singer with hostility, shock, and fear. Whatever you choose, make it clear to your players which forms Alethi might assume belong to enemy soldiers, and which forms a singer PC could use to disguise themself as a slave.

#### **Singers in Chapter 3**

[[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|Chapter 3]] takes place in a bandit camp and the entire party is assumed to be outsiders; as such, a singer in any form won’t raise additional questions.

#### **Singers in Chapters 4–7**

[[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|Chapter 4]] through [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]] take place during and after the Everstorm, during a period of singer awakening and emancipation. Singers are no longer enslaved or regarded as parshmen. During the Everstorm, singer characters have a chance to unlock forms of power.

In Karanak and Sesemalex Dar—cities visited in [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]] and [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|chapter 5]]—singer characters are treated with suspicion but respect. When the party reaches Rall Elorim in [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|chapter 6]], the circumstances are reversed, with human party members experiencing greater scrutiny than singer party members.

![[pg008_Singer parshman_ESTHER SCHRADER.webp]]

**A Listener disguised as a parshman** **-** **ESTHER SCHRADER**

### Storms and Forms

Make sure singer characters have ample opportunity to change forms. Highstorms appear in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]] and [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]]. The Everstorm takes place during [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]]. You can also assume that the periods of travel during or between each of the first five chapters include highstorms, during which singer characters can change forms.

### Suggested Backstories

Most singers during the *Stonewalkers* time period are trapped in slaveform, and the remainder are listeners fighting a losing war against the Alethi. To more easily incorporate singers into the *Stonewalkers* story, consider these backstory suggestions.

#### **Listener Spy**

Parshman servants are ubiquitous in enough of Roshar that it’s easy for a discreet listener to pass as one. Listeners might choose to go undercover in human territory to help with the war effort, or they might do so for their own personal gain.

The other party members should know about and enable this ruse, allowing the listener to fully participate in party decision-making. For more inspiration, the Agent heroic path in [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]] of the *Stormlight Handbook* presents the Undercover Workform iconic build. As GM, you might consider introducing Tuxli— a listener spy in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|chapter 3]]—into this character’s backstory.

#### **Diplomat**

For players who don’t want their character to travel incognito, a listener character could serve as a diplomat with a powerful governmental patron, providing immunity to the party as they journey through the Shattered Plains. Give the listener character an official document to enable safe travel, and consider having other characters aid in this mission.

## Adventure Summary

The *Stonewalkers* adventure follows a group of heroes as they uncover a plot to steal an Honorblade and supplant a Herald.

Before starting [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]], run a session zero using the [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Character Connections]] section at the end of this introduction. This helps establish the party’s connection to the Shin monk Taszo, whose death in chapter 1 propels the PCs into finding the Honorblade and stopping Ylt.

The following sections summarize the adventure background and its seven chapters.

![[p009_Adventure Flowchart.webp]]

## Adventure Background

A contingent of Shin Stone Shamans leaves Shinovar on their secret mission to retrieve Taln’s Honorblade. During this mission, the monks are attacked by Ylt and his sect of acolytes loyal to the Herald Pailiah, known as the Eyes of Pala. Ylt is an egotistical Radiant gifted with powerful future sight. Sensing the great change coming to Roshar, Ylt also seeks the Blade, convinced that he alone can stand against it.

Ylt’s band slays all the monks except Taszo. Fearing for his life, Taszo recruits the heroes to escort him safely to the Shattered Plains.

## Chapter 1 Summary

At the start of the adventure, Taszo and the heroes come upon an Alethi caravan transporting the very Honorblade that Taszo and his fellow monks sought. Shortly after the PCs discover this, the caravan is attacked by bandits, who provide a distraction while Ylt and the Eyes of Pala steal the Honorblade. During the altercation, Taszo is mortally wounded. As he dies, he asks the PCs to complete his mission to retrieve the Honorblade. He entrusts them with his peakspren, Po’ahu, with whom he had begun to form a nascent Radiant bond. Taszo tells the PCs to seek the Shamanate’s contact Liss on the Shattered Plains.

## Chapter 2 Summary

The heroes arrive at the Shattered Plains. They can explore the warcamps and seek the Stone Shamans’ contact Liss. Upon finding Liss, she shares that she doesn’t know who stole the Honorblade, but advises that the Herald Taln is held in the ardentia monastery and can locate the Blade through his Heraldic Connection. The heroes infiltrate the monastery, where Po’ahu spurs Taln to lucidity long enough to reveal that the Blade is in the ruined city of Rathalas.

## Chapter 3 Summary

The party arrives in Rathalas to find it overrun by the same bandits who attacked during the Honorblade theft. They learn that the bandits began worshipping Ylt after his acolytes killed the old Bandit Queen and promised to teach them Radiant powers. The characters can choose a direct approach, or they can capitalize on the local opposition to Ylt’s takeover by starting a prison riot. In the prison, the characters also meet Axies, a spren scholar, and Kaiana, Ylt’s conflicted lieutenant. The characters can encourage Kaiana to question Ylt’s leadership, though she’s unwilling to betray him at this stage.

Finally, the party has the chance to confront Ylt in the bandits’ grand hall and instigate a massive battle for control of Rathalas. During the fight, Ylt flees to an unknown destination.

In the aftermath, Axies suggests the characters speak with the Gray Remnant, a mysterious spren below Rathalas. If they do, the Gray Remnant offers to reveal Ylt’s plans in exchange for sharing a “painful truth.” Upon completing the bargain, the characters learn Ylt is en route to the Valley of the Nightwatcher, where he seeks a boon from the legendary spren.

## Chapter 4 Summary

The new bandit leaders provide the heroes with a ship and crew so the party can follow Ylt. Early in the journey, the Everstorm strikes, causing destruction and restoring the singers to full autonomy.

The party continues to chase Ylt to Greater Hexi and the Valley of the Nightwatcher. The valley shows the PCs disturbing visions of Ylt’s fall to Odium, then brings them to Kaiana and Ylt. On their arrival, Ylt beseeches the Nightwatcher for the power to kill a Herald. The Nightwatcher offers Ylt a raysium knife, but with the curse that he must convince another to wield it. A desperate struggle ensues between Ylt and the heroes, during which Ylt and the PCs fight for Kaiana’s allegiance and for control of the knife.

If Ylt loses, he flees once more, this time to the base of the Eyes of Pala in Rall Elorim. The Nightwatcher asks the PCs to deliver the knife to Nale.

## Chapter 5 Summary

The characters make their way to Ashiqqil Keep, a fortress on the border of Emul and Tukar. There, they fight their way through a battle in the Eighty’s War. Inside the keep, they find Nale, who plans to execute an idealistic Radiant.

Delivering the raysium knife in exchange for information (and possibly for the Radiant’s life), the party gains safe passage to the city of Sesemalex Dar. They can continue their pursuit of Ylt by finding a ship bound for Rall Elorim.

## Chapter 6 Summary

Ylt has retreated to Rall Elorim to supplant the Herald Pailiah, but the party is close behind. Upon landing in Rall Elorim, they find the city occupied by Odium’s Fused forces.

The heroes must navigate the city and its politics, including deciding whether to help the local singer resistance. Their snooping catches the attention of the destructive Fused Lilinum, who violently intercepts them moments before they enter the vaults below Rall Elorim.

## Chapter 7 Summary

The heroes approach Pailiah’s Vault as Ylt enacts his violent coup to take over the Eyes of Pala. In Pailiah’s inner sanctum, the party has a final chance to stop Ylt—but not before his most faithful followers sacrifice themselves to release the vengeful spirits of the shadowform singers.

After the battle, a confused Pailiah emerges from her study, surprised at the carnage. The PCs can attempt to convince the Herald to reengage with the world. The adventure ends with them as the stewards of Taln’s Honorblade. If the heroes so choose, their next step is delivering it to the Shin.

## Notable Characters

Over the course of the adventure, the heroes meet allies and adversaries from across the lands of Roshar. The following sections detail the named characters in Stonewalkers.

## [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt the Truthwatcher]]

Ylt is the main antagonist in *Stonewalkers*. After he steals the Honorblade in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]], the PCs learn of his plot to supplant a Herald and must fight to stop him.

### Ylt’s Story

Even compared to other devout Iriali, Ylt had a fanatical devotion to the One. During a pilgrimage to Kasitor, Ylt’s self-assured faith caught the attention of the timid Enlightened mistspren, Tyche (he/him). Tyche showed Ylt a vision of the city’s foremost religious leader stealing from the faithful. On the same day Ylt revealed this treachery to the community, he spoke the Words and became Radiant.

In bonding with Tyche, Ylt foretold himself offering his undying service to the Herald Pailiah. After traveling to Rall Elorim, Ylt pledged himself to Pailiah and revived her flagging cult of mundane acolytes who call themselves the Eyes of Pala. During this service, Ylt experienced a significant vision—war and devastation sweeping across Roshar. When Pailiah told Ylt that Taln’s Honorblade would play a key role in this future, Ylt swore to bring the artifact to her for safekeeping.

Following hints from his visions, Ylt traveled across Roshar to Alethkar. There, he killed the Bandit Queen of Rathalas and converted many of her people into zealots, employing them to scout for Taln’s caravan. With his gift of future sight, Ylt has identified the perfect window of opportunity to steal Taln’s Honorblade.

Over the course of the adventure, Ylt becomes convinced that he must wield the Blade, and later comes to believe that Pailiah isn’t fit to lead the Eyes of Pala or the Truthwatchers.

![[Ylt_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Ylt
> **Characteristics:** Adamant, corrupt, egotistical.
>
> **Goal:** Supplant the Herald Pailiah.
>
> **Appearance:** Ylt (“IHLT”; he/him) is a tall, statuesque Iriali human with golden skin and hair. The glyph of the Truthwatchers is painted over his face in a striking deep green.
>
> Ylt is devoted to truth, but he views it through the distorted lens of self-righteousness. Ylt’s initial visions empowered him to see hidden injustice and do meaningful good in the world, but ultimately, this ability gave him a sense of infallibility that he clings to. Increasingly, he rejects facts presented by others and believes the ends—his ends—justify violent means. While he suffers from profound delusions of grandeur, he’s often paranoid and cowardly in the face of real danger.

## [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana the Truthwatcher]]

Kaiana is Ylt’s conflicted second-in-command. Over the course of Stonewalkers, the party can convince her of Ylt’s treachery and potentially make her an ally.

### Kaiana’s Story

Two years ago, hoping to see the world, Kaiana took a job with Alethi merchants and aided them in their travels—until she realized part of their business included creating counterfeit goods and selling them in foreign markets. After exposing this scheme to the people they had swindled, Kaiana bonded her mistspren Horizons-Ever-Drifting (she/her).

Shortly after that, during Ylt’s journey across Roshar, he sought Kaiana out and offered to teach her about her Truthwatcher abilities. She spent a brief time training at the secret Eyes of Pala base in Rall Elorim, then she accompanied Ylt in his hunt for the Honorblade.

Ylt’s conviction, visions, and force of personality were initially compelling to Kaiana, but as he grows more erratic and dangerous, Kaiana has found herself privately questioning her allegiance.

![[Kaiana_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Kiana
> **Characteristics:** Adventurous, loyal, stubborn.
>
> **Goal:** See the world and make her mark by uncovering injustice and empowering the oppressed.
>
> **Appearance:** Kaiana (“KIE-ah-nuh”; she/her) is a Reshi human with round features and long, slickedback black hair. She most often wears a practical green dress. The glyph of the Truthwatchers is painted over her face in a striking deep green.
>
> Kaiana was raised in the Reshi Isles among a large and loving family, but she was always curious about the wider world of Roshar.
>
> She enjoys debate, though she hates appearing foolish or naive. She rarely holds her tongue, has a fiery temper, and is quick to point out falsehoods—at times to her own detriment. In calmer moments, she likes being by the water and delights in upbeat music, often drumming with her hands.
>
> While internally conflicted about her mentor Ylt, Kaiana outwardly remains protective of him, initially unwilling to admit he has led her astray.

## [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Axies the Collector|Axies the Collector]]

Axies, a friendly scholar who studies spren, meets the PCs in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|chapter 3]]. The party later reconnects with him as a potential ally in [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|chapter 6]] and [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]], as he seeks the shadowspren who are a key element of Ylt’s coup.

### Axies’s Story

Axies has lived long enough to have seen the destruction of his home, the Silver Kingdom of Aimia, thousands of years ago. Since then, he has been an itinerant wanderer, finding purpose by studying and cataloging rare spren.

As a Siah Aimian—an ancient, little-known, and functionally immortal people—Axies can change his appearance at will. He puts this ability to good use by tattooing his body with many ever-changing notes and reminders.

His goal is to eventually see every kind of spren on Roshar. While his near-immortality makes him brave, Axies isn’t a fighter and avoids physical conflict.

Axies is afflicted by something he refers to as the “Curse of Kind,” which frequently brings misfortune on him. Due to his immortality, turns of bad luck don’t anger him, but simply make him bemused and resigned—even when he’s imprisoned, maimed, or worse.

![[Axies_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Axies
> **Characteristics:** Gregarious, lackadaisical, single-minded.
>
> **Goal:** Learn as much as possible about rare spren.
>
> **Appearance:** Axies (“AK-seez”; he/him) is a Siah Aimian who appears as a lanky, pale blue-skinned human man covered in handwritten tattoos. His shadow points toward light sources.
>
> Axies is friendly and laid-back to a fault—except when consumed by his focus on finding rare spren. He prioritizes this mission over concerning himself with inconveniences, setbacks, or danger during his search. This philosophy extends to others; Axies is without malice, but when he’s hot on the trail of a rare spren, he doesn’t stop to consider how he might be endangering his mortal companions.

## Po’ahu

Po’ahu is the peakspren of the late Shin monk Taszo. She travels with the party as a guide and reminder of the importance of their task.

### Po’ahu’s Story

A few years prior to meeting Taszo, Po’ahu and two of her cousins chose to seek humans to bond. In so doing, they rebelled against the older generation of peakspren who considered it a pointless act to stand against Odium. The cousins made a promise to one another—they would prove their family wrong by bonding humans who would make great contributions in the fight against Odium.

Intending to find such a human, Po’ahu traveled Roshar. She passed over candidates with more accolades, instead finally choosing to follow a naive but open-hearted Stone Shaman disciple named Taszo, who she found during his long trek to Alethkar. During Taszo’s mission for the Honorblade, Po’ahu began to make herself known and watched over him, protecting him from Ylt’s assassination attempt that slew his comrades.

She was proud of him when he followed the Honorblade thieves into the storm—and felt gutted when his act of bravery wasn’t enough. Now she follows the characters, intent on making them keep their promise to finish Taszo’s mission.

![[Po'ahu_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Po'ahu
> **Characteristics:** Relentlessly supportive, optimistic, timid.
>
> **Goal:** Finish Taszo’s mission to retrieve Taln’s Honorblade.
>
> **Appearance:** Po’ahu (“poh-AH-hoo”; she/her) appears in the Physical Realm as roughly hewn brown-gray basalt stone. When she shows herself in the Physical Realm, she has a feminine humanoid shape. Her voice sounds like churning gravel.
>
> Po’ahu is a naturally upbeat spren. Though occasionally shy, she seeks out kind and extroverted humans. She is especially fond of friendly competition, as it reminds her of her family. She often encourages turning tasks into contests, even if the task makes for an odd competition.

## Adventure Goals

In Stonewalkers, the PCs start by traveling with or in service to Taszo, a Shin Stone Shaman who is seeking Taln’s Honorblade. All the PCs have been traveling with Taszo for at least a week, if not longer.

Each player should ensure their character has a goal related to traveling with or helping Taszo, representing a connection they made with him before the events of this adventure. You can work with players to make their own goals (see the upcoming [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#Custom Goals|Custom Goals]] section), or if you prefer, let players pick a goal from the six backstories in the Character Backstory Ideas table. This table provides the following:

1.  Suggested heroic paths the player might choose to fit the theme of their PC’s backstory.

2.  A reason the PC might be traveling with Taszo.

3.  Questions the player should answer to learn more about why their PC is traveling with Taszo and how this relates to their backstory.

4.  A suggested goal the player can use as inspiration to develop their own.

## Custom Goals

Each player can also come up with their own backstories and goals related to Taszo. Taszo sought aid for his journey to the Shattered Plains; he has shared that he’s searching for an ancient artifact, and that his previous companions, a retinue of Shin monks, were slain. Each PC might have agreed to help him for spheres or their own reasons. PCs might also be interested in the ancient artifact, Taln’s Honorblade, or Taszo’s unique understanding of history.

Though most PCs should have met Taszo during his travels, it’s possible one PC traveled as part of the original retinue of monks as a steward, local guide, or additional muscle.

## Character Relationships

Ahead of the adventure, it can be valuable to have the player characters establish their relationships with one another. When this adventure begins, the party has been traveling together for at least a week through the dangerous Unclaimed Hills. To help inspire the players as they determine their characters’ relationships, use the following prompts.

### Relationship Prompts

Go around the table once and ask the players to each pick one of the prompts from the following list. Each player should read their chosen prompt aloud, and then one other player should provide an answer based on their own character. Both players should build on each other and must agree on the connection they establish.

While each player should choose at least one of the following prompts, your group is also welcome to explore all of these questions or create your own:

- You don’t trust me. What suspicious act did you witness me doing?

- I had a moment of vulnerability with you on the road. What caused it, and why could you relate?

- You’ve either woken up early with me or stayed up late with me. What have we learned about each other?

- By coincidence, I have a friend or family member in common with you. Who is our mutual connection? How did I find this out?

- You and I have butted heads. What was our argument about?

- I’m fast friends with you. What pastime have we enjoyed together on our travels?

- I have a controversial opinion about Taszo. Why do you agree with me? How have we communicated away from the others?

- I’m fascinated by something about you or your life. What question of mine were you not ready to answer?

- One night over dinner, I shared a cultural practice or family tradition. Did you already know the tradition? If not, what made you join in?

- I needed a small favor along the way. Why did I approach you instead of another companion, and what did I ask you for?
