# 02: Weeper's Trail

With highstorms weathered, banditry suffered, friends made, and friends lost, the caravan finally leaves the hills. After several days of blessedly uneventful travel, the convoy makes it to the safety of the armies of Alethkar.

## Running This Chapter

When the caravan arrives at the Alethi warcamps on the Shattered Plains, Bordin takes the Herald Taln to a secure location and leaves the PCs to explore the warcamps. Proactive characters can search for the Stone Shamans’ contact Liss or seek information about the bandits’ strange glyphs. Others may wish to reconnoiter the camps for their own reasons. After meeting with Liss, the PCs must infiltrate the monastery in the Kholin camp and speak with Taln, who reveals that his Honorblade is in the burned city of Rathalas.

This chapter includes a few encounters with the Sons of Honor. This covert organization is obsessed with restoring the Vorin church’s dominance by resurrecting both the Voidbringer threat and the Heralds to fight them.

The *Stormlight World Guide* provides more information about the Shattered Plains (in chapter 4) and the Sons of Honor (in chapter 5).

This chapter is a player-driven sandbox where the party can split up to seek information in the relative safety of the warcamps. Before beginning, familiarize yourself with the six warcamp scenes detailed in [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|Searching the Warcamps]]. These can occur in any order. Be prepared to switch between groups in different scenes and keep the spotlight moving between characters.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 2nd level and advance to 3rd level after meeting with Taln.

The following decisions offer opportunities to introduce spren, strengthen spren bonds, or swear Ideals:

- How the PCs approach and interact in the six warcamp scenes.

- Whether the PCs choose to fight or peaceably negotiate with Calinai’s squad.

- Restoring Taln to lucidity.

- Negotiating with Meridas Amaram.

Characters with goals related to learning more about the Heralds, Honorblades, or Radiants have opportunities to do so when visiting Shansan in the Devotary of the Mind, speaking with Liss, and speaking with Taln.\

## Respite

Introduce the Shattered Plains by reading the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With quiet determination, the caravan drives through the Unclaimed Hills to the Shattered Plains. Here, deep chasms scar the lands, breaking the terrain into an endless expanse of arid plateaus.\
>      You travel onward to your destination at the western edge of the plains: ten enormous craters, each occupied by a warcamp from which the Alethi wage war against the Parshendi. Further to the west, a small market town collectively serves the camps, and the king’s palace looms over them all.

PCs with the Alethi expertise know of the ten highprinces who are camped here: Roion, Sadeas, Aladar, Kholin, Vamah, Ruthar, Thanadal, Hatham, Bethab, Sebarial. They also know the camps’ locations from north to south. PCs without the Alethi expertise must succeed on a DC 12 Lore test to recall the names, and they don’t know the locations of each camp.

As the caravan enters the Kholin camp, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Kholin-run camp runs on precision and discipline. The barracks are built into Soulcast stone buildings in straight, regimented rows. The sections of barracks form a rough circle around a central command complex, which houses the Kholin officers and their families.\
>      Sergeant Ellar barks orders for the Alethi soldiers to hurry their prisoner to Highprince Dalinar—though the soldiers wait for a nod of approval from Bordin before seeing to their task. Bordin himself peels away from his troops to offer you a terse farewell:\
>      “If you want a meal and some rest before you go on your way, you’re welcome to join the troops at the mess hall. After that… what’s next for you?”

For the sake of camaraderie, Bordin is willing to help the PCs get settled in the warcamps. He offers the following information:

- **Where can we find shelter?** “I’ll send word to set you up with a room at the Ornery Chull here in camp. The first night is on me.”

- **Where can we find information or track someone down?** “Find Brightness Bettani—she’s a scribe for Highprince Dalinar, and she fancies herself a librarian. As near as you can get on the Plains, anyway.”

- **Any advice on what to see or do here?** “My command doesn’t allow me much leisure time, but my men enjoy the menageries in the Outer Market. Just keep your wits about you outside the Kholin camp. War makes the undisciplined reckless.”

If the party formed a favorable relationship with Bordin in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]] or if they directly ask for his aid, he offers to help them find more information on the bandits’ glyphs. If the PCs mention her, he can also help them find Liss, the Stone Shamans’ contact. In exchange, though, he first needs their help. If the characters accept, run the [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Chasm Offensive|Chasm Offensive]] scene in [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|Searching the Warcamps]].

## Kholin Warcamp

The Kholin warcamp is a bastion of order in a storm of wartime activity. Though ostensibly the staging area for Dalinar Kholin’s army, Alethi traditions encourage soldiers and stewards to bring along their families. As such, the warcamp has become a self-sufficient community. Alongside homes and barracks, the camp hosts several taverns, inns, markets, and workshops to serve an army ten thousand strong.

In contrast to the other warcamps, the Kholin army maintains a utilitarian environment. Most permanent buildings are stark Soulcast bunkers, including homes for the upper-caste “lighteyes.” The warcamp isn’t entirely drab, however; the flags flying from military buildings and the soldiers’ uniforms are all adorned in the vibrant blue of House Kholin.

The Kholin camp can serve as the party’s base of operations, though they’re likely to also explore other warcamps and the Outer Market. Within the Kholin camp, this chapter expects that the PCs will visit the ardentia monastery, Bettani’s bunker, Shansan’s Devotary, and Sergeant Ellar’s barracks; each is detailed in later sections. However, the party may also wish to visit the following useful locations, depicted on [[Scenes Index|map 2.1]].

### The Spear Exchange

*Market*

The warcamps’ markets are clustered near the opening of the crater wall to allow for easy loading and unloading of goods. Although the available wares aren’t as varied or far traveled as those in the Outer Market, the Kholin army has cultivated a relationship with reliable and trustworthy merchants. Quartermasters attached to the camp’s barracks often negotiate repairs and equipment with the brokers based at the Spear Exchange.

Chief among the brokers at the exchange is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Yarren|Yarren]]** (guard, he/him), an excitable retired sergeant with an eye for good steel. Yarren’s shop offers all heavy and light weapons, along with armor worth up to 120 marks.

If the PCs impressed Bordin or if they assisted Yarren’s friend and colleague Nen in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]], Yarren has heard of their deeds and offers them a 20 percent discount.

### The Ornery Chull

*Tavern*

The dark, smoky common rooms of the Ornery Chull are alive with the drunken revelry of Kholin soldiers. Served by a woman named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Ka|Ka]]** (commoner, she/her), the bar provides only a limited selection of drinks; when celebrating a successful battle on the plateaus, the soldiers rarely care to spend their spheres on quality. However, the doors of the Chull are also open to more well-off guests. Wealthy merchants often hire out private back rooms to discuss their deals and ply clients with serviceable wines. The merchant Rebin, whom PCs may have befriended in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]], supplies the wine.

A PC looking for a place to stay can hire out one of the few available modest guest rooms for 2 marks a night. Any PC who liberally spends their spheres at the bar can convince Ka to let them sleep in the common room for free with a successful DC 10 Persuasion test.

### The Blueworks

*Workshop*

Given that the king’s mother Navani is a patron of the sciences, many ardents and scholars are keen to practice their crafts at the Kholin warcamp. The proprietor of the Blueworks, a young artifabrian named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Ishali|Ishali]]** (expert, she/her), is particularly militant about attracting customers. Her improvised songs about the latest fabrial technologies are a common refrain in nearby streets.

Ishali sells standard fabrials worth up to 500 marks. She’s also happy to share her crafting materials with any characters willing to advertise for her—especially if they can sing.

## Searching the Warcamps

The party has free rein to explore the warcamps and follow whichever hooks interest them. In the six scenes in this section, the PCs can learn about the Alethi and the conflict on the Shattered Plains, explore their characters’ goals and backstories, and progress the overarching plot by looking for Liss, the Stone Shamans’ Alethi contact.

## Leads and Favors

Each scene in this section can award the party a **lead**: a clue that’ll help the party track down Liss. These leads are detailed in the upcoming [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Finding Liss|Finding Liss]] section.

Many NPCs in this chapter also offer **favors**: a positive relationship that the party can leverage during this chapter’s final endeavor to reach Taln. Each time the party earns a favor from someone, be sure the players record who that person is; they may want to call in those favors during the [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|Herald of War]] section at the end of this chapter, or in the [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|Traveling to Rathalas]] section at the beginning of chapter 3.

The Warcamp Scenes table summarizes both hooks, while the scenes for each section detail the hooks. Regardless of which hook you choose, each scene provides a lead about Liss (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Finding Liss|Finding Liss]]).

### Finding Liss

Liss keeps a low profile and hasn’t used her name in any of her warcamp dealings. The Stone Shamans’ last meeting with her was in the Red Rockbud gambling den. Tracking Liss down requires following a series of leads, each of which is based on rumors from people who witnessed that meeting. The leads should be revealed in the following order:

1.  A Thaylen menagerie owner won a strange chicken while gambling with a mysterious Shin traveler.

2.  A Shin traveler visited the Aladar warcamp to attend the Red Rockbud, a gambling den.

3.  The Shin traveler met with a full-figured, attractive woman with dark hair in the Red Rockbud.

4.  A woman matching that description has been seen wandering the Outer Market near the Crem Oven bakery.

Move to [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|The Contact]] when the party either has obtained all four leads that they need to find Liss, or once they finish five scenes. If they don’t have enough leads to track down Liss, she finds the party instead.

### Po’ahu Stirs

Each time the party acquires a lead, Taszo’s peakspren Po’ahu grows more confident that they’re the right people to continue Taszo’s mission. However, since her connection to the world is weakened without Taszo’s bond, she can only communicate in limited ways.

Whenever a PC acquires a lead or acts with exceptional dependability (which is a core Stoneward tenet), roll on the Po’ahu’s Reactions table. Po’ahu fully emerges when the party meets Liss in [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|The Contact]]. If the PCs drift far off task and abandon Taszo’s request, Po’ahu’s rock makes cracking sounds and shows signs of crumbling to dust.

## Chasm Offensive

Bordin asks the characters to appease Sergeant Ellar, who insists on following up on reports of Parshendi scouts near the camps.

The party can encounter Sergeant Ellar through either the active hook or passive hook. After choosing your hook, proceed to [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Speaking With Bordin|Speaking With Bordin]].

### Active Hook

If the PCs ask Bordin for aid in finding information about Liss or the bandits’ glyphs, he agrees to help them if they can assist him with Ellar.

### Passive Hook

Knowing the PCs are capable and outside the chain of command, Bordin seeks them out for assistance.

### Speaking With Bordin

When Bordin speaks with the party, he exasperatedly explains his predicament:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “A scout reported seeing some potential enemy movement not far from the warcamps. It’s unlikely the report is accurate, but Ellar believes it’s a Parshendi strike force that’s organizing a counter squadron to ambush our troops. If you’re willing, I’d like you to volunteer for this mission. In the meantime, that’ll free up me and my men to follow up on your question.”

In addition, Bordin offers the PCs 20 marks each for their trouble. If they agree, Bordin informs Ellar that he’s assigned the party to assist Ellar, referencing their competence during the bandit attack. Though irritated, Ellar doesn’t question Bordin.

### The Search

When the party is ready, Sergeant Ellar leads them onto the Shattered Plains with orders to track the Parshendi squad, dispatch them, and return with a full report.

Each PC must make a DC 14 Survival test. If at least half of them succeed, they catch the listeners unaware and have plenty of time to prepare. Otherwise, the listeners realize they’re being tracked and ready themselves for a confrontation.

Regardless of whether the listeners notice the party, read the following to set the scene:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The trail leads from the blasted plateau onto the chasm floor. Here, the air is thick with moisture, and the walls are alive with moss and shelled plants. You come to a narrow gorge choked with storm debris: shattered rocks, wet crem, and broken bodies of Alethi and Parshendi soldiers alike.\
>      Four Parshendi soldiers step between the bodies, careful not to touch or disturb them. Three of the soldiers are powerfully built and armored with thick carapace. One stands apart from the others—she is slender, with delicate carapace covering her forearms and shoulders.

![[pg040_Chasm Battle Aftermath_LOGAN FELICIANO.webp]]

**LOGAN FELICIANO**

These singers are of the listener people who live on the Shattered Plains. They are led by **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Calinai|Calinai]]** (nimbleform singer, she/her); if the listeners are aware of the party’s approach, she alerts her companions as the PCs draw near.

Calinai is accompanied by three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singers]]**, whom she has persuaded to help search this chasm for her fallen sister’s body. Despite listener taboos about touching the dead, Calinai is determined to recover a treasured ancient heirloom given to the sisters by their late mother: a bracelet made from strung-up gemstones, etched with glyphs associated with the Radiant Order of Willshapers. The listeners are cautious this close to the warcamps and prepared to fight Alethi soldiers.

The listeners assume any approaching human is a combatant in their years-long war, and they attack on sight. If approached only by a singer character, the listeners explain their purpose peacefully until any human allies are revealed, then they attack.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> If a PC manages to talk down Calinai with only minor bloodshed on both sides, they may attract the attention of a lightspren or cultivationspren.

### Standing Down

The party can try to convince Calinai to stand down, though combat continues until they succeed. A PC can Use a Skill (1) to contribute to this conversation (see Resolving the Conversation). If the listeners were initially approached by just a singer PC, the listeners are more inclined to believe them, giving each PC an advantage on the first test they make to contribute.

#### **Resisting Influence**

Calinai has a Cognitive defense of 15, a Spiritual defense of 15, and 4 focus. When a PC succeeds on a test, Calinai spends focus to resist influence. After years of war with the Alethi, it only costs Calinai 1 focus to resist a human’s influence; for singer PCs, Calinai must spend 2 focus as usual.

#### **Resolving the Conversation**

When Calinai can no longer spend focus to resist, she asks her companions to stand down—but only if the party stops fighting too.

### **Rewards**

Once the party either defeats the listeners or convinces them to depart in peace, the PCs can find valuable equipment among the storm-wracked remains: weapons and armor worth 100 marks total.

If a PC succeeds on a DC 14 Deduction test to search the area, they also find Calinai’s treasured bracelet, worth 50 marks to merchants in the warcamps. If Calinai told the party what she was looking for and they try to return the bracelet, they can easily catch up to the listeners. If the party tries to barter the bracelet with her, she agrees to give them an uncut gem worth 25 marks. If the party offers the bracelet freely, she gratefully gives them the uncut gem and a favor.

Upon their return, Ellar grills the party for a full report. If the PCs omit facts, they must make a DC 14 Deception test. On a failure, or if the PCs admit to avoiding bloodshed, Ellar is furious and holds a grudge. On a success, Ellar begrudgingly accepts their report.

Regardless, Bordin finds the party and thanks them. He pays the promised 20 marks each and shares a lead about Liss. If the PCs want to know more about the glyphs found on the bandits, he sends them to the Devotary of the Mind (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Lost Radiants|Lost Radiants]]). If the characters manage to stay on Ellar’s good side, Bordin also owes them a favor.

## Guilty Spheres

A gambling den in the Aladar warcamp is the best place to learn about the clandestine comings and goings of the criminal world.

The party can find their way to the gambling den through either the active hook or passive hook. After choosing your hook, proceed to [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#The Red Rockbud|The Red Rockbud]].

### Active Hook

PCs who don’t trust the official records or military personnel might seek out underground sources of information. If they ask non-military folk or the merchants they met in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapter 1]], they’re pointed toward the Red Rockbud—a dive in the Aladar warcamp—and its well-connected proprietor Nel.

If the party has received lead 2, they’ve already learned about the Red Rockbud and can easily find it by asking anyone in camp.

### Passive Hook

After the PCs have earned a reputation for themselves by finishing at least two other scenes around the warcamps, Nel invites them to the Red Rockbud.

### The Red Rockbud

In a stark difference from the discipline of the Kholin warcamp, the guards at the entrance of the Aladar warcamp don’t mind people coming and going. When the party follows the directions to the Red Rockbud, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You follow the directions to a Soulcast bunker at the crater rim of the Aladar warcamp, alive with the shouts and laughter of the drunken patrons. True to the den’s name, a cluster of rockbuds grow around the bunker. Their rocky shells have been carelessly slapped with crimson paint. The dim glow of spherelight and the warmth of the hearth draw your attention to dug-out stairs leading down to the venue proper. Inside the Red Rockbud, rowdy guests share their drinks, stories, and forbidden games of chance.

As newcomers, the PCs are immediately taken to meet the Red Rockbud’s hostess **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Nel|Nel]]** (expert, she/her).

![[Nel_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Nel
> **Characteristics:** Ambitious, cunning, unassuming.
>
> **Goal:** Acquire information to sell.
>
> **Appearance:** Nel (“NEHL”; she/ her) is a dark-eyed Bavlander human with pale skin and stringy black hair. She wears a green cloak and breeches, and her safehand is exposed.
>
> Nel is a shrewd Bavlander who doesn’t care for Vorin traditions. Her eyes are set on greater things, and she came to the Shattered Plains in search of opportunity. The Alethi in the warcamps underestimate her talents, dismissing her as a backwater rube. She plays up her hapless persona to draw information and spheres out of her targets, selling whatever she learns to the highest bidder.

### Gambling

If the PCs ask Nel for information, she states she won’t share information with just anyone; she must consider them a friend first. A friend is someone to whom she owes a favor, and favor is her gambling currency of choice. She then invites them to a game of Pieces.

Ask which PCs choose to participate in the game with Nel. In addition, an Aladar soldier named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Lirdan|Lirdan]]** (guard, he/him) drunkenly joins the game uninvited.

Run this Pieces game as a special conversation (see Resolving the Game). Contributions are tests to play. When a character can’t resist influence (either by choice or because they have 0 focus), they fumble a bet, lose 1d6 marks, and must leave the Pieces game. Lirdan loses after the second time a PC succeeds on a test.

![[pg042_Game of pieces_SAMI RYTKÖNEN.webp]]

**SAMI RYTKÖNEN**

#### **Resisting Influence**

Nel has a Cognitive defense of 13, a Spiritual defense of 13, and 4 focus. When a PC succeeds on a test, Nel spends focus to resist influence. As an accomplished owner of a gambling den, it only costs Nel 1 focus to resist influence (rather than the usual 2 focus).

For a PC to resist influence, they must spend 2 focus and satisfy Nel’s curiosity by answering a question about their backstory. They can answer these questions truthfully, or they can make a Deception test against her Spiritual defense. On a failed Deception test, the PC loses an additional 1 focus. Nel asks pointed questions, hoping to either get a sense of who the truthful characters are or find blackmail material.

#### **Playing the Game**

Ask what strategy the PCs take. Characters who aren’t playing can sneakily aid those still in the game—though if they fail a test, they’re caught and removed from the building by two guards. Possible contributions to the conversation include:

- **Play by the Rules.** Characters can ask the best questions of the “mink” to ensure victory (DC 13 Deduction), mislead other players (DC 13 Deception), or build a coalition of players to peek at the right pieces and ensure victory through teamwork (DC 13 Leadership).

- **Mind Games.** Characters can remain poised and give away nothing about the arrangement of their pieces (DC 13 Discipline), concentrate on the reactions of their opponents (DC 13 Insight), or manipulate other players (DC 13 Persuasion). Since Nel vastly underestimates singer characters, singer PCs gain an advantage on these tests.

- **Cheating.** Characters can palm or switch some of the game pieces to win a crucial round (DC 13 Thievery).

> [!tip] Pieces
> Predicting the future is prohibited in Vorinism, especially in Alethkar and Jah Keved. Nevertheless, Vorins find other ways to gamble, and the game of Pieces is common in the warcamps. In Pieces, one player (the “mink”) arranges ten unique game tokens face down. The other players try to figure out the pieces’ order, and they bet on the outcome.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Gambling Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Gambling Opportunities and Complications]]

#### **Resolving the Game**

If Nel is unable to resist influence, the characters win the game. If all PCs drop out before Nel, the conversation fails.

**Success.** Nel is happy to pay what she owes: either `3d6` marks or a favor. Nel makes it clear that as a hostess of an illicit underground gambling den, she’s privy to all sorts of information, and sharing is her preferred way to repay favors. If the party asks Nel about Liss, she gives a new lead from the list.

**Failure.** Nel initially refuses to provide the party with information. However, Lirdan becomes incensed over his defeat. To save face, he challenges one of the PCs to a duel. If they defeat the upstart guard, Nel warms up to the characters and offers them a lead anyway.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> Sharing important or emotionally powerful secrets with Nel may attract the attention of a Cryptic spren.

## Lost Radiants

An ardent known as **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Shansan|Shansan]]** (expert, they/them) is the local expert on esoteric glyphs. They’re the perfect source of information about the glyphs borne by the strange bandits who attacked Bordin’s caravan.

The party can encounter Shansan through either the active hook or passive hook. After choosing your hook, proceed to [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Words of Radiance|Words of Radiance]].

### Active Hook

If the PCs ask around about the glyph found on the bandits, they’re pointed to a small temple bunker in Kholin warcamp dedicated to the Devotary of the Mind. When the party arrives, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The directions lead you to a small temple built into the wall of a military bunker. Inside, a shrine to the ten Heralds dominates the single room, and shelves overflow with religious texts and tablets.\
>      A pair of ardents work carefully: one at their desk, the other at the shrine. Both have shaved heads and wear gray robes. The ardent at the desk, a slight scholar with spectacles, looks up from their writing and narrows their eyes.\
>      “It’s you, isn’t it? The ones traveling with Sergeant Ellar?”

### Passive Hook

Bordin’s and Ellar’s soldiers have been telling tall tales about their exploits in the Unclaimed Hills, and rumors about bandits with strange glyphs have been spreading around the Kholin warcamp. Hearing these tales, Shansan actively seeks out the party when they rest in the warcamp. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A young ardent with a shaved head, gray robes, and glasses rushes to your side clutching a dusty tome in their slender hands.\
>      “It’s you, isn’t it!?” they exclaim. “You were the ones with Sergeant Ellar’s caravan!”

After striking up a conversation, Shansan asks the party to come by their temple when they have the time to spare.

![[Shansan_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Shansan
> **Characteristics:** Meticulous, well-read, dramatic.
>
> **Goal:** Research esoteric glyphs and share knowledge.
>
> **Appearance:** Shansan (“SHAN-suhn”; they/them) is a gray-robed human ardent with a shaved head and spectacles.
>
> Shansan is an earnest scholar with an eye for detail. They tend to babble and fidget, and their excited exclamations are often accompanied by sincerityspren. Shansan loves to quote obscure texts and deliver dramatic readings to a captive audience.

### Words of Radiance

Whether the PCs seek out Shansan or are found by them, the ardent is eager to discuss the events in the Unclaimed Hills. They help without expecting any compensation—except, perhaps, a good word to the Kholin officers who pay for their materials. Though Shansan is enthusiastic, they are accompanied by a far more skeptical ardent named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Kalay|Kalay]]** (expert, she/her).

Shansan has heard the rumors about bandits who bear strange glyphs attacking Ellar’s men. After the party describes their encounter with the bandits, Shansan excitedly fetches a worn copy of a tome called Words of Radiance—a record of the orders of the Knights Radiant and their abilities. However, Kalay is highly critical of the Radiants and heckles Shansan’s explanations.

- **What does the glyph mean?** Shansan says, “It is an archaic version of the ‘Palah’ glyph. I believe it represents the Truthwatchers, an order of the ancient Knights Radiant.” Kalay adds, “An ironic name, if ever I heard one.”

- **Who were the Knights Radiant?** Shansan says, “An ancient order said to possess amazing powers. However, they betrayed their oaths and abandoned humanity thousands of years ago.” Kalay adds, “They’re widely considered a disgrace!”

- **Who were the Truthwatchers?** Shansan says, “An order of Radiants concerned with… well, it’s a matter of some debate. Allow me to quote: ‘Now, as the Truthwatchers were esoteric in nature, their order being formed entirely of those who never spoke or wrote of what they did, in this lies frustration for those who would see their exceeding secrecy from the outside; they were not naturally inclined to explanation…’ I’m afraid much has been lost to time.”

- **Could the bandits be Truthwatchers?** Shansan says, “These bandits wouldn’t be the first to adopt the imagery of the disgraced Radiants, but it would be absurd to suggest they actually possess the Radiants’ mythical powers.” Kalay adds, “The Radiants were little better than bandits. It sounds fitting to me.”

- **What mythical powers did they have?** Kalay interjects, “This knowledge was lost for good reason.” Shansan adds, “Supposedly the Radiants were capable of ‘Surgebinding,’ a method of manipulating the fundamental forces of Roshar. Gravitation, Illumination, Cohesion—that sort of thing.”

- **What were the other orders?** Shansan can provide the names of the ten orders and the names of their surges, but little else. The ardent’s copy of Words of Radiance is missing many key pages—much to Kalay’s delight.

If pressed for more, Shansan regretfully states that they’d need to spend more time reading Words of Radiance, and to perhaps procure additional reading materials. A PC can convince Shansan to part with the book with a successful DC 16 Persuasion test.

A PC can otherwise impress Shansan with an interest in the Knights Radiant, and they can impress Kalay with a condemnation of the Radiants. Having identified a potential ally or source of information, the ardents offer a favor if it would help the characters track down the bandits.

If the party shows and describes Taszo’s strange stone to the ardents, Kalay asks the characters to leave immediately, as she’s worried about strange spren.

## Menacing Menagerie

A Thaylen named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Ryvlk|Ryvlk]]** (expert, he/him) has brought a traveling menagerie to the Shattered Plains, hoping to one-up an Azish rival. During this scene, his newly captured whitespines break free, endangering the PCs.

The party can encounter Ryvlk’s menagerie through either the active hook or passive hook. After choosing your hook, proceed to [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Fantastic Chickens|Fantastic Chickens]].

### Active Hook

If the party mentions the Stone Shamans or asks about Shin in the warcamps, they’re pointed toward Ryvlk’s menagerie, which is set up outside the crater wall of the Ruthar warcamp. Ryvlk has boasted about his dealings with the Shin and is currently exhibiting a collection of strange Shinovar chickens.

### Passive Hook

The PCs encounter Ryvlk’s menagerie while moving between warcamps. Ever the showman, Ryvlk draws the characters in to witness his wonders.

### Fantastic Chickens

When the party approaches Ryvlk’s menagerie, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A medley of painted wagons forms a half-circle outside the crater wall of the warcamp. Taking advantage of the beaten path between the highprinces’ domains, the Thaylen ringleader calls out to passersby. His long eyebrows and beard are dyed blue, and he wears a colorful motley vest.\
>      “Come see the weird and wonderful fauna from every corner of Roshar! From tiny cremling to lurching greatshell!” The man waves toward the cages stacked alongside his wagons. “We have rare fish all the way from the Purelake! Dare you face the fearsome whitespines? Or perhaps you seek the rare Shinovar chicken!”

Ryvlk is a performer and a charlatan, and he has pitched his menagerie here to avoid enemies he’s made in the Outer Market. Most of his display pieces are partially or wholly fake.

Ryvlk takes great pride in showing off his dozen colorful Shin chickens. A character who succeeds on a DC 12 Deduction or Lore test recognizes that most of them have dyed feathers and are the same kind of chickens enjoyed on dinner tables throughout Alethkar. However, Ryvlk has two legitimate treasures: a tiny red-breasted songbird from Shinovar and a goose from the Horneater Peaks.

If questioned about the songbird, Ryvlk lies that he won it in a test of wits against a mysterious Shin monk who wouldn’t speak. A PC can recognize the deception with a successful DC 8 Insight test. If called out on this ruse, Ryvlk relents and offers lead 2: He won the songbird gambling with a Shin traveler in the Aladar warcamp, which is “basically the same thing.”

If the party already has lead 2, Ryvlk instead offers lead 3: The Shin he gambled against in the Aladar warcamp spoke at length with a full-figured, attractive woman with dark hair. If the party already has both of those leads, he gives them the remaining lead: Ryvlk later saw the same woman wandering the Outer Market near the Crem Oven bakery.

> [!example] Roleplaying Ryvlk
> **Characteristics:** Flamboyant, fast-talking, reckless.
>
> **Goal:** Outdo his rivals to be the most magnificent menagerie in the warcamps.
>
> **Appearance:** Ryvlk (“RIH-vilk”; he/him) is a Thaylen human whose appearance is as striking as his personality. His long eyebrows and beard are dyed blue and his clothes are a riot of color.
>
> As far as Ryvlk is concerned, an exciting lie is worth far more than a boring truth. He talks loudly, spins tall tales, and drapes himself and his wares in as many colors as he can. His curiosity for the world’s wonders is matched only by his tendency to take great risks. Ryvlk is particularly keen to one-up an Azish menagerie keeper named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Volikk|Volikk]]** (commoner, she/her), who is pitched in the Outer Market. Ryvlk considers Volikk’s whitespine “dull, boring, and a travesty against entertainment.”

> [!tip] Splitting the Party

To compensate, you can let the escaping whitespine’s commotion alert the rest of the PCs to the danger so they can join the scene. Alternatively, consider reducing the scene to one juvenile whitespine instead of two.

## Scribe’s Spanreed

### Active Hook

If the PCs ask around for information about Liss, try to track someone down, or seek information about the warcamps, they’re pointed toward Bettani’s bunker. She’s a known people-watcher and coordinates messaging between the warcamps. When the characters enter her bunker, read the following:

### Passive Hook

Bettani corrals the characters while they wander through the Kholin warcamp. Read the following:

>      “You, there! Please! I need assistance!”\

> [!example] Roleplaying Bettani
> **Characteristics:** Industrious, scattered, proud.
>
> **Goal:** Get her spanreed repaired to ensure the delivery of urgent messages.
>
> **Appearance:** Bettani (“bet-TAH-nee”; she/her) is a visibly harried Alethi human with black hair and wearing a teal havah.

### Helping Bettani

Bettani explains that one of her spanreeds is broken, but she can’t take time away from work to fix it herself. There’s far too much transcription to be done, and far too many urgent messages to send.

Bettani can reveal a lead to the characters, but only after they either help fix her spanreed or run some urgent messages for her. Bettani’s missives pertain to purchases from several trading posts in the Sebarial warcamp. They need to be sent quickly before rival camps snap up the best deals. Though the Sebarial warcamp won’t allow anyone to enter without authorization, Bettani’s letters include the glyph granting permission to enter.

If the characters decide to help Bettani, run an endeavor (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). Potential approaches include:

**Fixing the Spanreed.** A critical component of the spanreed is broken. Identifying the issue requires knowledge of the science behind spanreed construction (DC 12 Lore), and fixing it requires technical handiwork (DC 14 Crafting). A character might also discover flaws in its original construction (DC 10 Deduction) or find a missing component among Bettani’s papers (DC 12 Perception).

**Delivering the Missives.** The party can try to run the letters to the Sebarial warcamp themselves. They can weave through the bustling crowds (DC 12 Agility), spot a shortcut (DC 12 Perception), or climb the crater walls of the camps (DC 14 Athletics).

**Finding Other Couriers.** The warcamps are filled with messengers. A PC could order one to help (DC 14 Leadership) or negotiate with one to deliver a message for 2 marks (DC 12 Persuasion).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Bettani Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 4 successes before 3 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

**Success.** The PCs earn Bettani’s goodwill by repairing the spanreed or delivering some of Bettani’s orders to the Sebarial traders in good time. Bettani is thrilled by either outcome. She offers the party a favor, along with a new ***spanreed*** paired with one of her own: enough to stay in touch in the future.

Bettani also gives the PCs access to her extensive collection of books and her records of comings and goings in the Kholin warcamp. If the PCs check the records, offer them a lead about Liss.

**Failure.** Either Bettani’s spanreed seems broken beyond repair without time to source new parts, or her missives arrive at the trading post long after another buyer scoops the deal. Bettani offers 20 marks for the effort but doesn’t make her records available.

## Weeper’s Eyes

When Liss hears rumors that someone may be looking for her, she sends a spy to keep an eye on the party. However, Liss has deliberately chosen a patsy—someone clumsy enough to get spotted—so Liss can get the measure of the characters by how they react.

Don’t introduce this scene until the party has completed at least one other scene in this [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|Searching the Warcamps]] section. There is no active hook for this scene, only a passive hook.

### Passive Hook

Whenever the party moves between locations in the warcamps and Outer Market, Liss’s spy makes a Stealth test (+5) against the PC with the highest Spiritual defense. On a failure, the PC spots him acting suspiciously. Read the following when he is spotted:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A bedraggled man with long wet hair leans out from the side of a bunker, fiddling nervously with a knife. His gaze closely follows you, and his eyes widen when he realizes he’s been spotted. He turns tail and scampers down the alleyway into a crowded street market.

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Vedolin|Vedolin]]** (expert, he/him), the spy, was once a textile merchant in the Outer Market. After losing his money on risky ventures, Vedolin has taken to begging and thievery to earn spheres for a ride back to Alethkar. Like many others, Liss has taken advantage of his precarity.

### Special Rule: Closing the Distance

If the characters decide to chase Vedolin, run a Pursuit endeavor (see “Pursuit Endeavors” in chapter 12 of the Stormlight Handbook). In this type of endeavor, instead of tracking successes and failures, you track the distance between pursuer and target. For this Pursuit, the starting distance is 3 and the escape distance is 5. Each time Vedolin succeeds on a test, the current distance increases by one. Each time a PC succeeds on a test, the current distance decreases by one. If the current distance reaches 0, the party catches him. If the current distance reaches 5 (the escape distance), Vedolin gets away (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]).

### Catching Vedolin

As a spy, give Vedolin an advantage on Agility and Stealth tests. As the party pursues him, potential approaches include:

**Taking on the Crowds.** The warcamps are busy with soldiers, merchants, and administrators about their business. A PC might navigate through while avoiding disturbance (Agility vs. his +2 Agility) or command the crowds (Leadership vs. his +3 Persuasion).

**Scaling the Rooftops.** From the upper floors of the bunkers and markets, a PC might track Vedolin (Perception vs. his +1 Stealth) or leap between rooftops to gain ground (Athletics vs. his +2 Agility).

**Looking for Shortcuts.** A chase isn’t all about speed! A PC could figure out a quicker route through the alleys (Deduction vs. his +2 Survival) or intuit Vedolin’s next route (Insight vs. his +2 Deception).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Vedolin Opportunities or Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Vedolin Opportunities and Complications]]

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party closes the current distance to 0 or otherwise apprehends Vedolin. The endeavor fails if the current distance reaches 5.

**Success.** The characters corner Vedolin and he surrenders. Under duress, he explains he was paid to watch them by a terrifying killer known as the Weeper. Vedolin says his employer protected her identity by hiding her face and meeting in darkness, but he did notice one useful thing—here, he offers the characters a lead about Liss.

With a successful DC 16 Intimidation test, Vedolin can also be convinced to offer a favor in the future.

**Failure.** Vedolin disappears into the hustle and bustle of the warcamps, never to be seen again.

> [!example] Roleplaying Vedolin
> **Characteristics:** Wary, desperate, slippery.
>
> **Goal:** Improve his station and opportunities.
>
> **Appearance:** Vedolin (“VEH-doh-lin”; he/him) is a bedraggled Alethi human with long wet hair.
>
> Vedolin knows full well that he’s being exploited, but he sees every dubious opportunity as a way out of his dire situation. He is skittish, a fast talker, and constantly looks over his shoulder.

## The Contact

However, if the party doesn’t acquire enough leads, they can still make progress. Once they’ve played through five of the scenes described in the previous section, Liss finds them instead. Read the following when the characters next rest:

## Liss’s Background

![[pg049_Liss, the Assassin_ERNANDA SOUZA.webp]]

**Liss, the Assassin** **-** **ERNANDA SOUZA**

The Stone Shamans’ contact **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Liss|Liss]]** (duelist Shardbearer, she/her) is a secretive woman who has worked as a spy and assassin for some of the most powerful people in Roshar.

To hide the fact she uses a Shardblade, Liss gouges out the eyes of her victims. She has therefore become known in underground circles as the Weeper.

Liss’s journey toward working with the Stone Shamans began several years ago. She became exposed to Shin culture while she “employed” a Shin servant named Szeth-son-son-Vallano. Szeth was later implicated in the murder of King Gavilar Kholin on the very same night that Liss met Jasnah Kholin to discuss a contract to spy on her sister-in-law.

In the aftermath of Szeth’s assassination attempt, Liss sought out answers of her own, curious to know more about her ex-servant’s strange powers. In doing so, she met the Stone Shamans, learned about the capabilities of the Honorblades, and agreed to a contract to be the shamans’ eyes and ears in the warcamps.

![[Liss_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Liss
> **Characteristics:** Disguised, ruthless, inquisitive.
>
> **Goal:** Help the Shin Stone Shamans acquire the Honorblade.
>
> **Appearance:** Liss (“LISS”; she/her) is a full-figured human with dark hair. Her dark dress and boots are stylish but practical, and her emerald jewelry adds a touch of sophistication.
>
> Liss deliberately drifts between a smooth Alethi accent and a brusque Bav accent to keep her origins mysterious. When she’s not working “in-character,” she’s casual with everyone she meets and isn’t intimidated by rank or title. Liss is always curious and eager for information.

## Share and Share Alike

Once Liss is sure the characters aren’t here to settle an old score, she presses them for details on what happened to Taszo’s Stone Shaman contingent. She answers the following questions freely:

- **Who are you?** “Call me Liss. I’m a specialist in keepin’ an eye on things, if you catch my meaning. I work with clients who value discretion.”

- **How did you get involved with the Stone Shamans?** “I once worked with a strange Shin bloke who got me interested in their people. I later met the Stone Shamans on my way west through Jah Keved. Turned out they had a need for people like me, and one thing led to another…”

- **What do you want with the Herald’s Blade?** “It’ll fulfill my contract to the shamans. They want it safely locked away in Shinovar with the others, and I figure it’s better to work with the folks who have the other nine Honorblades than to strike out on my own.”

- **What exactly is an Honorblade?** “According to legends and dogma, they’re the swords wielded by the ten Heralds. Each one is more than just a Shardblade—it grants powers lost since the fall of the Knights Radiant.”

- **Who are the Knights Radiant?** “More legends from ages past. Supposedly they used lost powers to save us from Desolations, but they vanished thousands of years ago.”

- **Who else might be hunting for the Honorblade?** “That Blade means power you could only dream of. More’n a Shardblade. More’n any Shardplate. More’n kingdoms. For that kind of risk, you need connections.”

### Po’ahu Emerges

## Ardentia Infiltration

If the PCs choose to sneak into the ardentia monastery, run it as an endeavor. [[Scenes Index|Map 2.2]] depicts the temple complex that houses the monastery, and [[Scenes Index|map 2.3]] depicts the interior of the monastery itself.

### Special Rule: Favors

During prior stages of the adventure, the party had the chance to meet and make connections with people in and around the warcamps (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Leads and Favors|Leads and Favors]]). If the PCs have a friendly relationship with a relevant NPC or if they’re owed a favor, they can leverage that relationship to gain an advantage on a test. They can do this once for each relationship or favor owed.

The NPC granting the favor need not be present in the scene to grant an advantage. Instead, a player can narrate a “flashback” to describe how their character learned or arranged something crucial in the past.

### Obstacles

During this endeavor, the party must overcome a series of five obstacles; the first four each require two successful tests to bypass (see [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). The PCs face the following obstacles in order:

- **Kholin Guards.** In the heart of the Kholin warcamp, several of the highprince’s **[[Actors/Adversaries/Guard|guards]]** watch for signs of trouble at the temple complex’s perimeter (see [[Scenes Index|map 2.2]]). The PCs must bypass them to enter the monastery. They could present forged orders for a meeting in the monastery (DC 15 Crafting), build rapport to convince the guards they’re no threat (DC 12 Persuasion), or scale the walls (DC 14 Athletics).

![[pg055_Map 2-3_Monastery Interior_JEFF TODD.webp]]

**Map 2 3: Monastery Interior** **-** **JEFF TODD**

- **Ardents on Duty.** Once the party reaches the monastery (see [[Scenes Index|map 2.3]]) in the northeast of the temple complex, they find that ardents are seeing patients in the outermost reception and treatment areas. The PCs could convince the ardents to let them pass (DC 12 Deception or Persuasion), sneak by without being seen (DC 14 Stealth) or find a secret entrance (DC 15 Deduction). Singers who aren’t obviously armed face less scrutiny from the ardents, who assume the singers are parshman workers with assigned duties. As a result, singer PCs gain an advantage on tests that don’t rely on speaking.

- **The Herald’s Cell.** Taln is one among many poor souls imprisoned in the monastery “for their own good.” The PCs must discover his exact location. Written records or senior ardents can reveal the inner hallway that leads to his cell. The PCs could watch ardents to glean information (DC 15 Insight), sneak into senior ardent offices (DC 14 Stealth), or search records for patient listings (DC 12 Deduction).

- **Whispers in the Dark.** According to the ardentia’s misguided practices, people with mental illness should be cared for by leaving them in darkness with as little stimulation as possible. To find Taln’s cell, the party must navigate the deepest corridors of the monastery while evading the patrolling ardents. The PCs could disguise themselves (DC 15 Crafting), use architectural or religious knowledge to intuit the layout (DC 12 Lore), or slip through the shadows (DC 14 Stealth).

- **The Cell Door.** Finally, the party must unlock Taln’s cell. This obstacle requires only one success to bypass. The PCs could convince an ardent to unlock the door (DC 16 Deception or Persuasion), pick the lock (DC 14 Thievery), or break the door open (DC 15 Athletics).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Monastery Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 9 successes before 4 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

### Talking to Taln

Po’ahu’s appearance grants Taln a few moments of lucidity, and he’s receptive to the party’s questions for the moment. Use this opportunity to impress the importance of Taszo’s mission in finding the Honorblade, steering the PCs toward the next stage of their adventure.

Taln can answer the following questions:

**What are the Desolations?** “The Voidbringers will return in Odium’s name, laying waste to all you’ve achieved. We must arm ourselves! We must prepare!”

**What should we do with the Honorblade?** “Find it. See it to safety. I… don’t know if I can wield it… as I am now.”

**Should we return the Blade to the Stone Shamans in Shinovar?** “The Shin people are our allies, yes. They will fight against the Voidbringers.”

**Where is the Blade?** Taln can draw on the bond between Herald and Honorblade to locate his Blade. He must give the party the information they need to continue their journey. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Herald of War stares into the darkness, takes a deep breath, and reaches out.\
>      “I can feel it. It is as if I could reach out and draw it through the mists, but it does not hear my call. It is in a sheltered city, within a rift. Yes, I know it: the city of Rathalathar.”

A PC with the Alethi expertise recognizes the description: this is Rathalas, a city burned to the ground by Dalinar Kholin several years ago. Other PCs who succeed on a DC 10 Deduction or Lore test recall that “Rathalathar” is an ancient name for the rift city in Alethkar. If the party returns to Liss to share this information, she points them to Rathalas.

After his moment of clarity, Taln withdraws and begins his mantra once more.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Saying the Words
> In [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|chapters 1]] and [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]], the PCs had opportunities to attract the attention of several different kinds of Radiant spren, including the peakspren Po’ahu. If a PC started bonding a spren, this scene with Taln should be a climactic moment, especially if the PC has already marked two milestones toward their First Ideal goal. Use this opportunity to have them mark the final milestone—and if it feels narratively appropriate, their spren now encourages them to “speak the Words.” The fledgling Radiant intuitively knows these words: “Life before death. Strength before weakness. Journey before destination.” If a PC hasn’t started bonding Po’ahu, then whichever PC has been most attentive to Po’ahu should consider taking the First Ideal talent when advancing to 3rd level.

## Son of Honor

The soldiers are six **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** from the Sadeas warcamp, loyal to Highmarshal Amaram. A PC who succeeds on a DC 16 Deception test can talk their way past the soldiers, who allow the party to pass without a fight. Otherwise, the soldiers are eager for a fight and they attack.

### Deceiving Amaram

If the PCs attempt to deceive or deflect, run the scene as a conversation (see Resolving the Conversation). Amaram is savvy, and his sterling reputation and close relationship with Dalinar give him authority in every warcamp, making this a challenging conversation.

#### **Resisting Influence**

Amaram has a Cognitive defense of 15, a Spiritual defense of 16, and 5 focus. He uses his Jarring Insight ability to make veiled threats, and he spends up to 4 focus to resist the party’s social tests. However, he’s not willing to spend his last focus on this conversation.

#### **Resolving the Conversation**

The conversation succeeds if Amaram would need to spend his last focus (which he chooses not to do) or if the PCs otherwise convince Amaram they have no information of value. The conversation fails if the characters are unconvincing, stubbornly aggressive, or caught in a lie.

Whether the PCs fail or play along, attracting the attention of this evil organization could create long-term challenges if the party ever returns to the warcamps.

- **Success.** Convinced the characters have no information of value, Amaram withdraws with his soldiers.

- **Failure.** Amaram laughs at the party’s attempts to talk their way out of a situation where he’s clearly in command. He tells them, “You’ve made an enemy today. As long as you stay in the warcamps, the Sons of Honor will be watching you.”

## Journeying Onward

After the party speaks with Amaram (or avoids attracting attention in the monastery), they can leave without further issue. As the characters regroup, rest, and debrief, they advance to 3rd level.
