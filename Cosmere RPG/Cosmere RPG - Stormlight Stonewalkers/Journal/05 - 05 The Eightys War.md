# 05: The Eighty's War

## Chapter Summary

While the party thwarted Ylt’s immediate plans in the Valley of the Nightwatcher, he remains a threat to the Herald Pailiah. The Nightwatcher encourages the PCs to travel to Emul's war-torn border, delivering a dangerous artifact to Nale.

## Running This Chapter

In this chapter, the Nightwatcher tasks the characters with safely delivering the raysium knife to the Herald Nale. To reach him, the party must navigate a battle between Emuli and Tukari forces. In this battle of the Eighty’s War, Emuli forces fight to retake Ashiqqil Keep and rescue an imprisoned Radiant named Vanzi.

After surviving this battle, the party engages the Herald of Justice in a high-stakes negotiation over Vanzi. This tense conversation could allow the party to gain valuable information about the Herald Pailiah— and perhaps even save the imprisoned Dustbringer.

This chapter closes as the party travels from the port city of Sesemalex Dar across the sea to Rall Elorim. This sea journey offers the opportunity for downtime, where each PC can advance their personal goals and reflect on their experiences so far.

Warfare is a major part of the Stormlight setting, and this chapter allows players a chance to play the pivotal role in a clash between scores of soldiers. To prepare for this chapter and learn how such a battle differs from typical combat, see the flowchart later in this chapter, and familiarize yourself with the “Mass Warfare” section in chapter 13 of the *Stormlight Handbook*.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 5th level and advance to 6th level during their ocean voyage to Rall Elorim.

The following decisions offer opportunities to introduce spren, strengthen spren bonds, or swear Ideals:

- A PC’s leadership, teamwork, or quick thinking during the battle at Ashiqqil Keep.

- How the PCs handle the final negotiation with Nale, Herald of Justice.

Characters with goals related to learning more about the Heralds, Honorblades, or Radiants have opportunities to do so when speaking with Nale or Vanzi.

## Call to Arms

After taking the opportunity to rest, the party can travel west across the mountains toward Emul, where they stumble into a fierce battle. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After a long trek west across the mountains, you come to a valley plunging down toward the heart of Emul, cutting a path through the jagged terrain. Smoke drifts from the fires of a keep that stands sentinel across the pass. From the distance comes the thunder of marching soldiers and the clash of steel on steel.

This castle, Ashiqqil Keep, defends the trails into Emul’s eastern territory. With its strategic position in the valley, travelers from Hexi must pass beneath its watchful gaze. However, it’s currently occupied by soldiers from Tukar.

## Battle for Ashiqqil Keep

This skirmish is part of the Eighty’s War, a seven- year-long conflict in which the Tukari attempt to wrest control of the capital city Sesemalex Dar from Emul. For Emul, Sesemalex Dar is their ancestral homeland, which they claim was given to them by the Heralds. Meanwhile, Tukar fights for their tyrannical leader, the “god-priest Tezim,” who sees the port city as rightfully his.

Sesemalex Dar is many miles to the west, but the Tukari are attacking strategic targets across the Emuli border. They’ve captured Ashiqqil Keep because it defends an important mountain pass, and they’ve claimed a powerful prisoner inside. This prisoner is Vanzi, an imprisoned Emuli with Radiant abilities (see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Nale’s Prisoner|Nale’s Prisoner]] later in this chapter). Though the Herald Nale doesn’t intervene in the battle, he has arrived to preside over Vanzi’s final judgment and execution.

### Battle Phases

The party has the chance to enter the battle, either by joining the fray or attempting to sneak through. If they do, the battle includes the following phases:

- **The Approach.** The PCs gather information and decide how to approach the battle.

- **Assault on the Walls.** The Emuli attempt to enter the castle by breaking down the gatehouse door.

- **Bolster the Troops.** Moments before the Emuli charge, the PCs rally their troops.

- **Make a Break for It.** The PCs can choose to escape the chaotic battle and seek Nale.

- **Storm the Keep.** The Emuli storm the fortress to secure their entry, but they meet resistance from a stormform Regal.

Each phase includes a **phase objective** and a **phase outcome**. The phase objective defines what the party is trying to do and when the phase is completed. The phase outcome describes what happens next, depending on the results of the phase.

See the Eighty’s War Flowchart for an overview of these battle phases, and see “Mass Warfare” in chapter 13 of the *Stormlight Handbook* for more guidance on running them.

## The Approach

During the first phase, the PCs collect information to help them approach the battle. The party is approaching from the highlands, giving them a clear view of the terrain and the forces arrayed against one another.

### Phase Objective

After the party gathers information, they must decide whether to join the Emuli or seek to bypass the battle.

### Axoq’s Plea

If **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Axoq|Axoq]]** (expert) is traveling with the party, he explains that the Tukari are invaders who have occupied Emuli land. Axoq wants to immediately run to his Emuli comrades’ aid; if a PC wishes to convince him to do otherwise, they must make a robust argument and succeed on a Persuasion or Leadership test against Axoq’s corresponding defense (both defenses are 13). If the PC succeeds on this test, Axoq stays with the party but encourages them to join the Emuli.

If the party instead chooses to immediately follow Axoq into battle, proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Assault on the Walls|Assault on the Walls]].

### Gathering Information

If the PCs seek information before fighting, run this phase as an endeavor (see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Phase Outcome|Phase Outcome]]). If Axoq is alive and with the group, the endeavor starts with 1 success. Potential approaches include:

- **Current Politics.** PCs may attempt to recall details of the war or the current political situation (DC 12 Lore). PCs with the Azish expertise gain an advantage on related Lore tests.

- **Spying.** PCs may apply their understanding of military tactics (DC 13 Deduction) or observe the forces (DC 14 Perception), identifying the Emuli soldiers wearing yellow headwraps and the Tukari soldiers wearing blue robes.

- **Interrogating a Straggler.** The PCs could come upon a lost Tukari spearman and charm (DC 14 Persuasion), deceive (DC 14 Deception), or interrogate him (DC 13 Intimidation). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

### Phase Outcome

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 3 successes before 2 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

**Failure.** The party learns only basic information: The Emuli forces are outmatched and suffered major losses in a previous assault. The Emuli soldiers look like civilians in mismatched armor, while the Tukari are well-organized and hold a fortified position. Without aid, the Emuli will surely lose this battle.

**Success.** The party learns everything listed in “Failure” above. They additionally learn the details (mentioned in the later [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Nale’s Prisoner|Nale’s Prisoner]] section) about Vanzi, the Emuli Radiant captured in the fall of the keep. The Tukari are also rumored to have a “Voidbringer champion” fighting on their side. Characters who join the Emuli forces after gathering this information gain the Determined condition.

#### **The Assault Begins**

Immediately after the endeavor, a horn signals the assault is imminent. If the PCs want to join the battle, proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Assault on the Walls|Assault on the Walls]].

If the PCs choose to let the battle to play out without intervention, the Tukari achieve a decisive victory, and if Axoq took part, he dies. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]].

## Assault on the Walls

If the characters join the battle, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Scores of Emuli soldiers stand in uneasy ranks, fearspren and anticipationspren gathering at their feet. Their armor is mismatched and they wear no uniforms, though they are distinguished by their brightly colored hair wraps. Spear infantry ready their weapons, while others lift ladders onto their shoulders. Nearby, four men heave a battering ram, steering it toward the fort’s gate. You’ve been tasked with protecting the ram.\
>      Atop the castle walls, Tukari soldiers are dressed in heavy blue robes with ringed mail armor. Their motions are precise and practiced as they await the ragtag assault.\
>      “For Vanzi!” the Emuli call out from below.\
>      The Tukari shout back from the battlements: “For the god-priest Tezim!”

### Phase Objective

If the PCs join the combat, they aid with one of the critical battering ram offensives, aimed at the main gate. They must guard the offensive long enough to break through the gate (shown on [[Scenes Index|map 5.1]]).

![[pg095_Map 5-1_Ashiqqi Keep_JEFF TODD.webp]]

**Map 5.1: Battle for Ashiqqil Keep** **-** **JEFF TODD**

### Running the Battle

Each force consists of scores of soldiers, but when running the battle, focus on only those that surround the PCs. Treat the large-scale conflict as an epic backdrop for the scene, while “zooming in” on how the party’s actions turn the tide of the battle.

The Emuli offensive contingent includes four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** carrying a battering ram together, with their weapons and shields slung to their backs. The party carries two ladders, which they can give to whichever two PCs they wish. The PCs and the Emuli troops start 30 feet from the keep walls, which are 15 feet high.

Eight Tukari **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]** are stationed on the walls, ready to rain down arrows on the attackers.

#### **Tukari Tactics**

Unless the Tukari archers find themselves in melee with a PC, they focus fire on anyone holding the ram, using their Immobilizing Shot (r) to target the closest characters.

#### **The Gate and the Battering Ram**

The Emuli spear infantry carrying the ram act on the same turn. Holding the ram requires both hands, and at least four characters are required to move and use the battering ram. Each turn, they Move (1) toward the gate (moving at the speed of the slowest character), and they may Brace (1). Using the Brace action with the ram protects only from attacks from the ramparts above.

### Event: Ashiqqil Gate Falls

Once the ram reaches the gate, create an Ashiqqil Gate Falls event. This positive event, which has three slots, tracks the structural integrity of the gate.

![[pg096_event card_Ashiqquil Gate Falls example.webp]]

#### **Progressing the Event**

As long as the battering ram is next to the gate and at least four characters hold it, Opportunities progress the event.

If a PC is one of the people holding the ram, they can Use a Skill (1) to make a DC 20 Athletics or Leadership test. On a success, they can progress the event by 1.

#### **Triggering the Event**

If you fill all three slots with Opportunities, see the “Ashiqqil Gate Falls” outcome in the [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Phase Outcome|Phase Outcome]] section.

### Battlefield Effects

The following effects are active during this phase of the battle:

- **Ladders.** The 20-foot-long ladders are used to scale the walls. A character can heave a ladder into place against the wall as 1. Once in place, anyone can climb the ladders to the top of the 15-foot-high walls. While climbing, a character is Slowed.

- **Ramparts.** Characters on the walls overlooking the battle can Brace (1) against the ramparts to defend against attacks from below.

- **Raised Stakes.** The battle is a high-stakes environment. Raise the stakes during at least one fast turn a round, and during at least one slow turn a round.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Tukari Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Tukari Opportunities and Complications]]

### Phase Outcome

What happens next is determined by whether the Ashiqqil Gate Falls event is triggered.

- **Ashiqqil Gate Falls.** If the event is triggered, then it’s clear the gate is about to break. At this point, the PCs have a chance to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Make a Break for It|Make a Break for It]]. If the party instead continues to fight, three spear infantry join them as they proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Storm the Keep|Storm the Keep]].

- **Ashiqqil Gate Holds.** The Emuli soldiers are defeated if the gate stands, and the four Emuli spear infantry with the PCs are killed. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Bolster the Emuli Troops|Bolster the Emuli Troops]].

## Bolster the Emuli Troops

![[pg097_Emuli Fortress Battle_KEVIN O’NEILL.webp]]

**KEVIN O’NEILL**

As Emuli soldiers fall at the gates, morale across the battlefield is threatened. Read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The gate to Ashiqqil Keep is splintered, but still standing. Behind you on the battlefield, the tide is turning against the Emuli.\
>      A small battalion of exhausted Emuli soldiers, many bloodied, run to you as arrows fly. “Captain Bexin is dead, we cannot take the gate! Fall back! Now is the only chance to save ourselves!” shouts the one in the lead.

The lead soldier is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Gamish|Gamish]]** (spear infantry, he/him).

### Phase Objective

The characters can rally the troops by convincing Gamish and those listening that the fight can be won. If they choose to do so, run a conversation with Gamish (see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Phase Outcome|Phase Outcome]]).

### Resisting Influence

Gamish has a Cognitive defense of 12, a Spiritual defense of 13, and 3 focus. He is a father of two— a fact that can be gleaned if a PC succeeds on a DC 12 Insight test to notice a small bracelet, a good luck charm from his youngest. He spends focus to resist arguments to stay and fight, unless the arguments mention his family.

### Phase Outcome

The conversation succeeds if the PCs convince Gamish to stay, either by making a particularly compelling argument or when he no longer has focus to resist their arguments. The conversation fails if the PCs fail two tests against him.

- **Success.** Inspired, Gamish calls out to other soldiers on the battlefield, who shout back with a rallying cry. He and three other spear infantry charge forward, pick up the battering ram, and bring down the gate with one final heave. At this point, the PCs can choose to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Make a Break for It|Make a Break for It]] or [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Storm the Keep|Storm the Keep]].

- **Failure.** No reinforcements arrive and the Emuli forces begin to retreat. Move straight to the [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Phase Outcome|Phase Outcome]] subsection of the "Storm the Keep" section to play out the end of the battle.

## Make a Break for It

When the gate falls, the PCs can attempt to slip past the chaos and seek the Herald of Justice inside the keep.

### Phase Objective

If the PCs wish to slip inside the keep, run an endeavor. Potential approaches include deceiving friendly or enemy combatants (DC 14 Deception), barreling through the battle (DC 15 Athletics), or finding a convenient entrance into the inner keep (DC 16 Perception). As they’re doing this during a pitched battle, raise the stakes often.

### Phase Outcome

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 4 successes before 3 failures or if they otherwise complete the objective.

**Success.** The party successfully escapes the battle and finds Nale. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]].

**Failure.** The PCs each take 5 (2d4) keen damage as they fail to escape the crush of soldiers, and they find themselves stuck in the storming of the keep. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Storm the Keep|Storm the Keep]]”

## Storm the Keep

If the characters stay to fight (or fail to escape inside the keep), they join the battle inside the keep’s outer wall.

### Phase Objective

Let the players know that the PCs will win the battle if three Emuli soldiers break past Tukari defenses (reaching any of the three keep entrances at the top of [[Scenes Index|map 5.1]]) or if Farriqa—the Regal accompanying the Tukari—is defeated.

![[pg098_Tukari Soldier_ARTUR MÓSCA.webp]]

**Tukari Soldier** **-** **ARTUR MÓSCA**

### Running the Battle

As the battle begins, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With a resonant crack, the ram bursts through the keep’s gate. Through the splintered wood, a figure stands within the keep, blocking your path: a strange-looking singer who stands over six feet tall. Her carapace is a latticework of red stripes, accented at barbed points. On each side of her stand two Tukari fighters, spears held toward you.\
>      The singer beholds you with calm hatred. Softly, her hairstrands begin to float. The air is thick with ozone and feels alive, buzzing. She points her spear at you. “Cross me at your peril.” Her words are accompanied by an ominous resonant rhythm.

All surviving soldiers from the last scene join the fray, starting just outside the gate or wherever they ended the last scene. In addition, four Tukari **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** bolster the troops from within the walls. Finally, the Tukari are aided by **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Farriqa|Farriqa]]** (stormform Regal, she/her), a Regal who came to the keep to argue for Vanzi’s execution and to discuss a potential alliance with Tukari forces in the area.

Farriqa and the Tukari spear infantry focus on stopping Emuli soldiers from breaking past the line, then they turn their attention to the PCs. The archers focus on immobilizing and killing Emuli spear infantry, then they turn their attention the PCs.

### Battlefield Effects

The following effect is active during this phase of the battle:

**Burning Oil.** The Tukari have readied burning oil to pour through murder holes onto Emuli soldiers storming the gatehouse. Once per round, a character on the walls above the gatehouse can Ready (2) an Interact action (1) to turn a crank that tips a massive barrel of hot oil as a target enters the passage. When that target enters the passage, they and all other characters in the passage take 7 (`2d6`) energy damage and must succeed on a DC 12 Discipline test or become Surprised until the end of their next turn. A maximum of two oil barrels can be poured in this way during the battle.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Final Push Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Final Push Opportunities and Complications]]

### Phase Outcome

If the PCs defeat Farriqa or if enough Emuli soldiers break into the inner keep, the tide of battle is turned. Emuli forces pour into the gates and the Tukari forces surrender. Either way, the PCs can enter the inner grounds of the fortification. The subsequent events depend on which faction was victorious.

If the PCs are defeated or surrender, the Tukari prove victorious.

**Tukari Victory.** The Emuli soldiers flee into the valley, abandoning their camps. Tukari soldiers surround the party and call for “the judge” to sentence the prisoners. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]].

**Emuli Victory.** The Emuli clear out the remaining Tukari occupiers and place any noncombatants under strict guard. The Emuli commander, Jazima (expert, she/her), is an old woman with bushy gray eyebrows peeking out from beneath her headwrap. She thanks the party for their aid during the battle, gifting them a fine Emuli longbow worth 100 marks and five doses of anesthetic made from medicinal winterwort. She then implores the PCs to accompany her to deal with a confounding Azish judge who currently holds the Emuli prisoner Vanzi captive. Proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]].

## Herald of Justice

The beginning of this scene depends on the outcomes of the previous battle:

- **Made a Break for It.** If the PCs chose to skip some or all of the fight, they find the judge—the Herald Nale—in the officers’ temporary rooms, observing the ongoing battle. Tukar will win the battle shortly, but there is time to talk.

- **Led to Victory.** If the PCs led the Emuli to victory inside the keep, they can either follow Jazima to the officers’ temporary rooms, or you can have Nale bring his prisoner out into the yard.

- **Defeated.** If the PCs lost the battle or chose not to participate, a hostile contingent of four Tukari guards accompanies them to the officers’ temporary rooms.

Regardless of what outcomes led the party here, read the following to introduce the Herald of Justice:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the man in question steps forward, the air around him stills as if he’s caught in the eye of a storm. He is darkskinned, sharp-jawed, and his stiff black coat glints with two rows of silver buttons. His silhouette is reminiscent of a stern Azish constable. His cheek bears a faint mark in the shape of a crescent.\
>      Beside him stands an Emuli man bound in manacles. He wears a brightly colored Emuli headband, and his hands are patterned with long-healed burn scars. The prisoner looks to you in desperation, mouthing one word, “Help.”\
>      For the moment, you have the judge’s attention. He speaks coldly, “I am running out of patience. I have an execution to carry out. Tell me your purpose here and what you require of me.”

The judge is Nalan’Elin, also known as Nale (he/him). He is one of the Heralds, but he exists in society as a lawkeeper, working with local authorities across Roshar while pursuing his own ends. None of the Emuli or Tukari know he is a Herald; his papers identify him as an Azish constable with absolute authority to arrest and execute lawbreakers.

## Nale’s Prisoner

Nale came to Ashiqqil Keep following reports of the strange powers of the man in his captivity. **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Vanzi|Vanzi]]** (Dustbringer of the Second Ideal, he/him) is a Emuli engineer and sapper who has bonded an ashspren and sworn the Second Ideal of the Dustbringers.

![[pg101_Vanzi extended_ESTHER SCHRADER.webp]]

**ESTHER SCHRADER**

As an advocate for nonviolence, Vanzi had been planning to carefully use his surge of Division to undermine the occupied fort, and thereby enable the Emuli soldiers to take it without bloodshed. However, while trying to create a breach in the wall, he killed six Tukari soldiers and three civilians. Vanzi was captured and held in the castle for days until Nale arrived with a signed writ from god-priest Tezim to conduct Vanzi’s trial as a war criminal. Although Nale is prepared to carry out the execution, the letter of Emuli law entitles Vanzi to plead his case.

## A Deal With Divinity

Upon their meeting, Nale recognizes the danger of the raysium knife, and requests that it be handed over immediately. Offering it to him with no strings attached is enough to earn his favor. If the PCs don’t offer it up, or if they plead for Vanzi’s life, a tense Negotiation conversation ensues (see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Resolving the Conversation|Resolving the Conversation]]).

![[pg100_Nale_Dual_Blades_SVETLANA KOSTINA.webp]]

**Nale, Herald of Justice** **-** **SVETLANA KOSTINA**

### Conversation Circumstances

The following circumstances affect the ease of the conversation:

- **Battle Heroes.** If the PCs won the battle, the Emuli commander Jazima supports their arguments and lends credibility to their claims. Thanks to the commander’s backing, the PCs gain an advantage on all tests.

- **Defeated.** If the PCs lost the battle, the conversation is observed by a contingent of hostile guards. Each PC must succeed on a DC 13 Discipline test to keep calm or gain a disadvantage on tests against Nale.

- **Bringing Allies.** If Axoq or Kaiana are with the party for the conversation, they each help once during the scene by giving a PC an advantage on a test.

- **Other Heralds.** If the PCs reveal their meeting with Taln, Nale becomes distracted by guilt and loses 1 focus. If the PCs explain Ylt’s threat to Pailiah, Nale loses 2 focus.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> PCs who are willing to face destruction by arguing strongly against Nale may attract the attention of an ashspren. Those who try to save Vanzi for the sake of personal freedoms may attract a lightspren. Finally, PCs who are successful in appealing to Nale’s sense of law and justice may attract the attention of a highspren regardless of any deals—perhaps even a spren unaligned with Nale’s Skybreakers.

> [!example] Roleplaying Nale
> **Characteristics:** Lawful, merciless, unyielding.
>
> **Goal:** Seek justice, and find certainty in law.
>
> **Appearance:** Nale (“NAIL”; he/him) is a dark-skinned Makabaki human with close-shaved hair. He wears a stiff black double-breasted coat. His cheek bears a faint mark in the shape of a crescent.
>
> Nale embodies dispassionate justice. He claims a strict adherence to the laws of the land, trusting his morality to the social contracts crafted by Rosharan societies. Unique among the Heralds, Nale has bonded a spren and still secretly leads the Radiant Order of Skybreakers. The highspren share and encourage Nale’s strict lawfulness. However, just as the PCs found a moment of lucidity with Taln, they can break through Nale’s rigidity to find a more human perspective.

### Resisting Influence

This adventure doesn’t provide a stat block for Nale (see the later [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Asking Questions|Fighting Nale]] sidebar), but for the purpose of this conversation, he has a Cognitive defense of 19, a Spiritual defense of 18, and 6 focus. Nale is a stubborn negotiator. He chooses to resist most arguments by spending focus.

For arguments that rely on emotional appeals, he need only spend 1 focus to resist them. For arguments that appeal to law, authority, and logic, he must spend 2 focus as usual.

### Life in the Balance

In the ensuing conversation with Nale, inquisitive PCs might seek information or advice, and resourceful PCs might ask for a favor from a powerful man. Compassionate PCs might press to exchange the raysium knife for the life of the captive Radiant Vanzi—an outcome Jazima, Po’ahu, or other Radiant spren push for.

The party might ask Nale questions like the following examples:

- **Save Vanzi’s Life.** Nale is hesitant to let Vanzi go free, explaining that the Dustbringer’s careless use of dangerous Surgebinding led to the death of innocents. If Vanzi is allowed to speak, he expresses remorse but explains that he was trying to avoid a bloody battle by bringing down the keep’s wall. PCs specifically looking for legal justification to stay Nale’s hand can make a Deduction test against Nale’s Cognitive defense and, on a success, recall an obscure legal provision that exempts sappers from prosecution for collateral damage. Presenting Nale with such information allows the party to negotiate for Vanzi’s life in exchange for the raysium knife, and doing so costs Nale 3 focus to resist.

- **Aid in Stopping Ylt.** Nale considers aid and information relating to Ylt and the Eyes of Pala to be a worthy trade for the raysium knife. Nale shares that the Eyes of Pala keep to the shadows in Rall Elorim, where their patron Pailiah, Herald of Knowledge, maintains her vaults. Nale has pressing business elsewhere, but he provides paperwork that grants the party safe passage to Sesemalex Dar.

- **Seeking Radiance.** If a PC is audacious enough to ask for membership in Nale’s Skybreakers, or if they show an impressive command of legal codes, Nale asks the PC for evidence of their adherence to a moral code. PCs who offer an example can make a Discipline or Persuasion test against his Spiritual Defense. On a successful test, Nale doesn’t spend focus to resist influence, and he offers to introduce the successful PC to a highspren.

> [!tip] Herald of Honor… or Odium?
> Before the Everstorm, Nale sought to prevent a new Desolation by preventing nascent Radiants from swearing Ideals. He and his Skybreakers hunted down Radiants, finding legal rationale to execute them. With the return of the Everstorm, he has given up this quest. Now, in Nale’s relentless pursuit of justice, he recognizes that the awakened singers have the right to retake Roshar. He has aligned with the singer armies, and his Skybreakers have begun to support Odium’s forces in battles against the human Coalition of Monarchs. However, Nale’s interests aren’t at odds with the party’s goals: He still sees himself as opposed to Odium, he supports the goal of delivering Taln’s Honorblade to Shinovar, and he doesn’t want to see his fellow Heralds slain.

### Asking Questions

During the conversation, Nale is willing to answer several questions as a sign of good faith in the negotiations:

- **Who are you?** “I have had many names. Nalan, Nale, Nakku… But my name is not important. I am an instrument of the law.”

- **Who is your prisoner?** “Vanzi is an Emuli soldier charged, on Tukari soil, with the deaths of three noncombatants. I have a writ from the god-priest authorizing me to oversee his trial and execution.”

- **Why has Vanzi caught your attention?** “He has bonded an ashspren and commands Division, the most dangerous of the surges.”

- **Why won’t you help us hunt down and defeat Ylt?** “This Ylt sounds like a murderer and a thief, and I can see your cause is just. However, I have other battles to fight. The Everstorm has brought a new Desolation, but it has also healed a grave injustice. I am needed to uphold the oldest laws of this world.”

> [!tip] Fighting Nale
> If the PCs choose to fight Nale, they find him an overwhelming foe. As very high-tier characters, Heralds are beyond the scope of current Cosmere RPG materials, and they don’t have official adversary stat blocks. If a Herald chooses to join a fight, a tier 1 character simply can’t stop them. Nale brushes off attacks made against him, immediately healing his wounds with Stormlight. He ignores the first attack, continuing the conversation without hesitation. If attacked with the raysium knife or attacked a second time, Nale moves with superhuman speed, immediately confiscating the knife. This causes the conversation to fail. If the PCs continue to fight, Nale draws his Honorblade and responds to any attack by automatically dealing 23 ( `2d10+12` ) spirit damage to the attacker and causing them to suffer an injury from the “Shardblade Injury Duration” table in chapter 7 of the Stormlight Handbook. Once Nale has the knife, he has no interest in fighting and departs, promising that his Skybreakers will seek the party’s lawful execution once the proper warrants have been obtained.

### Resolving the Conversation

The conversation ends successfully if the party agrees to hand over the raysium knife or if Nale spends more than 6 focus resisting the PCs.

The conversation ends in failure if the party fails multiple attempts to sway Nale, especially if they appeal to his emotions. The conversation also fails if the party refuses to give Nale the knife. If the PCs attack Nale, see the [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Asking Questions|Fighting Nale]] sidebar.

- **Success.** What happens on a success depends on the party’s approach to persuading Nale.

  If the PCs hand over the raysium knife, Nale takes it and experiences a moment of clarity. Emotion briefly enters his voice as he says, “Please do what you can to help Pailiah. If you speak with her, know that her true name is Pralla.”

  If the PCs succeed on a skill test to influence Nale and he has no remaining focus to spend, he allows the Emuli Radiant to go free. Once Vanzi is freed, the characters can take the ***[[Journal/10 - Appendix B Items#Aluminum Shackles|aluminum shackles]]*** (detailed in appendix B) that Nale used to bind the Dustbringer. If the characters also made a compelling argument for why they should keep the raysium knife, Nale allows them to keep it—but he warns they will be harshly punished if they misuse it.

- **Failure.** Convinced of the party’s moral failings, Nale demands to take the raysium knife without any compensation for the party. If denied, Nale takes the raysium knife by force (see the [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Asking Questions|Fighting Nale]] sidebar), then takes flight and disappears into the sky. A subordinate **[[Actors/Adversaries/Radiant/Skybreaker of the Second Ideal|Skybreaker of the Second Ideal]]** working for Nale comes to retrieve Vanzi, whisking him off for execution.

## Travel Through Emul

Given the information the party received from Nale (in [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|Herald of Justice]]) and Kaiana (at the conclusion of [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]]), it should be clear to the party that to pursue Ylt and the Honorblade, they must seek passage onward to the city of Rall Elorim in Iri. To do so, they’ll need to hire a ship in the port city of Sesemalex Dar.

The trip from Ashiqqil Keep to Sesemalex Dar proves to be an uneventful sixteen-day trek through the valleys and across the plains of Emul. Read the following when the party approaches the city:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The ancient city of Sesemalex Dar is unmistakable. Its intricate streets are built into great gashes in the plains, as if some enormous beast has torn through the Emuli lowlands with its claws. The stormward edges of each fissure are adorned with crowns of stone, diverting the waters away from the city within. To the west, the fissures meet the sea by a large sheltered port that thrives with the colorful trappings of Emuli trade. Here, potters shape crem clay into elegant vessels while hawkers offer goods ranging from fragrant tallew dishes to casual headwraps.

Fortunately, the city is still thriving. The Eighty’s War has been fought between Tukar and Emul for years, and the Emuli army has made its capital’s defense a strategic priority. When the singers of Sesemalex Dar awakened and left in search of freedom, the humans of Sesemalex Dar managed to avoid armed conflict. The PCs can therefore rest and explore at their leisure, though singer characters will face some degree of scrutiny and suspicion.

## Sesemalex Dar

Considered by some scholars to be one of the ancient and treasured Dawncities, the Emuli capital of Sesemalex Dar is well recognized as one of Roshar’s wonders. The city’s religious significance and its strategic importance as a sheltered port are the underlying reasons for Tukar’s attempt to seize it.

During their stop in Sesemalex Dar, the PCs will certainly want to visit the city’s port to seek a ship to Rall Elorim (see the upcoming [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Finding Passage to Iri|Finding Passage to Iri]] section). In the couple of days it takes to secure passage and board their ship, the party might also choose to visit the following locations.

### The Dawnchain

*Market*

The Dawnchain is a market district spread through a single narrow street at the bottom of one of the city’s fissures. The market is managed by a broad-shouldered Emuli named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Odax|Odax]]** (expert, he/him) who insists on patrolling the length of the Dawnchain with a platter of free samples.

Since all military resources are directed toward the war effort, weapons are sold at a 50 percent markup.

### Kadasix Archives

*Library*

The Kadasix Archives are among the most complete historical records of the Makabaki peoples outside of the great libraries of Azimir. The imposing crem brick structure is built into a large fissure with a single staircase connecting the ten levels. **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/The Archivist Nattaqim|The Archivist Nattaqim]]** (expert, she/her) is a great believer in sharing the wealth of her stores with travelers, as is the Emuli way, but she noticeably winces and grimaces at every touch of her precious collection. If a PC demonstrates their knowledge and dedication to literature with a successful DC 15 Lore test, Nattaqim becomes excited to find another erudite scholar, allows them to check out books, and offers them a spanreed paired with her own.

### Sesemalex Guest Register

*Hospitality*

PCs looking for a place to rest and recuperate in the city are soon pointed toward the Sesemalex Guest Register. Embodying Emuli openness and hospitality toward foreigners, the register is not an inn or tavern itself; rather, it maintains noticeboards across the city listing households willing and able to host a band of travelers. Copies of the register are scattered across the ports and plazas of Sesemalex Dar, but most prominently in the Dawnchain markets.

Households offering food and a bed for a few days include:

- **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Chanil|Chanil]]** (commoner, he/him) and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Loxim|Loxim]]** (commoner, she/her), newlyweds who are building a nursery and are grateful for any aid.

- **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Yasiqqi|Yasiqqi]]** (expert, they/them), an elderly artist with strong opinions about the contemporary scene.

- **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Ashil|Ashil]]** (guard, he/him), a young firebrand excited to join the army to resist the Tukari.

## Finding Passage to Iri

The war with Tukar limits the free passage of Emuli ships toward the west. Since the Emuli are unwilling to risk provoking other nations into joining the conflict, most long-distance business is handled by non-Emuli vessels. However, in this busy and prosperous port of Sesemalex Dar, it takes little effort for the party to find a ship heading toward Rall Elorim.

When the PCs seek information about ships leaving Sesemalex Dar, they discover that the next ship leaving for Rall Elorim is the Measured Response, a vessel in the Aqasix Merchant Fleet that operates out of Azimir and runs a regular shipping route to Rall Elorim.

The Measured Response is captained by **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Vaqqui|Vaqqui]]** (expert, they/them), a curly-haired Azish with a love for literature. Their husband and pilot is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Jazirin|Jazirin]]** (expert, he/him), a tall Alethi with a wide smile. Wary of the ongoing war with Tukar and new conflicts with free singers across the Azish Empire, Vaqqui and Jazirin are eager to leave port as soon as possible.

### Boarding the Measured Response

Vaqqui and crew arrive in Sesemalex Dar the day after the party’s arrival. They stay in port for one day to load and unload before moving onward to Rall Elorim. The PCs therefore have two days of downtime to spend in the city, plus sixty-five days of downtime during their sea voyage (see [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#Downtime in Port and at Sea|Downtime in Port and at Sea]]).

Once the ship leaves port, the party has a chance to reminisce on their adventures so far; proceed to [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War#The Captain’s Table|The Captain’s Table]].

## Downtime in Port and at Sea

Once the PCs have spent two days securing passage and stocking up on supplies, they can begin their journey out of Sesemalex Dar, west through open ocean and north toward Iri. The voyage takes another sixty-five days, as the ship must hug the coastline and seek sheltered coves every nine days when the Everstorm passes. Fortunately, the route is far from the battles now being fought between Odium’s forces and the Coalition of Monarchs. Aboard the *Measured Response*, the PCs will have the time and safety to engage in downtime activities.

During downtime, each PC can address their individual goals, projects, and interests, as described in the “Downtime” section in chapter 9 of the *Stormlight Handbook* for more information. The following sections present some ideas for downtime activities.

![[pg104_Azish Ship_EREL MAATITA.webp]]

**EREL MAATITA**

### Crafting

Characters can find all the materials they need for crafting mundane goods in the Dawnchain markets. If a character wishes to craft fabrials, they can instead find artifabrian workshops in the Kadasix Archives. After acquiring their materials in Sesemalex Dar, they can continue their crafts at sea. The Measured Response is well-stocked with supplies and facilities for crafting, provided by the ship’s good-natured quartermaster **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 5/Batazi|Batazi]]** (commoner, she/her).

### Working as a Ship’s Hand

A character can earn a total of 130 marks by working aboard the ship during the journey, performing duties like cleaning the deck, repairing sails, and assisting the navigator. A character who spends the entire voyage working this way also gains an expertise in Sailing (without paying for supplies or materials).

### Recuperating

A character might spend time at sea recuperating from injuries. Each day of downtime spent dedicated to rest and recuperation counts as two days toward healing an injury.

### Researching and Training

If a character spends a total of 100 marks on supplies and materials, they can train for 20 days to gain a new expertise.

### Self-Reflection

If a character spends ten days in self-reflection, plus a total of 50 marks, they can rebuild elements of their character such as their choice of talents or skill ranks. Self-reflection might take the form of meditation in the ship’s small shrine to Jezrien, long talks with Kaiana, or sparring with another character on the ship’s deck.

## The Captain’s Table

During the party’s first evening aboard the Measured Response, Vaqqui and Jazirin gladly invite them to dinner. During that meal, encourage an in-character discussion of events thus far.

The following questions can be used to get the characters talking. Go around the table once, asking each player to pick one of the questions for their character to either answer or ask of another character:

- If Taszo were here, what do you think he’d say about everything that’s happened so far?

- How did it feel to meet the Heralds Nale and Taln, who seem to have stepped out of legend?

- Was it right to leave the raysium knife with Nale?

- What do you make of the visions in the grove?

- What is Ylt planning?

- Have you met the expectations of your Radiant oaths?

- Is the apparent return of the Knights Radiant a good thing?

- What does the new reality of the Everstorm mean for the group? What does it mean for Roshar?

## Journeying Onward

During their travel to Rall Elorim, the characters advance to 6th level. Their journey is lengthy but peaceful. In [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|chapter 6]], the party nears Ylt’s base— and with it, destiny.
