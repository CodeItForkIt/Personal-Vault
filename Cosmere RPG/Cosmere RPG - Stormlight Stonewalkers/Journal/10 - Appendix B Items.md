# Appendix B: Items

## Unique Items

This appendix details the unique items that appear in the Stonewalkers adventure. Other items mentioned in this adventure are detailed in chapter 7 of *the* *Stormlight Handbook*.

### Aluminum Shackles

*Equipment, 3 lb.*

These shackles are forged from Soulcast aluminum, a silvery metal known to singers as ralkalest. Aluminum is magically inert and can block Investiture. As a result, the shackles can’t be cut by a Shardblade, and they can’t be affected by Surgebinding or other Invested abilities.

The shackles come with a key, and they function as a lighter, slightly less sturdy pair of manacles: they bind the wrists or ankles of a Medium or smaller character. These manacles can be escaped with a successful DC 25 Agility test, broken by succeeding on a DC 20 Athletics test or by dealing 10 damage to them, or lockpicked by succeeding on a DC 20 Thievery test.

### Raysium Knife

*Special Weapon, 1 lb.*

**Skill:** Light Weaponry

**Damage:** 1d4 keen

**Range:** Melee

**Traits:** Discreet, Unique

**Expert Traits:** Offhand, Thrown [20/60]

This knife is forged of golden-white metal—raysium, the God Metal of Odium. Set in the knife’s pommel is a perfect emerald.

**Unique Traits.** The raysium knife has the following unique traits:

- **Drain Investiture.** On a hit, the attack’s target loses 1 Investiture.

- **Extract Soul.** This weapon can permanently kill a Cognitive Shadow in a physical body (such as a Herald or Fused). If such a character is reduced to 0 health by this weapon and suffers an injury, they immediately die and the raysium conducts their soul into the emerald. Their soul is captured in the gemstone, and for as long as their soul remains captured, they can’t be reborn in another body or escape to the Cognitive Realm. This process is traumatic enough to sever a Herald from the Oathpact. Any Cognitive Shadow captured with this knife will eventually fade, permanently destroying them, though it’s up to the GM how fast that captured soul fades. A captured soul is also destroyed if exposed to the corresponding anti-Light.

### Taln’s Honorblade

*Special Weapon, 4 lb.*

**Skill:** Heavy Weaponry or Light Weaponry

**Damage:** 2d10 spirit

**Traits:** Dangerous, Deadly, Unique

**Expert Traits:** Unique (loses Dangerous trait)

Taln’s Honorblade is one of ten unique weapons created by Honor to empower the Heralds in their battle against Odium’s forces. Whoever bonds this legendary blade gains access to the surges of a Stoneward. Like ordinary bonded Shardblades, it can be summoned or dismissed by its bearer, so it can’t be taken unless its bearer is slain.

**Unique Traits.** While you’re bonded to Taln’s Honorblade, it has the same unique traits as a Shardblade (see chapter 7 of the *Stormlight Handbook*), plus the following unique traits:

- **Herald of Stonewards.** Your ranks in the Cohesion and Tension surge skills increase to 3. If you already have 3 ranks or more in a skill, that skill instead increases by 1 rank, to a maximum of 5. However, if you roll a Complication while making a Cohesion or Tension test, you must double the Investiture cost of that test.

- **Wield Investiture.** If you don’t already have an Investiture pool, you gain one, and your maximum Investiture equals 2 + either your Awareness or Presence (whichever is higher). You can use the Breathe Stormlight, Enhance, and Regenerate actions.

- **Surge Talents.** You gain the Stone Spear talent from the Cohesion talent tree, and you gain the Tension Parry talent from the Tension talent tree. When you gain a level and thus gain a new talent, you can choose it from the Cohesion and Tension paths.

If you lose your bond to Taln’s Honorblade, you lose all skills and talents granted by the Honorblade. If you no longer meet the prerequisites for any Cohesion or Tension talents you chose when you gained a level, you can replace them using the Self-Reflection downtime activity (see chapter 9 of the *Stormlight Handbook*).@link
