# 07: What Was and What Could Be

## Chapter Summary

The *Stonewalkers* adventure comes to an epic conclusion in a decaying vault beneath the occupied city of Rall Elorim. Armed with the Honorblade, Ylt returns to his mentor Pailiah and stages a bloody coup, gathering his faithful acolytes to serve his new master, Odium. Can the party stop him? And can they restore hope and purpose to the ancient Order of Truthwatchers?

## Running This Chapter

This chapter opens in the caverns below Rall Elorim. If the PCs navigate poorly, they are spotted by a sentry. This triggers a Pursuit endeavor as the sentry tries to reach Pailiah’s Vault and alert her allies. If she succeeds, Ylt’s followers are on the lookout when the party arrives at the vault.

The outer areas of the vault offer three options for approaching the guarded entrance to Pailiah’s inner sanctum: the main hallway, an underwater passage, and a collapsed tunnel. Review [[Scenes Index|map 7.1]] (later in this chapter) before the characters enter the vault.

As the party passes through the inner vault, they find evidence of Ylt’s coup and confront the deluded villain himself. In the ensuing battle, the characters face off not only against Ylt, but against the enraged souls of shadowform singers, whom Ylt releases.

If the party wins, they meet the Herald Pailiah. The PCs can discuss the fate of the Honorblade, attempt to convince her to reengage with the world, and shape the future of the Order of Truthwatchers.

The adventure ends with the PCs becoming the stewards of Taln’s Honorblade, leaving them the chance to fulfill their promise to Taszo.

## Pailiah’s Vault

During the ancient wars known as Desolations, Heralds and Radiants fought side by side against the forces of Odium. Pailiah’s Vault dates back to that era, and it persisted for generations as a library and temple for the Order of Truthwatchers. Even after the Recreance— the day when Knights Radiant broke their oaths and disbanded—a few mundane acolytes remained as loyal caretakers for this hidden library beneath Rall Elorim.

Thousands of years ago, the Herald Pailiah retreated from the world. She spent many of those years lost in her own memories. At other times, she traveled in disguise as an ardent or librarian. Most recently, she has spent much of her time in the vault beneath Rall Elorim. A cult of acolytes, calling themselves the Eyes of Pala and imitating the mission and traditions of the lost Truthwatchers, gathered to serve her. But only two of them—a charismatic Iriali man named Ylt and a compassionate Reshi woman named Kaiana—bonded spren and spoke the Ideals.

## Ylt’s Plan

Disillusioned with Pailiah, Ylt plans to unseat her as Herald of the Truthwatchers. He misinterprets visions from his Enlightened mistspren Tyche as proof of his own significance and is convinced he is more fit to lead than the distracted Herald Pailiah.

Ylt is emboldened by the Honorblade and by his research into releasing the singers trapped in the shadowform gemhearts. Many of the Eyes of Pala acolytes have abandoned their loyalty to Pailiah and sided with Ylt. When the party arrives, he has already executed his bloody coup, killing any Eyes of Pala acolytes who remained loyal to Pailiah.

While Ylt has given up on slaying Pailiah herself, he knows she’s too lost in her memories to stop him.

### Releasing the Shadowform Singers

Millennia ago, a group of singers agreed to a risky plan, hoping to gain an edge in the war against the humans. Their leader, the legendary spren Ba-Ado-Mishram, sought to grant them shadowform, a form to transform them into incorporeal assassins. However, the shadowspren bond went horribly wrong and the singers’ bodies became permanent shadows, with only their gemhearts remaining solid. These trapped spirits now haunt Rall Elorim, where their gemhearts were laid to rest.

Ylt discovered these shadowform gemhearts in Pailiah’s inner vault. He has since discerned their true nature and devised a plan to release the shadowform singers by allowing them to consume the bodies of his most faithful acolytes. Ylt believes these new shadowform zealots will obey him. The shadowform zealots are no longer truly alive, but in the wake of the Everstorm, their Cognitive Shadows remember their original intent: to assassinate humans. This powerful force could bring catastrophic destruction to Rall Elorim.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 7th level. If you plan to continue your campaign after this adventure, the characters advance to level 8 after their conversation with Pailiah.

The life-and-death stakes of the entire chapter offer opportunities to strengthen spren bonds and swear Ideals. The climactic final battle with Ylt and the shadowform zealots is an excellent scene to have characters swear Ideals.

Characters with goals related to finishing Taszo’s mission or thwarting Ylt have ample opportunities to progress them throughout the chapter as they work to reclaim the Honorblade and stop Ylt. Goals related to learning more about the Lost Radiants or Heralds can be progressed when exploring Pailiah’s Vault or talking to the Herald herself.

## Searching the Depths

The characters navigate their way through the cavernous underground of Rall Elorim to reach Pailiah’s enclave. As they enter the depths, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Between you and Pailiah’s Vault lies a maze of underground passageways that weave past crypts, vaults, mausoleums, and other ancient still places. The deeper you go, the more solitary they become. However, scuff marks from boots and the remnants of a fire reveal that others have been here recently.

![[pg118_Depths Below Rall Elorim_KEVIN O’NEILL.webp]]

**KEVIN O’NEILL**

Ylt’s followers patrol these tunnels. The PC who takes the lead must succeed on a DC 20 Stealth or Survival test to lead the party past unnoticed. If another PC assists with navigating the tunnels, the navigator gains an advantage on this test. After learning the result of this test, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Your journey leads you to an echoing cavern with an underground stream running through it. On the far side of the water, a short figure in green and gold robes sits atop an aged culvert. Though she wears a green blindfold, her movements make it clear she’s alert and scanning her surroundings.

The figure is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 7/Ryel|Ryel]]** (Eyes of Pala watcher, she/her), a sentry for Ylt. She has white-gold hair and holds the highest rank in the Eyes of Pala: a watcher. Like all watchers, Ryel symbolically wears a green blindfold, signifying that her keen insight transcends the need for sight. She avoids combat at all costs.

If the party’s navigator failed the Stealth or Survival test, Ryel spots the party first and darts into the tunnels. If the party chases her, proceed to [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Catch the Sentry|Catch the Sentry]].

If the party’s navigator succeeded on the Stealth or Survival, the PCs can easily get the drop on her or sneak past unnoticed. If they approach, she realizes she’s outnumbered. Proceed to the success outcome of Catch the Sentry.

## Catch the Sentry

To determine whether the party catches Ryel, run a Pursuit endeavor (see “Pursuit Endeavors” in chapter 12 of *the* *Stormlight Handbook*). Ryel’s goal is to reach the entry hall (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|area Y4]] of the "Outer Vault" section) and report to Ylt’s acolytes there. She knows these tunnels well, and she’s prioritizing speed over stealth.

### Special Rule: Closing the Distance

In this Pursuit endeavor, instead of tracking successes and failures, you track the distance between pursuer and target. For this Pursuit, the starting distance is 3 and the escape distance is 6. Each time Ryel succeeds on a test, the current distance increases by one. Each time a PC succeeds on a test, the current distance decreases by one. If the current distance reaches 0, the party catches her. If the current distance reaches 6 (the escape distance), Ryel gets away (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]).

### Chasing Ryel

As the party pursues Ryel, she passes through the following locations:

- **Winding Tunnels.** Ryel first tries to lose the party in a series of winding tunnels. The tunnels weave back and forth and intersect at unexpected angles. A PC might try to intuit the path Ryel has taken (Insight vs. her +2 Deception). If Ryel can’t lose the characters quickly (Athletics vs. her +4 Athletics), she takes a detour into a series of abandoned underground structures. If Kaiana is with the party, the PC gains an advantage on tests to determine Ryel’s tactics.

- **Ruins and Refugees.** Ryel attempts to hide among the subterranean ruins (Perception vs. her +6 Stealth) before making a break for it. As the PCs pursue her, they encounter a small group of refugees—three humans and two singers—gathered around a small fire in the warren of ancient buildings. They’re terrified of the Fused and the changes in Rall Elorim. A PC can use strong words to convince them to share the sentry’s destination (DC 18 Intimidation or Leadership). If Axies is with the party, the party gains an advantage on tests to ingratiate themselves with the refugees.

- **The Treacherous Crevasse.** If Ryel’s gambit of hiding among the ruins fails, she slides down into a crevasse, taking a faster but more dangerous path to Pailiah’s Vault. The crevasse walls are unstable, and handholds and footholds crumble away under too much pressure (Agility or Athletics vs. her +4 Athletics). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Sentry Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party closes the current distance to 0 or otherwise apprehends Ryel. The endeavor fails if the current distance reaches 6.

**Success.** The characters catch Ryel. In exchange for her life, she offers the following information about Pailiah’s Vault:

- The inner vault doors are guarded by six Eyes of Pala acolytes. They’re trained fighters and loyal to Ylt.

- Pailiah stays in the inner vault, among her treasures, and she hasn’t been seen in weeks.

- Ylt is preparing for the Eyes of Pala to enter the world stage, starting first with Rall Elorim.

- Ryel believes that Ylt will soon become the new Herald of the Truthwatchers.

**Failure.** Ryel outpaces the characters and reaches Pailiah’s Vault in time to warn Ylt’s other followers. Ryel and two of the six Eyes of Pala acolytes are posted by the large bronze doors in the entry hall and everyone is on alert.

### Approaching the Vault

Regardless of whether the Pursuit succeeded or failed, the PCs can now approach Pailiah’s Vault. Proceed to [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Outer Vault]].

## Outer Vault

The locations in Pailiah’s Vault appear on [[Scenes Index|map 7.1]]. The party enters the outer portion of Pailiah’s Vault through the meditation room ([[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|area Y1]]).

Depending on how the PCs explore, they can potentially find three options for approaching the entry hall ([[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]] where Ylt’s rebels have gathered. These approaches include:

**Main Hallway.** A main hallway connects the meditation room [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y1)]] to the entry hall. This is the most obvious route, but also the most visible to Ylt’s followers in the entry hall.

**Underwater Passage.** The submerged portion of the flooded crypt [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y2)]] connects via an underwater passage to the pond in the entry hall. The party can swim through this entrance.

**Collapsed Tunnel.** A collapsed tunnel connects from the collapsed mausoleum [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y3)]] to the entry hall. The party can excavate this entrance.

## Y1: Meditation Room

This central hub serves as a rest area for Pailiah’s followers who hope to study under the Herald.

The meditation room connects via passageways to the flooded crypt [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y2)]], the collapsed mausoleum [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y3)]], and the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The center of this room is dominated by a wide pillar, which is carved with writing from the ground to the ceiling 50 feet above. It looks like a monument, an artifact lit reverently by the steady glow of emerald sphere lanterns bolted into the walls. Scattered about the room lie bedrolls and small personal effects, suggesting this is a place of meditation, study, or rest.

The pillar has thousands of names carved on it. A PC who succeeds on a DC 15 Lore test recognizes that the names are notable scholars and historic figures throughout the centuries, particularly those regarded as fair or compassionate leaders. If this test succeeds by 3 or more, the PC also discovers that the most recent addition to the monument is over five hundred years old, suggesting the decline of the Eyes of Pala as they drew away from observing the world. If Kaiana is with the party, the PCs gain an advantage on this test.

## Y2: Flooded Crypt

This partially flooded room is filled with monuments to the honored dead.

The crypt connects back to the meditation room [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y1)]] via a short hallway and hides a secret entrance to the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A series of urns on plinths decorate this room, reminiscent of a funeral procession. One section of the floor in the room has collapsed, leaving a flooded area of still water against the far eastern wall.

The intricately carved burial urns are filled with ashes and stand on grand stone plinths, which convey the achievements of those interred here.

Most of the dead are scholars, surgeons, and artists, though there are two generals and one Azish vizier.

### Underwater Passage

The east end of the room is flooded 4 feet deep with water. Any PC who investigates the water notices a glowing light that’s shining through a submerged natural stone passage. The PCs can swim through this 30-foot-long passage to reach the underground pond in the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]].

If nobody investigates the water but Axies is with the party, he jumps in with his usual curious nature and happily reports finding an underwater passage.

## Y3: Collapsed Mausoleum

This side room has suffered a significant collapse, but details suggest someone of great renown was interred here.

The mausoleum connects back to the meditation room [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y1)]] through a plain metal door. It also connects to the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]] via a collapsed tunnel.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Dust floats in the air as you disturb this room, a chamber which hasn’t been used in years. The ceiling has collapsed, dropping rubble and stonework all throughout and blocking the east corner of the room, but details of a grand interment are still evident.

Investigating the area reveals the shattered pottery shards of a large burial urn. A PC who tries to fit the pieces together and succeeds on a DC 18 Deduction test or a DC 16 Lore test discovers the urn was the final resting place of an ancient Knight Radiant.

### Collapsed Tunnel

A PC who investigates the collapsed area and succeeds on a DC 16 Crafting or Perception test discovers there was another entrance to this room, a tunnel which leads to the east. If Po’ahu is with the party, she can confirm that this tunnel connects to the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]].

The tunnel is blocked by 15 feet of stone. The party can clear the passage by succeeding on three DC 15 Athletics tests, raising the stakes. Each test takes 10 minutes of work. Using a Shardblade or relevant Surgebinding cuts this time in half and grants an advantage on these tests.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Excavation Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

## Y4: Entry Hall

Several of Ylt’s agents gather in this cavern, which holds a shrine surrounded by a pool.

This entry hall connects back to the meditation room [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y1)]] via the main hallway. It also has hidden connections to the collapsed mausoleum [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y3)]] and the flooded crypts [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y2)]]. The bronze doors to the east connect to the inner vault, leading first into the library [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y5)]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This massive cavern holds a pool of water crossed by three stone bridges. Infused spheres float in small bowls on the surface, casting an emerald glow across the water.\
>      The three bridges lead to a small island with decorated pillars which connect in graceful arches to the cavern’s ceiling, nearly 100 feet above. Two tarnished bronze doors lead further into the vault.\
>      Acolytes in green and gold robes drag similarly dressed bodies from the water and place them in a heap. The acolytes are armed with swords, knives, and bows.

If the sentry in [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Searching the Depths]] got away, also read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two more acolytes in green and gold stand watch at the bronze doors. Near them is a familiar short woman— the sentry who evaded you earlier. All three wear green blindfolds.

![[pg122_Pailiah's Vault Final_DAVID FRASHESKI.webp]]

**DAVID FRASHESKI**

There are four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Agent|Eyes of Pala agents]]** and two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Watcher|Eyes of Pala watchers]]** here, all acolytes loyal to Ylt. They are currently preparing to cremate the bodies of Eyes of Pala acolytes who opposed Ylt’s coup.

If the sentry Ryel was dealt with earlier, all six acolytes are wholly distracted by their task. If **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 7/Ryel|Ryel]]** escaped, she is here (Eyes of Pala watcher), and she stands guard with the other two Eyes of Pala watchers at the bronze doors while the agents sort corpses. They attack outsiders before asking questions.

The pool of water has an irregular bottom and is 20 feet deep at its deepest point. The bronze doors on the island are carved with ancient glyphs venerating knowledge, truth, persistence, and dedication.

If Kaiana is with the party, her eyes fill with tears upon seeing this betrayal, and she gains the Determined condition for this scene. She knows some of these acolytes, and she cautions the party that attacking directly could be highly dangerous, urging them to find a stealthier route.

> [!tip] Kaiana Sides with Ylt
> In [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]], if Kaiana left the Nightwatcher’s grove with Ylt, then replace one of the **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Agent|Eyes of Pala agents]]** with **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]]**. In this case, she has suppressed her misgivings about his violence and is resigned to supporting Ylt and his plans.

### Approaching the Inner Vault

If Axies or Kaiana is with the party, they offer to distract the acolytes, giving the PCs an advantage on all Stealth tests.

The party can attempt to bypass the acolytes by stealth, guile, or force:

- **Sneaking Through.** Each PC must make a Stealth test against the Spiritual defense (DC 16) of the Eyes of Pala watchers. If over half the PCs fail this test, one of the watchers notices them. This test gains a disadvantage if the sentry warned the watchers or if the PCs entered the room through the main hallway. This test gains an advantage if the party entered through the underwater passage or collapsed tunnel.

- **Fast-Talking.** If the sentry got away and recognizes the party, talking is nearly impossible. If she didn’t, the party can convince the acolytes to listen by succeeding on a DC 20 Deception, Intimidation, or Persuasion test, raising the stakes. You can spend a Complication to add a disadvantage to all subsequent tests to speak with the acolytes. The lead Eyes of Pailiah watcher spends 2 focus to resist being influenced, then once he’s been reduced to 1 focus, he gives in and lets the party pass. However, if the PCs fail two tests, the acolytes attack.

- **Fighting Ylt’s Acolytes.** This is a difficult fight for the party. If the sentry isn’t there and the PCs strike without warning, all acolytes are Surprised for the first round of combat. The acolytes fight to the death, believing their sacrifice serves Ylt and his grand plan.

## Inner Vault

Beyond the bronze doors lies the heart of the ancient Truthwatcher stronghold, now home to the Eyes of Pala. This sacred home of learning and knowledge is in obvious decline. Yet the vault holds valuable information and history—as well as a Truthwatcher embracing ambition beyond reason. Locations [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Y5]] and [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Y6]] appear on [[Scenes Index|map 7.1]].

## Y5: Library

Bronze doors divide the entry hall [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y4)]] from this library, a literal temple to knowledge. The door at the other end of the room is broken and connects to the antechamber [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y6)]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The chamber ahead smells of musty ancient texts and is crowded with bookshelves, desks, and large tables, each stacked with a large book pile. A metal door set into the east wall is partially open. Next to it, one of the stones in the wall has been removed, revealing a mess of broken fabrial machinery.\
>      Three bodies are in this room, two collapsed near the bronze door, and one seated at a desk. All are unmoving.

The bodies are those of an Iriali man, an Alethi woman, and a Thaylen man. All wear green robes and bear the burned-out eyes of Shardblade victims. The Alethi woman’s body is slumped at the desk.

The broken machinery is a destroyed fabrial. If a PC has the Fabrial Crafting expertise, or if they succeed on a DC 10 Crafting test, they identify the fabrial as a door lock.

### The Books

A PC who examines the books notices that the texts are richly made, but most are so ancient that their pages crumble as soon as they’re opened.

The two books closest to the Alethi woman’s body, both written in Iriali script, are newer and still legible. The woman spent her last moments researching, under duress, how to release the shadowform singers. If a PC examines the books, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two books lie open on the desk in front of the seated woman’s body, both written in Iriali. Closer to her lies a page of notes and translated passages, written in Alethi women’s script. The notes are difficult to make out, but they include a few legible phrases:\
> \
>      *“Since the new storm arrived, the shadowy crystals have been glowing with a strange violet light.”*\
>      *“Ba-Ado-Mishram sought to create a new form, a form of shadows, but her efforts were in vain.”*\
>      *“The singers’ physical forms were consumed by their own power, much as Yelig-Nar was said to have consumed his hosts.”*\
>      *“Could a new host provide a housing for the entity’s Cognitive Shadow?”*\
> \
> The notes include a hand-drawn sketch of a rough gemstone, faceted and sharpened at one end.

If Axies is with the party and reads these notes, he describes them as research on Investiture, Regal forms, and the Unmade. He theorizes that the Eyes of Pala must be trying to free the shadowform singers trapped in the gemstones. If a PC with the Listener expertise succeeds on a DC 12 Lore test, they remember an old singer song retelling the historical events mentioned in the [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Releasing the Shadowform Singers|Releasing the Shadowform Singers]] section at the beginning of this chapter.

The two books, written in Iriali, are treatises on the ancient enemies of the Knights Radiant: the Fused, Regals, and Unmade. A PC can study these books during downtime to gain the Odium’s Forces expertise (see “Downtime Activities” in chapter 9 of the *Stormlight Handbook*).

## Y6: Antechamber

The broken door from the library [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y5)]] leads to this small curved corridor. At the end of the corridor stands the Door of the Watchers, through which lies Pailiah’s Vault [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y7)]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Beyond the broken door, a short, curving hallway is lit by tall sphere lampposts. Faded murals on the walls depict a tower in the mountains, so tall it rivals the surrounding peaks. The murals must have been vibrant long ago, but the colors are muted, and some areas have completely fallen away.\
>      The hall ends in a formidable gold, silver, and bronze door set with emeralds and carved with ancient glyphs. Slumped beside the door is an unmoving body.

![[pg124_Puzzle Door_LOGAN FELICIANO.webp]]

**LOGAN FELICIANO**

The lampposts are sturdy and can be used to bar the broken door leading from the library.

If a PC has the Knights Radiant expertise, or if they succeed on a DC 16 Lore test, they determine that the tower depicted in the mural is the lost city of Urithiru, a legendary edifice dating back to the Silver Kingdoms era.

The body beside the door is an older Iriali man with a golden beard, green robes, and the burned-out eyes of someone killed by a Shardblade—another victim of Ylt’s recent coup.

### Door of the Watchers

The door is locked by a fabrial designed to grant entry only to Truthwatchers. When the PCs approach the inner door, the fabrial whirs to life. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Delicate script is carved into the stone above the doorway:\
>      *“For those who serve truth.”*\
> Inscribed into the door itself, simpler script bears three questions:\
>      *“What do we seek?”*\
>      *“Who most of all must be honest with others?”*\
>      *“What do we do with our knowledge, no matter the cost?”*\
> The door’s border is lined with glyphs, each with a small emerald next to them.

Any character who can read glyphs recognizes that the glyphs lining the door represent the following concepts: Watch, Secret, Power, Mentor, Protect, Knowledge, Merchant, Cut, Leader, Keep, Spren, Speak, Truth, Hide, Scholar, Remember, Warrior, Fate, Passion, and Honor.

If a character interacts with the door for too long without infusing one of the emeralds next to a glyph, the door hums menacingly. A character can spend 1 Investiture to infuse an emerald, causing it to glow. If a PC has the Fabrial Crafting expertise or succeeds on a DC 10 Deduction test, they realize the emeralds can be infused, and that interacting with the door incorrectly will trigger an energy release.

To open the door, the PCs must answer the three questions by infusing the emeralds corresponding to Truth, Leader, and Speak (in that order). Once done, the emeralds all glow and the door opens, its metal sliding apart like a complex mechanical puzzle.

If three emeralds are infused but any of the answers are incorrect or out of order, the door thrums with power but doesn’t open, and the emeralds’ light fades.

### Knowing the Answers

A PC can answer the first question by succeeding on a DC 12 Lore test about the Truthwatchers. This test’s difficulty increases to 14 and 16 for the subsequent questions. If Kaiana is with the party, she knows the answer to the first question.

### Other Tactics

A PC who examined the broken fabrial in the library [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y5)]] gains an advantage on any tests regarding this fabrial lock. The door can be opened with relative ease, but doing so triggers its defenses. The party might attempt the following:

- **Forcing the Door.** Forcing the door open requires either succeeding on a DC 20 Athletics test or cutting through it using a Shardblade. Either approach opens the door but triggers its defenses.

- **Disabling the Lock.** The lock can be disabled with a successful DC 15 Crafting or Thievery test. This opens the door but triggers its defenses.

- **Tricking the Fabrial.** With a successful DC 15 Crafting test, a PC can modify the fabrial, allowing them to infuse the emeralds on the door directly from another infused gemstone rather than spending Investiture to do so.

### The Door’s Defenses

If the door’s defenses are triggered, it flashes with a disorienting, intense light. Each character in the antechamber must succeed on a DC 18 Discipline test or become Stunned. This condition lasts until the next scene at the end of the first round of combat.

If the party fails three times to open the door through any method, including incorrectly infusing the emeralds, the door opens on the third failed attempt but triggers its defenses.

## The Savior of All

The party now must confront Ylt in Pailiah’s Vault, where his most loyal followers prepare to release the shadowform singers. His visions have convinced him the shadowforms will be powerful allies who can eliminate the humans in Rall Elorim then go forth to dispatch Odium’s enemies. Locations [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Y7]] and [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|Y8]] appear on [[Scenes Index|map 7.1]]

## Y7: Pailiah’s Vault

The Door of the Watchers in the antechamber [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y6)]] leads directly to Pailiah’s Vault (depicted in detail on [[Scenes Index|map 7.2]]). The only other exit is an unassuming and locked door to the left of the stage, which leads to Pailiah’s chamber [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y8)]]; during the battle, Pailiah remains in this chamber, deep in study and inaccessible.

As the party enters the vault, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The vault is a tiered lecture hall. Above hangs a scientific specimen: the reconstructed carapace of a greatshell.\
>      Below, every surface is covered by an overwhelming jumble, not of ancient fabrials or Shards, but of various mundane objects: worn boots, children’s dolls, tarnished utensils, and stacks on stacks of letters and journals.\
>      Ylt stands on a stage at the far north wall, his acolytes flanking him. He points the Honorblade at you. “Well, then. It was fated to come to this.”\
>      He pauses, his voice echoing in the mournful space, then continues.\
>      “You must admit this vault is undeniable proof. Pala has lost her way. Her sacred treasures are useless trinkets.” Pools of red angerspren boil up from the ground around him. “Truthwatchers of the past were scholars. Nothing was buried that we did not unearth. We need that drive again—you all have seen it. The Everstorm. The Fused. At first, I, too, refused to see this new reality, clinging to the past. But we must accept that the Final Desolation has come. Odium will triumph—and I will serve him as a new Herald of War and Truth.”

### Tactics

Ylt is a cunning fighter who relies on visions to give him an edge. He burns Investiture greedily and attacks relentlessly, targeting other Surgebinders first, then whomever his ego decrees. He fights to the death.

The shadowform zealots use their Drag Through Shadows (1) to bypass frontline fighters and attack characters in the back lines, as well as to isolate melee characters who try to gang up on Ylt. They target human characters over singer characters.

### Ylt’s Third Ideal

If Ylt is reduced to 30 health, in desperation, he shouts the Third Ideal of the Truthwatchers: “I will reveal truth to all who seek it!” When he does, he gains the effects listed in “Third Ideal Actions” in his stat block.

These effects make him Empowered, thus granting him an advantage on all tests and refilling his Investiture to maximum at the start of his turns. In addition, he can now summon Tyche as a Radiant Shardblade; he chooses to do so in the form of a battleaxe, which he can make Offhand Strikes with.

- **Bookshelves and Shadows.** Several large shelves block the light from the stage’s sphere lanterns, creating pools of deep shadow (shown on map 7.2). - **Clutter.** The vault is covered in clutter, making the floor difficult terrain.

- **Greatshell Carapace.** The reconstructed greatshell carapace is suspended from the ceiling above by steel wires. It hangs 20 feet above the floor, and it is 60 feet long and 20 feet wide.

### Aftermath

The shadowform zealots and Ylt fight to the death. When Ylt is defeated, ***[[Items/Stonewalkers Unique Items/Talns Honorblade|Taln’s Honorblade]]*** falls to the floor. If Kaiana is with the party, she sits beside Ylt’s corpse, mourning his death and furious at him for going astray.

Defeating the shadowform zealots releases shadowspren, an incredibly rare type of spren originally used to create the shadowform Regals. If Axies is with the party, he excitedly studies the shadowspren, considering his “research expedition” a rousing success.

The party can retrieve the Honorblade, but Pailiah’s entry prevents them from immediately leaving with it; proceed to [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|The Herald Herself]].

### Tyche

After the battle, Ylt’s Enlightened mistspren Tyche (he/him) appears and mourns him. Tyche forms as a red crystalline structure and reveals that although he loved Ylt, he’s grateful the party intervened. He couldn’t stop Ylt from abusing the visions their bond provided, and Ylt’s choices caused Tyche much pain. Tyche is reluctant to bond anyone immediately, but if he senses a PC might better wield the visions he provides, he follows them.

Tyche isn’t evil, but he’s deeply fearful of the future and tends to overanalyze the visions he gives his Radiant, presenting the most pessimistic perspective available.

## Y8: Pailiah’s Chamber

This small chamber to the west of the vault is Pailiah’s personal study. A plain stone desk carved from the wall stands alongside a plush reading chair. The desk has writing implements and fine tools including measuring tools, a magnifying glass, and scales. In a glass enclosure are three objects, meant for study and categorization: a single silk glove, a pair of dice, and a cracked bowl.

Pailiah remains in this room until Ylt is defeated, then emerges (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|The Herald Herself]]).

## The Herald Herself

When Ylt is defeated, Pailiah (she/her) steps carefully into the vault from her chamber [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|(area Y8)]]. The party has the chance to talk with her and try to convince her to reengage with the world. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “Oh.” A soft, rich feminine voice echoes off the walls of the vault and its treasures. A doorway beside the stage opens and a tan-skinned woman with long, wavy black hair, jewel-like green eyes, and a flowing emerald dress accented with gold steps into the vault. She carefully steps over a hand mirror and an overturned silver goblet. “Ylt… I wish you hadn’t.”\
>      She walks to his side and crouches over him, her eyes tracing him as if in search of something. From his pocket, she draws out a curved ivory comb. “This will do,” she murmurs to herself. “I would like to remember you this way. Ylt and his beautiful golden hair, once kept so fine.”\
>      Her gaze sweeps over her vault as she turns, searching for a place to display it. Finally her eyes fall on you, and she remembers there are others here. “You came to stop Ylt,” she says. “Why?”

Pailiah is a tragic figure, long since withdrawn into herself. If Axies is with the party, he keeps quiet but observes her with a curious, scholarly air. Kaiana is overwhelmed at first, but she joins the conversation once Pailiah gains some momentum, speaking on behalf of the Eyes of Pala acolytes that Pailiah failed to protect.

## What Pailiah Shares

Pailiah can answer the following questions:

- **What was your relationship with Ylt?** “He was a talented leader. As I prefer my studies over administration, I was happy for his help. He planned for the future as I preserved the past. We worked well together. I believed our differences were our strength, but I did not know Odium whispered to him.”

- **What should we do with Taln’s Honorblade?** “That Blade is a reminder of a promise broken. A reminder of the pain dear Taln endured. It must not be forgotten, but I do not wish it in my custody nor to wield it.”

- **What is this place?** “This is a library of sorts. These objects were crafted, held, used, loved, and passed down by those long gone. They come to us from across Roshar and the millennia. They are here so they are not forgotten.”

- **What can you tell us about history?** If the party asks her about the Desolations or history surrounding the Heralds, Pailiah gazes into the distance, lost in the past for a few long moments. She doesn’t answer and changes the topic.

## To Relight the Flame of Knowledge

Pailiah is in a more lucid and accessible state than she’s experienced in years. The loss of Ylt, the coming of the Everstorm, and the sight of Taln’s Honorblade have brought her to a moment where she could take a step toward responsibility or slip further into decline.

If the party tries to encourage Pailiah to reengage with the outside world, lead the Eyes of Pala, or even rebuild the lost Truthwatcher order, they can attempt to convince her with a conversation (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Resolving the Conversation|Resolving the Conversation]]).

### Resisting Influence

This adventure doesn’t provide a stat block for Pailiah, but for the purpose of this conversation, each test to influence her has a DC of 17, and she spends up to 8 focus to resist influence.

The PCs find that she yields more easily to arguments that outline the destruction of peoples, cultures, and knowledge. Pailiah resists influence from any arguments that suggest she should personally get involved in events, and she grows listless and despondent if confronted with accusations or anger.

### Inspiring Pailiah

During this conversation, the party might try arguments or lines of discussion such as the following:

- **Your Acolytes Need a Leader.** Pailiah shies away from talk of duty and oaths, including pressure to take responsibility for Ylt’s actions. Her discomfort shows as detachment or distraction. If a PC fails a related test, she stubbornly insists she has been tested before and isn’t interested in tests. She is tired. Pailiah spends focus to resist arguments for her to take responsibility for others’ actions or to lead. However, she’s open to suggestions that she empower another, such as Kaiana, to lead.

- **Sharing Sentimentality.** If a PC expresses sentimentality, such as by sharing a treasured object or memory in their argument, or by sincerely finding value in Pailiah’s collection, they gain an advantage on all subsequent tests. Once a test like this has succeeded, Pailiah becomes open to their arguments and no longer resists tests which encourage her to get involved personally.

- **Saving People, Cultures, and Lives.** Pailiah is receptive to arguments concerning the preservation of knowledge and history. She animatedly joins debates about the best way to protect centers of knowledge and learning, but she’s quick to point out flaws in the party’s suggestions. If she finds their contributions lacking, Pailiah becomes withdrawn and introspective. This is a good place to raise the stakes.

- **Pailiah’s True Name.** If the characters succeeded in their conversation with Nale in [[Journal/05 - 05 The Eightys War|chapter 5]], they may have learned Pailiah’s true name, Pralla. If they address her using that name, she loses 2 focus.

If a PC addresses the needs of the remaining Eyes of Pala acolytes, that test gains an advantage from Kaiana, as she argues that Pailiah must ensure peaceful leadership for the Eyes of Pala acolytes who follow her.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Pailiah Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Resolving the Conversation

The conversation succeeds if the party convinces Pailiah to rejoin events in the world, either through making especially compelling arguments or because Pailiah no longer has enough focus to resist influence. If the party is unconvincing and fails multiple attempts to inspire Pailiah, she grows distracted by the items in her vault, and the conversation ends in failure.

- **Success.** Pailiah is convinced the time has come for her to get involved in events once again. Use the party’s arguments and intentions to guide this outcome. Pailiah acts conservatively, but she does act.

- **Failure.** Pailiah wanders away and peruses her collection, refusing to acknowledge further arguments about changing her ways. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Herald grows quiet, her eyes going distant. She wanders toward one of her shelves and draws out a tarnished silver bracelet. “You see this? It belonged to a brave and strong soul from Rira two hundred and sixteen years ago. If I am not here, who will remember these things? I am needed.”

### A Final Favor

Whether the conversation succeeds or fails, Pailiah asks for one additional favor. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “You met him, didn’t you? Taln.” She puts her hand out toward the Honorblade, breathing in deeply, like she might catalog away the memory of it. “Tell me about him. And… may I have something from you? To remember you by?”

If the PCs agree, Pailiah takes any object of theirs they offer. The more personal it is, the more pleased she is. If the PCs refuse her, sorrow flickers over her features, but she accepts with a nod.

## Epilogue

When the party leaves Rall Elorim, read the following to begin closing this adventure:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You step out into a changed world. The Everstorm rages over the continent. Heralds walk among you. The Roshar before you will never be the same.\
>      As the cascades of the Stormfalls surge behind you, you know one thing is certain: Taszo-son-Clutio would be proud if he knew how far you’d come.\
>      You’ve proven that stonewalkers can keep their oaths, and Po’ahu’s echoing laughter confirms her approval.\
>      Only one question remains—what next?

It should be simple to tie up the adventure from here. The party can return the Honorblade to Shinovar for their reward (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Onward to Shinovar|Onward to Shinovar]]) or keep the Blade for themselves (see [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Keeping the Blade|Keeping the Blade]]). If the PCs befriended Kaiana, she may decide to travel with them; if not, she stays with Pailiah. Axies leaves in search of more unique spren.

But the party’s adventures need not stop here, if you wish! See [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Continuing Your Campaign|Continuing Your Campaign]] for ideas for future story arcs.

## Onward to Shinovar

If the PCs fulfill their quest and return the Honorblade to Shinovar, they can travel west by sea. There, Rit- daughter-Clutio (she/her), Taszo’s older sister, takes custody of the Honorblade and offers the characters rewards for tier 2 (see chapter 8 of the *Stormlight Handbook*).

These rewards could include priceless items such as a Shardblade, a large sum of 2,000 marks, a unique ancient fabrial that gives access to the surge of Cohesion, or animal companions unique to Shinovar.

Less tangible rewards earned could include deepening spren bonds, a new spren bond for a PC who doesn’t yet have one, or meeting the qualifications to become a Ryshadium steed’s chosen rider.

## Keeping the Blade

In the novels of the Stormlight Archive, Taln’s Honorblade is taken to Shinovar. This adventure fills in the gaps of how, where, and why these events occurred. However, you and the players should feel free to break away from canon if desired—this story is your own! Should the PCs wish to keep the Honorblade or leave it with Pailiah, let them. It’s the tale they want to tell.

If the party keeps the Honorblade, powerful figures in Roshar eventually come looking for it, such as Nale, Ishar, the Stone Shamans, or Liss.

## Continuing Your Campaign

After their conversation with Pailiah, the characters advance to 8th level, having played a key role in the fight against Odium. As the True Desolation begins in earnest, the characters have many options for how to proceed:

- Return to Rall Elorim to aid in the resistance movement.

- Join the refounded Knights Radiant in Urithiru.

- Find Nale and work with the Skybreakers.

- Return to Alethkar or Azir, now besieged by Odium’s forces.

- Travel across Roshar, helping communities and towns ruined by the Everstorm.

- Take the fight to Odium on their own, with or without helping Dalinar’s Coalition.

- Bond with Tyche and discover the machinations of Sja-anat, the Taker of Secrets.
