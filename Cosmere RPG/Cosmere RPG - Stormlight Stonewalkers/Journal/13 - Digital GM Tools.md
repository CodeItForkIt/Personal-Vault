# Digital GM Tools

## How to Use

Stonewalkers™ is the first campaign-length adventure for the Cosmere® Roleplaying Game. Featuring an original storyline by Brandon Sanderson and Dan Wells, it takes characters from Alethkar to Shinovar and from levels 1-8. In Stonewalkers, player characters will experience an epic canonical story while beginning their Radiant journey!

Gamemasters interested in running this adventure have access to a variety of optional accessories to enhance your players' experience in this foundry module:

- In the Scene Tab you will find both Battle Maps and Theatre of the Mind scenes that corispond to differnt points in the chapters already populated with NPCs as needed and organized by chapter.

- The Actor table contains many tokens/character sheets for every named NPC in the adventure as well as spren you can bond and adversaries you can face. Feel free to mix them in to scenes to really make the story yours.

- On top of that these Digital GM Tools will help further enhance your experience and help you GM your session of stonewalkers

This journal includes summaries of the main events in the Stonewalkers adventure for the Cosmere RPG.

You'll find the links to stat blocks after each chapter summary, along with section names listing their key combatants.

When there's an endeavor in play, the requirements are given as "(x/y)" where x is the number of successes needed before y failures.

For Tracking Endevors or Events we recomend the "[Challenge Tracker](https://foundryvtt.com/packages/challenge-tracker)" and "[Global Progress Clocks](https://foundryvtt.com/packages/global-progress-clocks)" modules for foundry.

We hope the Stonewalkers Digital GM Tools provide you with a convenient way to summarize key notes and reference adversaries. Enjoy these tools, and good luck with your campaign!

## Chapter 1: Honor beyond the Storm

## Shelter for the Storm

As a highstorm approaches, the PCs explore the merchant

caravan and Kholin caravan. They can:

- Notice Po’ahu.

- Learn about the Covenant Stone Crossroads.

- Share a story or lie to Nen (DC 13 Deception) for gossip.

- Work for [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Ellar|Ellar]] (DC 10 for task of choice) and learn gossip.

- Sneak into Kholin Caravan (DC 10 Stealth) and overhear gossip.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Caravan Gossip]]

### GM Tips

Give the characters time to explore the wagons before starting the next scene with Taszo.

## A Legend Returned

[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Taszo-son-Clutio|Taszo]] (he/him) and [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Ellar|Ellar]] (he/him) argue, giving PCs a chance to intervene and earn [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Bordin|Bordin's]] respect, or learn about the prisoner.

- Stop Ellar’s harassment of the prisoner (DC 13 Intimidation or Leadership; repeatable as Intimidation). [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Ellar|Ellar's Stat Block]].

- Learn about Taln’s Honorblade:

  - Taszo’s group was sent to retrieve the Honorblade from the warcamps.

  - Taszo’s group was assassinated.

  - The Honorblade is in the caravan.

  - Taln is the Herald he claims to be.

### GM Tips

Allow time to theorize and question Taszo before continuing.

## Wet and Waylaid

[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Tet Rebin|Tet Rebin's]] (he/him) merchant wagon is stuck nearby, and his daughter [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Hana|Hana]] (she/her) approaches the PCs for help. Characters can discover the coming ambush.

- Rebin: Cognitive defense 13, Spiritual defense 13.

- Realize the crem puddle is a trap (DC 12 Deduction or Survival).

- Persuade Rebin to leave (Relevant Skill vs. Rebin's Cognitive or Spiritual defense). A PC who makes an argument about Hana's safety gains an advantage.

- Pull the wagon free (endeavor 3/3)

  - The PCs can use brute force, flip the chull, or use engineering.

### GM Tips

The ambush begins immediately after the endevor is clear; skip to Bandit Ambush.

## Staying With the Caravan

PCs can choose not to help Rebin. They can try to break into the locked wagon to discover [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]] (she/her) attempting to steal the Honorblade.

- Break in to the wagon (endeavor 4/2)

- PCs can distract the guards, sneak past the guards, pick the lock, or find other entrances.

- Discover how Kaiana entered (DC 12 Perception).

- Recognize the Fabrial lock (relevant expertise or DC 16 Lore).

- Pick the Fabrial lock (DC 25 Thievery).

### GM Tips

Characters have only a brief moment to investigate inside the wagon before the Bandit Ambush begins.

## Bandit Ambush

Four

bandits facing off with the characters.

*If at the Merchant Wagon:*

- Use a crem puddle as a restraint (DC 10 Agility; on a failure, be Restrained).

- Wagon and chull provide cover; if freed, the chull can use their Defense Curl.

*If at the Honorblade Wagon:*

- The wagons provide cover.

- The PCs can retrieve scattered weapons.

- If the ambush triggers while the PCs are breaking into the Honorblade wagon, add two more bandits.

The bandits flee into the highstorm after 3 rounds, or when only one is left alive.

## Into the Storm

Taszo learns the bandits are allied with those who murdered his fellow shamans. He pursues them into the highstorm. PCs can choose to follow him or seek shelter during the storm.

*If they follow:*

- Press into the storm (DC 13 Athletics or Survival; on a failure, take `1d6` damage and fall Prone).

- Discipline tests; highest encounters {Po’ahu (she/her).

- Discover [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Veth|Veth]] has fatally wounded Taszo.

- Combat with Veth, during which the following effects are active:

- Highstorm: roll on the Highstorm Effects table at the start of each combat round.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Highstorm Effects]]

- Moving 50 feet or more from the protective Covenant Stone deals `1d12` damage each turn.

*If they seek shelter:*

- Stay safe and find Taszo mortally wounded after the storm.

### GM Tips

End this scene with Taszo’s funeral, re-establishing the following key points.

## Key Points

- The party gains Po’ahu, hidden in her mysterious stone.

- The party promises Taszo they’ll find the Honorblade.

- Gain leads on Taszo’s killers:

  - The bandits’ glyph-tattooed faces and Veth’s spear-head dagger.

  - Seek Liss in the warcamps.

## Chapter 2: Weeper's Trail

## Respite

Arrive at Alethi warcamps and give party a chance to orient.

- Alethi PCs know the names of the ten camps: Roion, Sadeas, Aladar, Dalinar Kholin, Vamah, Ruthar, Thanadal, Hatham, Bethab, and Sebarial.

## Searching the Warcamps

Each scene contains a lead toward finding Liss and offers rewards, including a favor that can be used in the Herald of War section. When a scene’s goal is resolved, reveal a lead and mark it on the Warcamp Scenes table. The leads in order are:

- A Thaylen menagerie owner won a strange chicken while gambling with a mysterious Shin traveler.

- A Shin traveler visited the Aladar warcamp to attend the Red Rockbud, a gambling den.

- The Shin traveler met with a full-figured, attractive woman with dark hair in the Red Rockbud.

- A woman matching that description has been seen wandering the Outer Market near the Crem Oven bakery.

### GM Tips

Each time the PCs discover a lead, Taszo’s peakspren Po’ahu stirs.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Warcamp Scenes]]

## Chasm Offensive

Sergeant Ellar is recruiting for an expedition to track down Parshendi scouts (3 **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singers]]**) and their leader **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Calinai|Calinai]]** (nimbleform singer, she/her).

- The singers can be noticed. Entire party makes a **DC14 Survival test**. If at least half succeed, the singers are caught unaware. Otherwise, the listeners are aware and prepare accordingly.

- PCs can convince the singers to stand down through a conversation while in combat.

- Calinai: Cognitive defense 15, Spiritual defense 15; 4 focus. Spends 1 focus to resist influence from humans.

- Calinai is searching for a treasured bracelet. The PCs can find it (**DC 14 Deduction**).

## Guilty Spheres

Play a game of Pieces with the Red Rockbud’s proprietor **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Nel|Nel]]** (expert, she/her) in exchange for information.

- **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Brightness Bettani|Betanni]]** (she/her, expert) must deliver missives from trading posts in Sebarial’s warcamp either via her broken spanreed or by hand (endeavor 4/3).

## Weeper’s Eyes

## The Contact

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Liss|Liss]]** (duelist shardbearer, she/her) presses for details on what happened to Taszo.

- After conversation with Liss, Po’ahu emerges from her stone.

- After PCs leave, a Sons of Honor captain (**[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Thrill Berserker|thrill beserker]]**), **[[Actors/Adversaries/Guard|guard]]**, and three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]** confront the party.

## Herald of War

PCs infiltrate the monastery (endeavor 9/4), find the Herald Taln, and ask him where the Honorblade has been taken.

PCs can use favors earned during Searching the Warcamps to gain advantage on a test.

Obstacles:

- Kholin Guards: Forged papers, convince the Guards, scale the walls.

- Ardents: Convince the ardents, sneak, find a secret entrance.

- The Herald’s Cell: Spy on ardents, sneak into offices, search records.

- Whispers in the Dark: Disguise, deduce layout, slip through shadows.

- The Cell Door: Convince an ardent, lockpick, break it down.

PCs who fail face 6 **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** and, after 2 rounds, **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 2/Brightlord Meridas Amaram|Brightlord Amaram]]** (duelist Shardbearer) attempts to de-escalate while learning what the PCs know about Lost Radiants.

## Chapter 3: The City That Smolders

## Traveling to Rathalas

Bordin, Liss, or a favor owed from chapter 2 can help the party get to Rathalas. Potential opportunities include joining a Kholin patrol (**DC 13 Leadership**) or escorting Brightness Betanni (**DC 12 Survival**).

The journey takes twenty days.

## A Fellow Traveler

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Lorn|Lorn]]** (he/him), a bandit deserter encountered on the road, offers a chance for the party to learn more about Ylt's presence in Rathalas.

- Recognize he’s a former bandit (**DC 13 Deduction or Insight**, or have the Underworld expertise).

- If traveling with a Kholin patrol, convince the patrol not to arrest Lorn (**DC 14 Intimidation, Leadership, or Persuasion**).

## Rathalas, the Burned City

Bandits used Rathalas as their hideout until Ylt arrived, killed

the Bandit Queen, and captured her Lieutenant Teryn.

### Approaching Rathalas

The party can scout ahead to identify three entrances to Rathalas (see map 3.1): the canyon floor (A1), watch post (A2), and cliff face (A3). Before the party chooses an entrance, describe Bandits on Alert event.

### Bandits on Alert

Negative event with 3 slots.

When triggered:

- Three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** and three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]** attack; retreating if half fall or taking PCs to prison.

## Chapter 4: Into the Valley

## Setting Sail

Gray Remnant says that Ylt travels to Cultivation’s Valley in Greater Hexi. Teryn lends the Storm’s Jest to pursue.

## The Everstorm

The party rushes to catch Ylt, but is slowed by the Everstorm.

### Writhing Clouds

Swarming skyeels batter the ship, dealing `1d4` impact damage unless PCs are behind cover or dodging (**DC 10 Agility test**).

- Two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Greater Skyeel|greater skyeels]]** and two normal **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Skyeel|skyeels]]** attack.

- PCs fight on a swaying deck surrounded by panicking skyeels. Battlefield effects:

- Skyeel panic deals `1d6 + 5` impact damage to the ship.

- Swaying deck (**DC 10 Agility**; on a failure, fall Prone and move 10 feet in a random direction).

- Skyeel swarm causes an additional skyeel to arrive.

- The battle ends once both greater skyeels are defeated.

## A New Storm

Odium’s Everstorm, newly formed, surprises the ship from the east.

Surviving the powerful winds, lightning strikes, and a breaking mast both damages the ship (endeavor 6/4).

### GM Tips

This storm gives Voidspren the opportunity to interact with any singer characters.

### Awakening

Tam and Veda take workform, then try to leave. PCs may attempt to talk about their experience in slaveform (**Persuasion DC 12**), but they will not be persuaded to stay.

## Pursuit of Truth

Karanak was also damaged by the Everstorm. The ship is likely in need of repair, but each day counts as 1 failure on the Following Ylt endeavor.

### Following Ylt

Pursuing Ylt across Greater Hexi (endeavor 3/3) abridges the weeks of journey across each location on the map:

- **Tarat Sea:** Chart a course, read the winds and weather, bolster supplies, survey the deck for concerns, make repairs, organize the crew.

- **Storm in Triax:** Seek shelter from a highstorm in a den of pirates and deserters, ward off threats, carouse and make dubious friends, or creep around looking for Ylt’s spies. PCs can also recharge their spheres and singer characters can change forms.

- **Shores of Hexi:** Make landfall and spot where Ylt docked, scout ahead on foot, or follow tracks from the Eyes of Pala.

### GM Tips

All tests on the ship have disadvantage if the party leaves without a full crew or fully repaired ship.

## Saving Axoq

## The Valley of the Nightwatcher

The Nightwatcher shows visions to each PC (**DC 12 Discipline**; on a failure, become Disoriented until the end of the first round of the next combat). Truthwatcher’s Folly begins.

### Truthwatcher’s Folly (See Map 4.2)

The Nightwatcher grants **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt]]** his ***[[Items/Stonewalkers Unique Items/Raysium Knife|raysium knife]]***, but with the curse that another must wield it. Ylt attempts to persuade Kaiana, tracked by the negative event Kaiana Aids Ylt.

- **Start:** Ylt and Kiana take fast turns to get Kaiana to take the ***[[Items/Stonewalkers Unique Items/Raysium Knife|raysium knife]]***. Ylt cannot hold the knife.

- **Event Progresses:** If PCs don’t attempt to sway Kaiana.

- **Event triggers when:** Kaiana can no longer be swayed.

- **Swaying Kaiana:** Raise the stakes (Leadership vs. Ylt’s +6 Leadership). Advantage if PCs succeeded on the chapter 3 conversation. Disadvantage if anyone attacks Kaiana. Kaiana spends 2 focus to resist.

Kaiana fights in self-defense, and refuses to kill Pailiah or leave the grove with the knife. Combat ends when Ylt is reduced to 40 health or below, or when Kaiana dies or sides with the party.

This can be run as an event. At the end of each round, if no PCs attempted to sway Kaiana that round, the event progresses by one. The Nightwatcher urges the party to take the ***[[Items/Stonewalkers Unique Items/Raysium Knife|raysium knife]]*** to Nale in Ashiqqil Keep. She also offers each PC a boon.\

## Chapter 5: The Eighty’s War

## Call to Arms

On the mountain road to Emul, the party stumbles into a fierce battle over the castle of Ashiqqil (see map 5.1).

### Battle Phases

Use the chart below to navigate the battle phases.

### The Approach

PCs choose whether to join the battle and who to aid. **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 4/Axoq|Axoq]]** pushes for support of the Emuli. The can choose to gather information first (endeavor 3/2):

- Gather information through knowledge of current politics, spying, by finding and interrogating a lost Tukari spearman, or by charming or deceiving them.

### Assault on the Walls

The characters join the battle.

- Four Emuli **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** attempt to enter the keep against the defense of Eight Tukari **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]**.

- The Ashiqqil Gate Falls is a positive event:

- Stakes raise one fast turn and one slow turn each round.

- PCs holding the ram can Use a Skill to make a **DC 20 Athletics or Leadership** test to progress the event by 1.

### Y1: Meditation Room and Main Hallway

The PCs can cross the meditation room (Y1) and enter directly. The pillar at the center of the room is carved with names that can be identified as notable scholars and leaders (DC 15 Lore). Approaching through Y1 is the most obvious route and also the most visible to Ylt’s followers in the entry hall.

### Y2: Flooded Crypt

This partially flooded crypt is filled with urns and ashes. PCs investigating the water notice light coming from a 30-foot underwater passage leading to the pool in Y4. If Axies is with the party and nobody investigates, he jumps in and reveals the passage.

### Y3: Collapsed Mausoleum

The collapsed ceiling of this mausoleum hides a tunnel connected to the entry hall. PCs can detect it (DC 16 Perception or Lore), and remove the rubble with three tests (DC 15 Athletics), each raising the stakes. If they fail to notice the tunnel and Po’ahu is with them, she indicates it to them.

### Y4: Entry Hall

Four Eyes of Pala agents and two Eyes of Pala watchers are removing corpses of acolytes who opposed Ylt’s coup. If Ryel was not stopped, they are alert and ready, otherwise PCs can attempt to approach the inner vault with stealth, force, or guile.

- Sneaking through (**DC 16 Stealth**).

- Fast-talking (**DC 20 Deception, Intimidation, or Persuasion**). The lead watcher spends 2 focus to resist being influenced.

- **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Watcher|Eyes of Pala watcher]]**: Cognitive defense 15, Spiritual defense 16, 5 focus. Spends spends 2 focus to resist being influenced; when reduced to 1 focus, he lets the PCs pass.

- Fighting Ylt’s acolytes: The acolytes fight to the death.

## Inner Vault (See Map 7.1)

Through the bronze doors is a sacred library in decline.

### Y5: Library

Three bodies are here, two near the door and one at the desk with a door lock fabrial (**DC 10 Crafting**). The books around the desk are notes on Investiture, Regal Forms, and the Unmade. Axies can theorize as to the release of Shadowform singers from this, or a PC with the Listener expertise can recall it (**DC 12 Lore**).

### Y6: Antechamber

PCs must infuse the emeralds set in the large fabrial door in the correct order ("Truth", "Leader", "Speak") to enter.

- Determine the order by answering questions with Lore tests about the Truthwatchers **(DC 12, 14, and 16**).

- PCs can also force the door, disable the lock, or modify the fabrial If the door’s defenses are triggered by failing either of the above, it flashes a bright light and each character must make a test (**DC 18 Discipline**; on a failure, become Stunned until the end of the

first combat round of the next scene).

If the party fails three times to open the door through any method, including incorrectly infusing the emeralds, the door opens on the third failed attempt but triggers its defenses.

## The Savior of All

Final confrontation with Ylt.

### Y7: Pailiah’s Vault (Map 7.2)

If Kaiana is with the party, she tries unsuccessfully to talk Ylt down. She joins the fight. If Axies is with the party, he refuses to join the battle.

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt]]** summons four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Shadowform Zealot|shadowform zealots]]** to aid in combat.

Pailiah remains in this chamber throughout the battle, and emerges from it after. In a conversation, PCs can attempt to influence her to join the battle against Odium, or seek her advice regarding Taln’s Honorblade. Tests to influence her are DC 17, and she spends up to 8 focus to resist influence.
