# 03: The City That Smolders

After their adventures on the Shattered Plains, the PCs know Taln’s Honorblade is in Rathalas. There, the ambitious Truthwatcher Ylt has taken control of a force of bandits—but not everyone is loyal to their zealous new leader.

## Running This Chapter

This chapter starts as the characters secure a method of travel to Rathalas by calling on favors from [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]]. On the road, they meet a bandit deserter who can tell them about the current situation in Rathalas.

The majority of the chapter is an infi ltration of the dungeon-like city of Rathalas. Familiarize yourself with the three entrances to the city and the map of Rathalas. As the characters navigate the ruined city, they must avoid bandit scrutiny to move safely.

Complications can be used to progress the Bandits on Alert event that triggers a fight with bandits. See [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Approaching Rathalas|Approaching Rathalas]] later in this chapter for guidance on how to track the event.

In the bandits’ prison, the party has a chance to talk to Kaiana, Ylt’s second-in-command. This conversation determines how likely Kaiana is to switch sides and join the party in [[Journal/04 - 04 Into the Valley|chapter 4]].

Ylt is always encountered in the [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|Heart of the Stronghold]] section, but PCs can engage in conversations and endeavors to gain information and allies among the bandits, potentially freeing Rathalas from his tyranny.

During this chapter, whether in the bandit prison or after the battle with Ylt’s zealots, the party meets Axies the Collector (see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction). Regardless of how the PCs handle Rathalas’s political situation, they must speak with the sole entity who knows Ylt’s true intentions and next destination: the Gray Remnant spren in Lower Rathalas. The fastest way to reach the spren is with Axies’s help.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 3rd level and advance to 4th level after speaking with the Gray Remnant.

The following decisions offer opportunities to introduce spren, strengthen spren bonds, or swear Ideals:

- How the PCs treat the ex-bandit Lorn.

- Whether the PCs decide to free prisoners or aid the bandit resistance.

- Whether a PC decides to reveal a painful truth to the Gray Remnant.

Characters with goals related to learning more about the Heralds, Honorblades, or Radiants have opportunities to do so when speaking with Kaiana or the Gray Remnant.

## Traveling to Rathalas

To secure transportation to Rathalas, the party can approach Bordin, Liss, or someone who owes them a favor. Potential approaches include:

- **Join a Kholin Patrol.** Calling in a favor or succeeding on a DC 13 Leadership test convinces Bordin, Ellar, or another Kholin representative to hire the party as bodyguards for a patrol headed north. Horses are provided, and the characters are each paid 30 marks in advance for their services.

- **Escorting Brightness Bettani.** The party can accompany Brightness Bettani, who is headed to the Thanadal princedom on urgent business. Horses are provided for the journey through the Unclaimed Hills, but their equipment is old and poorly maintained.

If the party chooses this option, each character must succeed on a DC 12 Survival test or become Exhausted [-1] upon arriving in Rathalas.

In either case, the journey takes twenty days. The PCs are accompanied by stable hands who take the horses back to the warcamps when the party’s escort duty is complete.

## A Fellow Traveler

As the party travels, they get a chance to learn about the bandits. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Another day of travel comes to a close. As your group searches for a place to camp, you come across a weathered old man settled into a comfortable lait, a natural formation that blocks winds from the east. This looks like a suitable place to camp, and the old man— though armed—seems friendly enough.

The man is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Lorn|Lorn]]** (bandit, he/him), a deserter who was loyal to the Bandit Queen and left when Ylt killed her. He is headed to the Shattered Plains to look for work, and he is interested in news about the warcamps. He offers the following information without incriminating himself as a former bandit:

- **What do you know about the region?** “The bandits have gotten more violent. Vicious. I hear their numbers are growing.”

- **What do you know about bandits?** “I heard their old queen was killed. Now they’ve got some new cultish leader pushing his own agenda.”

- **What do you know about the bandits’ leader?** “I heard he’s an Iriali Shardbearer. Hair like gold, and the biggest Shardblade you’ve ever seen.”

- **Is there anything else helpful you could tell us?** Lorn is hesitant to respond, but if a PC succeeds on a DC 12 Persuasion test, he reveals, “I’ve heard that not everybody in Rathalas likes their new leader. There’s a quartermaster named Ubo at the carpentry works near the canyon floor.” If the PC fails, Lorn simply says, “Not much else to say.”

Characters with the Underworld expertise or who succeed on a DC 13 Deduction or Insight test recognize that Lorn is a former bandit. If the party is traveling with the Kholin patrol, the patrol leader insists on arresting Lorn. Convincing the patrol to let Lorn go requires a successful DC 14 Intimidation, Leadership, or Persuasion test.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> A character who encourages Lorn to find a better life for himself may attract the attention of a lightspren, whereas a character who wants to turn him in for his crimes may attract the attention of a highspren.

## Rathalas, the Burned City

A decade ago, shortly after the unification of Alethkar, Rathalas was home to tens of thousands of Alethi ruled by a brightlord named Tanalan. The Rift, as the city was also called, was uniquely protected from highstorms by virtue of being in a massive canyon that split the ground.

When the city flirted with rebellion, King Gavilar sent Dalinar “the Blackthorn” Kholin to end the threat. After Brightlord Tanalan’s assassins killed the Blackthorn’s wife, Dalinar set the entire city on fire. With support from Highprince Sadeas, Rathalas became an example of what happens to rebels in Alethkar. Thousands of civilians perished in the flames. Now, burned timbers are all that remains of the homes and walkways that were once suspended across the chasm. The only areas that survived intact are those carved into the cliffside.

> [!tip] Secret History
> The actions of Highprinces Dalinar Kholin and Torol Sadeas at Rathalas were even harsher than the offcial story, but the history of Rathalas is presented as fact and believed by all the NPCs in this story.

## Current Events in Rathalas

In recent years, a group of bandits claimed the ruined city of Rathalas as their hideout. These bandits were led by a woman known only as the Bandit Queen. They raided caravans and took hostages, but didn’t relish bloodshed, and they killed only when they deemed it absolutely necessary.

Several weeks ago, Ylt arrived and everything changed. He killed the Bandit Queen and confined her trusted lieutenant, Teryn, in the prison. Ylt started to radicalize the bandits and promised amazing powers in exchange for their faith and worship. He traveled with his apprentice Kaiana and some converts to steal Taln’s Honorblade, and he now trains in the Blade’s use.

Ylt’s preaching and flaunting of the Honorblade has attracted new recruits and encouraged violence, escalating the brutality of the bandits’ activities in the Unclaimed Hills. Ylt’s most loyal bandit converts use the zealot stat block. They are identifiable not only by their green robes, but by the blades they carry, which are inscribed with the Truthwatcher glyph and match the dagger used to kill Taszo.

At the base of the canyon, a shallow river runs westward, flowing eventually to the Sea of Spears. In the past, people entered the city via ramps from the plains level, but now the ruined city’s western edge can be accessed from the canyon floor level. The remains of a once-mighty wall encircle the canyon, the fortification now jagged, unkempt, and covered in years of accumulated highstorm crem. Along the wall, the spherelights of a single watch post glitter.

### Event: Bandits on Alert

As the party approaches the city, most bandits assume they’re new recruits. Announce to the players that they’re beginning an event (see “Events” in chapter 9 of the *Stormlight Handbook*). This negative event, Bandits on Alert, has three slots to determine when the bandits discover there are intruders in their midst.

![[pg060_event card_Bandits on Alert example.webp]]

#### **Progressing the Event**

When a PC gains a Complication during any scene in Rathalas, you can spend it to progress the event by 1, trading an immediate Complication for the looming threat of being confronted.

#### **Triggering the Event**

If you fill all three slots with Complications, a horn sounds, putting the encampment on high alert. The prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A7)]] gains two more bandit guards.

Additionally, a group of three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** and three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]** intercept the party. If the PCs look out of place, these adversaries attack before asking questions. They retreat or surrender if half their number falls. However, if the party is defeated or surrenders, the bandits take them to the prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A7)]] and throw them in the barrel room [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area P3)]].

## A1: Canyon Floor

The western mouth of the canyon (see [[Scenes Index|map 3.2]]) is choked with oversized coastal rockbuds, eight to nine feet tall and swollen with moisture from the lake. Thick vines crisscross the stone between the rockbuds, making the area into difficult terrain.

![[pg061_Map 3-2_Canyon Floor_ZACH CREPEAU.webp]]

**Map 3 2: Canyon Floor** **-** **ZACH CREPEAU**

### Khornak Attack

Three amphibious **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Khornak|khornaks]]** lie in wait underneath the vines halfway through the rockbuds.

A PC who succeeds on a DC 15 Perception test spots the khornaks and can alert the party before the attack. This test gains a disadvantage, as the khornaks are hiding in cover. However, if the PCs scouted the canyon floor before entering (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Approaching Rathalas|Approaching Rathalas]]), they might’ve already spotted movement among the rockbuds; if so, this test also gains an advantage.

Unless detected, the khornaks unexpectedly burst from beneath the vines, and each PC gains the Surprised condition until the end of their first turn of combat. The khornaks fight to the death.

### Entering the City

As this scene ends and the party enters the city for the first time, see the earlier [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Navigating Rathalas|Navigating Rathalas]] section.

### Area Connections

This area connects to the carpenter’s yard [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A4)]].

## A2: Watch Post

Just inside the city gates, the bandits have constructed a series of ladders, ramps, and checkpoints to safely move from their base up to the plains. Their winch-and-pulley system can move their spoils up and down, but most of the bandits rely on the ladders rather than the swinging platform. There are eight bandit **[[Actors/Adversaries/Zealot|zealots]]** at this watch post on the plains level.

The bandits assume that anyone who knows of their hideout must be a new recruit, though they won’t let anyone pass without a successful DC 17 Deception test. On a success, the bandits usher the party in and direct them to begin manual labor in the devotary [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(Area A5)]]. On a failure, the bandits violently chase the party away, and the Bandits on Alert event progresses by 1 (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Navigating Rathalas|Navigating Rathalas]] for guidance on this event).

### Entering the City

If this scene ends successfully, the party enters the city for the first time. See the earlier [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Navigating Rathalas|Navigating Rathalas]] section.

### Area Connections

This area connects to the Devotary [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A5)]].

## A3: Cliff Face

Climbing down into Rathalas is dangerous, requiring each character to make a DC 13 Athletics test to reach stable footing. Raise the stakes on the first PC’s test when they pick the route. If that test gains an Opportunity, the other PCs gain an advantage on their test. If that test gains a Complication, the DC increases to DC 14 for everybody else, as one of the ledges crumbles and is no longer viable.

If a character fails their Athletics test to climb down, they can make a DC 15 Agility test to save themself. If this Agility test succeeds, they catch themself before falling but lose 1 focus after the close call. If this Agility test fails, they slide down the stone wall, then fall 20 feet into the charred husk of a building, taking 7 (`2d6`) impact damage.

The first time a character falls, progress the Bandits on Alert event by 1 (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Navigating Rathalas|Navigating Rathalas]] for guidance on this event). Subsequent falls don’t progress the event further.

## A4: Carpenter’s Yard

The fortified roof of a gutted building holds a carpentry yard where the bandits produce materials for their settlement. The yard also houses two ships in a hidden bay.

### Area Connections

This area connects to the canyon floor [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A1)]], the cliff face [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A3)]], and the maze of bridges [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A6]]).

### Inspiring the Quartermaster

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A dozen workers saw, chop, and shape wooden planks, ramps, and beams in this area. A small cantina holds workers taking their leisure and keeping to themselves. One person stands out from the crowd: a middle-aged Herdazian man whom the others treat with deference. He gives a wide berth to a group of six sour-faced, heavily armed bandits wearing green robes and proudly displaying glyph-inscribed swords.\
>      Jumping on a bench, the Herdazian claps once loudly. “All right, if you’re new here, come to me for assignments. Everyone else, you know the drill!”

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Ubo|Ubo the quartermaster]]** (bandit, he/him) is a middle- aged Herdazian who bears no love for Ylt. The six green-clad bandits (**[[Actors/Adversaries/Zealot|zealots]]**) are loyal to Ylt and monitor Ubo’s team.

![[pg063_Ubo_NELE KLUMPE.webp]]

**NELE KLUMPE**

> [!example] Roleplaying Ubo
> **Characteristics:** Cautious, kind, attentive.
>
> **Goal:** Ensure the safety of his friends and colleagues.
>
> **Appearance:** Ubo (“OO-boh”; he/him) is a fashionable middle-aged Herdazian human. His brightly painted fingernails coordinate perfectly with the colorful accents in his work clothes.
>
> Ubo was a merchant ship’s quartermaster until his ship was captured by the Rathalas bandits on the Sea of Spears. Now he oversees the carpenter’s yard, employing his strong sense for numbers—and displaying his fashion sense through his clothing.
>
> When conversing, Ubo listens but wants to avoid drawing attention. He ends a conversation abruptly if the exchange gets loud or heated.

#### **Conversation Approaches**

If the party approaches Ubo, he assumes they’re new recruits. PCs who wish to convince Ubo to help can attempt to do so with a conversation (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Resolving the Conversation|Resolving the Conversation]]). The following is an example of one approach the party might take:

- **Helping the People of Rathalas.** Ubo wants to protect as many friends as he can. If the PCs succeed on tests that demonstrate their competence and desire to help the people of Rathalas, Ubo is willing to share details about Teryn (the former Bandit Queen’s imprisoned lieutenant) and the prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(Area A7)]]. On failed tests, Ubo implores the characters to leave Rathalas and return with a highlord—but the PCs know that doing so would likely result in the Alethi claiming the Honorblade.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Ubo Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Ubo Opportunities and Complications]]

#### **Resisting Influence**

Ubo has a Cognitive defense of 11, a Spiritual defense of 13, and 2 focus. In conversations, he spends focus to resist arguments that endanger the carpentry yard workers, but he has no love for the bandits and can be convinced to help the PCs in low-risk ways.

#### **Resolving the Conversation**

The conversation ends successfully if the PCs convince Ubo that Rathalas can be saved from Ylt, or if Ubo no longer has enough focus to resist influence. If the party is unable to convince Ubo after several attempts, Ubo decides the exchange is too risky, and the conversation ends in failure.

- **Success.** Ubo is excited to have allies and explains the layout of the settlement. This provides the PCs an advantage on all tests to cross the maze of bridges [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A6)]]. If Ubo hasn’t mentioned Teryn yet, he does so and recommends freeing Teryn from the prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A7)]].

- **Failure.** Ubo ends the conversation by making himself scarce, joining a large group of workers.

## A5: Devotary

Ramps and ladders connect to a ruined devotary the bandits are clearing to use as storage for stolen goods.

### Area Connections

This area connects to the watch post [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A2)]], the maze of bridges [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A6)]], the prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A7)]], and the heart of the stronghold [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A8)]].

### Passing Through the Devotary

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Cut into the stone, half a building stands before you— its other half fell to the canyon floor years ago. What’s left is a vaulted hall that leads to a domed chamber. There, bandits clear out stone rubble to create a storage area for crates. In an archway framing a view of the burned city below, a dark-haired woman in worn leathers sits, watching with sharp eyes and yelling when workers slow their pace.

The woman is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 3/Nadari|Nadari]]** (thief, she/her) and she directs work in the devotary. She is accompanied by four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** and two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]** who guard the crates (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Special Rule: Sticky Fingers|Special Rule: Sticky Fingers]]).

Nadari is a weather-worn deserter from Highprince Thanadal’s forces on the Shattered Plains. There, she resented being relegated to messenger duties when she was a better scout than most of the men in that role. She’s a survivor, and she’s convinced Ylt can’t be defeated.

If the PCs pose as recruits, Nadari tasks them with clearing stone rubble. The work is punishing and Nadari is relentless. Ask the players if they do the work and for how long. If the PCs work for two hours without taking other actions, they must succeed on a DC 15 Athletics test or become Exhausted [-1]. After two hours, Nadari harshly instructs them to go to work in the [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|carpentry yard]] (area A4), accessible via the [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|maze of bridges]] (area A6).

If the party attempts to steal from the crates, or if they try to leave or sneak through the devotary, see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Pressing Forward|Pressing Forward]].

### Pressing Forward

If the party doesn’t work diligently for two hours, run an endeavor to see how their actions play out (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). Potential approaches include:

**Avoiding Bandit Suspicion.** PCs might move quietly to avoid notice (DC 13 Stealth) or bluff their way past with bravado (DC 14 Deception) or disguises (DC 12 Thievery). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

**Causing a Distraction.** PCs might attempt to draw the attention of the bandits to another section of the city (DC 12 Deception) or cause a collapse for the bandits to investigate (DC 10 Crafting).

**Climbing from Structure to Structure.** PCs could bypass the area by climbing across the cliff face (DC 12 Athletics) or moving carefully along scaffolding (DC 12 Agility). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

#### **Special Rule: Sticky Fingers**

PCs can examine the crates of stolen goods (DC 15 Thievery), but doing so increases the number of required successes for the endeavor by 1. This is a good place to raise the stakes. On a success, the PC swipes one item from the Stolen Goods table.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Stolen Goods]]

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Devotary Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Devotary Opportunities and Complications]]

#### **Resolving the Endeavor**

The endeavor succeeds if the party achieves 3 successes before 3 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective. (However, if the party attempts to steal anything, the number of required successes increases by 1 for each attempt.)

**Success.** The party passes through the devotary without issue.

**Failure.** Nadari confronts the party, accusing them of theft, and calls the bandits and archers guarding the crates to subdue them.

## A6: Maze of Bridges

This large gorge separates the major inhabited sections of Rathalas. The bandits have created several safe paths, but navigating the tangle of construction is difficult— and potentially dangerous—for people who don’t know the way.

![[pg065_Maze of Bridges_Kevin ONeill.webp]]

**KEVIN O’NEILL**

### Area Connections

This area connects to the cliff face [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A3)]], the carpenter’s yard [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A4)]], the devotary [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A5)]], and the prison [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(A7)]].

### Crossing the Maze of Bridges

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A large gorge filled with blackened debris separates the inhabited areas of Rathalas. Narrow rope bridges connect ledges and platforms to ruins, and ramps reach upward and downward. Some paths lead through buildings or into tunnels in the stone, and many show evidence of activity—either by bandits or feral animals. All the routes look treacherous, and the lowest levels are completely impassable.

The first time the party attempts to cross the maze of bridges, they must find a safe route while avoiding bandits and a pack of feral axehounds. After the PCs pick a destination, run an endeavor (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). Potential approaches include:

- **Inspecting Bridges.** Many of the bridges look structurally unsound. Checking their construction (DC 12 Crafting) before crossing would be wise.

- **Navigating Rubble.** Finding a shortcut could call for balancing on burned timbers (DC 12 Agility) or climbing short distances (DC 13 Athletics). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

- **Scouting.** PCs might want to look ahead to see if a route is viable (DC 12 Perception) or look for signs of regular usage (DC 13 Survival).

If the party befriended Ubo the quartermaster in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|area A4]], they gain an advantage on tests to cross these bridges.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Bridges Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Bridges Opportunities and Complications]]

#### **Resolving the Endeavor**

The endeavor succeeds if the party achieves 5 successes before 3 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

- **Success.** The party finds a safe way across the maze of bridges and reaches their intended destination.

- **Failure.** The party spends too much time in the maze of bridges and is cornered by six **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Axehound|axehounds]]**. Once the axehounds are defeated, the PCs reach their destination without incident.

### Confronting Kaiana

Kaiana isn’t expecting the PCs. She’s earnest and willing to talk, but she’s wary of infiltrators, has a fiery temper when provoked, and is protective of Ylt. For more information on roleplaying Kaiana, see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction.

> [!tip] Teryn the Lieutenant
> Teryn was only thirteen years old when Dalinar “the Blackthorn” Kholin set fire to everything she knew. The daughter of a fisher of the seventh nahn, Teryn was saved by ardents who led the city’s children into the caverns behind their devotary. When survivors from Rathalas turned to banditry, she adapted, proving herself a strong fighter and eventually becoming the Bandit Queen’s second-in-command. After Ylt arrived and killed the Bandit Queen, Teryn walked a fine line; she refused to swear loyalty but did tell her supporters to stand down. She said she’d consider Ylt’s ideology—in response, he spared her life but threw her in prison.

Teryn watches with interest and gets involved only if the discussion includes the bandits.

Kaiana knows that the PCs outnumber her. If the PCs are civil, Kaiana advises them to leave. If the PCs threaten her with violence, she uses her Disorienting Flash ability to flee; see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Resolving the Conversation|Resolving the Conversation]] to learn what she says to the party as she leaves.

### Convincing Kaiana to Question Ylt

Characters who wish to convince Kaiana to help them or to turn on Ylt can attempt to do so with a conversation (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Resolving the Conversation|Resolving the Conversation]]). If chaos or fighting has broken out in the prison, the party gains a disadvantage on tests to convince Kaiana.

Whenever the PCs address (or ignore) the bandits’ welfare, Teryn speaks up and grants the PC an advantage (or disadvantage) on their test for that contribution. The PCs might discuss topics like the following:

- **Arguing That Ylt’s Actions Are Evil.** PCs notice that Kaiana is strained when forced to confront Ylt’s actions. She inherently understands the First Ideal of the Radiants and that the ends don’t justify the means. If a PC fails a test, she explains there is a wider perspective that the party isn’t considering. If a PC succeeds on a test, she resists by spending focus and offering weak defenses of Ylt. This is a good place to raise the stakes.

- **Explaining the Importance of Their Quest.** Kaiana is receptive to arguments that the Honorblade must be kept out of the wrong hands, but she claims she’s convinced Ylt is worthy. If a PC mentions the Stone Shamans, their next test gains an advantage, as Kaiana feels guilty for standing by when Ylt killed Taszo’s companions. If a PC fails a test, Kaiana explains that Ylt works at the behest of the Herald Pala herself. She might also ask about the party’s plans for the Blade.

- **Expressing Concern for Kaiana.** Kaiana is thrown off guard by PCs who ask about her feelings or show concern for her. The first time this happens, the test gains an advantage. If a PC fails a test, Kaiana feels patronized by the attempt. If a PC succeeds on a test, she doesn’t resist by spending focus, and instead shares that Ylt seems different these days from the fiercely supportive mentor who empowered her to explore her Radiant abilities.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Kaiana Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Kaiana Opportunities and Complications]]

### Resisting Influence

Kaiana has a Cognitive defense of 13, a Spiritual defense of 16, and 4 focus. She’s receptive to PCs who appeal to her compassion, but she spends 2 focus to resist threats or arguments that criticize Ylt.

If Kaiana feels backed into a corner by a particularly aggressive argument, she makes a Leadership test with a +4 bonus, declaring that Ylt speaks on behalf of the Herald Pala and his visions must be heeded. If Kaiana’s test equals or exceeds the Spiritual defense of a PC, that PC either must spend 2 focus to resist influence or must back down from the conversation.

### Resolving the Conversation

The conversation succeeds if the party convinces Kaiana to question her loyalty to Ylt, either because they make especially compelling arguments or because Kaiana no longer has enough focus to resist influence. If the PCs are unconvincing and fail multiple attempts to sway Kaiana, you should decide when she has had enough, then end the conversation in failure. (The conversation also fails if any PC acts with hostility.)

- **Success.** Kaiana concedes that the PCs have made good points, but she still believes that Ylt is far too dangerous—particularly with the Honorblade—for the characters to face. She commits to pushing against his violent tendencies but urges the party not to oppose him directly. She gives them the keys to Teryn’s jail cell, then leaves the area without warning the bandit guards.

- **Failure.** Kaiana ends the conversation and flees to warn Ylt about the intruders. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “You clearly aren’t prepared for the truth of what Roshar is becoming. I hope you’ll find peace, but I won’t be responsible for the choices you’ve made.” Kaiana waves her hand, and a blinding flash of light and disorienting bang like a thunderclap fill the small room—then she is gone. The door to the prison hangs open. In the distance, guards call to each other.

Kaiana uses her Disorienting Flash ability and runs out of the prison, taking either the tunnel or the hallway down to the stronghold [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area A8)]].

Once Kaiana flees, the party must now escape quickly. If they head straight out of the prison, they don’t encounter any guards. However, if the party tries to recruit them, see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#Confronting the Guards|Confronting the Guards]].

### Prison Break

If the PCs free the prisoners or confront the prison guards, they must overcome a few obstacles.

#### **Freeing Prisoners**

Teryn is manacled to the wall, but the keys to her restraints are beside the door in the cells [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area P4)]]. The captives in the barrel room [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|(area P3]]) are terrified, but Teryn can rouse the four imprisoned bandits to fight on the party’s behalf. Axies wishes to stay in Rathalas for his research but won’t aid in fighting.

#### **Confronting the Guards**

The prison guards are hostile, but if any freed bandit loyalists are with the party, the guards hesitate before attacking. There were originally four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandit]]** guards (in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|area P2]]), and if the party triggered the Bandits on Alert event, this added an additional two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** (in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|area P1]]). However, the party may have already defeated some of them.

If the PCs attempt to recruit the guards, have a PC make a DC 15 Leadership test and raise the stakes. They gain an advantage on this test if Teryn is with the group. On a success, half of the prison guards (rounded up) join the loyalists against Ylt, but the rest attack. If this test gains an Opportunity, one of the bandits fumbles, dropping their mace. If this test gains a Complication, these guards prove especially loyal to Ylt and fight to the death; otherwise they flee when reduced below half health.

### Bandit Allies

Once the prison situation is resolved, any recruited bandits continue to the [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|heart of the stronghold]] (area A8) to confront Ylt. Depending on how the party resolved this scene, they may be leading up to seven bandit loyalists (three prison guards and the four loyalists imprisoned in [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders|area P3]]), plus Teryn and Tuxli.

## Battle of the Hall

For simplicity, the party’s allies, the removed bandits who are loyal to Ylt, and the crowd aren’t part of this combat scene. Instead, narrate their struggle in the background of combat as the PCs engage with the spearmaster, archers, and remaining zealots.

The PCs likely begin this scene on the east side of the platform or by the east passage (see [[Scenes Index|map 3.4]]).

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt]]** and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]]** begin on the west ledge, accompanied by two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archers]]**; the circular dais on the ledge is 5 feet tall. Ylt’s other forces include six **[[Actors/Adversaries/Zealot|zealots]]** and a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spearmaster|spearmaster]]**; position them across the wooden platform to block access to Ylt and the archers.

For every two NPCs the party gained as allies, reduce the above number of enemy zealots by one as Teryn and her recruited loyalists join the fight. For example, if the PCs left the prison with four recruits, remove two zealots. (At maximum, remove four zealots.)

### Ylt’s Actions

Ylt begins combat on the stable stone ledge to the west, standing immediately above the heavy chain that he just cut. However, this combat is not designed to include him, and he makes his exit as soon as possible. To facilitate that, he can use any action in his stat block as 1 during this scene, even if it usually requires 2.

Ylt has both a fast and slow turn, and he uses his Boss feature to spend focus and gain an extra action after each PC turn.

At the earliest opportunity, Ylt uses Stone Spear (as 1) to fully collapse the platform the PCs—and his own zealots—are standing on. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ylt raises an open hand. The stone at his feet shifts, rippling away from him. Moments later, a massive stone spike erupts from below the platform, exploding through the wood. The gathering area lurches and drops.

The platform crashes 15 feet down to the rubble below (see [[Scenes Index|map 3.5]]), and characters on the platform move with it. Each PC on the platform must make a DC 12 Agility test, taking 3 (1d6) impact damage on a failure. Half of the zealots take 3 damage each from the rough landing.

As the platform collapses, Ylt’s Flowing Earth feature moves him 15 feet to the western passage (no action required). He harshly commands Kaiana to follow him—and if he can do so before she takes a turn, he uses his Vaulting Stone (1) to move her to the tunnel himself. Once Kaiana is in the passage, Ylt uses the surge of Cohesion (1) granted to him by Taln’s Honorblade, closing the stone behind him and Kaiana, blocking the exit to the west.

### Aftermath

Ylt’s zealots fight to the death, consumed by their devotion to Ylt’s warped teachings. If the zealots are victorious, Teryn dies in battle and the PCs are disarmed and imprisoned; in this case, it’s up to you how the story unfolds from here. If the zealots are defeated, Teryn and her loyalist bandits treat the PCs with respect and appreciation. Teryn’s people provide medical care and offer a safe place to sleep.

Ylt and Kaiana are nowhere to be found. Their departure leaves a patch of oddly smooth stone covering the once-open hallway. If the PCs weren’t close enough to witness it for some reason, one of Teryn’s loyalists can describe seeing Ylt and Kaiana retreat through the stone. ## Lower Rathalas

When the party is ready to seek the Gray Remnant, Axies the Collector haphazardly attempts to lead the party through ancient passages below the ruins of present-day Rathalas. He may be doing so to pay a debt for his freedom, or he may have asked for assistance navigating the ancient tunnels. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The tunnel leading down to Lower Rathalas is remarkably smooth, but Axies muses that it doesn’t look as if it was cut by a Shardblade. The passages are musty with seepage from the nearby Sea of Spears. Among the rotspren and echoing walls, there are unexpected details—hints where the walls were once worked or placed stone.

If the PCs follow Axies without question, it becomes clear he doesn’t fully know where he’s going. A PC can take over navigation and make a DC 14 Survival test. If the PCs examine the murals, a successful DC 13 Deduction, Lore, or Perception test reveals additional details:

- Each knight is accompanied by a companion figure that resembles a spren.

- The Radiants fight with a variety of weapons, not just Shardblades.

- This mural is more than two thousand years old—pre-Recreance. A PC with the Religion expertise knows that the Day of Recreance was the event where the Lost Radiants betrayed humanity and disbanded.

## Chamber of the Heralds

As the party continues further into the temple, they reach the Chamber of the Heralds. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You enter a plain circular chamber, about fifty feet across, that rises to a coffered, domed ceiling. Ten alcoves are evenly spaced along the stone walls, each holding a life-sized statue on a raised plinth.

The statues are in varying states of decay or disrepair, and only three of them retain enough detail to make out their features. A character with the Religion expertise or who succeeds on a DC 8 Deduction or Lore test recognizes these as representations of the following Heralds (pictured in [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction#History of Roshar|History of Roshar]] in this book's introduction

- **Taln, Herald of War.** A thickly muscled Makabaki man with a neatly trimmed beard and an impressive suit of armor.

- **Nale, Herald of Justice.** A bald Makabaki man with a crescent-shaped mark on his right cheek.

- **Pailiah, Herald of Knowledge.** A long-haired Alethi or Veden woman with robes similar to the costumes of Ylt and his acolytes.

Anyone who examines the other seven statues can make a DC 15 Perception test. On a success, they notice the spren Cipher.

### Cipher

Behind one of the decaying statues, Cipher (they / them) is watching the party. They’re a Cryptic—also known as a liespren—with a pattern resembling infinite shifting architecture with columns, staircases, and interlocking arches.

Cipher studies the lies people tell themselves, and they’re especially attracted to the boastful or overly confident. They were fascinated with Ylt and observed as he first took over the bandits, then through outright deceit and self-delusion, transformed the bandits into fanatics.

Any PC who reveals a painful truth to the Gray Remnant (see [[Journal/03 - 03 The City That Smolders#The Gray Remnant|The Gray Remnant]]) catches Cipher’s attention, especially if the PC admits to lying about aspects of themself. When the party departs, the spren follows them out of Rathalas.

## The Gray Remnant

Once the characters have studied the statues, or if one of them calls out in search of the Gray Remnant, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A droplet of wet clay falls to the ground beside you, then another. Above you, the recessed panels of the ceiling have filled with crem-like sludge. The clay moves and roils as if alive, and then in unsettling unison, fingers press out from under the surface, moving toward you and revealing dozens of emaciated hands. Soon a multitude of arms reach out, no longer moving in unison but gesturing with different intents—supplication, violence, desperation, and more.\
>      A low, raspy voice seeps from the writhing clay. “You seek truth—so do I, but it must carry pain.”

### A Painful Truth

Spren are expressions of thought and perception, and because the Gray Remnant has been forgotten, he’s no longer as powerful or coherent as he was in the past.

Spiritually connecting with a character strengthens his mind. Thus, in exchange for his knowledge, the Gray Remnant asks for one PC to share a painful truth with him. For example, an event or realization tied to the PC’s obstacle can be an excellent painful truth. Sharing this truth should feel like a sacrifice, like abandoning a comforting lie or facing something the PC has been avoiding.

### Another Truth

Once a painful truth is shared, use the following information to guide the Gray Remnant’s conversation, with Axies occasionally interjecting questions of his own:

- **What do you know about Ylt?** “He is a Truthwatcher in service to the Herald Pailiah, but I also sense that an ancient enemy is influencing his mind. Ylt believes that giving the Honorblade to the Shin Stone Shamans will hasten the destruction of the world. He is gifted with future sight, but I fear his prophecies are clouded and twisted by his ambition.”

- **What did Ylt ask you about?** “He sought old knowledge, stories of the Heralds and how they came to be. He wished to join them, but I warned there must only be ten.”

- **Are Pailiah and the other Heralds trustworthy?** “The Heralds were broken long ago. I believe they will be even more unreliable stewards now.”

- **Axies asks,** **“What are the shadows of Rall Elorim?”** “They are lost souls merged with shadowspren. They are creatures neither living nor dead.” (Rall Elorim, a city on the northwest edge of Roshar, is a key location in [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|chapter 6]] and [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]].)

- **Axies asks,** **“How can I reach these shadowspren?”** “Their essences were trapped within gemstones and hidden below the City of Shadows.”

- **Do you know where Ylt is going?** This is the most important information the Gray Remnant can share, so if the PCs don’t ask, Axies does. The Gray Remnant explains, “He and his acolytes seek a boon from my mother, the Nightwatcher. They are traveling to her Valley.” A PC with the Folklore or Religion expertise knows that the Nightwatcher grants a boon and a curse, which the Alethi call the Old Magic. A PC with at least one rank in Lore is aware that the Nightwatcher’s Valley can be reached through the nation of Greater Hexi.

The Gray Remnant can also answer questions about the nature of surges, the Nahel bond, and the First Ideal.

> [!example] Roleplaying the Gray Remnant
> **Characteristics:** Muddled, watchful, weary.
>
> **Goal:** Learn painful truths to maintain his connection to the Physical Realm.
>
> **Appearance:** The Gray Remnant (he/him) appears as grasping hands and converses in the raspy voice of a sickly old man.
>
> This ancient spren has slumbered in the stones below Rathalas for thousands of years. Vorin priests hid the spren’s existence to prevent people from seeking his knowledge—an act the priests considered to be uncomfortably close to predicting the future, which is heretical in Vorinism.
>
> The Gray Remnant can withdraw into the stones if he feels threatened.

Once the party has all necessary information, the Gray Remnant issues a final warning, then withdraws. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The spren’s strange form ripples, and once more his voice rasps, “Now I offer you a painful truth. The way ahead will not be easy. The Everstorm comes. Heralds will fall. You and your oaths will be tested.” The Gray Remnant’s grasping hands suddenly still. “I am weary and must rest. Thank you for your gift.”\
>      The spren sinks into the ceiling, receding into seams in the ancient stone.

## Journeying Onward

With the Gray Remnant’s information in hand, the party may leave Rathalas and pursue Ylt and his acolytes to Greater Hexi. Ylt and Kaiana have taken one of the bandits’ ships, but Teryn’s loyalists offer to lend the PCs a boat to chase down Ylt.

After their adventures in the burned city, the characters advance to 4th level.
