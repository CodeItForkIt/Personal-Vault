# 01: Honor Beyond the Storm

Taln’s Honorblade is a sacred, legendary sword wielded by the Herald of War, Talenel’Elin.

Long ago, Taln and nine other Heralds led the fight to protect Roshar during cataclysmic battles called the Desolations. After struggling against the Voidbringers for millennia, the other Heralds abandoned him. Trapped on a hellish planet called Braize, Taln endured four thousand years of torture—for as long as he remained unbroken, the Desolations couldn’t return.

These days, the immortal Heralds are seen as religious myths. In reality, they’re still on Roshar but live in seclusion or incognito—and the Herald of War has just returned.

## Chapter Background

Taln’s return portends a new Desolation, the first in millennia. Most people ignore these warnings, but a few understand the true danger that threatens Roshar. They now seek Taln’s legendary Blade.

The Stone Shamans of Shinovar are looking for the Blade. They’ve already gathered the other Honorblades to protect against the coming Desolation. Now, upon hearing of Taln’s return, they’ve sent a retinue of shamans to retrieve his Blade.

Ylt, a Truthwatcher from the distant nation of Iri, also seeks the Blade. He serves the Herald Pailiah, who he refers to by the Iriali name Pala. While Ylt begins the adventure loyal to her, he has bonded a so-called “Enlightened” spren who grants him prophetic visions. Ylt’s growing delusions of grandeur have twisted these visions, which have shown him that the bearer of Taln’s Blade will secure Roshar’s future. Though he believes himself altruistic, Ylt seeks Taln’s Honorblade for his own glory.

A mundane sect of acolytes, loyal to the Herald Pailiah, call themselves the Eyes of Pala. These acolytes accompany Ylt on his mission. He has also infiltrated a bandit group operating in the nearby Unclaimed Hills. He now leads his newly indoctrinated bandits and the Eyes of Pala, determined to stop the shamans and steal the Blade for himself.

Ylt has succeeded in assassinating most of the Shin monks, but one named Taszo-son-Clutio survived. Taszo has now recruited the player characters and travels toward the Shattered Plains, which is where our adventure begins.

## Running This Chapter

Before running this chapter, the players should create character connections with Taszo (see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Character Connections]] in this book’s introduction), who is their friend or employer at the start of this adventure.

During this chapter, Ylt sends his mentee Kaiana and a group of bandit zealots to ambush the Alethi caravan carrying Taln’s Honorblade. The Eyes of Pala plan to distract the caravan’s guards while Kaiana steals the Blade.

Taszo’s role in this adventure is to help you communicate key information and set off the events that pull the party into the adventure. Familiarize yourself with [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Taszo’s Story|Taszo’s Story]] and ensure the following occur:

- The PCs witness [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Ellar’s Outburst|Ellar’s Outburst]].

- Taszo initiates a discussion about the Honorblade in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Suspicions Revealed|Suspicions Revealed]].

- The PCs have the chance to realize the bandits are a diversion for the theft of the Honorblade. If the PCs don’t figure this out, Taszo does so in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Taszo’s Realization|Taszo’s Realization]].

- Taszo asks the party to take over his quest in [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#A Promise|A Promise]].

The chapter ends with the party inheriting Taszo’s peakspren Po’ahu. Po’ahu observes the PCs throughout the chapter and, depending on each character’s actions, might choose to begin bonding a PC who wishes to be a Stoneward. For more details, see the [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Taszo’s Stone]] section at the end of this chapter, as well as Po’ahu’s description in the [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] section of this book’s introduction.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 1st level and advance to 2nd level after Taszo’s death.

The following decisions offer opportunities to introduce spren or strengthen spren bonds:

- If a PC intervenes during Ellar’s outburst.

- If a PC aids and protects Tet Rebin and Hana.

- If a PC follows Taszo into the storm.

Characters with goals related to learning more about the Heralds, Honorblades, or Radiants have opportunities to do so if they observe Taln, identify the Truthwatcher glyph on Veth or a bandit, or discover evidence of Kaiana, Taszo, or Ylt’s Radiant powers.

## Shelter From the Storm

To start the adventure, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You’re traveling through the Unclaimed Hills, a sparsely inhabited region of uneven terrain, shrubs, and brown stone. Thick gray clouds hang heavy with the threat of a highstorm, and bright-blue windspren race through the sky.\
>      Your good-natured companion Taszo fidgets with the cast on his broken arm and looks apprehensively upward. He is a curly-haired Shin monk on a mission to the warcamps on the Shattered Plains. Taszo has traveled far, seeking an artifact sacred to his people.\
>      As you crest the next hill, Taszo exhales in relief. Below you lies the sheltered cliffside of the Covenant Stone Crossroads. The cliff wall stands five hundred feet high and twice as long. It provides a natural barrier against highstorms, making it a popular waypoint for caravans. A few such caravans sit there now, including a large military caravan flying blue House Kholin banners.\
>      “Spirits of the stones protect us.” Taszo’s eyes pause on the banners. “The highstorm could hit any moment. Let’s make some friends, and quick!”

A few caravans have gathered at the Covenant Stone Crossroads. Some have already taken refuge, but two are still preparing for the highstorm. The party should decide which they approach first (although the next scene, [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|A Legend Returned]], occurs regardless of this choice):

- A modest caravan shared by a weapon merchant and a cloth merchant.

- A larger House Kholin caravan consisting of ten wagons and a company of one hundred Alethi soldiers. Unbeknownst to the PCs, this caravan carries the Herald Taln and his Honorblade.

Characters can talk to Taszo or otherwise establish what they know about him. Use the information in the [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Taszo-Son-Clutio|Taszo-Son-Clutio]] section to guide this conversation. If asked for his opinion on what to do, Taszo encourages the characters to befriend the House Kholin caravan.

If the party dallies, the Alethi sergeant Ellar (see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#House Kholin Caravan|House Kholin Caravan]]) approaches them and puts them to work.

If you want to use a map for any scenes unfolding at the crossroads, see [[Scenes Index|map 1.2]] later in this chapter.

## Taszo-Son-Clutio

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Taszo-son-Clutio|Taszo]]** (he/him) is an earnest Shin Stone Shaman who has recruited and traveled with the PCs for the last week.

### Taszo’s Story

The Shin Shamanate recently learned an important Shin religious artifact was traveling toward the warcamps. Taszo was part of a retinue of four Shin shamans sent to retrieve the artifact.

A couple weeks ago, en route to the Shattered Plains, Taszo’s companions were attacked by assassins whose faces were painted with green glyphs. The assassins murdered his companions, but Taszo escaped with a broken arm. Taszo believes these assassins seek the same artifact.

Taszo survived in part because he has a secret: He has attracted a peakspren named Po’ahu (she/her). Taszo hasn’t yet sworn the First Ideal of the Stonewards nor gained access to Stormlight healing, but Po’ahu is subtly guiding him. Though the Shin tend to be insular, she convinced him to seek the party’s help.

Once at the warcamps, Taszo plans to find the shamans’ Alethi contact, a savvy spy named Liss who was hired to retrieve the Blade.

![[Tazso_portrait.webp]]

> [!example] Roleplaying Taszo
> **Characteristics:** Duty-bound, empathetic, inquisitive.
>
> **Goal:** Protect Roshar by delivering Taln’s Honorblade to Shinovar.
>
> **Appearance:** Taszo-son-Clutio (“TAHzo-suhn KLOO-tee-oh”; he/him) is a Shin human in his early thirties. He is short—even by Shin standards—with pale pink skin, round dark-blue eyes, and a crop of curly dusty-blond hair. He currently has a broken arm.
>
> Taszo is compassionate, sometimes to his own detriment. He wants to be useful to the PCs—and to anyone else he can be—though he’s hindered by his broken arm. Taszo is also curious, especially about people and their lives.

### What Taszo Shares

Taszo has shared his story with the characters either as they approach the crossroads or earlier on the road. However, he avoids revealing two facts:

- **Mysterious Spren.** Taszo keeps Po’ahu’s existence secret, though characters with at least 1 rank in Insight have noticed he carries a stone that he treats with reverence.

- **Taln’s Honorblade.** Taszo has shared only that he seeks an important Shin religious artifact, but not what the artifact is. He doesn’t reveal that he seeks Taln’s Honorblade, nor that the Stone Shamans train their most skilled adherents to wield the Honorblades.

Taszo isn’t forthcoming with this information, but if the PCs are friendly and pry, Taszo shares the details from the later [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Suspicions Revealed|Suspicions Revealed]] section. Taszo isn’t fully aware of what Po’ahu is, but if pressed, he sheepishly shares that the spirits of the stones protect him.

## Covenant Stone Crossroads

The crossroads are named after the Covenant Stone, a thousand-foot-long gray slab that offers protection from the destructive highstorms that regularly move westward and ravage Roshar. Caravans camp at this resting spot for protection against the storms—and protection from the less scrupulous denizens of the Unclaimed Hills.

The cliff wall, a well-known landmark where promises are made, is covered in burned and painted glyphs. A character with the Alethi expertise, or who succeeds on a DC 10 Lore test, recognizes this landmark and knows that since the War of Reckoning started, a number of business deals, minor truces, and oaths of fealty were conducted at this waypoint.

## Merchant Caravan

If the PCs approach the merchant caravan, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A circle of four merchant wagons is nearly secured for the approaching highstorm. An Alethi man with dark, wavy hair inspects the ropes tying bundles of spears, staffs, and other polearms. He nods at you and looks up at the sky. “You’re getting in right under the Stormfather’s nose.”\
>      Nearby, a fashionable Thaylen woman sits atop a wagon marked with a bright-blue glyph resembling a spool of thread. Angstspren skitter around her as she huddles against the foreboding winds, her eyes searching westward. “Did you see any wagons on the road coming in? My friend and his daughter were supposed to be here by now.”

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Svalka|Svalka]]** (commoner, she/her) introduces herself. She is a Thaylen cloth merchant with drooping white eyebrows and a caring demeanor. The wagon she asks after is her friend Tet Rebin’s wagon. Rebin was meant to arrive at the crossroads hours ago, but Svalka hasn’t seen him or his teenage daughter Hana.

The Alethi merchant is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Nen|Nen]]** (commoner, he/him). He deals in polearms for the warcamps. Habitually optimistic, he assures Svalka that Rebin will arrive. Nen jumps in to play host; while he prepares additional seats in his stormwagon for them, he peppers the party with questions, including asking what brings them to the Shattered Plains. If the PCs answer truthfully or succeed on a DC 13 Deception test, Nen shares gossip about the Alethi caravan (see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#House Kholin Caravan|House Kholin Caravan]]), which you can choose or roll for on the Caravan Gossip table.

## House Kholin Caravan

The party can integrate themselves with the Kholin caravan and learn some gossip by being useful to Bordin’s soldiers. If the PCs approach the Kholin caravan, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This caravan encampment buzzes with activity, orchestrated by an Alethi sergeant with close-shaved hair who is shouting orders. Soldiers scramble to tie down the chulls, wagons, and unsecured supplies in preparation for the storm. Two of the caravan wagons stand out from the rest. One looks like a coach, but bars cover its windows. The other is wrapped in heavy iron chains and surrounded by four soldiers who stand alert, their expressions stoic.\
>      The Alethi sergeant turns in your direction. “Newcomers! If you can hold the right end of a spear, I have a job for you. Otherwise, quit your gawking and join the civilians.”

The sergeant shouting orders is named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Ellar|Ellar]]** (guard, he/him). He views the party with disdain unless they prove themselves useful.

> [!example] Roleplaying Ellar
> **Characteristics:** Belligerent, intolerant, self-important.
>
> **Goal:** Be seen and treated as a respected authority figure.
>
> **Appearance:** Ellar (“EH-lar”; he/him) is a human Alethi sergeant with close-shaved hair.
>
> Ellar enjoys his position of relative power in the caravan. He relishes shouting orders and makes quick, surfacelevel judgments about those he meets.

### Put to Work by Ellar

Ellar entertains conversation only if the party agrees to help prepare for the highstorm, whether by standing lookout, tying down wagons, or building the Alethi stormshelter. If a PC offers to help, have the player describe how they do so and make a DC 10 test with a relevant skill.

On a success, Ellar grudgingly expresses thanks. He now allows the party to freely travel anywhere besides the chained wagon, and the caravan soldiers eagerly share a piece of gossip with them from the Caravan Gossip table.

On a failure, the PC fumbles and an annoyed Ellar sends them to the nearby merchant caravan (see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Merchant Caravan|Merchant Caravan]]).

Alternatively, the commotion surrounding highstorm preparations allows any character who succeeds on a DC 10 Stealth test to remain in the Kholin caravan unobserved. Plainly dressed singer characters face less scrutiny than humans and gain an advantage on this test.

If the PCs linger here—either because Ellar allows them to or because they remain unnoticed—they can overhear interesting gossip with a successful DC 14 Perception test. You can choose or roll for gossip from the Caravan Gossip table.

Only Bordin knows that this chain-wrapped wagon transports a Shardblade. He has only seen the locked case it travels in, not the weapon itself.

## A Legend Returned

After the PCs have explored the caravans, Taszo seeks out the Alethi caravan and accidentally draws Ellar’s ire. The party can attempt to intervene, gaining Bordin’s respect. Even if they don’t, they gain valuable information about the caravan’s mysterious passenger.

Any PCs in the Kholin caravan observe the entire interaction up close, while other PCs witness the interaction from afar once it becomes heated.

## Ellar’s Outburst

The scene starts when Taszo approaches Taln’s coach and listens to his mantra (see the [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Taln and the Coach|Roleplaying Taln]] sidebar). Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Taszo stares in wonder at the coach with barred windows, awespren bursting above his head in rings of blue smoke. Eyeing the prisoner inside, Taszo tentatively holds his hand up to the bars.\
>      A loud voice cuts over the wind, “Move back, stranger!” Ellar steps forward. “That prisoner is a delusional fool and blasphemer. He spends all day muttering about being some great Herald.”\
>      The sergeant shakes his head in contempt, then shouts into the wagon. “Can you save us from the storm, great Herald? Or just sit there talking to yourself?” He spits between the bars at the man inside.

If the party intervenes to stop Ellar’s harassment, a PC must make a DC 13 Intimidation or Leadership test.

On a success, if the PC appealed to his ego and status, he begrudgingly backs down. Otherwise, he spends 2 focus to resist their influence, requiring another successful DC 13 Intimidation test to convince him to back down. If the characters succeed on both tests (or just one if he didn’t resist them), he backs down but still insists Taln is a fraud.

On a failure, Ellar scorns whoever confronted him, asking if they’re delusional enough to believe that this man is a Herald who deserves coddling. If the PCs try to fight him, **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Ellar|Ellar]]** uses the guard stat block but only makes unarmed attacks; he has a +5 bonus to attack and deals 7 (1d4 + 5) impact damage on a hit.

No matter what the PCs choose to do, Bordin quickly intervenes. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> One of the soldiers guarding the chained wagon frowns disapprovingly and leaves his post to intercept Ellar. Other soldiers move out of the way, their backs straightening in respect. The soldier’s low voice rumbles as his firm hand settles on Ellar’s shoulder, “You’re done. Stand down and cool off.”\

If the PCs resolve the scene nonviolently, add the following from Bordin:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “Thank you for that. It’s been a long few days, but that’s no excuse for his behavior.”

Bordin asks the party for help chaining down Taln’s stormwagon in preparation for the coming storm. Taszo offers to help if the PCs do not. Throughout this scene, Taln repeats his mantra.

## Suspicions Revealed

After Ellar’s outburst, Taszo gathers the party in private to discuss suspicions. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Taszo motions you over to a secluded spot by the cliff. The stone here is covered with burned glyphs of varying sizes, ages, and handwriting, stretching over ten feet up the steep cliff wall. The wind picks up and he leans in close. “What do you make of the Alethi and who they are transporting?”

Taszo allows the PCs a few moments to theorize, listening intently. After they’ve had some time to discuss, depending on what Taszo has already shared, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Taszo quickly glances around to make sure no one else is listening, then speaks again. “The artifact I seek is a weapon of the Heralds: an Honorblade. I know it seems impossible, but that man truly is a Herald, the one known in the East as Talenelat, the Herald of War.”\
>      Taszo motions to the dark wagon.\
>      “With the mighty Herald here, though, I believe his Blade is not within the warcamps, but in this very caravan…”

Taszo answers questions and shares openly what he knows:

- **Why is the Honorblade important?** “The Stone Shamans live to prepare for the return of the Voidbringers, the return of a new Desolation that will sweep across the world and destroy humankind. The only way this disaster can be averted is if the Shamanate can secure the Heralds’ Honorblades and use their power to turn the tide.”

- **What claim do the Shin have to Taln’s Honorblade?** “The Heralds are lost or… fallen, unable to fight. My people are the sacred keepers of the Honorblades in their stead. We have the other Heralds’ Blades, and for millennia, a chosen few have trained to use the Blades to protect Roshar.”

- **Why are you telling us this?** “You all helped me in my time of need. What can I do but trust? This I know, the quest is too big for me alone. Stonewalker assassins killed my comrades and threaten to take the Blade.”

- **What do the assassins want with the Honorblades?** “I don’t know, but I fear it can’t be good. We’ve kept the Honorblades secret for years, for fear of what thoughtless leaders would do if they knew about them.”

- **What should we do?** “We find ourselves surrounded by a battalion of hot-headed soldiers and I don’t want strife or bloodshed. Let’s travel with the caravan until the warcamps and learn what we can. The Stone Shamans have a contact in the warcamps who’ll know what to do next.”

Whatever the PCs suggest or ask, Taszo implores them to act with utmost secrecy.

## Wet and Waylaid

After speaking with Taszo, give the party a few moments to discuss what they plan to do. Once they reach a decision (or an impasse), read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A commotion draws the attention of both caravans. In the center, a teenage girl wildly gesticulates toward the road. Purple fearspren gather at her feet, and her voice is frantic.\
>      “The wagon, it’s stuck and pa won’t leave it, we need help. Please, it’s everything we have!”\
>      The Alethi soldiers seem concerned, but after a glance up at the gathering storm, they shift in place, trying to ignore the girl.\
>      Svalka, the fashionable Thaylen merchant, cuts in, “We cart your weapons and wine—surely you can spare a few soldiers!”

If the PCs approach, whomever they’re most friendly with fills them in. The girl is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Hana|Hana]]** (commoner, she/ her), the daughter of Tet Rebin, the missing wine merchant. Rebin’s wagon is stuck in a deep puddle of crem mud a few hundred feet down the road. As the party speaks to her, the low rumble of thunder sounds in the distance.

Both the merchants and Bordin ask the party to either free the wagon or convince Rebin to leave it. Bordin explains he’s short-staffed and offers the party 50 marks and his personal gratitude if they bring Rebin—with or without the wagon—to shelter before the storm hits. If the PCs seem hesitant, Svalka offers to contribute 50 marks more. Taszo is eager to help, but admits he’s unlikely to be much use with a broken arm.

If the PCs agree, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Stuck in the Crem|Stuck in the Crem]]. If they refuse, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Staying With the Caravan]].

## Stuck in the Crem

![[pg025_Map 1-1 Stuck in the Crem_RYAN SMITH.webp]]

**Map 1.1: Stuck in the Crem** **-** **RYAN SMITH**

If the characters go to help Rebin, Hana leads them quickly to her father. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A little down the western path, a wagon and chull are stuck in the muck. The crab-like chull waves its legs uselessly, having fallen at an awkward angle into a large puddle of wet crem. As the wind picks up, a balding redhaired Veden man strains with all his might while tugging on the rope attached to the beast.

The man is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Tet Rebin|Tet Rebin]]** (expert, he/him), and he futilely tries to move the weight of his chull-drawn wagon (shown on [[Scenes Index|map 1.1]]). He only notices the party if they speak to him. A PC who examines the puddle of crem and succeeds on a DC 12 Deduction or Survival test realizes it was intentionally placed—created by bandits for an ambush.

### Persuading Rebin

To convince Rebin to leave his wagon, a PC must succeed on a test using a relevant skill against either his Cognitive defense of 13 or his Spiritual defense of 13. A PC who makes an argument about Hana’s safety gains an advantage on the test. However, Rebin has 4 focus, and he spends 2 focus to resist any argument to leave the wagon, explaining that his family’s entire livelihood is in the wagon. If Rebin is convinced or if the PCs fail to persuade him, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Bandit Ambush]].

### Pulling the Wagon Free

Characters can also try to help Rebin by extracting his wagon from the crem. This is an endeavor in which the PCs try to succeed on multiple skill tests (see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]); each success helps to haul the wagon a bit more out of the muck. Potential approaches include:

- **Brute Force.** Pulling the wagon with the rope is hard, but possible (DC 18 Athletics). A PC can also jump into the wagon and remove the wine barrels to lighten it (DC 13 Athletics).

- **Chull-Flipping.** Flipping the chull allows it to free itself and help pull the wagon. A PC can do this manually (DC 14 Athletics), or they can calm the chull and get it to flip itself (DC 12 Survival). This is a good place to raise the stakes.

- **Engineering.** With more rope, which Rebin can provide, a quick-thinking PC can engineer a solution (DC 12 Crafting) and coordinate everyone to pull the wagon free together (DC 12 Leadership).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Wagon Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Wagon Opportunities and Complications]]

#### **Resolving the Endeavor**

The endeavor succeeds if the party accrues 3 successes before 3 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

- **Success.** The party manages to pull the wagon and chull free.

- **Failure.** The wagon is hopelessly mired, a fact that even Rebin finally admits. The storm is drawing near and he recognizes it’s time to flee for shelter.

> [!info] Cart Tile Movement
> Using the Tile Selection tool a GM can move the cart and chull out of the crem and into a reasonable location. Simply right click on the cart with the Tile Selection tool and click the lock icon to unlock its movement. Then drag it to the desired location like a token. Don't forget to re-lock it and switch back to token selection when you are done.

## Staying With the Caravan

If the PCs refuse to help Rebin free his wagon, Bordin reluctantly sends three spear infantry from his company of soldiers. The remaining soldiers continue to guard the wagons and prepare for the storm.

PCs who stay with the caravan can try to steal the Honorblade. Taszo doesn’t think this is wise, but he helps if the characters are resolute. In this case, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Stealing the Honorblade|Stealing the Honorblade]].

PCs can also stay behind to seek shelter, but before they can find an empty stormwagon, bandits attack. Proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Bandit Ambush]].

## Stealing the Honorblade

If the party wants to sneak into the locked wagon, run their attempted heist as an endeavor (see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). Potential approaches include:

- **Speaking to the Guards.** Four **spear infantry** guard the locked wagon. A PC can try to distract them (DC 15 Deception or Persuasion) while other PCs look for a way inside. This test gains a disadvantage unless the character who’s distracting the guards previously helped the Kholin caravan.

- **Sneaking Past.** PCs can attempt to sneak to the locked wagon (DC 16 Stealth).

- **Getting Inside.** The wagon’s lock is well made but can be picked (DC 15 Thievery). A PC searching the wagon for other entrances can discover the loose panel (DC 12 Perception; see [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Discovering Kaiana|Discovering Kaiana]]).

### Special Rule: High-Stakes Heist

The wagon guards are on alert; raise the stakes on any test made in their presence. If a Complication is gained, the endeavor immediately fails. If an Opportunity is gained, one of the guards is called away, granting an advantage on the party’s next Stealth or Thievery test.

### Resolving the Endeavor

The endeavor succeeds if the party achieves 4 successes before 2 failures, or if they otherwise complete the objective.

**Success.** The characters open the wagon and see Kaiana, Ylt’s disciple, just after she swaps the blades and begins to make her retreat. Proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Discovering Kaiana|Discovering Kaiana]].

**Failure.** A soldier spots the characters and goes to question them. Moments later, the caravans are ambushed by bandits; proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Discovering Kaiana|Bandit Ambush]].

### Discovering Kaiana

If the party gains access to the wagon, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A black-haired woman crouches in the wagon over an open chest. She’s wearing a brown cloak over a green dress and holding a Shardblade shaped like a massive spike. She looks at you, eyes wide, and you notice a green glyph painted on her round face. Suddenly, there’s a blinding flash of light.

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]]** (she/her) has finished what she came here to do—secretly swapping the Honorblade for another Shardblade. She uses her Disorienting Flash ability and flees through a plank she cut, replacing it behind her. After the flash, Kaiana is gone. In the center of the wagon lies a long metal chest with a complex lock; both the wagon and chest appear untouched.

Characters might try to investigate or interact with the following elements of this scene:

- **The Cut Plank.** A successful DC 12 Perception test made to search the wagon reveals a plank on the floor, cut at an angle so its weight holds it in place. Prying it loose creates an opening large enough for a Medium creature. A PC who examines the plank and succeeds on a DC 12 Deduction test realizes it was cut by a Shardblade.

- **The Lock.** The chest is sealed with a fabrial lock, powered by a small amethyst gem. PCs with the Fabrial Crafting expertise or who succeed on a DC 16 Lore test understand that the device functions as a secure combination lock. Picking the lock requires a successful DC 25 Thievery test. The chest contains a Shardblade that Kaiana left in place of the stolen Honorblade.

- **Kaiana’s Glyph.** If a PC tries to identify the green glyph on Kaiana’s face, use the information in the upcoming [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Coordinated Retreat|Coordinated Retreat]] section. However, the PC gains a disadvantage on this test because they only saw Kaiana’s glyph for a moment.

After the PCs have a brief moment to investigate inside the wagon, the bandits attack. Proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Bandit Ambush]].

## Bandit Ambush

Bandits attack the caravans (shown on [[Scenes Index|map 1.2]]) and Rebin’s wagon on the road (shown on [[Scenes Index|map 1.1]]). Their goal is to distract the company of soldiers, allowing Kaiana to complete the Honorblade swap. Twenty bandits participate in the attack, but most focus on attacking the Kholin soldiers, using hit-and-run tactics to draw them out.

Regardless of the party’s location, four **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** get close enough to attack the PCs directly. Depending on whether the characters are at the caravan or near the wagon, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Ambush at the Caravan|Ambush at the Caravan]] or [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Ambush on the Road|Ambush on the Road]].

Any PCs who just failed an endeavor or who are in conversation when the attack starts gain the Surprised condition.

If **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Taszo-son-Clutio|Taszo]]** is with the party, he helps them fight. The bandits focus on the PCs, and Taszo retreats and hides if hit.

## Ambush on the Road

If the party tried to free Rebin’s wagon (regardless of whether that endeavor ended in success or failure), the bandits attack immediately after that scene, blocking the party’s path back to the Covenant Stone. After three rounds of combat, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Coordinated Retreat|Coordinated Retreat]].

During the attack, [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Tet Rebin|Rebin]] (expert) and [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 1/Hana|Hana]] (commoner) cower, and the bandits ignore them, focusing on the party instead.

Characters might try to investigate or interact with the following elements of this scene:

- **Crem Puddle.** The crem puddle is difficult terrain. In addition, every move made through the crem requires a successful DC 10 Agility test. On a failure, a character becomes Restrained. A trapped character or another character who can reach them can Use a Skill (1) to make a DC 10 Agility or Athletics test, freeing the trapped character on a success.

- **Wagon and Chull.** The wagon and chull can both provide cover. If freed from the muck, the chull uses its Defense Curl on each of its turns.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Road Ambush Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

## Ambush at the Caravan

If the PCs are trying to get into the locked Honorblade wagon when the bandits attack, add two additional **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandits]]** to those attacking the party.

Some Alethi soldiers have already sought shelter when the bandits attack, but an entire platoon remains ready to fight. You don’t need to make tests or track health for bandits and soldiers who are fighting each other near the characters; they simply serve as a backdrop while you focus the narrative on the PCs fighting the bandits closest to them. After three rounds of combat, proceed to Coordinated Retreat.

Characters might try to investigate or interact with the following elements of this scene:

- **Wagons.** The various wagons around camp can provide cover (see [[Scenes Index|map 1.2]]).

- **Weapon Bundles.** Bundles of weapons lie beside Nen’s wagon in the merchant caravan, and several weapons lie near the Kholin soldiers’ wagons. PCs can use the Interact action to grab a ***javelin***, ***shortspear***, or ***longspear***.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Caravan Ambush Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

## Coordinated Retreat

The bandits retreat after three rounds or when only one of the bandits fighting the PCs remains alive, whichever occurs first. Once this happens, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A sharp whistle cuts through the battle. On cue, the bandits' stance shifts to defensive. They retreat, running toward the roiling mass of a stormwall just visible on the horizon.

This whistle is Kaiana’s signal to the bandits that her theft is complete. Any remaining bandits flee, heading straight toward the encroaching highstorm. The nearby soldiers scoff at the bandits, wondering aloud what kind of fools attack with a highstorm so near. After the party has a moment to examine the bandits’ bodies if they wish, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Into the Storm]].

### Examining the Bodies

The defeated bandits carry their weapons and 1d6 marks each, and one bandit carries a ***lockpick***.

As a follower of Ylt, one bandit has a crudely tattooed Truthwatcher glyph on their wrist; any PC who saw Kaiana recognizes this glyph matches the one painted on her face. Characters with the Religion expertise or who succeed on a DC 14 Lore test identify the glyph as a variant of “Palah,” one of the ten fundamental glyphs of Vorin theology. Palah is associated with emeralds, plant life, knowledge, and generosity.

Taszo finds this glyph if the characters don’t, which leads to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Taszo’s Realization|Taszo’s Realization]].

## Into the Storm

After the bandit attack, the party has a few minutes before the full force of the highstorm hits.

If the characters helped Rebin, they arrive back at the Covenant Stone Crossroads to find the aftermath of a bandit attack that occurred there. A few soldiers are limping and there is some superficial damage to the merchant caravans, but everyone is otherwise safe. The Alethi head to a makeshift stormshelter, and the merchants head to Nen’s stormwagon. Nen yells for the characters to join.

## Taszo’s Realization

At some point during or directly after the attack, Taszo realizes the bandit attack is a diversion and the bandits are aligned with the same assassins who are after the Honorblade.

When Taszo sees the glyph or hears mention of a woman with face paint, he pales and explains that this symbol is the same as the one worn by the assassins who killed his friends. With this revelation, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The shaman looks behind at the fast-approaching stormwall, where the bandits ran. Understanding dawns on his face. “They have the Honorblade. I don’t know how they did it, but they have the Blade!” He sprints off, jumping over rocks and shrubs toward the looming stormwall, his broken arm swinging awkwardly.

Ask whether any of the PCs follow Taszo. The stormwall is close, so the party must choose now whether to seek the safety of a nearby stormwagon. If they follow, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Following Taszo|Following Taszo]]. Otherwise, proceed to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Staying Safe|Staying Safe]].

## Following Taszo

If any of the PCs follow Taszo, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stormwall stretches thousands of miles in both directions, swallowing the hills as it rushes toward you. It hits the Covenant Stone Cliffs at an angle, a hundred or so feet to the east. Rain rattles the ground, creating a deafening drumbeat. A dozen feet ahead of you, Taszo looks impossibly small against the enormity of the storm.

Ask the players why they followed Taszo and what their characters think about as the stormwall rushes toward them. After they share their thoughts, give each PC the Determined condition; this condition allows them to add an Opportunity to one test they fail before the end of the upcoming combat. Then read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> One second, Taszo is in front of you, pitching against the wind and using the cliff wall to keep steady. The next moment, he is gone, engulfed.

Give players a moment to describe if they do anything before the stormwall hits, then read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stormwall hits. Knives of icy cold water and choking debris clouds pummel you. The wind is strong enough to lift a person even with the protective cliff wall. Above the thunderous wind and rain, there’s a crash. A boulder lands just ten feet away from you.

Though the PCs are partially sheltered by the cliff as the stormwall breaks overhead, they must still navigate the storm’s wrath. The inside of the storm is chaos, obscuring sight and hearing. Have each PC make a DC 13 Athletics or Survival test to press forward in the direction where Taszo disappeared; characters with the Listener expertise gain an advantage due to their familiarity with navigating highstorms. On a failure, a character takes 3 (`1d6`) impact damage from the battering storm and falls Prone. On a success, they take half as much damage and don’t fall Prone.

Also have each PC make a Discipline test. Read the following to the character with the highest test result:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you struggle amid the fury of the storm, you notice a repetitive sensation of something bumping against your feet. Searching for the source, you find a rough stone— not much larger than a pebble—circling swiftly around you. As soon as you discover its presence, the stone begins to roll away in the direction Taszo was headed.\
>      As the unusual stone bounces along the ground, the sky lights up with lightning, illuminating three humanoid figures standing along the cliff wall. The stone is headed straight in their direction.

Hidden within this stone is [[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Poahu|Po’ahu]], Taszo’s peakspren. She is desperately trying to get the party’s help. If a PC picks up the stone (whether now or later), it’s strangely warm to the touch, and it tugs them as if pulled by some unseen force.

> [!tip] Singers and Storms
> During the highstorm, characters with singer ancestry can choose to change forms using their Change Form talent.

### Terse Exchange

Ahead stand Kaiana, Ylt (he/him), and a Ylt loyalist named Veth (he/him)—who has just stabbed Taszo in the gut. If the PCs follow Po’ahu or move toward the shapes on their own, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two voices ring out, the first sharp and upset.\
>      “You said you wouldn’t kill any more!” The second voice is lower and assured.\
>      “It was necessary, Kaiana. I foresaw he would lead others to us.”

Ask the players if their characters keep moving forward. If so, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud

Beside him, a Reshi woman looks upset. Her straight jet-black hair frames a round face, and her brown cloak matches the muted browns of the surrounding hills. A third figure, an Alethi man with a shaved head, stands armed with a sword. The same green glyph adorns all three of their faces.\
>      At their feet lies Taszo. He groans and clutches his side, from which a dagger protrudes, and blood mixes with the driving rain. Next to Taszo, a small stone gently bumps into his leg.\
>      “We need to keep moving.” The golden-skinned man’s eyes flash a brilliant green, and eerily, his head immediately snaps in your direction. “Veth, there’s more. Deal with them.

### Battlefield Effects

During the combat with Veth (see **map 1.3**), the following effects are active:

- **Highstorm.** The nearby cliffside blocks most of the highstorm’s wrath. Even so, at the start of each round of combat, roll on the Highstorm Effects table and have each combatant make the associated test (no action required).

- **Leaving Shelter.** Following Ylt and Kaiana into the storm is impossible without the Stormlight healing that protects them both. If a PC moves more than 50 feet away from the Covenant Stone, warn that they will be exposed to the full force of the highstorm. If a PC enters the storm anyway, they take 6 (`1d12`) impact damage at the start of each of their turns.

### After the Battle

By the time the characters reach Taszo, he’s already mortally wounded. As the storm continues to rage, they can have their final conversation with Taszo. Continue to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#A Promise|A Promise]].

## Staying Safe

Bordin understands if the party doesn’t run after Taszo into the danger of the highstorm. He directs them to Nen’s stormwagon, which has extra seats. A minute later, the storm hits. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The impossibly high stormwall engulfs the hills, reducing Taszo to a speck as he runs into it. Nen locks the door of the stormwagon, plunging you into darkness.

Ask the players what their characters think about as they wait for the storm. Then read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> It sounds like a roar, low at first and then impossibly loud. The stormwall hits. The floor beneath you shakes, the many secured objects jittering. There is only the darkness, the pounding rain, and the screaming of the wind. Nobody speaks.

The storm passes in half an hour. Everyone emerges cautiously, in a somber mood. The fortifications against the storm hold and the caravan’s persons and goods are unharmed.

If the PCs don’t do so on their own, Bordin tells them they should find Taszo. The characters find him huddled in a ball near the edge of the cliffside. An odd stone formation, like a small wave, protects his body. A successful DC 8 Survival test reveals the stone formation isn’t natural. Though the PCs don’t know it, this was a spontaneous manifestation of Taszo’s nascent Stoneward abilities. Continue to [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#A Promise|A Promise]].

## A Promise

Taszo is dying when the party finds him. He got a good look at Kaiana, Ylt, Veth, and the Honorblade—but at great cost, for Veth mortally wounded Taszo with a glyph-inscribed dagger to his gut. (See [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Aftermath|Aftermath]] for details on this dagger.)

If a PC succeeds on a DC 12 Medicine test or otherwise attempts to heal Taszo, he gratefully expresses that they’ve eased his pain, but he feels his life slipping away.

As he lies dying, he reaches out and grabs the hand of the closest PC if they allow him to. He speaks faintly but maintains a firm grip and an intense gaze. Read the following text:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “I saw it. The Honorblade. The assassins are led by a golden-skinned man. He… they were too powerful—the storm meant nothing to them.” He coughs, and blood flecks his lips before he continues. “The Blade was my mission. Please, you must finish it. You must unite Taln’s Honorblade with the others in Shinovar. Go to the warcamps. My Stone Shaman brothers have hired a powerful ally. Her name is Liss. Find her, she will know what to do.”\
>      Taszo pushes himself up, then falls back against the ground, his breath rattling in his ribs. He forces the next words out. “The Shamanate will reward you, this I swear. You are stonewalkers, but you may be all that stands between this world and the next Desolation.

If nobody previously picked up the mysterious stone, Po’ahu is still by Taszo’s side. Otherwise, he pats his clothes and the ground frantically, asking, “Have you seen my stone spirit?” Once he finds Po’ahu, he places her in the hand of the PC who seems most moved by his plea. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “Swear on this stone.” His clenched fist opens to reveal a rock. With his remaining strength, he pushes it toward you.

The PCs might immediately respond and swear to find the Honorblade; if they don’t, ask the players if any of their characters promise to help Taszo. If they agree to do so, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The words leave you, echoing on the cliff. A woman’s heartbroken voice whispers like gravel, seemingly spoken from the very cliff itself, “This oath… you will… not… break!"

As Po’ahu’s voice fades and the promise is complete, Taszo finishes:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> “You, my beautiful friends—how lucky I am to have found you.” He pulls at his collar, ensuring his face is clear, pointed to the sky. “I do not hide my face from the gods. I do not die in shame. My quest is given—and I die among friends.” He smiles as his eyes close, and his body slackens.

See the [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm|Taszo’s Stone]] section at the end of this chapter for details on how Po’ahu interacts with the party.

## Aftermath

Bordin helps the party hold a simple funeral for Taszo, ritually burning his body beneath the Covenant Stone. Taszo’s only noteworthy possession was his ***sidesword***.

### The Merchants

If the PCs helped Rebin and Hana, all the merchants look kindly on the characters and may later reappear to help them in the warcamps. Nen offers each of “the fine heroes” a weapon of their choice: either a ***javelin***, ***shortspear***, or ***longspear***.

If the PCs saved the wagon, Rebin gives the characters a bottle of ***alcohol (auburn wine)*** worth 150 marks and tells them to find him at the Ornery Chull in the Kholin warcamp.

If the PCs didn’t help Rebin, a hollow-eyed Hana explains her father was killed during the ambush, along with Bordin’s spear infantry.

### Veth’s Dagger

The highstorm erased most signs of the bandits, but their bodies remain. If the party fought Veth, his body is easy to find. If the party didn’t fight Veth, their only leads are the dagger they found in Taszo’s body, and the unusual glyph that appears both on that dagger and on the dead bandit’s tattoo:

- **Dagger.** The dagger, made from a spearhead, is etched with the same glyph that was on the bandit’s wrist and on the faces of Kaiana, Ylt, and Veth.

- **Glyph.** See the earlier [[Journal/14 - 01 Honor Beyond the Storm#Coordinated Retreat|Coordinated Retreat]] section for an explanation of the glyph. Nobody in the caravans recognizes it.

Bordin explains that the bandit problem is intractable and has been going on for years. While nobody knows where their base is, Bordin encourages the party to seek answers in the Alethi warcamps.

### Reporting the Stolen Blade

If the PCs report the theft of the Blade, Bordin listens with concern. He admits that he has never seen the weapon, as it’s locked away with a specific glyph sequence entrusted to Bordin by Navani Kholin. He explains that if a thief had forced the lock without knowing the combination, it would be apparent.

To convince Bordin to investigate further, a PC must succeed on a DC 15 Persuasion test. If they do, Bordin checks the case himself and shows the party what’s inside: a cleaver-like Shardblade. After seeing this, he dismisses any further concerns, believing that only fools would steal a priceless Blade only to leave another in its place.

For the rest of the journey, Bordin doubles the number of guards watching the Shardblade, and he joins them whenever he’s awake.

### Bordin’s Favor

If the party talked Ellar down, rescued Rebin, or tried to rescue Taszo, Bordin thinks highly of them. This earns his favor and positively impacts interactions with him on the Shattered Plains.

If the party refused to help Rebin or were caught behaving suspiciously around the Blade wagon, Bordin looks less kindly on the characters, which negatively impacts interactions with him on the Shattered Plains.

## Taszo’s Stone

Po’ahu is a peakspren who was in the process of bonding Taszo. She’ll eventually reveal more of herself in [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]], but for now, she conceals herself in a roughly hewn brown-gray basalt stone that feels warm to the touch. After her initial whisper to the characters, she grieves Taszo, slipping into a state of hurt and dissociation. For more about Po’ahu, see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction.

## A New Bond

Po’ahu watches each of the PCs, especially anyone who followed Taszo into the storm. She is drawn to a character who displays the traits of dependability, extroversion, or joy.

Until Po’ahu reveals herself, the developing spren bond is unconscious. If no characters begin bonding with Po’ahu, she remains a potential spren bond throughout the adventure.

## Honoring an Oath

For Taszo’s sake, Po’ahu wants to stay with the party to make sure they stay true to their promise. If a PC attempts to discard her, they find her later in a pocket on their clothing or pack.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Attracting a Spren
> For now, Po’ahu is a mystery to the characters, but you can inform the players that a spren is watching the party. Discuss whether anyone in the group is interested in having their character form a Nahel bond (see “Playing a Radiant” in chapter 5 of the *Stormlight Handbook*). If multiple players are interested, Po’ahu chooses the character who most fervently stood up for Taszo. Encourage that player to take the First Ideal (Stoneward) talent as their 2nd-level talent, if they’re interested in doing so.

## Journeying Onward

As the true journey toward the Shattered Plains begins the following dawn, the characters advance to 2nd level.

The remaining journey is far less eventful, and [[Journal/02 - 02 Weepers Trail|chapter 2]] begins with the party’s arrival at the Alethi warcamps.
