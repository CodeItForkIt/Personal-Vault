# Appendix A: Adversaries

## Archer

Archers can be found across Roshar, from the ranks of military conflicts to smaller mercenary groups.

![[pg132_Soldier_Archer_Andrea Montalto.webp]]

**ANDREA TENTORI MONTALTO**

> [!tip] Archer Tactics
> Soldiers are trained to be effective on a battlefield in their specialized roles. An **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Archer|archer]]** fights at a distance. They use Immobilizing Shot to immediately take the upper hand, then follow up with Longbow.

## Axehound

Axehounds are a motley species of domesticated predators native to Roshar. The largest of them reach the height of an average human or singer’s waist. They have six legs, fish-like tails, feathery antenna, and a hide made of smooth interlocking segments. An axehound’s jaw and mandibles allow them to vocalize by trumping— making a unique call that sounds like two overlapping and echoing voices. When content, an axehound’s trumping reverberates like a hum, but this lowers to a bellow when they’re in pain or enraged.

![[pg133_AxehoundsGEORGE BENNETT.webp]]

**GEORGE BENNETT**

> [!tip] Axehound Tactics
> An **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Axehound|axehound]]** is rarely encountered alone, as they hunt best with a pack. Their immediate goal is to knock an enemy prone with their Bite (with an advantage from Pack Instincts, if possible), allowing other axehounds to move in for the kill with their On the Hunt reaction. Axehounds typically swarm a single foe rather than disperse themselves amongst a party, and they often drag an unwitting enemy away from allies to more easily defeat them.

## Axies

Axies the Collector is a Siah Aimian, a mysterious species of humanoids who once populated the island nation of Aimia. Axies can shift his form in subtle ways, allowing him to “tattoo” research notes on his own body. He doesn’t age and is difficult to kill through physical means. For more information on Axies, see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction.

![[pg134_Axies The Collector_PETAR PENEV.webp]]

**PETAR PENEV**

> [!tip] Axies’s Tactics
> Despite his immortality, **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Axies the Collector|Axies]]** is a scholar who prefers to avoid any kind of physical conflict. If he finds himself in the middle of a fight, he generally proves unhelpful. He’s always happy to use his Fun Fact to share some arcane lore about spren, but his Curse of Kind trait means his words are likely to distract his allies.

## Bandit

Bandits are a regular menace in the Unclaimed Hills, most commonly targeting merchant caravans headed to the Alethi warcamps. Following Ylt’s ousting of the Bandit Queen in Rathalas, their attacks have become more violent.

![[pg135_Bandits_Artur Mosca.webp]]

> [!tip] Bandit Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Bandit|bandit]]** is a simple but efficient attacker. They attack with their Shortbow from a distance, chipping away at an unsuspecting caravanner’s health as they move in. Once within range, the bandit charges their target to gain Momentum, then ruthlessly debilitates them with their Mace. After overcoming the civilians (and usually ending their lives), the bandit makes off with their ill-gotten gains.

## Chull

Found all across Roshar, chulls are a species of massive crustacean commonly used as pack animals. These docile creatures have large, porous stone-like shells which they can retract their head and thick legs into. They have whip-like antenna and two large claws.

![[pg136_journal_chull_BEN MCSWEENEY & DARREN CALVERT.webp]]

**Shallan’s notes on chull ecology.** **-** **BEN MCSWEENEY & DARREN CALVERT**

> [!tip] Chull Tactics
> Immensely docile, a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Chull Large|chull]]** almost always retreats into their shell with their Defense Curl during combat. However, once pushed to the brink, they use their large claws to defend themselves; a pinch from a chull hurts.

## Commoner

Commoners are the typical citizens found in any nation across Roshar. A farmer, baker, weaver, scribe, or any other person who leads a simple life might be a commoner.

> [!tip] Commoner Tactics
> In combat, a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Civilian/Commoner|commoner]]** generally flees and seeks safety unless their loved ones are in immediate danger. When forced to fight, the commoner might resort to an Improvised Weapon, or they might Distract to aid a more capable ally. In conversations, they usually concede to those they consider more competent.

## Deepest One

The Deepest Ones — called makay-im in the Dawnchant — are Fused who use the surge of Cohesion. Their powers enable them to glide through stone.

A Deepest One has smooth skin and entirely lacks hairstrands. Their minimal carapace covers only their genitals and their head. They have a sinuous form with long limbs, though they’re not abnormally tall, and their eerie white eyes glow red from behind.

> [!tip] Deepest One Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Deepest One|Deepest One]]** is an eerie foe, unsettling and ruthless. They freely move within solid surfaces, striking with their Carapace Nails or Trip Attack while maintaining their protection. They can reshape the battlefield with their Surge of Cohesion, hinder foes with Entrap, or drag a single enemy down to suffocate with their Grasping Hands—especially one they already knocked prone with Trip Attack.

## Direform Regal

Singers who bond callousspren become direform Regals, often looming over seven feet tall. They have an armor- like carapace nearly as tough as Shardplate that covers their head, leaving only their face visible. Through using Voidlight to increase their strength and speed, direforms serve as some of Odium’s most fearsome enforcers.

![[pg139_Direform_William OBrien.webp]]

**WILL O’BRIEN**

> [!tip] Direform Regal Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Direform Regal|direform Regal]]** is a brutal foe who seeks to dominate in hand-to-hand combat. Until an enemy draws close enough for the Regal to charge in with their Hammer, the direform peppers enemies with their Longbow. Once a foe nears, the direform closes the distance, uses Invested Enhancement, and begins hammering away, mixing in the Brace action to take advantage of their Spiked Carapace. When a weakened foe attempts to flee, the direform uses Tackle to cut off their escape.

## Duelist Shardbearer

Shardblade duelists have bonded a Shardblade and are trained in its use. They can summon the Blade in a mere ten heartbeats, useful on and off the battlefield.

![[pg140_Duelist Shardbearer_Amaram_Jason Engle.webp]]

**JASON ENGLE**

> [!tip] Duelist Shardbearer Tactics
> Shardbearers are incredible warriors who control the battlefield. A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Duelist Shardbearer|duelist Shardbearer]]** disrupts their opponents with Feint and uses their exceptional mobility to position themselves for Flamestance or Windstance. With Jarring Insight, a duelist is a threat to their opponent’s plans both in and out of combat.

## Dustbringer of the Second Ideal

Dustbringers are an order of the Knights Radiant, bonded with ashspren, with access to the surges of Division and Abrasion.

![[pg141_Dustbringer_Vanzi_ESTHER SCHRADER.webp]]

**ESTHER SCHRADER**

> [!tip] Dustbringer Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Radiant/Dustbringer of the Second Ideal|Dustbringer of the Second Ideal]]** carves a destructive path in and out of melee range with their foes. They use Skate to close the distance, then attack with their Sidesword before disengaging. Burst of Flame becomes their primary tactic for groups of enemies, and Decaying Touch saps the strength of their bulkier foes. Ultimately, the Dustbringer devastates an area and is hard to pin down—and their Eroding Escape ensures that.

## Expert

Experts are those who stand out from the crowd due to unique competence or convictions. Experts might be local leaders, headstrong youths, or savvy traders.

> [!tip] Expert Tactics
> An **[[Actors/Adversaries/Civilian/Expert|expert]]** usually fights cautiously with their Improvised Weapon, but unlike most commoners, they might run straight into danger if they believe in a cause or see a chance for glory. In conversations, they’re more likely than commoners stand their ground. If a PC challenges an expert’s beliefs, the expert is more likely to spend focus to resist influence. If sufficiently provoked, they may use their Counter Argument and Stern Countenance to make things difficult for their opponent.

## Eyes of Pala (Agent & Watcher)

## Eyes of Pala Agent

Eyes of Pala agents are cunning and methodical. In service to the Herald of Knowledge, they pursue the fundamental truths of the universe and ensure that those in power share truth with their people. Through reasoning and piercing insight, these agents bring sharp acuity to bear when they engage others in combat.

![[pg143_agent of pailiah_VALERIA CASALE.webp]]

**Eyes of Pala Agent - VALERIA CASALE**

## Eyes of Pala Watcher

Eyes of Pala watchers are adroit and highly trusted operatives who doggedly pursue the Herald Pailiah’s goals. Each watcher wears a ceremonial green blindfold, marking their rank and signifying that their keen insight transcends the need for sight. Truth, knowledge, and wise leadership are their calling, and they wield both honed intuition and battlefield prowess to achieve results.

![[pg143_watcher of pailiah_VALERIA CASALE_.webp]]

**Eyes of Pala Watcher - VALERIA CASALE**

> [!tip] Eyes of Pala Tactics
> An **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Agent|Eyes of Pala agent]]** uses their honed intuition to navigate the battlefield. They identify the largest threat and use Insightful Defense on that target to better avoid deadly attacks. By working together, agents who favor the shortbow and those who favor the sidesword can knock down opponents and deplete their focus. If needed, agents coordinate with their allies, using Quick Escape to retreat when a peer gives them an opening.
>
> An **[[Actors/Adversaries/Eyes of Pala Watcher|Eyes of Pala Watcher]]** uses Insightful Defense either at the start of combat or amidst battle to gain the upper hand over an opponent. Exploitative Escape allows them to retreat after an enemy’s misstep, then they move to an opportune position to hit multiple foes with Fan of Blades.

## Guard

Guards serve as the backbone of defense for centers of civilization and persons of power throughout Roshar. In a city, a disciplined and well-trained guard force can be the difference between order and anarchy. For a person they protect, they can be the difference between life and death.

![[pg144_Guard_Joel Chaim Holzman.webp]]

**OEL CHAIM HOLTZMAN**

> [!tip] Guard Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Guard|guard]]** quickly incapacitates their targets, especially those discovered committing a crime. They aim to knock an opponent prone with their Longspear, then use Debilitate to inflict significant damage. While accompanied by other guards or allies, a guard uses Work as One, quickly and efficiently coordinating to subdue opponents.

## Juvenile Whitespine

Held by the Thaylen merchant Ryvlk in the warcamps, these juvenile whitespines may be small, but they’re still dangerous. These person-sized predators are named for the row of spikes that runs down the carapace on their backs. They move on two legs and have two sets of arms with long, wicked claws.

![[pg145_Whitespine_FLORIAN STITZ.webp]]

**FLORIAN STITZ**

> [!tip] Whitespine Tactics
> Angry about being kept in captivity, a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Juvenile Whitespine|juvenile whitespine]]** lashes out at anyone they can. They prefer to charge at distant, weak opponents to deal extra damage with their Goring Tusks, then follow with their Claws to leave prey bleeding out. A whitespine is quick to leave an enemy’s reach to charge yet another with their Goring Tusks for more extra damage. When a fight goes poorly, a whitespine uses Pheromone Release, stupefying their opponents so the whitespine can retreat or overwhelm them.

## Kaiana

Kaiana is a Reshi human and a Truthwatcher bonded to the mistspren Horizons-Ever-Drifting. She is Ylt’s mentee and has aligned herself with Pailiah. For those who earn her loyalty, she makes a helpful ally. For more information on Kaiana, see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction.

![[pg146_Kaiana_JESSICA LIU.webp]]

**JESSICA LIU**

> [!tip] Kaiana’s Tactics
> **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Kaiana the Truthwatcher|Kaiana]]** is particularly skilled in Progression and uses it to support her allies: Her Reflexive Growth deters attackers, and when needed, she heals allies with Regrowth. Meanwhile, her Distracting Illusion can protect weaker or injured allies. When she isn’t needed to react and protect (or when she runs out of Investiture), she uses her Passionate Ally to give allies an opening, and she uses her Knife to impede enemies.

## Khornak

Lurking in the shallow waters of coastal areas, the khornak are brutal hunters who thrive in packs—but even one is frightening on their own. They are most dangerous in close range, where they unveil their primary weapon: a massive jaw lined with many rows of jagged teeth made for gouging prey, latching into skin, and dragging victims into the water. Khornaks have webbed limbs protected by carapace, along with eight beady eyes and jagged tails. The khornaks’ bond with spren allows their hefty frames to glide effortlessly through the water in search of prey.

![[pg147_Khornak_Tom Babbey.webp]]

**TOM BABBEY**

> [!tip] Khornak Tactics
> A dangerous and deadly foe, a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Khornak|khornak]]** first ensnares and tears into their prey with their Crushing Jaw, strengthened by their Terrifying Ambush. While the khornak restrains this enemy in their jaws, they repel other opponents with their Tail Sweep, and they Drag their prey away to feast. If their meal manages to escape, the khornak hunts foes down with Ruthless Predator.

## Lilinum, the Devastating One

Lilinum is a Shardblade-wielding Devastating One and one of the two Fused enforcers sent to subjugate Rall Elorim. Her carapace is chaotically striped in white and red, and her fighting style reflects her preference for chaos. As a Devastating One, Lilinum is skilled in the surge of Division. She delights in effortlessly turning objects and structures to rubble, her own form constantly trailing a cloud of wispy dust.

![[pg148_Dirgehollow Lilinum_SVETLANA KOSTINA.webp]]

**Lilinum and her Shardblade in Dirgehollow.**

**-** **SVETLANA KOSTINA & DAVID FRASHESKI**

> [!tip] Lilinum’s Tactics
> **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Lilinum the Devastating One|Lilinum]]** fights with a playful recklessness, engaging foes for maximum destruction and chaos with little heed for her safety or that of her allies. She favors her Shardblade in combat, then follows it with her Devastating Touch or Claws. If a group of characters is within a structure, she uses her Surge of Division to collapse it on them.

## Nimbleform Singer

Nimbleform is a singer form that is suited for tasks involving focus, speed, and dexterity. Singers in this form are lithe, lack carapace, and have longer hairstrands.

![[pg150_Nimbleform Singer_Heth_SVETLANA KOSTINA.webp]]

**SVETLANA KOSTINA**

> [!tip] Nimbleform Singer Tactics
> While **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Nimbleform Singer|nimbleform singers]]** rarely fight, they’re experts at tracking creatures and surveying an area, and they can easily exploit an enemy’s weakness using their Chosen Target action.

## Shadowform Zealot

Caught between the Physical and Cognitive Realms, the shadowform Regals were the result of a plan enacted by Ba-Ado-Mishram to create powerful assassins. They each bound a shadowspren to their gemheart, making their bodies incorporeal—but unexpectedly, their gemhearts stayed solid. Ever since, the shadowform singers have been torn between two realms and have endured millennia of torment.

When the Everstorm reignited these gemhearts with Voidlight, Ylt’s zealots recognized shadowform’s potential as a weapon. Shadowform zealots are created when a human pierces their heart with a sharpened shadowform gemstone, allowing their body to be consumed. The resulting entities are driven by the fragmented memories and passions of the ancient singers. They collectively hum a condemning dirge as they seek to slay humans, especially Knights Radiant.

![[pg151_Shadowform Zealot_Elisa Serio.webp]]

**ELISA SERIO**

> [!tip] Shadowform Zealot Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Shadowform Zealot|shadowform zealot]]** is a slippery foe who takes advantage of tactical positioning. They move opponents into areas of shadow using Drag Through Shadows, then gather around to strike using their Shadow Claws. They wear down significant threats with Grieving Dirge, and if they need to position themselves or escape, shadowform zealots rely on Unbodied and Drag Through Shadows.

## Shellmite

Shellmites are crustaceans who live in large nests, working together to support a single colony. They resemble plate-sized crabs with rocky shells, but when they retract their limbs, they’re almost perfectly camouflaged as rocks. Different shellmites play specialized roles to support their nest, from lighter- shelled workers to sharp-shelled soldiers. An individual shellmite presents little threat, and their exoskeletons contain succulent meat. But a group of shellmites can be a death trap, and hunters mark suspected nests with bright red paint as a warning to steer clear.

![[pg152_Shellmite_TOM BABBEY.webp]]

**TOM BABBEY**

> [!tip] Shellmite Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Shellmite|shellmite]]** uses their Rugged Camouflage to hide among common rocks. When unlucky travelers walk into a nest of shellmites, these crustaceans strike quickly with a Pincer, gaining an advantage on their first attack. Shellmites then use their Feeding Frenzy to overwhelm intruders, tearing and ripping at clothing and armor with their strong claws, while sticking together to take advantage of their numbers. Shellmites are reliant on surprise, so try placing multiple hidden shellmites throughout an area to keep PCs on their toes.

## Skybreaker of the Second Ideal

Widely seen as fanatics, members of the Skybreaker order of the Knights Radiant believe in a strict moral code, and they uphold the law above nearly all other things. The Skybreakers were the only Radiant order not to abandon their oaths during the Recreance, instead operating in secret for millennia under the guidance of Nale, the Herald of Justice. In the aftermath of the Everstorm, Nale and most other Skybreakers have chosen to follow the singers, believing that the singers have the right to Roshar as the original owners of the land.

## Surgebinding

Skybreakers use the surges of Gravitation and Division, which they receive from bonding with highspren.

Through the surge of Gravitation, Skybreakers can change the direction and strength of an object’s gravitational attraction; they can use this to fly and run up walls, or even to send other people and objects flying.

Using the surge of Division, Skybreakers can touch an object and cause it (or part of it) to burn, turn to dust, or decay, such as by burning a pattern on a piece of wood.

> [!tip] Skybreaker Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Radiant/Skybreaker of the Second Ideal|Skybreaker of the Second Ideal]]** is an aerial combatant who wields Gravitation to devastating effect. They begin combat with Gravitational Support to gain a tactical advantage, then use Offensive Gravitation and Lashing Shot against foes both near and far, and they freely Menace combatants.
>
> Skybreakers dedicate themselves to pursuing justice. As such, they’re likely to prioritize foes who they deem criminals, initially ignoring others who they don’t feel the need to apprehend and punish.

## Skyeel

Skyeels are amphibious creatures who are as at home in the air as they are in the water. They’re long and slender with a dorsal fin that runs the length of their body, accompanied by wing-like fins on each side. When out of the water, a skyeel holds themself aloft with inflatable gas pouches under their side fins. Most adult skyeels grow to 4 or 5 feet long, but the rare greater skyeels can grow to 7 or 8 feet. While skyeels are carnivores, they feed predominantly on small land mammals and fish, rarely attacking humans. They travel alone or in flocks and hunt from the sky by expunging their gas sacks and diving toward prey. After diving, skyeels must spend time digesting their prey and refilling their sacks before they can take to the air again.

Skyeels are often accompanied by white arrowhead- shaped luckspren who swim in schools around them.

![[pg154_Skyeel_BEN MCSWEENEY & SAMI RYTKÖNEN.webp]]

**Skyeels soar above the seas of Roshar.** **-** **BEN MCSWEENEY & SAMI RYTKÖNEN**

![[pg155_Greater Skyeel_FLORIAN STITZ.webp]]

**Under normal circumstances, skyeels never attack humans.** **-** **FLORIAN STITZ**

> [!tip] Skyeel Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Skyeel|skyeel]]** remains in flight and hidden until they choose a target, then the skyeel uses their Dive Bomb to bite and restrain their prey. In the rare instance where a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Animal/Greater Skyeel|greater skyeel]]** attacks a larger target, they use Dive Bomb then Constrict their Restrained prey, hoping for an easy meal.

## Spear Infantry

Spear infantry are rank-and-file combatants who appear in countless armies, from the Alethi who vie for the Shattered Plains to the Emuli forces fighting the Eighty’s War.

![[pg156_Soldiers_Spear_Infantry_ANTTI HAKOSAARI.webp]]

**ANTTI HAKOSAARI**

> [!tip] Spear Infantry Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spear Infantry|spear infantry]]** topples enemies with their Shield Bash and then strikes with their Shortspear. Their Military Tactics can be used to pin down fleeing targets or bolster their fellow soldiers, depending on how the battle is going.

## Spearmaster

In Vorin nations where ordinary soldiers are forbidden to use swords, mastery of the spear has become an art.

> [!tip] Spearmaster Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Spearmaster|spearmaster]]** is a skilled and careful combatant, taking slow turns so they can make multiple attacks. They spend focus to knock targets Prone with their Longspear, then prioritize attacking Prone targets to take advantage of Merciless. If an enemy gets too close, the spearmaster tries to Kick them away and line up a Run Through attack. When they have an extra action, they spend it on Gain Advantage or Brace, depending on whether they find themself on the offensive or the defensive.

## Stormform Regal

A singer can attain stormform, a Regal form of power, by bonding with a stormspren. Stormform Regals have subtle armor and barbed carapace. The air around stormform singers is frequently electrified by flashes of red lightning.

Stormform is notably weak to water. Contact with a small amount only diminishes their control over their lightning powers, but if a stormform singer is drenched, they can’t summon lightning until they dry off.

![[pg158_Stormform_Irina Kuzmina.webp]]

**IRINA NORDSOL**

> [!tip] Stormform Regal Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Stormform Regal|stormform Regal]]** is a cunning foe, one specialized in closing distances and defending themself. The Regal uses Storm Leap to jump to a weak-looking foe, hoping to goad them into taking damage from Electrical Charge. Meanwhile, the Regal alternates between using their Shortspear and Bolt of Lightning to strike injured enemies.

## Taszo-son-Clutio

Taszo is an earnest and inquisitive Shin human. As a Stone Shaman, Taszo is skilled in fighting and self- defense. He left Shinovar with a convoy on a critical mission to retrieve Taln’s Honorblade. After a deadly attack on his convoy, Taszo became the sole surviving member of his original group.

![[pg159_Taszo-son-Clutio_DARKO STOJANOVIC.webp]]

**ARKO STOJANOVIC**

> [!tip] Taszo’s Tactics
> **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Taszo-son-Clutio|Taszo]]** seeks to be helpful, though he is unaccustomed to fighting with his broken arm. He assists whoever is most threatened, spending his focus on A Friend in Need to aid allies in tough situations.

## Thief

In the shadows of Roshar’s urban centers lurk many common criminals, including thieves and pickpockets.

![[pg160_The Thief_NELE KLUMPE.webp]]

**NELE KLUMPE**

> [!tip] Thief Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Thief|thief]]** begins by using Disorienting Distraction and—if trying to avoid a fight—they immediately follow it with Pickpocket to steal away their prize. In a fight, their Disorienting Distraction is equally useful for making their Dagger strike more lethal. Should they get pinned down, they use Slippery to flee the scene.

## Thrill Berserker

The Thrill is a phenomenon described as a consuming desire to compete and win. It is most commonly sought after and experienced by Alethi soldiers. Those who lose themselves to the Thrill in the heat of battle are Thrill berserkers.

![[pg161_Thrill_Berserker_ANTTI HAKOSAARI.webp]]

**ANTTI HAKOSAARI**

> [!tip] Thrill Berserker Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Soldier/Thrill Berserker|Thrill berserker]]** rushes into battle, undeterred by formidable enemies and careful fortifications. They fight with alarming ferocity, using Berserker Rage and Shield Bash to cleave their path, regardless of whether friend or foe stands in the way.

## Veth

Veth is an Alethi human who witnessed Ylt’s rise to prominence among Pailiah’s disciples and swore to aid in the retrieval of the Honorblade. He is an unquestioning believer in Ylt’s vision for the Eyes of Palas’ future.

Veth has spent years honing a fighting style that employs his lightning-fast reflexes and ability to read opponents, making him a deadly enemy even when outnumbered.

![[pg162_Veth_PETAR PENEV.webp]]

**PETAR PENEV**

> [!tip] Veth’s Tactics
> **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Veth|Veth]]** uses Insightful Defense against the most obvious martial threat, then engages them in close combat. He spends focus quickly to gain extra turns, and he switches to secondary targets using Exploit Advantage. To benefit from his Targeted Strikes, he favors targets he has an advantage against, such as Prone targets. If harried with ranged attacks, he uses Intuitive Redirect to make any missed attacks hit his enemies.

## Warform Singer

In the brutal clashes on the Shattered Plains, most singers have assumed warform, a singer form suited for the brutality of combat. Warform singers have thick carapace armor, and their strength and stamina are greater than singers in other forms.

![[pg163_Singer_Warpair_CAIO EDUARDO SANTOS.webp]]

**CAIO EDUARDO SANTOS**

> [!tip] Warform Singer Tactics
> A **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singer]]** is a brutally efficient combatant, especially when fighting alongside their warpair—another **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singer]]**. Warpairs descend on a target together with their Warpair Coordination and, using their Martial Experience, they make multiple Axe attacks against a single foe to fell them. In a tight spot, the warform singer relies on their External Carapace for relief from an onslaught while their warpair continues fighting.

## Ylt

Ylt is an arrogant Iriali Truthwatcher bonded to the Enlightened mistspren Tyche. As an influential leader for the Eyes of Pala, he works closely with Pailiah to secure Taln’s Honorblade—but his future sight has sparked far more ambitious plans, and he now dares to dream of supplanting a Herald. For more information about Ylt, see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction.

![[pg164_Ylt_PETAR PENEV.webp]]

**PETAR PENEV**

> [!tip] Ylt’s Tactics
> Before the final battle in [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]], **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Ylt the Truthwatcher|Ylt]]** uses his Illusory Flourish, Vaulting Stone, and Tension Parry to stay out of harm’s way. He can use Vaulting Stone to move close to weaker targets, or he can strike from afar with Stone Spear. In melee, he attacks with the Honorblade, uses Illusory Flourish to disorient opponents, then retreats to a safe distance. If the tide of battle turns, he heals with Regrowth—but doesn’t extend the same aid to his allies.

## Zealot

When Ylt overthrew the Bandit Queen, he demanded that every bandit in Rathalas swear fealty to him. Some bandits did so reluctantly, but others followed him with fanatical zeal. These zealots whisper that he is blessed by the Heralds themselves, and that by obeying his commands, they can gain incredible powers like his. Wearing green robes and carrying swords normally forbidden to common citizens of Alethkar, zealots revere Ylt and obey his orders without question.

> [!tip] Zealot Tactics

As a battle grows more violent and their companions become Frenzied, a zealot uses Inspire Zeal to encourage courage and brutality in the face of death.
