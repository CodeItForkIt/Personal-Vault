# 06: Precipice of Change

## Chapter Summary

After the party’s encounter with the Herald of Justice and their journey across the sea, the PCs are hot on Ylt’s trail once more.

Arriving in Rall Elorim, they must navigate an occupied city to track down Ylt and his cultists.

## Running This Chapter

Ylt has fled to the secret Eyes of Pala stronghold in the city of Rall Elorim. Upon the party’s arrival in the city, they find it occupied by Odium’s forces—most notably by two Fused, Dymab and Lilinum, who clash in their handling of the city.

After the party navigates a checkpoint run by a Regal singer, they’re drawn into a conflict between singer enforcers and an Iriali resistance group. Once the dust has settled, the party can explore Rall Elorim to discover where Ylt and the Eyes of Pala make their base of operations.

In a short series of nonlinear scenes involving the resistance, Axies the Collector, and Kaiana (if she’s with the party), the party discovers that Ylt’s followers must be in the vaults beneath the city, accessible from a well in the shadowy Dirgehollow district. However, before they can reach Ylt, the Fused Lilinum catches wind of their interest in the tunnels.

## Character Advancement

The characters start this chapter at 6th level and advance to 7th level when they reach the entrance to the vaults in Rall Elorim at the end of this chapter.

The following decision offers an opportunity to introduce spren, strengthen spren bonds, or swear Ideals:

- A PC’s decision to help the resistance or the loyalists.

- Offering empathy and advice to Kaiana when she visits Cabsan.

Characters with goals related to learning more about threats to Roshar or about the Everstorm’s effect on singers have opportunities to do so throughout the scenes in occupied Rall Elorim.

## The City of Shadows

The sea journey passed peacefully for the party—but as they traveled, the rest of Roshar descended into war. By the time the PCs arrive in Rall Elorim, Odium’s forces have taken Iri and most of northwestern Roshar. Furthermore, Ylt remains one step ahead of the party, as he arrived in the city a full week before the *Measured Response*.

When the party finally arrives in Rall Elorim, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The vast city of Rall Elorim lies beneath the prominent natural overhang of a mountain. Some buildings cling to the roof of that overhang, carved into hollowed-out stalactites. Water cuts paths down the mountain, creating a sweep of Stormfalls that pour into a reservoir at the base of the city. Even from a distance, the unique architecture stands out. Algae and vines cover prayer statues of all shapes and sizes that adorn the streets, and the walls are painted with colorful murals.\<\
>      As the ship nears the port, security is high: armed singer warriors patrol the harbor, and flying singers soar above the city.

Rall Elorim has been spared the worst of the Everstorms, though there’s evidence of minor damage around the city, such as wilted plant life, cracked statues, shattered ceramics, and a few damaged stone buildings held up by makeshift supports.

## What Kaiana Knows

If Kaiana is with the party, she expresses concerned surprise upon seeing the changed Rall Elorim. Though she trained in Pailiah’s Vault, the arrival of the Everstorm has made her knowledge of the city outdated. If PCs ask her how to access the vault, she agrees to take them via a tunnel by the Stormfalls—but after entering the city, she quickly realizes the entrance she knew is no longer accessible, having collapsed during the Everstorm. (See [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Kaiana’s Trail|Kaiana’s Trail]] for what she remembers of the alternative entrance.)

## About Rall Elorim

Before the party enters the city, make yourself familiar with the following information.

### A Change in Leadership

More notable than the superficial damage is Rall Elorim’s changed political situation. Following the singer awakening, the humans of Iri acknowledged the singers as rightful residents of Roshar, giving them full Iriali citizenship. Shortly after, Odium’s force of Regals (singers who have accepted a form of power) and Fused (immortal singers Invested with powers over the surges) arrived. The Iriali government agreed to a tentative peace with these new, powerful outsiders, in return for protection and aid in the rebuilding.

However, not everyone welcomes Odium’s occupying force. Fractured groups of singers and humans resist from the shadows, hoping to push Odium’s influence out of the city. The party must navigate this new political reality of Rall Elorim, moving through the occupied city in their quest for the Honorblade. See chapter 6 of the Stormlight World Guide for more about Rall Elorim.

### Odium’s Enforcers

Rall Elorim’s day-to-day law enforcement is led by two Fused, **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Dymab|Dymab]]** (Deepest One, he/him) and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Lilinum the Devastating One|Lilinum]]** (she/her). Dymab is a Deepest One obsessed with order, and the Shardbearer Lilinum is his chaotic counterpart.

Dymab is a highly competent administrator tasked with maintaining order and ensuring that the local Iriali hold up their end of the peace treaty. He has a passion for new fabrial technology, and he believes social problems can be solved through systems, technology, and law.

Lilinum has a dark sense of humor and delights in being unpredictable. She’s feared throughout the city, and her reputation for being erratically violent and wielding a Shardblade has sparked many rumors. Though she’s ostensibly in Rall Elorim to aid Dymab’s assignment, her disregard for his authority makes her a thorn in his side. Dymab has tasked her with rooting out the resistance—a duty she routinely shirks, as she’s more interested in the mysterious Dirgehollow shadows and pushing Dymab’s buttons.

The PCs are likely to meet Dymab if they choose to help the resistance thwart his fabrial surveillance plans (See [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Heth’s Mission|Heth’s Mission]]). The party also hears rumors of the Shardbearer Lilinum throughout the city, and they meet her in Dirgehollow at the end of the chapter (see [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|Into the Deep]]).

### Singers Behind Enemy Lines

The recent singer awakening has made many of Odium’s forces warier of humans and their motivations. When interacting with any of Odium’s forces in the city, singer PCs gain an advantage on Deception, Insight, Leadership, and Persuasion tests.

## Checkpoint

Whether the party opts to dock at Rall Elorim’s port or to leave the Measured Response further away and head into the city on foot, the PCs must contend with a checkpoint to enter the city proper. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The entrance to the city is blocked by a barricade of broken wood and stone. In true Iriali fashion, it’s decorated with brightly colored paints.\
>      Flanked by a dozen guards armored with thick carapace, a tall and lithe singer with black and red marbled skin watches over the crowds with an imperious gaze. Clearly in control, he wears fine garments, and a crown of ornamental carapace grows from his brow.\
>      “You there,” he demands in your native tongue. “What business have you in the Free City of Rall Elorim?”

This singer is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Nanath|Nanath]]** (expert, he/him), a Regal chosen by the god Odium and blessed with envoyform. The form grants him the ability to speak all languages; because of this, Dymab has assigned him to oversee arrivals and departures from the city. Nanath questions the party about their purpose in Rall Elorim.

During this light interrogation, Nanath is content to answer any questions about the city, including the following:

- **What happened here?** “The Everstorm freed the singers from an age of oppression. Now we reclaim this city—and all the lands of Roshar that were taken from us.”

- **What happened to the humans of Iri?** “We’ve made a peace agreement with the humans, despite everything they did to us. They remain with us to help rebuild Rall Elorim as a new bastion of singer culture.”

- **Who is in charge?** “The Fused have divine authority here. They are our ancient gods returned to lead us to victory. As the Heavenly Ones soar above us, so do the Deepest Ones watch from below. I serve the Fused Dymab. Reports of your activities shall go to him.”

- **Why so much security?** “The situation here has not yet stabilized. Some have not yet accepted our new world. And there are… concerning reports of deaths in the shadows. Our scrutiny here is a matter of safety for our people.”

If the party wants to lie about their intentions, a PC must make a Deception test against Nanath’s Spiritual defense (13). On a success, Nanath allows the party into the city with no further questions. On a failure, or if the PCs are honest about tracking Ylt, Nanath allows them entry, but requires they be accompanied by two escorts: **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Yana|Yana]]** (warform singer, she/her) and **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Teba|Teba]]** (nimbleform singer, she/her).

> [!tip] Lost Shadows
> Though the city’s vast overhanging mountain is chiefly responsible for Rall Elorim’s epithet, “City of Shadows,” local legends also credit hauntings by shadowspren. Stories claim that one can hear the shadows themselves wailing in agony, especially in the Dirgehollow district. These stories arise from the forgotten tragedy of the shadowform singers who were trapped in incorporeal forms millennia ago due to a disastrous bonding with the rare shadowspren. All that remains are their gemhearts, which are part of Pailiah’s collection in her vaults below the city.
>
> Ylt now controls the shadowform gemhearts, and he plans to release the trapped shadowform singers to further his and Odium’s influence. For more details on the nature of the shadowform singers and Ylt’s plan, see the [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Running This Chapter|Running This Chapter]] section in chapter 7.

## The Resistance

Once inside Rall Elorim (with or without an escort), the party can seek out an inn, tavern, or shop for personal reasons, or they can look for a lead to find Ylt and the Eyes of Pala’s base of operations. Whatever location the PCs choose, their timing proves unfortunate—for as they conduct their business, that location is raided by singers loyal to Odium and sent by Lilinum. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The front door abruptly opens with a bang, nearly knocking it off its hinges, and a small group of singers push into the room. Their red-eyed leader stands seven feet tall, spiked carapace covering him like a full suit of armor. Behind him, warform singers stand guard.\
>      “Nobody move!” the red-eyed singer bellows. “This business is under investigation for harboring dissidents. Surrender your spheres and your weapons!”\
>      One of the warform soldiers seizes a customer by the neck. “The Devastating One is impatient, you best hurry before we burn this place to the ground.”

![[pg108_Direform_William OBrien.webp]]

**Lilinum’s Enforcer** **-** **WILL O’BRIEN**

The squad harassing the business owner and customers consists of one **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Direform Regal|direform Regal]]** and three **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singers]]**. If left unchallenged, they break objects and roughly interrogate the humans here, claiming that the Devastating One watches—referring to Lilinum—and she’ll bring fire and her Shardblade if the rebels don’t reveal themselves.

Give the party a chance to intervene if they wish. If they do, combat ensues between the singers and the PCs; you can refer to **map 6.1** if it’s a good fit for this location. Read the following either after the first round of combat or (if the party doesn’t start a fight) after the singers start seizing assets:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A section of the wall bursts open, revealing a hidden door—and behind it, a lithe singer with black and red marbled skin and red hairstrands slicked over his head. Beside him stands an imposing and beautiful singer, her carapace decorated with painted white patterns. Brandishing long knives, the two singers charge into the fray.\
>      “For a free Iri!”

The Regal’s accusations have proven true. The resistance fighters emerge from their hidden room with every intent to silence Odium’s loyalists. The resistance leader is a singer named **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Heth|Heth]]** (nimbleform singer, he/him), and his second-in-command is **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Shull|Shull]]** (warform singer, she/her). If Nanath assigned Yana and Teba to escort the party in the [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Checkpoint|Checkpoint]] section, they fight with Odium’s loyalists.

### An Audience

Any PCs who look outside see they have an audience. Lilinum lounges at a café across the street, idly carving a rude glyph into the stone wall with her Shardblade while watching the show. She has bright red hairstrands and wickedly sharp carapace horns, and a trail of dust moves in her wake.

Lilinum makes eye contact and waves playfully at any PC who notices her, but she eventually loses interest and leaves before the battle ends.

### Battlefield Effects

The following effect is active during this close- quarters combat:

- **Burn it Down!** Following Lilinum’s instructions, the singer enforcers brought oil and torches to burn down the homes and businesses here. The enforcers can set a 5-foot-radius area alight as an action (1). It becomes difficult terrain, dealing 4 (`1d8`) energy damage each time a character moves into that area or starts their turn there.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Lilinum’s Raid Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Aftermath

When Odium’s loyal singers are defeated, an uneasy peace returns to the business. In addition to their weapons, the defeated singers collectively possess 75 **marks**, three flasks of ***oil***, and a ***flint and steel***.

### Meeting Heth

The singer resistance leader Heth tries to gauge the PCs. If the party fought Odium’s singers before he and Shull showed themselves, he enthusiastically introduces himself and explains his purpose of freeing singers in Iri from Fused rule. If the party fought only after being targeted themselves, Heth is more guarded but still tries to win the PCs to his cause.

Shull, like most of Heth’s movement, is wary of humans. She stays quiet but regards human characters with distrust.

Conscious of the attention this place will now draw, Heth invites the party to join him in his safe house in the Dirgehollow district. He describes its location in an abandoned warehouse marked with a large, cracked sphere lamp over the door. Before he departs, he warns the PCs that the Fused are likely on the lookout for them. If a PC mentions seeing Lilinum, he grows more anxious and insists on leaving immediately.

## Finding Ylt

Once the dust has settled, the party can resume their key mission in Rall Elorim: finding Ylt’s base of operations. The PCs need to meet with two of the following three NPCs to gain enough leads to the Eyes of Pala’s base:

- **Kaiana.** The Truthwatcher does her best to reconnect with her spren and her order. Proceed to the [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Kaiana’s Trail|Kaiana’s Trail]] scene.

- **Heth of the Resistance.** If the characters take Heth’s offer to visit his safehouse, he’s prepared to trade information for a favor. Proceed to the [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Heth’s Mission|Heth’s Mission]] scene.

- **Axies the Collector.** Axies stumbles upon the PCs while they move about the Dirgehollow district. This chance encounter can occur at any point during this section. Proceed to the [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Collecting Shadows|Collecting Shadows]] scene.

The first two sources visited each offer one of these leads, in order:

- The Eyes of Pala are likely based in the vaults. These subterranean tunnels are accessible from a derelict well in the Dirgehollow district.

- The passage beneath the well is only traversable for one hour when the moon Nomon is highest in the sky and the waters retreat.

After the PCs have both leads and head to the derelict well, see [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|Into the Deep]].

> [!tip] Siding With the Fused
> Despite the Fused’s violent suppression of the resistance movement in Rall Elorim, many Fused wish for their new city to prosper, and they’re prepared to work with the party where they see mutual benefit. Odium’s forces can therefore act as an additional source of information for characters searching for Ylt.
>
> However, some Fused may exact a high price for that information. For example, Dymab refuses to share any information unless the PCs betray what they know about Heth and the resistance—a course of action that most Radiant spren, especially Po’ahu, strongly object to.

## Kaiana’s Trail

If Kaiana is with the party, she already knows one of the leads. While she won’t withhold this information, she asks for a small favor in exchange for revealing it: Faced with opposing her former mentor and fellow acolytes, she feels unsure and lost, so before confronting Ylt, she’d like time to seek the Trail of Stars.

Kaiana invites one or more PCs to join her in visiting **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Cabsan|Cabsan]]** (expert, he/him), an Iriali shoemaker belonging to the tradition of the Trail. Trail adherents believe in helping people find their way in their unique life’s path, in part by providing footwear, and in part through cryptic storytelling. Use this as a chance for reflection, bonding, and addressing the party’s goals or oaths.

For her part, Kaiana questions the basis of her beliefs, many of which solidified during her mentorship under Ylt. She shares details of her personal history (see [[Journal/01 - 00 Introduction|Notable Characters]] in this book’s introduction) and her fears over facing Ylt.

## Heth’s Mission

If the party follows Heth directly to the resistance safehouse, or if they later seek him using the directions he gave them, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Heth’s directions lead to a district deep into Rall Elorim, covered completely by the mountainous overhang that protects the city from highstorms. The shadow of the mountain plunges the neighborhood into darkness, but despite the gloom, it’s clear the buildings here were in disrepair even before the new westward storm.\
>      Rows of rough-hewn prayer statues line the streets, each depicting a figure in mourning. The few people who dare to walk the streets tread quickly and avoid looking at anything but their feet.\
>      Among an abandoned block of dilapidated algaecovered stone warehouses, you find one with a cracked sphere lamp over the door: the resistance safehouse.

The resistance leader Heth has sent word of the party’s arrival, and Shull meets the party at the door. She leads them to the ragtag band of Iriali singers camped here. The singers meet any human PCs with trepidation, and Shull keeps them under her watchful eye.

Heth offers the PCs warm cups of stew and cots to rest, though he regrets he has few resources. Now out of sight of the Fused and their loyalists, Heth is happy to answer more questions about this organization:

- **Who is the resistance?** “We oppose the new tyranny that the Fused impose on us. We stand together to find a better way in this new world.”

- **Why are the Iriali singers resisting Odium?** “Odium’s rule is just another form of forceful subjugation for singers. This is about creating a future where everyone gets to walk their own path. We want to build a free and peaceful Rall Elorim for all.”

- **Why are there no humans here?** “Humans enslaved us, and the memory of our time in servitude is a fresh wound. To their credit, after my people awakened in the first Everstorm, the Iriali humans accepted us as the true owners of this land. But any path to reconciliation was shattered when the Fused arrived. Odium’s servants are violent and disingenuously claim to fight in the name of singers—but while they don’t fight for us, that nuance is hard to explain. I know we can’t win this fight without everyone resisting Odium working together, and I know some humans feel the same, but trust is in short supply.”

- **What do the Fused fight for?** “Apparently they’re the ancient, immortal gods of our people. But they steal the bodies and lives of singers and turn them to their own purposes, speaking with strange, hateful rhythms. I’m not sure they remember what it was like to be free any more than we do.”

- **Who are the Fused that rule this city?** “The two giving us the most trouble are Dymab and Lilinum. Since the singer enforcers were ready to destroy the building, Lilinum must’ve been behind the raid today. Storms knows how, but she’s got a Shardblade; she’s terrifying if you have her attention, but she’s seemed distracted lately. The real worry is Dymab. He’s less theatrical but he’s thorough, and tougher to evade.” If the party presses further, Heth knows everything from the earlier [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Odium’s Enforcers|Odium’s Enforcers]] section, including about the tension between Dymab and Lilinum.

After speaking with the PCs, Heth proposes a deal: He and the resistance will help the party find what they’re here for if they help him with a crucial—but nonviolent—resistance mission. If the party includes human PCs, he quietly shares that if they help the singer movement, it’ll go a small way toward showing his group that some humans can be trusted.

> [!tip] Path to Radiance: Ideals of the Willshapers
> Characters who unhesitatingly pledge support for Heth and the resistance may attract the attention of a lightspren. The endeavor or combats within the Dirgehollow are good moments for Willshapers to swear a new Ideal.

### The Fabrial Survey

![[pg112_The Fabriel Survey_JÁNOS BRUMAR.webp]]

**JÁNOS BRUMAR**

If the party agrees to Heth’s mission, he explains that resistance activity in the Dirgehollow has attracted **[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Ch. 6/Dymab|Dymab]]** (Deepest One, he/him). Rather than raid establishments, Dymab has ordered his loyalists to set up a series of alerter fabrials to track resistance movements. Heth requests that the PCs find and sabotage them.

Run the sabotage mission as an endeavor (see [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Resolving the Endeavor|Resolving the Endeavor]]). It’s unlikely the party’s activities will lead to encountering Dymab himself, but if so, see [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Resolving the Endeavor|Roleplaying Dymab]]. Potential approaches include:

- **Disrupt Supply Lines.** Dymab’s loyalists keep bringing in new parts and gems to repair fabrials damaged by the resistance. A PC might figure out how the fabrials are being transported (DC 14 Deduction), steal critical components in transit (DC 15 Thievery), or press Iriali artifabrians to stop supplying the Fused (DC 16 Intimidation).

- **Break or Reconfigure the Fabrials.** The fabrials sense trespassers and raise alarms unless the wearer holds a special jade coin—but that coin is given only to singers Dymab trusts. A PC who watches Dymab’s workers might notice these coins (DC 14 Perception). Directly approaching an alerter fabrial sets it off, but a PC can quickly sabotage a vital hidden component despite the blaring alarm (DC 15 Crafting). A particularly skilled artifabrian can instead modify the alerter fabrial, changing its function instead of breaking it (DC 18 Crafting).

- **Fooling the Patrols.** The singers under Dymab’s command move about the Dirgehollow to follow any alarms, but they can be manipulated. A PC might scare or trick the patrols into abandoning a site (DC 16 Deception) or lead them on a wild chicken chase away from resistance activity (DC 15 Agility or Athletics).

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Sabotage Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

#### [[Journal/11 - Appendix C Tables|Sabotage Opportunities and Complications]]

### Resolving the Endeavor

To sabotage all six fabrials, the party must accrue 6 successes before 4 failures, or otherwise complete the objective.

**Success.** Dymab’s band of singers are unable to install their network of fabrials. They abandon the project, giving the resistance free rein of the Dirgehollow (for now). Heth thanks the party and suggests they take two of the remaining ***alerter*** fabrials. Heth also gladly upholds his end of the bargain, providing the party with one of the leads on how to reach the Eyes of Pala’s base. Even Shull begrudgingly thanks the PCs for their help.

**Failure.** Dymab’s fabrials are activated widely, stifling the resistance movement in the Dirgehollow. If the party returns to the safehouse, they find it ransacked. Heth was killed in the conflict, leaving Shull to preside over a fractured resistance movement. Bitter at the party’s failure, she still reveals a lead to the location of the Eyes of Pala’s base, but she wants no further contact with the PCs.

> [!example] Roleplaying Dymab
> **Characteristics:** Brutally honest, impatient, bureaucratic.
>
> **Goal:** Administrate Rall Elorim and keep the city cowed and peaceful.
>
> **Appearance:** Dymab (“DAI-mab”; he/him) is a short but long-limbed Fused, with dramatically marbled red-and-white skin.
>
> Dymab is one of the Fused who rule Rall Elorim. As a Deepest One, he can glide through stone, a power he uses to enter buildings unannounced. In conversations, he’s willing to engage in discussion if he has something to gain, and he doesn’t hide his annoyance with Lilinum’s erratic nature. He believes he can run the city by using strict laws, systems, and especially technology. He trusts in the power of innovation to solve people’s problems.

## Collecting Shadows

While the PCs are going about their business in Rall Elorim, they unexpectedly run into Axies the Collector again, likely in the Dirgehollow. After speaking with the Gray Remnant in Rathalas, Axies came to the city in search of the mysterious “shadows of Rall Elorim.” Read the following at any opportune moment, even during Heth’s mission:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> At the feet of a singer warrior, a familiar man with pale blue skin and a wealth of script-like tattoos lies bound in chains—Axies the Collector.\
>      The singer’s eyes glow red and sparks dance menacingly over their forearms.

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Notable Characters/Axies the Collector|Axies]]** has been detained by a **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Stormform Regal|stormform Regal]]** singer after drawing too much attention in his search for shadowspren in the Dirgehollow. A PC can free Axies by defeating the singer in battle or by persuading them to retreat. The latter requires a successful Intimidation test against the singer’s Cognitive defense (16), or a Deception or Persuasion test against their Spiritual defense (14). The singer was sent by Lilinum and reports the interaction to her.

![[pg113_Axies The Collector_CROPPED_PETAR PENEV.webp]]

**Axies** **-** **PETAR PENEV**

If freed, Axies thanks the party and laments his cursed bad luck. He answers the following questions:

- **Why are you here?** “I’ve come to find the shadowspren. The Gray Remnant told me they’re bound in gemstones here, neither living nor dead.”

- **What makes the shadowspren interesting?** “No one’s ever seen one before. Since the Everstorm, I’m convinced they must be bound to singers somehow, like our new ‘Regal’ friends. Shadowforms with shadowspren, lost to time. How poetic.”

- **Where are they?** “I’ve already searched the Dirgehollow for signs of them. If you listen to the shadows, you can hear their songs. They must be in the tunnels beneath the city—and I assume they’re kept with the Herald Pailiah down there in her vault of treasures. I bet your green-painted friends are down there too.”

Axies wants to get into the tunnels beneath the city to continue his research. He offers the PCs one of the leads to Ylt’s location—on the condition that they bring him along.

## Into the Deep

When the party has both leads (described at the beginning of [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change|Finding Ylt]]), they can head to the derelict well, depicted on [[Scenes Index|map 6.2]]. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In the darkest part of the city, you find a crumbling stone well surrounded by debris and deep pools of water.\
>      The shadows between the stalactites are heavier here, and they echo with a forlorn song as the wind blows between them.

A PC who approaches the well and succeeds on a DC 16 Perception test notices three shadowy figures behind nearby prayer statues, a thin line of sooty ash wafting from the well, and recessed footprints in the stone leading to the well (created as Lilinum’s footsteps disintegrated the stone beneath her). Within that well, Lilinum lies in wait.

## Reaching the Well

Lilinum is independently interested in the strange Dirgehollow shadows, and the party’s activities have caught her attention.

If a PC reaches the well, read the following:

**[[Actors/Adversaries/Named/Various/Minor Characters/Lilinum the Devastating One|Lilinum]]** attacks indiscriminately and violently, supported by two **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Warform Singer|warform singers]]** and one **[[Actors/Adversaries/Singer/Fused/Nimbleform Singer|nimbleform singer]]** loyal to her. While the party can’t stop her attack, she’s happy to engage them in banter while slaughtering them. If the PCs talk, they can learn the following either by sharing a strategy to undermine Dymab or by succeeding on a DC 12 Persuasion test:

- Lilinum can share the information in the earlier [[Journal/06 - 06 Precipice of Change#Odium’s Enforcers|Odium’s Enforcers]] section.

- Lilinum describes how her Shardblade screams to her. The screams sound like those of the Blade’s former Shardbearer whom she defeated, a warrior who opposed Odium’s eastern forces. She enjoys the sound.

- Lilinum tells stories of the shadowform singers she has overheard (described in the [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be#Releasing the Shadowform Singers|Releasing the Shadowform Singers]] section in chapter 7.)

### Battlefield Effects

The following effects are active during the combat:

- **Whispers in the Dark.** The dirge of the lost shadowform singers is particularly audible here. Characters who enter the shadowed areas on the map must succeed on a DC 15 Discipline test or be Disoriented until the end of their next turn.

- **Water Pools.** Deep puddles surround the broken well. They are difficult terrain.

If a character gains an Opportunity or Complication, use the Dirgehollow Well Opportunities and Complications table for inspiration.

### Aftermath

After Lilinum and her singer guards are defeated, the party can take Lilinum’s ***Shardblade***. They can then descend down the well into the depths of the vaults beneath the city. As the PCs learned from their leads, the passage is only free of water for one hour while the moon Nomon is high in the sky.

## Respite

After a few hours of slow travel, much of it spent squeezing through dripping, crumbling tunnels, the sound of rushing water greets the party. A cavern opens ahead, and in it stands a 40-foot-high ancient and eroded prayer statue. Water cascades over the statue, merging with a swiftly flowing river that bisects the cavern.

If the party searches the cavern, they find a dry secluded area near the statue; they can safely make camp and take a long rest here if they choose.

If the party tries to press forward, a PC who succeeds on a DC 10 Deduction or Survival test determines the underground river is high because of the tides and it’ll become easier to cross in a few hours when the tides once again recede. If the party decides to cross immediately, they must each succeed on a DC 12 Athletics test or lose one item of their choice to the rushing water.

## Journeying Onward

Having defeated Lilinum and entered the tunnels beneath Rall Elorim, the characters advance to 7th level. Once they cross the underground stream to venture further into the depths, proceed to [[Journal/07 - 07 What Was and What Could Be|chapter 7]].
