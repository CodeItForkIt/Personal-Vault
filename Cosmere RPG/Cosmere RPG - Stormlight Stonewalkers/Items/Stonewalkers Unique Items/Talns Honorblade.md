**Damage** 2d10 spirit; **Range** Melee; **Skill** Heavy Weaponry or Light Weaponry; **Traits** Dangerous, Deadly, Unique; **Expert Traits** Unique (loses Dangerous trait)

Taln’s Honorblade is one of ten unique weapons created by Honor to empower the Heralds in their battle against Odium’s forces. Whoever bonds this legendary blade gains access to the surges of a Stoneward. Like ordinary bonded Shardblades, it can be summoned or dismissed by its bearer, so it can’t be taken unless its bearer is slain.

**Unique Traits.** While you’re bonded to Taln’s Honorblade, it has the same unique traits as a Shard­ blade (see chapter 7 of the Stormlight Handbook), plus the following unique traits:

**Herald of Stonewards.** Your ranks in the [[Items/Surges/Cohesion Surge|Cohesion]] and [[Items/Surges/Tension Surge|Tension]] surge skills increase to 3. If you already have 3 ranks or more in a skill, that skill instead increases by 1 rank, to a maximum of 5. However, if you roll a Complication while making a **Cohesion** or **Tension** test, you must double the Investiture cost of that test.

**Wield Investiture.** If you don’t already have an Investiture pool, you gain one, and your maximum Investiture equals 2 + either your Awareness or Presence (whichever is higher). You can use the [[Items/Stormlight/Breathe Stormlight|Breathe Stormlight]], [[Items/Stormlight/Enhance|Enhance]], and [[Items/Stormlight/Regenerate|Regenerate]] actions.

**Surge Talents.** You gain the [[Items/Surges/Cohesion/Stone Spear|Stone Spear]] talent from the [[Items/Surges/Cohesion/Cohesion Talents|Cohesion talent tree]], and you gain the [[Items/Surges/Tension/Tension Parry|Tension Parry]] talent from the [[Items/Surges/Tension/Tension Talents|Tension talent tree]]. When you gain a level and thus gain a new talent, you can choose it from the Cohesion and Tension paths.

If you lose your bond to Taln’s Honorblade, you lose all skills and talents granted by the Honorblade. If you no longer meet the prerequisites for any **Cohesion** or **Tension** talents you chose when you gained a level, you can replace them using the Self ­Reflection downtime activity (see chapter 9 of the Stormlight Handbook).
