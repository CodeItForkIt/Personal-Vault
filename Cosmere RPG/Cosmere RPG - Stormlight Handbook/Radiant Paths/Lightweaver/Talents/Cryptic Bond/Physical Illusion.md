**Activation:** 2

*By mixing Transformation and Illumination, you create illusions with weight and tangibility.*

Spend 1 **Investiture** or more to infuse a physical illusion in thin air. This illusion follows the usual rules for controlling illusions created with your Illumination surge, and it must be of a size you can create with your ranks in Illumination (see the “Surge Scaling" table below).

#### Surge Scaling

| Ranks | Die | Size                 |
|-------|-----|----------------------|
| 1     | d4  | Small (2.5 feet)     |
| 2     | d6  | Medium (5 feet)      |
| 3     | d8  | Large (10 feet)      |
| 4     | d10 | Huge (15 feet)       |
| 5     | d12 | Gargantuan (20 feet) |

However, unlike other illusions, this physical illusion occupies its space as if it were real, and if it’s Medium or larger, it can be used as cover. Physical objects can’t pass through it, and it weighs half as much as the object or character it appears to be. This illusion has health equal to 5 × your ranks in Transformation, and each of its defenses equals 10 + your Transformation modifier. If the illusion is reduced to 0 health, its infusion ends early and any infused **Investiture** is lost.

When you control your physical illusion, it can interact with its environment. You can spend 1 to task it with something that requires a test or your careful attention, such as attacking someone or writing something.

When the illusion’s task requires a test (such as an attack test or **Atheltics** test), you must be able to sense its target, and you use your Illumination instead of that test’s usual skill. For example, if the illusion is wielding a real weapon, test Illumination but otherwise follow the normal rules for that attack.

While the illusion is wielding an illusionary weapon, it can make a melee attack using Illumination against the Physical defense of a target within its reach. The size of this attack’s damage dice increases with your ranks in Transformation: at 2 ranks, roll 2d6 damage; at 3 ranks, roll 2d8 damage; and so on. As usual, on a hit, add your Illumination modifier to damage, and on a miss, you can spend 1 focus to graze with that attack. The type of damage is either keen or impact based on the illusion’s weapon.
